Does Hair Grow Faster After Big Chop

Written by Christine Jul 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're considering a big chop, you might be wondering if your hair will grow faster once you make the cut. The answer is a bit complicated, but the short answer is no - your hair won't grow faster after a big chop.

Does Cutting Your Hair Affect Its Growth Rate?

The length of your hair doesn't actually have any impact on its growth rate. Hair growth is primarily determined by genetics, hormones, and overall health. So, no matter how long your hair is, it's still going to grow at the same rate.

However, cutting your hair can make it look and feel healthier. When you cut off damaged or split ends, it can help prevent further breakage and give your hair a fresh start. This can make your hair look and feel thicker, which can give the illusion of faster growth.

How to Care for Your Hair After a Big Chop

If you've recently done a big chop or are thinking about it, it's important to take good care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

1. Moisturize Regularly

Keeping your hair moisturized is key to preventing breakage and promoting growth. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Protect Your Hair

When your hair is short, it's more vulnerable to damage from the elements. Protect your hair by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

3. Get Regular Trims

Even though cutting your hair doesn't affect its growth rate, getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

Your hair needs proper nutrition to grow strong and healthy. Eat a diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does hair grow faster if you cut it shorter?

No, cutting your hair shorter doesn't actually affect its growth rate. Hair growth is determined by genetics, hormones, and overall health.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after a big chop?

The amount of time it takes for your hair to grow back after a big chop will vary depending on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it could take several months or even a year to grow back to its previous length.

Does hair grow faster in the summer?

There's no scientific evidence to suggest that hair grows faster in the summer. However, your hair might feel like it's growing faster because you're spending more time outside and getting more vitamin D, which can promote healthy hair growth.

Final Thoughts

While cutting your hair won't make it grow faster, it can help improve the overall health of your hair and prevent breakage. If you're thinking about doing a big chop, make sure to take good care of your hair to promote healthy growth.
