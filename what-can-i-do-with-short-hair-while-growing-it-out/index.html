What Can I Do With Short Hair While Growing It Out

Written by Aprilia May 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're in the process of growing out your short hair, you may be feeling frustrated with the limited styling options available to you. But fear not, there are plenty of ways to switch up your look while you wait for those locks to grow! In this article, we'll explore various hairstyles, tips, and hair care routines to help make your hair growth journey a little more bearable.

Hairstyles for Short Hair

While growing out your hair, it's important to keep it trimmed regularly to avoid split ends and breakage. But that doesn't mean you can't experiment with different styles! Here are a few options:

Pixie Cut

If you're starting with a super short pixie cut, you can play around with different lengths by keeping the sides and back short while letting the top grow out. This will create a layered look that can be styled in various ways.

Bob

A classic bob is a great option for those with slightly longer hair. You can keep it chin-length or shoulder-length, depending on your preference. It's a versatile style that can be straight, wavy, or curly.

Braids

Braids are a great way to keep your hair out of your face while adding some texture to your look. Try a French braid along the hairline or a side braid for a boho vibe.

How to Style Short Hair While Growing It Out

Styling short hair can be tricky, but there are a few tricks to make it easier:

Blow Dry

Use a round brush and blow dryer to add volume and shape to your hair. This will help create the illusion of longer hair while keeping it under control.

Hairspray

Use a light hold hairspray to keep your hair in place. Don't overdo it, though, as too much hairspray can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Accessories

Add some flair to your hair with accessories like headbands, clips, and scarves. These can help keep your hair out of your face while adding some personality to your look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Hair

Here's a simple routine for styling short hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Blow dry hair using a round brush to add volume and shape.
  3. Add hairspray to keep hair in place.
  4. Use accessories like headbands or clips to add some personality.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair While Growing It Out

Here are a few tips to keep your short hair healthy and looking its best:

Trim Regularly

Regular trims will keep split ends at bay, which can lead to breakage and slow down hair growth. Aim for a trim every six to eight weeks.

Protect from Heat Damage

Heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair, so use them sparingly. When you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.

Eat a Balanced Diet

Your hair needs nutrients to grow, so be sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables.

FAQs About Growing Out Short Hair

Q: How long does it take to grow out short hair?

A: It depends on how long you want your hair to be, but on average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

Q: Can I dye my hair while growing it out?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a hair dye that's gentle and won't cause damage to your hair. It's also a good idea to wait until your hair is a little longer before dyeing it, as the dye can cause breakage on shorter strands.

Q: Should I use hair extensions while growing out my hair?

A: It's up to you! Hair extensions can be a great way to add length and volume while you wait for your hair to grow, but they can also be expensive and time-consuming to maintain.

Hair Care Routine for Growing Out Short Hair

Here's a simple hair care routine to keep your short hair healthy:

  1. Wash hair every other day with a gentle shampoo.
  2. Condition hair with a moisturizing conditioner.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle hair while it's wet.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  5. Protect hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Remember, growing out your hair takes time and patience. But with the right styling techniques and hair care routine, you can make the process a little more enjoyable!
