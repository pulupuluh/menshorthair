How To Cut Hair Longer In Front Shorter In Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Feb 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a new and trendy hairstyle, cutting your hair longer in front and shorter in the back is a great option. This style, also known as an angled bob or a graduated bob, can be customized to fit any face shape and hair texture. In this article, we'll show you how to cut hair longer in front shorter in back, step by step.

Description

The longer-in-front, shorter-in-back hairstyle is a popular haircut that has been around for decades. It is a versatile style that can be worn by people of all ages and hair types. The style is characterized by longer layers in the front that gradually get shorter towards the back. This creates a dramatic and edgy look that is perfect for those who want to make a statement with their hair.

How to Cut Hair Longer in Front Shorter in Back

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and will ensure that you get an even cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Section

Starting with the bottom section, comb your hair straight down and trim the ends. Make sure to cut in a straight line and avoid cutting too much off at once.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Section

Next, release the middle section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the bottom section as a guide and cut the middle section to the same length.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle section as a guide and cut the top section to the same length.

Step 6: Blend Your Layers

Once you've cut all three sections of your hair, it's time to blend your layers. To do this, take small sections of hair and hold them straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating a soft and blended look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair longer in front shorter in back:

  • Start with small cuts and gradually work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Consider visiting a professional hairstylist if you're not confident in your cutting skills.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting hair longer in front shorter in back:

Is this hairstyle suitable for all face shapes?

Yes, this hairstyle can be customized to fit any face shape. Your hairstylist can help you determine the best length and angle for your face.

How often should I get this haircut?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

Can I style this haircut in different ways?

Yes, this haircut is versatile and can be styled in many different ways. You can wear it straight, curly, or wavy, and you can also add layers or bangs to change up the look.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair longer in front shorter in back, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.

Cutting your hair longer in front shorter in back is a great way to switch up your hairstyle and add some edge to your look. With these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a trendy and versatile haircut that will turn heads.
