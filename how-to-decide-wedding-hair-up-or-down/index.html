How To Decide Wedding Hair Up Or Down

Written by Petter Jul 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Choosing how to style your hair on your wedding day can be a tough decision. You want to look your best, but you also want to feel comfortable and confident. One of the biggest decisions you'll have to make is whether to wear your hair up or down. In this article, we'll go over how to decide wedding hair up or down and provide some helpful tips along the way.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Decide
  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to Decide

When deciding between wearing your hair up or down, consider the following:

1. Dress Style

Your wedding dress can play a big role in deciding how to style your hair. If you have a dress with intricate detailing at the back, you may want to wear your hair up to show it off. If your dress has a low back, you might want to wear your hair down to cover it.

2. Face Shape

Your face shape can also play a role in deciding how to style your hair. If you have a round face, wearing your hair down may make your face appear fuller. If you have a long face, wearing your hair up can help elongate your face.

3. Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also play a role in deciding how to style your hair. If you have thick, heavy hair, wearing your hair up can help keep you cool and comfortable throughout the day. If you have fine hair, wearing your hair down can help give the appearance of more volume.

4. Wedding Theme

Your wedding theme can also play a role in deciding how to style your hair. If you're having a beach wedding, wearing your hair down can create a more relaxed, bohemian look. If you're having a formal wedding, wearing your hair up can create a more elegant, sophisticated look.

Step by Step

Once you've decided whether you want to wear your hair up or down, follow these steps:

1. Choose Your Hairstyle

Choose the specific hairstyle you want based on your decision to wear your hair up or down. Look for inspiration on Pinterest or in bridal magazines.

2. Book a Trial Run

Book a trial run with your hairstylist to ensure the hairstyle you've chosen works well with your hair texture and face shape.

3. Choose Your Hair Accessories

Choose any hair accessories you want to wear, such as a veil or hairpiece, and bring them to your trial run so your stylist can incorporate them into your hairstyle.

4. Practice Your Look

Practice your look at home to ensure it's comfortable and stays in place throughout the day. If you're wearing your hair up, make sure it's not too tight or causing any discomfort.

Tips

  • Consider the weather when deciding how to style your hair. If it's going to be hot and humid, wearing your hair up can help keep you cool and comfortable.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for input from your bridesmaids, friends, or family members. They may have suggestions you haven't thought of.
  • Remember to bring any hair accessories you want to wear to your trial run so your stylist can incorporate them into your hairstyle.

FAQ

1. What if I can't decide whether to wear my hair up or down?

If you're having trouble deciding, consider a half-up, half-down hairstyle. This can give you the best of both worlds.

2. Should I wash my hair the day of my wedding?

No, it's best to wash your hair the day before your wedding. This will help your hair hold its style better.

3. How do I keep my hair in place throughout the day?

Use plenty of hairspray and bobby pins to keep your hair in place. If you're wearing your hair up, consider using a hair net to help keep it secure.

Hair Care

In the weeks leading up to your wedding, make sure to take extra care of your hair. This can include getting regular trims, deep conditioning treatments, and using heat protectant when styling your hair. On the day of your wedding, try to avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage.

Now that you know how to decide wedding hair up or down, you can make an informed decision based on your dress style, face shape, hair texture, and wedding theme. Remember to practice your look beforehand, and take good care of your hair in the weeks leading up to your wedding. With the right hairstyle, you'll feel confident and beautiful on your special day.
