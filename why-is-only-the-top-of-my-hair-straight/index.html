Why Is Only The Top Of My Hair Straight

Written by Eveline Aug 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you struggling with a hair dilemma where only the top of your hair is straight, and the rest is curly or wavy? If yes, then you are not alone in this. Many people face this problem, and they find it challenging to manage their hair. In this blog post, we will discuss the reasons behind this issue and some tips and tricks to manage it.

Description

Have you ever noticed that only the top section of your hair is straight, while the rest of your hair is curly or wavy? This hair issue is not uncommon among many people, and it can be due to several reasons. The top section of your hair is called the crown area, and it is the most exposed part of your hair.

The crown area is the section where the hair is most affected by environmental factors such as pollution, humidity, and direct sunlight, which can cause damage to the hair. The damage can lead to the loss of curl pattern, and the hair becomes straight.

How to

If you are wondering how to manage this hair issue, then the following tips might help you:

Tip #1: Get a Haircut

Getting a haircut is one of the best ways to manage this issue. A professional hairstylist can cut your hair in a way that can balance the curl pattern throughout your hair. They can also give you a haircut that suits your face shape and hair type.

Tip #2: Use a Curl-Enhancing Product

Using a curl-enhancing product can help you define your curls and reduce the straightness in the crown area. You can use a curl cream, gel, or mousse to enhance your curls. Apply the product to damp hair and scrunch your hair upwards to create curls.

Tip #3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and lead to the loss of curl pattern. If you want to manage this hair issue, then avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers.

Step by Step

If you want to manage this hair issue step by step, then follow the steps below:

Step #1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo that is suitable for your hair type. Rinse your hair thoroughly and avoid using hot water as it can strip the natural oils from your hair.

Step #2: Apply a Conditioner

Apply a conditioner to your hair and focus on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes and rinse it off with cool water.

Step #3: Apply a Curl-Enhancing Product

Apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair while it is still damp. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step #4: Air Dry Your Hair

Air dry your hair by scrunching your hair upwards. Avoid touching your hair while it is drying as it can cause frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to manage this hair issue:

Tip #1: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help you maintain the health of your hair. Use a deep conditioning mask that is suitable for your hair type and leave it on for 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

Tip #2: Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from environmental factors such as pollution, humidity, and direct sunlight. You can use a scarf, hat, or umbrella to protect your hair from these factors.

Tip #3: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can reduce the friction between your hair and the pillow, which can cause frizz and breakage.

FAQ

Q: Why is Only the Top of My Hair Straight?

A: The top section of your hair is the most exposed part of your hair and is most affected by environmental factors such as pollution, humidity, and direct sunlight. The damage can lead to the loss of curl pattern, and the hair becomes straight.

Q: How Can I Manage This Hair Issue?

A: You can manage this hair issue by getting a haircut, using a curl-enhancing product, avoiding heat styling, deep conditioning your hair, protecting your hair, and using a silk pillowcase.

Hair Care

Managing your hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques, you can achieve healthy and beautiful hair. It is essential to take care of your hair and protect it from environmental factors that can damage your hair. Follow the tips and tricks mentioned in this blog post to manage the hair issue where only the top of your hair is straight.
