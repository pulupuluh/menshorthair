How To Cut Curtain Bangs Like A Professional A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curtain bangs are a popular hairstyle that can add a chic and stylish touch to any look. However, cutting them yourself can be a daunting task. In this guide, we will take you through the steps to achieve the perfect curtain bangs in the comfort of your own home.

Description

Curtain bangs are a type of fringe that is parted in the middle, framing the face on each side. They are versatile and can be styled in different ways, making them a popular choice among many women. Cutting curtain bangs at home can save you money and time spent at the salon, but it requires careful attention to detail to get them just right.

How to Cut Curtain Bangs Like a Professional

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before getting started, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It is essential to start with clean and dry hair to ensure accuracy when cutting your bangs. Blow-dry your hair and straighten it if necessary.

Step 3: Part Your Hair in the Middle

Using a comb, create a center part that runs from your forehead to the nape of your neck. This will help you create even and symmetrical bangs.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Clip away the hair you don't want to cut, leaving only the hair that will become your bangs. It is essential to work with small sections of hair to ensure precision.

Step 5: Determine the Length

Decide how long you want your bangs to be. Start with longer bangs and gradually cut them shorter if necessary. Remember to err on the side of caution and cut less rather than more.

Step 6: Cut the Bangs

Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across with your scissors. Make sure to cut in a downward motion to avoid uneven lines.

Step 7: Check for Symmetry

Release the clipped sections and use a mirror to check for symmetry. If necessary, make any small adjustments to even out the length on both sides.

Step 8: Style Your Bangs

Using a round brush, blow-dry your bangs to give them volume and shape. You can also use a flat iron to straighten or add curls to your bangs for a different look.

Tips for Cutting Curtain Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with these tips, you can achieve a professional look:

  • Start with longer bangs and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Work with small sections of hair to ensure accuracy.
  • Check for symmetry as you go.
  • Remember to cut less rather than more to avoid mistakes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I trim my curtain bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how regularly you style your bangs. It is recommended to trim them every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and length.

Can I cut my curtain bangs with regular scissors?

While it is possible to cut your bangs with regular scissors, it is not recommended. Sharp scissors are essential to achieving a clean and precise cut.

Can I style my curtain bangs differently?

Yes! Curtain bangs are versatile and can be styled in different ways. You can add curls or waves to your bangs for a more playful look, or straighten them for a sleeker style.

Hair Care for Curtain Bangs

To keep your curtain bangs looking their best, follow these tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools on your hair.
  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid over-washing and drying out your hair.
  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too often to prevent oil buildup.

Cutting curtain bangs like a professional can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the perfect look in no time. Remember to take your time, work with small sections of hair, and check for symmetry as you go. Happy cutting!
