How To Cut Hair With Clippers By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by William May 01, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts every month? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair with clippers? If so, you’ve come to the right place! In this step-by-step guide, we’ll show you how to cut hair with clippers by yourself.

How to Cut Hair with Clippers by Yourself

Cutting your own hair with clippers can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Cape or old towel

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

When it comes to clippers, there are a variety of options to choose from. The most important factor is the blade size. For beginners, a #4 or #5 blade is recommended. These blades are longer and easier to control.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the length and texture of your hair.

Step 3: Cut the Sides and Back

Start by attaching the #4 or #5 blade to your clippers. Begin at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up the back of your head. Use the comb to guide the clippers and create an even cut. Repeat this process on both sides of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Next, switch to a longer blade (such as a #6 or #7) and begin cutting the top of your head. Use the scissors to trim any longer pieces.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use the #4 or #5 blade to blend the sides and top of your hair together. Be sure to use the comb to guide the clippers and create a seamless transition.

Step 6: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Use the mirror to check the back and sides of your head for any uneven spots. Use the scissors to trim any longer pieces.

Step 7: Clean Up Your Neckline

Use the clippers or scissors to clean up your neckline. Be sure to follow the natural shape of your hairline.

Step 8: Clean Up Your Ears

Use the clippers or scissors to clean up any hair around your ears. Be sure to follow the natural shape of your ears.

Step 9: Clean Up Your Sideburns

Use the clippers or scissors to clean up your sideburns. Be sure to follow the natural shape of your sideburns.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. Use hair gel or pomade to add texture and hold.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Clippers by Yourself

  • Start with a longer blade and work your way down to a shorter blade.
  • Use the comb to guide the clippers and create an even cut.
  • Always cut against the grain for a closer cut.
  • Take your time and go slowly.
  • Use a mirror to check the back and sides of your head.

FAQs About Cutting Hair with Clippers by Yourself

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers. It may take some practice, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done.

What type of clippers should I use?

For beginners, a #4 or #5 blade is recommended. These blades are longer and easier to control.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. Use the scissors to trim any longer pieces and try to blend the hair together.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair with Clippers

After cutting your hair with clippers, it’s important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair (such as blow dryers or straighteners).
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.

With these tips and techniques, you can save money and cut your own hair with clippers. Remember to take your time and go slowly. With practice, you’ll be a pro in no time!
