How To Cut Your Own Short Layered Hair At Home

Written by Christine Aug 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for an easy and affordable way to maintain your short layered haircut, cutting your own hair at home can be a great option. Not only will you save money on salon visits, but you'll also have more control over the final result. In this article, we'll show you step-by-step how to cut your own short layered hair at home, as well as provide some tips and frequently asked questions.

Description: What You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Hand mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

How to: Cutting Your Short Layered Hair at Home

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair.

Make sure your hair is clean and completely dry before you start cutting. This will help you see the true length and texture of your hair, and will make it easier to cut evenly.

Step 2: Section your hair.

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and clip each side up. Then, divide each side into two sections, clipping the top sections away from the bottom sections. This will create four sections of hair.

Step 3: Cut the bottom section of hair.

Starting with the bottom section of hair, use your comb to create a straight line across the back of your head. Then, use your scissors to cut straight across that line. This will create a baseline for the rest of your haircut.

Step 4: Cut the middle section of hair.

Next, unclip the middle section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will create the layers for your haircut.

Step 5: Cut the top section of hair.

Unclip the top section of hair and repeat the same process as the middle section, cutting at an angle to create layers. Be sure to blend the middle and top sections of hair together.

Step 6: Check for symmetry.

Use your hand mirror to check the back of your head for symmetry. Make any necessary adjustments by trimming small pieces of hair until both sides are even.

Step 7: Style your hair.

Once you're happy with the length and shape of your haircut, style your hair as desired using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Short Layered Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts and trim as you go to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use hair clips to keep the sections of hair separate and organized.
  • Keep your scissors sharp to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as wet hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  • Take breaks if you need to, and don't rush the process.

FAQ: Cutting Your Own Short Layered Hair at Home

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair at home. However, it's important to cut it when it's dry and to use a technique that takes into account the natural curl pattern of your hair.

How often should I cut my short layered hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain your short layered haircut. However, if you're cutting your own hair at home, you can adjust the timing based on your personal preference.

What if I mess up my haircut?

If you make a mistake while cutting your own hair, don't panic. You can always trim it shorter or seek help from a professional stylist to fix any major issues.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Short Layered Hair at Home

After cutting your own hair at home, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment every few weeks to keep your hair healthy and strong.

With these tips and step-by-step instructions, you should be able to cut your own short layered hair at home with ease. Happy cutting!
