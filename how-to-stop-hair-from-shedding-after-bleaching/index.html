How To Stop Hair From Shedding After Bleaching

Written by Robby Sep 10, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you worried about your hair shedding after bleaching? Bleaching your hair can be a fun and exciting way to change your look, but it can also be damaging to your hair. Bleaching can cause your hair to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage, which can lead to shedding. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks to stop hair from shedding after bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair from Shedding After Bleaching
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding After Bleaching
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Bleaching

How to Stop Hair from Shedding After Bleaching

If you want to stop your hair from shedding after bleaching, you need to take extra care of your hair. Here are some steps you can follow:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help repair the damage caused by bleaching. It can strengthen your hair, making it less prone to breakage and shedding. You can use a protein treatment once a week or as recommended by your hairstylist.

2. Moisturize Your Hair

Bleaching can make your hair dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage and shedding. To prevent this, you need to moisturize your hair. You can use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner or a hair mask to keep your hair hydrated.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can further damage your hair, making it more prone to shedding. If possible, avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize the damage.

4. Don't Brush Your Hair When Wet

Wet hair is more fragile and prone to breakage. If you brush your hair when it's wet, you can cause more damage, leading to shedding. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to remove any knots or tangles.

5. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can lead to shedding. You should trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent breakage.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop hair from shedding after bleaching:

Step 1: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a week or as recommended by your hairstylist. Leave it on for the recommended time, then rinse it off with warm water.

Step 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner or a hair mask to keep your hair hydrated. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it off with warm water.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize the damage.

Step 4: Don't Brush Your Hair When Wet

Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to remove any knots or tangles. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet to prevent breakage and shedding.

Step 5: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Get regular trims every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding After Bleaching

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair shedding after bleaching:

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and make it prone to shedding. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent breakage and shedding. Silk is gentle on your hair and can help prevent tangles.

4. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet can help keep your hair healthy and prevent shedding. Eat foods rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and biotin.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does it take for hair to stop shedding after bleaching?

It can take several weeks or months for your hair to stop shedding after bleaching. Be patient and continue to take good care of your hair.

2. Can hair shedding after bleaching be permanent?

In some cases, hair shedding after bleaching can be permanent. If you're experiencing excessive shedding, consult a hairstylist or a dermatologist.

3. Can I dye my hair after bleaching it?

Yes, you can dye your hair after bleaching it. However, you should wait at least two weeks before dyeing your hair to allow it to recover.

Hair Care After Bleaching

Bleaching can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to take extra care of your hair after bleaching. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Color-Safe Shampoo

Use a color-safe shampoo to prevent your hair from fading or becoming brassy.

2. Avoid Chlorine

Avoid swimming in chlorinated water, as it can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage and shedding.

3. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, causing breakage and shedding.

5. Limit Heat Styling

Limit your use of heat styling tools to prevent further damage to your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can stop your hair from shedding after bleaching. Remember to take extra care of your hair, and be patient as it recovers from the damage caused by bleaching.
