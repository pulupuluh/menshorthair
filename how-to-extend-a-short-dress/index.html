How To Extend A Short Dress

Written by Frank Jun 02, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you struggling to find the perfect dress for your upcoming event, but everything you try on is just too short? Don't worry, there's a solution! In this article, we'll share with you some tips and tricks on how to extend a short dress to make it more suitable for your needs. Read on to find out more!

Description

Short dresses can be a fun and flirty choice for many occasions, but they're not always appropriate or comfortable for everyone. Whether you're attending a formal event or just prefer a longer length, extending a short dress is easier than you might think. With a few simple alterations or accessories, you can transform your dress into a more modest and elegant style.

How to Extend a Short Dress

There are several ways to extend a short dress, depending on your preferences and the style of the dress. Here are some ideas:

Adding a Skirt or Petticoat

If the dress is too short for your liking, you can add a skirt or petticoat underneath to add length and volume. This works particularly well for dresses with a flared or A-line shape. Choose a skirt or petticoat that matches the color and style of your dress, and make sure it's the right length to show just enough of the hemline.

Wearing Leggings or Tights

An easy and comfortable way to extend a short dress is to wear leggings or tights underneath. This is a great option for colder weather or when you want a more casual look. Choose a pair of leggings or tights that complement the color and style of your dress, and make sure they're opaque enough to cover your legs completely.

Adding a Lace or Tulle Hemline

If you want to add some extra length and detail to your dress, you can sew a lace or tulle hemline onto the bottom. This will not only extend the dress, but also create a beautiful and feminine look. Choose a lace or tulle fabric that matches the color and style of your dress, and sew it onto the hemline with a simple running stitch.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to extend a short dress:

  1. Decide on the method you want to use to extend the dress (e.g. adding a skirt, wearing leggings, adding a hemline).
  2. Choose the materials you'll need, such as a skirt, leggings, lace or tulle fabric, needle and thread.
  3. Try on the dress and measure how much extra length you need.
  4. If you're adding a skirt or petticoat, attach it to the dress with safety pins or sew it on with a needle and thread.
  5. If you're wearing leggings or tights, choose a pair that matches the dress and put them on underneath.
  6. If you're adding a lace or tulle hemline, cut the fabric to the right length and sew it onto the hemline with a running stitch.
  7. Try on the dress again to make sure the length is right and everything looks good.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when extending a short dress:

  • Choose materials and colors that complement the dress and create a cohesive look.
  • Be careful not to add too much length or volume, as this can overwhelm the dress and make it look awkward.
  • Test out different methods and accessories to find the one that works best for you and the dress.
  • Consider the occasion and dress code when extending a dress, as some styles may not be appropriate for formal events.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about extending a short dress:

Q: Will extending a short dress ruin the original design?

A: Not necessarily. By choosing the right accessories and alterations, you can enhance the original design and create a unique and personalized look.

Q: Can I still wear a short dress after extending it?

A: Yes, you can. Depending on the method you use to extend the dress, you can always remove the extra length and go back to the original style.

Q: What if I don't know how to sew or alter clothing?

A: You can always take your dress to a professional tailor or seamstress, who can help you extend it in a way that suits your needs and style.

Hair Care

When wearing a short dress, it's important to choose a hairstyle that complements the overall look. Here are some ideas:

  • For a formal event, consider an updo or sleek ponytail to create a sophisticated and elegant style.
  • For a more casual look, try loose waves or a messy bun to add some texture and volume.
  • Choose accessories that match the dress and hairstyle, such as a statement necklace or earrings.
  • Don't forget to use hair products that keep your style in place and protect your hair from damage.

With these tips and tricks, you can extend a short dress and create a stylish and comfortable look for any occasion. Whether you choose to add a skirt, wear leggings, or sew a hemline, the possibilities are endless. Give it a try and see how you can transform your wardrobe!
