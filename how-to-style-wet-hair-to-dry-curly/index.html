How To Style Wet Hair To Dry Curly

Written by William Jul 06, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of straightening your hair every day? Do you want to embrace your natural curls? Styling wet hair to dry curly is easier than you might think. In this blog post, we will show you how to achieve beautiful, bouncy curls without using any heat. Follow these simple steps, and you'll be rocking your natural curls in no time!

How to

The first step in styling wet hair to dry curly is to wash your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. Make sure to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. After washing, gently pat your hair dry with a towel. Avoid rubbing your hair, as this can cause frizz.

Next, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. This will help to keep your curls hydrated and prevent frizz. Apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Once you have applied the leave-in conditioner, it's time to apply a curl-defining product. This can be a cream, gel, or mousse, depending on your hair type. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly from root to tip.

Next, divide your hair into sections. Start at the bottom and work your way up. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle each section, making sure to separate your curls. This will help to define your curls and prevent them from clumping together.

Once you have detangled each section, scrunch your hair upwards towards your scalp. This will encourage your curls to form and create volume. Repeat this process for each section until your entire head is scrunched.

Finally, let your hair air dry. Avoid using a hairdryer, as this can cause frizz and damage your curls. Once your hair is completely dry, scrunch it again to break up any crunchiness from the product. You can also use a little bit of oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. This will help to reduce frizz.
  • Don't touch your hair while it's drying. This can disturb your curls and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using too much product. Start with a small amount and add more if needed.
  • If you have very thick hair, you may need to clip your hair up while it's drying to prevent it from becoming too heavy and pulling your curls down.

FAQ

Can I use a hairdryer to dry my hair?

Avoid using a hairdryer, as this can cause frizz and damage your curls. It's best to let your hair air dry.

How often should I wash my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so you may not need to wash it as often. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

What should I do if my curls are flat?

If your curls are flat, you may need to add some volume. Try flipping your hair upside down and scrunching it from the bottom up. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to add volume.

Hair Care

To keep your curls looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can style wet hair to dry curly and embrace your natural curls. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type. Happy styling!
