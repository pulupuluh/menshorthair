How To Thin Out Short Thick Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 16, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Do you have short thick hair that feels heavy and unmanageable? Thinning out your hair can be a great solution to make it more manageable and easier to style. While you can go to a salon to have your hair thinned out, it can be expensive and time-consuming. Fortunately, you can also thin out your hair at home with the right tools and techniques. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out short thick hair at home in a few easy steps.

Description

Thinning out your hair involves removing some of the bulk and weight from your hair strands. This can help your hair look and feel lighter, bouncier, and more voluminous. Thinning out your hair can also make it easier to style and manage, especially if you have thick, coarse hair that tends to get frizzy or tangled easily. However, it's important to note that thinning out your hair too much or too often can damage your hair and make it weaker or more prone to breakage.

How to Thin Out Short Thick Hair at Home: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start thinning out your hair, you'll need to gather some tools and materials. Here are the things you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils that might make it harder to cut. Pat your hair gently with a towel to remove excess water, then let it air-dry or blow-dry it on a low heat setting. Your hair should be clean, dry, and tangle-free before you start thinning it out.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. You can either create a parting down the middle of your head and then divide your hair into two halves, or you can create a zig-zag parting from the crown of your head to the nape of your neck to create smaller sections. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 4: Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to make small, diagonal cuts into the hair. Make sure to cut at an angle and not straight across, as this will create a more natural look. Cut only a small amount of hair at a time, and check your progress in the mirror as you go. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

Once you've thinned out one section of hair, move on to the next section and repeat the same process. Make sure to blend the hair from one section to the next so that there are no visible lines or gaps. Use your comb to comb through your hair and check for any uneven spots or areas that need more thinning.

Step 6: Finish Up

Once you've thinned out all of your hair, comb it through and style it as desired. You should notice that your hair feels lighter and more voluminous, with less bulk and weight. If you're happy with the results, you're done!

Tips for Thinning Out Short Thick Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up gradually. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Don't thin out your hair too often, as this can damage your hair and make it weaker.
  • Don't use dull scissors or other cutting tools, as this can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning out your hair can take a while, especially if you have a lot of hair.

FAQ

Is it safe to thin out short thick hair at home?

Yes, as long as you use the right tools and techniques and don't thin out your hair too much or too often. It's important to be careful and patient when thinning out your hair, and to seek professional help if you're unsure or uncomfortable doing it yourself.

How often should I thin out my short thick hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly your hair grows. As a general rule, you should only thin out your hair every few months, or whenever you feel like it's getting too heavy or unmanageable. Over-thinning your hair can damage it and make it weaker or more prone to breakage.

Can I use thinning shears to thin out my short thick hair?

Thinning shears can be a useful tool for thinning out your hair, but they should be used with caution. Thinning shears can create uneven cuts or damage your hair if used improperly, so it's important to get professional advice or training before using them at home.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Short Thick Hair

Once you've thinned out your short thick hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat or styling products, as these can damage your hair and make it frizzy or dry.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your hair.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to nourish your hair from the inside out.

Thinning out your short thick hair at home can be a great way to make it more manageable and stylish. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a lighter, bouncier, and more voluminous look that you'll love. Just remember to be patient, take your time, and take care of your hair properly afterwards.
