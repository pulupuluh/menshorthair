How To Get Rid Of A Disconnected Undercut

Written by Eveline Feb 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've ever had a disconnected undercut, you know that it can be a tricky style to maintain. While it can look great when it's freshly cut, it can quickly become unruly and difficult to style as it grows out. But fear not! With a few simple tips and tricks, you can easily get rid of a disconnected undercut and keep your hair looking great.

Description

A disconnected undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the top of the head is longer than the hair on the sides and back. The longer hair on top is disconnected from the shorter hair on the sides and back, creating a dramatic contrast. This style has been popular in recent years, but it can be difficult to maintain as the hair grows out.

How to

There are a few different ways to get rid of a disconnected undercut, depending on how much length you want to remove and how drastic a change you're looking for. Here are a few options:

Option 1: Grow out the undercut

If you're not ready for a drastic change, you can simply let the undercut grow out. This will take some time, but it will eventually blend in with the rest of your hair. In the meantime, you can style your hair to minimize the contrast between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Option 2: Blend the hair with scissors

If you want a more subtle change, you can blend the hair using scissors. This involves cutting the longer hair on top so that it gradually blends into the shorter hair on the sides and back. This can be a bit tricky, so it's best to leave it to a professional stylist.

Option 3: Buzz the hair

If you're ready for a more drastic change, you can buzz the hair on the sides and back so that it's the same length as the hair on top. This will create a more uniform look and eliminate the disconnected undercut altogether.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting rid of a disconnected undercut:

Step 1: Decide how much hair you want to remove

Before you do anything, decide how much length you want to remove. If you're not sure, consult with a stylist.

Step 2: Choose the right option

Choose the option that best suits your needs: growing out the undercut, blending the hair with scissors, or buzzing the hair.

Step 3: Book an appointment with a stylist

If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, book an appointment with a stylist who has experience with disconnected undercuts.

Step 4: Communicate your desired outcome

Make sure to communicate your desired outcome with your stylist. Show them pictures if you can so they have a clear idea of what you're looking for.

Step 5: Sit back and relax

Once you're in the stylist's chair, sit back and relax. They'll take care of the rest!

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when getting rid of a disconnected undercut:

  • Be patient. Growing out an undercut can take some time.
  • Consult with a stylist if you're not sure what to do.
  • Use styling products to help blend the hair as it grows out.
  • Consider trying a new hairstyle altogether!

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about getting rid of a disconnected undercut:

Can I get rid of a disconnected undercut myself?

While it's technically possible to get rid of a disconnected undercut yourself, it's best to leave it to a professional stylist. They'll have the tools and expertise to get the job done right.

How long will it take to grow out a disconnected undercut?

It can take several months to a year to fully grow out a disconnected undercut. The exact timeline will depend on the length of your hair and how quickly it grows.

Will getting rid of a disconnected undercut damage my hair?

No, getting rid of a disconnected undercut shouldn't damage your hair as long as it's done properly. However, if you're buzzing the hair, you may notice a bit of breakage or split ends.

Hair Care

Once you've gotten rid of your disconnected undercut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

By following these tips and getting rid of your disconnected undercut, you can have healthy, stylish hair that looks great every day!
