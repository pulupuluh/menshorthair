How To Style Your Hair When Growing Out A Pixie Cut

Are you growing out your pixie cut, but having trouble styling your hair in the meantime? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for styling your hair during the awkward in-between phase of growing out a pixie cut. Whether you're dealing with awkward layers or just trying to keep your hair out of your face, we've got the solutions you need.

How to Style Your Hair When Growing Out a Pixie Cut

The first thing you need to do when growing out a pixie cut is to be patient. It can take several months to a year to grow out your hair to a length that you're happy with. In the meantime, there are several ways to style your hair to make the transition easier.

1. Embrace the Layers

As your hair grows out, you'll likely have some awkward layers. Embrace them by using a texturizing spray or mousse to give your hair some volume and definition. You can also use a curling iron or flat iron to create loose waves or curls that will help blend the layers together.

2. Accessorize

When all else fails, accessorize! Headbands, scarves, and hats are all great options for keeping your hair out of your face while you're growing it out. Plus, they can add a fun pop of color or pattern to your outfit.

3. Experiment with Parting

Changing up your part can make a big difference in how your hair looks. Try a deep side part, a middle part, or even a zigzag part to mix things up. You can also use bobby pins or hair clips to pin back any awkward pieces that won't stay in place.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps for an easy and stylish way to manage your growing out pixie cut:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, then towel dry or blow dry until your hair is about 80% dry.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product such as a texturizing spray or mousse to add volume and definition to your hair.

Step 3: Part Your Hair

Part your hair in a way that flatters your face shape and works with your hair's natural texture.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Use a curling iron or flat iron to create loose waves or curls, or use bobby pins or hair clips to pin back any awkward pieces that won't stay in place.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish with a light mist of hairspray to keep your hair in place all day.

Tips for Styling Your Hair

Here are a few additional tips for styling your hair when growing out a pixie cut:

1. Invest in Good Hair Accessories

Headbands, scarves, and hats are all great options for keeping your hair out of your face. Invest in a few high-quality accessories that you love and that work with your personal style.

2. Get Regular Trims

Even though you're growing out your hair, it's still important to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and to prevent split ends.

3. Be Patient

Growing out a pixie cut can be frustrating, but remember that it's a process. Be patient and try to enjoy the journey!

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

A: It can take anywhere from several months to a year or more to grow out a pixie cut. The length of time will depend on your hair's natural growth rate and the length you're trying to achieve.

Q: Can I still use heat styling tools when growing out a pixie cut?

A: Yes, you can still use heat styling tools such as curling irons and flat irons when growing out a pixie cut. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: What should I do if I don't like how my hair looks while growing out a pixie cut?

A: If you're not happy with how your hair looks while growing out a pixie cut, try experimenting with different styles and products to find what works best for you. You can also consult with a hairstylist for advice on how to style your hair during the awkward in-between phase.

Hair Care Tips

Here are a few additional hair care tips to keep in mind while growing out a pixie cut:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and strong.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Avoid Over-Brushing

Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can cause breakage and split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your hair with confidence while growing out a pixie cut. Remember to be patient and have fun with your hair!
