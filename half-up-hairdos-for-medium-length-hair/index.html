Half Up Hairdos For Medium Length Hair A Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Medium length hair can be a bit tricky to style at times, but half up hairdos are a great way to add some variety to your everyday look. Whether you're heading to work or out for a night on the town, a half up hairstyle can bring some much-needed volume and texture to your hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through some simple yet stylish half up hairdos for medium length hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Half Up Your Medium Length Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Half Up Hairdos
  • Tips for Styling Half Up Hairdos
  • FAQs about Half Up Hairdos for Medium Length Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Medium Length Hair

How to Half Up Your Medium Length Hair

Before we dive into the different half up hairdos you can try, let's go over the basic steps for creating a half up hairstyle:

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and use a hair tie or clip to secure it in place.
  3. You can leave the rest of your hair down or style it in a way that complements your half up hairdo.
  4. If you want a more polished look, you can use a curling iron or straightener to add some waves or curls to your hair.
  5. Finish off with some hairspray or other styling products to keep your hair in place.

Step-by-Step Guide to Half Up Hairdos

Now that you know how to create a basic half up hairstyle, let's explore some different ways you can style your hair:

The Classic Half Up

This is the most straightforward half up hairstyle. Simply gather a section of hair from the crown of your head and secure it with a hair tie or clip. You can leave the rest of your hair down or style it in loose waves or curls.

The Braided Half Up

If you're feeling a bit more adventurous, try adding a braid to your half up hairstyle. You can do a classic three-strand braid or try something more intricate like a fishtail or Dutch braid. The braid can be wrapped around the hair tie or left to hang loose.

The Twisted Half Up

This hairstyle involves twisting two sections of hair and securing them at the back of your head. To achieve this look, take two sections of hair from either side of your head and twist them together. Secure them at the back of your head with a hair tie or clip.

Tips for Styling Half Up Hairdos

Here are some tips to help you perfect your half up hairstyle:

  • Experiment with different hair ties, clips, and accessories to add some variety to your look.
  • If you have fine or thin hair, try teasing the crown of your head before creating your half up hairstyle to add some volume.
  • Use some dry shampoo or texturizing spray to add some grip and texture to your hair before styling.
  • Don't be afraid to play around with different braids, twists, and knots to create unique half up hairstyles.

FAQs about Half Up Hairdos for Medium Length Hair

Can I create a half up hairstyle with short hair?

While it may be a bit more challenging, you can still create a half up hairstyle with short hair. Use bobby pins or hair clips to secure the hair at the back of your head, and leave the rest of your hair down.

What hair accessories can I use for my half up hairstyle?

You can use a variety of hair accessories to add some flair to your half up hairstyle. Some popular options include hair ties, scrunchies, barrettes, and headbands.

How can I make my half up hairstyle last all day?

Using styling products like hairspray or texturizing spray can help keep your half up hairstyle in place all day. You can also try using bobby pins or hair clips to secure any loose sections of hair.

Hair Care Tips for Medium Length Hair

No matter what half up hairstyle you choose, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips to help you maintain your medium length hair:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush and use it gently to avoid pulling or breaking your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create a variety of half up hairdos for medium length hair. Whether you're going for a casual or formal look, a half up hairstyle is a great way to switch things up and add some volume to your hair.
