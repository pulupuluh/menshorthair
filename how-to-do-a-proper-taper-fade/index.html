How To Do A Proper Taper Fade Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 17, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking to keep up with the latest men's hairstyle trends, a proper taper fade is a must-have. Whether you're a barber or just want to learn how to do it yourself, this step-by-step guide will teach you how to do a proper taper fade in relaxed English language.

Description

A taper fade is a popular hairstyle that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter as it moves down the sides and back of the head. It leaves a clean, polished look that is perfect for any occasion. This style works well with all hair types, including curly, straight, and wavy hair.

How to Do a Proper Taper Fade

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to do a proper taper fade:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a few tools to get started, including:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel to cover the client’s shoulders
  • Clipper guards of different sizes
  • Brush to remove hair
  • Water spray bottle

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Wash the hair with shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Dry the hair completely before starting to cut.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Using the clippers and a guard, decide on the length of the hair you want to keep at the top of the head. This will usually be between 1 and 3 inches, depending on the client's preference.

Step 4: Start the Fade

Using a shorter guard, start to fade the hair from the top to the sides and back. Use longer guards as you move down the head, creating a gradient effect. Blend the hair in between the different guard lengths using the clippers or scissors.

Step 5: Define the Hairline

Use the clippers to define the hairline around the ears and neckline. This will give the haircut a clean and polished look.

Step 6: Check for Balance

Check for balance and symmetry by standing in front of the client and looking at the cut from different angles. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that the fade is even on both sides.

Step 7: Finish the Cut

Brush away any loose hair and use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that were missed during the fading process. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for a Perfect Taper Fade

Follow these tips to achieve a perfect taper fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to shorter guards to create a natural gradient effect.
  • Use a comb to blend the hair between different guard lengths.
  • Use scissors to trim any stray hairs that were missed during the fading process.
  • Check for balance and symmetry by standing in front of the client and looking at the cut from different angles.
  • Finish the cut with a style that complements the client's face shape and hair texture.

FAQ

1. How often should I get a taper fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. Most people get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain a fresh look.

2. Can I do a taper fade at home?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can do a taper fade at home. Just be sure to follow the steps carefully and take your time.

3. Will a taper fade work with my hair type?

Yes, a taper fade works well with all hair types, including curly, straight, and wavy hair.

Hair Care

To maintain a proper taper fade, it's important to take good care of your hair. This includes washing and conditioning regularly, using a moisturizing product, and avoiding excessive heat styling. Regular haircuts every 4-6 weeks will also help keep your fade looking fresh.

Now that you know how to do a proper taper fade, you can keep up with the latest men's hairstyle trends and impress your clients with your skills. Follow these steps carefully and don't be afraid to experiment with different guard lengths and styles to find the perfect look for your client.
