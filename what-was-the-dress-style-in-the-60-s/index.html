What Was The Dress Style In The 60 S

What Was the Dress Style in the 60's?

Description

The 1960's were a time of great change in fashion. The previous decade had been dominated by conservative styles, but the 60's saw a shift towards more youthful and daring looks. The dress style in the 60's was characterized by bright colors, bold patterns, and a rejection of traditional gender roles. Women's clothing became more relaxed and comfortable, with mini skirts and shift dresses replacing the restrictive styles of the past. Men's fashion also evolved, with the emergence of mod culture and the iconic Beatles haircut. Read on to learn more about what was the dress style in the 60's and how you can recreate these retro looks today.

How to

Recreate 60's Fashion Today

One way to incorporate 60's style into your wardrobe is to look for vintage clothing. Thrift stores and online marketplaces are great places to find authentic pieces from the era. Look for mini skirts, shift dresses, and bell-bottom pants in bright colors and bold patterns.

If you prefer to buy new clothing, many modern brands offer retro-inspired pieces that capture the spirit of the 60's. Look for A-line skirts, high-waisted pants, and tie-dye prints.

Accessorize with 60's-Inspired Accessories

Accessories were an important part of 60's fashion. Look for oversized sunglasses, headbands, and hoop earrings to add a retro touch to your outfit. Men can opt for skinny ties and stylish fedoras.

Don't forget about footwear! Platform shoes were all the rage in the 60's, and they're still a popular choice today. Look for chunky heels or sandals with thick soles.

Step by Step

To create a 60's-inspired look, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a bright, bold piece of clothing as the focal point of your outfit.
  2. Add accessories like oversized sunglasses, headbands, or hoop earrings.
  3. Complete the look with platform shoes or chunky heels.

Tips

  • Don't be afraid to mix and match patterns and colors.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, like a bouffant or a sleek bob.
  • Look for vintage clothing in good condition and have it tailored to fit you perfectly.

FAQ

What were popular fabrics in the 60's?

Cotton, polyester, and nylon were popular fabrics in the 60's. Synthetic materials were also on the rise, as they were cheaper and easier to care for than natural fibers.

Were there any iconic designers in the 60's?

Yes, many iconic designers emerged in the 60's, including Mary Quant, André Courrèges, and Paco Rabanne. These designers were known for their innovative use of materials and bold designs.

Hair Care

Hair was an important part of 60's fashion. Women's hairstyles were often voluminous and styled with large rollers or backcombing. The bouffant, a hairstyle that involved teasing the hair upwards and securing it with hairspray, was particularly popular. Men's hairstyles were shorter than in previous decades, with the Beatles haircut becoming a cultural phenomenon.

To recreate these hairstyles today, invest in some good quality styling products like hairspray and mousse. Look for tutorials online or visit a professional stylist to get the look just right.
