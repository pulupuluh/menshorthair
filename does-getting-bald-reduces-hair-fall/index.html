Does Getting Bald Reduce Hair Fall Separating Fact From Fiction

Written by Bardi Aug 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

The idea that getting bald reduces hair fall is a common myth that has been circulating for years. While it is true that bald individuals have less hair to lose, it is not necessarily true that the act of going bald reduces hair fall. In this article, we will delve into the truth behind this myth and provide you with helpful tips on how to reduce hair fall and take care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • What Causes Hair Fall?
  • Does Getting Bald Reduce Hair Fall?
  • How to Reduce Hair Fall?
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQ

What Causes Hair Fall?

There are several reasons why hair fall occurs. Some of the most common reasons include:

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes
  • Poor nutrition
  • Stress
  • Medications
  • Hairstyles that pull on the hair

If you are experiencing hair fall, it is important to identify the root cause before seeking treatment. This will help you identify the best course of action to take.

Does Getting Bald Reduce Hair Fall?

The short answer is no. While it is true that bald individuals have less hair to lose, the act of going bald does not reduce hair fall. In fact, hair fall can still occur on the remaining hair on the scalp. Baldness is simply a result of hair follicles shrinking and eventually ceasing to produce hair.

However, there are some treatments for baldness that can help reduce hair fall. These treatments include:

  • Medications like minoxidil and finasteride
  • Hair transplant surgery
  • Scalp micropigmentation

If you are experiencing hair fall, it is important to consult a healthcare professional to identify the root cause and determine the best course of treatment for you.

How to Reduce Hair Fall?

There are several things you can do to reduce hair fall. These include:

  • Eating a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Reducing stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques
  • Avoiding hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Using gentle hair care products that do not contain harsh chemicals
  • Avoiding heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons

If you are experiencing hair fall, it is important to be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh treatments that can further damage your hair.

Tips for Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for reducing hair fall and promoting healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Condition your hair every time you wash it
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel when drying it
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair

By following these tips, you can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce the risk of hair fall.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause hair fall?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair fall. Stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and lead to hair fall.

Q: Can hair fall be reversed?

A: In some cases, hair fall can be reversed. This depends on the root cause of the hair fall. It is important to consult a healthcare professional to determine the best course of treatment for you.

Q: Is it normal to lose hair every day?

A: Yes, it is normal to lose hair every day. The average person loses between 50-100 hairs per day.

In conclusion, getting bald does not reduce hair fall. Hair fall can still occur on the remaining hair on the scalp. However, there are treatments for baldness that can help reduce hair fall. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can help reduce hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.
