What Dogs Don t Shed Much Hair A Guide To Low Shedding Breeds

Written by Petter Apr 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a dog that won't leave fur all over your house, you're not alone. Many people prefer low-shedding breeds because they're easier to maintain and can be a good choice for people with allergies. In this guide, we'll take a look at some of the best dogs that don't shed much hair.

What Does It Mean When a Dog Doesn't Shed Much Hair?

First, let's define what we mean by "low-shedding." All dogs shed to some extent, but some breeds shed much less than others. Typically, low-shedding dogs have hair rather than fur, which means they don't have an undercoat that sheds seasonally. Instead, their hair grows continuously, similar to human hair, and needs to be trimmed periodically.

Top Breeds That Don't Shed Much Hair

1. Poodle

Poodles are a classic choice for people who want a low-shedding dog. They come in three sizes (toy, miniature, and standard) and have curly or wavy hair that doesn't shed much. However, they do require regular grooming to prevent matting and keep their coats in good condition.

2. Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is another small breed that doesn't shed much hair. Their coats are curly and fluffy, which gives them a teddy bear-like appearance. They're also known for being friendly and good with children, making them a popular family pet.

3. Portuguese Water Dog

The Portuguese Water Dog has a thick, curly coat that doesn't shed much. They were originally bred to help fishermen in Portugal, so they're good swimmers and enjoy being in the water. They're also intelligent and trainable, which makes them a good choice for people who want an active dog.

4. Schnauzer

Schnauzers come in three sizes (miniature, standard, and giant) and have wiry hair that doesn't shed much. They're known for being loyal and protective of their families, and they're also good with children. However, they do require regular grooming to maintain their distinctive appearance.

5. Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or "Yorkie," is a small breed that doesn't shed much hair. They have long, silky hair that needs to be brushed regularly to prevent matting. They're also known for being affectionate and adaptable, making them a good choice for apartment living.

How to Care for a Low-Shedding Dog's Hair

Even though low-shedding dogs don't leave much hair around your house, they still require regular grooming to keep their coats healthy and free of tangles. Here are some tips for caring for a low-shedding dog's hair:

  • Brush their hair regularly to prevent matting and remove loose hair.
  • Trim their hair every few months to keep it at a manageable length.
  • Use a high-quality dog shampoo and conditioner to keep their coat clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid over-bathing your dog, as this can strip their coat of natural oils.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Low-Shedding Dogs Need Less Exercise?

No, low-shedding dogs still need regular exercise to stay healthy and happy. However, some breeds may be better suited to apartment living than others, so it's important to choose a dog that fits your lifestyle.

Are Low-Shedding Dogs Hypoallergenic?

No dog breed is completely hypoallergenic, but low-shedding dogs may be a good choice for people with allergies. However, if you have severe allergies, it's important to spend time with a dog before bringing them home to see if you have a reaction.

Can I Groom My Low-Shedding Dog at Home?

Yes, you can groom your low-shedding dog at home if you have the right tools and knowledge. However, if you're not comfortable trimming their hair yourself, it's best to take them to a professional groomer.

Conclusion

If you're looking for a dog that doesn't shed much hair, there are plenty of options to choose from. Whether you prefer a small, fluffy breed or a larger, athletic breed, there's a low-shedding dog that will fit your lifestyle. Just remember to take good care of their hair to keep them looking and feeling their best.
