How To Cut Shoulder Length Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Feb 24, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and looking for a change? If you have shoulder length layered hair, why not try giving yourself a haircut? With the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut your own hair at home. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut shoulder length layered hair in relaxed English language.

How to Cut Shoulder Length Layered Hair

To get started, you’ll need a few tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle of water

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It’s best to start with clean, dry hair. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would and make sure it’s completely dry before you begin cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use sectioning clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut the ends with your scissors. Work your way up, taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the desired length. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Cut the Front

Move on to the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut the ends with your scissors. Work your way up, taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the desired length. Make sure to cut each side evenly.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all of your hair to the desired length, it’s time to blend the layers. Take small sections of hair and hold them up at a 45-degree angle. Cut the ends of the hair at an angle, following the shape of the hair. This will create a layered effect.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After you’ve finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror to check for evenness. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them until your hair is even.

Tips for Cutting Shoulder Length Layered Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own shoulder length layered hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more if necessary.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired or if you need to step back and assess your progress.

FAQs about Cutting Shoulder Length Layered Hair

Can I cut my own shoulder length layered hair?

Yes, you can cut your own shoulder length layered hair with the right tools and techniques.

What kind of scissors should I use to cut my hair?

Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

How much hair should I cut at once?

Start with small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more if necessary.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Shoulder Length Layered Hair

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking fresh.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

Cutting your own shoulder length layered hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a fresh new look without leaving your home. Follow these steps and tips to cut your hair like a pro!
