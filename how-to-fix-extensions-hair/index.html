How To Fix Extensions Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Apr 30, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're someone who loves to experiment with their hair, chances are you've tried hair extensions at least once in your life. Hair extensions can give you the length and volume you've always wanted, but they can also be a bit tricky to maintain. In this article, we'll go over how to fix extensions hair in a relaxed English language, so you can achieve the perfect look every time.

Description

Fixing hair extensions can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure where to start. Whether you've got clip-ins, tape-ins, or sew-ins, there are a few things you need to keep in mind when fixing your extensions. In this guide, we'll cover everything from detangling and washing to styling and caring for your extensions.

How to Fix Extensions Hair

Step 1: Detangle Your Extensions

The first step in fixing your extensions is to detangle them. Use a wide-tooth comb or a special detangling brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Start at the ends of your extensions and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or tug too hard.

Step 2: Wash Your Extensions

Next, it's time to wash your extensions. Fill a sink or basin with lukewarm water and add a small amount of shampoo. Gently swish your extensions around in the water, being careful not to rub or scrub too hard. Rinse your extensions thoroughly and then apply conditioner, concentrating on the ends. Rinse again and let your extensions air dry.

Step 3: Style Your Extensions

Once your extensions are dry, it's time to style them. You can use a curling iron, flat iron, or other heat styling tools to create the look you want. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your extensions.

Step 4: Care for Your Extensions

Finally, it's important to care for your extensions properly to ensure they last as long as possible. Avoid sleeping in your extensions, as this can cause tangling and matting. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and keep your extensions looking their best. When you're not wearing your extensions, store them in a cool, dry place to prevent damage.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when fixing your extensions:

  • Always use a heat protectant spray when styling your extensions
  • Don't use too much product, as this can weigh down your extensions
  • Avoid brushing your extensions when they're wet, as this can cause damage
  • Be gentle when detangling your extensions, as pulling too hard can cause breakage

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about fixing extensions:

Q: How often should I wash my extensions?

A: It's best to wash your extensions every 6-8 wears to keep them looking their best.

Q: Can I dye my extensions?

A: It depends on the type of extensions you have. Clip-in extensions can usually be dyed, but tape-in and sew-in extensions should be dyed by a professional.

Q: How long do extensions last?

A: The lifespan of your extensions depends on how well you care for them. With proper care, extensions can last up to 6 months.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take care of your natural hair as well as your extensions. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do. With proper care, your hair and extensions will look beautiful and healthy for years to come.
