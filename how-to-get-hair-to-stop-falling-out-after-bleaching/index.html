How To Get Hair To Stop Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Robby May 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have bleached your hair and are now experiencing hair loss or thinning, you are not alone. Bleaching can be damaging to your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage and hair loss. In this article, we will discuss how to get hair to stop falling out after bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Hair to Stop Falling Out After Bleaching
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

How to Get Hair to Stop Falling Out After Bleaching

There are several things you can do to help stop hair loss after bleaching:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for products that contain keratin or hydrolyzed silk. These ingredients can help to repair damaged hair and prevent further damage.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Deep conditioning treatments can help to moisturize your hair and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle. Look for products that contain natural oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil. These oils can help to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to your hair, especially if it is already weak from bleaching. Try to avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as much as possible. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to help protect your hair.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, can put stress on your hair and cause breakage. Try to avoid wearing tight hairstyles, or at least give your hair a break from tight hairstyles every few days.

5. Be Gentle When Brushing or Combing Your Hair

When your hair is weak from bleaching, it is more prone to breakage. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically designed for detangling.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions to help you get hair to stop falling out after bleaching:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

2. Apply a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

3. Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as much as possible. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to help protect your hair.

5. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, as much as possible. If you must wear a tight hairstyle, loosen it every few hours to give your hair a break.

6. Be Gentle When Brushing or Combing Your Hair

Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically designed for detangling.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get hair to stop falling out after bleaching:

  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or perms.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins, such as vitamin A, B, C, and E.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

FAQ

Q: Can I bleach my hair again if it is falling out?

A: No, it is not recommended to bleach your hair again if it is falling out. This can make the problem worse and cause more damage to your hair.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to stop falling out after bleaching?

A: It can take several weeks or even months for your hair to stop falling out after bleaching. It is important to be patient and take good care of your hair during this time.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important to prevent hair loss and keep your hair healthy. Here are some additional tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can help to prevent hair loss and keep your hair healthy and strong.
