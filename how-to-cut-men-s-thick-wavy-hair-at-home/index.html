How To Cut Men s Thick Wavy Hair At Home

Written by Luffy Apr 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a man with thick, wavy hair, you know how difficult it can be to maintain your hairstyle. Going to a barber or hair stylist can be expensive, so many men choose to cut their own hair at home. But cutting thick, wavy hair can be challenging. In this article, we'll show you how to cut men's thick wavy hair at home in relaxed English language. Follow our step-by-step guide, and you'll be able to achieve a great haircut without leaving your home.

How to Cut Men's Thick Wavy Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of scissors, a comb, and a clipper with attachments. If you don't have a clipper, you can use scissors for the entire haircut, but it will take longer.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair thoroughly before cutting it. This will remove any product buildup and make it easier to work with.

Step 3: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want for your hair. Use the clipper attachments or scissors to achieve the desired length.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair using the comb. Start at the front and comb your hair straight back. Create a part down the middle of your head, and then comb each section forward. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 5: Cut the Sides and Back

Start cutting the sides and back of your hair. Use the clipper or scissors to trim the hair to the desired length. Start at the bottom and work your way up.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Unclip the top section of your hair and comb it forward. Cut the hair to the desired length using scissors or the clipper with attachments. Be careful not to cut too much off the top, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 7: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair using the clipper or scissors. Make sure there are no harsh lines between the different sections of your hair.

Step 8: Check for Any Uneven Areas

Check for any uneven areas and trim them as needed. Use the comb to comb your hair in different directions to ensure that it's even.

Step 9: Clean Up the Neckline and Ears

Use the clipper or scissors to clean up the neckline and ears. Use a smaller clipper attachment or scissors to trim any hair around the ears.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a hair product to add texture and hold.

Tips for Cutting Men's Thick Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting men's thick wavy hair:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more off if needed.
  • Use the comb to create a natural-looking blend between the different sections of your hair.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting hair takes practice, so don't worry if it doesn't turn out perfectly the first time.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as this can cause it to shrink up when it dries.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if it's very thick and wavy?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's thick and wavy. Just be patient and follow our step-by-step guide.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't worry. Hair grows back, and you can always go to a barber or hair stylist to fix it.

How often should I cut my hair?

You should cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your hairstyle.

Hair Care for Men's Thick Wavy Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your thick wavy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut your men's thick wavy hair at home with ease. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you'll achieve a great haircut every time.
