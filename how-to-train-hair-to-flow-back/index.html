How To Train Hair To Flow Back

Written by Frank Aug 31, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your hair falling in your face? Do you want a sleek, polished look that will turn heads? Training your hair to flow back is the answer! In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this look, and provide some helpful tips to keep your hair healthy and stylish.

Table of Contents

  • How to Train Hair to Flow Back
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Train Hair to Flow Back

Training your hair to flow back requires patience and dedication, but the results are worth it. The key is to use products that will help your hair stay in place, without damaging it. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner. This will remove any buildup and prepare your hair for styling.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product, such as a pomade or gel, to damp hair. Work the product through your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the tips.

Step 3: Brush Hair Back

Using a brush, comb your hair back away from your face. Make sure to brush your hair in the direction you want it to flow.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer, dry your hair on a low to medium heat setting. Use your brush to guide your hair back as you dry it.

Step 5: Use a Hairspray

Once your hair is dry, use a hairspray to set it in place. This will help your hair stay in place throughout the day.

Step by Step Guide

To make it easier, here's a step by step guide to help you train your hair to flow back:

  1. Wash your hair with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a styling product, such as a pomade or gel, to damp hair.
  3. Brush your hair back away from your face.
  4. Blow dry your hair on a low to medium heat setting, using a brush to guide your hair back.
  5. Use a hairspray to set your hair in place.

Tips for Success

Training your hair to flow back takes time and patience. Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Apply a styling product to damp hair, as this will help it stay in place.
  • Use a brush to guide your hair back in the direction you want it to flow.
  • Blow dry your hair on a low to medium heat setting to avoid heat damage.
  • Use a hairspray to set your hair in place, and reapply as needed throughout the day.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about training hair to flow back:

How long does it take to train hair to flow back?

It can take several weeks or even months to train your hair to flow back, depending on your hair type and how often you style it.

Can I train my hair to flow back if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can train your hair to flow back if you have curly hair. However, it may take longer to achieve the desired look, and you may need to use different styling products.

What if my hair won't stay in place?

If your hair won't stay in place, try using a stronger styling product, such as a wax or clay. You can also try blow drying your hair on a high heat setting to set it in place.

Hair Care Tips

Training your hair to flow back requires some extra care to keep it healthy. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often, as this can cause heat damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as bleach and hair dye, as these can damage your hair.

By following these tips and steps, you'll be able to train your hair to flow back in no time. Remember to be patient, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you. With a little practice, you'll have a sleek, polished look that will turn heads wherever you go!
