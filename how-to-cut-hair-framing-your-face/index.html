How To Cut Hair Framing Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Mar 10, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle and frame your face with a fresh new cut? Cutting your hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to cut hair framing your face and provide tips for maintaining your new style.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Cut Hair Framing Your Face
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Maintaining Your New Style
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care

How to Cut Hair Framing Your Face

Cutting hair to frame your face is all about creating layers that flatter your features. Whether you have long hair, short hair, or something in between, this technique can work for you. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your hair and frame your face:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Divide your hair into three sections: one at the top of your head, one in the middle, and one at the nape of your neck.
  2. Clip the top section out of the way with a hair clip or elastic.
  3. Take the middle section and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers and cut them straight across. This will create a guide for the rest of your layers.
  4. Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers and cut them at an angle, starting from the guide you created in step 3. Make sure to cut the hair on both sides of your face evenly.
  5. Release the bottom section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers and cut them at an angle, starting from the guide you created in step 3. Make sure to cut the hair on both sides of your face evenly.
  6. Blend the layers by combing your hair and cutting any stray pieces that stick out.
  7. Use a mirror to check the back of your hair for any uneven pieces. Trim as needed.

Congratulations! You’ve successfully cut your hair and framed your face. Now it’s time to style your new look.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Style

Here are some tips for maintaining your new haircut:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my hair framing my face if I have curly hair?

A: Yes! This technique can work for all hair types, including curly hair. Just make sure to cut your hair when it’s dry so you can see the shape of your curls.

Q: Should I cut my hair when it’s wet or dry?

A: It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in a shorter cut than intended.

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your haircut.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is important to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Now that you know how to cut hair framing your face, you can experiment with different styles and create a look that flatters your features. Happy cutting!
