What To Do When Your Baby Hair Is Falling Out

Written by Christine Sep 04, 2023 · 5 min read

If you are a new parent, you may be wondering why your baby's hair is falling out. In most cases, this is a completely normal and temporary occurrence. However, it can be unsettling to see your baby's hair falling out. In this article, we will discuss what to do when your baby hair is falling out.

Table of Content

  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first thing to do when your baby's hair is falling out is to remain calm. As we mentioned earlier, this is a normal occurrence in most cases. However, there are a few things that you can do to help the process along and ensure that your baby's scalp stays healthy.

Step by step

Step 1: Gently brush your baby's hair with a soft-bristled brush. This will help to remove any loose hairs and stimulate the scalp.

Step 2: Avoid using tight hair accessories or pulling your baby's hair too tightly. This can cause additional hair loss and damage to the scalp.

Step 3: Keep your baby's scalp clean and moisturized. Use a gentle baby shampoo and avoid using any harsh chemicals or products on your baby's head.

Step 4: Be patient. In most cases, your baby's hair will grow back on its own within a few months.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when your baby's hair is falling out:

  • Don't panic. This is a normal occurrence and your baby's hair will grow back.
  • Avoid using adult shampoos or hair products on your baby's head.
  • Try a gentle scalp massage to stimulate hair growth.
  • Make sure your baby is getting enough nutrients, including vitamins and minerals.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about baby hair loss:

Why is my baby's hair falling out?

In most cases, baby hair loss is completely normal and temporary. It is usually caused by hormonal changes or rubbing against bedding or clothing.

When will my baby's hair grow back?

Your baby's hair will likely start to grow back within a few months. However, it may take up to a year for the hair to fully grow back.

Should I be concerned if my baby's hair is falling out?

If your baby is otherwise healthy and happy, there is usually no cause for concern. However, if you are worried, it is always a good idea to speak with your pediatrician.

Hair Care

When your baby's hair is falling out, it is important to take good care of their scalp. This means using gentle baby shampoo and avoiding any harsh chemicals or products. You should also avoid using tight hair accessories or pulling your baby's hair too tightly.

In conclusion, baby hair loss is a normal and temporary occurrence. By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can help to ensure that your baby's scalp stays healthy and that their hair grows back in a timely manner.
