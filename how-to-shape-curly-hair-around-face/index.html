How to Shape Curly Hair Around Face

Shaping your curly hair around your face can help to create a more defined and polished look. Hereâs how to do it:

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair

Before you begin shaping your hair, itâs important to start with a clean slate. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for curly hair to help keep your curls hydrated and defined.

2. Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product to your damp hair to help define your curls and keep them in place. A curl cream or gel can work well for this.

3. Section your hair

Section your hair into manageable pieces so that you can work on one section at a time. Use clips or hair ties to keep the other sections out of the way.

4. Use a diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your curls without causing frizz. Start at the roots and work your way down, scrunching your hair as you go.

5. Shape your curls

Once your curls are dry, use your fingers to shape them around your face. You can also use a wide-tooth comb or pick to help define your curls.

6. Finish with a hairspray

Finish off your look with a light hairspray to help hold your curls in place. Be sure to use a hairspray that wonât weigh down your curls or make them look greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Hereâs a more detailed step-by-step guide to shaping your curly hair around your face:

Step 1: Start with clean, conditioned hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for curly hair. Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to avoid any product buildup.

Step 2: Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product to your damp hair to help define your curls and keep them in place. A curl cream or gel can work well for this. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into manageable pieces so that you can work on one section at a time. Use clips or hair ties to keep the other sections out of the way.

Step 4: Use a diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your curls without causing frizz. Start at the roots and work your way down, scrunching your hair as you go. Be sure to dry your hair completely to avoid any frizz or flyaways.

Step 5: Shape your curls

Once your curls are dry, use your fingers to shape them around your face. You can also use a wide-tooth comb or pick to help define your curls. Be gentle when shaping your curls to avoid causing any frizz or breakage.

Step 6: Finish with a hairspray

Finish off your look with a light hairspray to help hold your curls in place. Be sure to use a hairspray that wonât weigh down your curls or make them look greasy.

Tips for Shaping Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you shape your curly hair around your face:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or pick to help define your curls.
  • Be gentle when shaping your curls to avoid causing any frizz or breakage.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your curls without causing frizz.
  • Try using a curl cream or gel to help define your curls and keep them in place.
  • Finish off your look with a light hairspray to help hold your curls in place.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: Itâs best to wash your curly hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying it out. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for curly hair.

Q: How do I avoid frizz when shaping my curly hair around my face?

A: Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help dry your curls without causing frizz. Be gentle when shaping your curls and avoid using too much product.

Q: What should I do if my curls lose their shape throughout the day?

A: Try using a curl refreshing spray to help revive your curls throughout the day. You can also use a small amount of styling product to help reshape your curls.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help care for your curly hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or pick to help detangle your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to help prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can create a beautiful, defined look that highlights your natural curls. Remember to be gentle when shaping your curls and use the right products to keep them looking their best.
