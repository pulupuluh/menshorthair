How To Make A Dress That Is Too Short Longer

Written by Eveline Apr 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having to wear leggings or tights under your favorite dress because it's too short? Don't worry, you don't have to give up on that dress just yet. In this article, we'll show you how to make a dress that is too short longer. Follow these easy steps and you'll be able to wear that dress with confidence again!

Description

Making a dress longer can seem like a daunting task, but it's actually quite simple. You'll just need some basic sewing skills and a few supplies. In this article, we'll guide you through the process step-by-step so you can make your dress longer in no time.

How to

The first step in making a dress longer is to determine how much length you need to add. Try the dress on and see where it falls on your body. If it's just a few inches too short, you may only need to add a little bit of length. If it's significantly shorter, you may need to add a lot more length.

Next, you'll need to choose a fabric that matches your dress. This could be the same fabric as your dress or a similar fabric that matches the color and texture. You'll also need a sewing machine, thread, and scissors.

Once you have your fabric and supplies ready, it's time to start sewing. Here are the steps to follow:

Step-by-Step

  1. Measure the length that you need to add to your dress.
  2. Cut a strip of fabric that is the same length as the measurement you took in step 1, and about 2 inches wide.
  3. Pin the strip of fabric to the bottom of your dress so that the right sides are facing each other.
  4. Sew the fabric strip to the dress using a straight stitch. Make sure to backstitch at the beginning and end of the seam.
  5. Trim any excess fabric from the seam allowance.
  6. Press the seam allowance towards the fabric strip.
  7. Flip the fabric strip down so that it covers the seam allowance.
  8. Pin the fabric strip in place.
  9. Sew the fabric strip in place using a straight stitch. Make sure to backstitch at the beginning and end of the seam.
  10. Trim any excess fabric from the fabric strip.
  11. Press the fabric strip down so that it lays flat against the dress.

And that's it! Your dress is now longer and ready to wear.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when making a dress longer:

  • Make sure to choose a fabric that matches your dress as closely as possible.
  • Take your time when sewing to make sure the seam is straight and neat.
  • If you're not confident in your sewing skills, consider taking your dress to a tailor or seamstress.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making a dress longer:

How much fabric do I need?

You'll need enough fabric to add the length that you need. Measure the length of fabric that you need and add a few inches to account for seam allowances.

Can I use a different color fabric?

You can, but it's best to choose a fabric that matches your dress as closely as possible. Using a different color fabric may make the seam more noticeable.

Do I need a sewing machine?

You can hand-sew the fabric strip to your dress, but it will take longer and may not be as neat as using a sewing machine.

Hair Care

Don't forget to style your hair to match your new longer dress! Consider trying a new hairstyle or using different hair accessories to complete your look.

Now that you know how to make a dress that is too short longer, you can wear all of your favorite dresses with confidence. Follow these steps and you'll be able to add length to any dress in no time.
