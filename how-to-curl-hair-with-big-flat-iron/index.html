How To Curl Hair With Big Flat Iron A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a way to create beautiful, bouncy curls without using a curling iron, then using a big flat iron might just be your solution. However, it can be a bit tricky to get the hang of at first. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide on how to curl hair with big flat iron.

How to Curl Hair with Big Flat Iron

Curling hair with a big flat iron is similar to straightening it, but with a few tweaks and techniques. Here are the basic steps you need to follow:

1. Prep Your Hair

Before you start curling, make sure your hair is clean, dry, and free from tangles. Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the heat of the flat iron.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use clips to hold each section in place.

3. Heat Up Your Flat Iron

Make sure your flat iron is heated to the appropriate temperature for your hair type. For fine hair, use a lower heat setting, while thicker hair can handle a higher temperature.

4. Start Curling

Take a small section of hair, about 1-2 inches wide, and clamp the flat iron around it near the roots. Twist the flat iron away from your face, keeping the hair taut, and glide it down the length of the section of hair. Release the hair and let it cool before moving onto the next section.

5. Repeat

Continue curling each section of hair, working your way up towards the top of your head. For a more natural look, alternate the direction of the curls, twisting some sections towards your face and others away from it.

6. Finish with Hairspray

Once you've curled all of your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to help hold the curls in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to curl hair with big flat iron:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and allowing it to air dry or blow drying it. Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from the heat of the flat iron.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, working from the bottom up. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Heat Up Your Flat Iron

Turn on your flat iron and set it to the appropriate temperature for your hair type.

Step 4: Start Curling

Take a small section of hair, about 1-2 inches wide, and clamp the flat iron around it near the roots. Twist the flat iron away from your face, keeping the hair taut, and glide it down the length of the section of hair. Release the hair and let it cool before moving onto the next section.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue curling each section of hair, working your way up towards the top of your head. For a more natural look, alternate the direction of the curls, twisting some sections towards your face and others away from it.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've curled all of your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to help hold the curls in place.

Tips for Curling Hair with a Flat Iron

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your big flat iron:

1. Use the Right Temperature

Make sure you're using the appropriate temperature for your hair type. Fine hair can be damaged by high temperatures, while thicker hair needs more heat to curl properly.

2. Use Small Sections

When curling with a flat iron, it's best to use small sections of hair. This will help ensure that each section is heated evenly and that the curls are consistent throughout your hair.

3. Twist the Flat Iron

Twisting the flat iron as you curl will help create more natural-looking curls.

4. Cool the Curls

Allow each curl to cool before moving onto the next section. This will help the curls set and last longer.

5. Finish with Hairspray

Use a light mist of hairspray to help hold the curls in place without weighing them down.

FAQs

Q: How long will the curls last?

A: The length of time your curls will last depends on your hair type and the products you use. On average, curls created with a flat iron can last anywhere from 4-8 hours.

Q: Can I curl my hair with a straightener?

A: Yes, you can curl your hair with a straightener. In fact, a flat iron can be a great tool for creating loose waves or bouncy curls.

Q: What's the best way to prevent heat damage when curling with a flat iron?

A: Use a heat protectant spray before curling and make sure your flat iron isn't too hot. It's also important to allow each curl to cool before moving onto the next section.

Hair Care and Maintenance

Curling your hair with a flat iron can be a great way to switch up your look, but it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Don't Overdo It

Try not to use heat styling tools every day, and give your hair a break from heat styling every once in a while.

3. Condition Regularly

Make sure to condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and hydrated.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to create beautiful, bouncy curls with a big flat iron. Happy styling!
