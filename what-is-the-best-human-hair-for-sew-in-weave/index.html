What Is The Best Human Hair For Sew In Weave

Are you considering getting a sew-in weave but not sure what type of human hair to use? In this article, we will discuss the best human hair for sew in weave, as well as some tips and hair care advice to help you maintain your new hairstyle.

Description

A sew-in weave is a popular hairstyle that involves braiding your natural hair and sewing in extension hair onto the braids. This hairstyle can last for several weeks and allows you to try different hair textures and lengths without damaging your natural hair.

When it comes to choosing the best human hair for sew in weave, there are several factors to consider. The quality of the hair, the texture, and the length are all important factors that can affect the overall look and feel of your sew-in weave.

How to Choose the Best Human Hair for Sew In Weave

When choosing the best human hair for sew in weave, there are a few things to consider:

  • Quality: Look for high-quality human hair that is unprocessed and has not been chemically treated. This will ensure that the hair is strong, healthy, and will last longer.
  • Texture: Consider the texture of your natural hair and choose a hair texture that will blend well with your natural hair. If you have straight hair, consider using straight hair extensions. If you have curly hair, consider using curly hair extensions.
  • Length: Choose a hair length that you are comfortable with and that will suit your face shape.

Step by Step Guide to Installing a Sew In Weave

If you are new to sew-in weaves, it is recommended that you get it installed by a professional hairstylist. However, if you are confident in your skills, you can follow these steps to install a sew-in weave:

  1. Wash and condition your natural hair.
  2. Braid your natural hair into cornrows. The number of braids will depend on the density of your hair.
  3. Sew in the hair extensions onto the cornrows using a needle and thread.
  4. Once all the extensions are sewn in, cut and style the hair to your desired length and shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Sew In Weave

Here are some tips to help you maintain your sew-in weave:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your hair as it can weigh down the extensions and cause tangling.
  • Wrap your hair at night with a silk or satin scarf to prevent tangling and breakage.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling as it can damage the hair extensions.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about sew-in weaves:

  • How long does a sew-in weave last? A sew-in weave can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks depending on how well you take care of it.
  • Can I wash my sew-in weave? Yes, you can wash your sew-in weave, but you should avoid washing it too often as it can cause tangling and damage.
  • Can I swim with a sew-in weave? Yes, you can swim with a sew-in weave, but you should wear a swim cap to protect the hair from chlorine and saltwater.

Hair Care Advice for Sew In Weave

Here are some additional hair care tips for sew-in weaves:

  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized to prevent itching and dryness.
  • Avoid scratching your scalp as it can cause irritation and damage to the hair extensions.
  • Trim the ends of the hair extensions regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Consider getting a touch-up every 2-3 weeks to maintain the appearance of the sew-in weave.

Overall, the best human hair for sew in weave is high-quality, unprocessed, and matches the texture and length of your natural hair. With proper care and maintenance, your sew-in weave can last for several weeks and give you a beautiful, natural-looking hairstyle.
