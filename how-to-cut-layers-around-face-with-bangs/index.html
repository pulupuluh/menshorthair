How To Cut Layers Around Face With Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 25, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting layers around your face with bangs can add dimension and frame your features. However, it can be daunting to attempt a DIY haircut, especially if you're not sure where to start. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut layers around your face with bangs, along with some tips and hair care advice to keep your new style looking fresh.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Layers Around Face with Bangs

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over the basics of cutting layers around your face with bangs:

  • Tools: You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.
  • Technique: The key to cutting layers is to start with longer pieces and gradually work your way up. For bangs, you'll want to start with a longer length and trim small sections at a time.
  • Face shape: Consider your face shape when deciding on the length and angle of your layers and bangs. For example, if you have a round face, longer layers can help elongate your features.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's get into the nitty-gritty of how to cut layers around your face with bangs:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair thoroughly and section off the front portion that you want to cut into bangs.
  2. Using your comb, separate a small section of hair at the top of the front portion. This will be your guide for the length of your bangs.
  3. Hold the hair between your fingers and trim off a small amount at a time. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.
  4. Continue trimming small sections until you reach your desired length.
  5. For the layers around your face, start with the longest layer and work your way up. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up from your head.
  6. Trim the ends of the hair at a slight angle to create a layered effect. Repeat this process with small sections of hair around your face until you've achieved your desired look.
  7. Blend the layers together by combing through your hair and checking for any uneven pieces. Make small adjustments as needed.
  8. Finish with a spritz of hairspray or other styling product to hold your new style in place.

Tips for Cutting Layers and Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers and bangs:

  • Start slow: It's always better to start with longer pieces and gradually work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.
  • Consider your face shape: The length and angle of your layers and bangs can help flatter your features.
  • Don't cut too much: It's easy to get carried away with cutting, but remember that your hair will bounce up after it's cut. If in doubt, cut less rather than more.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers and bangs:

  • Can I cut my own layers and bangs? Yes, you can cut your own layers and bangs, but it's important to go slow and be patient. If you're not confident in your skills, it's always better to go to a professional stylist.
  • What kind of bangs should I get? The type of bangs you should get depends on your face shape and personal style. Some popular types of bangs include blunt, side-swept, and wispy.
  • How often should I trim my layers and bangs? It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Layered Hair with Bangs

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a gentle shampoo: Look for a shampoo that's free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition regularly: Conditioner can help hydrate your hair and prevent breakage. Use a conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair over time. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: UV rays can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF when you're out in the sun.
  • Get regular trims: As mentioned earlier, it's important to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you're ready to cut layers around your face with bangs and rock a new hairstyle. Remember to take it slow, be patient, and take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
