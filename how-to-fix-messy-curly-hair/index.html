How To Fix Messy Curly Hair A Guide For 2023

Written by Robby Sep 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. On the one hand, you have defined, voluminous curls that many people envy. On the other hand, you also have to deal with frizz, tangles, and a general lack of control. If you're tired of dealing with messy curly hair, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix your messy curly hair for a more polished, put-together look.

How to Fix Messy Curly Hair

Fixing messy curly hair requires a multi-step approach that involves both styling and hair care. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to fixing your messy curly hair is to start with clean hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse your hair and prepare it for styling. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your curls and keep them hydrated. This will also help to prevent frizz and keep your curls defined. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Use a Curl-Defining Product

To help define your curls and keep them in place, use a curl-defining product such as a curl cream or gel. Apply the product to your hair in sections, starting at the back and working your way forward. Use your fingers to scrunch your curls as you apply the product, to help create more definition.

Step 4: Diffuse Your Hair

Once you've applied your styling products, it's time to dry your hair. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help dry your curls without causing frizz. Start by diffusing the roots of your hair, then work your way down to the ends. Continue scrunching your curls as you dry them to help create more definition.

Step 5: Finish with a Hair Oil

To add shine and moisture to your curls, finish off your hairstyle with a hair oil. Apply a small amount of oil to your palms, then scrunch it into your hair. This will help to tame any remaining frizz and give your curls a more polished look.

Tips for Fixing Messy Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you fix your messy curly hair:

  • Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair, as this will help to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Try sleeping on a satin or silk pillowcase, as this will help to prevent tangles and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

FAQ: Fixing Messy Curly Hair

Q: Can I fix my messy curly hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can fix your messy curly hair without using heat. Try air-drying your hair, or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without causing frizz.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash their hair every day, while others only wash it once or twice a week. Experiment to find a washing schedule that works best for your hair.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to fix my messy curly hair?

A: While you can use a hair straightener to temporarily straighten your curls, this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz in the long run. Instead, try to embrace your natural curls and use products and styling techniques that will enhance their definition and reduce frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Keeping your curly hair healthy and hydrated is key to preventing frizz and achieving a polished, put-together look. Here are some hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls, and avoid brushing your hair when it's dry.
  • Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and techniques, you can fix your messy curly hair and achieve a more polished, put-together look. Whether you're heading to work or out for a night on the town, your curls will be looking their best.
