Does Blow Drying Your Hair Make It Less Frizzy

Written by William May 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Many people struggle with frizzy hair, and it can be frustrating trying to find a solution that works. One common question is whether blow drying your hair can make it less frizzy. In this article, we'll explore this question and provide tips on how to blow dry your hair to reduce frizz.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Blow Dry Your Hair to Reduce Frizz
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Reducing Frizz While Blow Drying
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care Tips for Managing Frizzy Hair

Description

Frizz occurs when the hair cuticle is lifted, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. This can happen due to a variety of factors, including humidity, heat styling, and chemical treatments. Blow drying can help reduce frizz by sealing the hair cuticle and smoothing the hair shaft. However, if not done properly, blow drying can actually make frizz worse.

How to Blow Dry Your Hair to Reduce Frizz

The key to reducing frizz while blow drying is to use the right technique and tools. Here are some tips:

Use a Heat Protectant

Before blow drying, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair healthy.

Choose the Right Blow Dryer

Invest in a high-quality blow dryer with multiple heat and speed settings. Look for a dryer with a diffuser attachment, which can help distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.

Use the Right Brush

Choose a brush with natural bristles, which are gentler on the hair and help distribute natural oils. Avoid using a metal or plastic brush, which can cause static and increase frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to blow dry your hair to reduce frizz:

  1. Start by towel drying your hair to remove excess moisture.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips.
  4. Starting at the roots, use a round brush to lift and direct the hair while blowing it dry.
  5. Work in small sections, using the brush to smooth the hair and direct the heat downward.
  6. Use the cool shot button on your blow dryer to lock in the style and reduce frizz.
  7. Finish with a light mist of hairspray or anti-frizz serum.

Tips for Reducing Frizz While Blow Drying

Here are some additional tips for reducing frizz while blow drying:

  • Avoid using high heat settings, as this can cause damage and increase frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.
  • Blow dry your hair in a downward direction to smooth the hair cuticle.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much while blow drying, as this can cause static and increase frizz.
  • Finish with a cool shot of air to seal the hair cuticle and reduce frizz.

FAQ

Can blow drying cause frizz?

Blow drying can cause frizz if not done properly. Using high heat settings, using the wrong brush, and touching your hair too much can all contribute to frizz.

What is the best brush to use for blow drying?

A round brush with natural bristles is best for blow drying. This type of brush is gentle on the hair and helps distribute natural oils, reducing frizz.

What is a diffuser attachment?

A diffuser attachment is a tool that attaches to the end of your blow dryer. It helps distribute heat evenly and reduces frizz by diffusing the airflow.

Hair Care Tips for Managing Frizzy Hair

In addition to blow drying, there are other steps you can take to manage frizzy hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the hair of natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis, as this can cause damage and increase frizz.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

In conclusion, blow drying can help reduce frizz if done properly. Use the right technique and tools, and follow these tips to achieve smooth, frizz-free hair.
