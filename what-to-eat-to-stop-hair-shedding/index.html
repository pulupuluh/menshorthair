What To Eat To Stop Hair Shedding A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Sep 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of seeing clumps of hair in the shower drain or on your brush? Hair shedding can be a frustrating and confidence-draining experience. While there are many factors that can contribute to hair loss, diet is one area that you can control. In this article, we'll explore what to eat to stop hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Shedding with Diet
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Eating for Healthy Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining a Hair-Healthy Diet
  • FAQs About Eating for Healthy Hair
  • Other Hair Care Tips to Reduce Hair Shedding

How to Stop Hair Shedding with Diet

When it comes to hair health, it's not just what you put on your hair that matters, it's also what you put in your body. Your hair is made up of protein, so it's important to get enough of this nutrient in your diet. Other nutrients that are important for hair health include:

  • Iron
  • Zinc
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin A
  • Biotin
  • Niacin

Deficiencies in these nutrients can lead to hair shedding and other hair health issues. By including foods that are rich in these nutrients in your diet, you can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce hair shedding.

Protein

As we mentioned earlier, protein is a key nutrient for healthy hair growth. You can get protein from a variety of sources, including:

  • Meat, poultry, and fish
  • Eggs
  • Beans and legumes
  • Nuts and seeds

Try to include a source of protein in every meal to help promote healthy hair growth.

Iron and Zinc

Iron and zinc are two minerals that are important for hair health. Iron helps to carry oxygen to the hair follicles, while zinc helps to keep the oil glands around the hair follicles healthy. You can get iron from:

  • Red meat
  • Poultry
  • Seafood
  • Beans and legumes
  • Spinach and other leafy greens

Zinc can be found in:

  • Shellfish
  • Meat and poultry
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Beans and legumes

Try to include a variety of these foods in your diet to make sure you're getting enough iron and zinc.

Vitamins A, D, and Biotin

Vitamin A helps to produce sebum, which is the oil that helps to moisturize your scalp and keep your hair healthy. You can get vitamin A from:

  • Sweet potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Pumpkin
  • Spinach and other leafy greens

Vitamin D is important for hair growth, but it can be difficult to get enough of this nutrient from food alone. Your body produces vitamin D when your skin is exposed to sunlight, so try to spend some time outside each day to help boost your vitamin D levels.

Biotin is a B vitamin that is important for healthy hair growth. You can get biotin from:

  • Eggs
  • Almonds
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Spinach and other leafy greens

Step-by-Step Guide to Eating for Healthy Hair

Now that you know what nutrients are important for hair health, here's a step-by-step guide to eating for healthy hair:

  1. Include a source of protein in every meal.
  2. Eat a variety of fruits and vegetables to get a range of vitamins and minerals.
  3. Choose whole grains over refined grains.
  4. Include healthy fats in your diet, such as nuts, seeds, and avocado.
  5. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

By following these steps, you can help ensure that you're getting the nutrients your body needs to promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Maintaining a Hair-Healthy Diet

Here are some additional tips for maintaining a hair-healthy diet:

  • Try to eat a balanced diet that includes a variety of foods.
  • Avoid crash diets or extreme calorie restriction, as these can lead to hair shedding.
  • If you're concerned that you're not getting enough of a certain nutrient, talk to your doctor about taking a supplement.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair or using harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

FAQs About Eating for Healthy Hair

Q: Can eating a healthy diet really stop hair shedding?

A: While there are many factors that can contribute to hair shedding, including genetics and hormones, eating a healthy diet can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce shedding.

Q: Can certain foods cause hair shedding?

A: Some research suggests that a diet that is high in sugar and refined carbohydrates may contribute to hair loss. However, more research is needed in this area.

Q: How long does it take to see the effects of a hair-healthy diet?

A: It can take several weeks or even months to see the effects of a healthy diet on hair growth and shedding.

Other Hair Care Tips to Reduce Hair Shedding

In addition to eating a healthy diet, there are other steps you can take to reduce hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your scalp of its natural oils.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons and straighteners too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider taking a break from chemical treatments such as relaxers or perms that can weaken your hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb to detangle wet hair.

By following these tips and eating a nutrient-rich diet, you can help reduce hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth.
