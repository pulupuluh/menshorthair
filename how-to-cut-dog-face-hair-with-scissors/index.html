How To Cut Dog Face Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a dog owner, you know how important it is to keep your furry friend looking their best. One of the most challenging areas to groom is your dog's face. But don't worry; we're here to help! In this article, we'll show you how to cut your dog's face hair with scissors.

How to Cut Dog Face Hair with Scissors

Cutting your dog's face hair with scissors may seem daunting, but with a little patience and practice, it's easy to do. Here's how:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Treats (to keep your dog calm and happy)

Step 2: Brush Your Dog's Hair

Before you start cutting, brush your dog's hair to remove any tangles or mats. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a smoother finish.

Step 3: Start Cutting

With your scissors, carefully trim around your dog's eyes, ears, and mouth. Start by trimming a little at a time and using the comb to guide the hair into the scissors. Make sure you're cutting with the scissors' tips and not the full blade to avoid accidental cuts.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After a few cuts, step back and check your work. Make sure you're happy with the length and shape of your dog's face hair. If not, keep trimming a little at a time until you achieve the desired look.

Step 5: Finish with Clippers (Optional)

If your dog has long or thick hair, you may want to use clippers to finish the job. Use a comb attachment to achieve an even cut and go over any areas you missed with the scissors.

Tips for Cutting Your Dog's Face Hair with Scissors

Cutting your dog's face hair with scissors can be challenging, but these tips will make the process easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or tearing your dog's hair.
  • Keep a firm grip on your dog's head to avoid accidental cuts.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Use treats to keep your dog calm and happy during the grooming process.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my dog's face hair?

A: It depends on your dog's breed and hair type. Dogs with long hair may need more frequent grooming, while dogs with short hair can go longer between cuts. Generally, it's a good idea to trim your dog's face hair every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I use human scissors to cut my dog's hair?

A: No, you should always use scissors specifically designed for pet grooming. Human scissors are not sharp enough and can damage your dog's hair.

Hair Care for Dogs

Cutting your dog's hair is just one part of their grooming routine. Here are some other hair care tips:

  • Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Use a dog-specific shampoo when bathing your pet.
  • Trim your dog's nails regularly to avoid overgrowth and discomfort.
  • Take your dog to a professional groomer for a full grooming session every few months.

Conclusion

Cutting your dog's face hair with scissors can seem intimidating, but with a little practice and patience, it's easy to do. Just remember to use the right tools, take your time, and be gentle with your furry friend. Happy grooming!
