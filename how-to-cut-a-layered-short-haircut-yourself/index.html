How To Cut A Layered Short Haircut Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending hundreds of dollars at the salon for a simple haircut? Or maybe you just don't have the time to make an appointment and wait for your turn. Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and time. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a layered short haircut yourself in a few easy steps.

Description

The layered short haircut is a popular choice for many women, as it adds volume and texture to the hair. This style is suitable for all face shapes, and it can be customized to suit your individual preferences. The layers can be cut at different lengths to create a variety of looks, from a choppy bob to a sleek pixie cut.

How to Cut a Layered Short Haircut Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather some essential tools. These include:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Wash your hair thoroughly and towel dry it. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Then, divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair and securing it with a clip or hair tie.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle. Repeat this process for each section, making sure to cut at a slight angle to create layers.

Once you've finished the bottom layer, release the top layer and repeat the process. Make sure to cut the top layer slightly shorter than the bottom layer to create more volume and texture.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After you've finished cutting your hair, check your progress in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments or touch-ups. Remember, it's always better to cut less than to cut too much.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your haircut, style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry your hair or let it air dry. Use a styling product to add volume or texture if desired.

Tips

  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair and gradually work your way up.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can be more difficult to gauge the length.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut and style. It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink and appear shorter once it dries.

Can I cut my own hair if I have long hair?

Yes, you can. However, cutting long hair can be more challenging and time-consuming. It's best to start with small sections and work your way up.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain a healthy appearance and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain a healthy appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. Use a hair mask or treatment once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.

Cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and time. With these easy steps, you can achieve a layered short haircut at home. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and check your progress in the mirror. Happy cutting!
