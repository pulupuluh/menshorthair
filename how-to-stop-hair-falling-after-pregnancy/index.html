How To Stop Hair Falling After Pregnancy Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver May 23, 2023 · 8 min read

Experiencing hair fall after pregnancy is a common occurrence, and it can be distressing for new mothers. The hormonal changes that occur during pregnancy can cause hair to appear thicker and fuller, but once the baby is born, the hair can start to shed. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to stop hair falling after pregnancy. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know, from the causes of postpartum hair loss to the best hair care tips to prevent it.

Causes of Hair Fall After Pregnancy

Postpartum hair loss is caused by hormonal changes in the body. During pregnancy, estrogen levels rise, which can cause hair to thicken and grow faster. After childbirth, estrogen levels drop, and hair can enter a resting phase, which leads to hair loss. The hair loss typically starts around three months after giving birth and can last up to a year.

Other causes of hair fall after pregnancy include stress, nutrient deficiencies, and genetics. If you have a family history of hair loss, you may be more prone to experiencing postpartum hair loss.

How to Stop Hair Falling After Pregnancy

While you can’t entirely prevent postpartum hair loss, there are steps you can take to minimize it. Here are some ways to stop hair falling after pregnancy:

1. Improve Your Diet

Eating a healthy, balanced diet can help prevent hair loss. Make sure you’re consuming enough protein, iron, and vitamins, particularly vitamin D and B12. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth. Foods like eggs, nuts, and leafy greens are excellent sources of these nutrients.

2. Consider Hair Loss Treatments

If you’re experiencing significant hair loss, you may want to consider hair loss treatments. Minoxidil is a topical solution that can help promote hair growth. It’s safe to use while breastfeeding, but you should talk to your doctor before using it.

3. Take Care of Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh treatments like coloring or perming. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails that can stress the hair. Make sure you’re using a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s free of sulfates and parabens.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Fall After Pregnancy

Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you prevent hair fall after pregnancy:

Step 1: Consult with Your Doctor

Talk to your doctor if you’re experiencing significant hair loss. They can help rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be causing it.

Step 2: Improve Your Diet

Eat a healthy, balanced diet that’s rich in protein, iron, and vitamins. Consider taking a multivitamin if you’re not getting enough nutrients from your diet.

Step 3: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid harsh treatments like coloring or perming, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Avoid tight hairstyles that can stress the hair, and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s free of sulfates and parabens.

Step 4: Consider Hair Loss Treatments

If you’re experiencing significant hair loss, talk to your doctor about hair loss treatments like minoxidil.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall After Pregnancy

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair fall after pregnancy:

1. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss is temporary and will eventually resolve on its own. Be patient and don’t stress too much about it.

2. Avoid Stress

Stress can exacerbate hair loss, so try to avoid stressful situations as much as possible. Practice relaxation techniques like meditation or yoga to help manage stress.

3. Get Enough Sleep

Sleep is essential for overall health, including hair health. Aim to get at least seven to eight hours of sleep per night.

FAQs

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss typically starts around three months after giving birth and can last up to a year.

Can Postpartum Hair Loss be Prevented?

You can’t entirely prevent postpartum hair loss, but you can take steps to minimize it.

Is Postpartum Hair Loss Normal?

Yes, postpartum hair loss is entirely normal and affects many new mothers.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Hair Fall After Pregnancy

Here are some additional hair care tips that can help prevent hair fall after pregnancy:

1. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to the hair follicles, promoting hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp gently.

2. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help nourish and strengthen your hair. You can make a DIY hair mask using ingredients like avocado, honey, and coconut oil.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends, which can lead to hair breakage and loss.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can minimize postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
