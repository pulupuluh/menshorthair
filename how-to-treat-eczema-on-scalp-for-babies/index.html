How To Treat Eczema On Scalp For Babies

Written by Daniel Jun 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As a parent, it can be tough to see your baby suffer from eczema on their scalp. Eczema is a common skin condition that affects people of all ages, including babies. It can cause redness, itching, and flaking of the skin, which can be uncomfortable and painful for your little one. In this article, we will discuss how to treat eczema on scalp for babies.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Treat Eczema on Scalp for Babies
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Eczema is a skin condition that causes inflammation and irritation. It is often characterized by red, itchy, and flaky patches on the skin. Eczema on the scalp can be particularly uncomfortable for babies, as it can cause crusting, scaling, and hair loss.

While eczema is not curable, it can be managed with proper treatment. There are a variety of things you can do to help soothe your baby's eczema on their scalp.

How to Treat Eczema on Scalp for Babies

Here are some steps you can take to help treat your baby's eczema on their scalp:

1. Use a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo

Choose a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo that is specifically formulated for babies. Avoid using harsh shampoos or soaps, as they can irritate the skin and make eczema worse.

2. Keep the scalp moisturized

Apply a gentle, fragrance-free moisturizer to your baby's scalp after bath time. This can help soothe the skin and prevent dryness, which can worsen eczema symptoms.

3. Avoid scratching the scalp

Scratching can make eczema symptoms worse, so it's important to avoid scratching your baby's scalp. You can help prevent scratching by keeping your baby's nails short and using mittens or socks to cover their hands.

4. Try a medicated shampoo

If your baby's eczema is severe, your doctor may recommend a medicated shampoo to help soothe the scalp. These shampoos contain ingredients like coal tar or salicylic acid, which can help reduce inflammation and itching.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you can follow to treat eczema on scalp for babies:

  1. Choose a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo.
  2. Wash your baby's hair with the shampoo, being careful not to scrub too hard.
  3. Rinse the shampoo out thoroughly.
  4. Pat your baby's scalp dry with a soft towel.
  5. Apply a gentle, fragrance-free moisturizer to your baby's scalp.
  6. Avoid scratching your baby's scalp.
  7. If your baby's eczema is severe, talk to your doctor about a medicated shampoo.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help manage eczema on scalp for babies:

  • Use a humidifier in your baby's room to help keep the air moist.
  • Avoid using hats or headbands that can irritate the scalp.
  • Dress your baby in soft, breathable clothing that won't irritate the skin.

FAQ

Q: Is eczema on the scalp common in babies?

A: Yes, eczema on the scalp is a common skin condition in babies. It can cause redness, itching, and flaking of the skin.

Q: Can eczema on the scalp cause hair loss?

A: Yes, severe eczema on the scalp can cause hair loss. It's important to manage eczema symptoms to prevent hair loss.

Q: How can I prevent my baby from scratching their scalp?

A: You can prevent your baby from scratching their scalp by keeping their nails short and using mittens or socks to cover their hands.

Hair Care

When your baby has eczema on their scalp, it's important to take extra care when washing and styling their hair. Here are some tips for caring for your baby's hair:

  • Use a soft-bristled brush to gently detangle the hair.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or fragrances.
  • Don't tie your baby's hair too tightly, as this can irritate the scalp.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers or straighteners.

By following these tips, you can help manage your baby's eczema on their scalp and keep their hair healthy and happy.
