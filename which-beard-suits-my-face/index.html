Which Beard Suits My Face Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi May 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your clean-shaven look and want to try a new style? Growing a beard can be a great way to change up your appearance and give you a new sense of confidence. However, not all beards are created equal, and it's important to find the one that suits your face shape and features. In this guide, we'll explore the different types of beards and help you determine which one is right for you.

Table of Contents

  1. Determining Your Face Shape
  2. Types of Beards
  3. Finding Your Style
  4. Beard Maintenance Tips
  5. FAQ

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the right beard for your face is to determine your face shape. There are six common face shapes: round, square, oval, oblong, diamond, and heart. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and use a tape measure to measure the length and width of your face.

If your face is roughly the same length and width, you have a round face. If your face is longer than it is wide and has a prominent jawline, you have a square face. If your face is longer than it is wide and has soft, rounded features, you have an oval face. If your face is longer and more narrow than an oval face, you have an oblong face. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline, you have a diamond face. If your forehead is wider than your jawline and your chin comes to a point, you have a heart-shaped face.

Types of Beards

Now that you've determined your face shape, it's time to explore the different types of beards. Here are some popular styles:

Full Beard

The full beard is a classic style that works well with most face shapes. It's a great option if you want a bold, masculine look. However, it does require some maintenance to keep it looking tidy.

Stubble

Stubble is a great option for those who want a more low-maintenance look. It works well with most face shapes and can help define your jawline.

Goatee

The goatee is a popular style that involves growing hair on the chin only. It works well with most face shapes but is particularly good for those with round or square faces.

Soul Patch

The soul patch is a small patch of hair just below the bottom lip. It's a good option for those who want a subtle, stylish look. It works well with most face shapes but is particularly good for those with heart-shaped faces.

Mutton Chops

Mutton chops involve growing long sideburns that extend down to the jawline. They work well with square and oval face shapes and can give you a bold, retro look.

Finding Your Style

Now that you're familiar with the different types of beards, it's time to find the one that suits you best. Consider your face shape, hair type, and personal style when choosing a beard. Some styles may require more maintenance than others, so be sure to choose one that you're willing to commit to.

It's also a good idea to consult with a barber or stylist before making any major changes to your beard. They can offer advice on which styles will work best for your face shape and help you achieve your desired look.

Beard Maintenance Tips

Once you've chosen a beard style, it's important to maintain it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Invest in a good beard trimmer or scissors.
  • Trim your beard regularly to keep it looking tidy.
  • Use beard oil or balm to keep your beard soft and moisturized.
  • Wash your beard regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Comb or brush your beard daily to prevent tangles.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing the right beard:

Do all beard styles work with all face shapes?

No, certain styles work better with certain face shapes. It's important to choose a style that complements your features.

How long does it take to grow a beard?

The amount of time it takes to grow a beard varies from person to person. On average, it takes about 2-4 months to grow a full beard.

What should I do if my beard is patchy?

If your beard is patchy, you may need to choose a different style or wait until it fills in more. You can also try using beard oil or balm to stimulate growth.

Do I need to trim my beard?

Yes, regular trimming is important to keep your beard looking tidy and well-groomed.

In conclusion, choosing the right beard for your face shape and features can be a great way to switch up your style and give you a new sense of confidence. By following these tips and consulting with a professional, you can find the perfect beard for you and maintain it properly for a polished, stylish look.
