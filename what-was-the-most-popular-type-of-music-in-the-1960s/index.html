What Was The Most Popular Type Of Music In The 1960S

Written by Luffy Mar 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

The 1960s was a decade of significant cultural and social change in the United States, with music playing a huge role in shaping the attitudes and beliefs of the younger generation. The music of the 1960s was diverse, but there were some genres that were particularly popular during this time. In this article, we will explore what was the most popular type of music in the 1960s.

Table of Content

  • Rock
  • Folk
  • Soul
  • Pop
  • Jazz
  • Country

Rock

Rock music was arguably the most popular genre of music in the 1960s. The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, The Who, and Jimi Hendrix were just a few of the rock bands that dominated the music scene during this time. The popularity of rock music can be attributed to the rebellious and anti-establishment attitudes of the younger generation, who were seeking to break away from the conservative values of their parents.

Folk

Folk music was another popular genre of music in the 1960s. Folk music was characterized by its simplicity and its focus on storytelling. Artists like Bob Dylan, Joan Baez, and Pete Seeger were instrumental in popularizing this genre of music. Folk music was often associated with the civil rights movement and the anti-war movement, as many of the songs were political in nature.

Soul

Soul music was a genre of music that emerged in the 1960s, primarily among African American musicians. Soul music was characterized by its passionate vocals, heartfelt lyrics, and the use of gospel-influenced rhythms. Artists like Aretha Franklin, Otis Redding, and James Brown were some of the biggest names in soul music during this time.

Pop

Pop music was another popular genre of music in the 1960s. Pop music was characterized by its catchy melodies, upbeat rhythms, and its focus on love and romance. The Beatles were perhaps the biggest pop band of the 1960s, with hits like "I Want to Hold Your Hand" and "Hey Jude" topping the charts.

Jazz

Jazz music was another genre of music that was popular in the 1960s. Jazz music was characterized by its improvisation, complex harmonies, and its focus on instrumental virtuosity. Artists like Miles Davis, John Coltrane, and Charlie Parker were some of the biggest names in jazz during this time.

Country

Country music was another genre of music that was popular in the 1960s. Country music was characterized by its focus on storytelling, its use of simple instrumentation, and its emphasis on traditional values. Artists like Johnny Cash, Patsy Cline, and Loretta Lynn were some of the biggest names in country music during this time.

Tips for Listening to 1960s Music

If you're interested in exploring the music of the 1960s, here are some tips to get you started:

  • Start with the biggest names in each genre - The Beatles for rock, Bob Dylan for folk, Aretha Franklin for soul, etc.
  • Listen to the lyrics - Many songs from the 1960s were political or social commentary, so pay attention to the lyrics to understand the message behind the music.
  • Watch live performances - Many of the biggest names in 1960s music were known for their live performances, so watch some of their concerts on YouTube to get a sense of their energy and stage presence.

FAQs

Q: What was the most popular type of music in the 1960s?

A: Rock music was arguably the most popular genre of music in the 1960s, followed closely by folk, soul, pop, jazz, and country.

Q: Who were some of the biggest names in 1960s music?

A: Some of the biggest names in 1960s music included The Beatles, Bob Dylan, Aretha Franklin, James Brown, and Johnny Cash.

Hair Care and 1960s Music

The music of the 1960s was not just about the music - it was also about the fashion and the hairstyles. Many of the biggest names in 1960s music were known for their distinctive hairstyles, from The Beatles' mop tops to Jimi Hendrix's afro. If you're interested in recreating some of these iconic hairstyles, here are some tips:

  • Invest in the right hair products - Whether you're looking to achieve a sleek, straight hairstyle or a voluminous afro, the right hair products are key.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles - The 1960s was a time of experimentation, so don't be afraid to try out different hairstyles to find the one that works best for you.
  • Accessorize - From headbands to flower crowns, accessories were a big part of 1960s hairstyles. Experiment with different accessories to add some flair to your hairstyle.

Overall, the music of the 1960s was incredibly diverse, with something for everyone. Whether you're a fan of rock, folk, soul, pop, jazz, or country, there's no denying the impact that 1960s music had on the cultural landscape of the United States.
