What To Do If Your Hair Falls Out From Bleach

Description

Bleaching your hair can be a fun way to change up your look, but it can also be damaging to your hair. If you've recently bleached your hair and notice that it's falling out, you may be wondering what to do next. Hair falling out from bleach is a common problem, but it can be managed if you take the right steps.

How to

The first thing you should do if your hair is falling out from bleach is to stop using any hair products that contain bleach. This includes hair dyes, shampoos, and conditioners. You should also avoid using any heat styling tools on your hair, as they can further damage it.

Next, you should start using a deep conditioning treatment on your hair. This will help to restore some of the moisture that has been lost due to the bleach. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner, or you can make your own using ingredients like honey, coconut oil, and avocado.

If your hair is falling out in clumps, you may want to consider cutting it short. This will help to remove any damaged hair and give your hair a chance to grow back healthy. You can also consider wearing a wig or hair extensions while your hair is growing back.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you should take if your hair is falling out from bleach:

  1. Stop using any hair products that contain bleach.
  2. Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  3. Start using a deep conditioning treatment on your hair.
  4. Consider cutting your hair short.
  5. Consider wearing a wig or hair extensions while your hair is growing back.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you manage hair fall-out from bleach:

  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or clips that can pull on your hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf when you're outside to protect your hair from the sun.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair fall-out from bleach:

Q: Can I dye my hair again after it falls out from bleach?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before dyeing it again.

Q: Will my hair grow back after it falls out from bleach?

A: Yes, your hair should grow back eventually. However, it may take some time for it to grow back fully.

Q: Can I use hair growth products to help my hair grow back after it falls out from bleach?

A: Yes, there are many hair growth products on the market that may help your hair grow back faster.

Hair Care

After your hair has fallen out from bleach, it's important to take good care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's designed for damaged hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Be gentle when towel-drying your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Get regular trims to remove any damaged hair.

In conclusion, if your hair falls out from bleach, don't panic. There are steps you can take to manage the problem and help your hair grow back healthy. By avoiding further damage, using a deep conditioning treatment, cutting your hair short if necessary, and taking good care of your hair, you can help your hair recover from the damage caused by bleach.
