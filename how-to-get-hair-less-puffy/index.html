How To Get Hair Less Puffy Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jul 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly or frizzy hair, you probably know the struggle of dealing with puffy hair. While some people love the volume and bounce that comes with puffy hair, others prefer a sleeker, more polished look. Fortunately, there are several ways to get hair less puffy without spending a lot of time or money.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How to Get Hair Less Puffy
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks

Hair Care

Before we dive into the different ways to get hair less puffy, it's important to talk about hair care. Taking care of your hair can go a long way in preventing frizz and puffiness. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often. Over-washing can also strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid towel-drying your hair. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

How to Get Hair Less Puffy

Now that we've covered hair care, let's talk about some ways to get hair less puffy:

  • Use a hair serum or cream: Hair serums and creams can help tame frizz and puffiness. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.
  • Blow-dry your hair with a round brush: Blow-drying your hair with a round brush can help smooth out any puffiness. Start at the roots and work your way down, pulling the brush through your hair as you go.
  • Sleep with a silk pillowcase: Silk pillowcases are gentler on your hair than cotton pillowcases, which can cause friction and lead to frizz.
  • Try a hair mask: A hair mask can help hydrate your hair and reduce puffiness. Look for masks that contain ingredients like avocado oil, honey, or coconut milk.
  • Get a keratin treatment: Keratin treatments can help smooth out frizz and make your hair more manageable. However, they can be expensive and time-consuming.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get hair less puffy:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a hair serum or cream to damp hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Blow-dry your hair with a round brush, starting at the roots and working your way down.
  4. Sleep with a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz.
  5. Once a week, apply a hair mask to hydrate your hair and reduce puffiness.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for getting hair less puffy:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much. The oils from your hands can make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • If you're in a rush, try using a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and add volume.
  • If you're going to use heat tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting hair less puffy:

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people can go several days without washing their hair, while others need to wash it every day. As a general rule, try to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

What is a keratin treatment?

A keratin treatment is a hair-smoothing treatment that uses keratin, a protein found in hair, to help tame frizz and make hair more manageable. The treatment involves applying a keratin solution to the hair, then flat-ironing it to seal in the product.

Can I get rid of puffy hair permanently?

There's no permanent solution to puffy hair, but there are several ways to manage it. Experiment with different hair care products and styling techniques to find what works best for you.


In conclusion, getting hair less puffy is all about taking care of your hair and finding the right products and techniques that work for you. Experiment with different methods until you find what gives you the sleek, polished look you're after. With a little patience and persistence, you can have smooth, manageable hair that you'll love showing off.
