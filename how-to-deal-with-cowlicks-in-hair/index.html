How To Deal With Cowlicks In Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Sep 15, 2023 · 9 min read

Cowlicks can be a frustrating hair problem for many people. These stubborn strands of hair that grow in a different direction from the rest of your hair can be difficult to style and manage. However, with the right techniques and products, you can tame your cowlicks and achieve a stylish and polished look. In this article, we'll show you how to deal with cowlicks in hair and share some tips and tricks to help you get the best results.

Description

A cowlick is a section of hair that grows in a different direction from the rest of your hair. It's usually found at the crown of the head or at the hairline. Cowlicks can be caused by genetics, hair texture, or the way you sleep at night. They can be hard to style and manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can keep them under control.

How to Deal with Cowlicks in Hair

If you're dealing with cowlicks in your hair, here are some tips and tricks to help you manage them:

1. Choose the Right Haircut

The right haircut can make a big difference when it comes to dealing with cowlicks. A layered cut can help to blend the cowlick into the rest of your hair, while a short cut can make it easier to manage. Talk to your hairstylist about the best options for your hair type and face shape.

2. Blow-Dry Your Hair in the Right Direction

Blow-drying your hair in the right direction can help to tame your cowlicks. Use a round brush to pull your hair in the opposite direction of the cowlick while blow-drying. This will help to smooth out the hair and make it easier to style.

3. Use the Right Hair Products

The right hair products can also help to manage your cowlicks. A styling cream or pomade can help to hold the hair in place and smooth out any frizz. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type and texture.

4. Train Your Hair

You can train your hair to grow in a certain direction by using a hair clip or bobby pin to hold the hair in place while it dries. This can help to retrain the hair to grow in a different direction and make it easier to style.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to dealing with cowlicks in your hair:

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

Talk to your hairstylist about the best haircut for your hair type and face shape. A layered cut can help to blend the cowlick into the rest of your hair, while a short cut can make it easier to manage.

Step 2: Blow-Dry Your Hair in the Right Direction

Use a round brush to pull your hair in the opposite direction of the cowlick while blow-drying. This will help to smooth out the hair and make it easier to style.

Step 3: Use the Right Hair Products

Apply a styling cream or pomade to hold the hair in place and smooth out any frizz. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type and texture.

Step 4: Train Your Hair

Use a hair clip or bobby pin to hold the hair in place while it dries. This can help to retrain the hair to grow in a different direction and make it easier to style.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you deal with cowlicks in your hair:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

A satin pillowcase can help to reduce frizz and keep your hair smooth while you sleep. This can help to prevent cowlicks from forming in the first place.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz and cowlicks. Try to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

3. Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools on your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage and breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can cowlicks be permanently removed?

A: Unfortunately, cowlicks can't be permanently removed. However, with the right techniques and products, you can manage them and keep them under control.

Q: Can hair products cause cowlicks?

A: Hair products themselves don't cause cowlicks, but using the wrong products or applying them incorrectly can make cowlicks more noticeable.

Q: Can cowlicks be a sign of a medical condition?

A: In rare cases, cowlicks can be a sign of a medical condition such as alopecia areata. If you're concerned about your cowlicks or notice any other changes in your hair, talk to your doctor or dermatologist.

Hair Care

Dealing with cowlicks in your hair can be frustrating, but with the right techniques and products, you can manage them and achieve a polished and stylish look. Remember to choose the right haircut, blow-dry your hair in the right direction, use the right hair products, and train your hair to grow in a different direction. With a little patience and persistence, you can tame your cowlicks and love your hair.
