Medium Length Hairstyles For Thin Hair Over 50 With Round Face

Written by Petter Apr 14, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’re looking for a new hairstyle to suit your thin hair over 50 with a round face, medium length hairstyles could be just what you need. Not too long, not too short, medium length hair is perfect for those who want a versatile and low-maintenance style that looks great with minimal effort. In this post, we’ll look at some of the best medium length hairstyles for thin hair over 50 with a round face, and offer tips on how to care for your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.

Description

As we age, our hair tends to become thinner and more fragile. This can be particularly challenging for those with a round face, as it can be difficult to find a hairstyle that flatters your features without making your hair look even thinner. Medium length hairstyles offer a great solution to this problem, as they are long enough to add volume and texture to your hair, but short enough to be easy to manage.

How to Choose the Right Medium Length Hairstyle for Your Thin Hair and Round Face

When choosing a medium length hairstyle, there are a few key factors to consider:

Your Face Shape

If you have a round face, you should look for a hairstyle that adds height and length to your face. Avoid styles that are too voluminous or that add too much width to your face.

Your Hair Texture

If you have thin hair, you should look for a hairstyle that adds volume and texture to your hair. Avoid styles that are too flat or that make your hair look even thinner.

Your Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when choosing a hairstyle. If you’re busy and don’t have a lot of time to style your hair, look for a low-maintenance style that doesn’t require a lot of upkeep.

Step by Step Guide to Styling Your Hair

Once you’ve chosen a medium length hairstyle that works for your thin hair and round face, here are some tips for styling your hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair. Use a volumizing mousse or a texturizing spray to add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and shape. If you have thin hair, avoid using a high heat setting, as this can damage your hair.

Step 4: Add Finishing Touches

Once your hair is dry, add some finishing touches to your style. Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking its best:

Tip 1: Use Gentle Products

Use gentle products that are designed for thin, fragile hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling tools that can damage your hair.

Tip 2: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Tip 4: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and nutrients to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

FAQs

What are the best hairstyles for thin hair over 50 with a round face?

Medium length hairstyles are a great choice for thin hair over 50 with a round face. Look for styles that add volume and texture to your hair without making it look too flat.

How can I add volume to my thin hair?

You can add volume to your thin hair by using volumizing products, blow drying your hair with a round brush, and using a texturizing spray to add texture.

What should I avoid when styling my thin hair?

Avoid using too much product, using a high heat setting on your styling tools, and using harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

Hair Care

When it comes to taking care of your hair, it’s important to use gentle products that are designed for thin, fragile hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals and styling tools that can damage your hair, and protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, and eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and nutrients to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.
