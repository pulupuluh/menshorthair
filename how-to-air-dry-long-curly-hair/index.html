How To Air Dry Long Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 06, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of using heat tools on your long curly hair? Air drying is a great alternative to give your tresses a break from the heat. Not to mention, it's a low maintenance way to care for your curls. In this article, we'll show you how to air dry long curly hair and give you some tips on how to keep your curls looking healthy and bouncy.

How to Air Dry Long Curly Hair

Air drying your long curly hair is a simple process that can be done in a few easy steps. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step to air drying your long curly hair is to wash it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair to keep your locks moisturized and frizz-free. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water to remove all the product.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair while it's still wet. Don't use a brush as it can damage your curls and cause frizz.

Step 3: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz. Use a dime-sized amount on each section of your hair and distribute it evenly with your fingers.

Step 4: Scrunch Your Hair

Scrunch your hair with a microfiber towel or an old T-shirt to remove excess water. Don't rub your hair with a towel as it can cause frizz.

Step 5: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Let your hair air dry naturally. Don't use a hair dryer or any heat tools as they can damage your curls and cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to air dry long curly hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water to remove all the product.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair while it's still wet. Start from the ends and work your way up to the roots. Don't use a brush as it can damage your curls and cause frizz.

Step 3: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz. Use a dime-sized amount on each section of your hair and distribute it evenly with your fingers.

Step 4: Scrunch Your Hair

Scrunch your hair with a microfiber towel or an old T-shirt to remove excess water. Don't rub your hair with a towel as it can cause frizz.

Step 5: Part Your Hair

Part your hair in the way you want it to dry. This will help your hair dry evenly and prevent frizz.

Step 6: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Let your hair air dry naturally. Don't use a hair dryer or any heat tools as they can damage your curls and cause frizz. You can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer if you want to speed up the drying process, but make sure you use it on the cool setting.

Tips for Air Drying Long Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep your curls looking healthy and bouncy:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid touching your hair while it's drying as this can cause frizz.
  • Don't tie your hair up in a ponytail or bun while it's wet as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQs

Can I air dry my hair overnight?

Yes, you can air dry your hair overnight. Just make sure your hair is completely dry before you go to bed to prevent breakage.

Can I use a hair dryer on cool setting?

Yes, you can use a hair dryer on the cool setting with a diffuser attachment to speed up the drying process. Just make sure you don't use heat as it can damage your curls.

Hair Care for Long Curly Hair

Air drying your long curly hair is just one part of your hair care routine. Here are some tips to keep your curls healthy and bouncy:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Don't wash your hair every day as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get a trim every 8-12 weeks to prevent split ends.

Air drying your long curly hair is a great way to give your locks a break from heat tools. Follow these simple steps and tips to keep your curls looking healthy and bouncy. With a little patience and care, you can achieve beautiful, natural-looking curls.
