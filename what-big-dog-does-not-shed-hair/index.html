What Big Dog Does Not Shed Hair A Guide To Low Shedding Breeds

Written by Eveline Feb 23, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're a dog lover but suffer from allergies or simply can't stand the sight of dog hair everywhere, you're not alone. Fortunately, there are several big dog breeds that don't shed much or at all. In this post, we'll explore the best low-shedding breeds, how to care for their hair, and tips for keeping your home clean.

What Big Dog Breeds Don't Shed Hair?

There are several big dog breeds that don't shed much or at all. Here are some of the most popular ones:

1. Poodle

Poodles are one of the most popular low-shedding breeds. Their curly coat doesn't shed much, but it does require regular grooming to prevent matting and tangling.

2. Irish Water Spaniel

The Irish Water Spaniel is a large, curly-coated breed that doesn't shed much. They're also known for their intelligence and athleticism.

3. Portuguese Water Dog

The Portuguese Water Dog is another curly-coated breed that doesn't shed much. They were originally bred to help fishermen in Portugal and have a natural love for water.

4. Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier

The Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier has a soft, wavy coat that doesn't shed much. They're known for their friendly and outgoing personalities.

5. Giant Schnauzer

The Giant Schnauzer has a wiry, non-shedding coat that requires regular grooming. They're known for their loyalty and protectiveness.

How to Care for Low-Shedding Dog Hair

Even though low-shedding breeds don't shed much, their hair still requires regular care. Here are some tips for keeping their coat healthy:

1. Brush regularly

Regular brushing will prevent matting and tangling, which can be uncomfortable for your dog. Brush at least once a week, or more often if your dog has a long or curly coat.

2. Bathe occasionally

Bathing too often can strip your dog's coat of its natural oils. Bathe your dog only when necessary, and use a gentle dog shampoo.

3. Cut hair regularly

If your dog has long hair, consider getting it trimmed regularly. This will prevent matting and tangling and keep your dog's coat looking healthy.

Tips for Keeping Your Home Clean

Even though low-shedding breeds don't shed much, there will still be some hair around your home. Here are some tips for keeping your home clean:

1. Vacuum regularly

Vacuum your floors and furniture regularly to pick up any loose hair.

2. Use a lint roller

Use a lint roller to pick up hair from clothing and upholstery.

3. Wash bedding regularly

Wash your dog's bedding regularly to remove any hair or dander.

FAQs

Q: Do low-shedding dogs still cause allergies?

A: Yes, low-shedding dogs can still cause allergies. Allergies are caused by dander, saliva, and urine, which can all be present even in low-shedding breeds.

Q: Do low-shedding dogs require less grooming?

A: No, low-shedding dogs still require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and prevent matting and tangling.

Conclusion

If you're looking for a big dog that doesn't shed much or at all, there are several breeds to choose from. Whether you opt for a curly-coated Poodle or an athletic Irish Water Spaniel, remember that regular grooming is key to keeping their coat healthy. And with some simple cleaning tips, you can keep your home hair-free and allergy-friendly.
