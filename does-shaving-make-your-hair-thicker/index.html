Does Shaving Make Your Hair Thicker

Written by Robby Jun 21, 2023 · 6 min read

Welcome to our blog post about one of the most common hair myths – does shaving make your hair thicker? This topic has been debated for years, and we're here to provide you with all the information you need to know. In this article, we'll discuss the science behind hair growth, how shaving affects hair, tips and tricks for shaving, and hair care to ensure healthy hair growth.

Table of Contents

  • The Science Behind Hair Growth
  • How Shaving Affects Hair
  • Tips and Tricks for Shaving
  • Hair Care for Healthy Hair Growth
  • FAQ

The Science Behind Hair Growth

To understand whether shaving makes your hair thicker, we first need to understand how hair growth works. Hair grows from hair follicles located in the dermis, the second layer of skin. Each hair follicle contains a hair shaft, which is made up of dead cells that are pushed up through the skin by new cells that are constantly forming at the base of the follicle.

The thickness and texture of your hair are determined by genetics and hormones. Androgens, a group of hormones that includes testosterone, play a significant role in hair growth. These hormones stimulate hair follicles to produce thicker, coarser hair in areas such as the beard, chest, and pubic region.

How Shaving Affects Hair

Shaving has no effect on hair growth or thickness. When you shave, you're cutting the hair shaft at the surface of the skin, leaving the hair follicle intact. The hair follicle is still producing hair at the same rate and thickness as before.

However, after shaving, the hair that grows back may feel thicker or coarser. This is because the hair that was shaved had a tapered end, which made it feel softer and thinner. The new hair that grows back after shaving has a blunt end, which can make it feel rougher and thicker.

Tips and Tricks for Shaving

If you choose to shave, it's important to do it correctly to avoid irritation and ingrown hairs. Here are some tips and tricks for a smooth shave:

  • Start with a clean face
  • Use a sharp razor
  • Shave in the direction of hair growth
  • Use shaving cream or gel
  • Rinse with cold water
  • Apply an aftershave lotion or balm

Hair Care for Healthy Hair Growth

While shaving doesn't affect hair growth or thickness, taking care of your hair can ensure healthy growth. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Massage your scalp to stimulate blood flow
  • Trim split ends regularly

FAQ

Q: Does shaving make hair grow back thicker?
A: No, shaving has no effect on hair growth or thickness. The hair follicle is still producing hair at the same rate and thickness as before. Q: How often should I shave?
A: This depends on personal preference and hair growth rate. Some people prefer to shave every day, while others may only need to shave once a week. Q: How can I prevent ingrown hairs?
A: To prevent ingrown hairs, exfoliate regularly, use a sharp razor, shave in the direction of hair growth, and avoid shaving too closely to the skin.

In conclusion, shaving does not make your hair thicker. It only affects the texture of the hair that grows back. If you choose to shave, be sure to do it correctly to avoid irritation and ingrown hairs. And remember, taking care of your hair through a balanced diet and proper hair care can ensure healthy hair growth.
