How To Cut Your Own Short Hair Evenly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every few weeks to maintain your short hair? Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps of how to cut your own short hair evenly.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You Will Need
  2. Preparing Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Blending Your Hair
  6. Finishing Your Hair
  7. Tips and Tricks
  8. Frequently Asked Questions
  9. Hair Care After Cutting
  10. Conclusion

Tools You Will Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, it is important to have the necessary tools on hand. Here are the items you will need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Clipper with guards (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape

Preparing Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before cutting. Wet hair is easier to cut and will give you a more accurate result. Comb your hair straight and remove any tangles. You can also use a towel or cape to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Sectioning Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections. Use hair ties or clips to secure the hair that you are not cutting. Start by sectioning your hair horizontally across the back of your head. Make sure the sections are even and straight.

Cutting Your Hair

Start cutting the bottom section of your hair in a straight line. Use the comb to hold the hair straight and cut the hair with the scissors. Make sure to cut slowly and in small sections to avoid any mistakes. Continue cutting the hair in the back until you have reached the desired length.

Cutting the Sides

When cutting the sides of your hair, start by creating a guide. Use your fingers or a comb to create a straight line from the back of your head to the front. Cut the hair in small sections, using the guide to ensure that you are cutting the hair evenly. Repeat on the other side.

Blending Your Hair

Blend the hair on the top of your head with the sides and back. Use the comb to hold the hair straight and cut the hair in small sections. Make sure that the hair on top of your head is longer than the sides and back for a more natural look.

Finishing Your Hair

After you have finished cutting your hair, use the clipper with guards to trim the hair around your ears and the nape of your neck. This will give your hair a clean and polished look. Use the scissors to make any final adjustments to the length of your hair.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Don't cut too much at once to avoid mistakes.
  • Use the right tools for the job.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

Wet hair is easier to cut and will give you a more accurate result. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's dry, make sure it is clean and combed straight.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style you want to maintain. Short hair usually needs to be trimmed every four to six weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, wash and condition it as usual. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals on your hair for at least a week after cutting to prevent damage.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you can cut your own short hair evenly and achieve a professional-looking result. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the tips and tricks for the best outcome. With a little practice, you can save time and money by cutting your own hair at home.
