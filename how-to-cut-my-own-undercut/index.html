How To Cut Your Own Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on salon visits just to maintain your undercut? Why not try cutting it yourself? It may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a professional-looking undercut right at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own undercut in relaxed English language. So, let's get started!

What You'll Need:

Before we begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • A pair of clippers
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A pair of scissors
  • A spray bottle with water
  • A towel or cape to protect your clothes

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Use a clip to secure the top part of your hair, leaving the bottom part exposed.

Step 3: Clipper the Sides and Back

Using the clippers, start from the bottom and work your way up. Use a number 3 or 4 guard for a shorter undercut, or a number 5 or 6 guard for a longer one. Be sure to blend the sides and the back, and make sure the lines are even.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Unclip the top part of your hair and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. For a longer undercut, you can leave more length on top. For a shorter undercut, you can cut the hair shorter.

Step 5: Blend the Top and Sides

Using your scissors, blend the top part of your hair with the sides and back. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle, creating a seamless blend between the top and the sides.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Use your mirror to check for evenness on both sides. Make any necessary adjustments and go over any uneven spots with your clippers or scissors.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline

Use your clippers to clean up the neckline. Start from the middle and work your way outwards, creating a clean and straight line.

Step 8: Style Your Undercut

Apply your favorite styling product to your hair and style it as desired. You can use a hairdryer and a brush to create volume, or use your fingers to create a more textured look.

Tips:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter undercut.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start with a small section and see how it turns out.

FAQ:

Can I cut my own undercut if I have curly or wavy hair?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as curly or wavy hair can appear longer when wet.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix it for you. Alternatively, you can wear a hat or style your hair in a way that covers up the mistake.

Hair Care:

To maintain your undercut, make sure to trim it every 4-6 weeks. You can use the same technique outlined in this guide to do so. Additionally, make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much styling product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it harder to style.

Cutting your own undercut can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can achieve a professional-looking cut right at home. So, go ahead and give it a try!
