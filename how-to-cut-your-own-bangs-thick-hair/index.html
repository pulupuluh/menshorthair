How To Cut Your Own Bangs For Thick Hair

Written by Christine May 20, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon to get your bangs trimmed? Do you have thick hair that requires a little extra attention when it comes to cutting your own bangs? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with all the necessary tips and steps to achieve salon-worthy bangs in the comfort of your own home.

Description

Cutting your own bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hairstyle. Not to mention, it can be a fun and empowering experience to take control of your own hair.

How to

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 1: Section your hair

Using the fine-tooth comb, section off the front portion of your hair that you want to cut into bangs. Make sure the section is even and straight.

Step 2: Wet your hair

Spray the section of hair with water to dampen it. This will make it easier to cut and prevent any hair from flying around.

Step 3: Determine the length

Decide on the length you want your bangs to be. It's always better to start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 4: Cut your hair

Using your scissors, cut your hair in a straight line across. Be sure to cut slowly and carefully to avoid any mistakes.

Step 5: Check for evenness

After cutting, use the fine-tooth comb to check for evenness. If you notice any uneven areas, trim them as needed.

Step 6: Style your hair

Now that your bangs are cut, you can style them as desired. Use a straightener or curling iron to add some flair, or leave them natural for a casual look.

Tips

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry to prevent any surprises in length.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure your bangs are even.
  • Start with small cuts and trim as needed to avoid any major mistakes.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own bangs, consider consulting a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my bangs without any experience?

Yes, anyone can cut their own bangs with the right tools and techniques. Just make sure you start with small cuts and go slow to avoid any mistakes.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim your bangs further or visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and length.

Hair Care

After cutting your own bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent any damage or breakage. Use a nourishing hair oil or serum to keep your bangs looking healthy and shiny. Also, avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent any heat damage.

By following these steps and tips, you can successfully cut your own bangs for thick hair. Not only will you save money and time, but you'll also feel empowered and confident with your new hairstyle. Happy cutting!
