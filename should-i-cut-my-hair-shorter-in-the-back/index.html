Should I Cut My Hair Shorter In The Back

Description

Many people wonder whether they should cut their hair shorter in the back. This is a common question for those who are considering a new hairstyle or just want to change up their look. There are several factors to consider when deciding whether to cut your hair shorter in the back, including your face shape, hair type, and personal style. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair shorter in the back, as well as tips for maintaining your new hairstyle.

How to Cut Your Hair Shorter in the Back

If you are considering cutting your hair shorter in the back, it is important to have a plan in place before you start. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Evaluate Your Hair Type and Face Shape

The first step in cutting your hair shorter in the back is to evaluate your hair type and face shape. This will help you determine the best style for your hair. If you have thick hair, you may want to consider a layered cut to remove some of the weight. If you have fine hair, a blunt cut may be better suited to add volume. Similarly, if you have a round face, you may want to consider a longer cut to elongate your face, while those with an oval face may opt for a shorter cut.

2. Choose Your Style

Once you have evaluated your hair type and face shape, you can choose your style. There are many options for cutting your hair shorter in the back, including a graduated bob, a pixie cut, or a blunt cut. You can also add layers or bangs to your new style to create a more unique look.

3. Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need to gather your tools. This includes sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. You may also want to invest in a hair cutting cape to protect your clothing from hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have a plan in place, you can start cutting your hair shorter in the back. Here is a step-by-step guide:

1. Section Off Your Hair

Start by sectioning off your hair. Use hair clips to separate the hair at the crown of your head from the hair at the nape of your neck. This will make it easier to cut your hair in layers.

2. Cut Your Hair

Using sharp scissors, start cutting your hair at the back of your head. Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure that you are cutting evenly. If you are creating a graduated bob, cut the hair at the back of your head shorter than the hair at the front. If you are creating a blunt cut, cut your hair straight across.

3. Blend Your Layers

If you are cutting your hair in layers, you will need to blend them together. Use your scissors to cut upward into the hair to create a seamless blend.

4. Style Your Hair

Once you have cut your hair shorter in the back, you can style it as desired. Blow dry your hair straight or add some curls for a more voluminous look.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair Shorter in the Back

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair shorter in the back:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure that you are cutting evenly.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid splitting the hair.
  • Keep your hair wet while cutting to ensure that you are cutting evenly.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut a little at a time to avoid any mistakes.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your hair shorter in the back:

Q: Will cutting my hair shorter in the back make my face look longer?

A: Yes, cutting your hair shorter in the back can elongate your face, especially if you have a round face shape.

Q: What is the best hairstyle for someone with fine hair?

A: A blunt cut is a great option for someone with fine hair, as it can add volume and the illusion of thicker hair.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Hair Shorter in the Back

After cutting your hair shorter in the back, it is important to take care of your new hairstyle. Here are some tips for maintaining your new look:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your new style and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.

Overall, cutting your hair shorter in the back can be a great way to change up your look and create a more modern hairstyle. By following these tips and using the right tools, you can achieve a beautiful new style that will make you feel confident and stylish.
