How Can I Cut My Short Hair In Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to switch up your short haircut, adding layers can give it a fresh and modern look. But you don't have to go to a salon - with the right tools and technique, you can easily cut your short hair in layers at home. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to do it, as well as some tips and advice for maintaining your new hairstyle.

How to Cut Your Short Hair in Layers at Home

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and movement of your hair, which will help you determine where to cut.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Use your comb to section off your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. Keep in mind that the shorter the layers, the more dramatic the effect will be.

Step 4: Cut the Top Section

Start with the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want your layers to be. Repeat this process for the rest of the top section, working from the front to the back.

Step 5: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight out from your head. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want your layers to be. Repeat this process for the rest of the side sections, working from the front to the back.

Step 6: Cut the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want your layers to be. Repeat this process for the rest of the back section, working from the bottom to the top.

Step 7: Blend Your Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and snip off any uneven ends.

Tips for Cutting Your Short Hair in Layers at Home

Here are some additional tips and advice for achieving the perfect layered haircut at home:

  • Always use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven ends.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to start with a conservative cut and work your way up, rather than cutting off too much and regretting it later.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress. It's important to see your hair from all angles to make sure you're cutting evenly.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short hair in layers at home:

Can I cut my short hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut your short hair in layers if it's curly. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up when it dries and change the length of your layers.

How often should I trim my layered short hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and length of your layers.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair in layers, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut your short hair in layers at home. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
