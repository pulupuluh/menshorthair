How To Make Deep Wave Curls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a way to add some volume and bounce to your hair, deep wave curls might just be the perfect solution for you! These curls are loose and relaxed, creating a natural and effortless look. In this article, we'll show you how to make deep wave curls in just a few simple steps. So, let's get started!

Description

Deep wave curls are a popular hairstyle that adds volume and texture to your hair. These curls are loose and relaxed, creating a natural and effortless look. They're perfect for any occasion, whether you're heading to the office or out for a night on the town. The best part? You can easily create these curls at home with just a few simple tools.

How to Make Deep Wave Curls

Follow these simple steps to create deep wave curls:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Make sure to use a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before you start using any hot tools on your hair, it's important to apply a heat protectant. This will help to prevent damage to your hair and keep it looking healthy. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small sections. The smaller the section, the tighter the curl will be. Use clips to keep each section separate and out of the way.

Step 4: Use a Curling Iron

Take your curling iron and clamp it onto the first section of hair. Hold the curling iron vertically, with the barrel facing downward. Wrap the hair around the barrel, making sure to leave the ends out. Hold for a few seconds and release.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat this process on each section of hair. Make sure to curl each section in the same direction to create a uniform look. For deep wave curls, you'll want to curl the hair in a "S" shape, alternating the direction of the curl with each section.

Step 6: Let Your Hair Cool

Once you've curled your entire head of hair, let it cool for a few minutes. This will help to set the curls and keep them in place.

Step 7: Brush Out Your Curls

Using a wide-toothed comb or your fingers, gently brush out your curls. This will help to create a natural and relaxed look.

Step 8: Apply a Texturizing Spray

To add some texture and hold to your curls, apply a texturizing spray. This will help to keep your curls in place and prevent them from falling flat throughout the day.

Step 9: Finish with Hairspray

To keep your curls looking great all day long, finish with a light hold hairspray. This will help to set your curls and prevent them from frizzing or falling flat.

Tips for Making Deep Wave Curls

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when making deep wave curls:

  • Use a curling iron with a large barrel to create loose, relaxed curls.
  • For tighter curls, use a smaller barrel and wrap the hair tightly around the barrel.
  • Make sure to curl each section of hair in the same direction to create a uniform look.
  • Don't forget to apply a heat protectant before using any hot tools on your hair.
  • For added volume, tease your hair at the roots before curling.

FAQ About Deep Wave Curls

What kind of curling iron should I use to make deep wave curls?

You should use a curling iron with a large barrel to create loose, relaxed curls. A 1 1/4 inch or 1 1/2 inch barrel is ideal for deep wave curls.

How long will my deep wave curls last?

Your deep wave curls should last all day if you use a texturizing spray and hairspray to set them in place.

Can I make deep wave curls on short hair?

Yes, you can make deep wave curls on short hair. Just make sure to use a smaller barrel curling iron and wrap the hair tightly around the barrel.

Hair Care for Deep Wave Curls

To keep your deep wave curls looking great, make sure to take care of your hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and always use a heat protectant before using any hot tools. To maintain your curls, use a texturizing spray and hairspray to set them in place.
