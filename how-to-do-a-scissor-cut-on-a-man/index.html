How To Do A Scissor Cut On A Man A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Scissor cuts are a popular hairstyle among men. It gives a classic, clean-cut look that is perfect for any occasion. If you're looking to do a scissor cut on a man, this guide will walk you through the process step-by-step. From preparing the hair to finishing the cut, we've got you covered.

Description

A scissor cut is a popular haircut for men that involves using scissors to cut the hair to a desired length. This haircut is versatile and can be tailored to suit any face shape or hair type. Scissor cuts are popular among men who want a classic, timeless look that is easy to maintain.

How to Do a Scissor Cut on a Man

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare the hair properly. Start by washing the hair with shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry the hair until it's slightly damp. Comb the hair in the direction you want it to fall. This will make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Use a comb to create a part down the center of the head. Then, use the comb to create a part from the center part to the back of the ear on each side. Clip the hair on the top of the head out of the way for now.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair at the nape of the neck. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Be sure to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut. Work your way up the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Once you've finished cutting the back, move on to the sides. Use the same technique as before, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Be sure to blend the hair as you go, to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Now it's time to cut the top. Unclip the hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Again, be sure to blend the hair as you go, to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting the hair, it's important to check for evenness. Use a comb to comb the hair in different directions. Check for any areas that are longer or shorter than others. Trim any stray hairs as needed.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use clippers to clean up the edges around the ears and neckline. Be sure to use a guard that is appropriate for the length of hair you're cutting. This will give the haircut a clean, polished look.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the hair, it's time to style it. Use a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to give the hair a polished look. Use a comb or your fingers to style the hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing a scissor cut on a man:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure evenness.
  • Blend the hair as you go to avoid any harsh lines.
  • Use clippers to clean up the edges for a polished look.
  • Use a styling product to give the hair a polished look.

FAQ

Q: How long does a scissor cut last?

A: A scissor cut typically lasts about 4-6 weeks, depending on how fast the hair grows.

Q: Can I do a scissor cut on myself?

A: It's not recommended to do a scissor cut on yourself. It's best to go to a professional stylist to ensure a clean, even cut.

Q: What face shape is best suited for a scissor cut?

A: A scissor cut is versatile and can be tailored to suit any face shape.

Hair Care

After getting a scissor cut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. Avoid using too much heat on the hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners. Use a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to give the hair a polished look. Trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.
