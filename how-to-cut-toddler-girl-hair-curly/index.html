How To Cut Toddler Girl Hair Curly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 02, 2023 · 6 min read

Curly toddler hair can be a challenge to cut, but with the right technique and tools, it can be done at home. This guide will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut toddler girl hair curly. From preparing your tools to hair care tips, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • How to Cut Toddler Girl Hair Curly
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Toddler Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Preparation

Before you start cutting your toddler’s curly hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or detangling brush
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle with water

It’s also a good idea to have a distraction for your toddler, such as a favorite toy or video, to keep them still during the process.

How to Cut Toddler Girl Hair Curly

Follow these steps to cut curly toddler hair:

  1. Start by wetting your toddler’s hair with the spray bottle.
  2. Use the comb or detangling brush to remove any knots or tangles.
  3. Section the hair into four parts: top, back, and two sides. Use the sectioning clips to keep the hair in place.
  4. Starting with the back section, take a small amount of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends of the hair at a slight angle, making sure not to cut too much at once.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around the head.
  6. Move on to the side sections, cutting the hair at the same slight angle.
  7. Finally, cut the top section of hair, blending it into the sides and back.
  8. Check for any uneven spots and trim as needed.
  9. Once you’re satisfied with the length, use the comb or brush to style the hair as desired.
  10. Remove the sectioning clips and let your toddler enjoy their new haircut!

Tips for Cutting Curly Toddler Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your toddler’s curly hair:

  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to take small sections and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Keep the hair wet throughout the cutting process to make it easier to work with.
  • Hold the hair between your fingers rather than using a comb or brush to cut, as this will help you see the natural curl pattern and avoid cutting too much.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your toddler’s head for any missed spots.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about cutting curly toddler hair:

  • How often should I cut my toddler’s hair? It’s recommended to cut curly toddler hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.
  • What if my toddler won’t sit still? Try distracting them with a favorite toy or video, or consider cutting their hair while they’re asleep.
  • Should I cut curly hair dry or wet? It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s wet, as this makes it easier to see the natural curl pattern and avoid cutting too much.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your toddler’s curly hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain the shape and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and protect the curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage the hair and disrupt the natural curl pattern.

Cutting toddler girl hair curly doesn’t have to be a daunting task. With the right tools, technique, and care, you can give your little one a fresh new look while keeping their curls healthy and beautiful.
