Best Pomade To Slick Back Hair Get The Sleek Look You Want

Do you struggle with finding the right product to slick back your hair? Look no further than pomade. Pomade is a versatile hair product that can be used to achieve a variety of styles, but it is particularly effective for slicked back hair. In this article, we'll discuss the best pomades to use for this style and provide tips on how to achieve the perfect look.

Description

Pomade is a hair product that provides a strong hold and a shiny finish. It can be used to achieve a variety of styles, including slicked back hair. The best pomades for this style are those that provide a strong hold without making the hair look greasy or weighed down. These pomades typically have a water-based formula, which makes them easier to wash out than oil-based pomades.

How to Slick Back Hair

Slicked back hair is a classic style that has been popular for decades. To achieve this look, you'll need to have hair that is at least 2-3 inches long on top. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup from your hair. Once you've washed your hair, towel dry it to remove any excess moisture.

Step 2: Apply pomade

Take a small amount of pomade (about the size of a dime) and rub it between your palms to warm it up. Then, apply it evenly to your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward. Be sure to distribute the pomade evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Comb your hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure to comb your hair all the way to the back of your head to create a smooth, even look. You can also use your fingers to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 4: Finish the look

Once you've combed your hair back, you can either leave it as is or style it further. You can add a side part or a quiff to create a more modern look, or you can leave it slicked back for a classic look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to slicking back your hair:

Step 1: Choose the right pomade

As mentioned earlier, the best pomades for slicked back hair are those that provide a strong hold without making your hair look greasy. Look for pomades with a water-based formula, as these are easier to wash out and won't leave any residue in your hair.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair to remove any excess moisture, but don't blow dry it completely. You want some moisture in your hair to help the pomade spread evenly.

Step 3: Apply the pomade

Take a small amount of pomade (about the size of a dime) and rub it between your palms to warm it up. Then, apply it evenly to your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward. Use your fingers to distribute the pomade evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Comb your hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure to comb all the way to the back of your head to create a smooth, even look. You can also use your fingers to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 5: Finish the look

Once you've combed your hair back, you can either leave it as is or style it further. You can add a side part or a quiff to create a more modern look, or you can leave it slicked back for a classic look.

Tips for Slicking Back Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect slicked back look:

  • Start with clean, damp hair to help the pomade spread more evenly.
  • Use a comb with fine teeth to create a smooth, even look.
  • Don't use too much pomade, as this can make your hair look greasy.
  • If you have thick or curly hair, use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to help set the style.
  • Experiment with different types of pomade to find the one that works best for your hair type.

FAQs

What is the best pomade for slicked back hair?

There are many great pomades on the market that can be used for slicked back hair. Some of the best include:

  • Imperial Barber Classic Pomade
  • Baxter of California Clay Pomade
  • Uppercut Deluxe Pomade
  • Reuzel Blue Pomade

How often should I wash my hair if I use pomade?

You should wash your hair at least once a week if you use pomade regularly. This will help to remove any buildup from the product and keep your hair healthy.

Can I use pomade on short hair?

Yes, you can use pomade on short hair to create a variety of styles. However, pomade works best on hair that is at least 2-3 inches long on top.

Hair Care for Slicked Back Hair

If you regularly slick back your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much pomade, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to help set the style.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep it looking neat.

Conclusion

Slicked back hair is a classic style that never goes out of fashion. With the right pomade and a little practice, you can achieve the perfect look. Remember to choose a pomade that provides a strong hold without making your hair look greasy, and to use a fine-toothed comb to create a smooth, even look. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock the slicked back look with confidence.
