How To Layer Curly Hair Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that finding the right haircut can be a challenge. One of the most popular styles for curly hair is a layered cut, which can help to add shape, definition, and movement to your curls. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to get a layered curly hair cut, including tips and FAQs to help you achieve your dream hairstyle.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Layer Curly Hair Cut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Layer Curly Hair Cut

The first step to getting a layered curly hair cut is to find a hairstylist who specializes in curly hair. Curly hair has unique needs and requires different techniques than straight hair, so it's important to find someone who knows how to work with your hair type.

When you meet with your hairstylist, be sure to bring pictures of the type of layered cut you want. This will help your hairstylist understand your vision and make suggestions based on your hair texture and face shape. You should also discuss how much length you want to take off and whether you want any additional services, such as color or highlights.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting a layered curly hair cut:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your curls.
  2. Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to avoid frizz.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls.
  4. Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back.
  5. Starting at the back of your head, your hairstylist will cut your hair section by section, using a technique called "twist cutting." This involves twisting each section of hair and cutting the ends at an angle to create layers.
  6. After the back is complete, your hairstylist will move on to the sides and then the top, using the same technique.
  7. Your hairstylist may also use thinning shears to remove bulk and create more movement in your curls.
  8. Once your hair is cut, your hairstylist will style it using a diffuser attachment on a blow dryer or by air-drying it.
  9. Your hairstylist may also recommend products to help maintain your new haircut, such as a curl refresher or hair oil.

Tips for Layering Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your layered curly hair cut:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, which can damage your curls.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase or use a satin hair bonnet to protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing your curls, which can lead to frizz and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your curls looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering curly hair:

How often should I get my curly hair cut?

It depends on the length of your hair and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 8-12 weeks to keep your curls looking healthy.

Is it okay to cut curly hair dry?

Yes, cutting curly hair dry can help your hairstylist see the shape and movement of your curls and create a more customized haircut.

Can I still straighten my curly hair after getting a layered cut?

Yes, you can still straighten your curly hair after getting a layered cut. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your curls.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

To keep your curly hair looking its best, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of moisture. Use lukewarm or cool water instead.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using towels to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to blot your hair dry.
  • Avoid brushing your curls, which can lead to frizz and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers instead.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase or use a satin hair bonnet to protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Get regular trims to keep your curls looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and flattering layered curly hair cut that showcases your natural curls. Remember to work with a hairstylist who understands your hair type and follow a regular hair care routine to keep your curls looking their best.
