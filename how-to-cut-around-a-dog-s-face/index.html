How To Cut Around A Dog s Face A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia May 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a dog owner, you know that grooming is an important part of keeping your furry friend healthy and happy. However, cutting around your dog's face can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure where to start. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut around a dog's face step-by-step, as well as provide tips for keeping your pup's hair looking great. Let's get started!

How to Cut Around a Dog's Face

Before you begin cutting your dog's hair, it's important to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of scissors specifically designed for dog grooming, as well as a comb and some clippers if your dog has longer hair. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Brush Your Dog's Hair

Start by brushing your dog's hair to remove any tangles or mats. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your dog's hair looks smooth and even. Use a comb to gently work through any knots, being careful not to tug on your dog's skin.

Step 2: Trim the Hair Around Your Dog's Eyes

Use your scissors to carefully trim the hair around your dog's eyes. Be sure to use blunt-tipped scissors and take your time to avoid accidentally poking your dog's eyes. Start by trimming a small amount of hair at a time, and stop frequently to check your work and make sure you're not cutting too much off.

Step 3: Cut the Hair Around Your Dog's Mouth

Next, use your scissors to trim the hair around your dog's mouth. This will help prevent food and water from getting caught in your dog's fur, and keep your pup looking neat and tidy. Again, start by trimming a small amount of hair at a time and check your work frequently to avoid cutting too much off.

Step 4: Trim Your Dog's Ears

Use your scissors to trim the hair around your dog's ears, being careful not to cut into the ear itself. This will help prevent ear infections and keep your dog's ears clean and healthy.

Step 5: Finish with Clippers

If your dog has longer hair, you may need to use clippers to finish the job. Use a comb to separate the hair and then run the clippers over the hair to trim it to the desired length. Be sure to use a guard to avoid accidentally cutting your dog's skin.

Tips for Cutting Around Your Dog's Face

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting around your dog's face:

  • Take your time and be patient. It's better to make small cuts and check your work frequently than to cut too much off at once.
  • Use the right tools. Make sure you have scissors specifically designed for dog grooming, as well as a comb and clippers if necessary.
  • Be careful around your dog's eyes and ears. These areas are sensitive and require extra care.
  • Offer treats and praise to keep your dog calm and happy during the grooming process.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my dog's hair with regular scissors?

A: No, regular scissors are not designed for dog grooming and may be too sharp or pointy, which can be dangerous for your dog.

Q: How often should I cut around my dog's face?

A: This depends on your dog's breed and hair type. Some dogs may need to be groomed every 4-6 weeks, while others can go longer between grooming sessions.

Q: What if I accidentally cut my dog's skin?

A: If you accidentally cut your dog's skin, apply pressure to the area with a clean cloth and contact your vet if the bleeding doesn't stop or the cut is deep.

Hair Care for Your Dog

Cutting around your dog's face is just one part of keeping your pup's hair healthy and looking great. Here are a few other tips for caring for your dog's hair:

  • Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Bathe your dog as needed, but be careful not to over-bathe, as this can dry out your dog's skin.
  • Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from getting too long.
  • Feed your dog a healthy diet to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and techniques, you can keep your dog looking and feeling their best. Happy grooming!
