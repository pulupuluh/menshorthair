How To Get Your Hair To Lay Back Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Jul 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're struggling with hair that won't stay put, you're not alone. Many people find it challenging to get their hair to lay back and stay in place. Fortunately, there are some simple tips and tricks you can use to achieve this look. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about getting your hair to lay back, including step-by-step instructions, common questions, and hair care tips.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Choose the Right Product
  • Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair
  • Step 3: Apply Product to Your Hair
  • Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair
  • Step 5: Style Your Hair
  • Tips for Getting Your Hair to Lay Back
  • FAQs About Getting Your Hair to Lay Back
  • Hair Care Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy

Step 1: Choose the Right Product

The first step in getting your hair to lay back is to choose the right product. Look for a styling product that provides hold without making your hair stiff or crunchy. Pomades, waxes, and gels can all work well, depending on your hair type and texture.

If you have thin or fine hair, choose a lightweight product that won't weigh your hair down. For thicker or coarser hair, a heavier product may be necessary to provide the hold you need. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for you.

Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before applying any styling product, it's important to start with clean, conditioned hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse your hair and remove any buildup.

If you have particularly dry or damaged hair, consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture and nourishment.

Step 3: Apply Product to Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and conditioned, it's time to apply your styling product. Start with a small amount and work it through your hair, focusing on the roots and working your way down to the tips.

Be careful not to use too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy or weighed down. If you accidentally use too much, simply wash your hair and start over.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

To get your hair to lay back, you'll need to use a blow dryer. Start by using your fingers to comb your hair back, then use a round brush to smooth your hair and create volume.

Use the blow dryer on a low setting to avoid damaging your hair, and aim it at the roots to create lift and volume. Once your hair is dry, use a cool setting to set the style in place.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a comb or your fingers to style your hair back. You can create a sleek, polished look by using a comb to smooth your hair, or a more relaxed, tousled look by using your fingers to create texture.

Remember to use a small amount of product to keep your hair in place throughout the day.

Tips for Getting Your Hair to Lay Back

Here are a few additional tips to help you get the perfect laid-back look:

  • Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and texture.
  • Use a round brush to create volume and lift when blow drying your hair.
  • Use a cool setting on your blow dryer to set your style in place.
  • Use a small amount of product to avoid weighing your hair down.
  • Don't be afraid to use your fingers to create texture and a more relaxed look.

FAQs About Getting Your Hair to Lay Back

Q: How can I get my hair to stay in place all day?

A: Use a styling product with a strong hold, and apply it evenly throughout your hair. Use a blow dryer to set your style in place, and touch up throughout the day with a small amount of product.

Q: Can I get a laid-back look with short hair?

A: Absolutely! Short hair can be styled to create a sleek, polished look or a more relaxed, tousled look. Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find the look that works best for you.

Q: Is it okay to blow dry my hair every day?

A: While blow drying can be damaging to your hair over time, it's generally okay to blow dry your hair every day as long as you use a low heat setting and protect your hair with a heat protectant spray.

Hair Care Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy

To keep your hair looking and feeling healthy, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips to help you keep your hair in great condition:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture and nourishment to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day, as this can be damaging over time.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

With a little bit of effort and the right products, you can get your hair to lay back and stay in place all day. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it looking and feeling healthy.
