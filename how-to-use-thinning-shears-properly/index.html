How To Use Thinning Shears Properly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for giving your hair a textured, layered look. They can also help to remove bulk and make your hair easier to manage. However, using thinning shears improperly can result in a disaster. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears properly so that you can achieve the look you desire without damaging your hair.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth-like blades that are used to remove bulk from hair. They are designed to thin out thick hair or to create a textured, layered look. Thinning shears come in different sizes and shapes, and they can be used on all types and lengths of hair. However, it is essential to use them properly to avoid damaging your hair.

How to Use Thinning Shears Properly

Step 1: Choose the Right Pair of Thinning Shears

Thinning shears come in different sizes and shapes. Choose the right pair of thinning shears that is suitable for your hair type and length.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before using thinning shears. Wet hair can be difficult to cut, and it can also cause the blades of the thinning shears to rust. Therefore, it is essential to dry your hair thoroughly before using thinning shears.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier for you to work with your hair and ensure that you do not miss any sections.

Step 4: Hold the Thinning Shears Properly

Hold the thinning shears properly. Hold them with your thumb and middle finger, and keep your index finger on the blade of the shears. This will enable you to control the shears better and prevent them from slipping.

Step 5: Use the Thinning Shears on the Ends of Your Hair

Use the thinning shears on the ends of your hair. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up. Use the shears to remove small sections of hair at a time. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair by using the thinning shears in a back-and-forth motion. This will create a textured, layered look.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

Check your progress frequently in a mirror. This will enable you to see if you have missed any sections or if you have cut too much hair.

Step 8: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process on all sections of your hair until you achieve the desired look.

Tips

  • Use sharp thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Do not use thinning shears on wet hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Blend the hair to create a natural look.
  • Practice on a small section of hair before using thinning shears on your entire head.

FAQ

Can thinning shears damage your hair?

Yes, they can. Using thinning shears improperly can result in split ends, breakage, and damage to your hair.

What is the difference between thinning shears and regular scissors?

Thinning shears have teeth-like blades that are designed to remove bulk from hair, while regular scissors have straight blades that are designed to cut hair evenly.

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Yes, they can. Thinning shears can be used on all types and lengths of hair.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears, it is essential to take care of your hair properly. Use a hair conditioner to keep your hair moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to use thinning shears properly, you can achieve the look you desire without damaging your hair. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to get the best results.
