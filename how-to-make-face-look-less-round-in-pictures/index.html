How To Make Your Face Look Less Round In Pictures

Written by Luffy Sep 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Do you ever feel self-conscious about your face looking too round in pictures? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, but luckily there are some easy tips and tricks you can use to make your face appear slimmer and more defined in photos. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to make your face look less round in pictures.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How to Make Your Face Look Less Round in Pictures
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ

Hair Care

Believe it or not, your hairstyle can actually have a big impact on how your face looks in pictures. If you have a round face, try to avoid hairstyles that add too much volume to the top of your head, as this can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, opt for styles that have more volume on the sides, such as a layered bob or a long, side-swept fringe.

Additionally, consider getting highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair. This will help draw attention away from your face and create the illusion of a more angular jawline.

How to Make Your Face Look Less Round in Pictures

Now, let's dive into some specific tips and tricks for making your face look less round in pictures.

Use the Right Camera Angle

The angle of the camera can have a huge impact on the way your face looks in pictures. To make your face appear slimmer and more defined, try tilting your head slightly to one side or the other. This will create shadows on your face that can help to contour your features. Additionally, try holding the camera slightly above eye level, as this can also help to elongate your face.

Pose Your Body Strategically

Your body position can also play a role in how your face looks in pictures. To make your face appear less round, try standing at an angle to the camera instead of facing it straight on. This will create a more flattering silhouette and help to elongate your face. Additionally, try crossing your legs at the ankle instead of the knee, as this can also help to create a more slender appearance.

Use Makeup to Contour Your Face

Makeup can be a powerful tool for creating the illusion of a slimmer face. Use a matte bronzer to contour your cheekbones, temples, and jawline. This will help to create shadows on your face that can make it appear more defined. Additionally, try using a highlighter on the high points of your face, such as your cheekbones and the bridge of your nose, to create the illusion of depth and dimension.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to making your face look less round in pictures:

  1. Choose a flattering hairstyle that adds volume to the sides of your head.
  2. Stand at an angle to the camera instead of facing it straight on.
  3. Tilt your head slightly to one side or the other to create shadows on your face.
  4. Hold the camera slightly above eye level to elongate your face.
  5. Use a matte bronzer to contour your cheekbones, temples, and jawline.
  6. Use a highlighter on the high points of your face to create depth and dimension.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for making your face look less round in pictures:

  • Wear clothing with vertical stripes to elongate your body.
  • Avoid wearing clothing with horizontal stripes or bold patterns, as these can make your body appear wider.
  • Stand up straight and pull your shoulders back to create a more confident posture.
  • Practice your poses in front of a mirror to find your best angles.
  • Experiment with different lighting conditions to find the most flattering setup.

FAQ

Q: Can I use photo editing software to make my face look less round?

A: While it's possible to use photo editing software to slim down your face, we recommend using natural methods first. This will help you look and feel more confident in your own skin.

Q: Is there a certain type of makeup that works best for contouring?

A: We recommend using a matte bronzer for contouring, as this will create the most natural-looking shadows on your face.

Q: How do I find my best angles for pictures?

A: Practice posing in front of a mirror to find your best angles. Experiment with tilting your head, changing the position of your arms, and standing at different angles to the camera.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create the illusion of a slimmer, more defined face in pictures. Remember to embrace your natural beauty and have fun with your photos!
