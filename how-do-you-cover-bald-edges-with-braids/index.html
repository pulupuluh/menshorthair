How To Cover Bald Edges With Braids Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Sep 02, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly worrying about your bald edges when getting braids? Look no further, as we have the solution for you. In this article, we’ll walk you through step-by-step on how to cover bald edges with braids. From tips to hair care, we’ve got you covered. Let’s get started!

Description

Braids are a popular protective hairstyle for many, but they can also cause damage to your edges if they are too tight or if you’re not careful. Bald edges are a common issue that many women face, but don’t let that discourage you from getting braids. With the right techniques and tips, you can cover up those bald edges and still enjoy the benefits of braids.

How to Cover Bald Edges with Braids

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to cover bald edges with braids:

Step 1: Choose the Right Hair Extensions

The first step in covering bald edges with braids is to choose the right type of hair extensions. Opt for extensions that are lightweight and won’t put too much tension on your scalp. Avoid using heavy extensions, as they can cause breakage and damage to your already fragile edges.

Step 2: Moisturize Your Edges

Before getting braids, it’s important to moisturize your edges to prevent breakage and damage. Apply a moisturizing oil or leave-in conditioner to your edges and massage it in gently. This will help to promote healthy hair growth and protect your edges from any potential damage.

Step 3: Use a Protective Gel

Using a protective gel around your edges can help to prevent any further damage while getting your braids done. Apply a small amount of gel around your edges and along your hairline to create a barrier between your hair and the braids.

Step 4: Braid Your Hair

Now it’s time to start braiding your hair. Make sure to start with small sections and avoid pulling too tightly on your edges. Leave a small section of hair out at the front to cover your edges.

Step 5: Cover Your Edges

Once you’ve finished braiding your hair, it’s time to cover your edges. Take the small section of hair you left out at the front and braid it towards the back of your head. Use bobby pins to secure the braid in place and cover your edges.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you cover bald edges with braids:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your edges
  • Take breaks from braids to give your edges a chance to rest and recover
  • Use a silk or satin scarf to wrap your hair at night to prevent breakage
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your edges, as they can cause damage

FAQ

Can I still get braids if I have bald edges?

Yes, you can still get braids if you have bald edges. However, it’s important to take the necessary precautions to protect your edges and promote healthy hair growth.

Will covering my edges with braids make my hair grow back?

Covering your edges with braids can help to protect them and promote healthy hair growth. However, it’s important to also take care of your hair by moisturizing and avoiding tight hairstyles.

Hair Care

When it comes to covering bald edges with braids, hair care is essential. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage and damage
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and nutrients to promote healthy hair growth
  • Take breaks from braids to give your hair a chance to rest and recover

With these tips and tricks, you can cover your bald edges with braids and still maintain healthy hair. Remember to take care of your hair and give it the love and attention it deserves. Happy braiding!
