How To Repair Completely Damaged Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are struggling with completely damaged hair, don't worry, you are not alone. Many people experience hair damage due to various reasons such as excessive heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental stressors. However, the good news is that it is possible to repair completely damaged hair with the right approach and regimen. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to repair completely damaged hair and restore its health and vitality.

Description

Damaged hair can be a nightmare, especially when it becomes completely damaged. Completely damaged hair is hair that has been severely weakened, and its natural state has been compromised. This type of hair is prone to breakage, split ends, and dullness. Completely damaged hair requires extra care and attention to restore its health and vitality.

How to Repair Completely Damaged Hair

Step 1: Identify the Cause of Damage

The first step in repairing completely damaged hair is to identify the cause of damage. This will help you determine the best approach to repair your hair. Common causes of hair damage include excessive heat styling, chemical treatments, environmental stressors, and poor hair care practices.

Step 2: Trim Your Hair

Trimming your hair is an essential step in repairing completely damaged hair. This will help remove split ends and prevent further damage. We recommend trimming your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Step 3: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning is a crucial step in repairing completely damaged hair. It helps to nourish and moisturize your hair, making it less prone to breakage and split ends. We recommend using a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Look for a deep conditioner that contains natural ingredients such as avocado oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Step 4: Use Protein Treatments

Protein treatments help to strengthen your hair and restore its elasticity. They are particularly beneficial for completely damaged hair. We recommend using a protein treatment once every four to six weeks. Look for a protein treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin, collagen, or silk amino acids.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage completely damaged hair. We recommend avoiding heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use them on the lowest heat setting and apply a heat protectant spray beforehand.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help repair completely damaged hair:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals such as bleach and hair dyes.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and updos.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.

FAQ

Can completely damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, completely damaged hair can be repaired with the right approach and regimen.

How long does it take to repair completely damaged hair?

The time it takes to repair completely damaged hair depends on the severity of the damage. It can take anywhere from six months to a year to completely repair severely damaged hair.

What are some natural remedies for completely damaged hair?

Some natural remedies for completely damaged hair include using coconut oil, olive oil, and avocado oil as a hair mask. These oils help to nourish and moisturize your hair, making it less prone to breakage and split ends.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner after shampooing.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as bleach and hair dyes.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and updos.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

By following these tips and the steps outlined in this article, you can repair completely damaged hair and restore its health and vitality. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care regimen, and you will see results over time.
