Why Does My Hair Flick Out At The Ends

Written by Oliver Jul 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're wondering why your hair seems to have a mind of its own and flicks out at the ends, you're not alone. This is a common concern for many people, and it can be frustrating to deal with. Fortunately, there are several reasons why your hair may be doing this, and there are also some simple solutions to help you tame those flyaway ends.

Description

First, it's important to understand what causes your hair to flick out at the ends. This can happen for a variety of reasons, including:

  • Split ends
  • Heat damage
  • Humidity
  • Using the wrong products

Each of these factors can contribute to your hair flicking out at the ends, and it's important to address them in order to achieve the sleek, smooth style you're looking for.

How to Fix Flicky Hair Ends

Now that you know what may be causing your hair to flick out, let's talk about how to fix it. Here are some tips to help you get your hair under control:

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Split ends are one of the most common causes of flicky hair ends. To combat this, make sure you're getting regular trims every 6-8 weeks. This will help to remove any split ends and keep your hair healthy.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat damage can also contribute to flyaway ends. Try to avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners as much as possible, and opt for heatless styles instead.

Use the Right Products

The products you use on your hair can make a big difference in how it behaves. Look for products that are designed to smooth and tame flyaway ends, like anti-frizz serums and smoothing creams.

Try a Different Hairstyle

If all else fails, it may be time to try a different hairstyle that's better suited to your hair type. Talk to your stylist about options that will help to control those pesky flyaways.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Flicky Hair Ends

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get your hair under control:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type. This will help to keep your hair healthy and reduce the likelihood of split ends.

Step 2: Apply a Smoothing Product

Next, apply a smoothing product to your hair to help tame flyaway ends. This could be an anti-frizz serum, a smoothing cream, or a leave-in conditioner.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer to dry your hair, using a round brush to help smooth the ends. Make sure to point the nozzle of the dryer downwards to help reduce flyaways.

Step 4: Use a Flat Iron

If you still have flyaway ends after blow drying, use a flat iron to smooth them out. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray first to avoid heat damage.

Tips for Preventing Flicky Hair Ends

Prevention is key when it comes to flyaway ends. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair looking smooth and sleek:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair
  • Wear a hat or scarf on humid days to prevent frizz
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel to dry it

FAQs

Q: Can flicky hair ends be fixed?

A: Yes, flicky hair ends can be fixed. Try trimming your hair regularly, avoiding heat styling tools, and using the right products to help tame flyaways.

Q: What causes flicky hair ends?

A: Flicky hair ends can be caused by split ends, heat damage, humidity, and using the wrong products on your hair.

Q: How can I prevent flicky hair ends?

A: To prevent flicky hair ends, avoid over-washing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb, wear a hat or scarf on humid days, and avoid rubbing your hair with a towel to dry it.

Hair Care for Flicky Hair Ends

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair in order to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking smooth, sleek, and flyaway-free!
