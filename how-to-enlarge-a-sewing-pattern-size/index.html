How To Enlarge A Sewing Pattern Size In 2023

Written by Luffy Jun 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are looking to create a piece of clothing using a sewing pattern and need to enlarge the size, you may feel overwhelmed at first. However, with a few simple steps and tips, you can easily increase the size of your pattern to fit your measurements. In this blog post, we will guide you through how to enlarge a sewing pattern size in relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Measure Yourself
  • Step 2: Determine the Amount of Enlargement
  • Step 3: Enlarge the Pattern
  • Tips for Enlarging a Sewing Pattern Size
  • FAQs About Enlarging a Sewing Pattern Size
  • Hair Care While Sewing

Step 1: Measure Yourself

The first step in enlarging a sewing pattern size is to measure yourself. You will need to take accurate measurements of your bust, waist, and hips. You can use a measuring tape to do this. Make sure to measure around the fullest part of your bust, your natural waistline, and the widest part of your hips. Write down your measurements so you can refer to them later.

Step 2: Determine the Amount of Enlargement

Once you have your measurements, you will need to determine how much you need to enlarge the pattern. Look at the size chart on the pattern and find the size that is closest to your measurements. If your measurements fall between two sizes, choose the larger size. Subtract your measurements from the measurements on the pattern to determine how much you need to enlarge the pattern.

Step 3: Enlarge the Pattern

Now that you know how much you need to enlarge the pattern, it's time to make the adjustments. You can use a photocopier or scanner to enlarge the pattern. If you don't have access to a photocopier or scanner, you can use a ruler and pencil to redraw the pattern in the larger size. Make sure to add the same amount of extra space to each piece of the pattern.

Once you have enlarged the pattern, cut out the pieces and follow the instructions on the pattern to sew your garment together. You may need to make some additional adjustments to the fit as you go along, so try on the garment frequently as you sew.

Tips for Enlarging a Sewing Pattern Size

  • Always make a muslin or test garment before sewing the final piece to ensure a good fit.
  • Use a curved ruler to adjust the armhole and neckline of the pattern.
  • When enlarging a pattern, make sure to keep the proportions of the pattern pieces the same.
  • Don't be afraid to seek help from a professional seamstress or tailor if you are unsure about the process.

FAQs About Enlarging a Sewing Pattern Size

Q: Can I enlarge a pattern by just adding extra fabric?

A: No, adding extra fabric to a pattern will not create a good fit. You need to adjust the pattern pieces to ensure a good fit.

Q: Can I use the same method to reduce the size of a pattern?

A: Yes, you can use the same method to reduce the size of a pattern. Measure yourself and determine how much you need to reduce the pattern. Then, use a photocopier or scanner to reduce the pattern or redraw it in the smaller size.

Q: What should I do if the pattern doesn't include my size?

A: If the pattern doesn't include your size, you may need to make some additional adjustments to the pattern. You can use the same method to enlarge the pattern and make further adjustments as needed.

Hair Care While Sewing

While sewing, it's important to take care of your hair. You can wear a headscarf or bandana to keep your hair out of your face while you work. If you have long hair, you may want to tie it back to prevent it from getting caught in the sewing machine or tangled in the fabric. You can also wear a hairnet to keep your hair contained.

We hope this guide on how to enlarge a sewing pattern size in 2023 has been helpful to you. With these simple steps and tips, you can easily adjust a pattern to fit your measurements and create a piece of clothing that fits perfectly. Happy sewing!
