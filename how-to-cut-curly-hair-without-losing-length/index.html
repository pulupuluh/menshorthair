How To Cut Curly Hair Without Losing Length

Written by Daniel Jul 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of losing length every time you cut your curly hair? Are you looking for a way to maintain your curls while still getting a fresh new look? Look no further! In this article, we will show you how to cut curly hair without losing length.

Description

Cutting curly hair can be tricky. Curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, which can make it difficult to maintain length. Additionally, cutting curly hair too short can cause it to lose its shape and definition. That's why it's important to know how to cut curly hair without losing length.

How to Cut Curly Hair Without Losing Length

Before you start cutting your curly hair, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a wide-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here's how to cut curly hair without losing length:

Step 1: Start with Dry Hair

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be deceiving. When hair is wet, it tends to look longer than it actually is. Cutting dry hair will give you a better idea of how much length you're actually cutting.

Step 2: Divide Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and maintain your length. Start with the bottom section and work your way up.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Using your scissors, trim the ends of your hair. Start by cutting off a small amount, about 1/4 inch or less. You can always trim more if needed, but it's best to start small. Make sure to cut your hair straight across, rather than at an angle, to maintain your length.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After you've trimmed the ends of your hair, check for evenness. Use your wide-tooth comb to comb through your hair and make sure both sides are even. If you notice any unevenness, make small adjustments until your hair is even.

Step 5: Style as Usual

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it as usual. Your curls should maintain their shape and definition, and you should still have your desired length.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you cut curly hair without losing length:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut too much at once. Start small and make adjustments as needed.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting curly hair can take some time, so don't rush it.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

We recommend cutting curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be deceiving. When hair is wet, it tends to look longer than it actually is. Cutting dry hair will give you a better idea of how much length you're actually cutting.

How much should I trim off my hair?

Start by trimming off a small amount, about 1/4 inch or less. You can always trim more if needed, but it's best to start small. Make sure to cut your hair straight across, rather than at an angle, to maintain your length.

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, if you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

Hair Care

To keep your curly hair healthy and strong, make sure to follow a proper hair care routine. This includes using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, deep conditioning your hair regularly, and using a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.

Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, you can maintain your length and still get a fresh new look. Follow these tips and tricks to cut your curly hair without losing length, and enjoy your beautiful, healthy curls!
