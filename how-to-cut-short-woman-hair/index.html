How To Cut Short Woman Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 08, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you considering cutting your short woman hair but don't know where to start? Look no further! In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair at home. From necessary tools to helpful tips, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Blending Your Hair
  6. Helpful Tips
  7. FAQs
  8. Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before we begin, make sure you have the necessary tools to cut your hair. Here's what you'll need:

  • Professional hair cutting scissors
  • Clippers with different guard sizes (optional)
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Make sure your tools are clean and sharp before you start cutting. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's free of any tangles or knots. It's easier to cut your hair when it's dry, so avoid cutting it when it's wet.

Next, choose the length you want to cut your hair. It's important to have a clear idea of the length before you start cutting. If you're not sure, it's better to start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is essential for an even cut. Start by parting your hair down the middle and bringing it forward over your shoulders. Clip the front sections away from the back sections.

Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process for each section, gradually moving towards the front of your head.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you've cut the back sections, unclip the front sections and comb them straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the same length as the back sections.

If you want to add layers or texture to your hair, use the point-cutting technique. Hold your scissors vertically and make small snips into the ends of your hair. This creates a soft, textured look.

Blending Your Hair

To blend your hair, take small sections and comb them straight up. Use your scissors to cut the ends at a slight angle, creating a blended effect.

If you have thicker hair, you may want to use clippers to blend the hair around your ears and neckline. Use a guard size that matches your desired length and start at the bottom, moving upwards.

Helpful Tips

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • If you're not confident cutting your hair, ask a friend or family member for help.

FAQs

How often should I cut my short hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, you should try to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and avoid split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting?

Don't panic! It's easy to fix small mistakes. If you accidentally cut too much, try using the point-cutting technique to add texture and blend the hair. If you're still not happy, visit a professional stylist for a correction.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it's essential to use professional hair cutting scissors. Regular scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, take care of it with regular conditioning treatments and avoid using heat styling tools too often. This will help keep your hair healthy and looking great.

Congratulations, you've successfully cut your short woman hair! With practice and patience, you can achieve salon-quality results at home.
