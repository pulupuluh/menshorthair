How To Thin A Pixie Cut Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your thick and unruly pixie cut? Don't worry; thinning it out can make all the difference. Thinning a pixie cut can give it a more manageable and stylish look. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to thin a pixie cut, step-by-step.

Why Thin a Pixie Cut?

A pixie cut can be a great short hairstyle. However, it can also be a challenge to style and manage, especially if you have thick hair. Thinning out your pixie cut can make it easier to manage and style. It can also give your hair more texture and movement.

How to Thin a Pixie Cut

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start thinning your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the areas that need thinning. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will help you focus on one section at a time and ensure that you don't thin out too much hair in one go.

Step 3: Thin Out Your Hair

Using thinning shears, start thinning out your hair section by section. Begin at the ends of your hair and work your way up. Be careful not to thin out too much hair. It's better to thin out small sections at a time than to thin out too much hair at once.

Make sure to only thin out the middle and ends of your hair. Avoid thinning out the roots, as this can make your hair look thin and wispy.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Once you've thinned out all the sections of your hair, blend them together. Use a comb and your fingers to blend the sections together, making sure that there are no visible lines or gaps.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Now that you've thinned out your hair, it's time to style it. Use a hair dryer and a round brush to style your hair. You can also use styling products to add texture and volume to your hair.

Tips for Thinning a Pixie Cut

  • Start with small sections of hair
  • Don't thin out too much hair at once
  • Avoid thinning out the roots
  • Blend the sections of hair together
  • Use a hair dryer and a round brush to style your hair
  • Use styling products to add texture and volume to your hair

FAQ About Thinning a Pixie Cut

How often should I thin out my pixie cut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how thick it is. On average, you should thin out your pixie cut every 6-8 weeks.

Can I thin out my pixie cut at home?

Yes, you can thin out your pixie cut at home. However, it's best to go to a professional stylist if you're unsure about how to do it.

Will thinning out my pixie cut damage my hair?

No, thinning out your pixie cut won't damage your hair. However, if you thin out too much hair, it can make your hair look thin and wispy.

Hair Care for Thinned Pixie Cut

After thinning out your pixie cut, it's essential to take good care of your hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and style. You can also use hair masks and oils to nourish and strengthen your hair.

Conclusion

Thinning out your pixie cut can give it a more manageable and stylish look. Follow our step-by-step guide to thin your pixie cut at home. Don't forget to take good care of your hair after thinning it out!
