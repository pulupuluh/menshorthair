How to Train Your Hair to Slick Back

Slick back hairstyles have become increasingly popular in recent years. This classic look is versatile and can be worn in both formal and casual settings. However, achieving the perfect slick back can be tricky, especially if you have stubborn hair. Here are some steps you can take to train your hair to slick back:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step in training your hair to slick back is to start with clean hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair. This will help your hair to lay flat and make it easier to style.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protection

Before using any heat styling tools, it is essential to apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will prevent damage and breakage, which can make it harder to achieve a sleek look. Apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, paying special attention to the ends.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer is essential when training your hair to slick back. Start by blow-drying your hair in the opposite direction of where you want it to go. This will help to create volume and lift at the roots. Once your hair is mostly dry, switch to a round brush and blow-dry your hair in the direction you want it to go.

Step 4: Apply Styling Product

Once your hair is dry, it is time to apply styling product. Use a pomade or hair gel to slick back your hair. Start at the crown of your head and work the product through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly. Use a comb to smooth your hair back, starting at the hairline and working your way back.

Step 5: Style and Set

Once your hair is slicked back, use a hair dryer to set the style. This will help to keep your hair in place and prevent it from falling flat. Use a cool setting on your hair dryer to prevent heat damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to train your hair to slick back:

  1. Start with clean hair
  2. Apply heat protection
  3. Blow dry your hair
  4. Apply styling product
  5. Style and set

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect slick back:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup and residue from your hair
  • Apply a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools
  • Blow dry your hair in the opposite direction of where you want it to go to create volume and lift at the roots
  • Use a pomade or hair gel to slick back your hair
  • Use a comb to smooth your hair back, starting at the hairline and working your way back
  • Use a hair dryer to set the style

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I slick back my hair if I have curly hair?

Yes! Slick back hairstyles can be achieved with any hair type. However, if you have curly hair, you may need to use additional styling products to achieve a sleek look.

How often should I train my hair to slick back?

To maintain a slicked back hairstyle, you will need to train your hair on a regular basis. This could be daily or every other day, depending on your hair type and how quickly it reverts back to its natural state.

Can I use hairspray to set my hair?

While hairspray can be used to set your hair, it is not recommended for slick back hairstyles. Hairspray can make your hair stiff and crunchy, which can ruin the sleek look you are trying to achieve.

Hair Care Tips

Maintaining healthy hair is essential when training your hair to slick back. Here are some hair care tips to keep your locks looking their best:

  • Use a gentle shampoo that is free from sulfates and parabens
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to hydrate your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as bleach or relaxers

By following these tips, you can maintain a healthy mane and achieve the perfect slick back hairstyle.

Training your hair to slick back takes time and patience, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a sleek, sophisticated look. Remember to start with clean hair, apply heat protection, blow dry your hair, apply styling product, and style and set your hair. With these steps and tips, you'll be rocking a slick back hairstyle in no time!
