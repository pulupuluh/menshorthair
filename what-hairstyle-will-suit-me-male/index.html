What Hairstyle Will Suit Me Male? A Guide for Men

Are you a man looking for a new hairstyle but don't know where to start? With so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for you. In this guide, we'll break down what hairstyles will suit you based on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

Table of Contents:

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Understanding Your Hair Texture
  • Matching Your Personal Style
  • Maintaining Your New Hairstyle
  • FAQ

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a hairstyle that suits you is to determine your face shape. The most common face shapes for men are round, square, oval, and heart-shaped. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and examine the outline of your face. Is it widest at the forehead or jawline?

If your face is round, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that adds length and height to your face. A short back and sides with volume on top will work well. If your face is square, a style with longer hair on top and shorter sides will soften your features. Oval faces can pull off almost any hairstyle, but a classic short back and sides is always a safe bet. Heart-shaped faces look great with a longer, layered hairstyle that frames the face.

Understanding Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture is another important factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle. If you have thick, coarse hair, you'll want to avoid styles that require a lot of maintenance or product. A short, textured cut will work well. If you have fine hair, a longer hairstyle with layers will add volume and dimension.

Curly hair can be difficult to manage, but a short, textured cut with some length on top will allow you to embrace your natural curl. Straight hair can handle almost any style, but a longer, layered cut will add movement and interest.

Matching Your Personal Style

Your personal style is the final piece of the puzzle when it comes to choosing a hairstyle that suits you. Are you a classic, preppy guy? A short, side-parted cut will work well. If you're more of a rocker, a longer, messy style with some texture will suit you. Professional men will want to stick with a clean, classic cut.

Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles until you find one that feels like you. Your hairstyle should be an extension of your personal style and make you feel confident and comfortable.

Maintaining Your New Hairstyle

Once you've chosen a hairstyle that suits you, it's important to take care of it. Regular haircuts will keep your style looking fresh and neat. Invest in quality hair products that are tailored to your hair type, whether that's a volumizing mousse or a smoothing serum.

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often. If you have curly hair, consider investing in a diffuser attachment for your blow dryer to help define your curls.

FAQ

What if I have a receding hairline?

If you're experiencing hair loss or a receding hairline, a short, textured cut will work well. Avoid styles that require a lot of hair on top, as this will only draw attention to your hairline.

What if I have thinning hair?

A longer, layered cut will add volume and dimension to thinning hair. Avoid styles that are too short or require a lot of product, as this can make your hair look even thinner.

What if I have a unique hair color?

Your hair color can add interest and personality to your hairstyle. If you have a unique hair color, consider a cut that shows it off, like a short, textured cut with highlights or a longer, layered cut with vibrant color.

How often should I get a haircut?

Most men will need a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain their style. If you have a shorter cut, you may need to go more often to keep your style looking neat.

Can I style my hair differently for different occasions?

Absolutely! Your hairstyle should be versatile and able to adapt to different situations. Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find a look that works for you.

What if I'm not sure what hairstyle I want?

If you're feeling overwhelmed or unsure about what hairstyle will suit you, don't be afraid to ask your stylist for advice. They can help you determine your face shape and hair texture, and recommend styles that will work well for you.

Choosing a hairstyle that suits you can be a daunting task, but with the right guidance, it can be a fun and rewarding experience. Use this guide to help you determine what hairstyle will suit you based on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.
