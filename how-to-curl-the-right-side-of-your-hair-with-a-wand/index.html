How To Curl The Right Side Of Your Hair With A Wand

Written by Robby Aug 26, 2023 · 7 min read

Curls are always in fashion, and if you're looking to add some volume and texture to your hair, then curling is the way to go. While most people focus on curling the left side of their hair, the right side often gets neglected. In this blog post, we'll show you how to curl the right side of your hair with a wand, so that you can achieve a balanced and beautiful look.

Description

Curling the right side of your hair can be challenging because of the angle and the placement of the wand. However, with the right technique, you can achieve a beautiful and natural-looking curl that will last all day. We'll start by discussing the tools you'll need and some tips for preparing your hair for curling. Then we'll go through the step-by-step process of curling the right side of your hair with a wand. Finally, we'll provide some tips for maintaining your curls and keeping your hair healthy.

How to

Tools You'll Need

Before you start curling, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • A curling wand
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Hairspray

Make sure your curling wand is the right size for the curls you want. A smaller wand will give you tighter curls, while a larger wand will give you looser waves.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start curling, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and dry your hair thoroughly
  • Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage
  • Section your hair using clips or hair ties

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to curl the right side without getting tangled or caught up in the left side.

Curling the Right Side of Your Hair with a Wand

Now that you have your tools and your hair is prepped, it's time to start curling. Follow these steps:

  1. Take a small section of hair on the right side of your head, about 1-2 inches wide.
  2. Hold the wand vertically and wrap the section of hair around it, away from your face.
  3. Hold the hair in place for 5-10 seconds, then release.
  4. Repeat steps 1-3 until you have curled all of the right side of your hair.
  5. Once you've curled the right side, move on to the left side and repeat the process.

Remember to curl each section of hair away from your face to achieve a natural-looking curl.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you curl the right side of your hair:

  • Use a lower heat setting to prevent damage to your hair
  • Don't curl your hair too tightly, as this can make it look unnatural
  • Use hairspray to hold your curls in place
  • Don't touch your curls until they have cooled down, as this can cause them to lose their shape

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curling the right side of your hair:

Q: How do I prevent my curls from falling out?

A: Use hairspray to hold your curls in place and don't touch them until they have cooled down.

Q: How often should I curl my hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: How do I maintain my curls?

A: Use a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and tangles, and apply a hair oil to keep your curls hydrated.

Hair Care

Curling your hair can cause damage if you don't take care of it properly. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before heat styling
  • Limit heat styling to once or twice a week
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and dyes on your hair

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful while still enjoying the benefits of heat styling.

Conclusion

Curling the right side of your hair with a wand can be challenging, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful and balanced look. Remember to use a heat protectant spray, section your hair, and curl each section away from your face. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create natural-looking curls that will last all day.
