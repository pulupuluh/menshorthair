How To Cut Layered Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline May 08, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting men's hair can be intimidating, especially when it comes to creating layers. But with the right tools and techniques, it can be easy to achieve a layered look that is stylish and low maintenance. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting layered men's hair, including tips and tricks for achieving the perfect cut.

How to Cut Layered Men's Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to help keep the hair damp while you cut.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use the comb to section the hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Clip each section to keep it separate from the others.

Step 3: Start with the Back

Begin by cutting the back section of the hair. Use the comb to lift a section of hair straight up from the head, then use the scissors to snip off the ends. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the back until you have achieved the desired length.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

Once you have finished the back, move on to the sides. Use the same technique to cut the hair on each side, lifting sections straight up and snipping off the ends.

Step 5: Layer the Hair

To create layers, use the scissors to cut into the hair at an angle. Start near the top of the head and work your way down, cutting small sections at a time. This will create a gradual, layered effect.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have created the layers, use the scissors to blend them together. This will create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 7: Trim the Bangs

If your client has bangs, use the scissors to trim them to the desired length.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Use the mirror to check your work from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments until you are satisfied with the final result.

Step 9: Clean Up

Use a brush to remove any loose hairs from the client's neck and shoulders. Offer a towel or paper towel to help them clean up.

Tips for Cutting Layered Men's Hair

- Work with damp hair to make it easier to cut and style.

- Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp as you cut.

- Make small cuts and work your way down to avoid cutting off too much hair at once.

- Use a comb to section the hair and keep it organized.

- Check your work from all angles to ensure a balanced and even cut.

FAQs

How long does layered men's hair last?

Layered men's hair can last anywhere from 6-8 weeks, depending on how fast the hair grows. It's a good idea to schedule regular haircuts to maintain the layered look.

How do I know how much hair to cut off?

It's best to start with small cuts and work your way down. Remember, you can always cut more off, but you can't put it back on!

Hair Care for Layered Men's Hair

To keep layered men's hair looking its best, it's important to use high-quality hair care products. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for your client's hair type, such as fine or thick hair. Use a styling product to add texture and hold, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.
