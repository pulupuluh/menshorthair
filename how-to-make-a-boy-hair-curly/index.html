How To Make A Boy s Hair Curly Step By Step Guide

Curly hair is always in style, and if you have a boy who wants curly locks, there are a few ways you can achieve this look. In this article, we will go over how to make a boy's hair curly, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Follow our step-by-step guide to get the curly hairstyle your boy desires.

How to Make a Boy's Hair Curly

There are a few different methods you can use to make a boy's hair curly, including:

  • Using a curling iron or wand
  • Using braids or twists
  • Using a perm

Each of these methods has its pros and cons, so it's important to choose the one that works best for your boy's hair type and desired style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Method 1: Using a Curling Iron or Wand

This is the most straightforward method to make a boy's hair curly. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash and towel-dry your boy's hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to his hair to prevent damage.
  3. Divide his hair into sections and clip them out of the way.
  4. Take one section and wrap it around the curling iron or wand, holding for a few seconds.
  5. Release the hair and repeat with the remaining sections.
  6. Once all the sections are curled, use your fingers to gently separate and loosen the curls for a more natural-looking style.
  7. Finish with a light hold hairspray.

Method 2: Using Braids or Twists

This method involves braiding or twisting the hair while it's damp and leaving it to dry overnight. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash your boy's hair and towel-dry it until it's damp.
  2. Section his hair into small sections and braid or twist each section tightly.
  3. Once all the hair is braided or twisted, cover his head with a silk scarf or bonnet to protect the hair and prevent frizz.
  4. Leave the braids or twists in overnight to allow the hair to dry completely.
  5. Remove the braids or twists in the morning and use your fingers to separate and loosen the curls for a more natural-looking style.
  6. Finish with a light hold hairspray.

Method 3: Using a Perm

A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the hair's structure, creating permanent curls. This method should only be done by a professional stylist. Follow these steps:

  1. Consult with a professional stylist to determine if your boy's hair is healthy enough for a perm.
  2. Choose the desired curl size and style.
  3. The stylist will apply a chemical solution to the hair and wrap it around perm rods.
  4. The hair will be left to process for a specific amount of time.
  5. The rods will be removed, and the hair will be rinsed and neutralized to stop the chemical process.
  6. Follow the stylist's instructions for aftercare, including avoiding washing or styling the hair for a few days.

Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to maintain curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle instead.
  • Avoid using hot tools on curly hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep curly hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol, as this can dry out the hair.

FAQs

Can I make my boy's hair curly without using heat?

Yes, you can use braids or twists to create curls without using heat. However, this method takes longer and requires leaving the hair in overnight.

How long do curls last?

The length of time curls last depends on the method used and how well the hair is maintained. Curls created with a curling iron or wand may last a few hours to a day, while curls created with a perm can last several months.

How do I prevent frizz in curly hair?

To prevent frizz in curly hair, avoid brushing when it's dry, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using products with alcohol.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

To maintain curly hair, it's important to follow a proper hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash curly hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep curly hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on curly hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle curly hair when it's wet, starting from the ends and working your way up.

With these tips and methods, you can make a boy's hair curly and maintain its health and style. Remember to choose the method that works best for your boy's hair type and desired style, and always take proper care of curly hair to keep it looking its best.
