How To Thin Out Your Hair With Regular Scissors

Written by Luffy May 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having thick, bulky hair that's difficult to manage? One solution is to thin out your hair with regular scissors. Thinning out your hair can make it more manageable, easier to style, and give it a more natural look. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to thin out your hair with regular scissors.

How to Thin Out Your Hair With Regular Scissors

Thinning out your hair with regular scissors is a simple process that you can do at home. Here's what you'll need:

  • Regular hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start thinning out your hair, it's important to wash it thoroughly. This will ensure that your hair is clean and easy to work with. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or ties. This will make it easier to thin out your hair without accidentally cutting too much. Start with the bottom section of your hair and work your way up.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a comb, comb through the section of hair you want to thin out. This will help you see which parts of your hair are the thickest.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Using your regular hair cutting scissors, cut into your hair at a 45-degree angle. This will create a more natural look and prevent your hair from looking choppy. Start by cutting small sections of your hair at a time and work your way up. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as this can lead to uneven hair.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

After you've cut your hair, use your scissors to blend the cut sections with the rest of your hair. This will create a more natural look and prevent your hair from looking too thin in certain areas.

Tips for Thinning Out Your Hair

Thinning out your hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you can achieve the look you want:

  • Start small: Cut less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more if you need to.
  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can cause your hair to look frayed and uneven.
  • Blend carefully: Take your time blending your hair to prevent it from looking choppy.
  • Don't overdo it: It's easy to get carried away when thinning out your hair. Remember to step back and assess your progress.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair with regular scissors?

Yes, you can. However, it's important to use sharp scissors and take your time to prevent your hair from looking uneven.

How often should I thin out my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin out your hair every 3-6 months.

Can thinning out my hair make it look healthier?

Yes, thinning out your hair can make it look healthier by removing split ends and reducing bulkiness.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Your Hair

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a conditioner that's suitable for your hair type to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your hair's shape.
  • Avoid using too many hair products, as this can weigh down your hair.

Thinning out your hair with regular scissors is a great way to achieve a more natural, manageable look. By following these tips and taking care of your hair properly, you can maintain your new look and keep your hair healthy and shiny.
