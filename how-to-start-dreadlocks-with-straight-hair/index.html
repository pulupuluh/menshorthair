How To Start Dreadlocks With Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 26, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to try something new? Dreadlocks might be the perfect solution for you! In this article, we will guide you on how to start dreadlocks with straight hair. We will cover everything from how to prepare your hair for dreadlocks to how to take care of them once they are locked. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparing Your Hair for Dreadlocks
  • Methods for Starting Dreadlocks with Straight Hair
  • Maintenance and Care for Dreadlocks
  • FAQs

Preparing Your Hair for Dreadlocks

Before you start the dreadlocking process, it is important to prepare your hair. This will help your hair to lock better and reduce the risk of breakage. Here are the steps to prepare your hair:

Step 1: Clean Your Hair

Wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, oil, or dirt. This will help your hair to lock better and reduce the risk of mold and mildew.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Dry your hair completely using a towel or blow dryer. It is important to start the locking process with dry hair to prevent any mildew or mold growth.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to section your hair and start the dreadlocking process.

Methods for Starting Dreadlocks with Straight Hair

There are several methods for starting dreadlocks with straight hair. You can choose the method that suits you best depending on your hair type and texture. Here are some common methods:

Backcombing

Backcombing is a popular method for starting dreadlocks with straight hair. It involves teasing the hair strands backward to create knots that will eventually form dreadlocks.

Twist and Rip

The twist and rip method involves twisting and ripping the hair strands to create knots. This method is less damaging than backcombing and creates more natural-looking dreadlocks.

Crochet Hook

The crochet hook method involves using a crochet hook to pull the hair strands through themselves to create knots. This method is less damaging than backcombing and creates more uniform dreadlocks.

Maintenance and Care for Dreadlocks

Once you have started your dreadlocks, it is important to take care of them to prevent breakage and maintain their shape. Here are some tips for maintaining and caring for your dreadlocks:

Wash Your Hair

It is important to wash your hair regularly to prevent any buildup of dirt, oil, or product. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner as it can loosen the knots.

Retwist Your Hair

Retwist your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of your dreadlocks. Use a locking gel or beeswax to help the hair strands lock together.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your dreadlocks and cause breakage. Opt for loose hairstyles that do not put too much tension on your hair.

FAQs

Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, it is important to wait at least 6-8 weeks after relaxing your hair before starting the locking process. This will give your hair time to recover and reduce the risk of breakage.

Can I wash my hair after starting dreadlocks?

Yes, you can wash your hair after starting dreadlocks. It is important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner as it can loosen the knots.

How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

It can take anywhere from 3-6 months for dreadlocks to form depending on your hair type and texture. Be patient and allow your hair to lock naturally.

Can I color my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can color your dreadlocks. However, it is important to use a hair dye that is safe for dreadlocks and avoid using bleach as it can weaken the hair strands.

Starting dreadlocks with straight hair can be a fun and exciting journey. Follow these tips and methods to start your dreadlocks and take care of them properly. Remember to be patient and let your hair lock naturally. Happy dreading!
