How To Cut Self Hair At Home With Trimmer

Introduction

With the pandemic still ongoing, many people are avoiding going to hair salons and opting to cut their hair at home. Cutting self hair at home can be daunting, especially if you don't have the right tools. However, with a trimmer, it's easier than ever to cut your hair at home. In this article, we will discuss how to cut self hair at home with a trimmer.

How to Cut Self Hair at Home with Trimmer

Cutting self hair at home with a trimmer is a simple process. However, before you begin, it's important to have the right tools. You will need:

  • A trimmer with a guard
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A towel or cape to catch hair

Once you have these tools, follow these steps:

Step by Step Guide:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  2. Attach the guard to your trimmer. The guard will determine the length of your hair. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed.
  3. Comb your hair in the direction it naturally falls.
  4. Begin cutting your hair, starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. Use the trimmer in an upward motion, against the direction of hair growth.
  5. Be sure to cut your hair evenly on both sides. Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  6. Once you have cut the back and sides of your hair, move to the top. Use the same technique, cutting your hair in the direction it naturally falls.
  7. Trim any stray hairs around your ears and neck.
  8. Remove the guard from your trimmer and use the bare blades to clean up your neckline and sideburns.
  9. After you've finished cutting your hair, use the comb to remove any loose hairs from your head and neck.
  10. Shower and shampoo your hair to remove any remaining loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Self Hair at Home with Trimmer:

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting self hair at home with a trimmer:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back.
  • Invest in a high-quality trimmer. A good trimmer will help you achieve a clean, even cut.
  • Use a towel or cape to catch hair and make clean up easier.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so take breaks as needed to avoid mistakes.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting self hair at home with a trimmer:

  • Can I cut my hair wet? It's not recommended to cut your hair wet. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • How often should I cut my hair? It depends on your hair type and style. As a general rule, it's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style.
  • Can I use a trimmer to cut my bangs? Yes, you can use a trimmer to cut your bangs. Use a smaller guard or no guard at all to achieve the desired length.

Hair Care After Cutting Self Hair at Home with Trimmer:

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain your new style. Here are some tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to prevent dryness and split ends.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your style and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Cutting self hair at home with a trimmer can be a great way to save money and time. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a clean, even cut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully, and you'll have a great new hairstyle in no time.
