What Music Genres Were Popular In The 60 S

Written by Robby Jul 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

The 60's were a time of great cultural change and music played a huge role in this era. From rock and roll to soul, there were a variety of music genres that dominated the airwaves. In this article, we will explore the most popular music genres of the 60's.

Rock and Roll

Rock and roll was the most popular music genre of the 60's. It originated in the United States in the 1950s and quickly spread to other parts of the world. Rock and roll was characterized by its heavy use of electric guitars, drums, and bass. Some of the most popular rock and roll bands of the 60's included The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, and The Beach Boys.

The Beatles

The Beatles were a British rock band that formed in Liverpool in 1960. They are considered one of the most influential bands of all time and helped to popularize rock and roll in the 60's. Some of their most popular songs include "Hey Jude," "Let It Be," and "A Hard Day's Night."

The Rolling Stones

The Rolling Stones were an English rock band that formed in London in 1962. They were known for their bluesy sound and rebellious image. Some of their most popular songs include "Satisfaction," "Paint It Black," and "Jumpin' Jack Flash."

The Beach Boys

The Beach Boys were an American rock band that formed in California in 1961. They were known for their harmonies and songs about surfing, cars, and girls. Some of their most popular songs include "Surfin' USA," "Good Vibrations," and "California Girls."

Soul

Soul music was another popular genre of the 60's. It originated in African American communities in the United States and was characterized by its use of gospel-inspired vocals and rhythms. Some of the most popular soul artists of the 60's included Aretha Franklin, James Brown, and Otis Redding.

Aretha Franklin

Aretha Franklin was an American singer and songwriter who is considered one of the greatest singers of all time. She was known for her powerful voice and soulful style. Some of her most popular songs include "Respect," "Chain of Fools," and "I Say a Little Prayer."

James Brown

James Brown was an American singer and songwriter who is known as the "Godfather of Soul." He was known for his electrifying performances and his unique style of funk music. Some of his most popular songs include "Papa's Got a Brand New Bag," "I Got You (I Feel Good)," and "Sex Machine."

Otis Redding

Otis Redding was an American singer and songwriter who is considered one of the greatest soul singers of all time. He was known for his emotional performances and his ability to convey deep feelings through his music. Some of his most popular songs include "Sittin' on the Dock of the Bay," "Try a Little Tenderness," and "Pain in My Heart."

Folk

Folk music was another popular genre of the 60's. It originated in the United States and was characterized by its acoustic sound and its focus on storytelling. Some of the most popular folk artists of the 60's included Bob Dylan, Joan Baez, and Simon & Garfunkel.

Bob Dylan

Bob Dylan was an American singer and songwriter who is considered one of the most influential musicians of all time. He was known for his poetic lyrics and his ability to capture the spirit of the 60's. Some of his most popular songs include "Blowin' in the Wind," "The Times They Are a-Changin'," and "Like a Rolling Stone."

Joan Baez

Joan Baez was an American singer and songwriter who is known for her activism and her beautiful voice. She was one of the most important voices of the folk music movement and helped to popularize the genre in the 60's. Some of her most popular songs include "Diamonds and Rust," "The Night They Drove Old Dixie Down," and "We Shall Overcome."

Simon & Garfunkel

Simon & Garfunkel were an American folk duo who formed in New York City in 1957. They were known for their close harmonies and their ability to write beautiful songs. Some of their most popular songs include "The Sound of Silence," "Bridge Over Troubled Water," and "Mrs. Robinson."

Conclusion

The 60's were a time of great change and music played an important role in shaping the culture of the era. From rock and roll to soul to folk, there were a variety of music genres that dominated the airwaves. While many of these genres have evolved over time, their influence can still be felt in modern music today.
