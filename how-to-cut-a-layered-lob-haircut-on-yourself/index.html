How To Cut A Layered Lob Haircut On Yourself

If you're looking for a new haircut that's easy to maintain and style, a layered lob might be just what you need. And the best part? You can cut it yourself! In this post, we'll guide you through the steps of cutting a layered lob on yourself. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Cut a Layered Lob Haircut on Yourself
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

A layered lob is a shoulder-length haircut that features layers throughout the hair. This haircut is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance style that still looks chic and trendy. With the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut a layered lob on yourself.

How to Cut a Layered Lob Haircut on Yourself

To cut a layered lob on yourself, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties

Before you start cutting, it's important to have a clear idea of what you want your haircut to look like. You can search for inspiration online or bring a photo of the haircut you want to your own hair. This will help you visualize the layers and overall shape of the haircut.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to follow when cutting a layered lob on yourself:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.
  2. Section off your hair. Start by dividing your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Use hair ties to hold each section in place.
  3. Cut the bottom section. Start with the bottom section of your hair. Use your comb to comb through the hair and make sure it's smooth. Then, use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Cut the hair straight across, making sure to keep the scissors parallel to the floor.
  4. Cut the middle section. Release the middle section of your hair and comb it through. Position the scissors vertically and start cutting the layers. Cut the hair at an angle, pulling the hair outwards and away from your head as you cut. This will create layers that blend into the bottom section of your hair.
  5. Cut the top section. Release the top section of your hair and comb it through. Position the scissors vertically and start cutting the layers. Cut the hair at an angle, pulling the hair outwards and away from your head as you cut. This will create layers that blend into the middle section of your hair.
  6. Blend the layers. Once all sections are cut, use your comb to blend the layers together. Comb through the hair from top to bottom, making sure the layers are even and blend seamlessly into one another.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting a layered lob on yourself:

  • Start with small cuts. It's better to cut less hair than too much hair at once.
  • Take breaks. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so take breaks if you need to.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • Don't cut too much. It's better to cut less hair than too much hair at once.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a layered lob:

Can I cut a layered lob on myself if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut a layered lob on yourself if you have curly hair. Just make sure your hair is dry and use your fingers to separate the curls as you cut.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and fix it later. It's better to cut less hair than too much hair at once.

How often should I cut my layered lob?

You should cut your layered lob every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care

After cutting your layered lob, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

That's it! You now know how to cut a layered lob on yourself. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a chic and trendy haircut that's easy to maintain and style.
