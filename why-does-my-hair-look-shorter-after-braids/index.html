Why Does My Hair Look Shorter After Braids

Written by Robby Aug 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you've ever gotten braids, you might have noticed something strange when you take them out. Your hair suddenly looks shorter than it did before you got the braids. But why does this happen? In this article, we'll explain why your hair looks shorter after braids and what you can do about it.

Description

Getting braids is a popular way to protect your hair and give it a break from styling. Braids can also be a great way to switch up your look and try something new. However, when you take out your braids, you might notice that your hair looks shorter than it did before. This can be frustrating, especially if you were hoping to see some growth.

How to

The first step in understanding why your hair looks shorter after braids is to take a closer look at what's happening. When you get braids, your hair is tightly wrapped around the braid, which can cause it to stretch. This stretching can make your hair appear longer than it actually is. When you take out the braids, your hair returns to its natural state, and the stretching disappears. This can make your hair look shorter than you remember.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step breakdown of why your hair looks shorter after braids:

  1. Your hair is wrapped tightly around the braid, causing it to stretch and appear longer.
  2. When you take out the braids, the stretching disappears, and your hair returns to its natural length.
  3. This sudden change in length can make your hair look shorter than it did before.

Tips

If you're worried about your hair looking shorter after braids, there are a few things you can do to minimize the effect:

  • Take pictures of your hair before and after getting braids so you can see the difference for yourself.
  • Keep track of your hair growth over time so you can see the progress you're making.
  • Be patient! Hair growth takes time, and it's important to give your hair the care and attention it needs.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about why your hair looks shorter after braids:

Q: Will my hair grow back to its original length?

A: Yes! Your hair will eventually grow back to its original length, but it may take some time.

Q: Can I do anything to make my hair grow faster?

A: There's no magic formula for making your hair grow faster, but there are things you can do to promote healthy hair growth. This includes eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling.

Q: Are braids bad for my hair?

A: Braids can be a great way to protect your hair, but they can also cause damage if they're too tight or if you leave them in for too long. It's important to take breaks between braiding and to make sure your braids aren't too tight.

Hair Care

One of the best ways to keep your hair healthy and growing is by taking good care of it. This includes using gentle products, avoiding heat styling, and protecting your hair from the elements. You should also make sure to get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

By taking good care of your hair and being patient, you can help it grow back to its original length after braids. Remember that everyone's hair grows at a different rate, so don't get discouraged if it takes longer than you expected. With time and care, your hair will be healthy and beautiful.
