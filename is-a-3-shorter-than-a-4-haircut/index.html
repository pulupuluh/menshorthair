Is A 3 Shorter Than A 4 Haircut

Written by Aprilia Apr 28, 2023 · 6 min read

When it comes to getting a haircut, it's important to know the different clipper guard sizes and what they mean. Two of the most commonly used clipper guard sizes are 3 and 4. But is a 3 shorter than a 4 haircut? In this blog post, we'll explore the difference between the two and help you decide which one is right for you.

Description

A clipper guard is a plastic attachment that fits onto a set of clippers. The guard determines how much hair is left on your head after the clipper passes over it. The number on the guard corresponds to the length of the hair that will be left behind. For example, a #3 guard will leave hair that is 3/8 of an inch long, while a #4 guard will leave hair that is 1/2 an inch long.

How to

If you're looking to get a haircut with a #3 or #4 guard, the process is fairly simple. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Choose Your Guard

Decide whether you want a #3 or #4 guard. Keep in mind that a #3 guard will leave your hair shorter than a #4 guard.

Step 2: Attach the Guard

Place the guard onto your clippers, making sure it clicks into place securely.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting your hair, starting at the sides and working your way up to the top. Be sure to use a comb to lift the hair and make cutting easier.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the hair with the guard, use scissors or a comb to blend the hair and make sure there are no harsh lines.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a #3 or #4 haircut:

  • If you're not sure which guard to choose, start with the longer one (i.e. #4) and work your way down if you want it shorter.
  • Be sure to clean your clippers after each use to keep them in good condition.
  • If you're cutting someone else's hair, be sure to communicate with them throughout the process to ensure they're happy with the length.

FAQ

Q: Is a 3 shorter than a 4?

A: Yes, a #3 guard will leave your hair shorter than a #4 guard.

Q: Can I use a #3 or #4 guard with any type of hair?

A: Yes, these guards can be used on all hair types, but keep in mind that the length of the hair left behind may look different on different hair textures.

Q: Can I use a #3 or #4 guard to cut my own hair?

A: Yes, but it may be difficult to get an even cut on the back of your head without the help of a mirror or another person.

Hair Care

After getting a #3 or #4 haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair (i.e. blow dryers, flat irons, etc.).
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.

So, is a 3 shorter than a 4 haircut? Yes, it is. But whether you choose a #3 or #4 guard ultimately depends on your personal preference and desired length. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut at home or in the salon.
