How To Cut Long Layers On Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your short hair feeling flat and lifeless? Adding long layers can give your hair some much-needed dimension and movement. But, how do you cut long layers on short hair without making a mess of it? Don't worry, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide.

Description

Long layers can add texture and shape to short hair, but it's important to approach the cut with caution. Cutting hair is a skilled art, and if you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to leave it to a professional. However, if you're feeling brave and want to give it a go yourself, read on.

How to Cut Long Layers on Short Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section the hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. Clip each section out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Slide your fingers down to the desired length, and snip the hair with your scissors. Repeat this process until you've cut all of the hair in the bottom section.

Step 4: Move Upwards

Release the next section of hair and repeat the cutting process. The key to cutting long layers is to make sure each layer is slightly shorter than the one below it. This will create a gradual, layered effect.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, release the top section of hair and cut it to the desired length. Be careful not to cut too much off, as this can make the layers look choppy.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair off than to add it back on.
  • Use a comb to make sure each section of hair is smooth and tangle-free before cutting.
  • Keep your scissors sharp to ensure a clean cut.
  • If you're unsure about how to cut long layers, practice on a mannequin head first.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long layers on short hair:

Can I cut long layers on my own?

While it's possible to cut long layers on your own, it's best to seek the help of a professional if you're not confident in your abilities.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends.

Can I still style my hair after cutting long layers?

Absolutely! In fact, cutting long layers can make it easier to style your hair, as it adds texture and movement.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Conclusion

Cutting long layers on short hair can be a great way to add texture and movement to your hair. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections, and seek the help of a professional if you're not confident in your abilities. Happy cutting!
