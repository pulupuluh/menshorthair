How To Stop Hair Fall After Pregnancy In Hindi Effective Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Welcoming a new life into your world is undoubtedly one of the most joyous moments of your life. However, it is also common for new mothers to experience hair fall after pregnancy. This is due to hormonal changes and other factors. If you are wondering how to stop hair fall after pregnancy in Hindi, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will discuss some effective tips and tricks that can help you prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.

What Causes Hair Fall After Pregnancy?

Before we dive into the tips, it's essential to understand the root cause of hair fall after pregnancy. Here are some common reasons why new mothers experience hair fall:

Hormonal Changes:

During pregnancy, the body experiences a surge in estrogen levels, which promotes healthy hair growth. However, after delivery, the hormone levels drop significantly, leading to hair fall.

Stress:

Bringing up a newborn can be stressful, and stress is a leading cause of hair fall.

Nutritional Deficiencies:

New mothers often neglect their own nutritional needs as they focus on their baby's health. This can lead to hair fall due to nutrient deficiencies.

How to Stop Hair Fall After Pregnancy in Hindi: Tips and Tricks

1. Eat a Balanced Diet:

Include foods rich in vitamins, minerals, and proteins in your diet. Vitamin B, C, D, E, and iron are essential for healthy hair growth.

2. Take Supplements:

If you are unable to get all the necessary nutrients from your diet, consider taking supplements. Consult your doctor before taking any supplements.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles:

Avoid pulling your hair into tight hairstyles like buns and ponytails. This can cause hair breakage and lead to hair fall.

4. Use a Gentle Shampoo:

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals. Avoid washing your hair too often as it can strip the hair of its natural oils.

5. Massage Your Scalp:

Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation and promote healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp gently.

6. De-Stress:

Take time out for yourself and practice relaxation techniques like yoga and meditation to manage stress.

7. Get Enough Sleep:

Getting enough sleep is crucial for overall health and hair growth. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep each night.

8. Stay Hydrated:

Drink plenty of water to keep your body hydrated. This can help prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.

9. Avoid Heat Styling:

Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and hair straighteners. These can damage your hair and lead to hair fall.

10. Consult a Doctor:

If your hair fall is severe and persists for a long time, consult a doctor. They can diagnose any underlying medical conditions and prescribe appropriate treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. How long does hair fall last after pregnancy?

Ans: Hair fall after pregnancy is temporary and usually lasts for 3-6 months.

Q2. Can postpartum hair fall be prevented?

Ans: Yes, postpartum hair fall can be prevented by following a healthy lifestyle, eating a balanced diet, and managing stress.

Q3. When should I consult a doctor for hair fall after pregnancy?

Ans: If your hair fall is severe and persists for a long time, consult a doctor.

Hair Care Tips for New Mothers

As a new mother, taking care of your hair may not be on top of your priority list. However, following these simple hair care tips can help you prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to harsh sunlight and pollution.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hair styling products that contain harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet as it can cause hair breakage.

Following these tips can help you prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth after pregnancy. Remember to be patient, as healthy hair growth takes time. In the meantime, enjoy the precious moments with your little one!
