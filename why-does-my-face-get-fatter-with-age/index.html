Why Does My Face Get Fatter With Age

Written by Robby Aug 17, 2023 · 6 min read

As we age, many of us start to notice that our faces seem to be getting fuller and rounder. This can be frustrating, as we often associate a slim face with youth and beauty. But why does this happen? In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind facial fat gain and what you can do to combat it.

What Causes Facial Fat Gain?

There are several factors that can contribute to facial fat gain as we age:

1. Loss of Collagen and Elastin

Collagen and elastin are two proteins that keep our skin looking firm and tight. As we age, our bodies produce less of these proteins, which can lead to sagging skin and a loss of facial definition.

2. Hormonal Changes

As we age, our hormone levels naturally fluctuate. This can lead to an increase in fat storage, particularly in the face.

3. Genetics

Some people are simply genetically predisposed to storing more fat in their face as they age.

How Can I Combat Facial Fat Gain?

While there's no surefire way to prevent facial fat gain, there are a few things you can do to minimize its effects:

1. Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in whole foods and low in processed foods can help keep your weight in check, which can in turn help prevent facial fat gain.

2. Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise can help you maintain a healthy weight and keep your facial muscles toned.

3. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your skin looking plump and hydrated, which can minimize the appearance of facial fat.

4. Consider Cosmetic Procedures

If you're unhappy with the appearance of your face despite your best efforts, there are several cosmetic procedures that can help. These include facelifts, cheek implants, and liposuction.

FAQ

Q: Is facial fat gain inevitable as we age?

A: No, it's not inevitable. While some degree of facial fat gain is natural as we age, there are several things you can do to minimize its effects.

Q: Can facial exercises help prevent facial fat gain?

A: While there's no scientific evidence to support the idea that facial exercises can prevent facial fat gain, some people swear by them.

Q: Is liposuction a safe procedure?

A: Like any surgical procedure, liposuction comes with certain risks. However, when performed by a qualified and experienced surgeon, it's generally considered safe.

Hair Care Tips to Complement Your Facial Fat Combat Plan

While there's no direct link between hair care and facial fat gain, taking good care of your hair can help you look and feel your best. Here are some tips to get you started:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Harsh shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Look for a gentle, sulfate-free formula.

2. Condition Regularly

Conditioner helps keep your hair hydrated and can prevent breakage and split ends.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try air-drying your hair or using heat-free styling methods instead.

4. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, while facial fat gain is a natural part of the aging process, there are several things you can do to minimize its effects. By maintaining a healthy diet and exercise routine, staying hydrated, and considering cosmetic procedures, you can help keep your face looking youthful and defined. And by taking good care of your hair, you can complement your facial fat combat plan and look and feel your best.
