How To Cut Women s Short Hair Layer Haircut Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 18, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut that’s modern, chic, and easy to maintain? Then a short layered haircut might be perfect for you. This style is versatile, and there are many ways to customize it to your liking. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to cut women’s short hair layer haircut, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. Description of Short Layered Haircuts
  2. How to Cut Women’s Short Hair Layer Haircut
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Cutting Short Layered Haircuts
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Short Layered Haircuts

Description of Short Layered Haircuts

Short layered haircuts are a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. They work well for all hair types, from fine to thick, and can be styled in many different ways. Short layered haircuts are also very low maintenance and easy to style, making them perfect for busy women who want to look great without spending a lot of time on their hair.

How to Cut Women’s Short Hair Layer Haircut

Cutting a short layered haircut is a bit more challenging than cutting a simple blunt cut, but it’s not as difficult as you might think. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying the hair. Make sure it’s completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section the hair into four parts: the crown, the sides, and the back.

Step 3: Begin cutting the hair in the back, starting at the nape of the neck. Use a comb to create a guide for the length you want, and then cut along the guide.

Step 4: Work your way up the back of the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Make sure you’re cutting at an angle to create layers.

Step 5: Move on to the sides of the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Use the same technique as you did in the back, cutting at an angle to create layers.

Step 6: Finally, cut the crown of the head. This is the trickiest part, as you want to create volume without making the hair too heavy. Use thinning shears to create texture and remove excess weight.

Step 7: Once you’ve finished cutting, use a comb to check for any uneven areas. Trim any stray hairs until you’re happy with the result.

Tips for Cutting Short Layered Haircuts

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short layered haircuts:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections as you become more confident.
  • Use thinning shears to create texture and remove excess bulk.
  • Don’t be afraid to make mistakes – hair grows back!

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own short layered haircut?

A: Yes, you can cut your own short layered haircut. However, it’s best to start with a simple cut and work your way up to more complicated styles.

Q: How often should I cut my short layered haircut?

A: It’s best to cut short layered haircuts every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care for Short Layered Haircuts

Short layered haircuts are easy to maintain, but they still require some care to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a light, volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush that can create frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own short layered haircut at home. Remember to start with simple cuts and work your way up to more complicated styles as you become more confident. With a little practice and patience, you’ll be able to create a haircut that’s perfect for you!
