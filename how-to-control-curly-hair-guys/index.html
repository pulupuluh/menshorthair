How To Control Curly Hair Guys A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Apr 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Curly hair is a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it adds volume and texture to your look, but on the other, it can be difficult to manage. If you're a guy with curly hair, you know the struggle of trying to keep your curls under control. Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll go over how to control curly hair guys, step by step. From hair care to styling tips, we've got everything you need to know to keep your curls looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • How to Control Curly Hair Guys
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair Guys
  • Styling Tips for Curly Hair Guys
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Control Curly Hair Guys

The first step to controlling curly hair is to find the right haircut. A good haircut can make all the difference in how your curls look and behave. Look for a stylist who specializes in curly hair and ask for a cut that works with your natural curl pattern. This might mean keeping some length on top and cutting the sides shorter, or it might mean going for a shorter, more uniform cut.

Once you have the right cut, it's time to focus on hair care. Curly hair requires a bit more attention than straight hair, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking their best.

Hair Care for Curly Hair Guys

The key to managing curly hair is to keep it moisturized. Curly hair tends to be dry, so you'll want to use products that hydrate and nourish your hair. Here are some tips for curly hair care:

1. Shampoo less often

Curly hair doesn't need to be washed as often as straight hair. In fact, shampooing too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Aim to shampoo your hair once or twice a week, and use a sulfate-free shampoo that won't dry out your curls.

2. Condition, condition, condition

Conditioner is your best friend when it comes to curly hair. Use a conditioner that's designed for curly hair and apply it liberally after shampooing. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water. This will help to lock in moisture and keep your curls looking soft and shiny.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your curls hydrated throughout the day. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair after showering, then comb it through with a wide-toothed comb. This will help to distribute the product evenly and prevent tangles.

4. Don't towel-dry your hair

Towel-drying can rough up your hair cuticle and cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry. This will help to absorb excess moisture without disrupting your curls.

Styling Tips for Curly Hair Guys

Now that you know how to care for your curls, it's time to talk styling. Here are some tips for styling curly hair:

1. Use a styling product

A good styling product can help to define your curls and keep them in place. Look for a product that's designed for curly hair, such as a curl cream or a gel. Apply the product to damp hair, then scrunch your hair gently to encourage your curls to form.

2. Try the "pineapple" method

The pineapple method is a great way to preserve your curls overnight. To do this, gather your hair at the top of your head and secure it with a scrunchie or a hair tie. This will help to prevent your curls from getting flattened while you sleep.

3. Invest in a diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help to enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Use the diffuser on a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair as you dry it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: Curly hair doesn't need to be washed as often as straight hair. Aim to shampoo your hair once or twice a week, and use a sulfate-free shampoo that won't dry out your curls.

Q: What's the best haircut for curly hair?

A: The best haircut for curly hair depends on your curl pattern and face shape. Look for a stylist who specializes in curly hair and ask for a cut that works with your natural curl pattern.

Q: How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

A: To reduce frizz, make sure you're using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid towel-drying your hair. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry, and use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but be aware that heat styling can be damaging to your hair. If you do choose to straighten your hair, use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting on your styling tool.

Conclusion

Curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right care and styling techniques, you can keep your curls looking their best. Remember to focus on hydration and moisture, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styling products and techniques. With a little patience and practice, you'll be able to control your curly hair like a pro!
