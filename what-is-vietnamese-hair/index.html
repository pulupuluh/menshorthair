What Is Vietnamese Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 27, 2023 · 7 min read

If you are in the market for hair extensions, you might have heard of Vietnamese hair. Vietnamese hair is considered one of the best types of hair for extensions because of its thickness, softness, and durability. In this article, we’ll discuss what Vietnamese hair is, how to take care of it, and some important tips to keep in mind when buying Vietnamese hair extensions.

Description of Vietnamese Hair

Vietnamese hair is known for its thickness and softness. It is sourced from Vietnamese women who have long, straight, and healthy hair. Vietnamese hair is usually black or dark brown in color and has a natural shine to it. The hair strands are thick and strong, making it easy to style and maintain. Vietnamese hair is also known to last longer than other types of hair extensions.

How to Identify Vietnamese Hair

When shopping for Vietnamese hair, it’s important to know how to identify it. Vietnamese hair is typically unprocessed, meaning it has not been chemically treated or colored. It also has a natural wave or slight curl to it, which is a characteristic of Asian hair. Vietnamese hair is also thick and heavy, so it’s easy to tell the difference between it and other types of hair extensions.

Step by Step: How to Take Care of Vietnamese Hair

Proper hair care is essential to make sure your Vietnamese hair extensions last as long as possible. Here are some steps you can take to care for your Vietnamese hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair every two to three days with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair clean and moisturized without stripping it of its natural oils.

Step 2: Brush Your Hair

Brush your hair gently using a wide-tooth comb or a brush designed for hair extensions. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Avoid Heat

Avoid using heat on your Vietnamese hair extensions as much as possible. If you need to use heat, use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting.

Step 4: Store Your Hair

When you’re not wearing your Vietnamese hair extensions, store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. You can also use a hair extension hanger to keep them organized and tangle-free.

Tips for Buying Vietnamese Hair Extensions

When buying Vietnamese hair extensions, keep these tips in mind:

Tip 1: Look for Authenticity

Make sure you buy Vietnamese hair from a reputable supplier who can guarantee its authenticity. Look for suppliers who source their hair directly from Vietnam.

Tip 2: Check the Quality

Check the quality of the hair before you buy it. Look for hair that has a consistent texture, thickness, and color. Make sure the hair is soft and doesn’t tangle easily.

Tip 3: Know Your Needs

Know what you need before you buy Vietnamese hair extensions. Consider the length, color, and texture of the hair, as well as the method of attachment.

FAQs about Vietnamese Hair

Q: How long does Vietnamese hair last?

A: Vietnamese hair can last up to two years with proper care.

Q: Can Vietnamese hair be colored?

A: Yes, Vietnamese hair can be colored. However, it’s best to have a professional colorist do it to avoid damaging the hair.

Q: How much does Vietnamese hair cost?

A: The cost of Vietnamese hair extensions varies depending on the length, texture, and quality of the hair. Expect to pay anywhere from $100 to $500 or more for a full head of extensions.

Conclusion

Vietnamese hair is a popular choice for hair extensions because of its thickness, softness, and durability. By knowing how to identify Vietnamese hair, how to take care of it, and what to look for when buying it, you can ensure you get the best possible extensions for your needs.
