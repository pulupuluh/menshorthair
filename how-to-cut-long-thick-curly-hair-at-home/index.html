How To Cut Long Thick Curly Hair At Home

Written by Oliver Jul 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon to tame your long, thick, curly locks? With a little patience and practice, you can learn how to cut your own hair at home. Not only will you save money, but you'll also have the freedom to experiment with different styles and cuts. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting long thick curly hair at home.

How to Cut Long Thick Curly Hair at Home

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're dealing with long, thick, curly locks. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. Here's what you'll need:

Tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide:

1. Start by washing and detangling your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently remove any knots or tangles.

2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start with a small section at the nape of your neck and work your way up.

3. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. This will help you see the length of the hair and prevent you from cutting too much off.

4. Use the scissors to trim the ends of the twisted section. Start with a small amount and work your way up, checking the length after each cut.

5. Continue this process, working your way through each section of hair.

6. Once you've finished cutting all of your hair, style as desired.

Tips:

  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much off.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.
  • Consider seeking out online tutorials or consulting with a professional stylist before attempting a major haircut.

FAQ:

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended that you get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

A: It's generally recommended that you cut your hair when it's dry to ensure an accurate cut. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can result in a shorter cut than intended.

Hair Care

Now that you know how to cut your long, thick, curly hair at home, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your locks soft and manageable.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and cuts - after all, it's your hair!
