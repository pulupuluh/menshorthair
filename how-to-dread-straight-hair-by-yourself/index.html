How To Dread Straight Hair By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 25, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have straight hair and want to dread it, you may think it's impossible without the help of a professional. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can create dreadlocks on your own at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to dread straight hair by yourself, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  • How to Dread Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Dreading Straight Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Dread Straight Hair

Dreadlocks are created by matting and twisting sections of hair. If you have straight hair, you can still create dreadlocks, but it may take longer to achieve the desired look. Here's what you'll need:

  • Dread wax or locking gel
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Hair ties or rubber bands
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to dread straight hair:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry. It's easier to work with clean hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using a comb. Start with small sections, about the size of a pencil, and secure them with hair ties or rubber bands.
  3. Apply a small amount of dread wax or locking gel to each section. Rub the product between your palms to warm it up before applying it to your hair.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly. Use your fingers to roll the hair between your palms in a clockwise direction. Keep twisting until the hair starts to knot up.
  5. Use a fine-toothed comb to tease the knots. Backcomb the hair in the opposite direction of the twist, being careful not to pull too hard and damage the hair.
  6. Repeat the twisting and teasing process for each section of hair. Be patient, as it may take several weeks or even months for the knots to form and the dreads to set.
  7. Once your hair is completely dreaded, use a hair dryer on low heat to help set the knots.

Tips for Dreading Straight Hair

Here are some tips to help you dread straight hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from your hair before starting the dreading process.
  • Be gentle when backcombing your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it difficult to create knots.
  • Wear a scarf or wrap your hair at night to prevent your dreads from unraveling.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreading straight hair:

  • How long does it take to dread straight hair? It can take several weeks or even months for the knots to form and the dreads to set.
  • Can I wash my hair with dreads? Yes, but be gentle when washing and avoid using hot water, as it can cause the dreads to unravel.
  • Can I undo my dreads? Yes, but it may take some time and effort to comb them out.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you've dreaded your straight hair, you'll need to take care of your new style to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils, as they can cause buildup in your dreads.
  • Protect your dreads from sun exposure and chlorine.

Now that you know how to dread straight hair by yourself, you can create your own unique style. Remember to be patient and take care of your dreads to keep them looking their best.
