How To Cut Curly Hair Evenly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 13, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that finding a stylist who knows how to cut your hair can be a challenge. It's important to find someone who understands the unique needs of curly hair and can cut it evenly. But what if you want to cut your curly hair yourself? It's definitely possible, but it requires a bit of knowledge and patience. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of how to cut curly hair evenly.

Description

Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right techniques, anyone can do it. Whether you're looking to trim your ends or give yourself a full haircut, there are a few things you need to keep in mind when cutting curly hair. In this article, we'll cover the basics of how to cut curly hair evenly, including tools you'll need, techniques to use, and tips for maintaining your curly locks.

How to Cut Curly Hair Evenly

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your curly hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts. You'll also want to make sure you have a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, sectioning clips to keep your hair in place, and a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while you cut.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll want to make sure it's detangled. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently comb through your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will help prevent breakage and make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is detangled, it's time to section it. Use sectioning clips to divide your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and ensure you don't miss any sections.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Start with one of the back sections and clip the other sections out of the way. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. Then, use your scissors to cut off the ends of the twist. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way through the section. Once you've finished one section, unclip the next section and repeat the process.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting all four sections, it's time to check for evenness. Comb through your hair and look for any areas that are longer or shorter than the rest. If you notice any uneven sections, go back and trim them until your hair is even all around.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Evenly

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure evenness.
  • Twist each section tightly before cutting to prevent split ends.
  • Trim your hair while it's damp to prevent frizz.
  • Check for evenness as you go to avoid any major mistakes.
  • Don't be afraid to seek professional help if you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself.

FAQ

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself, but it requires knowledge and patience.

What tools do I need to cut my curly hair?

You'll need sharp scissors, a wide-tooth comb, sectioning clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

How do I cut my curly hair evenly?

Section your hair, twist each section tightly, and cut off the ends of the twist. Check for evenness as you go.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair evenly is just one part of maintaining healthy, beautiful curls. Here are a few tips for keeping your curly hair in top shape:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and frizz.
  • Sleep with your hair in a pineapple or silk scarf to prevent tangling.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to cut your curly hair evenly and maintain healthy, beautiful curls!
