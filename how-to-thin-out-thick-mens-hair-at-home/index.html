How To Thin Out Thick Men s Hair At Home

Written by Petter May 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse, especially for men. While thick hair can be a sign of good health, it can also be difficult to manage, style, and maintain. That's why thinning out thick hair is a common request at the barbershop. However, you don't have to go to a professional to thin out your hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can thin out your thick hair at home. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick men's hair at home.

How To

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in thinning out your hair is to wash it thoroughly. Make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. This will make it easier to style and cut your hair.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

You will need a few tools to thin out your hair at home. These include a pair of thinning shears, regular scissors, a comb, and hair clips. You can find these tools at your local beauty supply store or online.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Once you have all your tools ready, you can start sectioning your hair. Use hair clips to separate your hair into different sections. This will make it easier to cut and thin out your hair.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using your regular scissors, start cutting your hair at the desired length. Be careful not to cut too much. You can always cut more later.

Step 5: Use Thinning Shears

Once you have cut your hair to the desired length, it's time to use the thinning shears. Thinning shears are designed to remove bulk from your hair without removing too much length. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, using the thinning shears to remove excess hair.

Step 6: Blend Your Hair

After using the thinning shears, it's time to blend your hair. Use your regular scissors to blend the hair that you have cut with the thinning shears. This will create a more natural look.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

Throughout the process, make sure to check your progress in a mirror. This will help you see if you need to cut more or if you have missed any spots.

Step 8: Repeat

Continue cutting and thinning out your hair until you have achieved the desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair at home:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly to avoid creating too many layers.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your hair too quickly can result in an uneven look.
  • Practice makes perfect. The more you thin out your hair, the easier it will become.

FAQ

Can I thin out my hair if it's already short?

Yes, you can still thin out your hair if it's already short. However, be careful not to cut too much at once.

Can thinning out my hair cause damage?

No, thinning out your hair should not cause any damage as long as you use the proper tools and techniques.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Typically, it's recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Regular trims and haircuts can also help maintain your hair's thickness and health.

Thinning out your hair at home can be a cost-effective and convenient way to achieve the desired look. Just make sure to use the right tools and techniques, and take your time to avoid any mistakes.
