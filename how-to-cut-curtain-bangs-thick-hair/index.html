How To Cut Curtain Bangs For Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curtain bangs have been trending for a couple of years now, and it seems like they are here to stay. They are versatile, low-maintenance, and can suit any face shape or hair type. However, cutting curtain bangs on thick hair can be a bit challenging, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut curtain bangs for thick hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curtain Bangs for Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curtain Bangs for Thick Hair
  • FAQs

How to Cut Curtain Bangs for Thick Hair

Cutting curtain bangs for thick hair requires precision and patience. You need to be careful not to cut too much hair or make the bangs uneven. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it with a towel. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair tends to shrink, and you might end up cutting more than you intended.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair by parting it down the middle. Use a comb to create a straight line from your forehead to the back of your head. Make sure the line is straight, and the hair on both sides is even.

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Take a small section of hair from the middle of your forehead. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Start with a small amount of hair and cut more if needed. Repeat the process on the other side, making sure the bangs are even.

Step 4: Texturize the Bangs

Texturizing the bangs will make them look less blunt and more natural. Use thinning shears to cut into the ends of the bangs. Cut at a slight angle to create a soft, feathered look. Be careful not to overdo it, as too much texturizing can make the hair look wispy.

Step 5: Style the Bangs

Style your bangs by combing them to the side. Use a round brush to blow-dry the bangs and create volume. You can also use a flat iron to curl the ends of the bangs for a more playful look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut curtain bangs for thick hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair by parting it down the middle.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your forehead.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across.
  5. Repeat the process on the other side.
  6. Texturize the bangs using thinning shears.
  7. Style the bangs by combing them to the side.
  8. Use a round brush to blow-dry the bangs and create volume.
  9. Use a flat iron to curl the ends of the bangs for a more playful look.
  10. Trim the bangs if needed, but be careful not to cut too much hair.

Tips for Cutting Curtain Bangs for Thick Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut curtain bangs for thick hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Texturize the bangs carefully to avoid making the hair look wispy.
  • Blow-dry the bangs with a round brush to create volume.
  • Use a flat iron to curl the ends of the bangs for a playful look.

FAQs

How often should I trim my curtain bangs?

You should trim your curtain bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and length.

Can I cut my own curtain bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own curtain bangs, but it's better to have a professional do it, especially if you're not experienced in cutting hair.

Can I style my curtain bangs differently?

Yes, you can style your curtain bangs differently. You can part them in the middle, comb them to the side, or pin them back.

Hair Care for Curtain Bangs

Curtain bangs are easy to maintain, but you still need to take care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it hydrated.
  • Use a hair serum to control frizz and add shine to your hair.

Cutting curtain bangs for thick hair requires skill and patience, but with the right tools and technique, you can do it yourself. Follow the steps and tips we've provided, and you'll have a stylish and low-maintenance haircut in no time.
