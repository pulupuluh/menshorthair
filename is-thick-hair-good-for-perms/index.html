Is Thick Hair Good For Perms

Written by Eveline Aug 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Perming is a popular hair treatment that can give you bouncy, curly, or wavy hair. If you have thick hair, you may be wondering if perming is a good option for you. The answer is: it depends. In this article, we'll explore whether thick hair is good for perms and share some tips for taking care of your hair after a perm.

Description

Perming is a chemical process that changes the natural structure of your hair. It involves applying a solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that give it its shape. Once the bonds are broken, your hair can be styled into curls or waves using curling rods or rollers. After the curls are set, a neutralizer is applied to your hair to re-form the broken bonds and set the new shape of your hair.

Thick hair is characterized by having a high density of hair follicles on your scalp. This means that you have more hair strands per square inch of your scalp than someone with fine hair. Thick hair can be coarse, frizzy, or hard to manage if not properly taken care of. Perming can be a good option for thick hair if you want to add some texture, volume, or shape to your hair.

How to Perm Thick Hair

If you have thick hair and want to perm it, here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Perm Solution

There are different types of perm solutions available, including alkaline, acid, and exothermic. Alkaline solutions are best for thick, coarse hair, as they can penetrate the hair shaft more easily. Acid solutions are best for fine, fragile hair, as they are gentler and less damaging. Exothermic solutions generate heat during the perming process, which can be beneficial for thick hair as it helps the solution to penetrate the hair shaft more effectively.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before perming your hair, make sure it is clean and free of any styling products. Do not wash your hair for at least 24 hours before perming, as this can make your hair more susceptible to damage. Comb your hair carefully to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 3: Apply the Perm Solution

Section your hair into small sections and apply the perm solution evenly using a perm rod or roller. Be careful not to apply too much solution, as this can cause your hair to become too curly or frizzy. Leave the solution on your hair for the recommended amount of time, depending on the type of solution you are using and the desired outcome.

Step 4: Rinse and Neutralize

After the perm solution has been on your hair for the recommended amount of time, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Apply the neutralizer to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time. This will re-form the broken bonds in your hair and set the new shape of your curls or waves.

Tips for Taking Care of Permed Hair

After you have permed your hair, it is important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a shampoo that is gentle and sulfate-free to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can cause your curls to become frizzy.

Tip 2: Condition Your Hair Regularly

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished. This will help to prevent breakage and split ends.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers, as they can damage your permed hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Tip 4: Get Regular Trims

Get your hair trimmed regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends. This will also help to maintain the shape of your curls or waves.

FAQ

Can I Perm My Hair If It Is Damaged?

No, it is not recommended to perm your hair if it is already damaged or fragile. Perming can further damage your hair and cause breakage, split ends, or hair loss.

How Long Will My Permed Hair Last?

Your permed hair will last for about 3-6 months, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of it. You may need to get touch-ups or re-perm your hair after this time.

Can I Color My Permed Hair?

Yes, you can color your permed hair, but it is recommended to wait at least two weeks after perming to avoid damaging your hair. Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to maintain the color and health of your hair.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Perming can be a fun and exciting way to add some texture and volume to your hair. If you have thick hair, perming can be a good option for you. Just remember to choose the right perm solution, prepare your hair properly, and take good care of it after the perm. Use gentle shampoos, condition your hair regularly, avoid heat styling, and get regular trims to keep your permed hair looking healthy and beautiful.
