How To Fade Your Own Hair With A Beard Trimmer A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a way to save money and time on haircuts, learning how to fade your own hair with a beard trimmer is a great skill to have. With just a few tools and some patience, you can achieve a professional-looking fade right from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll show you exactly how to do it.

Description

Fading your own hair with a beard trimmer can be a daunting task, but it's entirely possible with the right tools and technique. A fade is a popular haircut style that involves blending the hair from short to long, creating a seamless transition from one length to another. With a beard trimmer, you can achieve this look by trimming the hair in a gradual manner.

How to Fade Your Own Hair with a Beard Trimmer: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a beard trimmer with adjustable guards, a mirror, a comb, and a pair of scissors. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle of water to dampen your hair.

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size

Start by choosing the guard size that matches the length you want for the top of your hair. Attach the guard to your beard trimmer and make sure it's securely in place.

Step 3: Trim the Top of Your Hair

Using the beard trimmer, trim the top of your hair to the desired length. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, making sure to keep the trimmer level and even. Use the comb to lift the hair as you go for a more precise cut.

Step 4: Choose Your Next Guard Size

Once you've trimmed the top of your hair, it's time to move on to the sides. Choose a guard size that's one size shorter than the one you used for the top of your hair.

Step 5: Trim the Sides

Using the new guard size, trim the sides of your hair, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Use a sweeping motion to create a smooth blend between the top and sides of your hair. Repeat this step with a shorter guard size until you've achieved the desired fade.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline

Use the trimmer without a guard to clean up the neckline and create a defined shape. Start at the center and work your way outwards, using a light touch to avoid cutting too much hair.

Step 7: Blend the Top and Sides

Using scissors, blend the hair between the top and sides for a more natural look. Take small sections of hair and snip away any harsh lines or unevenness.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you've achieved your desired fade, style your hair as you normally would. Use a hair product like pomade or gel to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Fading Your Own Hair with a Beard Trimmer

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid overcutting or creating an uneven fade.
  • Use a light touch and avoid pressing too hard with the trimmer or scissors to prevent uneven cuts.
  • Follow the natural shape of your head and hairline for the most flattering look.
  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be discouraged if your first attempt isn't perfect.

FAQs

Can I fade my own hair without a beard trimmer?

While a beard trimmer is the easiest tool to use for fading your own hair, you can also achieve the look with scissors and clippers. However, it may take more time and practice to get the desired results.

What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic! You can always go back and fix it by blending the hair or cutting it shorter. If you're unsure about how to fix a mistake, it's best to seek the help of a professional barber.

How often should I fade my hair?

The frequency of your haircuts depends on your personal preference and hair type. Generally, fades need to be touched up every 2-4 weeks to maintain the look.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking its best, it's important to take care of it between haircuts. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat styling or harsh chemicals. Regular trims can also help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to fade your own hair with a beard trimmer like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and practice regularly to achieve the perfect fade. Happy cutting!
