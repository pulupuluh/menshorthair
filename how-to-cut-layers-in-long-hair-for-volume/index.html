How To Cut Layers In Long Hair For Volume

Written by Petter Apr 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you want to add volume to your long hair, cutting layers is one of the best ways to achieve it. Layers can create movement, texture, and volume in your hair, making it look fuller and more voluminous. In this blog post, we will show you how to cut layers in long hair for volume step by step.

How to Cut Layers in Long Hair for Volume

Before we start, it's important to note that cutting your hair at home can be risky, especially if you don't have experience. If you're not confident, we recommend going to a professional hairstylist to get your layers cut. However, if you're feeling brave and want to do it yourself, follow these steps carefully.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can stretch, making it difficult to cut evenly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by creating a part down the middle of your head, then divide each side into two sections.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut a straight line across. Repeat this process for all the sections of hair in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Next, move on to the middle layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the middle section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle, moving towards the ends of your hair. Repeat this process for all the sections of hair in the middle layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the top section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a steeper angle, moving towards the ends of your hair. Repeat this process for all the sections of hair in the top layer.

Tips

- Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.

- Take small sections of hair to ensure even layers.

- Cut less hair than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't undo a bad cut.

- Don't cut too much off at once. Gradual layering is key to achieving volume without sacrificing length.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my layers?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows, but we recommend getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut layers in curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, we recommend going to a hairstylist who specializes in curly hair to ensure a good cut.

Hair Care

Cutting layers can be a great way to add volume to your hair, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for hair care:

- Use a heat protectant before using hot tools on your hair.

- Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and hair dye.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

- Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

- Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.

With these tips and tricks, you can cut layers in long hair for volume and keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Happy cutting!
