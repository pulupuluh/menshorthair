How To Updo Hairstyles For Medium Length Hair

Do you have medium length hair and struggle to find new ways to style it? Updos can be a great option for any occasion, whether it's a formal event or just a casual day out. In this article, we'll go over how to create updo hairstyles for medium length hair in a relaxed and easy-to-follow language. We'll also provide tips, step-by-step instructions, and answer some commonly asked questions about updos. Let's get started!

How to Create Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Before we start with the specific updo hairstyles, let's go over some basic steps you should follow to create any updo:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Brush your hair thoroughly to remove any knots or tangles.
  3. Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.
  4. Decide on the type of updo you want to create and gather the necessary tools and accessories, such as bobby pins, hair ties, and hair clips.
  5. Take your time and be patient. Updos require some practice, but once you get the hang of it, they can be done quickly and easily.

1. Messy Bun

The messy bun is an effortless and chic updo that's perfect for a casual day out or a lazy day at home. Here's how to create it:

  1. Gather your hair at the back of your head and twist it into a loose bun.
  2. Secure the bun with bobby pins or a hair tie.
  3. Pull out a few strands of hair around your face to create a soft and romantic look.
  4. Spray some hairspray to hold the style in place.

2. Half-Up Half-Down

The half-up half-down hairstyle is a versatile and classic updo that works for any occasion. Here's how to create it:

  1. Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and tie it with a hair tie.
  2. Smooth out any bumps or flyaways with a comb.
  3. Curl the loose hair with a curling iron or wand.
  4. Tease the tied section of hair to add volume and texture.
  5. Wrap the tied section of hair around the hair tie to hide it.
  6. Secure the wrapped hair with bobby pins.
  7. Spray some hairspray to hold the style in place.

3. French Twist

The French twist is a sophisticated and elegant updo that's perfect for a formal event. Here's how to create it:

  1. Gather your hair at the nape of your neck and twist it upwards.
  2. Secure the twist with bobby pins, leaving some hair loose at the top.
  3. Take the loose hair and tuck it into the twist, creating a rolled effect.
  4. Secure the rolled hair with bobby pins.
  5. Spray some hairspray to hold the style in place.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to create updo hairstyles for medium length hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Brush your hair thoroughly to remove any knots or tangles.
  3. Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.
  4. Decide on the type of updo you want to create and gather the necessary tools and accessories, such as bobby pins, hair ties, and hair clips.
  5. Follow the specific steps for the updo you want to create (messy bun, half-up half-down, or French twist).
  6. Adjust the updo as needed, adding or removing hair to create the desired effect.
  7. Spray some hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you create the perfect updo:

  • Experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.
  • Don't be afraid to use hair extensions if you want to add length or volume to your hair.
  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to create a seamless look.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't get discouraged if your first few attempts don't turn out as expected.

FAQs

Q: Can I create updo hairstyles for medium length hair if my hair is thin?

A: Yes, you can. Use volumizing products and tease your hair to add texture and volume. You can also use hair extensions to add thickness and length.

Q: Can I create updo hairstyles for medium length hair if my hair is curly?

A: Yes, you can. Follow the same steps as for straight hair, but you may need to use more bobby pins to secure your hair in place.

Q: Can I create updo hairstyles for medium length hair if I have bangs?

A: Yes, you can. You can either incorporate your bangs into the updo or leave them out for a more natural look.

Hair Care for Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair before and after creating an updo:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or other styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Don't use elastic hair ties, as they can damage your hair. Use fabric hair ties instead.

Now that you know how to create updo hairstyles for medium length hair, you can experiment with different styles and accessories to create the perfect look for any occasion. Remember to be patient and practice, and don't be afraid to try new things. Happy styling!
