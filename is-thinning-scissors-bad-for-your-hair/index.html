Is Thinning Scissors Bad For Your Hair

Written by Eveline Aug 14, 2023 · 9 min read

Thinning scissors are a popular tool used by hairdressers to create a textured and layered look in hair. They are designed to remove bulk from thick hair or to blend layers in finer hair types. However, there is a lot of debate surrounding the use of thinning scissors and whether or not they are harmful to your hair. In this article, we will explore this topic in-depth and provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Use Thinning Scissors
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Thinning Scissors
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have a serrated edge on one or both of the blades. They are designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some behind, creating a textured and layered look. Thinning scissors are commonly used by hairdressers to remove bulk from thick hair or to blend layers in finer hair types. They can be a useful tool for achieving a specific look, but they can also cause damage to your hair if used incorrectly.

How to Use Thinning Scissors

Using thinning scissors correctly requires a certain level of skill and knowledge. If you are not a professional hairdresser, it is recommended that you do not attempt to use thinning scissors on your hair. However, if you are a hairdresser or have received training on how to use thinning scissors, here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Scissors

There are many different types of thinning scissors available, each with their own unique features. It is important to choose the right scissors for the job. Look for scissors that have a comfortable grip and a serrated edge that is appropriate for the type of hair you will be cutting.

Step 2: Prepare the Hair

Before using thinning scissors, it is important to prepare the hair. This involves washing and drying the hair thoroughly and combing out any tangles or knots. It is also a good idea to section the hair to make it easier to work with.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

To use thinning scissors, hold the hair in your non-dominant hand and the scissors in your dominant hand. Place the serrated edge of the scissors against the hair and make small, swift cuts. It is important to work slowly and carefully, making sure not to cut too much hair at once.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide to using thinning scissors:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that the hair is clean and free from any product buildup.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Section the hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 3: Use the Thinning Scissors

Hold the hair in your non-dominant hand and the scissors in your dominant hand. Place the serrated edge of the scissors against the hair and make small, swift cuts. Work slowly and carefully, making sure not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After you have used the thinning scissors, blend the hair by combing it out and checking for any uneven sections. Use regular scissors to trim any sections that need adjusting.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips for using thinning scissors:

  • Only use thinning scissors on healthy hair that is free from damage.
  • Work slowly and carefully, making small cuts to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use the right type of thinning scissors for the job.
  • Section the hair before cutting to make it easier to work with.
  • Blend the hair after using thinning scissors to ensure a natural look.

FAQ

Q: Can thinning scissors damage your hair?

A: Thinning scissors can cause damage to your hair if used incorrectly. It is important to work slowly and carefully, making sure not to cut too much hair at once.

Q: Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

A: Thinning scissors are best suited for thick hair or to blend layers in finer hair types. They may not be suitable for all hair types.

Q: Should I attempt to use thinning scissors on my own hair?

A: If you are not a professional hairdresser, it is not recommended that you attempt to use thinning scissors on your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips for caring for your hair after using thinning scissors:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dye or bleach.

Overall, thinning scissors can be a useful tool for achieving a specific look, but they can also cause damage to your hair if used incorrectly. If you are not a professional hairdresser, it is recommended that you do not attempt to use thinning scissors on your hair. If you do decide to use them, make sure to follow the steps outlined in this article and be sure to take good care of your hair afterwards.
