What Kind Of Bangs Are Best For My Face Shape

Are you thinking of getting bangs, but not sure which style would suit your face shape? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the different types of bangs that work best for your face shape, and give you some tips on how to style and care for them.

Face Shapes

Before we dive into the different types of bangs, it's important to determine your face shape. There are six main face shapes: round, square, oval, heart, diamond, and long.

Round Face: Round faces have soft features and a circular shape. The width and length of the face are almost equal.

Square Face: Square faces have a strong jawline and a square-shaped forehead. The width and length of the face are similar.

Oval Face: Oval faces have balanced features and a slightly rounded hairline. The face is longer than it is wide.

Heart Face: Heart faces have a broad forehead and a narrow chin. The face is widest at the temples.

Diamond Face: Diamond faces have a narrow forehead and chin, with wide cheekbones. The face is angular and pointed at the chin.

Long Face: Long faces have a high forehead and a long, narrow shape.

Bangs Styles

Now that you've determined your face shape, let's take a look at the different types of bangs that work best for each shape.

Round Face

For round faces, side-swept bangs are the best option. They help elongate the face and create a more oval shape. Avoid blunt or straight-cut bangs, as they can make the face appear rounder.

Square Face

Soft, wispy bangs work best for square faces. They help soften the strong jawline and create a more rounded shape. Avoid straight-cut or blunt bangs, as they can make the face appear more angular.

Oval Face

Oval faces can pull off almost any type of bangs, from blunt to side-swept to wispy. The key is to choose a style that complements your features and doesn't overwhelm your face.

Heart Face

Side-swept bangs or long, layered bangs work best for heart-shaped faces. They help balance out the broad forehead and narrow chin, creating a more harmonious look. Avoid blunt or heavy bangs, as they can make the forehead appear even wider.

Diamond Face

Side-swept bangs or long, layered bangs work well for diamond faces. They help soften the angular cheekbones and create a more balanced shape. Avoid blunt or heavy bangs, as they can make the face appear more angular.

Long Face

Long, wispy bangs work best for long faces. They help shorten the face and create a more balanced shape. Avoid heavy or blunt bangs, as they can make the face appear longer.

How to Style Your Bangs

Now that you've chosen the perfect bangs for your face shape, it's time to style them. Here are some tips to help you achieve the look you want:

  • Blow dry your bangs in the direction you want them to fall. Use a round brush to add volume and shape.
  • Use a flat iron to straighten your bangs, or a curling iron to add a slight wave or curl.
  • Apply a small amount of styling product, such as hair wax or pomade, to keep your bangs in place.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

Proper hair care is important to keep your bangs looking their best. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy, beautiful bangs:

  • Wash your bangs every 2-3 days, or as needed. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain their shape.

FAQs

Q: Can I pull off bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still rock bangs if you have curly hair. However, you may need to choose a style that works well with your hair's natural texture, such as soft, side-swept bangs or long, layered bangs.

Q: Can bangs make my face look slimmer?

Yes, the right type of bangs can help create a more elongated, slimming effect on the face. Side-swept bangs are particularly effective for this.

Q: How do I know if bangs are right for me?

If you're not sure whether bangs are right for you, try clipping in a temporary set of bangs to see how they look. You can also consult with a hairstylist for advice on which type of bangs would work best for your face shape and hair type.

Now that you know which type of bangs are best for your face shape, it's time to book that appointment with your hairstylist. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and lengths until you find the perfect look for you.
