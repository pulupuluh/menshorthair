How To Do Face Framing Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without making a drastic change, face framing bangs are a great option. Not only do they add some dimension and movement to your hair, but they're also a low-maintenance way to switch things up. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to do face framing bangs step-by-step.

Description

Face framing bangs are a type of bangs that are cut to frame the face. They're usually shorter on the sides and longer in the middle, creating a flattering, face-framing effect. Face framing bangs can be customized to suit any face shape, hair type, and personal style, making them a versatile option for any hair type.

How to

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use the fine-tooth comb to create a part down the middle of your head, then comb your hair forward. Use the comb to section off the hair you want to cut, making sure to include the hair around your face.

Step 2: Wet Your Hair

Once you've sectioned off your hair, use the spray bottle to wet it. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get a clean, even cut.

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Using your hair-cutting scissors, start cutting your bangs. Begin by cutting the hair in the middle of your forehead. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle and working your way outwards. Make sure to keep the scissors parallel to the hairline, and don't cut too much at once.

As you work your way towards the sides of your face, gradually increase the length of the hair you're cutting. This will create a more natural-looking, face-framing effect.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your bangs, take a step back and check your work. Make sure the length is even and that the bangs are framing your face the way you want them to.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Now that you've cut your bangs, it's time to style them. You can blow dry them straight, or use a curling iron to create some waves. You can also use a bit of styling product to help keep them in place.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting and styling your face framing bangs:

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't put hair back on once it's been cut.
  • Take your time and be patient. Don't rush the process, or you may end up with uneven bangs.
  • When styling your bangs, use a heat protectant product to prevent damage from heat styling tools.
  • Consider using a round brush when blow drying your bangs. This will help create a smooth, polished look.

FAQ

What face shape is best for face framing bangs?

Face framing bangs can work for any face shape, but they're particularly flattering on oval and heart-shaped faces. If you have a round face, consider cutting your bangs slightly longer to create a more slimming effect.

Can I cut my own face framing bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own face framing bangs. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time. If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your face framing bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it looking its best.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to create face framing bangs that flatter your face shape and suit your personal style. So go ahead and give it a try!
