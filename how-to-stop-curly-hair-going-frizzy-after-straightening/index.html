How To Stop Curly Hair Going Frizzy After Straightening

Written by Luffy May 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be when you want to switch up your look and straighten it, only to have it turn into a frizzy mess. Luckily, there are steps you can take to prevent this from happening. In this article, we'll go over how to stop curly hair going frizzy after straightening, including tips and tricks for maintaining your hair's health and texture.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The key to preventing curly hair from going frizzy after straightening is to properly prepare your hair before straightening it. This means using the right products and tools, and taking care not to damage your hair.

Here are the steps you can take to stop your curly hair from going frizzy after straightening:

Step by Step

Step 1: Cleanse and Condition

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it more prone to frizz. Instead, look for a gentle, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that will help to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, it's important to protect it from heat damage. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking healthy.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to help prevent frizz. Use a low heat setting and work in small sections, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a flat iron to straighten it. Again, work in small sections and use a low heat setting to prevent damage. Be sure to use a comb or brush to help guide the hair through the flat iron, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Step 5: Finish with a Serum or Oil

Once you've finished straightening your hair, apply a small amount of serum or oil to the ends to help prevent frizz and add shine.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent curly hair from going frizzy after straightening:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before blow-drying and straightening.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils from your hands to your hair and make it more oily and prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's already frizzy?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your hair if it's already frizzy, as this can make it look even more damaged and dry. Instead, focus on nourishing your hair with a deep conditioning treatment and using products that will help to tame frizz.

Q: How often should I straighten my curly hair?

A: It's best to limit how often you straighten your curly hair, as too much heat can cause damage and make it more prone to frizz. Try to limit straightening to once a week, or less if possible.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent frizz and keep it looking healthy. This means using gentle, nourishing products that are free of harsh chemicals, and avoiding heat styling as much as possible. Additionally, be sure to get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent curly hair from going frizzy after straightening and maintain your hair's health and texture.
