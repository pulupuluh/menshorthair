Best Bangs For Over 60 A Guide For Relaxed Hair

If you're over 60 and looking to change up your hairstyle, adding bangs can be a great option. However, choosing the right type of bangs can be challenging, especially if you have relaxed hair. In this guide, we'll discuss the best bangs for over 60 with relaxed hair, how to style them, and tips on maintaining them.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Best Bangs for Over 60
  • Step by Step Guide on Styling Your Bangs
  • Tips on Maintaining Your Bangs

  • Hair Care Tips for Relaxed Hair

Description

Bangs can be a great way to add a youthful and fresh look to your hairstyle. However, not all bangs are created equal, and some types may not be suitable for those with relaxed hair. The best bangs for over 60 with relaxed hair are those that are easy to maintain, complement your face shape, and work well with your hair texture. In this guide, we'll help you choose the best bangs for your hair type and face shape.

How to Choose the Best Bangs for Over 60

Choosing the best bangs for over 60 with relaxed hair requires some consideration. Here are some factors to consider when choosing your bangs:

Face Shape

The shape of your face can determine which type of bangs will suit you best. For example, if you have a round face, you might want to choose side-swept bangs to add length to your face. If you have a heart-shaped face, you might want to try blunt, straight-across bangs to balance out your features.

Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also affect which type of bangs will work well for you. If you have fine hair, you might want to avoid thick, heavy bangs that can weigh down your hair. Instead, try wispy, textured bangs. If you have thick hair, you might want to try blunt, straight-across bangs that will create a clean, polished look.

Personal Style

Your personal style is also an important factor to consider when choosing your bangs. If you prefer a classic, timeless look, you might want to try blunt, straight-across bangs. If you're more adventurous and like to experiment with your hair, you might want to try choppy, textured bangs.

Step by Step Guide on Styling Your Bangs

Styling your bangs can be easy with the right techniques. Here's a step by step guide on how to style your bangs:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before styling your bangs, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.

Step 2: Section Off Your Bangs

Section off your bangs from the rest of your hair by using a comb or your fingers. Make sure your bangs are damp before styling them.

Step 3: Style Your Bangs

Depending on the type of bangs you have, you can style them in different ways. For example, if you have side-swept bangs, you can use a round brush and a hair dryer to create volume and curl. If you have blunt, straight-across bangs, you can use a flat iron to create a sleek and polished look.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've styled your bangs, finish with a light mist of hairspray to keep them in place.

Tips on Maintaining Your Bangs

Maintaining your bangs can be easy with these tips:

Trim Your Bangs Regularly

To keep your bangs looking their best, trim them every 4-6 weeks. This will prevent them from getting too long and falling in your eyes.

Wash Your Bangs Separately

If you have oily skin, you might want to wash your bangs separately from the rest of your hair. This will prevent them from getting greasy and flat.

Blow Dry Your Bangs Every Day

Blow drying your bangs every day will help them stay in place and maintain their shape.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best bangs for over 60 with relaxed hair?

The best bangs for over 60 with relaxed hair are those that are easy to maintain, complement your face shape, and work well with your hair texture. Some examples include side-swept bangs, wispy bangs, and blunt, straight-across bangs.

How often should I trim my bangs?

You should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking their best.

Can I style my bangs with a flat iron?

Yes, you can use a flat iron to style blunt, straight-across bangs for a sleek and polished look.

Hair Care Tips for Relaxed Hair

Relaxed hair requires special care to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips on caring for your relaxed hair:

Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for relaxed hair. Look for products that are moisturizing and nourishing.

Deep Condition Your Hair Regularly

Deep conditioning your hair once a week will help keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Use a deep conditioner that is designed for relaxed hair.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to relaxed hair. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

Protect Your Hair at Night

Wear a silk or satin scarf or bonnet at night to protect your hair from breakage and damage.

With these tips, you can choose the best bangs for over 60 with relaxed hair and maintain them easily. Remember to care for your hair properly to keep it healthy and strong.
