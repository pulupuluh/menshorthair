How To Give A Bald Fade Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to give a bald fade, you've come to the right place. In this post, we will walk you through the process of giving a bald fade, step-by-step. This guide will include tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you achieve a flawless bald fade. Let's get started!

What is a Bald Fade?

A bald fade is a popular haircut style that involves shaving the hair very short on the sides and back of the head, gradually blending into longer hair on top. The end result is a smooth, seamless transition from short to long hair. This style is perfect for men who want a low-maintenance, clean-cut look.

How to Give a Bald Fade: Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to give a bald fade:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You will need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Trimmer
  • Cape
  • Brush
  • Clipper guards
  • Shears

Make sure your tools are clean and well-maintained before you begin.

Step 2: Determine the Length of the Fade

Decide how short you want the hair to be on the sides and back of the head. This will determine the length of the fade. Use a clipper guard to achieve the desired length, starting with a longer guard and gradually going shorter as you move down the head.

Step 3: Create a Guideline

Using your clippers, create a guideline around the perimeter of the head. This will help you achieve a clean, even cut.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Using your clipper without a guard, blend the hair around the guideline. Make sure to use a light touch and move in a circular motion to create a seamless transition from short to long hair.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Using your scissors or shears, cut the hair on top of the head to the desired length. Use a comb to section the hair and make sure it is even.

Step 6: Blend the Top

Using your trimmer, create a seamless blend between the top of the head and the sides and back. Make sure to use a light touch and move in a circular motion to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 7: Clean up the Edges

Using your trimmer or shears, clean up any stray hairs around the edges of the haircut. Make sure to use a light touch and take your time to achieve a clean, polished look.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Use a brush or comb to style the hair on top of the head. You can use a little bit of pomade or gel to add some hold and shine.

Step 9: Clean Up

Use a brush to clean up any loose hair on the client's neck and shoulders. Remove the cape and wipe down the client's neck with a towel.

Step 10: Hair Care

Advise your client on hair care techniques to maintain their new bald fade. This includes regular trims, washing and conditioning the hair, and using hair products that are appropriate for their hair type.

Tips for Giving a Bald Fade

  • Take your time and use a light touch to avoid any harsh lines or mistakes.
  • Communicate with your client to ensure you are giving them the desired haircut.
  • Practice, practice, practice! The more you give bald fades, the better you will become.
  • Invest in high-quality tools to achieve the best results.

FAQs

What is the difference between a bald fade and a skin fade?

A bald fade involves shaving the hair very short on the sides and back of the head, gradually blending into longer hair on top. A skin fade involves shaving the hair down to the skin, creating a very short, seamless transition from hair to skin.

How often should I get a bald fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade starts to grow out. Generally, you should get a bald fade every 2-3 weeks to maintain the look.

Can I give myself a bald fade?

It is not recommended to give yourself a bald fade, as it can be difficult to achieve an even, seamless look without the help of a professional.

Hair Care for Bald Fades

To maintain your bald fade, it is important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use hair products that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to give a flawless bald fade every time. Practice makes perfect, so keep honing your skills and you'll become a pro in no time!
