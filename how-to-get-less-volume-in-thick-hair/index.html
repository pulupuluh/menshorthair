How To Get Less Volume In Thick Hair

Written by Luffy Mar 02, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have thick hair, you may find that it has a tendency to become voluminous and difficult to manage. While some people love the look of voluminous hair, it can be a struggle to tame it on a daily basis. Fortunately, there are a number of techniques you can use to get less volume in your thick hair. In this article, we'll explore some of the most effective tips and tricks for achieving sleek, manageable hair.

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's great to have a full head of hair, it can be difficult to manage and style. One of the biggest challenges that people with thick hair face is managing volume. If you're tired of your hair looking big and puffy, there are a few things you can do to get less volume and more control.

How to

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

When you have thick hair, it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed to help reduce volume. Look for products that are specifically formulated for thick or coarse hair, and avoid anything that promises to add volume or body. You may also want to consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

One of the easiest ways to reduce volume in your hair is to blow dry it upside down. This technique helps to smooth the hair cuticle and prevent frizz, which can contribute to volume. To do this, flip your head over and blow dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

3. Use a Flat Iron

If you have thick hair, a flat iron can be your best friend. Using a flat iron to straighten your hair can help to reduce volume and create a sleek, polished look. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and work in small sections to ensure that you get every strand of hair.

4. Don't Overload Your Hair with Products

While it can be tempting to use a lot of products to control your hair, this can actually make the problem worse. When you have thick hair, it's important to use products sparingly to avoid weighing down your hair and making it look greasy. Stick to a lightweight serum or oil to help smooth and tame your hair without adding extra volume.

5. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts are essential for managing thick hair. If you let your hair grow too long, it can become heavy and difficult to manage, which can lead to more volume. A stylist can help you choose a cut that works with your hair type and face shape, and can also thin out your hair to help reduce volume.

Step by Step

1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair

Before you begin styling your hair, make sure it's clean and towel-dried. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for thick hair to help reduce volume.

2. Apply a heat protectant spray

To protect your hair from damage, apply a heat protectant spray before blow drying or using a flat iron.

3. Blow dry your hair upside down

Flip your head over and blow dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends. Use a round brush to help smooth your hair and prevent frizz.

4. Use a flat iron

Once your hair is dry, use a flat iron to straighten your hair in small sections. Use a heat protectant spray and work slowly to avoid damaging your hair.

5. Finish with a lightweight serum or oil

To help tame any remaining frizz and add shine, apply a small amount of lightweight serum or oil to your hair.

Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before blow drying or using a flat iron
  • Avoid using a brush on wet hair, as this can cause breakage
  • Try sleeping with your hair in a loose braid to help prevent tangling and reduce volume
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage to your hair

FAQ

What causes hair to be voluminous?

There are a number of factors that can contribute to voluminous hair, including genetics, humidity, and the use of certain hair products.

Can thinning hair reduce volume?

Yes, thinning out your hair can help to reduce volume and make it more manageable. This can be done by a professional stylist or with thinning shears at home.

Can diet affect hair volume?

Yes, a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair and reduce volume. Eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables can help to keep your hair looking its best.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for keeping it healthy and reducing volume. Be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for thick hair, and avoid overloading your hair with products. Regular haircuts can also help to keep your hair looking sleek and manageable.

With these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to voluminous hair and hello to sleek, manageable locks!
