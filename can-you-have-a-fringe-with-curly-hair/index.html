Can You Have A Fringe With Curly Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you might have wondered whether you can pull off a fringe. The answer is yes, you absolutely can! A fringe can add a stylish touch to your curls and frame your face beautifully. In this article, we'll show you how to rock a curly fringe with confidence.

Description

A curly fringe, also known as bangs, is a section of hair that falls over the forehead. It can be cut straight across or at an angle, depending on the desired look. Fringes can be short and choppy or long and wispy. They're a great way to change up your hairstyle without making a drastic cut.

How to

If you're thinking about getting a fringe, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure you choose a style that complements your face shape. A side-swept fringe is a versatile option that works well with most face shapes. For curly hair, a longer fringe is usually better than a short one, as it will blend in better with your curls.

When it comes to cutting your fringe, it's best to leave it to a professional stylist. They'll be able to assess your hair type and create a fringe that works with your curls. If you do decide to cut your own fringe, make sure you have the right tools and take it slow. You can always trim it shorter later on, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut!

Styling your curly fringe is easy. Apply a volumizing mousse or curl-enhancing cream to your damp hair, scrunching it gently to encourage your curls. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your fringe, holding it in place with your fingers as you go. Once your fringe is dry, you can use a small amount of hair oil or serum to tame any frizz.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to creating a curly fringe:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product to your damp hair.
  3. Part your hair where you want your fringe to begin.
  4. Section off a small amount of hair where you want your fringe to end.
  5. Comb through the section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers.
  6. Cut the hair straight across or at an angle, depending on the desired look.
  7. Release the section of hair and let it fall naturally.
  8. Style your fringe using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.
  9. Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to tame any frizz.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling your curly fringe:

  • Don't cut your fringe too short, as it may look uneven when it dries.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to prevent your curls from getting too frizzy.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to your fringe to keep it looking smooth and shiny.
  • Experiment with different styles, such as a side-swept fringe or a longer, wispy fringe.
  • Don't be afraid to ask your stylist for advice on how to style your curly fringe.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curly fringes:

Can you have a fringe with naturally curly hair?

Yes, you can! A curly fringe can add a stylish touch to your curls and frame your face beautifully.

What type of fringe is best for curly hair?

A longer fringe is usually better than a short one, as it will blend in better with your curls. A side-swept fringe is a versatile option that works well with most face shapes.

How do you style a curly fringe?

Apply a volumizing mousse or curl-enhancing cream to your damp hair, scrunching it gently to encourage your curls. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your fringe, holding it in place with your fingers as you go. Once your fringe is dry, you can use a small amount of hair oil or serum to tame any frizz.

Hair Care

When it comes to caring for your curly fringe, it's important to use products that are specially formulated for curly hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, and argan oil. These ingredients will help to moisturize your hair and define your curls.

It's also important to avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and lead to breakage. If you do need to use a hair dryer or straightener, make sure you use a heat protectant spray first.

Finally, make sure you get regular trims to keep your curly fringe looking its best. A trim every 6-8 weeks will help to prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.
