How To Stop Hair Dye From Coming Out In Shower

Written by Bardi May 13, 2023 · 8 min read

If you've ever colored your hair, you know how frustrating it can be when the color starts to fade. One of the most common reasons for hair dye to come out in the shower is because of the water temperature. Hot water opens up the hair cuticles, which can cause the color to wash out more quickly. However, there are ways to prevent this from happening. In this article, we will discuss how to stop hair dye from coming out in the shower.

Description

When you dye your hair, you want the color to last as long as possible. However, this can be challenging when you shower every day. The water temperature, shampoo, and other factors can all contribute to the color fading faster than you'd like. In this article, we will share tips on how to keep your hair color vibrant and prevent it from washing out in the shower.

How to

1. Use Cold Water

One of the best ways to prevent hair dye from coming out in the shower is to use cold water. Cold water helps to close the hair cuticles, which locks in the color. You don't have to take a freezing cold shower, but try to avoid using hot water on your hair. Instead, use lukewarm or cool water to rinse your hair.

2. Use a Color-Protecting Shampoo

Another way to prevent hair dye from coming out in the shower is to use a color-protecting shampoo. These shampoos are formulated to help lock in the color and prevent it from fading. Look for shampoos that are designed specifically for colored hair, and avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip the color from your hair.

3. Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can cause the color to fade faster. Try to limit the number of times you wash your hair each week, and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh. When you do wash your hair, be gentle and avoid scrubbing your scalp too vigorously.

4. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to protect your hair color and prevent it from washing out in the shower. Apply the conditioner to your hair after washing it, and leave it in without rinsing. The conditioner will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent the color from fading.

Step by Step

Step 1: Use Cold Water

When you're ready to rinse your hair in the shower, turn the temperature down to cold or lukewarm. Avoid using hot water, as this can cause the color to fade more quickly.

Step 2: Use a Color-Protecting Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that is specifically designed for colored hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, which can help to protect the hair and prevent damage.

Step 3: Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Limit the number of times you wash your hair each week, and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

Step 4: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it, and leave it in without rinsing. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent the color from fading.

Tips

  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, as these can also cause the color to fade.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to chlorine or saltwater, as these can cause the color to fade more quickly.
  • Avoid using harsh hair products that contain alcohol or sulfates, as these can strip the color from your hair.

FAQ

How long does hair dye usually last?

Hair dye can last anywhere from 4-6 weeks, depending on the type of dye and how often you wash your hair.

Can I use regular shampoo on colored hair?

You can use regular shampoo on colored hair, but it's best to use a shampoo that is specifically designed for colored hair. Regular shampoos can strip the color from your hair more quickly.

What should I do if my hair color starts to fade?

If your hair color starts to fade, try using a color-protecting shampoo and conditioner, and avoid washing your hair too frequently. You can also use a hair gloss or toner to refresh the color.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential if you want your hair color to last. Be gentle when washing and styling your hair, and avoid using harsh products that can damage your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when brushing it. And remember to protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors that can cause damage.

By following these tips, you can help to prevent hair dye from coming out in the shower and keep your hair color looking vibrant and fresh.
