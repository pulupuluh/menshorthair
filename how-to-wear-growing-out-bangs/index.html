How To Wear Growing Out Bangs A Complete Guide

Are you growing out your bangs but don't know how to style them? Don't worry! We've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to wear growing out bangs in style. From hairstyles to hair care, we've got all the tips and tricks you need to know. So, let's get started!

How to Wear Growing Out Bangs

When it comes to growing out bangs, there are many ways to style them. Here are some popular options:

1. Side Sweep

The side sweep is a classic style that works great with growing out bangs. Simply part your hair to the side and sweep your bangs to the opposite side. You can use a bobby pin to secure them in place if needed.

2. Half Up, Half Down

The half up, half down style is another great option. Pull the top half of your hair back and secure it with a clip or elastic. This will keep your bangs out of your face while still leaving the rest of your hair down.

3. Braids

Braids are a fun and stylish way to wear growing out bangs. You can braid your bangs into a small braid and secure it with a bobby pin. Or, you can braid a small section of hair from each side of your head and pull them back into a half up, half down style.

4. Headbands

Headbands are a great accessory for growing out bangs. They can keep your bangs out of your face and add some style to your look. You can choose from a variety of styles, including thin, thick, braided, and embellished headbands.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wear growing out bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will keep your hair healthy and make it easier to style.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause damage and frizz.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and moisturize it. This will make it easier to style and prevent breakage.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and shape. Use the brush to pull your bangs to the side or back, depending on the style you want to achieve.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Now it's time to style your hair. Choose a style that works best for you, such as the side sweep, half up, half down, braids, or headbands. Use bobby pins or hair clips to secure your hair in place.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Finish your look with a light mist of hairspray to hold your hair in place. Be sure to choose a hairspray that won't weigh your hair down or make it look greasy.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you wear growing out bangs:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and breaking your hair.

2. Trim Your Bangs Regularly

To keep your bangs looking neat and tidy, trim them regularly. You can do this yourself or visit a hair salon for a professional trim.

3. Experiment with Accessories

Accessories like headbands, hair clips, and hats can add some extra style to your hair. Don't be afraid to experiment and try new things.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing out bangs:

1. How long does it take to grow out bangs?

It can take anywhere from 2 to 6 months to grow out bangs, depending on how fast your hair grows.

2. How do I keep my bangs out of my face?

You can use bobby pins, hair clips, headbands, or braids to keep your bangs out of your face.

3. How often should I trim my bangs?

You should trim your bangs every 4 to 6 weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that's gentle on your hair and scalp. Look for products that are free from sulfates, parabens, and fragrances.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause breakage. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling wands.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a UV-protective spray to shield your hair from the sun.

With these tips and tricks, you can wear growing out bangs with confidence and style. Whether you prefer a side sweep or a braided look, there's a style that's perfect for you. So, go ahead and experiment with different looks until you find the one that works best for you. Good luck!
