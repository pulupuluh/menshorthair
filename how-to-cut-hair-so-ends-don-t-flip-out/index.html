How To Cut Hair So Ends Don t Flip Out Tips And Tricks

How to Cut Hair So Ends Don't Flip Out | Tips and Tricks

If you have ever had a haircut where the ends of your hair flipped out, you know how frustrating it can be. The good news is that there are ways to prevent this from happening. In this article, we will show you how to cut hair so ends don't flip out. We'll cover everything from how to do it, step by step instructions, tips, FAQ, and hair care. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Hair So Ends Don't Flip Out
  2. Step by Step Instructions
  3. Tips for Cutting Hair
  4. FAQ

How to Cut Hair So Ends Don't Flip Out

To prevent your hair from flipping out after a haircut, you need to make sure that the ends are cut at the right angle. The technique is called point cutting. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. Leave the bottom section loose, and work your way up to the top section.

Step 3: Cut Small Sections of Hair

Take small sections of hair, and hold them between your fingers. Use a pair of scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle. This is the point cutting technique.

Step 4: Repeat

Repeat this process on each section of hair until you've cut all of your hair.

Step by Step Instructions

Here is a detailed step by step process to follow when cutting your hair to prevent the ends from flipping out:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Once you are done, dry your hair with a towel or blow dryer until it is completely dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into three parts. Start by creating a part in the middle of your head, running from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, make two more parts on either side of your head, dividing your hair into three sections.

Step 3: Cut the Hair at the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle using the point cutting technique.

Step 4: Cut the Hair on the Sides

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle using the point cutting technique.

Step 5: Cut the Hair on the Top

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle using the point cutting technique.

Tips for Cutting Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair to prevent the ends from flipping out:

  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts.
  • Comb your hair thoroughly before cutting to avoid tangles.
  • Take it slow, and cut small sections at a time.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will shrink when it dries.
  • If you're not confident, consider going to a professional hairdresser.

FAQ

What causes hair ends to flip out?

Hair ends can flip out when they are cut at a blunt angle. This can happen when the hair is cut too straight, or when the scissors are dull.

How often should you cut your hair?

You should cut your hair every 6 to 8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can you prevent hair ends from flipping out?

Yes, you can prevent hair ends from flipping out by cutting them at a slight angle using the point cutting technique.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and prevent split ends. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, like hair straighteners and curling irons.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as it can cause breakage.
  • Trim your hair every 6 to 8 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet, rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair health.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy, prevent split ends, and make sure that the ends don't flip out after a haircut.

Conclusion

By following the steps outlined in this article, you can prevent your hair ends from flipping out after a haircut. Remember to use the point cutting technique, cut small sections of hair at a time, and take care of your hair with regular trims and a healthy diet.
