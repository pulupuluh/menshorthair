How To Cut Layers In Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 10, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting layers in curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right technique and tools, it's possible to achieve a beautiful, bouncy look. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting layers in curly hair, from start to finish.

Description

Layers can add volume and movement to curly hair, but they need to be cut carefully to avoid creating a frizzy or uneven look. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you'll be able to create layers that enhance your natural curl pattern and give your hair a more polished appearance.

How to Cut Layers in Curly Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

To cut layers in curly hair, you'll need a few basic tools, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Small hair clips or elastics

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut curly hair when it's clean and dry, so wash and dry your hair before you begin. Once your hair is dry, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it and remove any knots.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair

Use your comb to divide your hair into small sections, starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. Use small hair clips or elastics to secure each section as you go.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Begin by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the ends of the hair at an angle, taking care not to cut off too much length. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way, working your way around the bottom layer of your hair.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, release the clips from the middle layer of your hair. Repeat the same process of cutting small sections of hair at an angle, taking care not to cut off too much length.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the clips from the top layer of your hair and repeat the same process of cutting small sections of hair at an angle.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers of your hair, use your comb to blend them together. This will help create a seamless, natural look.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much length.
  • Keep your scissors at an angle to create soft, natural-looking layers.
  • Remember to blend the layers together for a seamless look.

FAQ

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended that you get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your curly hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

It's possible to cut curly hair yourself, but it's recommended that you go to a professional stylist for best results.

Hair Care

To keep your curly hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to use the right products and techniques. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your curly hair, as this can cause frizz and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to help define your curls.

By following these tips and using the right technique, you can cut layers in curly hair with confidence and achieve a beautiful, bouncy look.
