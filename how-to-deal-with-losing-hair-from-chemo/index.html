How To Deal With Losing Hair From Chemo

Written by Eveline Apr 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you or a loved one is going through chemotherapy, one of the most difficult side effects to deal with is hair loss. Losing your hair can be a traumatic experience, but it's important to remember that it is only temporary. There are many ways to deal with hair loss from chemo, and we'll go over some of the most effective methods in this article.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step in dealing with hair loss from chemo is to accept that it is happening. This may be difficult, but it's important to remember that your hair will grow back once your treatment is over.

It's also important to talk to your doctor about your options. They may be able to prescribe medication that can help slow down or even prevent hair loss. They may also be able to recommend a wig or other hair replacement options.

Another option is to embrace the bald look. Many people find that once they shave their heads, they feel more in control of the situation. This can be a freeing experience, and can help you feel more confident during your treatment.

Step by Step

If you decide to shave your head, here are some steps you can follow:

  1. Start by trimming your hair with scissors or clippers. This will make the shaving process easier.
  2. Take a warm shower or bath to soften your hair.
  3. Apply shaving cream or gel to your head.
  4. Using a razor, shave your head in the direction of hair growth. Be sure to use a new razor, as a dull razor can cause irritation.
  5. Rinse your head with warm water, and apply a moisturizing lotion or oil to your scalp.

Tips

  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your scalp, such as hair dye or bleach.
  • Try using a satin pillowcase, as it is gentler on your hair and scalp.
  • Consider getting a wig or hairpiece, as they can help you feel more comfortable and confident.

FAQ

Will my hair grow back?

Yes, your hair will grow back once your treatment is over. It may take several months, but it will eventually grow back.

Is there anything I can do to prevent hair loss?

Your doctor may be able to prescribe medication that can help slow down or prevent hair loss. It's important to talk to your doctor about your options.

Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your scalp, such as hair dye or bleach. You can talk to your doctor about using gentle, natural hair dyes.

Hair Care

Once your hair starts to grow back, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dye or bleach.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers or flat irons.
  • Consider getting a haircut to help promote healthy hair growth.

Dealing with hair loss from chemo can be a difficult experience, but it's important to remember that it is only temporary. There are many ways to deal with hair loss, from shaving your head to embracing the bald look to getting a wig or hairpiece. It's important to talk to your doctor about your options, and to take care of your hair once it starts to grow back.
