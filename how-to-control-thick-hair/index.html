How To Control Thick Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by William Feb 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. On the one hand, you have a lot of volume and body to work with. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. But don't worry, with the right tips and tricks, you can learn how to control thick hair and make it work for you. In this article, we'll discuss some simple steps you can take to keep your thick hair under control.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Control Thick Hair

When it comes to controlling thick hair, the key is to find products and techniques that work for your hair type. Here are some tips to help:

Choose the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

When you have thick hair, it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type. Look for products that are labeled as "thickening" or "volumizing." These products will help to add body and texture to your hair, making it easier to manage and style.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When you have thick hair, it's important to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help to prevent breakage and damage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently combing through any knots or tangles.

Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

If you want to add volume to your hair, try blow drying it upside down. This will help to lift your roots and create more body and texture. Use a round brush to smooth out any frizz or flyaways.

Avoid Heavy Products

When you have thick hair, it can be tempting to use a lot of heavy products to tame it. However, this can actually weigh your hair down and make it more difficult to manage. Instead, opt for lightweight products that will add moisture and shine without weighing your hair down.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you control your thick hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a thickening or volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too hard.
  3. Apply a lightweight leave-in conditioner or detangler to help smooth out any knots or tangles.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. If you want to add volume, blow dry your hair upside down using a round brush.
  6. Finish with a lightweight styling product, such as a serum or oil, to add shine and control frizz.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you control your thick hair:

  • Get regular trims to help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often.
  • If you have curly or wavy hair, try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help define your curls.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids or updos, to find what works best for your hair type.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a brush instead of a comb on my thick hair?

A: It's generally best to use a wide-tooth comb on thick hair, as it's less likely to cause breakage and damage. However, if you prefer to use a brush, opt for a paddle brush or vent brush, which are less likely to snag or pull on your hair.

Q: Is it okay to wash my thick hair every day?

A: Washing your hair every day can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and damage. It's generally best to wash your hair every other day or every few days, depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

When it comes to caring for your thick hair, it's important to use products that are designed for your hair type. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are labeled as "thickening" or "volumizing," as these products will help to add body and texture to your hair. Additionally, it's important to avoid using heavy products that can weigh your hair down. Instead, opt for lightweight products that will add moisture and shine without making your hair feel greasy or weighed down.

By following these tips and tricks, you can learn how to control thick hair and make it work for you. With the right products and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy head of hair that you'll love showing off.
