How To Make Your Hair 50S Style

Written by Frank Apr 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to add some vintage flair to your hairstyle, a 50s style 'do is the perfect choice. This classic look is all about volume, curls, and sleekness, and it's surprisingly easy to achieve. In this article, we'll take you through the steps to create your own 50s style hairstyle, from choosing the right products to mastering the perfect waves.

Description

The 50s was an era of glamour and sophistication, and the hairstyles of the time reflected this. Women often wore their hair in voluminous, curled styles, with sleek, smooth finishes. This look was achieved with the help of a few key styling tools and products, including hot rollers, hairspray, and pomade.

How to

Step 1: Prep your hair

The key to a successful 50s style hairdo is to start with clean, dry hair. Apply a small amount of pomade or styling cream to your hair to add shine and texture.

Step 2: Add volume

Next, use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the tips. This will add volume and lift to your hair.

Step 3: Curl your hair

Once your hair is dry, it's time to start curling. Use hot rollers or a curling iron to create loose curls throughout your hair. Roll each section of hair around the roller or curling iron and hold it in place for a few seconds, then release.

Step 4: Brush out your curls

After your curls have cooled, use a soft-bristled brush to gently brush them out. This will create soft, bouncy waves that are typical of the 50s style.

Step 5: Smooth your hair

Use a small amount of pomade or styling cream to smooth down any flyaways or frizz. You can also use a flat iron to straighten any sections of hair that need it.

Step 6: Finish with hairspray

To set your style in place, give it a generous spritz of hairspray. This will keep your hair looking sleek and polished all day long.

Tips

- If you're short on time, you can skip the hot rollers and use a curling iron to create quick, easy waves.

- To add even more volume to your hair, tease the roots before you start curling.

- Experiment with different hair accessories, like headbands, scarves, or barrettes, to add some extra flair to your 50s style.

FAQ

Q: Can I create a 50s style hairstyle with short hair?

A: Yes! While longer hair is more typical of the 50s style, you can still create a retro look with a shorter cut. Try adding curls or waves to your hair with a curling iron, and use pomade to smooth down any flyaways.

Q: What kind of hairspray should I use?

A: Look for a strong-hold hairspray that will keep your hair in place all day. You can also try a hairspray that adds shine or texture to your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your 50s style hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy, and avoid using too much heat when styling. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to add moisture and shine to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create your own 50s style hairdo in no time. Whether you're going for a retro look or just want to add some volume and bounce to your hair, this classic style is sure to turn heads.
