How To Get Rid Of Head Lice In Long Thick Hair

Written by Christine Mar 19, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you're dealing with head lice in your long, thick hair, you're not alone. Head lice are a common problem, especially among children in school. These tiny insects can quickly spread and cause itching, discomfort, and embarrassment. But don't worry - getting rid of head lice in long thick hair is not as difficult as you might think. In this article, we'll discuss some effective ways to get rid of head lice and prevent them from coming back.

Description

Head lice are tiny wingless insects that live on the scalp and feed on blood. They are most commonly found in children, but anyone can get them. Head lice can be transmitted through direct contact with an infected person or by sharing personal items such as combs, brushes, hats, and headphones.

How to Get Rid of Head Lice in Long Thick Hair

There are several ways to get rid of head lice in long, thick hair. Here are some of the most effective methods:

1. Use a Lice Comb

One of the easiest and most effective ways to get rid of head lice is to use a lice comb. Start by washing your hair with a regular shampoo, then apply a generous amount of conditioner. Use a lice comb to comb through small sections of your hair, starting at the scalp and working your way down to the ends. Wipe the comb on a tissue or paper towel after each stroke to remove any lice or nits.

2. Apply Olive Oil

Olive oil is a natural remedy that can help suffocate and kill head lice. Apply a generous amount of olive oil to your hair, making sure to cover the scalp and all the way to the ends. Cover your hair with a shower cap and leave it on for at least 8 hours or overnight. Rinse your hair with warm water and comb through with a lice comb.

3. Use an Over-the-Counter Lice Treatment

There are several over-the-counter lice treatments available that can help get rid of head lice. Follow the instructions carefully and make sure to cover your scalp and all the way to the ends of your hair. After using the treatment, comb through your hair with a lice comb to remove any dead lice or nits.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions for getting rid of head lice in long, thick hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a regular shampoo. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Apply Conditioner

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, making sure to cover the scalp and all the way to the ends.

Step 3: Comb Through Your Hair

Use a lice comb to comb through small sections of your hair, starting at the scalp and working your way down to the ends. Wipe the comb on a tissue or paper towel after each stroke to remove any lice or nits.

Step 4: Apply Olive Oil

If you prefer to use olive oil, apply a generous amount to your hair, making sure to cover the scalp and all the way to the ends. Cover your hair with a shower cap and leave it on for at least 8 hours or overnight.

Step 5: Rinse and Comb

Rinse your hair with warm water and comb through with a lice comb to remove any dead lice or nits.

Step 6: Use an Over-the-Counter Lice Treatment

If you prefer to use an over-the-counter lice treatment, follow the instructions carefully and make sure to cover your scalp and all the way to the ends of your hair. After using the treatment, comb through your hair with a lice comb to remove any dead lice or nits.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get rid of head lice and prevent them from coming back:

Tip 1: Wash Personal Items

Wash all personal items such as combs, brushes, hats, and headphones in hot water and dry on high heat.

Tip 2: Vacuum Your Home

Vacuum your home thoroughly, paying special attention to areas where people sit or lie down.

Tip 3: Check for Lice Regularly

Check your hair and your family's hair regularly for head lice. Early detection can help prevent the spread of lice.

FAQ

Q: Can head lice jump or fly?

A: No, head lice cannot jump or fly. They can only crawl from one person to another.

Q: Can I get head lice from my pets?

A: No, head lice only affect humans. You cannot get head lice from your pets.

Q: Do I need to wash my bedding if I have head lice?

A: Yes, it's a good idea to wash your bedding in hot water and dry on high heat if you have head lice.

Hair Care

After getting rid of head lice, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent it from becoming dry and damaged. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo

Use a moisturizing shampoo to help keep your hair soft and hydrated.

Tip 2: Condition Regularly

Use a conditioner regularly to help detangle your hair and keep it smooth.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.

With these tips and methods, you can effectively get rid of head lice in long, thick hair and prevent them from coming back. Remember to check your hair regularly and take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
