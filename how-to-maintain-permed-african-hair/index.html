How To Maintain Permed African Hair Tips And Tricks

For those of us with permed African hair, maintaining our hair can be a bit tricky. However, with the right care and attention, it is possible to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. In this article, we'll be discussing some tips and tricks on how to maintain permed African hair.

How to Maintain Permed African Hair

Permed African hair requires a little extra care to maintain its texture and style. Here are some tips on how to maintain permed African hair:

1. Moisturize regularly

Permed African hair tends to be dry and fragile, so it's important to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfate can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair healthy.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat can damage permed African hair, so avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

4. Protect your hair at night

Use a satin or silk pillowcase to protect your hair from friction and breakage at night. You can also wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet.

5. Don't over-style

Permed African hair is already delicate, so avoid over-styling your hair. Too much manipulation can cause breakage and damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to maintain permed African hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Use a sulfate-free shampoo to wash your hair. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Apply conditioner

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and let it sit for at least 20 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil

Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.

Step 4: Style your hair

Style your hair using a wide-tooth comb, avoiding over-manipulation. Use a satin or silk scrunchie to tie your hair up at night.

Tips for Maintaining Permed African Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining permed African hair:

1. Trim your hair regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

2. Protect your hair from the sun

Wear a hat or headscarf to protect your hair from the sun's UV rays.

3. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like braids and weaves, as they can cause tension and breakage.

4. Use a protein treatment

Use a protein treatment once a month to strengthen your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about maintaining permed African hair:

1. Can I color my permed African hair?

Yes, but it's important to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye to avoid damaging your hair.

2. Can I swim with permed African hair?

Yes, but make sure to wear a swim cap and rinse your hair thoroughly with fresh water after swimming.

3. How often should I wash my permed African hair?

Wash your hair once a week to keep it clean and healthy.

Hair Care Products for Permed African Hair

Here are some hair care products that are great for permed African hair:

  • Shea Moisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl Enhancing Smoothie
  • Cantu Shea Butter Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream
  • Mielle Organics Mongongo Oil Thermal & Heat Protectant Spray
  • ORS Olive Oil Replenishing Conditioner
  • Creme of Nature Argan Oil Style & Shine Foaming Mousse

With these tips and tricks, you can maintain your permed African hair and keep it looking fabulous. Remember to moisturize regularly, avoid heat styling, and protect your hair at night. Happy styling!
