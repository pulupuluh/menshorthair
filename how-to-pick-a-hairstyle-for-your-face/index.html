Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking to switch things up? The first step is figuring out what hairstyle suits your face shape. In this article, we'll provide a complete guide on how to pick a hairstyle for your face. Here's what you'll learn:

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Picking a Hairstyle
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle
  • FAQs About Hairstyles for Different Face Shapes
  • Hair Care for Different Hairstyles

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before you can pick a hairstyle, you need to determine your face shape. Here are the most common face shapes:

  • Oval
  • Round
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Triangle

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror. Look at the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Here's a breakdown of each face shape:

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you have balanced features and a slightly rounded jawline. This face shape is versatile and can pull off most hairstyles.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you have full cheeks and a rounded chin. To elongate your face, choose hairstyles with height at the crown and volume on top. Avoid hairstyles that add width to your face.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you have a strong jawline and angular features. Choose hairstyles with softness and roundness to balance your face shape. Avoid straight and blunt haircuts that accentuate your square jawline.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you have a wide forehead and a narrow jawline. Choose hairstyles that add width to your jawline and balance your forehead. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your forehead.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you have a narrow forehead and jawline with wide cheekbones. Choose hairstyles that add width to your forehead and jawline. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your cheekbones.

Triangle Face Shape

If you have a triangle face shape, you have a narrow forehead and wide jawline. Choose hairstyles that add width to your forehead and balance your jawline. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your jawline.

Step-by-Step Guide to Picking a Hairstyle

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to pick a hairstyle. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Research hairstyles that suit your face shape. Look for inspiration in magazines, online, and on social media.
  2. Consider your hair type and lifestyle. Choose a hairstyle that's easy to maintain and fits your daily routine.
  3. Bring pictures of your desired hairstyle to your hairstylist. They can give you advice on whether the hairstyle will suit your face shape and hair type.
  4. Try on wigs or use virtual makeover tools to see how the hairstyle looks on you before committing to a cut.
  5. Communicate with your hairstylist about your preferences and concerns. They can make adjustments or suggest alternatives.
  6. After your haircut, ask your hairstylist for styling tips and product recommendations to maintain your new hairstyle.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a hairstyle:

  • Consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle when picking a hairstyle.
  • Bring pictures of your desired hairstyle to your hairstylist for guidance.
  • Try on wigs or use virtual makeover tools to see how the hairstyle looks on you before committing to a cut.
  • Communicate with your hairstylist about your preferences and concerns.
  • Consider the maintenance and upkeep required for the hairstyle.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what suits you best.

FAQs About Hairstyles for Different Face Shapes

Here are some frequently asked questions about hairstyles for different face shapes:

What hairstyle suits a round face?

Hairstyles that add height at the crown and volume on top elongate a round face. Avoid hairstyles that add width to your face.

What hairstyle suits a square face?

Hairstyles with softness and roundness balance a square face. Avoid straight and blunt haircuts that accentuate your square jawline.

What hairstyle suits a heart face?

Hairstyles that add width to your jawline and balance your forehead suit a heart face. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your forehead.

What hairstyle suits a diamond face?

Hairstyles that add width to your forehead and jawline balance a diamond face. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your cheekbones.

What hairstyle suits a triangle face?

Hairstyles that add width to your forehead and balance your jawline suit a triangle face. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to your jawline.

Hair Care for Different Hairstyles

Once you've picked a hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its shape and health. Here are some hair care tips for different hairstyles:

Short Hairstyles

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping natural oils.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Use a texturizing or volumizing product to add definition to your hairstyle.
  • Visit your hairstylist every 4-6 weeks for trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

Medium-Length Hairstyles

  • Wash your hair every 3-4 days to avoid stripping natural oils.
  • Use a hydrating conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Use a heat protectant before using hot tools to prevent damage.
  • Visit your hairstylist every 6-8 weeks for trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

Long Hairstyles

  • Wash your hair every 4-5 days to avoid stripping natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools frequently to prevent damage.
  • Visit your hairstylist every 8-12 weeks for trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and guidelines, you'll be able to pick a hairstyle that suits your face shape and lifestyle. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist and experiment with different hairstyles to find what suits you best. And don't forget to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape!
