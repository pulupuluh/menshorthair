Why Does The Back Of My Hair Feel Dry

Written by Christine Sep 13, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you experiencing dryness at the back of your hair? You're not alone as this is a common hair issue faced by many people. It can be frustrating and uncomfortable, but there are ways to address this problem. In this article, we'll discuss the reasons why the back of your hair feels dry and what you can do to fix it.

Reasons Why the Back of Your Hair Feels Dry

There are several reasons why the back of your hair feels dry:

1. Lack of Moisture

The back of your hair may be dry because it's not getting enough moisture. This can happen if you're not applying enough conditioner or not using a leave-in conditioner. Also, if you're not drinking enough water, your hair can become dry and brittle.

2. Heat Damage

If you use hot tools like a flat iron or blow dryer, the heat can cause damage to your hair strands. This can result in dryness, split ends, and breakage. Also, if you're not using a heat protectant before using hot tools, this can contribute to the dryness at the back of your hair.

3. Chemical Processing

If you've recently relaxed or permed your hair, the chemicals can cause damage and dryness. Chemical treatments can strip your hair of its natural oils and moisture, leaving it dry and brittle.

How to Moisturize the Back of Your Hair

The key to moisturizing the back of your hair is to use products that will add moisture to your hair. Here are some products that can help:

1. Conditioner

Use a moisturizing conditioner every time you wash your hair. Apply it to the back of your hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it off. This will help to add moisture to your hair.

2. Leave-in Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day. Apply it to the back of your hair after washing and conditioning your hair.

3. Hair Oil

Applying hair oil to the back of your hair can help to seal in moisture. Use a lightweight oil like argan oil or jojoba oil, and apply it sparingly to the back of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Moisturizing the Back of Your Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to moisturizing the back of your hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo. Make sure you rinse out all the shampoo before applying conditioner.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the back of your hair. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it off.

Step 3: Apply Leave-in Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to the back of your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Apply Hair Oil

Apply a small amount of hair oil to the back of your hair. Use your fingers to distribute the oil evenly throughout your hair.

Tips to Prevent Dryness at the Back of Your Hair

Here are some tips to prevent dryness at the back of your hair:

1. Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking plenty of water can help to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out. Aim to drink at least eight glasses of water a day.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

Before using hot tools on your hair, make sure you use a heat protectant. This will help to prevent heat damage and dryness.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to dryness at the back of your hair.

FAQs

1. Can overwashing cause dryness at the back of your hair?

Yes, overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and brittleness.

2. Can using too much hair oil cause greasiness at the back of your hair?

Yes, using too much hair oil can cause greasiness. Use it sparingly, and focus on applying it to the ends of your hair.

Hair Care Routine for Dry Hair

If you have dry hair, here's a hair care routine you can follow:

1. Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse out the conditioner thoroughly.

2. Apply Leave-in Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized throughout the day.

3. Use Hair Oil

Apply hair oil to the ends of your hair to seal in moisture.

4. Avoid Heat

Avoid using hot tools on your hair as much as possible. If you must use them, make sure you use a heat protectant.

5. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, the back of your hair may feel dry due to a lack of moisture, heat damage, or chemical processing. To moisturize the back of your hair, use products that will add moisture, like conditioner, leave-in conditioner, and hair oil. Follow a hair care routine for dry hair, and drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.
