How To Start Dreads On Short Straight Hair

Written by William Feb 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you ready to embark on a journey of self-expression and individuality? Starting dreadlocks on short straight hair can seem like a daunting task, but with the right technique and a little patience, you can achieve the look you desire. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads on short straight hair, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to keep your dreads healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads on Short Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreads
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreads

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start, make sure you have clean, dry hair that is free of any products or oils. Here's how to start dreads on short straight hair:

1. Section Your Hair

Using a rat-tail comb, section your hair into small, even squares or rectangles. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create. Use hair clips to keep the sections in place.

2. Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb each section of hair. Hold the section firmly and comb downwards towards the scalp, then back up again. Repeat this motion until the hair starts to tangle and form a dreadlock. Be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.

3. Roll Your Hair

Once you have backcombed each section, it's time to roll your hair between your palms to help the dreads form. Roll the hair back and forth in a twisting motion until it starts to tighten and hold its shape. You can also use a dread comb to help tighten the dreads and smooth out any bumps or lumps.

4. Secure Your Dreads

Once your dreads are formed, you can use small rubber bands or hair ties to secure the ends and prevent them from unraveling. Be sure to choose bands that won't damage your hair or break easily.

Tips for Starting Dreads

Starting dreadlocks can be a long process, so here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Be patient - it can take several months for your dreads to fully form.
  • Avoid using wax or heavy products, as they can cause buildup and prevent your dreads from tightening.
  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to keep your scalp and hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or harsh chemicals, as they can damage your hair and slow down the dreadlocking process.
  • Be gentle with your dreads - avoid pulling or tugging on them, as this can cause them to break or weaken.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads on short straight hair:

Can I start dreads on relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreads on relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for the dreads to form and you may need to be gentler with your hair to avoid breakage or damage.

Do I need to cut my hair to start dreads?

No, you don't need to cut your hair to start dreads. However, if your hair is very short, it may be more difficult to create dreads and you may need to wait until your hair grows longer.

Can I wash my hair with dreads?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreads. In fact, it's important to keep your scalp and hair clean to prevent buildup and maintain healthy dreads. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can dry out your hair and scalp.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have started your dreads, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for hair care for dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can cause buildup.
  • Be gentle with your dreads and avoid pulling or tugging on them.
  • Use a dread comb to help separate and maintain your dreads.
  • Avoid over-washing or over-brushing your dreads, as this can cause them to unravel or lose their shape.

Starting dreads on short straight hair may take time and patience, but the end result is worth it. With the right technique and hair care, you can achieve beautiful, healthy dreadlocks that reflect your unique style and personality. So go ahead, embrace your inner rebel and start your dread journey today!
