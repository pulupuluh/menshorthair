How To Cut Long Thick Hair Short A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Jun 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having long, thick hair that takes forever to manage? Do you want to switch things up and try a shorter hairstyle? Cutting long, thick hair short can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stunning look that suits your face shape and personal style. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting long thick hair short, answer some frequently asked questions, and provide some tips for maintaining your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Long Thick Hair Short
  2. Step-by-Step Instructions
  3. Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair Short
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Long Thick Hair Short

How to Cut Long Thick Hair Short

If you're wondering how to cut long thick hair short, the first step is deciding on the length you want. A short haircut can mean anything from a pixie cut to a shoulder-length bob, so it's important to have a clear idea of what you want before you begin.

Once you've decided on a length, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. If you're not confident in your cutting abilities, it may be a good idea to invest in a pair of hair-cutting shears or seek the help of a professional stylist.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have your tools ready, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps for cutting long thick hair short:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an even trim.
  2. Section off your hair into manageable parts. Use hair clips to keep the sections in place.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, use your scissors to cut your hair to the desired length. Work in small sections and be sure to cut straight across.
  4. Move to the sides of your head and repeat the same process, taking care to keep the length even on both sides.
  5. Finally, trim any flyaway hairs or uneven pieces, taking care not to overdo it.

Remember, it's always better to start with a longer length and work your way shorter gradually.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair Short

Cutting long thick hair short can be a bit tricky, so here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with dry, clean hair. This will make it easier to see the length you're cutting and ensure that you get an even trim.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • Work in small sections. This will make it easier to control the length and ensure that you get an even cut.
  • Take breaks. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so take frequent breaks to rest your hands and arms.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long thick hair short:

Is it better to cut long thick hair short when it's wet or dry?

It's generally best to cut long thick hair short when it's dry. Wet hair tends to stretch and can make it difficult to determine the true length you're cutting.

How short should I cut my hair?

The length you cut your hair depends on your personal style and face shape. If you're unsure, it's always best to consult with a professional stylist.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always take a break and come back to it later. Alternatively, you can seek the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting Long Thick Hair Short

Once you've successfully cut your long thick hair short, it's important to take care of your new 'do. Here are some tips for maintaining your short hair:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to successfully cut your long thick hair short and feel confident in your new hairstyle. Happy cutting!
