How To Get Back Normal Hair After Straightening

Written by Bardi Apr 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you love straightening your hair, you know that the process can be damaging to your locks. While straightening your hair may give you the sleek, shiny look you desire, it can also cause dryness, breakage, and split ends. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to get back your healthy hair after straightening. In this post, we'll discuss how to get back normal hair after straightening, including tips, tricks, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Back Normal Hair After Straightening
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Restoring Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

How to Get Back Normal Hair After Straightening

If you want to get back your normal hair after straightening, you should start by giving your hair a break from heat styling tools. This will allow your hair to recover and start to repair itself.

Next, you should focus on moisturizing and nourishing your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for damaged hair, and avoid using hot water when washing your hair. Instead, use lukewarm water to help retain moisture.

You can also use hair masks or deep conditioning treatments to help restore your hair's health. These products are designed to penetrate deep into your hair shaft and provide the nutrients your hair needs to grow strong and healthy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you get back your normal hair after straightening:

  1. Stop using heat styling tools for a while to give your hair a break.
  2. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for damaged hair.
  3. Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  4. Use lukewarm water to help retain moisture.
  5. Apply a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week.
  6. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or perms.
  7. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.
  8. Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  9. Get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  10. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Tips for Restoring Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you restore your hair after straightening:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons as much as possible.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction on your hair while you sleep.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to get back normal hair after straightening:

How long does it take for hair to recover after straightening?

It can take several months for your hair to fully recover after straightening. However, by following a healthy hair care routine, you can start to see results within a few weeks.

Can I still straighten my hair after it has recovered?

Yes, you can still straighten your hair after it has recovered. However, you should use heat styling tools sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray.

What can I do if my hair is still dry and damaged after following these tips?

If your hair is still dry and damaged, you may want to consult with a hair care professional. They can recommend products and treatments that are specifically designed for your hair type and condition.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair only when necessary to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove product buildup.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip it of moisture.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and keep it moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

By following these tips and giving your hair the care it needs, you can get back your normal hair after straightening. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you'll soon start to see the results you desire.
