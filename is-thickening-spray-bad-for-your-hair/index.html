Is Thickening Spray Bad For Your Hair

Written by Robby Jul 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thickening sprays have become a popular hair styling product in recent years. They promise to add volume and fullness to your hair, making it look thicker and more luscious. However, with the rise in popularity comes the concern of whether or not thickening spray is bad for your hair. In this article, we will explore this question and provide you with some tips on how to use thickening spray safely.

What is Thickening Spray?

Thickening spray is a hair styling product that is designed to add volume and fullness to your hair. It is usually applied to damp hair and then blow-dried to achieve the desired look. Thickening spray works by coating the hair strands and plumping them up, making them appear thicker and fuller.

Is Thickening Spray Bad for Your Hair?

The short answer is no, thickening spray is not bad for your hair. However, like any hair styling product, it can cause damage if not used properly. Thickening spray contains chemicals that can dry out your hair and scalp if used too frequently. It can also weigh down your hair, making it look greasy and flat.

How to Use Thickening Spray Safely

To use thickening spray safely, follow these tips:

  • Use it in moderation. Only apply thickening spray when you need it, not every day.
  • Apply it correctly. Spray it onto damp hair and distribute it evenly with your fingers.
  • Use a heat protectant. Before blow-drying your hair, apply a heat protectant to prevent damage.
  • Don't overdo it. Use a small amount at a time, and build up to the desired look gradually.

Step-by-Step Guide to Using Thickening Spray

Here's a step-by-step guide to using thickening spray:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Shake the can of thickening spray well before use.
  3. Spray the product evenly all over your hair, focusing on the roots.
  4. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.
  5. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume.
  6. Style your hair as desired.
  7. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Using Thickening Spray

Here are some additional tips for using thickening spray:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using other hair styling products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Don't apply thickening spray to your roots if you have an oily scalp, as this can make your hair look greasy.
  • If you have fine hair, use a lightweight thickening spray to prevent weighing down your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thickening spray:

Q: Can thickening spray cause hair loss?

A: No, thickening spray does not cause hair loss. However, if you use it too frequently or apply it incorrectly, it can damage your hair and make it appear thinner.

Q: Can I use thickening spray on color-treated hair?

A: Yes, you can use thickening spray on color-treated hair. However, be sure to use a product that is specifically designed for color-treated hair to prevent fading.

Q: Can I use thickening spray on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use thickening spray on curly hair. However, be sure to apply it to damp hair and use a diffuser when blow-drying to prevent frizz.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy while using thickening spray, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.

Conclusion

In conclusion, thickening spray is not bad for your hair if used properly. Follow the tips and guidelines outlined in this article to use it safely and achieve the desired look. Remember to take care of your hair by following a healthy hair care routine to keep it looking its best.
