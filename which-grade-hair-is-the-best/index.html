Which Grade Hair Is The Best For Relaxed Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Apr 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have relaxed hair, you know how important it is to use the right products and techniques to keep your hair healthy and looking great. One of the most important decisions you'll make is choosing the right grade of hair for your extensions or weaves. In this guide, we'll explore the different grades of hair, and help you determine which one is the best fit for your relaxed hair.

What is Hair Grade?

Hair grade refers to the quality of human hair used in hair extensions or weaves. The grade is determined by the thickness, texture, and overall quality of the hair. The higher the grade, the higher the quality of the hair.

Which Grade Hair is the Best for Relaxed Hair?

The best grade of hair for relaxed hair is virgin hair. Virgin hair is hair that has never been chemically treated, and is therefore in its natural state. Virgin hair is often of the highest grade, and is the most expensive. However, the investment is worth it, as virgin hair is the most durable, long-lasting, and natural-looking option.

Another good option for relaxed hair is Remy hair. Remy hair is hair that has been collected from a single donor, and has been carefully sorted to ensure that all the cuticles are facing the same direction. This creates a more natural look, and reduces tangling.

How to Choose the Right Hair Grade for Your Relaxed Hair

When choosing the right hair grade for your relaxed hair, there are a few factors to consider:

  • Budget: Virgin hair is the most expensive option, but it is also the highest quality. Consider your budget, and choose the best quality hair you can afford.
  • Texture: Choose a hair texture that matches your natural hair. If you have relaxed hair, choose a straight or relaxed texture.
  • Length: Choose a length that is comfortable for you. If you're used to shorter hair, don't choose extensions that are too long, as they may be difficult to manage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Installing Hair Extensions

Installing hair extensions can be a daunting task, but with a little practice, you can do it yourself. Here's a step-by-step guide to installing hair extensions:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, and blow it dry.
  2. Section your hair into small sections using clips.
  3. Begin at the nape of your neck, and sew the hair extensions in using a needle and thread.
  4. Continue sewing in the hair extensions until you reach the top of your head.
  5. Once all the hair extensions are in place, cut and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Relaxed Hair with Hair Extensions

Maintaining your relaxed hair with hair extensions requires a little extra effort. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly: This will keep your hair and extensions clean and healthy.
  • Avoid heat: Heat can damage your hair and extensions. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles can cause breakage and damage to your hair and extensions.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase: This will reduce friction and prevent tangling.

FAQ

What is the difference between virgin hair and Remy hair?

Virgin hair is hair that has never been chemically treated, while Remy hair is hair that has been collected from a single donor and carefully sorted to ensure that all the cuticles are facing the same direction.

How long do hair extensions last?

Hair extensions can last up to 6 months with proper care and maintenance.

Can I color my hair extensions?

Yes, you can color your hair extensions. However, it's best to have a professional colorist do it for you, and to use a gentle, ammonia-free color.

Hair Care Tips for Relaxed Hair

Maintaining healthy, relaxed hair requires a little extra effort. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Moisturize regularly: Relaxed hair is prone to dryness, so be sure to moisturize regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid heat: Heat can damage your hair, so try to limit your use of hot tools.
  • Trim your ends regularly: Trimming your ends every 6-8 weeks will help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

By following these tips and choosing the right grade of hair for your extensions or weaves, you can keep your relaxed hair looking healthy and beautiful.
