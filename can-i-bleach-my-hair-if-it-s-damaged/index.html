Can I Bleach My Hair If It s Damaged

Description

Many people want to change their hair color, and bleaching is one of the most popular methods to do so. However, if your hair is already damaged, you might wonder if you can still bleach it. In this article, we will answer the question "Can I bleach my hair if it's damaged?" and provide you with tips on how to do it safely.

How To

Bleaching damaged hair requires a different approach than bleaching healthy hair. The first thing you should do is to assess the condition of your hair. Is it brittle, dry, or breaking? Bleaching can make these problems worse, so it's essential to take precautions to avoid further damage.

Step 1: Consult a Professional

If your hair is severely damaged, it's best to consult a professional before attempting to bleach it. A hair stylist can evaluate the condition of your hair and advise you on whether or not it's safe to bleach it. They can also recommend the best products to use to minimize damage.

Step 2: Use a High-Quality Bleach

When bleaching damaged hair, it's essential to use a high-quality bleach that's gentle on your hair. Avoid cheap, low-quality products, as they can cause further damage. Look for a bleach that's specifically designed for damaged hair, and follow the instructions carefully.

Step 3: Pre-Treat Your Hair

To minimize damage, it's a good idea to pre-treat your hair before bleaching it. Use a deep conditioning treatment a few days before bleaching, and avoid washing your hair for a few days before the treatment. This will help to protect your hair and reduce the risk of breakage.

Step 4: Use a Lower Volume Developer

When bleaching damaged hair, it's best to use a lower volume developer. A 10 or 20 volume developer is usually sufficient for most people. Using a higher volume developer can cause more damage to your hair. If you're not sure which developer to use, consult a professional.

Step By Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to bleach damaged hair:

  1. Assess the condition of your hair
  2. Consult a professional
  3. Choose a high-quality bleach
  4. Pre-treat your hair
  5. Mix the bleach according to the instructions
  6. Apply the bleach to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up
  7. Check your hair regularly to avoid over-processing
  8. Rinse the bleach out thoroughly with cool water
  9. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair
  10. Avoid washing your hair for a few days after bleaching

Tips

Here are some tips to help you bleach damaged hair safely:

  • Use a high-quality bleach
  • Pre-treat your hair with a deep conditioning treatment
  • Consult a professional if your hair is severely damaged
  • Use a lower volume developer
  • Avoid over-processing your hair
  • Rinse the bleach out thoroughly with cool water
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment after bleaching
  • Avoid washing your hair for a few days after bleaching

FAQ

Can I bleach my hair if it's severely damaged?

If your hair is severely damaged, it's best to consult a professional before attempting to bleach it. They can evaluate the condition of your hair and advise you on whether or not it's safe to bleach it.

What should I do if my hair becomes too damaged after bleaching?

If your hair becomes too damaged after bleaching, it's essential to treat it with care. Avoid heat styling, and use a deep conditioning treatment regularly. Consider cutting off the damaged ends or getting a shorter haircut.

How long should I wait before bleaching my hair again?

If you've already bleached your hair and it's damaged, it's best to wait at least a few weeks before bleaching it again. This will give your hair time to recover and minimize the risk of further damage.

Hair Care

Bleaching damaged hair requires extra care and attention. Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain healthy hair after bleaching:

  • Avoid heat styling
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly
  • Avoid washing your hair too often
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut to remove damaged ends

Bleaching damaged hair can be risky, but with the right precautions, you can achieve the hair color you want without causing further damage. Remember to take it slow, be patient, and always prioritize the health of your hair.
