How To Cut Thick Side Bangs Step By Step Guide

Written by William Apr 29, 2023 · 7 min read

Side bangs are a great way to change up your hairstyle without committing to a full fringe. If you have thick hair, cutting your own side bangs can seem daunting, but with a little practice, you can achieve the perfect look. Here’s our step-by-step guide on how to cut thick side bangs.

Description

Before we get started, it’s important to understand what side bangs are. Side bangs are a type of fringe that starts at the temple and sweeps across the forehead. They can be worn long or short and can be cut at an angle or straight across. Thick side bangs are perfect for framing the face and adding a touch of glamour to any hairstyle.

How to Cut Thick Side Bangs

Step 1: Choose the Right Scissors

When cutting your own hair, it’s important to use the right tools. Choose a pair of sharp scissors designed for cutting hair. Avoid using regular household scissors as they can cause split ends and damage to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section the hair you want to cut into a triangular shape, with the point of the triangle pointing towards the center of your forehead. Use a hair clip to secure the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Decide how long you want your side bangs to be. Remember, you can always cut more hair off, but you can’t stick it back on. Start with a longer length and gradually cut more off until you achieve the desired length.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Using the tips of the scissors, cut the hair at a slight angle. Start at the point of the triangle and work your way towards the outside of your forehead. Take your time and make small cuts, checking the length as you go.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you’ve cut the hair to the desired length, use the scissors to blend the hair into the rest of your hair. This will create a natural-looking transition between the side bangs and the rest of your hair.

Step 6: Style Your Side Bangs

Style your side bangs using a round brush and a hairdryer. Blow-dry your hair in the direction you want your side bangs to go. Use a little hairspray to keep them in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect side bangs:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more off until you achieve the desired length.
  • Don’t cut too much off at once. You can always cut more off, but you can’t stick it back on.
  • Use a mirror to check the length of your side bangs from different angles.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair takes time and practice.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick side bangs:

How often should I cut my side bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your side bangs to be. On average, you should trim your side bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Can I cut my side bangs when they’re wet?

It’s best to cut your side bangs when they’re dry as wet hair can shrink as it dries, causing you to cut more off than you intended.

What should I do if I cut my side bangs too short?

If you’ve cut your side bangs too short, don’t panic. You can use a hair clip to pin them back until they grow out or visit a professional hair stylist who can fix them for you.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy is important for achieving the perfect side bangs. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often as they can cause damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

Now that you know how to cut thick side bangs, why not give it a try? With a little practice, you can achieve the perfect look and change up your hairstyle in no time.
