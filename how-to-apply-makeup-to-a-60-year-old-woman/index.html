How To Apply Makeup To A 60 Year Old Woman Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Jun 27, 2023 · 8 min read

As we age, our skin goes through changes that can make it more difficult to apply makeup. However, with the right techniques and products, women over 60 can still look and feel beautiful. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to apply makeup to a 60 year old woman.

Table of Contents

  • How to Apply Makeup to a 60 Year Old Woman
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Applying Makeup to Older Skin
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Women Over 60

How to Apply Makeup to a 60 Year Old Woman

Before we jump into the step-by-step guide, it’s important to understand that makeup application for older skin requires a different approach than that of younger skin. Older skin tends to be drier and thinner, and may also have age spots, fine lines, and wrinkles.

The goal of makeup application for older skin is to create a natural, youthful look that enhances your features without accentuating any imperfections. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use moisturizer and primer to hydrate and smooth the skin
  • Choose a lightweight foundation that won’t settle into fine lines and wrinkles
  • Use a concealer to hide dark circles and age spots
  • Opt for cream or liquid blush instead of powder, which can accentuate wrinkles
  • Stick with neutral eyeshadow colors and avoid glitter or shimmer, which can draw attention to wrinkles
  • Use a brown or gray eyeliner instead of black, which can look harsh on older skin
  • Finish with a moisturizing lipstick or lip gloss that won’t dry out the lips

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let’s dive into the step-by-step guide on how to apply makeup to a 60 year old woman:

Step 1: Prep the Skin

Start by cleansing and moisturizing the skin to create a smooth base. Follow with a primer to help makeup last longer and fill in any fine lines or wrinkles.

Step 2: Apply Foundation

Choose a lightweight foundation that matches your skin tone and apply it evenly with a brush or sponge. Avoid heavy or thick formulas, as they can settle into fine lines and wrinkles.

Step 3: Conceal Dark Circles and Age Spots

Use a concealer that matches your skin tone to cover dark circles and age spots. Apply with a brush or sponge and blend well.

Step 4: Add Blush

Choose a cream or liquid blush in a neutral shade and apply it to the apples of your cheeks with your fingers or a brush. Blend well for a natural look.

Step 5: Define Your Eyes

Use a neutral eyeshadow shade on your eyelids and a slightly darker shade in the crease. Apply eyeliner in brown or gray along the upper lash line, and use a light hand to create a subtle wing at the outer corners.

Step 6: Finish with Lipstick or Lip Gloss

Choose a moisturizing lipstick or lip gloss in a neutral shade and apply it to your lips for a natural, youthful look.

Tips for Applying Makeup to Older Skin

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when applying makeup to older skin:

  • Use a light hand when applying makeup to avoid a heavy, cakey look
  • Choose products with hydrating ingredients, such as hyaluronic acid or glycerin
  • Avoid matte formulas, which can make skin look dry and accentuate wrinkles
  • Use a setting spray to keep makeup in place and prevent it from settling into fine lines and wrinkles
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you

FAQs

What kind of foundation should I use?

Choose a lightweight, hydrating foundation that matches your skin tone. Avoid heavy or thick formulas, which can settle into fine lines and wrinkles.

What’s the best way to apply blush?

Use a cream or liquid blush in a neutral shade and apply it to the apples of your cheeks with your fingers or a brush. Blend well for a natural look.

Can I still wear eyeshadow and eyeliner?

Absolutely! Stick with neutral eyeshadow colors and avoid glitter or shimmer, which can draw attention to wrinkles. Use a brown or gray eyeliner instead of black, which can look harsh on older skin.

Hair Care Tips for Women Over 60

While makeup is important, don’t forget about your hair! As we age, our hair can become thinner and more brittle. Here are some hair care tips for women over 60:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, which can damage hair
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s designed for your hair type
  • Choose a hairstyle that flatters your face shape and complements your features
  • Consider using a volumizing spray or mousse to add body and fullness to your hair
  • Don’t be afraid to try new hairstyles or colors to freshen up your look

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a natural, youthful look that enhances your features and makes you feel confident and beautiful. Remember to have fun and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you!
