How To Stop Chemically Damaged Hair From Falling Out

Written by Daniel Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Chemically damaged hair can be a nightmare for many people. It can lead to hair fall, breakage, and even bald patches. If you are experiencing this problem, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will share some tips and tricks that will help you stop chemically damaged hair from falling out.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Chemically treated hair is more prone to damage and hair fall. Chemical treatments such as coloring, perming, and relaxing can cause hair to become brittle, dry, and weak. Hair fall is a common problem for those who have chemically treated hair. In this article, we will provide you with some tips and tricks to prevent hair fall and damage.

How to

The first step to stop chemically damaged hair from falling out is to identify the cause. Once you know the cause, you can take the necessary steps to prevent further damage. Some common causes of hair fall are:

  • Over-processing
  • Using harsh chemicals
  • Not conditioning enough
  • Not using heat protectant

If you are experiencing hair fall due to any of these reasons, then you can follow these steps to stop it:

Step by step

  1. Reduce chemical treatments: The first step to stop chemically damaged hair from falling out is to reduce the number of chemical treatments you are doing. Avoid coloring, perming, or relaxing your hair too often. Give your hair time to recover between treatments.
  2. Use mild products: Use mild shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for chemically treated hair. Avoid using harsh products that contain sulfates and parabens.
  3. Condition regularly: Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. This will help prevent further damage and hair fall.
  4. Protect from heat: Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools such as straighteners, curling irons, or hair dryers. Excessive heat can damage your hair and cause it to fall out.
  5. Trim regularly: Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and prevent further damage. This will also help your hair grow stronger and healthier.
  6. Avoid tight hairstyles: Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, buns, and ponytails. These styles can pull on your hair and cause it to fall out.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you stop chemically damaged hair from falling out:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair. Hot water can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals. This will help nourish your hair from the inside out.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol. Alcohol can dry out your hair and cause it to become brittle.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about stopping chemically damaged hair from falling out:

Q: Can chemically treated hair grow back?

A: Yes, chemically treated hair can grow back. However, it may take some time and effort to restore your hair's health.

Q: How long does it take for hair to recover from chemical damage?

A: It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for hair to recover from chemical damage. The length of time will depend on the severity of the damage and how well you take care of your hair.

Hair Care

Here are some additional hair care tips to help prevent hair fall and damage:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to harsh chemicals such as chlorine and bleach.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

By following these tips and tricks, you can stop chemically damaged hair from falling out and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you will see results in no time.
