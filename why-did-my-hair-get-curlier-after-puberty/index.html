Why Did My Hair Get Curlier After Puberty

Written by Petter Apr 11, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you wondering why your hair has become curlier after puberty? This is a common question among many people, especially those who have had straight hair all their lives. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair may have changed and provide tips on how to take care of your curly locks.

Description

Puberty is a time of many changes for both boys and girls. For some, one of the changes that occur during puberty is the texture of their hair. Hair that was once straight may become wavy or curly, while curly hair may become even curlier. The reason for this change is due to hormonal fluctuations that occur during puberty.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Before you can take care of your curly hair, you need to determine your hair type. There are three types of curly hair: wavy, curly, and coily. To determine your hair type, wash your hair and let it air dry without using any products. Once your hair is dry, examine the curls and determine which type they fall under.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has loose, S-shaped curls that are usually easy to manage. This hair type may be prone to frizz, but with the right products, it can look beautiful and defined.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has tighter curls that form ringlets. This hair type may be prone to dryness and tangles, but with the right products and techniques, it can look healthy and defined.

Coily Hair

Coily hair has tight, springy curls that are prone to shrinkage. This hair type may be more fragile and require more moisture and gentle styling to prevent breakage.

Step by Step Guide to Taking Care of Curly Hair

Now that you know your hair type, it's time to learn how to take care of your curls to keep them healthy and defined.

Step 1: Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair. Sulfates can strip the natural oils from your hair, leaving it dry and frizzy.

Step 2: Detangle

After washing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls. Start from the ends and work your way up to prevent breakage.

Step 3: Apply Products

Apply a leave-in conditioner and a curl-defining product to your hair. Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair on low heat to prevent frizz.

Step 4: Refresh Your Curls

Use a spray bottle with water and a little bit of leave-in conditioner to refresh your curls throughout the day. Scrunch your curls with your hands to redefine them.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curls

Here are some additional tips to maintain healthy, defined curls:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a satin pillowcase or using a satin bonnet or scarf.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair if it's curly. However, it's important to use heat protectant products and not straighten your hair too frequently to prevent damage.

Q: Can I wash my hair every day?

A: It's not recommended to wash your curly hair every day, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair and cause dryness. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my curly hair?

A: It's best to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, as they are formulated to provide the moisture and definition that curly hair needs.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right products and techniques, it can look healthy and beautiful. Remember to determine your hair type, use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid heat styling tools. With these tips, you can maintain healthy, defined curls that you'll love.
