How To Wear Your Thick Hair A Relaxed Guide

Written by Petter Aug 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. It can be voluminous and beautiful, but it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you're struggling to find ways to wear your thick hair, this guide is for you. We'll provide step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice to help you make the most of your thick locks.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Wear Your Thick Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Managing Thick Hair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Wear Your Thick Hair

Before we dive into specific hairstyles, it's important to understand the basics of how to wear thick hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will prevent breakage and damage.
  • Invest in quality hair products that are designed for thick hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are hydrating and nourishing.
  • Try to avoid using heat styling tools too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray beforehand.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. The Half-Up, Half-Down:

This is a classic hairstyle that works well for thick hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle.
  2. Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and pull it back, securing it with a hair tie or clip.
  3. Optional: Tease the hair at the crown of your head for extra volume.

2. The Messy Bun:

The messy bun is a great option for when you're short on time. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by pulling your hair back into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun, securing it with a hair tie or bobby pins.
  3. Use your fingers to pull out a few pieces of hair around your face for a more relaxed look.

3. The French Braid:

A French braid is a stylish way to keep your hair out of your face. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Take a small section of hair at the top of your head and divide it into three pieces.
  3. Cross the right piece over the middle piece, then cross the left piece over the middle piece.
  4. As you continue braiding, add small sections of hair to each piece as you cross it over the middle piece.
  5. Continue braiding until you reach the nape of your neck, then secure the braid with a hair tie or bobby pins.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you manage your thick hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Consider using a hair oil or serum to add shine and tame frizz.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about styling thick hair:

Q: What's the best way to add volume to thick hair?

A: Teasing your hair at the crown can add volume and lift. You can also use volumizing products, like mousse or root-lifting spray.

Q: Is it better to wear my hair up or down if it's thick?

A: It depends on your personal preference and the occasion. Updos can be a great way to keep your hair out of your face, while wearing your hair down can show off its natural beauty.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Proper hair care is essential for keeping thick hair healthy and manageable. Here are a few key tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner that are designed for thick hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to wear your thick hair with confidence. Whether you opt for an updo, a braid, or a messy bun, you'll be able to show off your beautiful locks in style.
