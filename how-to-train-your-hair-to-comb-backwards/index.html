How To Train Your Hair To Comb Backwards

Written by Eveline Jun 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're tired of your hair always falling in your face or sticking up in awkward directions, you may want to consider training it to comb backwards. This style is classic and timeless, and it's easy to achieve with the right techniques. In this post, we'll go over how to train your hair to comb backwards and provide some tips for maintaining this style.

How to Train Your Hair to Comb Backwards

The first step in training your hair to comb backwards is to start with clean, damp hair. Comb your hair straight back from your forehead, and use a hair dryer to dry it in that position. This will help set your hair in the right direction.

Next, apply a styling product that will help hold your hair in place, such as a pomade or gel. Work the product through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Using a comb or brush, comb your hair straight back from your forehead, working the product through your hair as you go. Make sure to comb all the way to the back of your head, and smooth out any bumps or kinks.

Once your hair is combed back, use your fingers to create some texture and volume. Lift your hair at the roots to add some height, and use your fingers to separate and tousle your hair.

Finally, use a strong-hold hairspray to set your hair in place. Hold the can about 12 inches away from your head, and spray evenly all over your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to training your hair to comb backwards:

Step 1: Start with clean, damp hair

Make sure your hair is clean and damp before you start. Use a shampoo and conditioner that work well for your hair type, and towel dry your hair until it's just damp.

Step 2: Use a styling product

Apply a styling product that will help hold your hair in place. Pomade or gel work well for this style. Work the product through your hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the tips.

Step 3: Comb your hair straight back

Using a comb or brush, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure to comb all the way to the back of your head, and smooth out any bumps or kinks.

Step 4: Create texture and volume

Use your fingers to create some texture and volume in your hair. Lift your hair at the roots to add some height, and use your fingers to separate and tousle your hair.

Step 5: Use hairspray to set your hair

Finally, use a strong-hold hairspray to set your hair in place. Hold the can about 12 inches away from your head, and spray evenly all over your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you train your hair to comb backwards:

  • Start with clean, damp hair
  • Use a styling product that will help hold your hair in place
  • Comb your hair straight back from your forehead
  • Create texture and volume with your fingers
  • Use a strong-hold hairspray to set your hair in place
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about training your hair to comb backwards:

How long does it take to train your hair to comb backwards?

It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to train your hair to comb backwards, depending on the length and texture of your hair.

Can I train my hair to comb backwards if it's curly or wavy?

Yes, you can still train your hair to comb backwards if it's curly or wavy. However, you may need to use different products and techniques to achieve the desired style.

How often should I wash my hair when training it to comb backwards?

It's best to wash your hair every other day or every few days when training it to comb backwards. This will help maintain the natural oils in your hair and prevent it from drying out.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking its best when combing it backwards, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that work well for your hair type
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as from curling irons or straighteners
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy

With these tips and techniques, you can train your hair to comb backwards and achieve a classic, timeless look. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair, and don't be afraid to ask a stylist for advice if you need it.
