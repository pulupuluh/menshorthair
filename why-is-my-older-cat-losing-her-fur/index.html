Why Is My Older Cat Losing Her Fur

Written by Christine Feb 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As cat owners, we love our furry friends, and it can be concerning when we notice that they are losing their fur. If you have an older cat that is losing their fur, there could be several reasons why. In this blog post, we will explore the possible causes of fur loss in older cats and what you can do to help your feline friend.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Fur Loss in Older Cats
  • How to Help Your Cat with Fur Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Your Cat's Fur Loss

Causes of Fur Loss in Older Cats

There are several reasons why an older cat may experience fur loss. Some of the most common causes include:

1. Skin Infections

Older cats are more prone to skin infections, which can cause fur loss. These infections can be caused by bacteria, fungi, or parasites.

2. Stress

Stress can cause fur loss in cats of all ages, but older cats may be more susceptible. Stressful situations such as moving to a new home or the introduction of a new pet can cause fur loss.

3. Allergies

Older cats can develop allergies to certain foods, medications or environmental factors, which can cause fur loss, itching and skin irritation.

4. Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances can also cause fur loss in older cats. This can be caused by an underactive thyroid gland, diabetes or other medical conditions that affect hormone levels.

How to Help Your Cat with Fur Loss

If you notice that your older cat is losing their fur, it is important to take action to help them. Here are some things you can do:

1. Visit Your Veterinarian

The first step is to take your cat to the veterinarian for a check-up. Your veterinarian can identify any underlying medical conditions that may be causing the fur loss and recommend appropriate treatment.

2. Change Their Diet

If your cat is losing fur due to a food allergy, changing their diet can help. Your veterinarian can recommend a hypoallergenic diet or food that is free from common allergens.

3. Reduce Stress

If your cat is losing fur due to stress, it is important to try to reduce their stress levels. This can be achieved by providing a calm and quiet environment, avoiding sudden changes, and providing plenty of playtime and attention.

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Your Cat's Fur Loss

If your veterinarian has determined that there are no underlying medical conditions causing your cat's fur loss, there are several things you can do to help manage the problem:

1. Brush Your Cat's Fur Regularly

Brushing your cat's fur regularly can help to remove loose fur and stimulate hair growth. Use a soft-bristled brush and be gentle to avoid causing any further irritation to their skin.

2. Bathe Your Cat

Bathing your cat can help to remove any dirt, debris or allergens that may be causing their fur loss. Use a mild shampoo and make sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any irritation.

3. Apply a Moisturizing Lotion

Applying a moisturizing lotion or cream to your cat's skin can help to soothe any irritation and promote hair growth. Make sure to use a lotion that is safe for cats and avoid any products that contain fragrances or harsh chemicals.

Tips for Preventing Fur Loss in Older Cats

Preventing fur loss in older cats is all about keeping them healthy and happy. Here are some tips:

1. Provide a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is essential for maintaining healthy skin and fur. Make sure to feed your cat a high-quality diet that is appropriate for their age and health needs.

2. Keep Your Cat Active

Regular exercise and playtime can help to reduce stress and keep your cat healthy and happy.

3. Keep Your Cat's Environment Clean

A clean environment can help to prevent skin infections and allergies. Make sure to clean your cat's litter box regularly and keep their bedding and toys clean.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is fur loss in older cats normal?

Fur loss is not normal in older cats and can be a sign of an underlying medical condition.

2. What should I do if I notice my cat is losing fur?

If you notice that your cat is losing fur, it is important to take them to the veterinarian for a check-up.

3. Can stress cause fur loss in cats?

Yes, stress can cause fur loss in cats of all ages.

Hair Care Tips for Older Cats

Caring for your older cat's fur can help to prevent fur loss and keep their skin healthy. Here are some hair care tips:

1. Brush Your Cat's Fur Regularly

Regular brushing can help to remove loose fur and prevent matting.

2. Bathe Your Cat as Needed

Bathing your cat can help to remove dirt and debris that may be causing skin irritation.

3. Provide a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy skin and fur. Make sure to feed your cat a balanced and nutritious diet.

Fur loss in older cats can be a sign of an underlying medical condition or simply a result of aging. By taking steps to identify the cause and manage the problem, you can help to keep your feline friend healthy and happy for years to come.
