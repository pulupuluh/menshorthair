How To Thin Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 08, 2023 · 7 min read

Thinning out medium length hair is a great way to create volume and movement in your locks. Whether you have thick or fine hair, this technique can help you achieve a more manageable and stylish look. In this article, we’ll show you how to thin medium length hair in a relaxed and easy-to-follow language. Here’s a quick overview of what we’ll cover:

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Medium Length Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Medium Length Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Thinned Hair

How to Thin Medium Length Hair

Thinning out medium length hair involves removing some of the bulk or weight from your hair without sacrificing its length. This can be done using a variety of tools and techniques, including:

  • Thinning Shears: These are specialized scissors designed to cut hair at an angle, creating a softer and more natural look.
  • Razor Cut: This technique uses a razor blade to create a more textured and layered effect.
  • Point Cutting: This involves using scissors to cut individual strands of hair at different lengths, creating a more jagged and edgy look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to thin medium length hair using thinning shears:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly.
  2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Section off your hair into manageable parts using hair clips.
  4. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down.
  5. Hold the thinning shears parallel to the hair shaft and cut into the hair at an angle, about 1 inch from the ends.
  6. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, making sure to cut at different angles for a more natural look.
  7. Once you’ve finished thinning out your hair, use a comb to style it as desired.

Tips for Thinning Medium Length Hair

Thinning out medium length hair can be a tricky process, so here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with clean and dry hair to make it easier to see the areas that need thinning.
  • Use sharp and high-quality thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to avoid over-thinning.
  • Only thin the ends of your hair to avoid creating choppy or uneven layers.
  • Take breaks in between sections to avoid tiring out your hands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about thinning medium length hair:

Q: Can I thin out my own hair?
A: Yes, you can thin out your own hair using thinning shears or a razor blade. However, it’s best to have a professional stylist do it for you to avoid any mistakes or damage to your hair. Q: How often should I thin out my hair?
A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you can thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and volume. Q: Will thinning my hair make it look thinner?
A: No, thinning out your hair will actually make it look thicker and more voluminous by removing excess weight and bulk.

Hair Care Tips for Thinned Hair

After thinning out your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or serum to protect your hair from environmental stressors.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay and maintain the shape of your hair.

Now that you know how to thin medium length hair, you can experiment with different styles and looks. Whether you want a sleek and polished look or a more textured and edgy vibe, thinning out your hair can help you achieve your desired outcome.
