Does Waxing Hurt Less With Longer Hair

Written by Petter Jun 15, 2023 · 8 min read

If you are someone who is considering waxing as a hair removal option, you may be wondering if it hurts less with longer hair. In this blog post, we will answer this question and provide you with some tips and tricks to make your waxing experience as painless as possible. So, let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Does Hair Length Affect Waxing Pain?
  • How to Prepare for Waxing
  • Step-by-Step Waxing Process
  • Tips for a Painless Waxing Experience
  • Waxing FAQ
  • Hair Care After Waxing

Does Hair Length Affect Waxing Pain?

The short answer is yes, hair length does affect waxing pain. When your hair is longer, the wax has more to grip onto, which makes it easier for the hair to be pulled out from the root. This means that you will feel less pain during the waxing process.

On the other hand, if your hair is too short, the wax may not be able to grip onto it properly, which can cause the hair to break instead of being pulled out from the root. This can be more painful and can also result in ingrown hairs.

However, it's important to note that hair length is not the only factor that affects waxing pain. Other factors such as your skin type, the type of wax being used, and your pain tolerance can also play a role in how much pain you feel during waxing.

How to Prepare for Waxing

Before you get waxed, there are a few things you can do to prepare your skin and make the process less painful:

  • Exfoliate your skin a few days before your appointment to remove any dead skin cells and help the wax grip onto your hair better.
  • Moisturize your skin regularly in the days leading up to your appointment to keep your skin hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid shaving or trimming your hair for at least two weeks before your appointment to ensure that your hair is long enough for the wax to grip onto.
  • Avoid using any lotions, oils, or other products on your skin on the day of your appointment, as they can interfere with the wax's ability to grip onto your hair.

Step-by-Step Waxing Process

If you've never been waxed before, you may be wondering what to expect during the process. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you prepare:

  1. Cleanse the area to be waxed with a gentle cleanser to remove any dirt or oil.
  2. Apply a pre-wax oil or lotion to protect your skin and make it easier for the wax to grip onto your hair.
  3. Apply the wax in small sections, using a spatula or applicator stick. Make sure to apply the wax in the direction of hair growth.
  4. Place a cloth strip over the wax and press down firmly.
  5. Hold the skin taut with one hand and use the other hand to pull the cloth strip off in one quick motion, against the direction of hair growth.
  6. Repeat this process until all of the hair has been removed.
  7. Apply a soothing lotion or oil to help calm your skin and reduce any redness or irritation.

Tips for a Painless Waxing Experience

Here are some additional tips to help you make your waxing experience as painless as possible:

  • Breathe deeply and try to relax during the waxing process. Tensing up can make the pain worse.
  • Take a pain reliever such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen before your appointment to help reduce pain.
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol before your appointment, as they can make your skin more sensitive.
  • Choose a reputable waxing salon with experienced and licensed estheticians.
  • Consider using a numbing cream or spray before your appointment to help reduce pain.

Waxing FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about waxing:

How often should I get waxed?

This depends on your hair growth and personal preference. Most people get waxed every four to six weeks.

Can I wax at home?

Yes, you can wax at home, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully to avoid injury or irritation.

Is waxing safe?

When done by a licensed and experienced esthetician, waxing is generally safe. However, there is always a risk of injury or infection, so it's important to choose a reputable salon and follow proper aftercare instructions.

Hair Care After Waxing

After you've been waxed, it's important to take care of your skin to avoid irritation or infection:

  • Avoid using any products on the waxed area for at least 24 hours.
  • Avoid hot showers, saunas, and steam rooms for at least 24 hours.
  • Avoid tight clothing that can rub against the waxed area.
  • Exfoliate the waxed area gently a few days after your appointment to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Moisturize the waxed area regularly to keep your skin hydrated and healthy.

So, there you have it – everything you need to know about whether waxing hurts less with longer hair. Remember to prepare your skin properly before your appointment, choose a reputable salon, and take care of your skin after waxing to ensure the best possible experience. Happy waxing!
