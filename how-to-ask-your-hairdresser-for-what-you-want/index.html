How To Ask Your Hairdresser For What You Want In Relaxed English

Written by Bardi May 31, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of leaving the hair salon feeling disappointed with your haircut? It’s time to learn how to communicate with your hairdresser effectively. In this blog post, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to ask your hairdresser for what you want in relaxed English.

Table of Contents

  • How to Ask Your Hairdresser for What You Want
  • Step by Step Guide

  • FAQs

How to Ask Your Hairdresser for What You Want

Asking your hairdresser for what you want is not always easy, but it's essential if you want to achieve the desired result. Here are some tips on how to communicate effectively with your stylist:

Be Specific

It’s important to be specific when communicating with your hairdresser. Instead of saying “I want a trim,” tell them how much you want to cut off. For example, “I want to cut off an inch from the ends.”

Use Visual Aids

Visual aids can help you communicate what you want. Bring photos of the haircut you want and show them to your hairdresser. They can take a look and let you know if it is achievable with your hair type and face shape.

Be Honest

Don’t be afraid to express your concerns and be honest with your hairdresser. If you’re not comfortable with a certain style or length, let them know.

Ask Questions

Ask questions to ensure that you and your hairdresser are on the same page. For example, you can ask about the maintenance of the hairstyle, the products to use, and any styling tips.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to ask your hairdresser for what you want:

Step 1: Research

Research the hairstyle you want and gather some photos to show your hairdresser. Look for photos with different angles to give them a clear idea of what you want.

Step 2: Book an Appointment

Book an appointment with your hairdresser and let them know that you have some photos to show them.

Step 3: Consultation

During the consultation, show the photos to your hairdresser and explain what you like about them. Ask for their opinion on whether the style is suitable for your hair type and face shape.

Step 4: Be Clear

Be clear when communicating what you want. Use specific terms like layers, bangs, and undercut to describe the haircut you want.

Step 5: Ask Questions

Ask your hairdresser questions about the maintenance of the style, the products to use, and any styling tips.

Tips for Effective Communication

Here are some additional tips for effective communication with your hairdresser:

Be Realistic

Be realistic about what you want. If your hair is thin, asking for a voluminous hairstyle might not be achievable. Your hairdresser can suggest alternative styles that will suit your hair type.

Be Open to Suggestions

Be open to suggestions from your hairdresser. They have the experience and expertise to give you advice on what will suit your hair type and face shape.

Be Polite

Be polite and courteous when communicating with your hairdresser. Building a good relationship with them can help you achieve the desired result.

FAQs

What should I do if I’m not happy with my haircut?

If you’re not happy with your haircut, let your hairdresser know. They can fix it or suggest alternative styles that will suit you better.

How often should I get a haircut?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and the style you want. Generally, it’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

Use Quality Hair Products

Use quality hair products that are suitable for your hair type. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent damage.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons too often. Excessive heat can damage your hair and make it brittle.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF. The sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.

By following these tips and tricks, you can ask your hairdresser for what you want in relaxed English and achieve the desired result. Remember to be specific, use visual aids, be honest, and ask questions. Good communication with your hairdresser is key to getting the perfect haircut.
