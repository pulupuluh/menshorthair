How To Cut Your Own Hair Messy Bob

Written by Robby Apr 14, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you are looking for a low-maintenance hairstyle that looks chic and trendy, a messy bob might be the perfect choice for you. This short haircut is versatile and easy to style, and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal preferences. Best of all, you can save money and time by learning how to cut your own hair messy bob at home, without having to visit a salon. In this article, we will show you how to achieve a stylish and effortless messy bob in a few simple steps.

Description

A messy bob is a short haircut that looks tousled, textured, and relaxed. This hairstyle is characterized by its choppy layers, asymmetrical shape, and natural-looking waves or curls. A messy bob can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair textures, and can be styled in various ways, from sleek and polished to messy and tousled. This haircut is ideal for busy women who want a low-maintenance yet stylish look that can be achieved with minimal effort.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Messy Bob

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools and equipment. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, a mirror, and a spray bottle filled with water. You may also want to use a hair cutting cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Make sure your hair is completely dry before cutting it, as wet hair tends to look longer and can result in uneven cuts. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots, and divide it into sections using hair clips. Decide on the length and shape of your messy bob, and visualize how you want your hair to look once it is cut.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the back of your hair, using the comb and scissors. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Use the comb to lift the hair away from your head, and cut it at an angle to create a textured and layered effect. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in uneven layers and a choppy appearance.

Once you have cut the back of your hair, move on to the sides and front. Use the same technique of cutting small sections of hair at a time, and blend the layers together to create a seamless look. Remember to keep checking your progress in the mirror, and adjust the length and shape as needed.

Step 4: Add Texture and Volume

Once your hair is cut, you can add texture and volume to create a messy and tousled look. Use the scissors to create choppy layers and wispy ends, and use your fingers to scrunch and tousle your hair. You can also use a curling iron or a straightening iron to create waves or curls, or use a texturizing spray or pomade to add definition and hold.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and cut gradually, as you can always cut more hair but cannot add it back.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and frayed edges.
  • Experiment with different lengths, angles, and layers to find the perfect messy bob for your face shape and hair texture.
  • Be patient and take your time, as rushing can lead to mistakes and regrets.
  • Ask for help from a friend or family member if you are unsure or nervous about cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair messy bob if I have curly or straight hair?

A: Yes, a messy bob can be styled to suit different hair textures, from curly to straight. You may need to adjust the cutting technique and styling products to achieve the desired look.

Q: How often should I trim my messy bob?

A: It depends on your hair growth rate and personal preference, but a general rule of thumb is to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Q: What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

A: Don't panic! You can always visit a salon to fix any mistakes or uneven cuts. Alternatively, you can adjust the length and shape of your hair to create a new and unique look.

Hair Care

To keep your messy bob looking its best, make sure to take good care of your hair. Wash and condition your hair regularly, and use a hair mask or oil treatment to nourish and hydrate your hair. Avoid using too much heat or styling products, as they can damage your hair and make it look dull and lifeless. Instead, opt for natural and gentle hair care products, and let your hair air-dry as much as possible. With proper care and maintenance, your messy bob can be your go-to hairstyle for any occasion!
