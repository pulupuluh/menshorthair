How To Cut Relaxed Ends From Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Apr 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your relaxed ends damaging your natural hair? Do you want to embrace your natural texture and get rid of those chemically treated strands? If so, cutting your relaxed ends is the way to go. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut relaxed ends from natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Relaxed Ends from Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Relaxed Ends from Natural Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Cutting Relaxed Ends from Natural Hair

Description

Cutting your relaxed ends can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. However, it's essential to get rid of those chemically treated strands to prevent further damage to your natural hair. Not only will cutting your relaxed ends promote healthy hair growth, but it will also help you embrace your natural texture.

How to Cut Relaxed Ends from Natural Hair

Cutting your relaxed ends from natural hair requires patience, time, and the right tools. Here's a list of the things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut your relaxed ends from natural hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair, and detangle it with a wide-tooth comb
  2. Part your hair into small sections using hair clips
  3. Start with the first section and comb it straight
  4. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the point where you want to cut
  5. Trim the ends with sharp scissors, making sure to cut straight across
  6. Move on to the next section and repeat the process until you've cut all the relaxed ends
  7. After cutting all the relaxed ends, wash and condition your hair again

Tips for Cutting Relaxed Ends from Natural Hair

Here are some tips to make the process of cutting relaxed ends from natural hair easier:

  • Make sure you have a good light source and a mirror to see what you're doing
  • Don't cut too much hair at once; it's better to take small sections and cut gradually
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Make sure to cut straight across to avoid uneven ends
  • If you're not confident in cutting your hair on your own, seek the help of a professional hairstylist

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting relaxed ends from natural hair:

  • How often should I cut my relaxed ends?
    It depends on how fast your hair grows and how damaged your ends are. It's recommended to cut your relaxed ends every 3-4 months.
  • Can I cut my relaxed ends myself?
    Yes, you can cut your relaxed ends yourself as long as you have the right tools and follow the proper technique.
  • Will cutting my relaxed ends make my hair grow faster?
    Cutting your relaxed ends won't make your hair grow faster, but it will promote healthy hair growth by getting rid of damaged strands.

Hair Care after Cutting Relaxed Ends from Natural Hair

After cutting your relaxed ends from natural hair, it's essential to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or straightening
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or dyes
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth

Now that you know how to cut relaxed ends from natural hair, it's time to embrace your natural texture and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting your relaxed ends to keep it healthy and beautiful.
