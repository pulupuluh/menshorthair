Why Is The Bottom Of My Hair Curly And Not The Top

Written by Robby Jul 03, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Do you ever find yourself wondering why the bottom of your hair is curly, while the top remains straight? This is a common concern for many people, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we will explore the reasons why the bottom of your hair is curly and not the top, as well as provide tips on how to manage your curly locks.

Description

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. If the follicle is round, the hair will be straight, but if it is oval or asymmetrical, the hair will be curly. The shape of the follicle determines the angle at which the hair grows out of the scalp, which affects its texture and curl pattern.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Before we dive into the reasons why the bottom of your hair is curly and not the top, it's important to first identify your hair type. There are four main types of hair: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. To determine your hair type, look at the shape of your hair strands and the texture of your hair. Straight hair is typically smooth and shiny, while curly hair is bouncy and has more volume.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is the easiest hair type to manage. It is generally low maintenance and doesn't require much styling. It can be prone to becoming greasy quickly due to the natural oils produced by the scalp. If you have straight hair, you may find that the bottom of your hair is straight as well.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is a mixture of straight and curly hair. It has a slight wave pattern and often has more volume than straight hair. If you have wavy hair, you may find that the bottom of your hair is slightly curly.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a more defined curl pattern and can range from loose curls to tight coils. It is often thicker and more voluminous than straight hair. If you have curly hair, you may find that the bottom of your hair is the curliest.

Coily Hair

Coily hair is the tightest and most textured hair type. It has a zigzag pattern and can be prone to dryness and breakage. If you have coily hair, you may find that the bottom of your hair is the most textured.

Step by Step Guide: Why is the Bottom of My Hair Curly and Not the Top?

Now that you have identified your hair type, let's explore the reasons why the bottom of your hair is curly and not the top.

1. Genetics

Genetics plays a significant role in determining your hair texture and curl pattern. If you have naturally curly hair, it is likely that your parents or grandparents also have curly hair. The genes that determine hair texture and curl pattern are passed down through generations.

2. Hormones

Hormones can also affect your hair texture and curl pattern. Hormonal changes during puberty, pregnancy, and menopause can cause changes in hair texture and curl pattern. This can result in the bottom of your hair being curlier than the top.

3. Heat Damage

Heat damage can cause the hair to become straighter over time. If you use heat styling tools like flat irons or blow dryers regularly, it can damage the hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern. This can result in the bottom of your hair being curlier than the top.

4. Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments like relaxers or perms can also damage the hair and alter its natural curl pattern. Over time, chemical treatments can cause the hair to become straighter or frizzier. This can result in the bottom of your hair being curlier than the top.

Tips for Managing Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to maintain its texture and curl pattern. Here are some tips for managing curly hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that doesn't strip the hair of its natural oils. Look for shampoos that are sulfate-free and contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil.

2. Condition Regularly

Curly hair is prone to dryness, so it's essential to condition it regularly to keep it hydrated. Look for conditioners that are specifically formulated for curly hair and contain ingredients like avocado oil or argan oil.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or blow dryers regularly. Heat can damage the hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

4. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Embrace your natural texture and work with it instead of against it. Use products that are specifically formulated for your hair type and experiment with different hairstyles that accentuate your curls.

FAQs

Q: Can I make the top of my hair curly?

A: It's challenging to change the natural curl pattern of your hair. If you have naturally straight hair, you can try using curl enhancing products or getting a perm to achieve a curly look.

Q: Why is the bottom of my hair frizzy?

A: The bottom of your hair may be frizzy due to damage from heat styling or chemical treatments. Make sure to use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's recommended to wash curly hair once or twice a week to prevent it from becoming dry and brittle. However, this may vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In conclusion, the bottom of your hair is curly and not the top due to genetics, hormones, heat damage, or chemical treatments. If you have curly hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to maintain its texture and curl pattern. Use gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoid heat styling, and embrace your natural texture. With proper care, your curly locks will look healthy and beautiful.
