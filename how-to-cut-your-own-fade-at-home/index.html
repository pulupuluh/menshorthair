How To Cut Your Own Fade At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save some money and try your hand at cutting your own hair, a fade haircut is a great place to start. A fade is a popular men's haircut that involves gradually shortening the hair on the sides and back while leaving the hair on top longer. With a little practice and the right tools, you can achieve a professional-looking fade haircut from the comfort of your own home. Here's how to cut your own fade at home:

How to Cut Your Own Fade at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own fade at home is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, a comb, scissors, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Before you begin cutting, you'll need to decide on the length you want for your fade. This will depend on your personal preference and the style you're going for. You may want a high fade, a mid fade, or a low fade. Once you've decided on the length, use your clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back to that length.

Step 3: Create the Fade

To create the fade, you'll need to use different clipper guards to gradually shorten the hair as you move up from the sides and back to the top. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard as you move up. Use the comb to blend the hair between the different lengths.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Once you've created the fade, it's time to blend the hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back. Use your scissors to cut the hair on top to the desired length and blend it with the shorter hair on the sides and back using the comb.

Step 5: Clean Up the Edges

Finally, use your clippers to clean up the edges around your ears and neck. You can also use scissors to trim any stray hairs that you may have missed.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Fade

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to create a more gradual fade.
  • Use the comb to blend the hair between different lengths for a more natural look.
  • Take your time and go slowly to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure that the fade is even.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own fade:

Can I cut my own fade without clippers?

No, clippers are necessary for cutting a fade. You can't achieve the same level of precision with scissors alone.

What length should I start with for my fade?

This will depend on your personal preference and the style you're going for. It's a good idea to start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to create a more gradual fade.

What if I mess up my fade?

If you make a mistake, don't worry. Hair grows back, and you can always visit a professional barber to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Fade

After cutting your own fade, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it stays healthy and looks great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair soft and shiny.
  • Visit a professional barber every few months for a trim and to maintain your fade.

Now that you know how to cut your own fade at home, give it a try and see how it turns out. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking fade haircut that you'll be proud to show off.
