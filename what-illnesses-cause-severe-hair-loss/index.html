What Illnesses Cause Severe Hair Loss Understanding The Condition And How To Manage It

Written by Oliver Apr 13, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Hair loss is a common condition experienced by both men and women. However, when hair loss becomes excessive, it can be a sign of an underlying medical condition. In this article, we will discuss the illnesses that cause severe hair loss and how to manage the condition.

Table of Contents:

  1. What is Severe Hair Loss?
  2. Illnesses That Cause Severe Hair Loss
  3. Managing Severe Hair Loss
  4. How to Take Care of Your Hair
  5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is Severe Hair Loss?

Severe hair loss, also known as alopecia, is a condition that causes excessive hair loss from the scalp and other parts of the body. It is different from normal hair loss, which occurs as part of the hair growth cycle.

Severe hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and medical conditions. It can also be a side effect of certain medications.

Illnesses That Cause Severe Hair Loss

There are several illnesses that can cause severe hair loss. These include:

  • Autoimmune diseases, such as alopecia areata and lupus
  • Thyroid disorders, such as hyperthyroidism and hypothyroidism
  • Cancer and cancer treatments, such as chemotherapy and radiation therapy
  • Nutritional deficiencies, such as iron, vitamin D, and protein deficiencies
  • Infections, such as ringworm and folliculitis
  • Medications, such as blood thinners, retinoids, and beta blockers

If you are experiencing severe hair loss, it is important to see a doctor or a dermatologist to determine the underlying cause of the condition.

Managing Severe Hair Loss

The management of severe hair loss depends on the underlying cause of the condition. For example, if the hair loss is caused by an autoimmune disease, treatment may include medication to suppress the immune system. If the hair loss is caused by a thyroid disorder, treatment may include medication to regulate the thyroid hormone levels.

If the hair loss is caused by cancer treatments, such as chemotherapy and radiation therapy, the hair may grow back once the treatments are completed. However, in some cases, the hair loss may be permanent.

If the hair loss is caused by nutritional deficiencies, it is important to address the deficiencies through diet or supplements. Eating a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamin D can help promote hair growth.

How to Take Care of Your Hair

If you are experiencing severe hair loss, it is important to take care of your remaining hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers and dyes
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons
  • Use gentle hair products that are specifically formulated for thinning hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair, such as braids and ponytails
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamin D

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Can stress cause severe hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss. Stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and cause hair to enter the resting phase prematurely.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: The treatment for hair loss depends on the underlying cause of the condition. In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with treatment.

Q: Is hair loss a sign of cancer?

A: Hair loss can be a side effect of cancer treatments, such as chemotherapy and radiation therapy. However, it is not a definitive sign of cancer.

Severe hair loss can be a distressing condition, but it is important to remember that it can be managed with the right treatment and care. If you are experiencing severe hair loss, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist to determine the underlying cause of the condition and develop a treatment plan.
