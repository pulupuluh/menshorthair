How To Cut Short Hair Korean Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 14, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to change up your hairstyle? Why not try cutting your hair Korean style? Korean short hairstyles are known for their chic and trendy look. In this article, we’ll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut short hair Korean style.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Short Hair Korean Style
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Hair Korean Style
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Korean Short Hair

How to Cut Short Hair Korean Style

Before we get started, it’s important to note that cutting hair is a skill that takes time and practice to master. It’s always best to seek the help of a professional stylist if you’re not confident in your abilities. However, if you’re up for the challenge, here’s how to cut short hair Korean style:

1. Determine the length of your hair.

Decide on the length of your hair. Korean short hairstyles are usually cut above the shoulders. If you’re not sure what length to go for, consult with a stylist or look up some inspiration online.

2. Section your hair.

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to cut. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

3. Start cutting.

Start cutting your hair from the back, working your way to the front. Use a pair of sharp scissors to trim your hair. Remember to cut small sections at a time to avoid making any mistakes.

4. Trim the bangs.

If you want to have bangs, cut them last. Start by sectioning your bangs and then cut them to your desired length.

5. Check for symmetry.

After you’ve finished cutting your hair, check for symmetry. Make sure both sides are even and adjust if necessary.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to cutting short hair Korean style:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair.

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section your hair.

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.

Step 3: Cut the back of your hair.

Start cutting your hair from the back, using small sections at a time.

Step 4: Cut the sides of your hair.

Move on to the sides of your hair, cutting small sections at a time.

Step 5: Trim the bangs.

If you want to have bangs, section them off and cut to your desired length.

Step 6: Check for symmetry.

Check both sides of your hair for symmetry and adjust if necessary.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair Korean Style

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short hair Korean style:

1. Use sharp scissors.

Using sharp scissors will make it easier to cut your hair without damaging it.

2. Cut small sections at a time.

Cutting small sections at a time will help you avoid making any mistakes.

3. Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet.

Cutting your hair when it’s wet can make it harder to get an accurate length.

FAQs

Q. Can I cut my hair Korean style if I have curly hair?

A. Yes, you can still cut your hair Korean style if you have curly hair. However, make sure to consult with a stylist who has experience cutting curly hair.

Q. Can I cut my own hair Korean style?

A. Yes, you can cut your own hair Korean style. However, it’s always best to seek the help of a professional stylist if you’re not confident in your abilities.

Hair Care for Korean Short Hair

Once you’ve cut your hair Korean style, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining your Korean short hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo.

Avoid using shampoos that contain sulfates as they can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition regularly.

Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy.

3. Avoid using heat styling tools.

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, so try to avoid using them as much as possible.

4. Trim your hair regularly.

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and to prevent split ends.

There you have it, a step-by-step guide on how to cut short hair Korean style. Remember, cutting your own hair takes practice, so don’t be too hard on yourself if it doesn’t turn out perfect the first time. Happy cutting!
