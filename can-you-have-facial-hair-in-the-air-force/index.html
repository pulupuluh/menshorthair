Can You Have Facial Hair In The Air Force

Written by William May 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are considering joining the Air Force, you may be wondering if you are allowed to have facial hair. In this article, we will answer this question and provide you with all the necessary information you need to know about facial hair in the Air Force.

Description

The Air Force has a strict policy when it comes to grooming standards. According to the Air Force Instruction (AFI) 36-2903, all Air Force members are required to maintain a neat and professional appearance. This includes the regulation of facial hair.

How to

If you are planning to join the Air Force, it is important to know that you are not allowed to have a beard or any kind of facial hair other than a mustache. The mustache must be neatly trimmed and cannot extend beyond the corners of the mouth. The hair on the upper lip cannot be longer than a quarter-inch and cannot be styled to curl upwards or downwards.

If you are already in the Air Force and want to grow a beard, you will need to get a waiver from your commanding officer. The waiver will only be granted for medical or religious reasons. For example, if you have a skin condition that makes it difficult to shave or if you belong to a religious group that requires you to grow a beard, you may be eligible for a waiver.

Step by Step

Step 1: Determine if you are eligible for a waiver. If you have a medical or religious reason for growing a beard, you may be able to get a waiver from your commanding officer.

Step 2: Submit a request for a waiver to your commanding officer. The request should include a detailed explanation of why you need the waiver and any supporting documentation.

Step 3: Wait for a decision. Your commanding officer will review your request and make a decision. If your request is approved, you will be allowed to grow a beard.

Tips

If you are planning to join the Air Force, it is important to start preparing for the grooming standards early. Make sure you are familiar with the regulations and start practicing good grooming habits.

If you are already in the Air Force and have been granted a waiver to grow a beard, make sure you are taking good care of your facial hair. Keep it clean and well-groomed to maintain a professional appearance.

FAQ

Q: Can I have a beard in the Air Force?

A: No, you are not allowed to have a beard in the Air Force. You can only have a mustache that is neatly trimmed and no longer than a quarter-inch.

Q: Can I get a waiver to grow a beard?

A: Yes, you may be eligible for a waiver if you have a medical or religious reason for growing a beard. You will need to submit a request for a waiver to your commanding officer.

Q: What are the consequences if I violate the grooming standards?

A: If you violate the grooming standards, you may be subject to disciplinary action. This can include a verbal or written reprimand, loss of rank, or even separation from the Air Force.

Hair Care

If you are allowed to have a mustache in the Air Force, it is important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining a neat and professional mustache:

  • Trim your mustache regularly to keep it at the required length.
  • Use mustache wax or gel to keep it in place and prevent it from curling upwards or downwards.
  • Keep your mustache clean by washing it regularly with soap and water.
  • Avoid touching your mustache throughout the day as this can transfer dirt and oil onto it.

Remember, maintaining a neat and professional appearance is an important part of being in the Air Force. Make sure you are following the grooming standards and taking good care of your facial hair.
