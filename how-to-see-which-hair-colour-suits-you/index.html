How To See Which Hair Colour Suits You A Step By Step Guide

How to See Which Hair Colour Suits You | Step by Step Guide in 2023

If you're feeling bored with your hair colour, or simply want to change things up, it can be difficult to know which hair colour will suit you best. With so many different shades to choose from, it's easy to feel overwhelmed. But fear not! With this step by step guide, you'll be able to determine which hair colour suits you in no time.

  • How to Determine Which Hair Colour Suits You

How to Determine Which Hair Colour Suits You

Determining which hair colour suits you best can be a tricky process. However, there are a number of factors to take into consideration when making your decision. These include:

  • Your skin tone
  • Your eye colour
  • Your natural hair colour
  • Your personal style and preferences

By taking these factors into account, you can work out which hair colour will be most flattering for you.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to help you determine which hair colour suits you:

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in determining which hair colour suits you is to determine your skin tone. This can be done by looking at the veins in your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If you can't tell, you have a neutral skin tone.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Colour

Your eye colour can also play a role in determining which hair colour will suit you best. Generally, cool skin tones look best with cool hair colours, while warm skin tones look best with warm hair colours. However, there are exceptions to this rule, so it's important to take your personal preferences into account.

Step 3: Look at Your Natural Hair Colour

Your natural hair colour can also give you some clues as to which hair colour will suit you best. For example, if you have very dark hair, a very light hair colour may not be the most flattering option. Similarly, if you have very light hair, a very dark hair colour may not be the best choice.

Step 4: Consider Your Personal Style and Preferences

Finally, it's important to take your personal style and preferences into account when choosing a hair colour. If you're someone who likes to stand out, you may want to go for a bold hair colour. If you prefer a more natural look, a subtle change may be more appropriate.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Here are some additional tips to help you choose the right hair colour:

  • Consider the maintenance required for different hair colours
  • Take inspiration from celebrities or Instagram influencers
  • Consult with a professional hair stylist
  • Consider trying a temporary hair colour before committing to a permanent change

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing the right hair colour:

What if I have multiple skin tones?

If you have multiple skin tones, it's important to take the dominant tone into account. For example, if you have warm undertones but cool surface tones, you may want to consider warm hair colours.

What if I have grey hair?

If you have grey hair, you may want to consider a hair colour that complements your natural grey. For example, a cool hair colour can help to enhance the natural silver tones in your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen your new hair colour, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's specifically designed for coloured hair
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays with a hat or scarf
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy

By following these tips, you can keep your new hair colour looking vibrant and beautiful for longer.

So there you have it - our step by step guide to determining which hair colour suits you best. Remember to take your skin tone, eye colour, natural hair colour, and personal style into account, and don't be afraid to consult with a professional hair stylist if you need some extra guidance. With a little bit of experimentation, you're sure to find the perfect hair colour for you!
