Can You Do Dreads With Short Hair

Written by Frank Jul 31, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you wondering if it’s possible to do dreads with short hair? The answer is yes, you can! In fact, short hair can be the perfect length for starting dreadlocks. In this blog post, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to create dreads with short hair, along with some tips and frequently asked questions.

How to Do Dreads with Short Hair

If you have short hair, you can still create dreadlocks by following these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Clean hair is essential for starting dreads. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Use a comb to divide your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want. You can use rubber bands to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of hair and hold it taut. Use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb the hair from the ends towards the roots. Repeat this process until the hair starts to knot and form a dread. Use a dread wax or locking gel to help the hair hold its shape.

Step 4: Palm Roll Your Dreads

Once you have backcombed all of the sections, use your palms to roll each dread back and forth. This will help to smooth out any bumps and encourage the hair to knot together.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

As your dreads grow, you’ll need to maintain them by palm rolling and separating any sections that start to merge together. You can also use a crochet hook to tighten any loose hairs.

Tips for Doing Dreads with Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you create and maintain dreads with short hair:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup
  • Be patient – it can take several months for dreads to fully form
  • Avoid using conditioner, as it can make the hair too slippery to dread
  • Wear a durag or scarf at night to protect your dreads

FAQ About Doing Dreads with Short Hair

Can you start dreads with 4-inch hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with hair as short as 4 inches. However, the dreads may take longer to fully form and may be smaller in size.

Can you do dreads with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can do dreads with relaxed hair. However, the chemicals from the relaxer may weaken the hair, making it more prone to breakage. It’s important to take extra care when creating and maintaining dreads with relaxed hair.

Can you do dreads with thin hair?

Yes, you can do dreads with thin hair. However, the dreads may be smaller in size and may take longer to fully form. It’s important to avoid over-backcombing the hair, as this can cause breakage.

Hair Care for Dreads with Short Hair

Once you’ve created your dreads, it’s important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some hair care tips for dreads with short hair:

  • Wash your hair once a week with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using heavy oils or hair products that can build up in your dreads
  • Use a crochet hook to remove any loose hairs
  • Keep your scalp moisturized with a natural oil like tea tree or jojoba oil

With these tips and tricks, you can create and maintain beautiful dreadlocks with short hair. Whether you’re starting from scratch or maintaining existing dreads, taking care of your hair is key to keeping your locks healthy and looking great.
