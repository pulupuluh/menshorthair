How To Make Your Hair Curly Again After Bleaching A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jun 14, 2023 · 10 min read

If you’ve recently bleached your hair, you may have noticed that your once luscious curls have disappeared. Bleaching can damage your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and difficult to style. However, with the right techniques and products, you can make your hair curly again after bleaching. In this guide, we’ll show you how.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Your Hair Curly Again After Bleaching
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair After Bleaching
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

How to Make Your Hair Curly Again After Bleaching

The first step in making your hair curly again after bleaching is to nourish your hair with protein-rich products. Bleaching strips your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Protein helps to strengthen your hair and restore its elasticity, making it easier to style.

Once you’ve nourished your hair, you can start styling it. There are several ways to make your hair curly again after bleaching:

  • Use a curling iron
  • Use rollers
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer
  • Use a perm

Each of these methods has its own advantages and disadvantages, and the best one for you will depend on your hair type and the look you’re going for. We’ll cover each of these methods in more detail below.

Using a Curling Iron

A curling iron is a great tool for creating curls quickly and easily. To use a curling iron:

  1. Section your hair and clip it out of the way so you can work on one section at a time.
  2. Spray the section with a heat protectant spray.
  3. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, holding it in place for a few seconds.
  4. Release the curl and repeat on the rest of your hair.

Keep in mind that using a curling iron can be damaging to your hair if you don’t use a heat protectant spray and if you use it too frequently.

Using Rollers

Rollers are a great option for creating long-lasting curls without the use of heat. To use rollers:

  1. Section your hair and apply a curling lotion or mousse.
  2. Take a section of hair and wrap it around the roller, securing it in place with a clip.
  3. Repeat on the rest of your hair and leave the rollers in for at least an hour.
  4. Remove the rollers and style your curls as desired.

Using rollers can take a bit more time than using a curling iron, but it’s a gentler option for your hair.

Using a Diffuser Attachment on Your Hairdryer

A diffuser attachment on your hairdryer is a great option for creating natural-looking curls. To use a diffuser attachment:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a curl-enhancing product.
  2. Flip your head upside down and place your hair into the diffuser attachment, moving it around to dry your hair.
  3. Once your hair is dry, flip your head back up and style your curls as desired.

Using a diffuser attachment is a gentle option for your hair and can create natural-looking curls.

Using a Perm

A perm is a chemical treatment that creates long-lasting curls. It’s a more permanent option than using a curling iron or rollers, but it can be damaging to your hair if not done properly. To get a perm:

  1. Visit a professional stylist who specializes in perms.
  2. Discuss the type of curls you want and the condition of your hair.
  3. The stylist will apply a solution to your hair, which will break down the bonds that give your hair its shape.
  4. Your hair will be wrapped around perm rods and another solution will be applied to reset the bonds in a new shape.
  5. After a few hours, the rods will be removed and your hair will be rinsed and styled.

A perm is a more drastic option and should only be done by a professional stylist.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to make your hair curly again after bleaching:

  1. Start by nourishing your hair with protein-rich products to restore its elasticity.
  2. Choose a styling method that works best for your hair type and the look you’re going for.
  3. If using a curling iron or diffuser attachment, apply a heat protectant spray to your hair.
  4. If using rollers, apply a curling lotion or mousse to your hair.
  5. Section your hair and start styling it using your chosen method.
  6. Repeat on the rest of your hair until you’ve achieved your desired look.
  7. Finish with a hairspray to hold your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair After Bleaching

Maintaining curly hair after bleaching can be a challenge, but here are some tips to help:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as they can be damaging to your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant product.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making your hair curly again after bleaching:

Can I make my hair curly again after bleaching?

Yes, you can make your hair curly again after bleaching. It may take some time and effort, but with the right techniques and products, you can restore your curls.

What’s the best way to make my hair curly again after bleaching?

The best way to make your hair curly again after bleaching will depend on your hair type and the look you’re going for. Some options include using a curling iron, rollers, a diffuser attachment, or getting a perm.

Can I use heat styling tools on my hair after bleaching?

Yes, you can use heat styling tools on your hair after bleaching, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid further damage to your hair.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care products that are great for maintaining curly hair:

  • Curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Curling lotion or mousse
  • Hairspray
  • Heat protectant spray

Using these products can help keep your curly hair healthy, shiny, and bouncy.


In conclusion, making your hair curly again after bleaching is possible with the right techniques and products. Nourish your hair with protein-rich products, choose a styling method that works best for you, and maintain your curls with proper hair care. With these tips, you can restore your luscious curls and rock your new look.
