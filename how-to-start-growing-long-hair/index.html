How To Start Growing Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 01, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Long, luscious locks are the dream of many people. However, it can be difficult to know where to start when it comes to growing your hair. In this guide, we will go through the steps you need to take to start growing long hair, as well as some tips and tricks for taking care of your hair along the way.

Step 1: Start with a Healthy Scalp

The first step to growing long hair is to make sure your scalp is healthy. This means that you should be washing your hair regularly, but not too often. Over-washing can strip your scalp of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

You should also be exfoliating your scalp once a week to remove any buildup of dead skin cells and product residue. This can be done using a scalp scrub or a simple mixture of sugar and olive oil. Massage the mixture into your scalp using circular motions, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Be Patient

Growing long hair takes time, so it's important to be patient. Your hair will only grow about half an inch per month, so don't expect to see results overnight. It's also important to remember that genetics play a role in how quickly your hair grows, so some people may see results faster than others.

Instead of focusing on the length of your hair, focus on keeping it healthy. This will help prevent breakage and split ends, which can slow down the growth process.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

While it may seem counterintuitive, trimming your hair regularly can actually help it grow longer. This is because split ends and breakage can travel up the hair shaft, causing more damage and slowing down the growth process. By trimming your hair every six to eight weeks, you can prevent this damage and keep your hair healthy.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to your hair, leading to breakage and split ends. If you want to grow long hair, it's important to avoid using these tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature on the lowest setting possible.

Step 5: Use Protective Styles

Protective styles like braids and buns can help protect your hair from damage and breakage. These styles keep your hair tucked away and out of harm's way, allowing it to grow without interference. However, it's important not to keep your hair in these styles for too long, as this can cause tension and breakage.

Step 6: Eat a Healthy Diet

What you eat can have a big impact on the health of your hair. Make sure you are getting enough protein, as this is the building block of hair. Foods like eggs, nuts, and fish are great sources of protein. You should also be eating plenty of fruits and vegetables, which are rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair.

Step 7: Stay Hydrated

Drinking enough water is important for the overall health of your body, including your hair. When you are dehydrated, your hair can become dry and brittle, leading to breakage and split ends. Aim to drink at least eight glasses of water per day, and more if you are exercising or spending time in the sun.

Step 8: Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep is important for many aspects of your health, including the health of your hair. When you are sleep deprived, your body produces more stress hormones, which can lead to hair loss and breakage. Aim to get at least seven to eight hours of sleep per night to keep your hair healthy.

Tips for Growing Long Hair

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension and breakage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps, as these can cause breakage
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and split ends
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep

Frequently Asked Questions

How long will it take to grow long hair?

It can take anywhere from several months to several years to grow long hair, depending on your genetics and how well you take care of your hair. Remember to be patient and focus on keeping your hair healthy.

Can I dye my hair while I'm growing it out?

Yes, you can dye your hair while you're growing it out, but it's important to be gentle and avoid over-processing. Use a gentle dye and try to space out your appointments as much as possible to prevent damage.

What hair care products should I use?

Look for products that are designed for your hair type and are free of harsh chemicals like sulfates and parabens. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.

Hair Care Products for Long Hair

  • Gentle shampoo
  • Conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Hair oil
  • Scalp scrub

By following these steps and taking care of your hair, you can start growing long, healthy locks. Remember to be patient and focus on keeping your hair healthy, and you'll be well on your way to achieving your hair goals.
