How To Shape Overgrown Eyebrows

Written by Oliver Jul 04, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’ve been neglecting your eyebrows for some time, they may have become overgrown and unruly. This can be frustrating, but don’t worry, there are ways to tame them and get them looking great again. In this article, we’ll take you through the steps to shape overgrown eyebrows, as well as some tips to help you maintain them.

Description

Overgrown eyebrows can cause your face to look unkempt and unpolished. They can also make it difficult to apply makeup, and can even get in the way of your vision. Shaping overgrown eyebrows can help to tidy up your look, and give you more control over your appearance.

How to Shape Overgrown Eyebrows

Shaping overgrown eyebrows is a process that takes time and patience. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you’ll need a few tools. These include a pair of sharp, slanted tweezers, a small pair of scissors, and a spoolie brush. Make sure that your tweezers are sharp, as dull ones can cause hair breakage and ingrown hairs.

Step 2: Brush Your Brows

Using your spoolie brush, brush your eyebrows upwards and outwards. This will help you to see where the stray hairs are, and will also give you an idea of the shape you’re working towards.

Step 3: Trim the Long Hairs

Using your small scissors, trim any long hairs that are sticking out of your brow shape. Be careful not to cut too much off, as you can always trim more later.

Step 4: Tweeze the Stray Hairs

Using your tweezers, pluck any stray hairs that are outside of your desired brow shape. Start from the bottom of your brow, and work your way upwards. Be sure to pluck in the direction of hair growth, and avoid over-plucking.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Once you’ve finished tweezing, take a step back and check your progress. Use your spoolie brush to brush your brows upwards and outwards again, and make any final adjustments.

Tips for Maintaining Your Eyebrows

Now that you’ve shaped your overgrown eyebrows, you’ll want to keep them looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid over-plucking. It’s better to pluck a few hairs at a time, rather than going overboard.
  • Brush your brows daily. This will help to keep them in shape, and will also promote healthy hair growth.
  • Consider using eyebrow serum. This can help to nourish and strengthen your brows, and can also promote growth.
  • Get your eyebrows professionally shaped. If you’re not confident in your own abilities, or if your brows require a major overhaul, consider visiting a professional.

FAQ

How often should I shape my eyebrows?

This depends on the individual. Some people may need to shape their brows every few weeks, while others may only need to do it once a month. It’s important to keep an eye on your brows, and to pluck any stray hairs as soon as they appear.

Can I use a razor to shape my eyebrows?

We don’t recommend using a razor to shape your eyebrows, as it can be difficult to control, and can also cause ingrown hairs. Stick to tweezers and small scissors for best results.

Can I shape my eyebrows if I have sensitive skin?

Yes, but be sure to use a gentle touch, and avoid over-plucking. You may also want to apply a soothing cream or serum after shaping to help calm any irritation.

Hair Care

Just like the hair on your head, your eyebrows require care and maintenance. Here are some tips to help keep them healthy:

  • Avoid over-tweezing, as this can cause hair breakage and ingrown hairs.
  • Use a nourishing eyebrow serum to help promote growth and keep your brows healthy.
  • Get your eyebrows professionally shaped every few months to keep them looking their best.
