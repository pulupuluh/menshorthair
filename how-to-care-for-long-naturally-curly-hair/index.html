How To Care For Long Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 17, 2023 · 9 min read

Long curly hair can be stunningly beautiful, but it can also be a challenge to care for. If you have naturally curly hair that's long, you'll need to take extra care to keep it healthy and looking its best. In this article, we'll take you through the steps you need to follow to care for your long curly hair.

How to Care for Long Naturally Curly Hair

Caring for your long naturally curly hair may seem like a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. Follow these simple steps to keep your curls looking their best:

1. Cleanse Your Hair Regularly

It's important to keep your hair clean and free of dirt and oil buildup. However, washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. To strike a balance, aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days using a gentle shampoo that's formulated for curly hair. Follow up with a moisturizing conditioner to help lock in moisture.

2. Detangle Gently

Long curly hair is prone to tangling, so it's important to detangle it gently. Start by using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently remove any knots or tangles. Work in sections, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause breakage and damage to your curls.

3. Moisturize Regularly

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to hydrate your curls and lock in moisture. Apply the product to damp hair and work it through with your fingers. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your curls down and make them look greasy.

4. Style Your Hair with Care

When it comes to styling your long curly hair, less is often more. Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your curls. Instead, opt for natural styles, such as braids, twists, or buns. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

5. Protect Your Hair While You Sleep

Long curly hair can easily become tangled while you sleep. To prevent this, wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or use a silk or satin pillowcase. These materials are gentle on your hair and can help prevent breakage and damage.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to care for your long naturally curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair every 2-3 days using a gentle shampoo formulated for curly hair.
  2. Detangle your hair gently using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  3. Apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair and leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing.
  4. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to hydrate your curls and lock in moisture.
  5. Avoid using heat styling tools, and opt for natural styles instead.
  6. Wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or use a silk or satin pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep.

Tips for Long Naturally Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for your long naturally curly hair:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and strengthen your curls.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and damage.
  • Avoid using rubber bands or tight hair ties, as these can cause breakage and damage to your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and damage to your curls.

FAQs about Long Naturally Curly Hair

Q: How often should I wash my long naturally curly hair?

A: Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days using a gentle shampoo formulated for curly hair. However, if your hair feels particularly dirty or oily, you can wash it more often.

Q: How do I detangle my long naturally curly hair?

A: Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently remove any knots or tangles. Work in sections, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause breakage and damage to your curls.

Q: How can I prevent my long naturally curly hair from becoming tangled while I sleep?

A: Wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or use a silk or satin pillowcase. These materials are gentle on your hair and can help prevent breakage and damage.

Hair Care for Long Naturally Curly Hair

Caring for your long naturally curly hair doesn't have to be a challenge. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can keep your curls healthy, hydrated, and looking their best. Remember to cleanse your hair regularly, detangle gently, moisturize regularly, style with care, and protect your hair while you sleep. With a little bit of effort, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that will turn heads wherever you go!
