Fade Haircut With Long Curly Hair On Top A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you a fan of the fade haircut but also have long curly hair on top? Fear not, as this style can be achieved with a bit of skill and patience. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about getting a fade haircut with long curly hair on top, including how to achieve it, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Achieve a Fade Haircut with Long Curly Hair on Top
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Maintaining a Fade Haircut with Long Curly Hair on Top
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Fade Haircut with Long Curly Hair on Top

Description

A fade haircut with long curly hair on top is a versatile and trendy hairstyle that combines the edgy look of a fade with the natural texture of curly hair. This style is perfect for those who want to keep their curls but also want a modern and clean-cut look.

There are a few different types of fades that can be combined with long curly hair on top, including the skin fade, low fade, high fade, and mid fade. The type of fade you choose will depend on your personal preference and the shape of your face.

How to Achieve a Fade Haircut with Long Curly Hair on Top

The key to achieving a fade haircut with long curly hair on top is to find a skilled barber or stylist who has experience working with curly hair. They will be able to create a fade that complements your curls and creates a seamless transition between the short sides and longer top.

Before getting a fade, it's important to have a clear idea of the type of fade you want and the length you want your curls to be on top. You can bring pictures to show your barber or stylist to ensure that you both have a clear understanding of the final look.

Choosing the Right Products

Once you have your fade, it's important to use the right products to maintain your long curly hair on top. Look for products that are designed for curly hair, such as curl-defining creams, leave-in conditioners, and hair oils. These products will help keep your curls hydrated, defined, and frizz-free.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are the step-by-step instructions for achieving a fade haircut with long curly hair on top:

  1. Choose the type of fade you want (low, mid, high, or skin fade).
  2. Show your barber or stylist pictures of the final look you want.
  3. Ask your barber or stylist to cut the sides and back of your hair short, using clippers.
  4. Ask your barber or stylist to gradually blend the short sides into the longer curls on top.
  5. Ask your barber or stylist to leave the curls on top long enough to create a defined contrast with the short sides.
  6. After the cut is complete, use curl-defining products to enhance your curls and keep them looking their best.

Tips for Maintaining a Fade Haircut with Long Curly Hair on Top

Here are some tips for maintaining a fade haircut with long curly hair on top:

  • Use products designed for curly hair to keep your curls hydrated and defined.
  • Get regular trims to keep your fade looking fresh.
  • Style your curls using your fingers to avoid disrupting the natural texture.
  • Consider getting a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and breakage while you sleep.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fade haircuts with long curly hair on top:

Can I get a fade if I have very tight curls?

Yes, a fade can be achieved with any type of curly hair. It's important to find a barber or stylist who is experienced working with curly hair to ensure that the final look is seamless and flattering.

How often should I get a trim?

It's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your fade looking fresh and your curls healthy.

Hair Care for Fade Haircut with Long Curly Hair on Top

Here are some tips for keeping your long curly hair on top healthy and looking its best:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning mask once a week to hydrate your curls and prevent damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls and cause frizz.
  • Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disrupting the natural texture.
  • Avoid brushing your curls while they are dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls while they are wet.

By following these tips and maintaining a regular hair care routine, you can keep your fade haircut with long curly hair on top looking stylish and healthy.
