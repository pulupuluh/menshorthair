What To Do When Your Hair Starts Falling Out From Bleach

Written by Aprilia Jul 24, 2023 · 8 min read

When it comes to hair color, many of us opt for bleaching our hair to achieve the desired look. However, the harsh chemicals in bleach can cause damage to your hair, including hair loss. If you notice your hair falling out from bleach, don't panic. In this blog post, we will discuss what you can do to prevent further hair loss and promote hair growth.

How To Prevent Further Hair Loss

The first step in dealing with hair loss from bleach is to prevent further damage. Here are some steps you can take:

Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The most obvious step is to stop bleaching your hair altogether. Bleach damages your hair by breaking down the protein bonds that give your hair its strength and elasticity. This damage can result in hair breakage and hair loss. Avoid bleaching your hair until it has fully recovered.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can further damage your hair. The heat can weaken your hair and cause it to break, leading to more hair loss. Avoid using heat styling tools until your hair has fully recovered.

Use Gentle Hair Products

Choose hair products that are gentle and nourishing. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals like sulfates and parabens. Look for products that contain natural ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, and shea butter. These ingredients can help repair and strengthen your hair.

Step By Step Guide To Promote Hair Growth

If your hair is already falling out from bleach, it's essential to take steps to promote hair growth. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Trim Your Hair

The first step is to trim your hair. This will help remove any damaged hair and prevent further breakage. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy.

Step 2: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes every day. This will increase blood flow to your hair follicles and promote hair growth.

Step 3: Apply Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products available in the market that can help promote hair growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen. These ingredients can help strengthen your hair and promote hair growth.

Step 4: Eat A Healthy Diet

Your diet plays a crucial role in promoting hair growth. Eat a diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals. Include foods like eggs, nuts, leafy greens, and avocados in your diet. These foods are rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.

Tips To Prevent Hair Loss From Bleach

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair loss from bleach:

Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage. Limit hair washing to two to three times a week.

Use A Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using a brush on wet hair. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will prevent hair breakage and hair loss.

Protect Your Hair From The Sun

The sun's UV rays can damage your hair, leading to dryness and breakage. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.

FAQs

Can Bleached Hair Grow Back?

Yes, bleached hair can grow back. However, it's essential to take steps to promote hair growth and prevent further damage.

How Long Does It Take For Hair To Regrow After Bleaching?

The time it takes for hair to regrow after bleaching varies from person to person. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months for hair to regrow.

Hair Care

Finally, taking care of your hair is essential in preventing hair loss from bleach. Here are some hair care tips:

Oil Your Hair Regularly

Oiling your hair regularly can help moisturize and nourish your hair. Use natural oils like coconut oil, olive oil, or argan oil to oil your hair.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids. These hairstyles can pull on your hair, leading to breakage and hair loss.

Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy.

In conclusion, hair loss from bleach is a common problem. However, taking steps to prevent further damage and promote hair growth can help you get your healthy hair back. Follow the steps outlined in this blog post and take care of your hair to prevent further damage.
