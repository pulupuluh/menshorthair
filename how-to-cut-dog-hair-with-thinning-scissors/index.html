How To Cut Dog Hair With Thinning Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're a pet owner, you know how important it is to keep your furry friend looking and feeling good. Regular grooming is essential for maintaining your dog's health and happiness. However, taking your pet to the groomer can be expensive, and sometimes you just want to take matters into your own hands. If you're looking for a way to trim your dog's hair at home, thinning scissors can be a great tool. In this article, we'll show you how to cut dog hair with thinning scissors like a pro.

What are Thinning Scissors?

Thinning scissors are scissors with notched blades that are designed to thin out hair rather than cut it all at once. They have a serrated edge that removes some of the bulk from the hair without leaving a blunt edge. This makes them ideal for grooming dogs who have thick, coarse, or curly hair.

How to Cut Dog Hair with Thinning Scissors

Step 1: Prepare Your Dog

The first step in cutting your dog's hair is to make sure they are clean and dry. Give your dog a bath and allow them to dry completely before you begin grooming. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of their coat, making it easier to trim.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting your dog's hair, make sure you have all the tools you need. This includes a pair of thinning scissors, a comb, and a brush. You may also want to use some clips to keep your dog's hair out of the way while you work.

Step 3: Section Your Dog's Hair

Use the comb to section your dog's hair and clip it out of the way. You want to work on one section at a time, starting at the back of your dog's neck and working your way down to their tail.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning scissors at a slight angle and use them to cut into the hair, starting at the tips and working your way up. The notched blades will remove some of the bulk from the hair, creating a more natural look. Be sure to work in small sections and comb the hair regularly to check your progress.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

As you work your way down your dog's body, use the thinning scissors to blend the hair so that it looks even and natural. This will help avoid any choppy or uneven spots.

Step 6: Trim the Face

When trimming your dog's face, use the thinning scissors to create a natural-looking shape. Be careful not to cut too close to the eyes or mouth, as this can be uncomfortable for your pet.

Step 7: Finish Up

When you're finished cutting your dog's hair, use a brush to smooth out any rough spots. You can also use a pair of straight scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Dog Hair with Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors to cut your dog's hair:

  • Work in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing
  • Be patient and work slowly to avoid cutting too much hair at once
  • Use a comb to help guide the scissors and ensure an even cut
  • Take breaks if your dog becomes restless or uncomfortable
  • Consider watching some online tutorials to get a better idea of how to use thinning scissors effectively

FAQs about Cutting Dog Hair with Thinning Scissors

Can I use regular scissors to cut my dog's hair?

While you could technically use regular scissors to cut your dog's hair, it's not recommended. Regular scissors can leave a blunt edge, which can make your dog's coat look uneven and unnatural. Thinning scissors are specifically designed to thin out the hair and create a more natural look.

How often should I cut my dog's hair?

The frequency with which you should cut your dog's hair depends on their breed and coat type. Dogs with long, thick hair may need to be trimmed every 6-8 weeks, while those with shorter hair may only need a trim every 2-3 months. Consult with your veterinarian or a professional groomer for specific recommendations for your dog.

What should I do if I accidentally cut my dog's skin?

If you accidentally cut your dog's skin while trimming their hair, apply pressure to the wound with a clean cloth. If the bleeding doesn't stop within a few minutes, contact your veterinarian for further instructions.

Hair Care for Dogs

In addition to regular grooming, there are a few things you can do to keep your dog's coat looking and feeling great:

  • Feed your dog a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and essential fatty acids
  • Provide your dog with plenty of fresh water to keep them hydrated
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner designed specifically for dogs
  • Regularly brush your dog's coat to remove any tangles or mats

By following these tips and using thinning scissors to trim your dog's hair, you can help your furry friend look and feel their best. Remember to be patient, take your time, and always prioritize your dog's comfort and safety.
