How To Get Temporary Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 05, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you ever wanted to switch up your look and try out curly hair for a day or two? With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve temporary curly hair without damaging your natural hair. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to get temporary curly hair and some tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Temporary Curly Hair
  • Tips for Getting Temporary Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Temporary Curly Hair

How to Get Temporary Curly Hair

First, start with clean and dry hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the heat tools. Divide your hair into small sections and use a curling wand or curling iron to curl your hair. Start from the bottom and work your way up.

After you've finished curling all of your hair, gently brush through your curls with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to loosen them up. Then, use a hairspray to set your curls in place. If you want more volume, you can tease your hair at the roots using a comb.

You can also use rollers or flexi rods to achieve curls. Start by sectioning your hair and rolling each section onto the rollers or flexi rods. Leave them in for a few hours or overnight, then remove them and style your curls as desired.

If you have naturally straight hair and want to try out curly hair for a day, you can use a temporary hair color spray or hair chalk to add some color to your curls. This will enhance the curls and give them a fun, playful look.

Products You'll Need:

  • Curling wand or curling iron
  • Hairspray
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Wide-tooth comb or fingers
  • Rollers or flexi rods (optional)
  • Temporary hair color spray or hair chalk (optional)

Tips for Getting Temporary Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the heat tools.
  • Divide your hair into small sections to ensure each section is curled evenly.
  • Don't hold the curling wand or iron on your hair for too long to prevent heat damage.
  • Brush through your curls gently to avoid causing frizz.
  • Use a hairspray to set your curls in place.
  • If using rollers or flexi rods, make sure they're secure and won't fall out while you sleep.

FAQs

How long will the curls last?

The duration of your curls depends on your hair type and the products you use. On average, temporary curls can last anywhere from a few hours to a day or two.

Can I use this method on natural curly hair?

Yes, this method can be used on natural curly hair to enhance the curls and add more definition.

Will this damage my hair?

If you use a heat protectant spray and don't hold the curling wand or iron on your hair for too long, it shouldn't cause any damage to your hair. However, it's important to take care of your hair and not use heat tools too often.

Hair Care for Temporary Curly Hair

To maintain your temporary curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much to prevent frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent tangling and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve temporary curly hair without damaging your natural hair. Have fun experimenting with different styles and colors!
