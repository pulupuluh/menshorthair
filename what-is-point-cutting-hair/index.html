Description

If you're looking for a way to add texture and depth to your hair, point cutting might be just what you need. Point cutting is a hair cutting technique that involves using scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a textured, wispy look. This technique is commonly used on hair that is longer than shoulder length, but can be used on any length of hair.

Point cutting is a great way to add movement and volume to your hair, but it should be done by a trained stylist. When done incorrectly, point cutting can lead to uneven layers and jagged edges, which can be difficult to fix.

How to Point Cut Hair

Point cutting is a technique that can be used to add texture to any haircut. Here's how it's done:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into manageable sections. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 2: Hold the Scissors at an Angle

Hold the scissors at a slight angle, with the blades pointing upwards. This will create a jagged edge, which is what you want when point cutting.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Starting at the ends of the hair, make small, quick cuts at an angle. Be sure to cut at different angles to create a textured look. Repeat this process throughout the hair until you achieve the desired look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to point cutting:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an accurate cut.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Section the hair into manageable sections. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Hold the Scissors at an Angle

Hold the scissors at a slight angle, with the blades pointing upwards. This will create a jagged edge, which is what you want when point cutting.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Starting at the ends of the hair, make small, quick cuts at an angle. Be sure to cut at different angles to create a textured look. Repeat this process throughout the hair until you achieve the desired look.

Tips for Point Cutting Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when point cutting:

  • Start with small cuts and gradually work your way up to larger cuts.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Point cutting works best on hair that is longer than shoulder length.
  • Be sure to cut at different angles to create a textured look.

FAQs About Point Cutting Hair

Q: Can point cutting be done on any hair type?

A: Yes, point cutting can be done on any hair type, but it works best on hair that is longer than shoulder length.

Q: Is point cutting a difficult technique to master?

A: Point cutting can be difficult to master, as it requires a lot of practice and skill. It's best to have a trained stylist perform the technique.

Q: How often should I get my hair point cut?

A: It's recommended to get your hair point cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the textured look.

Hair Care After Point Cutting

After getting your hair point cut, it's important to take care of it to maintain the textured look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair, as this can lead to damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair, as this will prevent breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of the hair to keep them hydrated.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a textured, wispy look with point cutting. Remember to always have a trained stylist perform the technique to avoid any mistakes.
