How To Get Rid Of Frizz When Air Drying Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you know that air drying is the best way to keep your hair healthy and avoid damage from heat styling tools. However, air drying can also lead to frizz and unruly hair, which can be frustrating and difficult to manage. But fear not! In this article, weâll show you how to get rid of frizz when air drying hair and keep your locks looking beautiful and healthy.

Description

Frizz happens when the hair cuticle is raised, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft and causing it to swell. This can happen when the hair is exposed to humidity or dry air, or when itâs not properly moisturized. When air drying, itâs important to take steps to prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and shiny.

How to Get Rid of Frizz When Air Drying Hair

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before air drying your hair, make sure it's clean and free of any product buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any residue or buildup from your hair that may cause frizz. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water and gently squeeze out excess water with a towel.

Step 2: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair to help moisturize and protect it from frizz. Focus on the ends of your hair, as they tend to be the driest and most prone to frizz. You can also use a light oil or serum to help smooth your hair and add shine.

Step 3: Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

Avoid using a regular towel to dry your hair, as the rough texture can cause frizz and damage to your hair cuticles. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a soft cotton t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water. This will help prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

If you have curly or wavy hair, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair without disturbing your natural curl pattern. If you prefer to air dry, you can use a curl cream or mousse to help define your curls and prevent frizz. For straight hair, you can use a smoothing serum or cream to help control frizz and add shine.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get rid of frizz when air drying your hair:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying, as this can disturb the cuticle and cause frizz.
  • Don't brush or comb your hair while it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and lead to more frizz.
  • Try sleeping on a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz and hair breakage.
  • Consider getting a professional hair treatment, such as a keratin treatment or deep conditioning mask, to help reduce frizz and improve the overall health of your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I air dry my hair overnight?

A: Yes, you can air dry your hair overnight, but make sure it's completely dry before going to bed to prevent frizz and damage.

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my hair?

A: We recommend using a microfiber towel or a soft cotton t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz and damage to your hair cuticles.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others can go a few days between washes. Just make sure to use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair.

Hair Care

Remember, the key to preventing frizz when air drying your hair is to keep your hair moisturized and avoid rough handling. With the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, frizz-free hair that looks healthy and shiny. So go ahead and embrace your natural texture, and let your hair air dry with confidence!
