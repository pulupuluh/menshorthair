How To Cut Your Own Hair Fade Mens A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline May 18, 2023 · 7 min read

Do you want to save some money and cut your own hair fade mens at home? It’s not as difficult as you may think. With the right tools, a little patience, and some practice, you can achieve a professional-looking fade haircut right in your own bathroom. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair fade mens.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Fade Mens
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Fade Mens
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After the Haircut

How to Cut Your Own Hair Fade Mens

The first step in cutting your own hair fade mens is to gather the right tools. Here are the things you will need:

  • Electric hair clippers
  • Clipper guards of different sizes
  • Comb
  • Haircutting scissors
  • Mirror
  • Hair cutting cape or towel

Once you have all the necessary tools, you can start cutting your own hair fade mens.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair fade mens:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wash your hair and dry it thoroughly before starting the haircut. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get the desired length.
  2. Choose the right clipper guard. Attach the clipper guard to your electric hair clippers. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one until you achieve the desired fade look.
  3. Start cutting. Begin at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up. Use the clippers in an upward motion to create a smooth fade. Be sure to blend the different lengths as you move up your head.
  4. Cut the top. After you’ve finished the sides and back, it’s time to cut the top. Use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. You can use the comb to guide you as you cut.
  5. Blend the top and sides. Use the scissors and clippers to blend the top and sides together. This will ensure a smooth transition between the two lengths.
  6. Check your work. Use the mirror to check your work and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Clean up. Once you’re done cutting, clean up any loose hairs around your neck and ears with a small brush or towel.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Fade Mens

Here are some tips that can help you achieve a great fade haircut:

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to shorter ones until you achieve the desired length.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair fade mens requires patience and practice.
  • Use the mirror to check your work regularly.
  • If you’re not sure about a particular step, watch some online tutorials or ask a friend to help.
  • Invest in good quality hair clippers and scissors. They will make your job easier and give you better results.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does it take to cut your own hair fade mens?

The time it takes to cut your own hair fade mens depends on your skill level and the length of your hair. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour or more.

2. Can I achieve a professional-looking fade haircut at home?

Yes, with the right tools, patience, and practice, you can achieve a professional-looking fade haircut right in your own bathroom.

3. How often should I cut my own hair fade mens?

You should cut your own hair fade mens as often as necessary to maintain the desired length and style. This can vary depending on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade starts to grow out.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After you’ve finished cutting your own hair fade mens, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and health. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the desired length and style.
  • Use hair products like pomade or gel to style your hair and hold the fade in place.

With these tips and a little practice, you can master the art of cutting your own hair fade mens and save some money on haircuts in the long run.
