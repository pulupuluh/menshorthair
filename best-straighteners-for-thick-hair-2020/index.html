Best Straighteners For Thick Hair 2020 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that finding the right straightener can be a challenge. You need a tool that can handle your hair's volume and texture without causing damage or taking forever to get the job done. Fortunately, there are plenty of great straighteners on the market that are up to the task. In this guide, we'll take a look at some of the best straighteners for thick hair in 2020, as well as tips for using them effectively and caring for your hair.

How to Choose a Straightener for Thick Hair

When selecting a straightener for thick hair, there are a few key factors to consider. First and foremost, you want a straightener with high heat settings, as thick hair requires more heat to straighten effectively. Look for a tool with a temperature range of at least 350-400 degrees Fahrenheit. You should also consider the size of the plates; wider plates are better for thick hair, as they can cover more surface area and straighten larger sections at once. Finally, look for a straightener with ceramic or tourmaline plates, as these materials help reduce frizz and protect hair from heat damage.

Top Straighteners for Thick Hair in 2020

Here are some of the best straighteners for thick hair that you can buy in 2020:

  • GHD Platinum+ Styler
  • BaBylissPRO Nano Titanium-Plated Straightening Iron
  • HSI Professional Glider
  • Remington S5500 1" Anti-Static Flat Iron
  • CHI G2 Ceramic and Titanium Hairstyling Iron

Each of these straighteners offers high heat settings, wide plates, and ceramic or tourmaline technology, making them ideal for thick hair.

How to Use a Straightener on Thick Hair

To get the best results when straightening thick hair, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to help minimize damage.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips or ties.
  4. Take one section at a time and clamp it between the straightener plates, starting at the roots and slowly sliding down to the ends.
  5. Repeat on all sections until your hair is fully straightened.
  6. Finish with a smoothing serum or spray to help tame any remaining frizz.

It's important to use your straightener on the right heat setting for your hair type. If your hair is particularly thick or coarse, you may need to use a higher temperature, but be careful not to go too high, as this can cause damage.

Straightening Tips for Thick Hair

These tips can help you get the best results when straightening your thick hair:

  • Use a deep conditioner or hair mask once a week to help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using your straightener on wet hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Don't straighten the same section of hair multiple times, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a brush or comb to help distribute heat evenly and reduce tangles.
  • Let your hair cool completely before styling or brushing to help set the straightened style.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some answers to common questions about straightening thick hair:

How often should I straighten my thick hair?

It's best to avoid straightening your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage. Aim for no more than 2-3 times a week, and be sure to use a heat protectant spray each time.

Can I use a straightener on colored or chemically treated hair?

Yes, but it's important to use a lower heat setting and be gentle when straightening to avoid causing damage or fading the color.

Can I straighten my hair without a heat protectant spray?

It's not recommended. Heat protectant sprays help minimize damage and keep your hair healthy, so it's important to use one each time you straighten.

Caring for Your Hair

Along with using a high-quality straightener and following proper techniques, caring for your hair is key to achieving and maintaining a sleek, straight style. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help minimize dryness and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

With the right straightener and proper care, you can achieve a sleek, straight style that lasts. Follow these tips and recommendations to get the best results for your thick hair.
