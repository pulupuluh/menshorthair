When Do You Stop Losing Hair After Giving Birth

Written by Luffy Jun 13, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you are a new mom, postpartum hair loss may be a concern for you. You may wonder when this hair loss will stop and how long you will have to deal with it. In this article, we will discuss when you can expect to stop losing hair after giving birth and provide tips on how to care for your hair during this time.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Postpartum Hair Loss
  • When to Expect Hair Regrowth

Causes of Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is a temporary condition that affects many new moms. During pregnancy, high levels of hormones in the body cause the hair to stay in the growth phase, resulting in thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, hormone levels drop, causing hair to enter the resting phase and eventually fall out. This process usually begins around three months postpartum and can last up to a year.

When to Expect Hair Regrowth

After the shedding phase, you can expect to see new hair growth. However, it can take time for the hair to grow back to its pre-pregnancy thickness. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. So, if you lost three inches of hair during postpartum shedding, it can take six months to a year to see full regrowth.

Hair Care Tips

During postpartum hair loss, it is essential to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips to help minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth:

1. Be Gentle

Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage the hair. Instead, opt for gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners and air-dry your hair whenever possible.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help support healthy hair growth. Be sure to include plenty of protein, iron, and B vitamins in your diet.

3. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair and scalp hydrated, which is essential for healthy hair growth.

4. Take Hair Supplements

Supplements such as biotin and folic acid can help support healthy hair growth. However, be sure to talk to your doctor before taking any supplements to ensure they are safe for you and your baby.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Unfortunately, postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented. However, taking good care of your hair and scalp can help minimize hair loss and support healthy hair growth.

2. How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss can last up to a year, but it usually peaks around three to four months postpartum.

3. Will my hair return to its pre-pregnancy thickness?

Yes, with time, your hair should return to its pre-pregnancy thickness. However, it can take several months or even up to a year for full regrowth.

4. Is postpartum hair loss normal?

Yes, postpartum hair loss is a normal and temporary condition that affects many new moms.

Overall, postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating experience for new moms. However, by taking good care of your hair and scalp and being patient, you can minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. If you have any concerns about postpartum hair loss or hair regrowth, be sure to talk to your doctor or a qualified hair care professional.
