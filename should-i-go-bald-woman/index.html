Should I Go Bald Woman A Guide To Making The Decision

Written by Eveline Apr 20, 2023 · 6 min read

For some women, going bald can be a liberating and empowering choice. However, it’s not a decision that should be taken lightly. If you’re considering shaving your head, there are several factors to consider before making the leap. In this guide, we’ll explore the pros and cons of going bald, as well as tips for maintaining a bald head.

Table of Contents

  • Pros and Cons of Going Bald
  • How to Shave Your Head
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Shaving Your Head
  • Tips for Maintaining a Bald Head
  • FAQs about Going Bald
  • Hair Care for Bald Women

Pros and Cons of Going Bald

Before making the decision to go bald, it’s important to consider the pros and cons.

Pros:

  • Low maintenance: Bald heads require little to no maintenance, making them a great option for women who don’t want to spend hours styling their hair.
  • Affordable: You’ll save money on haircuts, styling products, and other hair-related expenses.
  • Empowering: Going bald can be a powerful statement, challenging traditional beauty standards and societal norms.

Cons:

  • Stigma: Unfortunately, bald women still face a stigma in society, with many people associating baldness with illness or lack of femininity.
  • Weather concerns: Bald heads offer little protection from the sun and cold, so you’ll need to be mindful of weather conditions.
  • Regrowth: If you decide you don’t like your bald head, regrowing your hair can take months or even years.

How to Shave Your Head

If you’ve decided to take the plunge and go bald, the first step is to shave your head. While you can certainly go to a salon or barbershop to have it done, it’s also possible to do it yourself at home.

Step-by-Step Guide to Shaving Your Head

Follow these steps to shave your head:

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. Use clippers to trim your hair as short as possible.
  2. Apply shaving cream or gel to your scalp.
  3. Using a razor, shave your head in the direction of hair growth.
  4. Rinse your head with warm water and pat dry with a towel.
  5. Apply moisturizer or aftershave to soothe your scalp.

Tips for Maintaining a Bald Head

Once you’ve shaved your head, you’ll need to take care of your scalp to keep it healthy and looking its best.

  • Moisturize daily: Use a gentle moisturizer to keep your scalp hydrated and prevent dryness.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun: Wear a hat or use sunscreen to protect your scalp from the sun’s damaging rays.
  • Exfoliate regularly: Use a gentle exfoliating scrub to remove dead skin cells and prevent ingrown hairs.

FAQs about Going Bald

Q: Will shaving my head make my hair grow back thicker?

A: No, this is a common myth. Shaving your head won’t change the thickness or texture of your hair.

Q: Will going bald damage my hair?

A: No, going bald won’t damage your hair. If you decide to grow your hair back, it may take some time to regain its previous length and texture.

Q: Will going bald affect my job prospects?

A: Unfortunately, some employers may discriminate against bald people. However, it’s important to remember that discrimination based on appearance is illegal in many countries.

Hair Care for Bald Women

If you’ve decided to keep your head bald, you’ll still need to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Cleanse your scalp: Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your scalp and remove any dirt or oil.
  • Moisturize: Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your scalp hydrated.
  • Protect your scalp: Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold.

Ultimately, the decision to go bald is a personal one. If you’re considering it, weigh the pros and cons carefully, and be sure to take care of your scalp to keep it healthy and looking its best.
