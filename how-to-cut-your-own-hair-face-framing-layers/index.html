How To Cut Your Own Hair Face Framing Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. One of the most popular haircuts is face framing layers, which can add dimension and movement to your hair. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair face framing layers.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Face Framing Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQ About Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair Face Framing Layers

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it's important to gather the necessary tools and products. You will need:

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Once you have these items, you're ready to begin cutting your own hair face framing layers.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair face framing layers:

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut. Use hair clips to hold the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Decide on the length of your layers. Hold the hair you want to cut straight out from your head and cut the ends at a slight angle.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair at a time, moving towards the front of your face. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, face-framing effect.
  5. Once you've cut both sides of your face framing layers, blend them together by cutting into the ends at an angle.
  6. Release the rest of your hair and check the overall length and shape of your layers. Make any necessary adjustments.

Congratulations, you've just cut your own hair face framing layers!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you cut your own hair face framing layers:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go. It's important to see the back of your head to ensure a symmetrical cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, ask a friend or family member to assist you.

FAQ About Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair:

Is it easy to cut your own hair face framing layers?

It can be challenging to cut your own hair, especially if you're new to it. However, with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a professional-looking cut.

How often should I cut my own hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and length. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my own hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your own hair, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or seek professional help if necessary.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shape. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools such as blow dryers and flat irons too frequently.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain your desired length.

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully cut your own hair face framing layers. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if needed. Happy cutting!
