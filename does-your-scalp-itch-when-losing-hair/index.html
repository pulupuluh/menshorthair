Does Your Scalp Itch When Losing Hair

Written by Petter Mar 06, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss and an itchy scalp? You're not alone. Many people who are losing hair also experience scalp itching. In this blog post, we will explore the connection between hair loss and scalp itching, and provide tips on how to alleviate your discomfort.

Table of Contents

  • Why Does Your Scalp Itch When Losing Hair?
  • How to Alleviate Scalp Itching
  • Tips for Healthy Hair and Scalp
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Why Does Your Scalp Itch When Losing Hair?

Scalp itching is a common symptom of hair loss, and it can be caused by a variety of factors. One of the main reasons for scalp itching is the buildup of sebum, a natural oil produced by the scalp. When hair follicles become clogged with sebum, it can cause inflammation and irritation, leading to itching.

Another possible cause of scalp itching is a fungal infection. Fungal infections can lead to hair loss and can also cause the scalp to become itchy and flaky. Other potential causes of scalp itching include stress, hormonal changes, and certain medications.

How to Alleviate Scalp Itching

If you're experiencing scalp itching, there are several things you can do to alleviate your discomfort. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Using a gentle shampoo can help to remove excess oil and buildup from your scalp without causing irritation. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for sensitive scalps or that contain natural ingredients like tea tree oil or aloe vera.

2. Avoid hot water

Hot water can strip your scalp of its natural oils and cause dryness and irritation. Try to use lukewarm water when washing your hair, and avoid using hot styling tools like hair dryers or straighteners.

3. Use a scalp treatment

There are a variety of scalp treatments available that can help to soothe and nourish your scalp. Look for products that contain ingredients like menthol or peppermint oil, which can provide a cooling sensation and relieve itching.

4. Reduce stress

Stress can exacerbate hair loss and scalp itching. Try to reduce your stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.

Tips for Healthy Hair and Scalp

Here are a few tips for maintaining healthy hair and scalp:

1. Eat a balanced diet

A balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

2. Stay hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help to keep your scalp and hair hydrated, which can prevent dryness and itching.

3. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals like sulfates, which can strip your hair and scalp of its natural oils.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun can cause damage to your hair and scalp. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair and scalp from harmful UV rays.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can hair loss cause scalp itching?

A: Yes, hair loss can cause scalp itching. When hair follicles become clogged with sebum, it can cause inflammation and irritation, leading to itching.

Q: What is the best shampoo for scalp itching?

A: Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for sensitive scalps or that contain natural ingredients like tea tree oil or aloe vera.

Q: Can stress cause scalp itching?

A: Yes, stress can exacerbate hair loss and scalp itching. Try to reduce your stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.

Q: Can a fungal infection cause scalp itching?

A: Yes, a fungal infection can cause hair loss and can also cause the scalp to become itchy and flaky.


In conclusion, if you're experiencing scalp itching while losing hair, it's important to address the issue as soon as possible. By following the tips outlined in this blog post, you can alleviate your discomfort and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and consult a healthcare professional if you have any concerns.
