Celebrities Over 60 Hairstyles

As we age, we may start to feel like our hair is no longer as youthful as it once was. But don't worry, there are plenty of celebrities over 60 who are rocking amazing hairstyles. In this article, we will discuss some of the most popular hairstyles for women over 60, along with tips on how to achieve them and take care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Women over 60 have a wide range of hairstyles to choose from, including short cuts, long layers, and even braids. It's all about finding a style that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Many celebrities over 60, such as Jane Fonda and Helen Mirren, have embraced their gray hair and opt for shorter, low-maintenance cuts that still look stylish and chic.

How To

The first step in achieving a great hairstyle is to find a stylist who understands your hair and can help you choose a flattering cut. Look for someone who has experience working with mature hair and can offer suggestions based on your face shape and lifestyle.

Choosing a Cut

When it comes to choosing a cut, there are a few things to keep in mind. Shorter cuts tend to be more low-maintenance and can make your face appear more lifted. Bangs can also be a great way to refresh your look and add some dimension to your hair.

Styling Your Hair

Once you have your cut, it's important to know how to style it. Many women over 60 opt for air-drying their hair and using minimal styling products to keep their hair looking natural and healthy. However, if you prefer a more polished look, there are plenty of styling products available that can help you achieve the look you want.

Step By Step

If you're looking for a specific hairstyle to try, we've got you covered. Here are step-by-step instructions for three popular hairstyles for women over 60:

The Pixie Cut

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use a small amount of styling wax or pomade to add texture and definition to your hair.
  3. Use your fingers to create volume at the crown of your head.
  4. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

The Bob

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, focusing on the ends to create a slight curl.
  3. Use a flat iron to straighten any areas that need it.
  4. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

The Shag

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use a curling iron to add loose waves throughout your hair.
  3. Use your fingers to create volume at the crown of your head.
  4. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling your hair:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Invest in a good quality hair dryer and styling tools.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Consider trying a new hair color to refresh your look.

FAQ

What are some low-maintenance hairstyles for women over 60?

Short cuts such as the pixie and bob are great options for women who want a low-maintenance style. You can also try a layered cut or a simple updo.

What hair color is best for women over 60?

Many women over 60 opt for natural, gray hair. However, if you want to add some color, consider trying a warm, golden blonde or a rich brown.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your hair healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much styling product, as it can cause buildup and make your hair look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.
