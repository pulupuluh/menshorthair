How To Make Your Hair Look Pretty For School Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Mar 10, 2023 · 11 min read

Heading back to school means getting back into the routine of early wake-ups, homework, and of course, looking your best. One of the key components of your daily look is your hair. In this guide, we’ll give you some tips and tricks on how to make your hair look pretty for school without spending too much time or effort. So, let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Your Hair Look Pretty for School
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Pretty Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Make Your Hair Look Pretty for School

There are many ways to make your hair look pretty for school, and the best one depends on your hair type, length, and personal preferences. Here are some popular options:

Braids

Braids are a classic hairstyle that can make you look chic, cute, or edgy, depending on the type of braid you choose. Some popular braids for school are:

  • Fishtail braid
  • Dutch braid
  • French braid
  • Waterfall braid
  • Milkmaid braid

Braids work well with all hair types and lengths, but they require some practice and patience, especially if you’re doing them on your own. You can find many tutorials on YouTube or Pinterest to help you master the art of braiding.

Ponytail

A ponytail is a quick and easy hairstyle that can look cute and sophisticated at the same time. To make your ponytail look prettier, you can:

  • Add a bow or ribbon
  • Create a voluminous ponytail by teasing your hair at the crown
  • Add some braids or twists
  • Make a messy bun or a braided bun

Ponytails work well with all hair types and lengths, but they can put some strain on your hair if you wear them too tight or too often. To avoid hair breakage or damage, use soft hair ties and don’t tie your hair too high or too low.

Curls

Curls can add volume, movement, and texture to your hair, making it look fuller and more glamorous. There are many ways to curl your hair, such as:

  • Using a curling iron
  • Using hot rollers
  • Using a flat iron
  • Using a hair dryer and a round brush

Curls work well with medium to long hair, but they can be tricky to achieve if you have short or fine hair. To make your curls last longer, use a heat protectant spray, a hairspray, or a mousse, and avoid touching your hair too much.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a simple step-by-step guide on how to make your hair look pretty for school:

  1. Start with clean and dry hair. If your hair is greasy or oily, wash it the night before or use a dry shampoo. If your hair is wet, blow-dry it or let it air-dry.
  2. Choose the hairstyle you want to do. You can use the ones we suggested earlier or come up with your own.
  3. Gather the tools and products you need. This may include a comb, a brush, hair ties, bobby pins, a curling iron, a flat iron, a hairspray, a heat protectant spray, a mousse, or any other styling product you prefer.
  4. Follow the instructions for your chosen hairstyle. You can use a tutorial, a YouTube video, or a friend’s help if you need it.
  5. Finish with a hairspray or a shine spray, if desired. This will help your hair stay in place and look polished.

Tips for Maintaining Your Pretty Hair

Once you’ve made your hair look pretty for school, here are some tips on how to keep it that way:

  • Don’t overdo it. Avoid using too much heat, chemicals, or styling products, as they can damage your hair and make it look dull or frizzy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and rain. Wear a hat, a scarf, or use a UV protectant spray to shield your hair from environmental stressors.
  • Get regular trims. Even if you’re growing your hair out, it’s important to trim the split ends every 6-8 weeks to prevent breakage and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Eat a healthy diet. Your hair needs nutrients like protein, iron, and vitamins to grow and thrive, so make sure you eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein.
  • Stay hydrated. Drinking enough water can help your hair stay moisturized and shiny, so aim for at least 8 glasses of water per day.

FAQs

Q: Can I make my hair look pretty for school without using heat?

A: Yes, you can. There are many heatless hairstyles that you can try, such as braids, twists, buns, and updos. You can also use foam rollers or braiding techniques to create curls or waves without heat.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or sweat a lot, you may need to wash your hair every other day. If you have dry or curly hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type and avoid washing your hair with hot water, as it can strip away the natural oils.

Q: Can I color my hair for school?

A: It depends on your school’s dress code and your personal preferences. If your school allows it, you can color your hair with semi-permanent or temporary dyes that wash out after a few weeks. If you want a more permanent color, you may need to get your parents’ permission or wait until you’re older.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some general hair care tips that can help you maintain healthy and pretty hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling or breaking your hair.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as it can cause frizz and damage. Instead, blot your hair gently with a soft towel or a T-shirt.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet or damp hair, as it can cause steam damage and breakage. Always use a heat protectant spray or cream before using hot tools.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids, buns, or ponytails. They can cause hair loss and damage over time.
  • Get enough sleep and manage your stress levels, as stress and lack of sleep can affect your hair health and growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can make your hair look pretty for school and feel confident and stylish all day long. Remember to take good care of your hair and experiment with different styles and products until you find the ones that work best for you.
