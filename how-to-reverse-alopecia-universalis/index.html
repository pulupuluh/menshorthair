How To Reverse Alopecia Universalis A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you or someone you know suffering from alopecia universalis? This autoimmune disorder causes hair loss on the entire body, including the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and even the pubic area. It can be a devastating condition that affects a person's self-esteem and confidence. But don't lose hope - there are ways to reverse alopecia universalis. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to do just that. Let's get started!

Description of Alopecia Universalis

Alopecia universalis is a rare autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss on the entire body, including the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and even the pubic area. It is a more severe form of alopecia areata, which causes patchy hair loss on the scalp. Alopecia universalis is thought to be caused by a malfunction in the immune system, which attacks the hair follicles and prevents hair growth. There is no known cure for alopecia universalis, but there are treatments that can help reverse the condition.

How to Reverse Alopecia Universalis

Step 1: Consult with a Dermatologist

If you suspect that you have alopecia universalis, the first step is to consult with a dermatologist. They will be able to diagnose the condition and recommend treatment options. Your dermatologist may also recommend blood tests to check for underlying conditions that may be contributing to your hair loss.

Step 2: Treat Underlying Conditions

If your dermatologist finds an underlying condition that is contributing to your hair loss, such as a thyroid disorder or vitamin deficiency, they will recommend treatment for that condition. Treating the underlying condition may help reverse your hair loss.

Step 3: Use Topical Treatments

There are several topical treatments that may help reverse alopecia universalis. These include corticosteroids, which reduce inflammation and suppress the immune system, and minoxidil, which promotes hair growth. Your dermatologist may also recommend a topical immunotherapy, which involves applying a chemical to the scalp to stimulate hair growth.

Step 4: Consider Oral Medications

In some cases, your dermatologist may recommend oral medications to help reverse alopecia universalis. These may include corticosteroids, immunosuppressants, and biologic drugs. These medications work by suppressing the immune system and reducing inflammation.

Step 5: Try Hair Restoration Surgery

If other treatments have not been successful, hair restoration surgery may be an option. This involves transplanting hair follicles from one area of the body to the scalp. Hair restoration surgery can be expensive and may not be covered by insurance.

Tips for Reversing Alopecia Universalis

Here are some tips that may help you reverse alopecia universalis:

  • Reduce stress in your life, as stress can trigger autoimmune disorders.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.
  • Take supplements, such as biotin and iron, to promote hair growth.
  • Avoid harsh hair treatments, such as coloring and perming, which can damage hair follicles.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or using sunscreen.

FAQs About Alopecia Universalis

Can alopecia universalis be cured?

There is no known cure for alopecia universalis, but there are treatments that can help reverse the condition.

What causes alopecia universalis?

Alopecia universalis is thought to be caused by a malfunction in the immune system, which attacks the hair follicles and prevents hair growth.

Is alopecia universalis hereditary?

Alopecia universalis is not always hereditary, but there may be a genetic component to the condition.

Hair Care for Alopecia Universalis

If you have alopecia universalis, it's important to take care of your remaining hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't damage your hair follicles.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, which can damage hair follicles.
  • Consider wearing a wig or hairpiece to protect your scalp from the sun and to boost your confidence.
  • Massage your scalp daily to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.

By following these tips and working with your dermatologist, you can take steps to reverse alopecia universalis and regain your confidence.
