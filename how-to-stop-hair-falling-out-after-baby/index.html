How To Stop Hair Falling Out After Baby Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Feb 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you a new mom experiencing postpartum hair loss? You're not alone. Many women experience hair loss after giving birth due to hormonal changes. But don't worry, there are ways to stop and prevent hair loss. Read on to learn how.

Description

Postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many new moms. During pregnancy, hormones prevent hair from falling out like it normally would. This often results in a full head of hair during pregnancy. However, after giving birth, hormones return to normal and hair may fall out in larger quantities than usual. This can be alarming for new moms, but it is a normal process that usually resolves on its own in a few months.

How to Stop Hair Falling Out After Baby

Step 1: Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet is important for overall health, but it can also help prevent hair loss. Make sure you're consuming enough nutrients, such as iron, zinc, and biotin, which are essential for healthy hair growth. Foods like spinach, beans, and eggs are great sources of these nutrients.

Step 2: Take Care of Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair and avoid pulling or tugging on it. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle hair instead of a brush. Avoid using heat styling tools, like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage hair and exacerbate hair loss.

Step 3: Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to find ways to manage stress levels. Practice relaxation techniques, like yoga or meditation, or engage in physical activity to reduce stress.

Step 4: Consult a Doctor

If you're experiencing excessive hair loss or if hair loss continues for more than a few months, consult a doctor. They may recommend supplements or other treatments to help prevent hair loss.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles, like braids or ponytails, which can damage hair
  • Avoid chemical treatments, like perms or hair dye, as they can damage hair
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid over-washing hair, as this can strip hair of natural oils

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, hair usually grows back after postpartum hair loss. However, it may take several months for hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

A: Breastfeeding does not directly cause hair loss. However, hormonal changes during breastfeeding can contribute to hair loss.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for preventing hair loss. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing hair. Be gentle when detangling hair and avoid using heat styling tools. Eating a healthy diet and managing stress levels can also help prevent hair loss.

By following these tips and tricks, you can stop and prevent hair loss after having a baby. Remember, postpartum hair loss is a normal process that usually resolves on its own over time. If you're concerned about excessive hair loss, consult a doctor.
