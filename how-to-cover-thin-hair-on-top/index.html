How To Cover Thin Hair On Top Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Aug 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Having thin hair on top can be a frustrating problem for both men and women. It can affect your overall appearance and confidence, especially when you're in public. Fortunately, there are several ways to cover thin hair on top without resorting to expensive treatments or surgeries. In this article, we'll show you how to deal with thin hair on top using practical and easy-to-follow tips.

How to Cover Thin Hair on Top

Here are some effective ways to cover thin hair on top:

1. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are tiny keratin fibers that cling to your existing hair, making it appear thicker and fuller. They are easy to apply and come in a variety of colors to match your hair. Simply sprinkle the fibers on your thinning areas and gently pat them down with your fingers.

2. Try a Hairpiece

A hairpiece, also known as a toupee, is a natural-looking wig that covers your thinning areas. They come in different sizes and styles to suit your preferences. You can choose from synthetic or human hairpieces depending on your budget and preference.

3. Use Hair Extensions

Hair extensions are a great way to add volume and thickness to your hair. There are several types of hair extensions available, including clip-in, tape-in, and sew-in extensions. Clip-in extensions are the most convenient and easy to use, while sew-in extensions are more permanent.

4. Get a Haircut

Getting a new haircut can also help cover thin hair on top. A shorter, layered haircut can add volume and texture to your hair, making it appear fuller. Avoid long hairstyles that can make your hair look flat and lifeless.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cover Thin Hair on Top

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cover thin hair on top:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add volume and texture to your hair, making it easier to style.

Step 2: Apply Hair Fibers

If you're using hair fibers, sprinkle them on your thinning areas and gently pat them down with your fingers. Use a hair fiber spray to set the fibers in place.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired using a hair dryer or styling tools. Use a hair spray or gel to keep your hair in place.

Step 4: Try a Hairpiece or Extension

If you're using a hairpiece or extension, follow the manufacturer's instructions on how to apply and style it. Make sure it looks natural and blends well with your existing hair.

Step 5: Finish with Hair Spray

Finish your hairstyle with a hair spray or gel to keep it in place. Avoid touching your hair too much to prevent the fibers or hairpiece from falling off.

Tips to Cover Thin Hair on Top

Here are some additional tips to cover thin hair on top:

1. Avoid Using Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dyes. These can damage your hair and make it thinner over time.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. This can cause breakage and make your hair thinner.

3. Choose the Right Hair Products

Choose hair products that are specifically designed for thinning hair. Look for volumizing shampoos, conditioners, and hair sprays that can add volume and texture to your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Are hair fibers safe to use?

A: Yes, hair fibers are safe to use and do not cause any damage to your hair or scalp.

Q: How long do hair extensions last?

A: Hair extensions can last up to six months, depending on the type and quality of the extensions.

Q: Can I swim or shower with a hairpiece?

A: Yes, you can swim or shower with a hairpiece, but make sure to use a waterproof adhesive to keep it in place.

Hair Care for Thin Hair

Here are some hair care tips for thin hair:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it thinner.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth. Use gentle circular motions with your fingertips.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eat a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and biotin. These nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Dealing with thin hair on top can be challenging, but there are several ways to cover it up and boost your confidence. Whether you choose to use hair fibers, hairpieces, or extensions, make sure to follow the instructions carefully and choose products that are safe for your hair. Remember to take good care of your hair by avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling, and eating a healthy diet. With the right approach, you can achieve a fuller, thicker-looking head of hair.
