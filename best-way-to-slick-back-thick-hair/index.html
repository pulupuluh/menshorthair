Best Way to Slick Back Thick Hair: How to, Tips, and FAQ

Slicked-back hair has been a popular hairstyle for men for decades. It’s a timeless look that can add sophistication and style to any outfit. However, for men with thick hair, achieving the perfect slicked-back look can be challenging. In this article, we’ll explore the best way to slick back thick hair, including how to, tips, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Slick Back Thick Hair
  • Tips for Slicking Back Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Slicking Back Thick Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Slicked-Back Hair

How to Slick Back Thick Hair

The first step in slicking back thick hair is to choose the right product. Look for hair wax or pomade with a strong hold. These products will help keep your hair in place throughout the day. Avoid using gel, as this can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Next, start with clean, dry hair. Apply a small amount of hair wax or pomade to your fingertips and rub it between your hands to warm it up. Then, work the product through your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward. Make sure to evenly distribute the product throughout your hair.

Tips for Slicking Back Thick Hair

If you have thick hair, you may need to apply more product than someone with thin hair. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different amounts of product to find what works best for you.

If you want a more natural, relaxed look, use a comb to gently loosen the slicked-back hair. This will create a more tousled, effortless style.

For a more polished look, use a comb to create a sharp part in your hair before slicking it back. This will give your hair a clean, sophisticated look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Slicking Back Thick Hair

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

2. Choose a hair wax or pomade with a strong hold.

3. Apply a small amount of product to your fingertips and rub it between your hands to warm it up.

4. Work the product through your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward.

5. Use a comb to create a sharp part in your hair, if desired.

6. Comb your hair back, starting at the crown of your head and working your way down.

7. Use your fingers to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

8. Use a comb to gently loosen the slicked-back hair, if desired.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Can I slick back thick hair without using product?

A. It’s possible to slick back thick hair without using product, but it may not stay in place as well. Using a hair wax or pomade will help keep your hair in place throughout the day.

Q. How often should I wash my hair if I’m slicking it back?

A. It’s best to wash your hair every other day if you’re slicking it back. This will help prevent your hair from becoming too oily or greasy.

Q. Can I slick back thick hair if it’s curly?

A. Yes, you can slick back curly hair. However, you may need to use more product to keep your curls in place.

Hair Care Tips for Slicked-Back Hair

If you’re slicking back your hair on a regular basis, it’s important to take good care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some hair care tips for slicked-back hair:

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and make it more difficult to slick back.

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this is gentler on your hair than a brush.

- Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your hairstyle.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect slicked-back hairstyle for thick hair. Experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you, and don’t be afraid to ask your stylist for advice.
