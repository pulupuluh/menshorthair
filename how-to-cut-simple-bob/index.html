How To Cut A Simple Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy style at home. The simple bob is a classic and versatile haircut that looks great on all face shapes and hair types. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut a simple bob in relaxed English, with easy-to-follow steps and helpful tips.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Prepping Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Texturizing Your Hair
  6. Finishing Your Hair
  7. Tips for Cutting a Simple Bob
  8. FAQs
  9. Hair Care After Cutting a Simple Bob

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Optional: Thinning shears for texturizing your hair

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting. Comb out any tangles and knots, and part your hair in the middle or on one side, depending on your preference.

Sectioning Your Hair

Using clips or hair ties, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Start by clipping the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Cutting Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, use your comb to comb the hair straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want to cut it, and cut straight across with your scissors. Repeat this step for each section of your hair, working your way up to the top section.

Texturizing Your Hair

If you want to add texture to your hair, use thinning shears to cut into the ends of your hair. Hold a small section of your hair between your fingers, and use the thinning shears to cut into the ends at an angle. This will create a more natural, layered look.

Finishing Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting and texturizing your hair, remove the clips or hair ties and shake out your hair. Use your comb to make any final adjustments and ensure that the length is even on both sides.

Tips for Cutting a Simple Bob

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the length you want. It's easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.
  • Use your fingers to measure the length you want to cut, rather than relying solely on your eyes.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it may appear longer than it actually is when dry.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be challenging, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

Can I cut a simple bob on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut a simple bob on curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so cut your hair slightly longer than you want it to be.

How often should I cut my simple bob?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your bob's shape and length.

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

No, you should use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.

Hair Care After Cutting a Simple Bob

After cutting your simple bob, keep your hair healthy and shiny by using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools. Trim your hair regularly to maintain your bob's shape and length.

Cutting a simple bob at home is a great way to save money and achieve a stylish haircut. With these simple steps and helpful tips, you can easily cut your own hair and look fabulous.
