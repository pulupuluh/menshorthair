How To Cut Small Layers In Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 26, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of having boring, flat hair? Adding small layers to your hair can give it more dimension and movement. But cutting layers can be intimidating, especially if you're doing it yourself. Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll go over how to cut small layers in hair, step by step.

How to Cut Small Layers in Hair

Cutting small layers in your hair is easier than you might think. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. You want to work with clean, dry hair to make the cutting process easier.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair. You can do this by parting your hair down the middle and then creating a part from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair away so you can work on the bottom section first.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting by taking a small section of hair from the bottom and holding it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, about 1/4 inch from the end. Be sure to cut at an angle, rather than straight across, to create a softer, more natural look.

Step 4: Repeat

Continue to take small sections of hair and cut into them at an angle. Make sure to keep your cuts consistent and even.

Step 5: Move to the Top Section

Once you've finished cutting the bottom section, unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the process.

Tips for Cutting Small Layers in Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting small layers in your hair:

  • Start with small cuts. You can always go back and cut more if you need to.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.
  • Take your time. Cutting too quickly can result in uneven cuts.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut small sections at a time to ensure that you don't remove too much hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting small layers in hair:

Can I cut small layers in my own hair? Yes, you can cut small layers in your own hair. Just be sure to take your time and use sharp scissors. How often should I cut small layers in my hair? It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want to maintain the style. Typically, small layers should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks. Can I cut small layers in curly hair? Yes, you can cut small layers in curly hair. Just be sure to cut them when your hair is dry, as curly hair can look different when wet.

Hair Care After Cutting Small Layers

After cutting small layers in your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your small layers.

Now that you know how to cut small layers in hair, you can try it out for yourself! Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll end up with beautiful, layered hair.
