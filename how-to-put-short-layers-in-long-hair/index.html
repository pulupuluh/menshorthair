How To Put Short Layers In Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and movement to your long locks, short layers are a great way to do it. Short layers can add volume and shape to your hair, making it easier to style and giving it a more dynamic look. In this article, we'll show you how to put short layers in long hair, step by step.

How to Put Short Layers in Long Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be tricky. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to see a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling adventurous and want to try cutting your hair at home, here's how to put short layers in long hair.

What You'll Need:

  • Sharp Hair Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair Clips
  • Water Spray Bottle
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, create a middle parting in your hair. Then, using the comb, create two sections on either side of your head. Secure each section with a hair clip.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how short you want your layers to be. If you're not sure, start with a small amount and work your way up. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 4: Cut Your First Layer

Starting with one of the sections, take a small section of hair from the top layer and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at the desired length. Use this piece of hair as a guide and cut the rest of the hair in that section to the same length. Repeat this step for the other section.

Step 5: Create the Next Layer

Unclip the top section of your hair and create a new section just below it. Repeat Step 4 for this section. Continue creating new sections and cutting them to the same length until you've reached the desired length for your layers.

Step 6: Blend Your Layers

Once you've cut all of your layers, it's time to blend them. Comb your hair straight down and use your scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a more natural, seamless look.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Check your work in the mirror to make sure your layers are even and blended. Make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Putting Short Layers in Long Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when putting short layers in long hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut a little at a time and gradually build up to the desired length.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be time-consuming, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQs

Can I put short layers in curly hair?

Absolutely! Short layers can work well with curly hair, adding volume and texture. However, it's important to keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut, and you may want to see a professional stylist if you're not confident in your abilities.

Will short layers make my hair look thinner?

No, short layers can actually make your hair look thicker and fuller. They can add volume and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic.

Hair Care Tips for Short Layered Hair

Once you've put short layers in your long hair, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some hair care tips for short layered hair:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair between washes.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can easily put short layers in your long hair and create a new, dynamic look. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to seek out professional help if you're not confident in your abilities.
