How To Style Curtain Bangs With Thick Hair A Complete Guide

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While you may have enviable volume and body, styling it can be a challenge. And if you've recently gotten curtain bangs, you may be wondering how to style them to best suit your thick hair.

Description

Curtain bangs are a popular hairstyle that can work well with thick hair. They frame the face and add a touch of femininity to any look. However, styling curtain bangs with thick hair can be a bit tricky. You want to avoid looking like you have a heavy fringe, but you also don't want your bangs to be too wispy.

In this article, we'll go over how to style curtain bangs with thick hair, step-by-step. We'll also provide some tips for making the most of your thick hair and answer some frequently asked questions about this trendy hairstyle.

How to Style Curtain Bangs with Thick Hair

When it comes to styling curtain bangs with thick hair, less is often more. You want to avoid using too much product or creating too much volume, as this can make your bangs look heavy and unflattering. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin styling your curtain bangs, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You can blow dry your hair upside down to add more volume, but avoid blow drying your bangs straight down as this can make them look too heavy.

Step 2: Section off your bangs

Using a comb, section off your bangs by parting your hair down the middle. The part should be in line with the arch of your eyebrows. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Use a round brush to curl your bangs

Using a round brush, curl your bangs inward towards your face. This will create a slight bend in your bangs and help them frame your face. Be sure to work in small sections and avoid using too much tension, as this can create unwanted volume.

Step 4: Use a flat iron to smooth out your bangs

After you've curled your bangs, use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves. This will create a sleek, polished look. Again, work in small sections and avoid using too much tension.

Step 5: Add some texture

To add some texture to your curtain bangs, you can use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo. Spray the product onto your roots and work it in with your fingers to create a tousled, lived-in look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling curtain bangs with thick hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Section off your bangs
  3. Use a round brush to curl your bangs inward
  4. Smooth out your bangs with a flat iron
  5. Add texture with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo

Tips for Styling Curtain Bangs with Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling curtain bangs with thick hair:

  • Use a light-hold hairspray to keep your bangs in place without weighing them down
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can make your hair look heavy and greasy
  • If you have fine hair, consider using a volumizing mousse or spray to add more body to your bangs
  • If you have coarse hair, use a smoothing serum to tame flyaways and add shine
  • Experiment with different partings to find the one that flatters your face shape the most

FAQs

Can you style curtain bangs with a straightener?

Yes, you can use a straightener to style your curtain bangs. Simply clamp down on the hair and pull the straightener downwards, angling it slightly towards your face to create a slight bend.

How often should you wash thick hair with curtain bangs?

It's best to wash thick hair every other day or every two days to avoid over-drying it. However, if your bangs become oily or greasy, you may need to wash them more frequently.

How do you prevent your curtain bangs from getting in your eyes?

You can use bobby pins or a small hair clip to pin your curtain bangs to the side when they start to get in your eyes. Alternatively, you can style them with a slight bend to keep them out of your face.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair and Curtain Bangs

If you have thick hair and curtain bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can dry out your hair and cause damage
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to moisturize your hair and prevent breakage
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add extra moisture and shine to your hair

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your curtain bangs with thick hair like a pro. Remember to experiment and have fun with your hairstyle!
