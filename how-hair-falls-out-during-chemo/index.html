How Hair Falls Out During Chemo Understanding The Process

Written by Bardi Jul 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Dealing with cancer is a tough journey, and chemotherapy is one of the most challenging treatments that cancer patients have to undergo. One of the side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss, which can be distressing for many patients. In this article, we will discuss how hair falls out during chemo, and provide tips on how to manage hair loss during cancer treatment.

How Does Chemotherapy Cause Hair Loss?

Chemotherapy is a systemic treatment that uses drugs to kill cancer cells. Unfortunately, chemotherapy drugs also affect healthy cells in the body, including hair follicle cells. Hair follicles are the structures in the skin that produce hair. When chemotherapy drugs enter the body, they target rapidly dividing cells, including hair follicle cells. The drugs damage the hair follicles, which causes the hair to fall out.

What Can Patients Expect During Hair Loss?

Patients may start to lose their hair within two to four weeks after the first chemotherapy session. Hair loss can occur all over the body, including the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and pubic area. The severity of hair loss varies among patients, depending on the type and dose of chemotherapy drugs used. Some patients may experience partial hair loss, while others may lose all their hair.

How Can Patients Manage Hair Loss?

Although there is no way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy, patients can take steps to manage it. Here are some tips:

Wear a Head Covering

Wearing a wig, hat, or scarf can help patients feel more comfortable and confident during hair loss. Patients can choose head coverings that match their style and personality.

Cut or Shave Hair Short

Some patients choose to cut or shave their hair short before it falls out. This can make hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage.

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Patients should use a gentle shampoo and avoid harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and perms, which can further damage the hair.

Protect the Scalp

Patients should protect their scalp from the sun and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf. They should also avoid using hot styling tools, such as curling irons and hair dryers, which can damage the hair.

When Does Hair Grow Back?

Hair usually starts to grow back within three to six months after the last chemotherapy session. However, the new hair may be different in texture, color, and thickness than the original hair. It may also grow back unevenly or in patches.

FAQ

Can hair loss be permanent after chemotherapy?

No, hair loss is usually temporary, and hair grows back after chemotherapy. However, in rare cases, some patients may experience permanent hair loss.

Does everyone lose their hair during chemotherapy?

No, not everyone loses their hair during chemotherapy. The likelihood and severity of hair loss depend on the type and dose of chemotherapy drugs used.

Can hair loss be prevented during chemotherapy?

No, there is no known way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy. However, some scalp cooling devices, such as cold caps or scalp cooling systems, may reduce the risk of hair loss by constricting blood vessels in the scalp and reducing the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles.

Hair Care During and After Chemotherapy

After chemotherapy, patients should take care of their hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

Be Gentle

Patients should be gentle when brushing and styling their hair. They should avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, which can pull on the hair.

Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals can promote healthy hair growth. Patients should eat foods such as lean meats, fish, eggs, nuts, fruits, and vegetables.

Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep the scalp and hair healthy.

Use Mild Products

Patients should use mild shampoos and conditioners that do not contain harsh chemicals. They should also avoid using hot styling tools and hair dyes until their hair has fully grown back.

In conclusion, hair loss during chemotherapy is a common side effect that can be distressing for many patients. However, with the right strategies and support, patients can manage hair loss during treatment and promote healthy hair growth after treatment.
