Best Hair Colour For Over 50 Uk A Guide To Flattering Hair Colour

Written by Christine Mar 29, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re over 50 and looking for a hair colour that will enhance your natural beauty, you’ve come to the right place. Whether you want to cover up grey hair or simply add some dimension to your locks, we’ve got you covered. In this guide, we’ll explore the best hair colour options for over 50 women in the UK, along with tips for maintaining your new shade. Let’s dive in!

Description

As we age, our skin tone changes, so it’s important to choose a hair colour that complements our skin tone. Additionally, some women find that their hair texture changes as they get older, so it’s important to choose a hair colour that works with your hair’s natural texture. Finally, some women may want to cover up grey hair, while others may want to embrace their natural grey. Whatever your preference, there’s a hair colour option for you.

How to: Choosing the Right Hair Colour

When choosing a hair colour, consider your skin tone, eye colour, and natural hair colour. If you have warm-toned skin, opt for warm hair colours like golden blondes, rich browns, and coppery reds. If you have cool-toned skin, opt for cool hair colours like ash blondes, cool browns, and burgundy reds. If you have neutral-toned skin, you can pull off both warm and cool hair colours.

It’s also important to consider your eye colour. If you have blue or green eyes, warm hair colours will make your eyes pop. If you have brown eyes, cool hair colours will enhance your eye colour.

Finally, consider your natural hair colour. If you have naturally dark hair, it’s best to stick with a hair colour that is no more than two shades lighter or darker than your natural colour. If you have naturally light hair, you can go bolder with your hair colour.

Step by Step: How to Dye Your Hair at Home

If you’re dyeing your hair at home, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a hair colour that complements your skin tone and eye colour.
  2. Do a strand test to make sure you’re happy with the colour.
  3. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline to prevent staining.
  4. Mix the hair dye according to the instructions.
  5. Apply the dye to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down.
  6. Leave the dye on for the recommended time.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  8. Apply the conditioner that comes with the hair dye.
  9. Rinse your hair again with cool water.

Remember to wear gloves and a towel around your shoulders to prevent staining.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Colour

To keep your new hair colour looking fresh, follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair with a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can fade your colour.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as they can also fade your colour.
  • Consider using a colour-depositing shampoo or conditioner to refresh your colour between dye jobs.

FAQs

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

A: Yes, but be sure to do a patch test first to make sure you’re not allergic to the hair dye.

Q: How often should I dye my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your colour fades. As a general rule, you should dye your hair every four to six weeks.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have grey hair?

A: Yes, many hair dyes are designed to cover grey hair. Just be sure to choose a hair dye that is specifically formulated for grey coverage.

Hair Care for Coloured Hair

In addition to following the tips above, it’s important to take good care of your hair if you’ve coloured it. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturised. Additionally, consider using a heat protectant spray if you’re using hot tools on your hair.

With the right hair colour and care, you can look and feel your best at any age. Experiment with different hair colours to find the one that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
