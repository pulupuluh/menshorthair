How To Get Thick Hair Thinned Out A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine May 13, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have thick hair, you know just how difficult it can be to manage. Thick hair can be unruly, heavy, and difficult to style. One solution is to get your hair thinned out. Thinning your hair can make it more manageable, lighter, and easier to style. In this guide, we will go over everything you need to know about how to get thick hair thinned out.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Thinning Out Thick Hair

Description

Thinning out your hair involves removing some of the bulk so that your hair is not as heavy or difficult to style. This process can be done in a number of ways, including cutting your hair, using thinning shears, or using chemical treatments.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

There are several ways to thin out thick hair:

1. Cutting Your Hair

One of the easiest ways to thin out thick hair is to simply cut it. A good haircut can remove a lot of the bulk from your hair, making it lighter and more manageable. A layered haircut is a good option for thinning out thick hair, as it can create movement and texture.

2. Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade. When used, the teeth remove some of the bulk from your hair while leaving the length intact. Thinning shears can be used on dry or damp hair and are a good option if you don't want to cut off too much length.

3. Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments, such as relaxing or texturizing, can also be used to thin out thick hair. These treatments work by breaking down the protein bonds in your hair, making it softer and more pliable. However, chemical treatments can be damaging to your hair and should only be done by a professional.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils. This will make it easier to work with your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Choose Your Method

Decide on the method you want to use to thin out your hair. If you are cutting your hair, make sure you have sharp scissors. If you are using thinning shears, make sure they are sharp and clean. If you are using chemical treatments, make sure you follow the instructions carefully.

Step 4: Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Start thinning out your hair by cutting or using thinning shears. Make sure you work on one section at a time and use a comb to separate the hair you want to thin out from the hair you want to keep.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Check your progress as you go to make sure you are achieving the look you want. Don't thin out too much hair at once, as you can't put it back once it's gone.

Step 6: Finish Up

Once you have thinned out your hair, wash it again and style it as usual.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out thick hair:

  • Start with a little hair and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair, but you can't put it back once it's gone.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors or thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't overdo it. Thinning out too much hair can leave your hair looking limp and lifeless.
  • If you are using chemical treatments, make sure you follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home using scissors or thinning shears. However, if you are using chemical treatments, it is best to go to a professional.

Will thinning out my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it grow back thicker. This is a common myth.

How often should I thin out my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, it is recommended to thin out your hair every three to six months.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Thick Hair

After thinning out your hair, it is important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best.

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can be damaging to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as a brush can be too harsh.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning out your thick hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. Whether you choose to cut your hair, use thinning shears, or try a chemical treatment, make sure you follow the instructions carefully and take good care of your hair afterwards.
