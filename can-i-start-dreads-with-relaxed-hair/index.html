Can I Start Dreads With Relaxed Hair

Written by Oliver Mar 02, 2023 · 8 min read

Starting dreadlocks is a popular hairstyle choice for many people. It's a natural and low-maintenance hairstyle that can last for years. However, if you have relaxed hair, you might be wondering if it's possible to start dreads without cutting your hair. The answer is yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair. In this article, we will discuss how to do it, step by step, tips, and frequently asked questions about starting dreads with relaxed hair.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Dreads with Relaxed Hair
  • Tips for Starting Dreads with Relaxed Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions About Starting Dreads with Relaxed Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Dreadlocks with Relaxed Hair

How to Start Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair requires patience and dedication. It can take anywhere from six months to a year to fully lock your hair, depending on your hair texture and the method you use. There are several methods of starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair, such as:

  • Twist and Rip Method
  • Backcombing Method
  • Crochet Method

Each method has its pros and cons, and you should choose the one that works best for your hair type and lifestyle. However, before starting, make sure your hair is healthy and strong enough to withstand the locking process. Relaxed hair is weaker and more prone to breakage, so you need to be extra careful and gentle with it.

Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

  1. Decide on the method you want to use and gather your supplies (such as a comb, clips, and dread wax).
  2. Wash your hair and let it dry completely.
  3. Separate your hair into small sections using clips or rubber bands.
  4. Take a section of hair and backcomb it from the ends to the roots using a comb.
  5. Twist the section of hair in one direction until it starts to form a knot.
  6. Apply dread wax to the knot and continue twisting until it forms a dreadlock.
  7. Repeat steps 4-6 for each section of hair.
  8. Once you've finished, leave your hair alone for a few weeks to allow the dreads to settle and lock.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Here are some tips to help you start dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair to make the locking process easier.
  • Be gentle when backcombing to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Use a small amount of dread wax to help the hair lock together.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Be patient and consistent with your maintenance routine to help your dreads form properly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Starting Dreads with Relaxed Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

Can I start dreads with relaxed hair without cutting my hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair without cutting your hair. However, it may take longer to lock because relaxed hair is weaker and more fragile than natural hair.

What method is best for starting dreads with relaxed hair?

There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question because it depends on your hair type and texture. However, the twist and rip method or the backcombing method are popular choices for starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair.

How often should I wash my hair after starting dreadlocks?

You should avoid washing your hair for the first few weeks after starting dreadlocks to allow them to settle and lock. After that, you can wash your hair once or twice a week with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup and keep your scalp clean.

Can I color my hair after starting dreadlocks?

Yes, you can color your hair after starting dreadlocks, but you should avoid bleach or harsh chemicals that can damage your hair. It's best to consult with a professional stylist who has experience with coloring dreadlocks.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Dreadlocks with Relaxed Hair

Once you've started dreadlocks with relaxed hair, it's important to take good care of them to prevent breakage and maintain their shape. Here are some hair care tips for maintaining dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

  • Avoid using tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause stress on your scalp.
  • Use a light oil or moisturizer to keep your hair hydrated and prevent dryness.
  • Protect your hair from heat and sun damage by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Regularly retwist your dreads to maintain their shape and prevent them from unraveling.
  • Trim the ends of your dreads every few months to prevent split ends and maintain their length.

With these tips and advice, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair and maintain them for years to come. Remember to be patient, gentle, and consistent with your maintenance routine, and you'll have beautiful and healthy dreadlocks in no time.
