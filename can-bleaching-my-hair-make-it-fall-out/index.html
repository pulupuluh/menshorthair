Can Bleaching My Hair Make It Fall Out The Truth Revealed

Are you planning to bleach your hair? Or have you already done it and now worrying about whether it can make your hair fall out? Bleaching your hair is a popular trend these days, but it comes with some risks. In this article, we will discuss the question "Can bleaching my hair make it fall out?" and give you all the information you need to know before you decide to bleach your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Bleach Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide To Bleach Your Hair
  • Tips To Prevent Hair Fall After Bleaching
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Bleaching

Description

Bleaching involves using chemicals to lighten your hair color. The process breaks down the natural melanin pigments in your hair, allowing the new color to be absorbed. However, the chemicals used in the process can be harsh and can damage your hair, making it fragile and prone to breakage. This damage can lead to hair fall if not taken care of properly.

How To Bleach Your Hair

Before we discuss whether bleaching can make your hair fall out, let's talk about how to bleach your hair. If you're planning to bleach your hair, it's best to do it at a salon with a professional hairdresser. However, if you want to do it at home, here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose the right bleach

There are different types of bleach available in the market, such as powder bleach, cream bleach, and oil bleach. Choose the one that suits your hair type and texture.

Step 2: Prepare your hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any styling products and natural oils. Dry your hair with a towel and comb it thoroughly to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Mix the bleach

Follow the instructions on the bleach packet to mix the bleach. Use a plastic bowl and brush to mix and apply the bleach.

Step 4: Apply the bleach

Wear gloves and apply the bleach to your hair using the brush. Start from the ends and work your way up to the roots. Avoid applying the bleach to your scalp as it can cause burns and irritation.

Step 5: Check the progress

Check your hair every 10-15 minutes to see if it has lightened to the desired level. If not, leave the bleach on for a few more minutes but avoid over-bleaching as it can damage your hair.

Step 6: Wash and condition your hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove the bleach. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide To Bleach Your Hair

Here is a step-by-step guide to bleach your hair:

  1. Choose the right bleach
  2. Prepare your hair
  3. Mix the bleach
  4. Apply the bleach
  5. Check the progress
  6. Wash and condition your hair

Tips To Prevent Hair Fall After Bleaching

Now that you know how to bleach your hair, let's discuss how to prevent hair fall after bleaching. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to restore moisture to your hair

FAQs

1. Can bleaching my hair make it fall out?

Yes, bleaching your hair can make it fall out if not done correctly or if not taken care of properly. Bleaching can damage your hair, making it fragile and prone to breakage.

2. How long does it take for hair to fall out after bleaching?

It can take up to a few weeks or months for hair to fall out after bleaching. The damage caused by bleaching can weaken the hair, leading to breakage and hair fall.

3. Can I bleach my hair if I have a sensitive scalp?

No, it's not recommended to bleach your hair if you have a sensitive scalp. The chemicals used in bleaching can cause burns and irritation on the scalp, leading to hair fall.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Conclusion

Bleaching your hair can be a fun way to change your look, but it comes with some risks. Can bleaching my hair make it fall out? Yes, it can if not done correctly or if not taken care of properly. Follow the tips and steps mentioned in this article to bleach your hair safely and prevent hair fall.
