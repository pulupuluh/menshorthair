What Does The Bible Say About Facial Hair

Written by Petter Jul 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Facial hair has been a topic of discussion for centuries, and it is no different in the Bible. Many people wonder what the Bible has to say about facial hair, and whether or not it is acceptable for men to grow beards or mustaches. In this article, we will explore what the Bible has to say about facial hair, and how it relates to modern-day beliefs and practices.

Description

The Bible does not explicitly say whether or not it is acceptable for men to grow facial hair. However, there are many references to beards and mustaches in the Bible, which indicate that they were common among men in biblical times. For example, the book of Leviticus 19:27 states, “You shall not round off the hair on your temples or mar the edges of your beard.” This suggests that beards were a natural and acceptable part of male grooming in ancient times.

Furthermore, there are several references to beards and mustaches in the New Testament. For example, in 1 Corinthians 11:14, it says, “Does not nature itself teach you that if a man wears long hair it is a disgrace for him, but if a woman has long hair, it is her glory? For his hair is given to him for a covering.” This suggests that men should not have long hair, but it does not say anything about beards or mustaches.

How to Grow Facial Hair According to the Bible

There is no specific method for growing facial hair according to the Bible. However, there are some general guidelines that can be followed in order to maintain a biblical appearance. For example, men should keep their beards and mustaches trimmed and neat, and they should avoid shaving the hair around their temples or edges of their beards.

In addition, men should avoid using any modern grooming products that may be considered inappropriate or unbecoming of a biblical appearance. This includes things like hair dye, hair gel, or other styling products that are designed to alter the natural appearance of facial hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Facial Hair in a Biblical Way

If you are interested in growing facial hair in a biblical way, here is a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Stop shaving the hair around your temples and edges of your beard.
  2. Allow your beard and mustache to grow naturally.
  3. Trim your beard and mustache regularly to keep them neat and tidy.
  4. Avoid using any modern grooming products that may be considered inappropriate or unbecoming of a biblical appearance.

Tips for Maintaining Facial Hair According to the Bible

If you want to maintain facial hair in a biblical way, here are some tips to help you:

  • Use a natural, gentle beard oil to keep your beard and mustache moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your beard and mustache regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Avoid using any modern grooming products that may be considered inappropriate or unbecoming of a biblical appearance.

Frequently Asked Questions About Facial Hair in the Bible

Is it a sin to shave my beard?

No, it is not a sin to shave your beard. However, the Bible does suggest that beards were a natural and acceptable part of male grooming in ancient times, so it is up to personal preference whether or not to grow and maintain facial hair.

What does the Bible say about mustaches?

The Bible does not specifically address mustaches, but it does mention beards as a natural and acceptable part of male grooming in ancient times. Therefore, it can be assumed that mustaches were also a common and acceptable part of male grooming in biblical times.

Can women have facial hair according to the Bible?

The Bible does not specifically address facial hair on women, but it is generally considered culturally unacceptable for women to have facial hair in modern times.

Hair Care According to the Bible

The Bible does not provide specific guidelines for hair care, but it does suggest that men should maintain a neat and tidy appearance. This includes keeping their hair trimmed and groomed, and avoiding any modern grooming products that may be considered inappropriate or unbecoming of a biblical appearance.

In conclusion, the Bible does not explicitly say whether or not it is acceptable for men to grow facial hair, but it does suggest that beards were a natural and acceptable part of male grooming in ancient times. If you are interested in maintaining a biblical appearance, you can follow general guidelines for growing and maintaining facial hair, and avoid using any modern grooming products that may be considered inappropriate or unbecoming of a biblical appearance.
