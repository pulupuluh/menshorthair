Best Way To Thin Out Curly Hair

Written by Christine Sep 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you look at it. While curly hair can give you a unique and beautiful look, it can also be challenging to manage, especially when it comes to volume. If your curly hair is too thick and unmanageable, don't worry; there are ways to thin it out without losing its natural beauty. In this blog post, we will share the best way to thin out curly hair, step by step, and provide you with tips on how to care for your hair after thinning it out.

How to Thin Out Curly Hair

There are several ways to thin out curly hair, but the best and most effective way is by using thinning shears. Thinning shears are specialized scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. They are designed to remove bulk from the hair without changing its length. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out your curly hair using thinning shears:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start, make sure that your hair is clean and free of tangles. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, then towel-dry it gently. Avoid using a blow dryer as it can cause more frizz and damage to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into four parts, from the crown of your head to your ears. This will make it easier for you to work on each section individually.

Step 3: Start Thinning Your Hair

Take one section of your hair and comb it straight. Hold the section of hair with your fingers, then use the thinning shears to cut into the section of hair at a slight angle. Repeat this process until you have thinned out the section of hair to your liking. Remember to avoid cutting too much hair at once, as it can result in a choppy and uneven look.

Step 4: Repeat the Process on the Other Sections

Repeat the thinning process on the other sections of your hair until you have thinned out all the sections to your liking. If you notice any uneven areas, you can use the thinning shears to fix them.

Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Thinning out curly hair can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly and avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Make sure to cut at a slight angle to avoid creating blunt lines.
  • Comb your hair often to ensure that it is even.
  • Don't forget to trim your ends to avoid split ends and breakage.

FAQs

Can I thin out my curly hair without thinning shears?

While thinning shears are the most effective way to thin out curly hair, you can also use a razor comb or scissors to remove bulk from your hair. However, using these tools requires more skill and can result in uneven cuts if not done correctly.

How often should I thin out my curly hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how thick it is. As a general rule, you should thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent it from becoming too thick and unmanageable.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Curly Hair

After thinning out your curly hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some tips on how to care for your hair after thinning it out:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to reduce frizz and add shine.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Thinning out curly hair can be a game-changer for those struggling with thick and unmanageable hair. By following the steps and tips mentioned above, you can achieve a beautiful and manageable look for your curly hair. Always remember to take care of your hair after thinning it out to maintain its health and beauty.
