How To Make My Curly Hair Straight A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read
If you have naturally curly hair, you might sometimes feel like you want a change. Straight hair can be sleek and sophisticated, and it's a style that works for most occasions. But if you're not used to straightening your hair, it can be a bit overwhelming. In this article, we'll show you how to make your curly hair straight with our step-by-step guide.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make My Curly Hair Straight

Description

Straightening curly hair can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. There are a few different methods you can use to straighten your hair, depending on your hair type and the tools you have available. In this article, we'll cover a few different methods so you can find the one that works best for you.

How to Make My Curly Hair Straight

Before we get started, it's important to note that straightening your hair can be damaging if you're not careful. Heat styling tools can dry out your hair and cause breakage, so it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to not straighten your hair too often. With that said, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to keep it moisturized.
  2. Blow dry your hair using a round brush. This will help smooth out your hair and prepare it for straightening.
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help prevent damage from the straightening iron.
  4. Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to straighten your hair one section at a time.
  5. Starting with the bottom section, clamp a ceramic straightening iron onto a small section of hair as close to the roots as possible.
  6. Slowly glide the straightening iron down the section of hair, making sure to keep the iron moving at a steady pace.
  7. Repeat this process with each section of hair until all of your hair is straightened.
  8. Apply a smoothing serum or cream to your hair to help keep it smooth and frizz-free.
  9. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips for Straightening Curly Hair

- Use a ceramic straightening iron, as it will distribute heat evenly and cause less damage to your hair. - Don't straighten your hair too often. Limit it to once or twice a week to avoid damage. - When blow drying your hair, use a nozzle attachment to direct the air flow downward. This will help smooth out your hair and prevent frizz. - Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair before straightening it. - Apply a heat protectant spray before straightening your hair to prevent damage.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair without a straightening iron?
A: Yes, you can use a blow dryer and a round brush to straighten your hair. It will take longer, but it's a good option if you don't have a straightening iron. Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's wet?
A: No, you should never straighten your hair when it's wet. This can cause damage and breakage. Q: How long will my straightened hair last?
A: This depends on your hair type and the humidity in your environment. Generally, straightened hair will last for a few days before it starts to revert back to its natural state.

Hair Care for Straightened Hair

After straightening your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and breakage. Here are a few tips: - Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. - Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends. - Avoid using heat styling tools too often. - Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. - Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz. Now that you know how to make your curly hair straight, you can switch up your style whenever you feel like it. Just remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
