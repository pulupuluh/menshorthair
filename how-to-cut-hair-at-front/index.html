How To Cut Hair At Front

Written by Luffy Feb 19, 2023 · 9 min read

If you’re looking to add a little bit of style to your hair without going to the salon, then learning how to cut your hair at the front is a great place to start. This guide will take you through everything you need to know to cut your hair at the front, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQ.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair at Front
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair at Front
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care after Cutting Hair at Front

How to Cut Hair at Front

Cutting hair at the front can be a little intimidating, but it’s actually quite simple. All you need is a pair of sharp scissors and a comb. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the length and shape of your hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. It’s a good idea to start with a little bit and work your way up, rather than cutting too much off at once.

Step 3: Part Your Hair

Part your hair where you want to cut it. This could be in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair Forward

Comb your hair forward so that it’s flat against your forehead. This will make it easier to see the length you want to cut.

Step 5: Cut Your Hair

Using your scissors, cut your hair at the length you’ve decided on. Start with small snips, and work your way up to the desired length.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Check your progress in the mirror to make sure you’re happy with the length and shape of your hair. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully and slowly.

Step by Step Guide

If you’re still feeling a little unsure about how to cut your hair at the front, here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have everything you need. This includes a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to keep your hair out of the way.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Decide on where you want to part your hair. This could be in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair Forward

Comb your hair forward so that it’s flat against your forehead. This will make it easier to see the length you want to cut.

Step 4: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. It’s a good idea to start with a little bit and work your way up, rather than cutting too much off at once.

Step 5: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into smaller sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 6: Cut Your Hair

Using your scissors, cut your hair at the length you’ve decided on. Start with small snips, and work your way up to the desired length.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

Check your progress in the mirror to make sure you’re happy with the length and shape of your hair. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully and slowly.

Tips for Cutting Hair at Front

Cutting your hair at the front can be a little tricky, but these tips will help make the process a little easier:

  • Start with a little bit of hair and work your way up to the desired length
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear longer when dry
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Use a mirror to check your progress

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair at the front myself?

Yes, you can. All you need is a pair of sharp scissors and a comb. Just make sure you take your time and start with a little bit of hair before cutting more off.

How often should I cut my hair at the front?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your style. Generally, it’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut my hair at the front if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, it’s important to be careful when cutting curly hair, as it can be difficult to see the length you’re cutting. It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s dry and styled in its natural state.

Hair Care after Cutting Hair at Front

After cutting your hair at the front, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Cutting your hair at the front can be a great way to change up your style without spending a lot of money at the salon. With these tips and instructions, you can achieve a great look right at home!
