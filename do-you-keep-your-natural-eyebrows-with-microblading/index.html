Do You Keep Your Natural Eyebrows With Microblading

Written by Oliver Jun 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our blog post about keeping your natural eyebrows with microblading! If you're considering getting microblading done, you may be wondering whether you'll still have your natural eyebrows after the procedure. In this article, we'll answer that question and provide you with some helpful tips and advice along the way.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Keep Your Natural Eyebrows with Microblading
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Your Natural Eyebrows
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Microbladed Eyebrows

Description

Microblading is a semi-permanent makeup procedure that involves using a small blade to create hair-like strokes on the eyebrows. The result is natural-looking and long-lasting eyebrows that require little to no maintenance. However, many people wonder whether they'll still have their natural eyebrows after getting microblading done.

The answer is yes! Microblading doesn't remove your natural eyebrows. Instead, it enhances them by filling in sparse areas and defining their shape. The end result is a more polished and put-together look that still looks natural.

How to Keep Your Natural Eyebrows with Microblading

If you want to keep your natural eyebrows with microblading, it's important to communicate this with your microblading technician. They'll work with you to create a look that enhances your natural eyebrows instead of completely replacing them.

During the microblading procedure, your technician will use a numbing cream to minimize any discomfort. They'll then use a small blade to create hair-like strokes in your eyebrows. The process typically takes about 2-3 hours, and you'll be able to see the results immediately.

After the procedure, it's important to follow your technician's aftercare instructions to ensure the best possible results. This may include avoiding getting your eyebrows wet for a few days and avoiding certain skincare products.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your natural eyebrows with microblading:

  1. Communicate with your microblading technician to ensure they know you want to keep your natural eyebrows.
  2. Arrive at your appointment with clean, makeup-free skin.
  3. Your technician will apply a numbing cream to minimize any discomfort.
  4. They'll then use a small blade to create hair-like strokes in your eyebrows.
  5. The process typically takes 2-3 hours.
  6. You'll be able to see the results immediately.
  7. Follow your technician's aftercare instructions to ensure the best possible results.

Tips for Keeping Your Natural Eyebrows

Here are some tips for keeping your natural eyebrows with microblading:

  • Communicate with your technician to ensure they know you want to keep your natural eyebrows.
  • Choose a technician who has experience working with a variety of eyebrow shapes and sizes.
  • Look at their before-and-after photos to ensure you like their work.
  • Follow their aftercare instructions to ensure the best possible results.
  • Avoid picking at your eyebrows or scratching them during the healing process.
  • Avoid excessive sweating, sun exposure, and swimming for at least a week after the procedure.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about keeping your natural eyebrows with microblading:

Will microblading remove my natural eyebrows?

No, microblading doesn't remove your natural eyebrows. Instead, it enhances them by filling in sparse areas and defining their shape.

Can I still shape my eyebrows after getting microblading done?

Yes, you can still shape your eyebrows after getting microblading done. However, it's important to wait until the healing process is complete before doing so.

How long does microblading last?

Microblading typically lasts 1-2 years, depending on your skin type and how well you take care of your eyebrows.

Hair Care for Microbladed Eyebrows

Here are some tips for taking care of your microbladed eyebrows:

  • Avoid getting your eyebrows wet for at least a week after the procedure.
  • Avoid using skincare products that contain acids or exfoliants on your eyebrows.
  • Avoid excessive sweating, sun exposure, and swimming for at least a week after the procedure.
  • Apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly to your eyebrows before showering to protect them from water.
  • Avoid picking at your eyebrows or scratching them during the healing process.

Overall, microblading is a great way to enhance your natural eyebrows without completely changing their shape or appearance. By communicating with your technician and following their aftercare instructions, you can ensure the best possible results and enjoy your new eyebrows for years to come.
