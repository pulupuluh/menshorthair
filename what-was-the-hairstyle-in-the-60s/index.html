The Iconic Hairstyles Of The 60S A Guide To Hair Trends Of The Past

Written by Oliver Mar 31, 2023 Â· 7 min read

The 1960s were a time of change, revolution, and experimentation. From fashion to music, everything was up for grabs. One of the most iconic aspects of this era was the hairstyles. The 60s saw a variety of hair trends that have been emulated and reimagined throughout the decades. In this article, we will explore what was the hairstyle in the 60s, how to recreate these looks, tips for maintaining the styles, and more.

Description

The 60s was a decade where freedom of expression was embraced. This included hairstyles that were unique and bold. The most popular hairstyles of this era were voluminous, textured, and often featured long hair. Women often wore their hair in a bouffant style with teased crowns, while men opted for longer hair with sideburns.

How To: Recreate 60s Hairstyles

If you're looking to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the 60s, you're in luck. Many of these styles are still popular today and can be achieved with a few simple steps.

Women's Hair

To achieve the bouffant style, start by sectioning off the crown of your hair. Tease the hair at the crown to create volume, then smooth it over with a comb. Pull the remaining hair into a low ponytail or bun, leaving out the teased section. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Another popular hairstyle of the 60s was the beehive. To create this look, start by sectioning off the hair at the crown and teasing it to create volume. Next, pin the hair in place, creating a cone shape. Smooth the remaining hair over the cone and pin it in place. Finish with hairspray.

Men's Hair

For men, the most popular hairstyle of the 60s was long hair with sideburns. To achieve this look, grow out your hair and let it fall naturally. Keep the sides trimmed and style the hair with a small amount of pomade or wax to create texture.

Another popular hairstyle for men was the shag. This style features long hair that is layered and textured. To achieve this look, start by growing out your hair and asking your stylist to layer it. Style with a small amount of pomade or wax to create texture.

Step by Step Guide

Women's Hair

  1. Section off the crown of your hair.
  2. Tease the hair at the crown to create volume.
  3. Smooth the teased hair over with a comb.
  4. Pull the remaining hair into a low ponytail or bun, leaving out the teased section.
  5. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Men's Hair

  1. Grow out your hair and let it fall naturally.
  2. Keep the sides trimmed.
  3. Style the hair with a small amount of pomade or wax to create texture.

Tips for Maintaining 60s Hairstyles

To keep your 60s hairstyle looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Tease your hair gently to avoid damage.
  • Use hairspray to hold your style in place.
  • Use a small amount of pomade or wax to create texture.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.

FAQs

What was the most popular hairstyle of the 60s?

The most popular hairstyle of the 60s for women was the bouffant, while men often wore their hair long with sideburns.

How do I recreate a 60s beehive?

To recreate a 60s beehive, tease the hair at the crown to create volume, then pin it in place to create a cone shape. Smooth the remaining hair over the cone and pin it in place. Finish with hairspray.

Hair Care for 60s Hairstyles

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Conclusion

The hairstyles of the 60s were bold, unique, and iconic. From the bouffant to the shag, these styles have stood the test of time and continue to inspire new hair trends today. By following our guide, you can recreate these hairstyles and make them your own. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful for years to come.
