Layered Haircuts For Thin Hair And Round Face

Written by Eveline May 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling to find the perfect haircut for your thin hair and round face? Look no further than layered haircuts! Layered haircuts not only add volume and texture to thin hair but also frame and slim a round face. In this article, we will discuss how to achieve layered haircuts for thin hair and round face, tips on styling and maintenance, and answer frequently asked questions.

How To Achieve Layered Haircuts for Thin Hair and Round Face

Before you start cutting your hair, it is important to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help determine the best length and layering techniques for your hair type and face shape. Here are some general guidelines to follow:

Length

For thin hair, it is best to avoid going too short as it can make your hair look even thinner. Opt for a length that falls between your chin and shoulders. For round faces, longer lengths tend to elongate the face, while shorter lengths can make the face appear wider.

Layering

Layering is key to adding volume and texture to thin hair. Ask your stylist to create long layers that start at the chin or collarbone. Layers that start too high can create a triangular shape, which is not flattering for round faces.

Face-Framing Layers

Face-framing layers can help slim a round face. Ask your stylist to add layers around your jawline and cheekbones. These layers will draw attention to your best features and create a more angular look.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to achieve layered haircuts for thin hair and round face:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional Stylist

Before you start cutting your hair, it is important to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help determine the best length and layering techniques for your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and create a soft curl at the ends.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into layers and clip them away from the rest of your hair.

Step 5: Cut Your Hair

Cut each layer at a slight angle to create a seamless blend. Make sure to leave the longest layer at chin or collarbone length.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Style your hair using a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and hold. Use a curling iron or flat iron to create loose waves or straighten your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips for styling and maintaining layered haircuts for thin hair and round face:

- Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and volume to your hair. - Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair. - Use a round brush when blow-drying your hair to add volume and create a soft curl at the ends. - Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Q: Can I still have layered haircuts if my hair is very thin?

A: Yes, you can still have layered haircuts if your hair is very thin. In fact, layered haircuts can add volume and texture to thin hair.

Q: Will layered haircuts make my face look rounder?

A: No, layered haircuts can actually slim a round face when done correctly. Ask your stylist to add face-framing layers around your jawline and cheekbones.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It is recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintain healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some tips for hair care:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. - Avoid using hot tools too often as it can damage your hair. - Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray. - Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote hair growth and health.
