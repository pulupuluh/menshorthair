How To Cut Long Thick Hair Into Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your long, thick hair feeling heavy and unmanageable? Layers can add dimension and movement to your hair, making it easier to style and more visually appealing. But how do you cut layers into long, thick hair without making a mess of it? Follow this step-by-step guide to achieve a beautiful layered look.

Description

Cutting long, thick hair into layers can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and effectively. This guide will show you how to section and cut your hair to achieve a layered look that suits your face shape and personal style.

How to Cut Long Thick Hair into Layers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips to section your hair, and a mirror to check your progress. Make sure your scissors are sharp to ensure a clean cut.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair, then allow it to air dry or use a blow dryer. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting, as wet hair can stretch and lead to uneven layers.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide each side into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Clip each section out of the way using hair clips.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take the middle section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, then cut the ends in a straight line. Repeat this process with the other two sections on the bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of the next layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, then cut the ends at an angle, starting from the length of the bottom layer and working your way up towards the top layer. This will create a graduated effect.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of the next layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, then cut the ends at a steeper angle, starting from the length of the middle layer and working your way up towards the top. This will create more movement and volume in your hair.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Release the clipped sections of hair and use your comb to blend the layers together. Check for any uneven spots or choppy edges, and trim as needed to create a seamless look.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair into Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Always cut your hair when it is dry to ensure an even cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and ensure you are cutting evenly.
  • If you are unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair?

It is recommended to get a haircut every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair into layers if it is curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair into layers to add movement and dimension. However, it is important to cut curly hair when it is dry to avoid shrinkage.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair into layers, it is important to take care of it to ensure it stays healthy and vibrant. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it looking its best.

With this step-by-step guide, cutting long thick hair into layers has never been easier. Follow these tips and techniques to achieve a beautiful layered look that will make your hair feel lighter and more manageable.
