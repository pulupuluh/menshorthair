Women s Short Haircuts 2021 Over 50 A Guide To The Best Hairstyles

Written by Petter Jul 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're over 50 and looking for a new hairstyle, a short haircut may be the perfect choice. Not only is short hair easy to maintain, but it can also be incredibly stylish and youthful. In this guide, we'll take a closer look at some of the best women's short haircuts for 2021 over 50, along with tips for styling and caring for your new look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Short Haircut
  • Step-by-Step: How to Style Short Hair

Description

Short haircuts for women over 50 can take many different forms, from classic bobs to edgy pixie cuts. The best hairstyle for you will depend on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. However, some of the most popular short haircuts for older women include:

  • Bob: A classic, chin-length cut with or without bangs.
  • Pixie: A short, layered cut that can be styled in a variety of ways.
  • Shag: A choppy, layered cut that creates volume and texture.
  • Crop: A very short cut that's perfect for women who want minimal maintenance.

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut

When choosing a short haircut, it's important to consider your face shape and hair texture. Some styles may look great on one person but not on another. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Round face: Chin-length bobs and layered cuts can help to elongate the face.
  • Square face: Soft, layered cuts can help to soften angular features.
  • Oval face: Most short haircuts will look great on an oval face.
  • Thin hair: Layered cuts can add volume and texture to thin hair.
  • Thick hair: Shorter cuts can help to control and manage thick hair.

Examples of Short Haircuts for Different Face Shapes

Here are some examples of short haircuts that work well for different face shapes:

  • Round face: Chin-length bob with side-swept bangs
  • Square face: Layered pixie cut with side-swept bangs
  • Oval face: Textured crop or shag
  • Thin hair: Layered bob with choppy ends
  • Thick hair: Short, layered pixie cut

Step-by-Step: How to Style Short Hair

Styling short hair can be quick and easy. Here's a basic guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Towel-dry your hair and apply a styling product, such as mousse or gel, to add texture and hold.
  3. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush, lifting at the roots to create volume.
  4. Use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and create waves or curls.
  5. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Short Haircut

Short haircuts require regular maintenance to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to maintain your shape.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to extend the life of your style between washes.

FAQ: Common Questions About Short Haircuts for Women Over 50

Q: Will a short haircut make me look older?

A: Not necessarily. In fact, a well-chosen short haircut can make you look more youthful and vibrant.

Q: Can I still wear my hair up with a short haircut?

A: Yes! Short hair can be styled in a variety of ways, including updos, braids, and half-up styles.

Q: Will a short haircut be easy to maintain?

A: Yes! Short haircuts require minimal maintenance and can be styled quickly and easily.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair is easy to care for, but it still needs some TLC. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying out your scalp.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

With the right short haircut and a little bit of care, you can look and feel your best at any age. Try one of these stylish and youthful cuts for women over 50, and enjoy your new look!
