How To Cut Thinning Men s Hair With Clippers

Written by William Sep 05, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you looking to cut your thinning hair at home with clippers? It’s an inexpensive and convenient option for those who don’t want to visit a barber or stylist regularly. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of cutting thinning men’s hair with clippers. From preparing your tools to aftercare, we’ve got you covered!

Description

Cutting thinning men’s hair with clippers requires patience and precision. It’s important to choose the right tools and techniques to ensure the best results. With a bit of practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.

How to Cut Thinning Men’s Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Clippers with various attachments
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to cover your shoulders

Make sure your clippers are clean and oiled before using them.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Wash your hair and towel dry it before cutting. Comb it out to remove any tangles and knots. If you have longer hair, consider cutting it shorter before using clippers.

Step 3: Choose Your Length

Select the clipper attachment that corresponds to the length you want. Start with a longer attachment and work your way down if necessary. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin at the back of your head and work your way up. Use the clippers in a steady, even motion. Make sure to overlap each pass slightly to ensure even cutting. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any longer strands with scissors.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Top

Switch to a shorter attachment to blend the sides and top. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut any uneven strands with scissors. Blend the top and sides together by using the clipper without an attachment.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns

Use the clippers without an attachment or a smaller attachment to clean up the neckline and sideburns. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any longer strands with scissors.

Step 7: Check for Unevenness

Check your hair in the mirror for any uneven spots. Use scissors to trim any longer strands and blend the hair together.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with a longer attachment and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Overlap each pass slightly to ensure even cutting.
  • Don’t rush the process. Take your time and be patient.
  • Use scissors to trim any longer strands and blend the hair together.
  • Check your hair in the mirror frequently for any uneven spots.

FAQ

What if I don’t have clippers?

You can still cut your hair with scissors, but it may take longer and be more difficult to achieve an even cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good timeframe.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, wash it with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners to prevent further damage to thinning hair. Consider using a hair growth serum or supplement to promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting thinning men’s hair with clippers is a great way to save money and time. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don’t hesitate to ask for help if needed.
