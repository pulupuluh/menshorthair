How To Cut Your Own Hair Choppy Layers

Written by Daniel Feb 28, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle but don't want to spend money at the salon, you can always try cutting your own hair at home. One popular hairstyle that you can easily achieve at home is choppy layers. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair choppy layers.

Table of Contents

  1. How To Cut Your Own Hair Choppy Layers
  2. Step By Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to Cut Your Own Hair Choppy Layers

The first thing you need to do is gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's important to use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

Next, decide how long you want your hair to be. It's best to start with longer hair and gradually cut it shorter as needed. Remember, you can always cut more hair off, but you can't add it back on.

Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use the comb to create a center part and divide your hair into two equal sections. Then, create a part down the back of your head to divide your hair into four sections. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Repeat with the other three sections.

Cut the Middle Layer

Next, cut the middle layer. Take a section of hair from the middle of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a choppy layer. Repeat with the other three sections.

Cut the Top Layer

Finally, cut the top layer. Take a section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a choppy layer. Repeat with the other three sections.

Step By Step

  1. Gather all necessary tools: sharp scissors, comb, and mirror.
  2. Decide on desired hair length.
  3. Section your hair into four equal parts.
  4. Cut the bottom layer straight across.
  5. Cut the middle layer at an angle to create a choppy layer.
  6. Cut the top layer at an angle to create a choppy layer.

Tips

  • Start with longer hair and gradually cut it shorter as needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can appear longer and can result in uneven cuts.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a salon to have it fixed or wait for it to grow out.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Trim your hair regularly to maintain healthy ends.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair choppy layers, give it a try and see how it turns out. Remember, practice makes perfect!
