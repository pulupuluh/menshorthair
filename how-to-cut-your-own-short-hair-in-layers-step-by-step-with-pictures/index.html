How To Cut Your Own Short Hair In Layers Step By Step With Pictures

Written by Christine May 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have short hair and want to add some layers to give it more texture and volume, you don't have to go to a salon. With the right tools and technique, you can cut your own hair in layers at home. This step by step guide will show you how to do it yourself with pictures to help you along the way.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Up

  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • Hand-held mirror or a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head

Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid damaging your hair or giving yourself split ends. If you don't have hair cutting scissors, you can buy them online or at a beauty supply store.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting it. Wet hair may look longer than it actually is, and you could end up cutting more than you intended.

Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to cut and give you a more even result.

Sectioning Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use clips to secure each section of hair.

Start with the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and cut the ends with your scissors. Cut a small amount at a time to avoid taking off too much hair.

Once you've cut the top section, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from one of the side sections and comb it straight out from your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends. Repeat on the other side.

Finally, move on to the back section. Comb the hair straight down and cut the ends. You may need to use a hand-held mirror or a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head to make sure you're cutting the hair evenly.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that you've cut the ends of each section, it's time to add layers. Take a small section of hair from the top section and comb it straight up. Hold it between your fingers and cut vertically into the hair. This will create layers and give your hair more texture and volume.

Repeat this process on each section of your hair. Make sure you're cutting each section evenly to avoid having one side longer than the other.

Finishing Up

Once you've finished cutting your hair, remove the clips and brush it out. Check to make sure the layers are even and adjust any sections that need it.

If you want to add more texture, you can use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from your hair. Hold the shears vertically and cut into the hair, rather than across it.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time.
  • Use a comb to make sure each section is even before you cut it.
  • If you're unsure how much to cut, start with less and work your way up.
  • Take breaks if you need them to avoid getting frustrated or tired.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair while it's wet?

No, it's best to cut your hair when it's completely dry. Wet hair may look longer than it actually is, and you could end up cutting more than you intended.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the hair that's uneven or wait for it to grow out. It's better to take your time and go slow than to rush and make a mistake.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized, and avoid using heat tools like straighteners or curling irons too often. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking its best.

Cutting your own hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, you can do it yourself. Follow these steps and tips to give your short hair more texture and volume with layers.
