How To Make Your Hair Part Down The Middle For Guys

Written by Bardi Jun 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a guy looking to try out a new hairstyle, parting your hair down the middle is a great option. It's a versatile style that can work for both casual and formal occasions. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this look and offer some tips for maintaining it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Part Your Hair Down the Middle
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Middle Part
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Middle Part

How to Part Your Hair Down the Middle

To achieve a middle part, you'll need to have medium to long hair. Short hair can be more difficult to part in the middle. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

If you don't already have medium to long hair, you'll need to grow it out or get a haircut that will allow for a middle part. The most common haircuts for a middle part include the shag, the undercut, and the layered cut. Talk to your stylist about what will work best for you.

Step 2: Determine Your Natural Part

Everyone's hair has a natural part, which is where the hair falls when you let it air dry. To find your natural part, wet your hair and let it air dry without combing or brushing it. Once it's dry, look in the mirror and see where the hair naturally falls. This is where you should part your hair.

Step 3: Create Your Part

Using a comb, create a straight line from the front of your hairline to the back of your head. Use the end of the comb to separate the hair into two even sections. Make sure the part is straight and even.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide on how to create a middle part:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet, it will be more difficult to create a straight part.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, create a straight line from the front of your hairline to the back of your head. Use the end of the comb to separate the hair into two even sections.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Use a comb to comb each section of hair straight down. Make sure there are no tangles or knots.

Step 4: Create Your Part

Starting at the front of your hairline, use the end of the comb to create a straight line down the middle of your head. Make sure the part is straight and even.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

You can style your hair in any way you like once you've created your part. You can leave it down, slick it back, or tie it up in a ponytail.

Tips for Maintaining Your Middle Part

Here are some tips for keeping your middle part looking great:

  • Use a comb to touch up your part throughout the day if it starts to look uneven.
  • Avoid wearing hats or headbands that can mess up your part.
  • Use a hair gel or pomade to keep your hair in place.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends and breakage.

FAQs

Can I create a middle part with short hair?

It's more difficult to create a middle part with short hair, but it's not impossible. You'll need to have at least a couple of inches of hair to work with. Talk to your stylist about what will work best for you.

How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended that you trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Your Middle Part

Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote hair growth and health.

With these tips and steps, you can create and maintain a middle part that suits your style and personality. Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for you.
