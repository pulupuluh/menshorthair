How To Find Out Which Hair Type You Have A Comprehensive Guide

If you want to have healthy, beautiful hair, the first step is to determine your hair type. By knowing your hair type, you can choose the best products and styling methods that will work for you. In this article, we will share with you some tips on how to find out which hair type you have.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Type
  • FAQs

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each hair type has its own unique characteristics and requires different care. Here are some factors that can help you determine your hair type:

  • Texture: The texture of your hair refers to the thickness or diameter of each individual strand.
  • Curl Pattern: The curl pattern of your hair refers to the shape of your hair strands.
  • Porosity: The porosity of your hair refers to how easily your hair absorbs and retains moisture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to determine your hair type:

  1. Look at your hair in its natural state.
  2. Determine your hair texture by taking a single strand of hair and rolling it between your fingers.
  3. Determine your curl pattern by observing the shape of your hair strands.
  4. Determine your hair porosity by doing a porosity test.

Here is a more detailed explanation of each step:

Step 1: Look at your hair in its natural state.

Before you can determine your hair type, it's important to look at your hair in its natural state. This means that you shouldn't have any products in your hair, and you shouldn't have styled it in any way.

Step 2: Determine your hair texture.

The texture of your hair is determined by the thickness or diameter of each individual strand. There are three main types of hair texture: fine, medium, and coarse.

To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and roll it between your fingers. If you can't feel the strand or it feels silky and smooth, you have fine hair. If you can feel the strand but it's not thick or thin, you have medium hair. If you can feel the strand and it feels thick or coarse, you have coarse hair.

Step 3: Determine your curl pattern.

The curl pattern of your hair is determined by the shape of your hair strands. There are four main types of curl patterns: straight, wavy, curly, and coily.

To determine your curl pattern, observe the shape of your hair strands. If your hair strands are straight and don't have any curls, you have straight hair. If your hair strands have a slight wave or curl, you have wavy hair. If your hair strands have a definite curl pattern, you have curly hair. If your hair strands have a tight coil or zigzag pattern, you have coily hair.

Step 4: Determine your hair porosity.

The porosity of your hair refers to how easily your hair absorbs and retains moisture. There are three levels of hair porosity: low, normal, and high.

To determine your hair porosity, you can do a porosity test. Take a single strand of hair and place it in a glass of water. If the hair sinks to the bottom of the glass, you have high porosity hair. If the hair floats on top of the water, you have low porosity hair. If the hair sinks to the middle of the glass, you have normal porosity hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Type

Once you have determined your hair type, it's important to take care of your hair properly. Here are some tips for maintaining each hair type:

  • Straight Hair: Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Wavy Hair: Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner, and style with a diffuser to enhance your natural waves.
  • Curly Hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and style with a curl-enhancing product to define your curls.
  • Coily Hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and style with a heavy oil or butter to seal in moisture and define your coils.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair types:

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

Q: Can I have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it's possible to have a combination of hair types. For example, you may have wavy hair on top and curly hair at the bottom.

Q: How often should I wash my hair based on my hair type?

A: The frequency of washing your hair depends on your hair type and your lifestyle. If you have fine hair, you may need to wash your hair more frequently than if you have coarse hair.

Hair Care for Each Hair Type

Here are some more tips on how to care for each hair type:

Straight Hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Don't over-brush your hair, as this can damage your hair strands.

Wavy Hair:

  • Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Style with a diffuser to enhance your natural waves.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as this can disrupt your wave pattern.

Curly Hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Style with a curl-enhancing product to define your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Coily Hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Style with a heavy oil or butter to seal in moisture and define your coils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy, beautiful, and looking its best.
