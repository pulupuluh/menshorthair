How To Grow Hair Back After Traction Alopecia

Written by Luffy Aug 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Traction alopecia is a form of hair loss that occurs when the hair is pulled too tightly for an extended period. This can happen due to hairstyles like braids, weaves, and extensions. If you have experienced traction alopecia, you might be wondering how to grow your hair back. In this article, we will discuss how to grow hair back after traction alopecia using natural remedies and other effective methods.

How to Grow Hair Back After Traction Alopecia

1. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp stimulates blood flow to the hair follicles, promoting hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp gently for five minutes every day. You can use a natural oil like coconut oil, jojoba oil, or castor oil for added benefits.

2. Use Essential Oils

Essential oils like peppermint, lavender, and rosemary can help promote hair growth. Mix a few drops of essential oil with a carrier oil like coconut or almond oil and massage it into your scalp. Leave it on for a few hours before washing it off. Repeat this process twice a week for best results.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals is essential for healthy hair growth. Include foods like leafy greens, nuts, and fish in your diet. You can also take supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and iron to promote hair growth.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull your hair too tight, like braids, weaves, and extensions. If you must wear these styles, ensure that they are not too tight and give your hair a break in between styles.

5. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase reduces friction and prevents hair breakage. This can help your hair grow back faster.

6. Try Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products available in the market that claim to promote hair growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like minoxidil, which is known to promote hair growth.

7. Consult a Dermatologist

If you have severe traction alopecia, it is best to consult a dermatologist. They can recommend treatments like corticosteroid injections, hair transplants, or scalp micropigmentation.

FAQ

Q: Can traction alopecia be reversed?

A: Yes, traction alopecia can be reversed if caught early. However, if the hair loss is severe, it might be difficult to reverse.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after traction alopecia?

A: It can take anywhere from a few months to a year for hair to grow back after traction alopecia.

Q: Can traction alopecia cause permanent hair loss?

A: Yes, severe and prolonged traction alopecia can cause permanent hair loss.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to prevent traction alopecia:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles
  • Give your hair a break between styles
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week

Conclusion

Traction alopecia can be a frustrating condition to deal with, but with patience and the right hair care routine, you can grow your hair back. Use the tips and methods mentioned in this article to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. If you have severe traction alopecia, consult a dermatologist for the best course of treatment.
