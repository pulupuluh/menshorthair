Big Chop Natural Hair 4C A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank May 12, 2023 · 7 min read

The big chop is a bold and empowering move for many women with natural hair. It involves cutting off all relaxed or chemically treated hair, leaving only natural hair. This process can be especially challenging for those with 4c hair, which is known for its tight curls and shrinkage. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the big chop process for 4c hair, including how to prepare, what to expect, and how to care for your new hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare for the Big Chop
  • The Big Chop Process
  • What to Expect After the Big Chop
  • Hair Care Tips for 4c Hair After the Big Chop
  • FAQ

How to Prepare for the Big Chop

Before you take the plunge and cut off your relaxed hair, it’s important to prepare yourself mentally and physically. Here are some steps you can take to get ready for the big chop:

  • Research different big chop styles and decide on the look you want to achieve.
  • Find a natural hair stylist who is experienced with cutting 4c hair.
  • Invest in hair care products that are specifically designed for 4c hair, such as moisturizing shampoos, conditioners, and leave-in treatments.
  • Start transitioning to natural hair by gradually trimming off your relaxed ends.
  • Talk to other women who have gone through the big chop process to get advice and support.

The Big Chop Process

Once you’re ready to take the plunge, it’s time to schedule your big chop appointment with your stylist. Here’s what you can expect during the process:

  • Your stylist will section your hair and use scissors to cut off your relaxed ends.
  • You may experience some discomfort or sensitivity as the scissors come into contact with your scalp.
  • Your stylist will shape your hair into the desired style, whether it’s a TWA (teeny weeny afro), a tapered cut, or another style.
  • Your stylist may also recommend a deep conditioning treatment to help nourish your new hair.

What to Expect After the Big Chop

After the big chop, you may experience a range of emotions, from excitement to anxiety. Here are some things you can expect in the days and weeks following the chop:

  • Your hair may look shorter than you expected due to shrinkage.
  • Your scalp may feel more sensitive or tender than usual.
  • You may notice a change in your hair texture as your natural hair grows out.
  • Your hair may require more frequent washing and conditioning to keep it moisturized.

Hair Care Tips for 4c Hair After the Big Chop

Now that you’ve gone through the big chop, it’s important to take care of your new hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips to help you care for your 4c hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase or wearing a satin bonnet.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as twist-outs, braid-outs, and wash-and-gos, to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after the big chop?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after the big chop. However, it may take some time for your hair to grow to your desired length.

Q: Is the big chop the only way to transition to natural hair?

A: No, the big chop is not the only way to transition to natural hair. You can also transition slowly by trimming off your relaxed ends over time.

Q: Will my hair be easier to manage after the big chop?

A: It depends on your hair texture and the style you choose. Some women find that their natural hair is easier to manage than relaxed hair, while others find it more challenging.

Q: How often should I wash my hair after the big chop?

A: It’s recommended to wash your hair every 7-10 days to keep it clean and moisturized.

The big chop is a major decision, but it can also be a liberating and empowering experience. By following these tips and taking care of your new hair, you can embrace your natural 4c hair and rock your new style with confidence.
