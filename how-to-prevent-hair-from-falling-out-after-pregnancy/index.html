How To Prevent Hair From Falling Out After Pregnancy

Written by Luffy Jul 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

One of the most common concerns for new mothers is hair loss after pregnancy. Many women experience hair shedding after giving birth, and it can be a frustrating and worrying experience. However, there are steps you can take to prevent hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to keep your locks looking luscious and thick.

Description

Hair loss after pregnancy, also known as postpartum hair shedding, is a normal and temporary condition that affects many new mothers. During pregnancy, your body experiences a surge of hormones that can cause your hair to become thicker and fuller. However, after giving birth, hormone levels return to normal, and your hair may enter a resting phase, leading to increased shedding. This shedding can be alarming for many women, but it usually subsides within a few months.

How to Prevent Hair Loss After Pregnancy

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

One of the most important steps you can take to prevent hair loss is to eat a balanced and nutritious diet. Your hair needs certain vitamins and minerals to grow and thrive, so make sure you're getting enough of the following:

  • Iron: Found in foods like spinach, red meat, and lentils
  • Protein: Found in foods like eggs, chicken, and fish
  • Vitamin C: Found in foods like citrus fruits, berries, and peppers
  • Vitamin D: Found in foods like fatty fish, egg yolks, and mushrooms
  • Zinc: Found in foods like oysters, beef, and pumpkin seeds

Additionally, make sure you're drinking plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Take Care of Your Scalp

Your scalp is the foundation for healthy hair, so make sure you're taking care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools like hairdryers and straighteners too often. If you have a dry scalp, try using a moisturizing hair mask once a week to help nourish your hair and scalp.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails can put stress on your hair and scalp, leading to increased shedding. Instead, try wearing your hair down or in loose styles like a messy bun or low ponytail. If you do wear your hair in a tight style, make sure to give your scalp a break by switching up your hairstyle every few days.

4. Manage Stress

Stress can have a big impact on your hair health, so make sure you're taking steps to manage stress in your life. Try practicing relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing. Additionally, make sure you're getting enough sleep each night to help your body recover and recharge.

5. See a Doctor

If you're experiencing excessive hair shedding or hair loss, it's important to see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions. Conditions like thyroid disorders or autoimmune diseases can cause hair loss, so it's important to get a proper diagnosis and treatment if necessary.

FAQs

How long does postpartum hair shedding last?

Postpartum hair shedding usually lasts for a few months after giving birth. You may notice increased shedding around 3-4 months postpartum, but it should start to improve after 6-12 months.

Will my hair grow back after postpartum shedding?

Yes, your hair should grow back after postpartum shedding. However, it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness and fullness.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips above, there are a few other things you can do to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss:

  • Avoid using harsh chemical treatments like perms or relaxers
  • Avoid over-brushing or over-styling your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to detangle wet hair
  • Consider taking a hair supplement like biotin or collagen

Conclusion

Postpartum hair shedding can be a frustrating experience for new mothers, but it's important to remember that it's a normal and temporary condition. By taking care of your scalp, eating a balanced diet, managing stress, and seeing a doctor if necessary, you can help prevent hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. With a little patience and care, your luscious locks will be back in no time.
