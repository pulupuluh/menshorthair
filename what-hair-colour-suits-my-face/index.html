What Hair Colour Suits My Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 15, 2023 Â· 11 min read

A new hair colour can give you a fresh and exciting look, but choosing the right colour that suits your face can be a daunting task. With so many shades and tones available, it's easy to get confused and make a wrong decision. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of selecting the perfect hair colour that complements your face and enhances your natural beauty.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Choose the Right Hair Colour
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Your hair colour is an essential aspect of your overall appearance, and it can make or break your look. Choosing the right hair colour that suits your skin tone, eye colour, and face shape can enhance your features and make you look younger and fresher. On the other hand, a wrong hair colour can make you look washed out, tired, and older than your actual age.

That's why it's essential to choose the right hair colour that brings out the best in you and complements your natural beauty. In the following sections, we'll show you how to choose the right hair colour and provide tips and tricks to maintain your hair colour and keep it looking fabulous.

How to Choose the Right Hair Colour

Choosing the right hair colour is not an easy task, and there are several factors to consider. Here are the things you need to consider before selecting a hair colour:

1. Skin Tone

Your skin tone is the most critical factor to consider when choosing a hair colour. There are three main skin tones: warm, cool, and neutral. Warm skin tones have yellow or golden undertones, cool skin tones have pink or blue undertones, and neutral skin tones have a mix of warm and cool undertones.

If you have a warm skin tone, go for hair colours with warm undertones, such as golden blonde, honey brown, or copper red. If you have a cool skin tone, go for hair colours with cool undertones, such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or burgundy. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can choose hair colours from both warm and cool tones.

2. Eye Colour

Your eye colour can also influence the hair colour you choose. If you have blue or green eyes, go for hair colours with cool tones, such as ash blonde or platinum blonde. If you have brown or hazel eyes, warm hair colours, such as caramel or chocolate brown, can complement your eyes.

3. Face Shape

Your face shape can also play a role in selecting the right hair colour. If you have a round face, go for hair colours that add height and length to your face, such as long layers or side-swept bangs. If you have a square face, go for hair colours that soften your jawline, such as long waves or textured layers.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to choosing the right hair colour:

1. Determine Your Skin Tone

Look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If you can't tell if your veins are blue or green, you have a neutral skin tone.

2. Decide on the Shade

Choose a hair colour shade that complements your skin tone. If you have a warm skin tone, go for warm-toned hair colours. If you have a cool skin tone, go for cool-toned hair colours.

3. Consider Your Eye Colour

Take into account your eye colour when choosing a hair colour. If you have blue or green eyes, go for cool-toned hair colours. If you have brown or hazel eyes, warm-toned hair colours can complement your eyes.

4. Think About Your Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when selecting a hair colour. If you have a busy lifestyle, choose a low-maintenance hair colour that requires minimal upkeep. If you have a lot of free time, you can experiment with more high-maintenance hair colours.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind when selecting a hair colour:

1. Consider Your Natural Hair Colour

Consider your natural hair colour when selecting a hair colour. If you have dark hair, going blonde may require multiple sessions and a lot of upkeep. If you have light hair, going darker may require a bit of adjustment.

2. Try on Wigs or Hair Extensions

If you're not sure about a hair colour, try on wigs or hair extensions in the desired shade before committing to it.

3. Consult a Professional

Consult a professional hairstylist before making a drastic hair colour change. They can provide expert advice and help you select the right hair colour that suits your face.

FAQs

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair if you have sensitive skin. However, you should use hair dye that is free from harsh chemicals and ammonia.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have grey hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair if you have grey hair. There are many hair dyes available that can cover grey hair and provide long-lasting colour.

Q: How often should I colour my hair?

A: It depends on the hair colour and the growth rate of your hair. Generally, you should colour your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the colour and cover any grey roots.

Hair Care after Colouring

After colouring your hair, it's essential to take proper care of it to maintain the colour and keep your hair healthy. Here are some tips for hair care after colouring:

1. Use Colour-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Use shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for coloured hair. These products are formulated to protect the hair colour and prevent fading.

2. Avoid Hot Styling Tools

Avoid using hot styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage the hair and cause the colour to fade.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains SPF.

Choosing the right hair colour that suits your face can enhance your natural beauty and give you a fresh and exciting look. By following the tips and tricks in this guide, you can select the perfect hair colour that complements your features and makes you look fabulous. Remember to take proper care of your hair after colouring to maintain the colour and keep your hair healthy and shiny.
