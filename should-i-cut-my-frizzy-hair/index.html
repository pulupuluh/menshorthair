Should I Cut My Frizzy Hair Tips And Faqs

Written by Petter Jul 29, 2023 · 6 min read

Frizzy hair can be quite frustrating to deal with. It’s difficult to manage, and can make styling your hair a time-consuming task. If you’re struggling with frizzy hair, you might be wondering if you should cut it. In this blog post, we’ll discuss some tips and answer some FAQs about cutting frizzy hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Hair

Description

Cutting frizzy hair can be a good idea if you’re looking for a change. It can also help to manage your hair and make styling easier. However, cutting your hair won’t necessarily solve the problem of frizz. Frizzy hair is usually caused by a lack of moisture, and cutting your hair won’t address this underlying issue.

How to Cut Your Frizzy Hair

If you’ve decided to cut your frizzy hair, you have a few options. You can either cut it yourself or go to a salon. It’s generally recommended to go to a salon, especially if you’re looking for a major change. A professional stylist will be able to give you advice on the best cut for your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re cutting your hair at home, here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Decide on the length you want to cut your hair.
  4. Divide your hair into sections.
  5. Start cutting one section at a time, using sharp scissors.
  6. Trim the ends of your hair to create a blunt cut.
  7. Blend the layers together by cutting at a slight angle.
  8. Finish by trimming any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Frizzy Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Cut your hair when it’s dry to get a more accurate length.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to a longer length.
  • Don’t try to cut too much hair at once.
  • Make sure you’re cutting your hair evenly on both sides.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting my frizzy hair make it less frizzy?

A: Cutting your hair won’t necessarily make it less frizzy. Frizzy hair is usually caused by a lack of moisture, so you’ll need to address this underlying issue to combat frizz.

Q: Should I cut my hair short or long?

A: The length you choose depends on your personal preference and face shape. A professional stylist can help you decide on the best length for your hair.

Q: Can I cut my own hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair. However, it’s generally recommended to go to a salon, especially if you’re looking for a major change.

Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Hair

To keep your frizzy hair under control, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a heat protectant when using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid using too many hair products.

Overall, cutting your frizzy hair can be a good idea if you’re looking for a change or want to make styling easier. However, cutting your hair won’t necessarily solve the problem of frizz. To combat frizz, you’ll need to address the underlying issue of a lack of moisture. Follow these hair care tips to keep your frizzy hair under control.
