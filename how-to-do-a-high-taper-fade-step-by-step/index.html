How To Do A High Taper Fade Step By Step

Written by Christine May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, a high taper fade might be just what you need. It's a trendy and stylish cut that can work for any hair type. In this article, we'll walk you through how to do a high taper fade step by step, so you can achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Gather Your Tools
  • Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size
  • Step 3: Trim the Top
  • Step 4: Create the Fade
  • Step 5: Blend the Sides and Back
  • Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns
  • Tips for a Perfect High Taper Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your High Taper Fade

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards (sizes 1-4)
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Trimming shears
  • Hair spray

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size

Determine the length you want your hair to be on top and choose the appropriate clipper guard size. If you're unsure, start with a #4 guard and work your way down.


Step 3: Trim the Top

Start by trimming the top of your hair. Use scissors to cut any stray hairs and create the desired length and shape. Then, use the clippers with the chosen guard to trim the sides and back of your head, leaving the top longer.


Step 4: Create the Fade

To create the fade, switch to a smaller guard size and start trimming the hair around the sides and back of your head, using the longest guard length near the top and gradually working your way down. Blend the hair as you go, creating a smooth transition from long to short.


Step 5: Blend the Sides and Back

Switch to an even smaller guard size and blend the sides and back of your head. Use the clippers to create a natural-looking taper, fading the hair from short to even shorter near the neckline.


Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns

Use the trimmers to clean up the neckline and sideburns, creating a straight and clean line. Finish with hair spray to keep your style in place.


Tips for a Perfect High Taper Fade

  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually.
  • Use the comb to hold the hair in place while you trim.
  • Blend the hair as you go for a smooth transition.
  • Keep the neckline and sideburns clean for a polished look.

FAQs

How often should I get a high taper fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the style loses its shape. Generally, you'll want to get a touch-up every 2-4 weeks to maintain the look.

Can I do a high taper fade at home?

Yes, you can do a high taper fade at home if you have the right tools and feel confident in your abilities. However, if you're unsure, it's always best to go to a professional stylist.

What hair types can rock a high taper fade?

A high taper fade can work for any hair type, from straight to curly, thick to thin. It's a versatile style that can be adapted to suit your individual hair texture and length.


Hair Care After Your High Taper Fade

After your high taper fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and keep it looking its best. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and nourished. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. If you have curly hair, use a curl-defining cream to keep your curls looking defined and bouncy. And remember to schedule regular touch-ups with your stylist to keep your high taper fade looking fresh.


Now that you know how to do a high taper fade step by step, you can confidently try this trendy and stylish look. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow our tips for a perfect fade. Good luck!
