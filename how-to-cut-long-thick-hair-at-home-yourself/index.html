How To Cut Long Thick Hair At Home Yourself

Are you tired of paying the high costs of haircuts at the salon? Do you have long thick hair that seems to grow faster than you can keep up with? Cutting your own hair at home can be a bit intimidating, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and some basic knowledge, you can successfully cut your own long thick hair at home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut long thick hair at home yourself.

How to Cut Long Thick Hair at Home Yourself

The first step in cutting long thick hair at home is to make sure that you have the right tools. Here are some of the tools that you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle of water

Once you have gathered all of the necessary tools, you can begin the process of cutting your own hair at home.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut your long thick hair at home:

  1. Start by washing your hair and allowing it to dry completely.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start by dividing your hair into two sections: top and bottom. Then, divide each of those sections into two more sections: left and right. This will give you four sections in total.
  3. Begin with the bottom section of hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the bottom section.
  4. Move on to the left and right sections of the bottom section of hair, and repeat the process of cutting each section straight across.
  5. Release the top section of hair and repeat the process of cutting each section of hair straight across.
  6. Move on to the left and right sections of the top section of hair, and repeat the process of cutting each section straight across.
  7. Once you have finished cutting all of the sections of hair, use the comb to blend the hair together. Comb the hair from the top of your head to the bottom, making sure that the hair is even and blended together.
  8. If necessary, go back and trim any areas that need it. Remember to cut small sections at a time and to comb the hair straight down before cutting.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you successfully cut your long thick hair at home:

  • Make sure that you are cutting the hair evenly. Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • If you are cutting your hair for the first time, start with a small trim to get comfortable with the process.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back.
  • Consider watching YouTube tutorials or asking a friend for help if you are unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long thick hair at home:

  • How often should I cut my long thick hair? It is recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.
  • What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair? Don't worry! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix any mistakes that you made while cutting your own hair.
  • Can I cut my hair when it is wet? It is generally recommended to cut hair when it is dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you have cut your long thick hair at home, it is important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye, as they can damage your hair.

Cutting your own long thick hair at home can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and some basic knowledge, you can successfully cut your hair at home. Remember to take your time, cut small sections at a time, and use sharp scissors. If you make a mistake, don't worry! Mistakes can be fixed, and hair grows back. With these tips and tricks, you can save money on haircuts and keep your long thick hair looking great.
