How Common Is It To Lose Hair After Pregnancy

Written by Christine Feb 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a new mom, you may have noticed that your hair isn't looking or feeling the way it used to. Many women experience hair loss after pregnancy, and it's completely normal. In fact, up to 50% of women experience hair shedding after giving birth. In this blog post, we'll explore why this happens and what you can do to care for your hair during this time.

Why Does Hair Loss Occur After Pregnancy?

During pregnancy, your body produces hormones that prevent your hair from shedding. This is why many women experience thicker, fuller hair during pregnancy. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels return to normal, and all the hair that was supposed to shed during pregnancy starts falling out at once. This can be alarming, but it's completely normal.

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for a few months, usually around three to six months after giving birth. Your hair will eventually grow back, but it may take up to a year or more for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.

How Can You Care for Your Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss?

While you can't completely prevent postpartum hair loss, there are some things you can do to care for your hair during this time:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Instead, opt for heatless styling methods like braids, twists, or buns.

2. Be Gentle When Brushing

Brushing your hair when it's wet can cause it to break more easily. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently work out any tangles.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help support healthy hair growth. Focus on eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats.

4. Consider Taking Supplements

Supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and omega-3 fatty acids can help support healthy hair growth. Talk to your doctor before starting any new supplement regimen.

5. Be Patient

Remember that postpartum hair loss is temporary and that your hair will eventually grow back. In the meantime, be patient and focus on caring for your hair as best you can.

FAQ About Postpartum Hair Loss

1. Is Postpartum Hair Loss Permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually grow back.

2. Can Breastfeeding Cause Hair Loss?

No, breastfeeding does not cause hair loss. However, the hormonal changes that occur during breastfeeding can contribute to postpartum hair loss.

3. Can Postpartum Hair Loss Be Prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to care for your hair during this time.

Conclusion

If you're experiencing postpartum hair loss, know that you're not alone. This is a common experience for many new moms, and it's completely normal. By taking care of your hair and being patient, you can help support healthy hair growth and eventually return to your pre-pregnancy hair state.
