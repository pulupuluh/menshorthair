How To Flat Iron Curly Frizzy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly frizzy hair can be challenging to tame. It can be frustrating to spend hours styling your hair, only to have it revert back to its natural state in a matter of minutes. One solution to this problem is flat ironing your hair. Flat ironing can give you the sleek, straight look you desire. In this article, we will guide you through the process of flat ironing curly frizzy hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Flat Iron Curly Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Flat Ironing Curly Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Frizzy Hair

How to Flat Iron Curly Frizzy Hair

Flat ironing curly frizzy hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the desired look. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before flat ironing your hair, it is important to wash and condition it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair. This will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent damage from heat styling.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a heat protectant. This will help to protect your hair from heat damage caused by the flat iron. Apply the heat protectant to your hair evenly, making sure to cover all areas.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Before flat ironing your hair, it is important to blow dry it. Use a round brush and a blow dryer to straighten your hair as much as possible. Avoid using high heat, as this can cause damage to your hair. Use low heat and a cool setting to dry your hair.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, section it into manageable pieces. Use hair clips to hold the sections in place. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair.

Step 5: Flat Iron Your Hair

Now it's time to flat iron your hair. Start with the bottom section and work your way up. Use a flat iron with ceramic plates, as these will help to distribute heat evenly. Use a low heat setting, and slowly run the flat iron over each section of hair. Repeat this process until all sections of hair are straightened.

Step 6: Finish with a Serum

To add some shine to your hair and reduce frizz, finish with a serum. Apply the serum evenly to your hair, making sure to cover all areas. This will help to keep your hair looking sleek and smooth.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to flat ironing curly frizzy hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Apply heat protectant.
  3. Blow dry your hair.
  4. Section your hair.
  5. Flat iron your hair.
  6. Finish with a serum.

Tips for Flat Ironing Curly Frizzy Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when flat ironing curly frizzy hair:

  • Use a flat iron with ceramic plates, as these will help to distribute heat evenly.
  • Avoid using high heat, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a low heat setting and slowly run the flat iron over each section of hair.
  • Apply a heat protectant before flat ironing your hair.
  • Section your hair into manageable pieces to make it easier to flat iron.
  • Finish with a serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

FAQs

Can I flat iron my hair if it is wet?

No, you should not flat iron your hair if it is wet. Always make sure your hair is completely dry before flat ironing.

How often should I flat iron my hair?

You should only flat iron your hair once or twice a week. Overusing a flat iron can cause damage to your hair.

What should I do if I accidentally burn my hair with a flat iron?

If you accidentally burn your hair with a flat iron, apply a hair mask to help repair the damage. You can also trim the damaged ends of your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Frizzy Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly frizzy hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using high heat when blow drying your hair.
  • Use a diffuser when blow drying your hair to enhance your natural curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it is wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it hydrated and healthy.

Flat ironing curly frizzy hair can be challenging, but with the right tools, techniques, and care, you can achieve the desired look. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this article to get the sleek, straight hair you desire.
