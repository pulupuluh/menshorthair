What Can You Do To Prevent Hair Loss During Chemo

Written by Petter May 30, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Dealing with cancer can be a tough journey, and one of the side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. Losing your hair can be a difficult experience, but there are things you can do to minimize the impact. Here are some tips on what you can do to prevent hair loss during chemo.

What Causes Hair Loss During Chemo?

Chemotherapy drugs are designed to kill rapidly dividing cancer cells, but they also target other fast-growing cells in the body, including hair follicle cells. This can lead to hair loss, which can be temporary or permanent, depending on the type and dosage of chemotherapy drugs used.

How to Prevent Hair Loss During Chemo

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemo, there are some things you can do to minimize its impact:

1. Scalp Cooling

Scalp cooling, also known as cold cap therapy, is a method of cooling your scalp during chemotherapy to reduce the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach your hair follicles. This can help to reduce hair loss or prevent it altogether. Scalp cooling works best with certain types of chemotherapy drugs, so talk to your doctor to see if it's a viable option for you.

2. Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a medication that's used to treat hair loss, and it's been shown to help prevent hair loss during chemotherapy. It's available as a topical solution that you can apply to your scalp before and after each chemotherapy session.

3. Hair Care

During chemotherapy, your hair can become fragile and prone to breakage, so it's important to be gentle with it. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, and use a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. You can also try using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction while you sleep.

4. Nutritional Support

A healthy diet can help to support hair growth, so try to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. You can also talk to your doctor about taking supplements that can help to support hair growth.

FAQ About Hair Loss During Chemo

Q: Will I definitely lose my hair during chemotherapy?

A: Not everyone who undergoes chemotherapy will experience hair loss, but it's a common side effect of many chemotherapy drugs.

Q: Is scalp cooling painful?

A: Scalp cooling can feel uncomfortable or cold, but it's not usually painful. Some people may experience headaches or scalp pain, but these side effects are rare.

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: In most cases, hair will grow back after chemotherapy, although it may be thinner or have a different texture than before. It can take several months or even a year for hair to fully regrow.

Hair Care Tips During Chemo

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your hair during chemotherapy:

1. Be Gentle

Use a gentle touch when washing and styling your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

2. Protect Your Scalp

Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.

3. Consider a Wig

If you're uncomfortable with the idea of losing your hair, consider getting a wig before you start chemotherapy.

4. Ask for Help

Don't be afraid to ask your friends and family for help with your hair care routine, especially if you're feeling tired or overwhelmed.

Conclusion

While hair loss during chemotherapy can be a difficult experience, there are things you can do to minimize its impact. Talk to your doctor about scalp cooling and minoxidil, and be gentle with your hair during chemotherapy. With the right care and support, you can get through this challenging time.
