How To Tame Short Hair That Sticks Up

Written by Bardi Aug 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Having short hair can be a great way to show off your features and highlight your face. However, when it comes to styling short hair, it can be a bit of a challenge. One common issue with short hair is that it can stick up in places, making it difficult to achieve a sleek and polished look. In this post, we'll provide some tips and tricks on how to tame short hair that sticks up, so you can achieve your desired look with ease.

Table of Contents

  • How to Tame Short Hair That Sticks Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

How to Tame Short Hair That Sticks Up

The first step in taming short hair that sticks up is to determine the cause. Short hair can stick up for a variety of reasons, such as natural texture, dryness, or damage. Once you know the cause, you can choose the appropriate products and techniques to tame your hair.

Identify the Cause

If you have short hair that sticks up, it could be due to a number of factors. Some common causes include:

  • Dryness: If your hair is dry, it can become brittle and prone to breakage, which can cause it to stick up.
  • Natural Texture: Some people have naturally textured hair that can be difficult to tame.
  • Damage: If your hair is damaged from heat styling or chemical treatments, it can be more prone to sticking up.
  • Sleep: If you sleep with wet hair, it can cause it to stick up in the morning.

Once you have identified the cause of your hair sticking up, you can choose the appropriate products and techniques to tame it.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are some steps you can take to tame short hair that sticks up:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a styling product that is appropriate for your hair type. For example, if you have dry hair, use a leave-in conditioner to add moisture. If you have naturally textured hair, use a curl-defining cream to enhance your curls.
  3. Use a comb or brush to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.
  4. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. This will help to enhance your natural texture and prevent your hair from sticking up.
  5. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Remember, the key to taming short hair that sticks up is to use the right products and techniques for your hair type.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you tame short hair that sticks up:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from sticking up while you sleep.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils and make it more prone to sticking up.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the ones that work best for your hair type.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about taming short hair that sticks up:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently.

Q: Can I use a straightening iron to tame my hair?

A: Yes, a straightening iron can be used to tame short hair that sticks up. However, be sure to use a heat protectant product to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: What kind of brush should I use on short hair?

A: A small, round brush is great for styling short hair. It can help to add volume and shape to your hair.

Hair Care for Short Hair

When it comes to caring for short hair, there are a few key things to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant product before heat styling.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can tame short hair that sticks up and achieve the sleek and polished look you desire.
