Why Is One Side Of My Hair Not As Curly

Written by Christine Jul 31, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you may have noticed that one side is not as curly as the other. This can be frustrating, especially if you are trying to achieve a certain style or look. In this blog post, we will discuss why this happens and what you can do to fix it.

Description

The reason why one side of your hair is not as curly as the other is usually due to the way you sleep or the direction in which you comb your hair. When you sleep, you may be lying on one side more than the other, which can cause your curls to flatten out. Additionally, if you comb your hair in one direction more than the other, this can also cause one side to be less curly.

How to

If you want to fix this issue, there are a few things you can do. First, try sleeping on your back or switching sides throughout the night to avoid flattening your curls. You can also try sleeping with a silk or satin pillowcase, as these materials are gentle on your hair and won't cause friction that can lead to frizz or flattened curls.

When it comes to combing your hair, try to comb it in different directions to distribute the curls evenly. You can also try using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls, as this will help maintain their shape and prevent them from becoming too stretched out.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for fixing one side of your hair that is less curly:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. After washing, gently squeeze out any excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  3. Apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair, such as a curl cream or gel.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls.
  5. Comb your hair in different directions to distribute the curls evenly.
  6. If you have time, let your hair air dry. If you need to use a hair dryer, use a diffuser attachment to prevent frizz and maintain your curls.
  7. If you want to add more definition to your curls, use a curling iron or wand on the less curly side.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your curls:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause your curls to lose their shape.
  • Try to avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or bonnet when you sleep to protect your curls and prevent them from getting tangled or flattened.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about why one side of your hair is not as curly:

Q: Is it normal for one side of my hair to be less curly?

A: Yes, this is a common issue for people with naturally curly hair. It is usually caused by sleeping on one side or combing your hair in one direction more than the other.

Q: Can I fix this issue permanently?

A: Unfortunately, there is no permanent fix for this issue, as it is usually caused by external factors such as sleeping or combing. However, you can take steps to prevent it from happening in the future.

Q: Will using a different shampoo or conditioner help?

A: It is possible that using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair may help enhance your curls and prevent them from becoming too stretched out. However, this may not necessarily fix the issue of one side being less curly.

Hair Care

When it comes to maintaining your curls, it is important to use products that are gentle on your hair and won't cause damage or frizz. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl creams, gels, and leave-in conditioners.

Additionally, be sure to deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy. You can also try incorporating natural oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil, into your hair care routine to add extra moisture and shine.

Remember, everyone's hair is different, so what works for one person may not work for another. Be patient and experiment with different products and techniques until you find what works best for you.
