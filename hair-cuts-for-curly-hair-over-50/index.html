Hair Cuts For Curly Hair Over 50 Tips And Ideas

Written by Robby Jun 23, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our hair changes in texture and volume. This is especially true for women with curly hair. If you have curly hair and you're over 50, you might be struggling to find a haircut that suits your hair type and complements your face shape. In this blog post, we'll explore some hair cuts for curly hair over 50 and provide tips on how to care for your hair.

How to Choose the Right Hair Cut for Curly Hair Over 50

Choosing the right hair cut can be challenging, especially if you have curly hair. Here are some tips to help you choose the best hair cut for your curly hair over 50:

Consider Your Face Shape

The first thing you need to do is consider your face shape. If you have a round face, you might want to go for a layered haircut that adds height and volume to the top of your head. If you have a long face, you might want to go for a haircut that adds volume to the sides.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Curly hair comes in different textures, from loose waves to tight coils. You need to choose a haircut that suits your hair texture. For example, if you have tight coils, you might want to go for a layered haircut that adds volume and movement. If you have loose waves, you might want to go for a blunt haircut that adds weight and definition.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle also plays a role in choosing the right haircut. If you have an active lifestyle and don't have much time to style your hair, you might want to go for a low-maintenance haircut that requires minimal styling. If you have a more relaxed lifestyle and enjoy experimenting with different styles, you might want to go for a more versatile haircut that allows you to try different looks.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Curly Hair

If you're planning to cut your curly hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide to help you:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you start cutting your curly hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair using a wide-toothed comb to detangle any knots.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Start with the back section and cut your hair in a straight line. Use a pair of sharp scissors and cut your hair in small sections.

Step 5: Move to the Sides

Once you've finished cutting the back section, move to the sides. Cut your hair in a straight line, following the same technique you used for the back section.

Step 6: Cut the Top Section

Finally, cut the top section. Use a pair of scissors to cut your hair in a straight line.

Tips for Taking Care of Curly Hair Over 50

Taking care of curly hair over 50 requires a different approach than taking care of straight hair. Here are some tips to help you take care of your curly hair:

Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's essential to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain natural ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz and breakage. Silk is gentle on curly hair and helps retain moisture.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy. Aim to trim your hair every six to eight weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Best Hair Cut for Curly Hair Over 50?

The best hair cut for curly hair over 50 depends on your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. Some popular hair cuts for curly hair over 50 include layered haircuts, blunt haircuts, and bob haircuts.

Can I Cut My Curly Hair at Home?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair at home, but it's essential to take your time and use the right tools. Make sure you have a pair of sharp scissors and a wide-toothed comb.

How Often Should I Wash My Curly Hair?

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's essential to avoid washing it too often. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week.

Conclusion

Curly hair can be challenging to manage, especially if you're over 50. However, with the right hair cut and hair care routine, you can keep your curly hair looking healthy and beautiful. Use the tips and ideas in this blog post to find the best hair cut for your curly hair over 50 and take care of your hair properly.
