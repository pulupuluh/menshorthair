How Do I Cut My Own Short Hair At Home

Written by Aprilia Feb 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to save some money and try out a new look, cutting your own short hair at home can be a great option. However, it can be daunting to take scissors to your own locks. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-quality cut without leaving your house. Here's how.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair bands
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to have clean, dry hair so you can see your natural texture and any uneven spots. Once your hair is dry, brush it out and part it how you normally wear it.

Next, section your hair into manageable sections using clips or hair bands. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly.

Cutting Your Hair

When you're ready to start cutting, begin with the sides of your head. Comb out a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair to your desired length, starting with small snips and working your way up. Be sure to cut in a straight line and use a mirror to check for symmetry.

Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around your head. If you want a layered look, hold the hair at a slight angle and cut into it, rather than straight across.

If you have clippers, you can use them to create a more precise cut around your neck and ears. Use a guard to ensure an even cut, and go slowly to avoid any mistakes.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting, use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven spots. Use a mirror to check for any missed areas or unevenness.

If you're happy with your cut, wash and style your hair as usual. If you're not satisfied, you can always make adjustments or seek out a professional stylist.

Tips for Success

  • Start with small snips and work your way up
  • Use a mirror to check for symmetry
  • Take breaks if you get tired or frustrated
  • Don't try to cut too much hair at once
  • Be patient and go slowly

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, it's important to cut it when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern and avoid any surprises.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or seek out a professional stylist to fix the issue.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and desired style. Short hair typically needs to be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape.

Hair Care After the Cut

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat tools too often, and protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather with a hat or scarf. With the right care, your short hair will look great until your next cut.
