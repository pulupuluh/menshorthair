Do Curls Grow Back After Haircut

Written by Bardi Apr 23, 2023 · 9 min read

If you’re someone who has naturally curly hair, you’ve probably had to deal with the question of whether or not your curls will grow back after getting a haircut. It’s a common concern for people who love their natural curls and want to maintain them. In this article, we’ll address this question and provide you with some helpful tips on how to care for your curly hair after a haircut.

Do Curls Grow Back After Haircut?

The simple answer is yes, curls do grow back after a haircut. However, the length and texture of your curls may be affected by the type of haircut you get. For example, a blunt cut may make your curls appear shorter and tighter while layers may add more volume and bounce to your curls.

It’s important to communicate with your hairstylist about your desired length and style before getting a haircut. This way, they can recommend a cut that will complement your natural curls and help them grow back in a healthy way.

How to Care for Curly Hair After a Haircut

Caring for your curly hair after a haircut is essential in maintaining healthy, growing curls. Here are some tips on how to care for your curly hair after a haircut:

1. Moisturize

Curly hair tends to be more dry and brittle than straight hair, so it’s important to keep it moisturized. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your curls hydrated and soft.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is the best tool for detangling curly hair without causing damage or breakage. Use it to gently comb through your curls after washing them.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can cause damage to curly hair. Instead, let your curls air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your natural curls.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Curly Hair After a Haircut

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to care for your curly hair after a haircut:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and following up with a deep conditioner. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls while the conditioner is still in your hair.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Blot your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel as it can cause frizz and breakage.

Step 3: Apply Styling Products

Apply a leave-in conditioner and a curl-defining cream to your hair while it’s still damp. Use your fingers to scrunch the product into your curls.

Step 4: Diffuse or Air Dry

Either use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls or let your hair air dry. Avoid touching your hair while it’s drying to prevent frizz.

Step 5: Style as Desired

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. You can use a hair oil or serum to add shine and tame any frizz.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair After a Haircut

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your curly hair after a haircut:

1. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims every 6-8 weeks can help keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.

2. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to protect your hair from the damaging effects of the sun.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I still straighten my hair after getting a curly haircut?

Yes, you can still straighten your hair after getting a curly haircut. However, it’s important to use a heat protectant spray and avoid heat styling too often to prevent damage to your curls.

How long does it take for curls to grow back after a haircut?

It can take anywhere from 3-6 months for your curls to grow back after a haircut, depending on how fast your hair grows.

What are some hairstyles that work well with curly hair?

Some hairstyles that work well with curly hair include layers, long bobs, and pixie cuts with curly bangs.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Avoid shampoos that contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness.

2. Don’t overwash your hair

Washing your hair too often can also cause dryness and damage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

3. Protect your hair while swimming

Wear a swim cap or apply a leave-in conditioner before swimming to protect your hair from chlorine and saltwater.

By following these tips and taking proper care of your curly hair, you can ensure that your curls grow back healthy and beautiful after a haircut.
