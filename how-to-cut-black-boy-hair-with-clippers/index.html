How To Cut Black Boy Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 27, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you a parent or caregiver of a black boy who needs a haircut? Cutting black boy hair with clippers can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools, techniques, and patience, it can be done easily and without any fuss. In this article, we’ll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to cut black boy hair with clippers. We’ll also share some tips, answer some FAQs, and give you some advice on how to care for your little one’s hair after the cut.

How to Cut Black Boy Hair with Clippers

Cutting black boy hair with clippers can be a bit different than cutting other types of hair. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you’ll need to gather all the necessary tools. This includes:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards (choose the right size for the length you want)
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair products (optional)

Make sure your clippers are charged and your scissors are sharp.

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before cutting, wash and dry the hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a more even result.

Step 3: Choose the Right Guard Size

Choose the right guard size for the length you want. If you’re not sure, start with a longer guard size and work your way down.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting from the bottom of the head and work your way up. Use the clipper with the guard attached to trim the hair to the desired length. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or to shape the hairline.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you’ve cut the hair to the desired length, blend the hair by using a shorter guard size at the bottom and gradually switching to a longer guard size as you move up towards the top of the head. This will create a more natural look.

Step 6: Clean Up

After you’re done cutting, clean up any loose hairs with a brush or vacuum. You can also wash the hair again to remove any loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Black Boy Hair with Clippers

Here are some tips to help you get the best result:

  • Take your time and don’t rush. Cutting hair takes patience.
  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down if needed.
  • Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or to shape the hairline.
  • Blend the hair for a more natural look.
  • Use hair products, such as oil or moisturizer, to keep the hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my black boy’s hair?

It depends on how fast the hair grows and how short you want the hair to be. As a general rule, you can cut the hair every 2-4 weeks.

2. What if I mess up?

Don’t worry! Hair grows back. If you’re not happy with the result, take your child to a professional barber or hairdresser to fix it.

3. Can I use regular clippers?

Yes, you can use regular clippers, but it’s better to use clippers designed for cutting hair. Hair clippers are designed to cut hair evenly and smoothly.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your black boy’s hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and moisturized. Here are some tips:

  • Use hair products, such as oil or moisturizer, to keep the hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Wash the hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Don’t pull or tug on the hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Cutting black boy hair with clippers can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools, techniques, and patience, it can be done easily and without any fuss. Just remember to take your time, start with a longer guard size, and blend the hair for a more natural look. And don’t forget to take care of your little one’s hair after the cut to keep it healthy and moisturized.
