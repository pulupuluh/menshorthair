How To Know If You Have A Long Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Aug 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you ever feel like your face looks longer than usual? It's possible that you have a long face shape. In this article, we will discuss how to know if you have a long face, what the characteristics of a long face are, and some tips on how to take care of your hair if you have a long face shape. Let's get started!

Table of Contents:

  • How to Know if You Have a Long Face
  • Characteristics of a Long Face
  • Tips for Hair Care with a Long Face Shape
  • FAQs

How to Know if You Have a Long Face

There are a few ways to know if you have a long face. Here are some characteristics to look out for:

  • Your face is longer than it is wide
  • Your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width
  • Your face has a rectangular shape
  • Your chin is elongated

If you have any of these characteristics, there's a good chance that you have a long face shape. However, it's important to note that everyone's face is unique and there are many variations of face shapes.

Step-by-Step Guide:

If you're still unsure whether you have a long face shape, here's a step-by-step guide to help you figure it out:

  1. Take a full-face photo of yourself or stand in front of a mirror.
  2. Measure the length of your face from the top of your forehead to the bottom of your chin.
  3. Measure the width of your face across your cheekbones.
  4. Compare the two measurements. If your face is longer than it is wide, you likely have a long face shape.

Characteristics of a Long Face

Now that you know how to identify a long face shape, let's talk about the characteristics of this face shape:

  • A long face has a vertical appearance
  • The forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width
  • The chin is elongated and may appear pointed
  • The face may appear narrow and angular
  • The temples may be more prominent than other face shapes

If you have a long face shape, it's important to keep these characteristics in mind when choosing hairstyles and makeup looks.

Tips for Hair Care with a Long Face Shape

Here are some tips on how to take care of your hair if you have a long face shape:

  • Avoid hairstyles that add height to the top of your head, as this can make your face appear even longer. Instead, opt for hairstyles that add width to your face, such as layers, waves, or curls.
  • Consider a side-swept fringe or bangs to shorten the appearance of your forehead.
  • Avoid straight, blunt cuts that end at your chin, as this can elongate your face even more. Instead, opt for haircuts that end just above or below your shoulders.
  • Consider a middle part or a zigzag part to break up the vertical appearance of your face.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands or hair clips, to add width to your face.

FAQs

What face shapes are similar to a long face?

Some face shapes that are similar to a long face include an oval face shape, a rectangular face shape, and a diamond face shape.

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a long face?

Avoid hairstyles that add height to the top of your head, such as slicked-back ponytails or high buns. Also, avoid straight, blunt cuts that end at your chin.

What hairstyles should I try if I have a long face?

Try hairstyles that add width to your face, such as layers, waves, or curls. Consider a side-swept fringe or bangs to shorten the appearance of your forehead. Experiment with different hair accessories to add width to your face.

Can makeup help shorten the appearance of a long face?

Yes, makeup can help shorten the appearance of a long face. Try contouring your cheekbones to add definition, and avoid blush that is applied too low on your face.

Conclusion

Knowing your face shape can help you choose hairstyles and makeup looks that flatter your features. If you have a long face shape, keep in mind the characteristics of this face shape and try out the tips we've provided to help you look and feel your best. Remember, everyone's face is unique, and there's no right or wrong way to look.
