How Long Does It Take To Grow Dreads From Short Hair

Written by William Feb 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking about growing dreads from short hair? It's a common question that many people ask, and the answer isn't as straightforward as you might think. The process of growing dreads from short hair can take anywhere from six months to two years, depending on a variety of factors. In this article, we'll dive into the details of how long it takes to grow dreads from short hair, and we'll provide you with some tips on how to make the process go as smoothly as possible.

Description

Before we get into the nitty-gritty of how long it takes to grow dreads from short hair, let's first define what dreadlocks are. Dreadlocks, or simply "dreads," are a hairstyle that involves allowing your hair to mat and tangle over time, creating long, rope-like strands. This hairstyle has been around for thousands of years and is associated with many different cultures, including Rastafarianism, Hinduism, and ancient Egyptian culture. Today, many people of all races and backgrounds wear dreadlocks as a fashion statement or as a way to express their cultural identity.

How To

If you're starting with short hair and want to grow dreads, there are a few things you'll need to do. Here's a step-by-step guide to getting started:

Step 1: Stop Cutting Your Hair

The first step in growing dreads from short hair is to stop cutting your hair. You'll need to grow your hair out to at least a few inches in length before you can start the dreadlocking process.

Step 2: Choose Your Method

Next, you'll need to decide which method you want to use to create your dreads. There are several different methods, including backcombing, twisting, and the neglect method. Each method has its pros and cons, so do some research and choose the one that's right for you.

Step 3: Start the Dreadlocking Process

Once you've chosen your method, it's time to start the dreadlocking process. This will involve a lot of twisting, backcombing, and palm rolling to create the knots that will eventually turn into dreads. Be prepared for this process to take some time, especially if you're starting with short hair.

Step 4: Be Patient

Finally, it's important to be patient as you grow your dreads. It can take anywhere from six months to two years for your dreads to fully mature, so don't get discouraged if it's taking longer than you expected. With time and patience, you'll have a head full of beautiful, natural dreads.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you grow dreads from short hair:

  • Regularly wash and condition your hair to keep it healthy and clean.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage it and slow down the dreadlocking process.
  • Consider using a special shampoo or wax designed for dreadlocks to help them form and stay tight.
  • Get regular maintenance, such as retwisting or palm rolling, to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing dreads from short hair:

Q: Can you grow dreads from short hair?

A: Yes, you can grow dreads from short hair, but it will take time and patience.

Q: How long does it take to grow dreads from short hair?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to two years to grow dreads from short hair, depending on a variety of factors.

Q: What's the best method for growing dreads from short hair?

A: There is no one "best" method for growing dreads from short hair. Each method has its pros and cons, so do some research and choose the one that's right for you.

Hair Care

It's important to take good care of your hair while you're growing dreads, especially if you're starting with short hair. Here are a few tips to keep your hair healthy and happy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage it and slow down the dreadlocking process.
  • Use a special shampoo or wax designed for dreadlocks to help them form and stay tight.
  • Get regular maintenance, such as retwisting or palm rolling, to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

In conclusion, growing dreads from short hair is a process that takes time and patience. It can take anywhere from six months to two years to fully mature, but with the right care and maintenance, you'll have a head full of beautiful, natural dreads in no time. Just be sure to choose the method that's right for you, and take good care of your hair along the way.
