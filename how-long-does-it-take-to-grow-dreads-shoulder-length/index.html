How Long Does It Take To Grow Dreads Shoulder Length

Are you thinking about getting dreadlocks but wondering how long it takes to grow them to shoulder length? Well, the answer is not straightforward as it depends on several factors, including hair texture, length, maintenance, and genetics. In this article, we'll explore how long it takes to grow dreads shoulder length, how to do it, tips, and hair care to ensure your locks are healthy and vibrant.

How to Grow Dreads Shoulder Length

Growing shoulder-length dreadlocks is a process that requires patience, dedication, and some degree of skill. You can grow dreadlocks naturally, using a variety of methods, including the neglect method, twist and rip, and backcombing. The method you choose will depend on your hair texture, length, and the desired outcome.

The neglect method involves letting your hair grow out freely without combing or brushing. This method is suitable for people with curly hair as it tends to form knots and tangles naturally. Twist and rip and backcombing methods involve sectioning your hair and twisting, ripping, or backcombing each section to create dreadlocks.

Step by Step Guide

If you're starting with straight hair, you may want to consider getting a perm or texturizer to help your hair lock faster. Here's a step by step guide on how to grow dreadlocks shoulder length:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  2. Section your hair into small sections, depending on the desired thickness of your locks.
  3. Apply a locking gel or wax to each section and twist, rip, or backcomb each section until it starts to form knots.
  4. Use a crochet needle to tighten and interlock each dread to create a seamless look.
  5. Avoid washing your hair for the first few weeks to let the dreads settle in.
  6. After the first few weeks, wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo once or twice a week.
  7. Re-twist and tighten your locks every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and length.
  8. Be patient and let your hair grow out naturally to shoulder length.

Tips for Growing Dreads Shoulder Length

Here are some tips to help you grow your dreadlocks shoulder length:

  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can clog your pores and cause breakouts.
  • Protect your locks from excessive heat and sun exposure by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Avoid using cotton pillowcases as they can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Be gentle when washing and detangling your locks to avoid breakage and damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing dreadlocks shoulder length:

How long does it take to grow locks shoulder length?

The time it takes to grow dreadlocks shoulder length varies depending on several factors, including hair texture, length, maintenance, and genetics. On average, it can take 1-2 years to grow shoulder-length dreadlocks.

Can you speed up the process of growing dreadlocks shoulder length?

While there's no guaranteed way to speed up the process, some things can help, such as getting a perm or texturizer, using locking gels or waxes, and regular maintenance.

Can you wash your hair with dreadlocks?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreadlocks. However, it's essential to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid excessive scrubbing to prevent frizz and breakage.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Dreadlocks require special care to remain healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your locks with a residue-free shampoo once or twice a week.
  • Use a locking gel or wax to help your hair lock faster.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup and clog your pores.
  • Protect your locks from excessive heat and sun exposure by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Maintain your locks regularly by re-twisting and tightening every 4-6 weeks.

With these tips, you can grow healthy, vibrant, and beautiful dreadlocks that will turn heads wherever you go. Remember, patience is key, and with time, dedication, and proper care, you'll achieve the shoulder-length dreadlocks of your dreams.
