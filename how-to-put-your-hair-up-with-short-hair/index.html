How To Put Your Hair Up With Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 02, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of always wearing your short hair down and want to switch things up? Putting your hair up might seem impossible with short hair, but it’s actually easier than you think. In this guide, we’ll show you how to put your hair up with short hair, including some tips and FAQs.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Putting Up Short Hair

How to Put Your Hair Up with Short Hair

When it comes to putting up short hair, you might immediately think of a messy bun or a ponytail. While those are great options, there are actually many other ways to put your hair up with short hair.

Here are some popular styles to consider:

  • Half up, half down
  • Pinned back with bobby pins
  • French twist
  • Braided crown
  • Low chignon

The key to putting up short hair is to use the right products and accessories. You’ll need bobby pins, hair ties, and possibly some styling products like hairspray or gel to keep your hair in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know some popular styles for putting up short hair, let’s go through a step-by-step guide on how to achieve them.

Half Up, Half Down

Step 1: Start by parting your hair down the middle or to one side.

Step 2: Take a small section of hair from the front on one side and twist it away from your face. Pin it back with a bobby pin.

Step 3: Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Brush out the rest of your hair and add some hairspray or texture spray for extra hold.

Pinned Back with Bobby Pins

Step 1: Part your hair to one side or down the middle.

Step 2: Take a small section of hair from one side and twist it away from your face. Pin it back with a bobby pin.

Step 3: Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Add more bobby pins as needed to secure any loose pieces.

French Twist

Step 1: Brush your hair back and gather it at the nape of your neck.

Step 2: Twist your hair upwards, tucking the ends under as you go.

Step 3: Pin your hair in place with bobby pins, making sure to secure any loose pieces.

Step 4: Finish with some hairspray to keep everything in place.

Braided Crown

Step 1: Part your hair down the middle.

Step 2: Take a small section of hair from one side and braid it all the way to the end. Secure with a clear elastic band.

Step 3: Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Bring one braid up and over your head, pinning it in place with bobby pins. Repeat with the other braid.

Step 5: Tuck the ends of the braids under each other and pin in place.

Low Chignon

Step 1: Gather your hair at the nape of your neck and twist it upwards, tucking the ends under as you go.

Step 2: Pin your hair in place with bobby pins, making sure to secure any loose pieces.

Step 3: Finish with some hairspray to keep everything in place.

Tips for Putting Up Short Hair

Putting up short hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you’ll be a pro in no time:

  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to avoid them being visible.
  • If you have layers, use hairspray or gel to keep them in place.
  • Don’t be afraid to add volume with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I put my short hair up without using bobby pins?

While bobby pins are a great tool for putting up short hair, you can also use hair ties, hair clips, or even a headband to create different styles.

How do I prevent my short hair from falling out of a ponytail?

Use a hair tie that’s tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not so tight that it causes headaches. You can also try backcombing the hair at the base of your ponytail to create more grip.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires regular trims to maintain its shape, so make sure to visit your hairstylist every 4-6 weeks. You can also use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair. Don’t forget to protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling.

Now that you know how to put your hair up with short hair, it’s time to experiment with different styles and have fun with your hair. Remember to use the right tools and products, and don’t be afraid to try new things.
