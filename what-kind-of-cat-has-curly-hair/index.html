What Kind Of Cat Has Curly Hair A Complete Guide

If you're a cat lover or an aspiring cat owner, you may have come across cats with curly hair. But what kind of cat has curly hair? In this article, we'll explore the types of cats that have curly hair, how to care for them, and more.

Types of Cats with Curly Hair

There are several types of cats that have curly hair, including:

Selkirk Rex

The Selkirk Rex is a relatively new breed of cat that was first discovered in Montana in 1987. This breed has a curly coat that is soft and plush to the touch. Selkirk Rexes come in a variety of colors and patterns, and they are known for their affectionate and laid-back personalities.

Devon Rex

The Devon Rex is a breed of cat that originated in England in the 1960s. This cat has a short, curly coat that is wavy in texture. Devon Rexes come in a variety of colors and patterns, and they are known for their playful and affectionate personalities.

Cornish Rex

The Cornish Rex is a breed of cat that originated in Cornwall, England in the 1950s. This cat has a short, curly coat that is soft and silky to the touch. Cornish Rexes come in a variety of colors and patterns, and they are known for their playful and affectionate personalities.

How to Care for Cats with Curly Hair

Cats with curly hair require special care to keep their coats healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your cat's coat regularly to prevent matting and tangling.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for cats with curly hair.
  • Avoid using a hairdryer, as it can damage your cat's coat.
  • Trim your cat's nails regularly to prevent them from getting caught in their curly coat.

Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming Cats with Curly Hair

Grooming cats with curly hair can be a bit more challenging than grooming cats with straight hair. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by combing out any tangles or mats in your cat's coat using a wide-toothed comb.
  2. Use a slicker brush to remove any loose hair from your cat's coat.
  3. Apply a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for cats with curly hair.
  4. Rinse your cat's coat thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Use a towel to gently pat your cat dry.
  6. Allow your cat's coat to air dry.
  7. Once your cat's coat is dry, use a wide-toothed comb to gently comb through their curls.
  8. Trim your cat's nails using a pair of cat nail clippers.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Cat Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your cat's curly coat:

  • Feed your cat a high-quality diet that is rich in protein to keep their coat healthy and shiny.
  • Provide your cat with plenty of fresh water to keep their skin and coat hydrated.
  • Keep your cat's environment clean and free of allergens that can irritate their skin.
  • Take your cat to the vet regularly for checkups and vaccinations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can cats with curly hair be hypoallergenic?

While no cat is completely hypoallergenic, some breeds, such as the Devon Rex, are known to produce fewer allergens than other breeds.

Do cats with curly hair shed?

Yes, cats with curly hair do shed, but their shedding is typically less noticeable than cats with straight hair.

How often should I groom my cat with curly hair?

You should groom your cat with curly hair at least once a week to prevent matting and tangling.

Can I use human shampoo on my cat with curly hair?

No, you should never use human shampoo on your cat. Human shampoo is too harsh for cats and can irritate their skin.

Do cats with curly hair require more grooming than cats with straight hair?

Yes, cats with curly hair require more grooming than cats with straight hair to keep their coats healthy and shiny.

Now that you know what kind of cat has curly hair and how to care for them, you can make an informed decision about whether a curly-haired cat is right for you. Remember to always provide your cat with love, care, and attention, and they will be a happy and healthy companion for years to come.
