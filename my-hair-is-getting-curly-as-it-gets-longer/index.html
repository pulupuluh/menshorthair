My Hair Is Getting Curly As It Gets Longer Understanding The Phenomenon

Written by Bardi Jul 16, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you have relaxed hair and have noticed that your locks are getting curlier as they grow longer, you are not alone. This phenomenon is not uncommon, and it often takes many women by surprise. In this article, we will explain why this happens and offer some tips on how to embrace your new curls with confidence.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Embrace Your Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Many women with relaxed hair are used to having straight locks that stay that way, regardless of how much their hair grows. However, as hair grows longer, it may start to take on a more curly or wavy texture. This is because the weight of the hair is pulling the curl pattern down, causing it to become more noticeable. Additionally, as relaxed hair grows, it may start to lose some of its chemical processing, which can also contribute to the appearance of curls.

How to Embrace Your Curly Hair

If your hair is getting curlier as it gets longer, it may take some getting used to. However, there are many ways to embrace your new texture and make the most of it. Here are a few tips:

1. Experiment with different styles

Curly hair can be incredibly versatile, so don't be afraid to try out different styles and see what works best for you. Whether you prefer to wear your hair in a sleek bun or let your curls run wild, there are plenty of options to choose from.

2. Use the right products

Using products specifically designed for curly hair can help enhance your natural texture and keep your curls looking their best. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil, which can help keep your hair hydrated and frizz-free.

3. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your curls look less defined. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair in top condition.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to styling curly hair, it can be helpful to have a step-by-step guide to follow. Here's a basic routine that you can use to keep your curls looking their best:

1. Start with clean, wet hair

Cleanse your hair with a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Follow up with a moisturizing conditioner, and comb through your hair with a wide-toothed comb to detangle.

2. Apply your styling products

Apply your styling products to your hair while it's still wet. If you have fine hair, use a lightweight mousse or gel. If you have thicker hair, you may want to use a heavier cream or leave-in conditioner.

3. Dry your hair

You can either air dry your hair or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to speed up the process. If you're using a blow dryer, use a low heat setting and hold the dryer a few inches away from your hair to avoid heat damage.

4. Scrunch your curls

Once your hair is dry, use your hands to scrunch your curls and enhance their shape. You can also use a curling iron or wand to define any sections that need a little extra help.

Tips for Styling Curly Hair

Styling curly hair can be a bit of a learning curve, but with a few tips, you can make the process much easier. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Use a wide-toothed comb

Curly hair is prone to tangling, so it's important to use a comb that won't pull or snag your curls. A wide-toothed comb is gentle on your hair and will help prevent breakage.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause your curls to lose their definition. Whenever possible, let your hair air dry, and use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer if you need to speed up the drying process.

3. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentle on your hair and won't cause friction that can lead to breakage. Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help keep your curls looking their best.

FAQs

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if it's getting curly?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair if you want to. Just keep in mind that heat styling can be damaging, so be sure to use a heat protectant and avoid using high heat settings.

Q: Can I get rid of my curls?

A: If you don't want to embrace your new curls, you can always get a relaxer or keratin treatment to straighten your hair. Just keep in mind that these treatments can be damaging and may require a lot of maintenance.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: How often you wash your hair depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. If you're active or have oily hair, you may need to wash it more often.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

1. Moisturize regularly

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to moisturize your hair regularly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and use a leave-in conditioner every time you wash your hair.

2. Avoid sulfates and silicones

Sulfates and silicones can be harsh on curly hair and can cause buildup, which can make your curls look dull and lifeless. Look for products that are sulfate- and silicone-free.

3. Protect your hair at night

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent breakage and keep your curls looking their best. You can also wrap your hair in a silk scarf to protect it while you sleep.

With these tips and tricks, you can embrace your new curls with confidence and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
