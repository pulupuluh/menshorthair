How To Cut Mohawk Fade Step By Step

Written by Petter Jun 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you want to achieve a fresh and edgy look, then the mohawk fade is definitely the hairstyle for you! This style is a combination of a classic mohawk and a fade, giving it a modern twist that is perfect for any occasion. In this article, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of cutting a mohawk fade, so you can rock this style with confidence!

Description

The mohawk fade is a popular hairstyle that features a strip of hair running from the front to the back of the head, with the sides shaved or faded. This style can be customized to suit your preferences, whether you want a high or low fade, a wide or narrow mohawk, or a bold or subtle look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a mohawk fade that is both stylish and easy to maintain.

How to Cut Mohawk Fade Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools on hand:

  • Clippers with various guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair spray or gel (optional)

Step 2: Choose Your Fade

The first step in cutting a mohawk fade is to decide how short you want the sides to be. You can choose from a high fade, where the hair on the sides is shaved almost to the skin, or a low fade, where the hair gradually gets shorter towards the ears. Use your clippers and guards to create the desired fade, starting with a longer guard and working your way down to a shorter one. It's important to blend the fade smoothly, so take your time and use a comb to check for any uneven spots.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Once you have the fade in place, it's time to cut the hair on the top of the head. Use your scissors to trim the hair to the desired length, keeping in mind that the mohawk should be longer than the sides. You can create a more dramatic look by leaving the hair in the center of the head longer and tapering it towards the back. Alternatively, you can keep the mohawk narrow and symmetrical for a more subtle effect.

Step 4: Trim the Edges

After you have the basic shape of the mohawk in place, use your scissors or clippers to clean up the edges. You can create a sharp line between the mohawk and the fade, or you can blend the two sections for a more natural look. Don't forget to trim any stray hairs around the ears and neckline for a polished finish.

Step 5: Style Your Mohawk

Once you have finished cutting your mohawk fade, you can style it in a variety of ways. You can use hair spray or gel to keep the hair in place, or you can leave it natural for a more casual look. Experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best!

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a comb to check for any uneven spots in the fade.
  • Trim the hair on the top of the head gradually to avoid cutting off too much.
  • Don't forget to trim the edges for a clean finish.
  • Experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best.

FAQ

Can I cut a mohawk fade at home?

Yes, you can cut a mohawk fade at home with the right tools and techniques. However, if you are not confident in your skills, it's best to visit a professional stylist to avoid any mistakes.

How often should I trim my mohawk fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. Generally, you should trim your mohawk fade every 2-4 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

What hair type is best for a mohawk fade?

A mohawk fade can work with any hair type, whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair. However, it's important to choose a style that complements your natural texture and face shape.

Hair Care

To keep your mohawk fade looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized, and avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair soft and shiny.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut a mohawk fade that is both stylish and easy to maintain. Whether you want a bold or subtle look, this hairstyle is sure to turn heads and make a statement. So grab your tools and get ready to rock your mohawk fade with confidence!
