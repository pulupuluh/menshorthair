How To Cut Face Framing Layers For Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be challenging to cut, but when done correctly, it can look stunning with face framing layers. These layers will add shape, definition, and volume to your curls. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut face framing layers for curly hair. We will also give you tips on how to take care of your hair once you have cut it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Face Framing Layers for Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Face framing layers are designed to highlight your best features and give your hair a more textured and voluminous look. They can be cut to suit any hair type and face shape, but in this article, we will be focusing on curly hair.

When cutting face framing layers for curly hair, it's important to take into account the texture and thickness of your curls. Curly hair tends to shrink when dry, so it's essential to cut it when it's wet, to get the right length.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers for Curly Hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut face framing layers for curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt or debris and make your curls more manageable.
  2. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. This will help to remove any knots and make it easier to section your hair.
  3. Section your hair into four parts, using hair clips. Start by dividing your hair into two parts, from the front to the back of your head. Then, divide each section in half, from ear to ear.
  4. Starting with the front section, take a small section of hair at the top of your head, near your hairline. This will be your guide section.
  5. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it to the desired length. This will be the length of your face framing layers.
  6. Using the guide section as a reference, cut the rest of the hair in the front section to the same length. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, following the shape of your face.
  7. Repeat this process on the other side of your head, making sure that the length and angle of your layers are even.
  8. Move on to the back sections of your head and repeat the same process. Remember to keep the length and angle of your layers consistent with the front sections.
  9. Check your hair in the mirror to make sure that your layers are even and the length is what you desire.
  10. Style your hair as desired, using a diffuser to enhance your curls and give them more volume.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting face framing layers for curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to get a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut gradually to get the desired length.
  • Make sure to cut your hair when it's wet, to get the right length.
  • Don't cut your hair too short, as curly hair tends to shrink when dry.
  • Work in a well-lit area, so you can see what you're doing.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting face framing layers for curly hair:

Q: Can I cut my own hair?
A: Yes, you can cut your own hair, but it's recommended to have a professional do it, especially if you're not experienced with cutting hair. Q: How often do I need to cut my hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your layers. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks. Q: Can I cut layers in my curly hair without face framing?
A: Yes, you can cut layers in your curly hair without face framing, but face framing layers will enhance your curls and give your hair more definition.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your hair, it's essential to take care of it to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid tangling and breakage.

By following these tips and the step-by-step guide, you can cut face framing layers for curly hair like a pro. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have beautiful, defined curls in no time.
