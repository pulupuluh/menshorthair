How To Cut Layers On Your Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 05, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your hair cut at a salon? Do you want to try cutting your own hair at home but don’t know where to start? Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this article, we’ll show you how to cut layers on your own short hair in a simple and easy-to-follow guide. You don’t need any prior experience or training, just a few basic tools and some patience. So, let’s get started!

How to Cut Layers on Your Own Short Hair

Cutting layers on short hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Here are the key steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair and blow dry it until it’s completely dry. Make sure your hair is free of any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into three sections: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip the top section away, leaving the sides and back loose.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Starting at the nape of your neck, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Using hair-cutting scissors, cut the hair straight across at the desired length. Repeat this process, working your way up to the crown of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

For the sides, use the previous section as a guide and cut the hair at the same length. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Top

For the top section, take a horizontal section of hair and hold it between your fingers at a 45-degree angle. Use the scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, creating a layered effect. Repeat this process, working your way from the front to the back of your head.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers, take small sections of hair from the top and bottom layers and cut them at a slight angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You’ll need a few basic tools to cut your own hair at home:

  • Hair-cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Step 7: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers, take small sections of hair from the top and bottom layers and cut them at a slight angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 8: Check for Symmetry

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, check for symmetry by comparing the lengths on both sides. Make any necessary adjustments to even out the layers.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Now that you’ve cut your hair, it’s time to style it. You can use a hair straightener, curling iron, or hair products to achieve your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors. Don’t use regular scissors, as they can damage your hair.
  • Work in a well-lit area with a large mirror.
  • Take your time and don’t rush. It’s better to make small cuts than to cut off too much hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it can be difficult to see the true length.
  • Use hair clips to keep sections of hair out of the way.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful.

FAQs

Can I cut layers on my own short hair?

Yes, you can! With the right technique and tools, you can achieve a layered haircut at home.

What kind of scissors should I use?

Use hair-cutting scissors, which are specifically designed for cutting hair. Don’t use regular scissors, as they can damage your hair.

Do I need any prior experience or training?

No, you don’t need any prior experience or training. Just follow the steps in this guide and take your time.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your haircut.

Now that you know how to cut layers on your own short hair, you can save money and achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Just remember to take your time, follow the steps in this guide, and use the right tools. Happy cutting!
