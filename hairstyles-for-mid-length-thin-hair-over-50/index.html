Hairstyles For Mid Length Thin Hair Over 50

Written by Bardi May 19, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our hair tends to become thinner and less voluminous. However, this doesn't mean that we can't have fabulous hairstyles that suit our personality and style. Mid length hair is a great option for women over 50, as it is versatile, easy to maintain, and can make you look younger. In this article, we will explore some of the best hairstyles for mid length thin hair over 50, along with tips on how to style and care for your hair.

Description

If you have thin hair, you may think that your styling options are limited. However, this is far from the truth. Mid length hair is a great choice for women over 50, as it can give you a youthful, chic look without requiring too much maintenance. Whether you prefer a bob, a layered cut, or a shaggy style, there are plenty of options to choose from. The key is to find a hairstyle that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

How to

When it comes to styling mid length thin hair, there are a few key tricks that can help you achieve a fuller, more voluminous look. Here are some tips:

1. Use volumizing products

To add volume and thickness to your hair, use volumizing products such as mousse, root lifters, and volumizing sprays. Apply these products to damp hair, focusing on the roots, and blow dry your hair upside down to create volume.

2. Get regular trims

To keep your hair looking healthy and full, get regular trims every 6-8 weeks. This will prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look thinner.

3. Experiment with layers

Adding layers to your mid length hair can create the illusion of thickness and volume. Ask your stylist for a layered cut that flatters your face shape and hair texture.

Step by step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for styling your mid length thin hair:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and let it air dry or blow dry it using a round brush.

2. Apply a volumizing product

Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots, and distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

3. Use a curling iron

Using a curling iron, curl small sections of your hair away from your face. This will create volume and movement.

4. Finish with hairspray

Spray your hair with a light-hold hairspray to keep your curls in place and add shine.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for styling and caring for your mid length thin hair:

1. Avoid over-styling

Over-styling your hair can cause damage and breakage, which can make your hair look thinner. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage.

3. Get regular scalp massages

Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation and stimulate hair growth. Use a scalp massager or your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes every day.

FAQ

What is the best haircut for mid length thin hair over 50?

The best haircut for mid length thin hair over 50 is a layered cut that adds volume and movement to your hair. Ask your stylist for a cut that flatters your face shape and hair texture.

Can I dye my thin hair?

Yes, you can dye your thin hair. However, it's important to choose a hair dye that is gentle and won't damage your hair. Ask your stylist for recommendations.

How often should I wash my thin hair?

You should aim to wash your thin hair every 2-3 days. Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils and make it look limp and lifeless.

Hair Care

To keep your mid length thin hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some hair care tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that is gentle and won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for products that are specifically designed for thin or fine hair.

2. Avoid hot water

Hot water can be damaging to your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Use lukewarm or cool water when washing your hair, and avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers and curling irons.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun can cause damage to your hair, making it dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

In conclusion, mid length hair is a great option for women over 50 with thin hair. With the right haircut, styling products, and care, you can have a fabulous hairstyle that makes you look and feel younger. Try out some of these tips and tricks to achieve a fuller, more voluminous look, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and cuts.
