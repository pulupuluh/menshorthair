How To Stop Top Layer Of Hair Frizzing Tips And Tricks

Written by William Jul 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have curly or wavy hair, you know that frizz is an inevitable part of life. But even those with straight hair can be plagued by frizz, especially on the top layer of their hair. Frizz can be caused by humidity, heat damage, and even genetics. But don't worry, there are ways to tame the frizz and achieve sleek, smooth hair. In this article, we'll show you how to stop top layer of hair frizzing with our tips and tricks.

How to Stop Top Layer of Hair Frizzing

Follow these simple steps to tame your frizzy hair and keep your top layer smooth and sleek.

Step 1: Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by choosing a shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for frizzy hair. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like argan oil, shea butter, and coconut oil. These ingredients will help hydrate your hair and keep it from drying out, which can lead to frizz.

Step 2: Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can cause it to become dry and frizzy. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even every three days if possible. If your hair gets oily in between washes, use a dry shampoo to absorb the excess oil.

Step 3: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

After you wash your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to the ends and mid-lengths of your hair. This will help lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause it to become frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first. And try to limit your use of these tools to once a week or less.

Step 5: Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

Regular towels can cause friction on your hair, which can lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry.

Step 6: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction on your hair, which can lead to frizz. Switch to a silk pillowcase, which will be gentler on your hair and help prevent frizz.

Tips for Preventing Frizz

In addition to the above steps, there are a few other things you can do to prevent frizz:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles like ponytails or braids
  • Use a humidifier in your home to keep the air from becoming too dry

FAQ

How can I tell if my hair is frizzy?

Frizzy hair looks dry, dull, and has a rough texture. It can also be difficult to style and may have flyaway hairs that stick out.

Can genetics cause frizzy hair?

Yes, genetics can play a role in whether or not you have frizzy hair. If your parents have frizzy hair, you may be more likely to have it too.

Can I use a hair serum to prevent frizz?

Yes, hair serums can help prevent frizz by coating your hair and smoothing down the cuticles. Look for a serum that is lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to preventing frizz. Be sure to use products that are formulated for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. And remember, be gentle with your hair â avoid tugging or pulling on it, and be sure to use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle it.

With these tips and tricks, you can stop top layer of hair frizzing and achieve sleek, smooth hair. Say goodbye to frizz and hello to your best hair days.
