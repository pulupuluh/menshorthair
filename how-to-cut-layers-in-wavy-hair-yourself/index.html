How To Cut Layers In Wavy Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi May 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have wavy hair, you know that it can be challenging to find the right haircut that works for your hair type. One popular hairstyle for wavy hair is the layered look. Layers can add volume, shape, and movement to your hair. However, visiting a salon and getting your hair done professionally can be expensive. That's why we've put together a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers in wavy hair yourself.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in Wavy Hair Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Wavy Hair

How to Cut Layers in Wavy Hair Yourself

Before we get into the nitty-gritty of cutting layers in wavy hair, it's essential to have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror

Now that you have your tools ready, let's dive into the steps of cutting layers in wavy hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Make sure your hair is clean and tangle-free before starting to cut your hair.

Step 2: Comb your hair and part it in the middle. Divide your hair into sections, and clip the top section away from the bottom section.

Step 3: Take the bottom section of your hair and comb it thoroughly. Decide how much hair you want to cut off the ends. Remember, it's better to cut less hair than to cut too much.

Step 4: Cut the hair straight across the bottom, making sure to cut all the hair in one go. This will ensure that the hair is even.

Step 5: Now, take the top section of your hair and comb it thoroughly. Decide where you want to start cutting the layers. You can cut the layers starting from your cheekbones or chin.

Step 6: Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and cut it. Repeat this process for all the sections of hair you want to cut.

Step 7: Once you've cut all the sections of hair you want to cut, comb your hair to see how it looks. If you want to cut more layers, repeat the process from step 5.

Step 8: Once you're happy with your new layers, style your hair as usual.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting layers in wavy hair:

  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair. You can always cut more hair if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Make sure your hair is dry while cutting because wet hair can appear longer than it is.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting layers in wavy hair can take a while.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. Remember, it's better to cut less hair than to cut too much.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

A: It's better to cut your hair when it's dry because wet hair can appear longer than it is.

Q: Should I cut my hair myself or go to a salon?

A: It depends on your comfort level and experience with cutting hair. If you're not confident, it's better to go to a salon.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Wavy Hair

After cutting layers in wavy hair, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to avoid damaging your hair while combing.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently. If you do, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

With these tips, you can easily cut layers in wavy hair yourself and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
