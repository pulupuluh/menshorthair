How To Cut Face Framing Bangs For Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your curly hair getting in your face all the time? Do you want to change up your look with some stylish bangs? If so, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll show you how to cut face framing bangs for curly hair in a few easy steps. Whether you have tight curls, loose waves, or anything in between, you'll be able to create a new and exciting look that will make you feel confident and beautiful.

Description

Face framing bangs are a great way to change up your look and add some variety to your curly hair. They can be cut in a variety of lengths and styles, depending on your personal preferences and face shape. Bangs can help to frame your face, highlight your best features, and even make you look younger.

How to Cut Face Framing Bangs for Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your bangs, you'll need to gather a few supplies. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to help section off your hair. You may also want to have a spray bottle filled with water to help dampen your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Start by parting your hair in the middle and combing it out.
  2. Next, section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. This will typically be the hair that falls in front of your ears and across your forehead.
  3. Use the comb to separate the hair into a triangular shape. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your forehead, and the base of the triangle should be at the top of your ears.
  4. Clip the rest of your hair back so that it's out of the way.
  5. Take the section of hair between your fingers and comb it straight down over your forehead.
  6. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across, just above your eyebrows. If you want shorter bangs, you can cut them higher up on your forehead. If you want longer bangs, you can cut them closer to your hairline.
  7. Release the clipped hair and check your work. If you need to make any adjustments, comb the hair back down and trim as needed.
  8. Repeat this process on the other side of your head, making sure that your bangs are even and symmetrical.
  9. Once you're happy with your bangs, you can style them as desired.

Tips for Cutting Bangs on Curly Hair

Cutting bangs on curly hair can be a bit tricky, but with these tips, you'll be able to create a beautiful and stylish look:

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry. Cutting curly hair when it's wet can result in shorter bangs than you intended.
  • Be conservative with your first cut. It's always better to start with longer bangs and trim as needed, rather than cutting too much off at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short. Remember that curly hair will shrink up as it dries, so what looks like the perfect length when wet may end up being too short later on.
  • Consider getting your bangs cut by a professional stylist if you're nervous about doing it yourself.

FAQs

Can I cut my bangs if I have really tight curls?

Yes, you can still cut bangs if you have tight curls. Just be sure to cut them when your hair is dry and use a conservative approach.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim your bangs a bit shorter or style them differently to hide the mistake. If you're really unhappy with the results, you may need to get your bangs professionally fixed.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Don't wash your hair too often. Curly hair can dry out easily, so washing it too frequently can make it look dull and lifeless.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to help hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet, as a regular brush can cause breakage.
  • Don't rub your hair dry with a towel. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water and let your hair air dry.

By following these tips and using the techniques outlined in this article, you'll be able to cut face framing bangs for curly hair with ease. Whether you're looking for a subtle change or a bold new look, bangs are a great way to switch things up and show off your beautiful curls.
