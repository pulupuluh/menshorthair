Easy Updo Hairstyles For Medium Length Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Welcome to our ultimate guide to easy updo hairstyles for medium length hair. Whether you're going to a special event, a night out with friends, or just want to switch up your everyday look, an updo is a great option. In this article, we'll provide step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice for achieving the perfect updo. Let's get started!

How to Create Easy Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Before we dive into specific updo styles, let's go over some basic tips on how to create an updo that will work with your hair length and texture:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add grip and texture to your hair
  • Invest in good quality hair pins and elastics
  • Don't be afraid to use hair accessories like headbands, clips, and scarves to add interest to your updo

Best Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Now that you have the basics down, let's look at some easy updo hairstyles for medium length hair:

1. Messy Bun

The messy bun is a classic updo that works for any occasion. To achieve this look, start by pulling your hair into a high ponytail. Then, twist the hair around the base of the ponytail and secure with bobby pins. Don't worry about making it too neat - the messier, the better!

2. Braided Updo

A braided updo is a great way to add interest to your hair while keeping it off your face. To create this look, start by braiding a small section of hair at the front of your head. Then, pull the rest of your hair back into a low ponytail and braid it. Finally, wrap the braids around each other and secure with bobby pins.

3. Half-Up Half-Down

The half-up half-down style is a great option if you want to show off your length while still keeping your hair out of your face. To create this look, simply pull the top half of your hair back and secure it with a hair tie or clip. For added interest, twist the hair before securing it.

Step-by-Step Guide to Easy Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Now that you've seen some examples of easy updo hairstyles for medium length hair, let's break down the steps to achieve each look:

Messy Bun

  1. Pull your hair into a high ponytail and secure with a hair tie
  2. Twist the hair around the base of the ponytail to create a bun
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins
  4. Use your fingers to loosen the hair around the bun to create a messy look
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place

Braided Updo

  1. Braid a small section of hair at the front of your head
  2. Pull the rest of your hair back into a low ponytail and braid it
  3. Wrap the braids around each other and secure with bobby pins
  4. Use your fingers to loosen the braids to create a messy look
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place

Half-Up Half-Down

  1. Part your hair down the middle
  2. Take a small section of hair from each side of your head and pull it back
  3. Secure the sections of hair at the back of your head with a hair tie or clip
  4. For added interest, twist the hair before securing it
  5. Spray with hairspray to hold the style in place

Tips for Achieving Perfect Updo Hairstyles

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when creating easy updo hairstyles for medium length hair:

  • Experiment with different hair accessories like headbands, clips, and scarves
  • Don't worry about making your updo too perfect - a messy look is on-trend
  • Use texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add grip and volume to your hair
  • Secure your updo with plenty of bobby pins to ensure it stays in place all day or night

FAQ: Updo Hairstyles for Medium Length Hair

Can I create an updo with short hair?

Yes, you can still create an updo with short hair. However, the style may be more limited than with medium length hair. Try experimenting with braids, twists, and buns to find a style that works for you.

What hair accessories should I use for an updo?

You can use a variety of hair accessories for an updo, including headbands, clips, and scarves. Choose accessories that complement your outfit and personal style.

How can I make my updo last all day?

Use plenty of bobby pins to secure your updo, and use hairspray to hold the style in place. You can also try using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add grip to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Medium Length Hair

Finally, to keep your medium length hair healthy and looking its best, be sure to follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage

We hope this guide to easy updo hairstyles for medium length hair has been helpful! Remember to experiment with different styles and have fun with your hair.
