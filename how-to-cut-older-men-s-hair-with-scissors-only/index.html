How To Cut Older Men s Hair With Scissors Only

Written by Aprilia Aug 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you an aspiring hairdresser or just a family member who wants to give a haircut to an older man? Cutting hair can be challenging, but it can be done with patience and practice. In this article, we will discuss how to cut older men's hair with scissors only.

Description

As we age, our hair tends to thin out and become weaker. Older men may have hair that is difficult to style or cut, so it's important to take precautions when cutting their hair. Cutting hair with scissors only can be a little tricky, but it's doable.

How to

Here are the steps you can follow to cut an older man's hair with scissors only:

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

Before you start cutting hair, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need a pair of scissors, a comb, clips to hold the hair in place, and a towel to cover the person's shoulders.

Step 2: Wash the Hair

Before cutting hair, it's essential to wash it thoroughly. This will ensure that the hair is clean and free from any product buildup.

Step 3: Comb the Hair

After washing the hair, comb it to remove any tangles. This will help you see the natural fall of the hair and decide where to cut.

Step 4: Section the Hair

Use clips to section the hair into manageable portions. Start at the back of the head and work your way to the top.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up. Cut small sections at a time, making sure to blend the hair as you go. Use your fingers to guide the hair and make sure to cut straight across.

Step 6: Cut the Sides

After cutting the back of the head, move on to the sides. Cut the hair to the desired length, making sure to blend it well with the back.

Step 7: Cut the Top

The top of the head may be the hardest part to cut. Use your fingers to measure the length and cut small sections at a time. Make sure to blend the hair well with the sides and back.

Step 8: Check for Uneven Spots

After cutting the hair, check for any uneven spots. Use your scissors to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 9: Clean Up

After cutting the hair, use a brush to remove any loose hair. Remove the towel from the person's shoulders and ask them to shake their head to remove any hair that may have fallen on their clothes.

Step 10: Style the Hair

If the person you're cutting hair for wants a particular hairstyle, use a comb and styling product to achieve the desired look.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to longer cuts.
  • Use your fingers to measure the length of the hair before cutting.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Make sure to blend the hair well to avoid any harsh lines.
  • Check for any uneven spots and make necessary adjustments.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to cut hair?

A: Cutting hair can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the length and thickness of the hair.

Q: Can you cut hair without washing it?

A: It's best to wash hair before cutting it to ensure that it's clean and free from product buildup.

Hair Care

After cutting hair, it's essential to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling the hair to avoid damage.
  • Trim the hair regularly to keep it looking healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting older men's hair with scissors only can be challenging, but it's doable with practice and patience. Follow the steps and tips listed in this article to achieve a great haircut. Remember to take care of the hair after cutting it to keep it healthy and looking great.
