How To Cut Naturally Wavy Hair Yourself

Are you tired of spending hundreds of dollars at the salon to get your wavy hair cut? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair and save money? Cutting your own hair can seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and rewarding process. In this article, we will show you how to cut naturally wavy hair yourself.

Description

Naturally wavy hair can be tricky to cut because of its texture and curl pattern. It's important to take extra care when cutting wavy hair to avoid uneven lengths and frizz. By following the tips and techniques in this article, you can achieve a beautiful, even haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Naturally Wavy Hair Yourself

Cutting naturally wavy hair yourself requires the right tools and preparation. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Mirror

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on. Now you're ready to start cutting!

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your naturally wavy hair yourself:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into sections. Use hair ties or clips to separate the top, sides, and back of your hair.
  2. Take a section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut.
  3. Using sharp scissors, make a horizontal cut across the hair. Don't cut straight across â angle the scissors slightly to avoid a blunt cut.
  4. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, working from the bottom up.
  5. If you want to add layers, take a section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers up to the length you want to layer.
  6. Using the scissors, make a diagonal cut into the hair. This will create a layered effect.
  7. Blend the layers into the rest of your hair by combing and cutting small sections at a time.
  8. Check your hair in the mirror to make sure the length is even.
  9. Use clippers to trim the hair around your ears and neckline, if desired.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Naturally Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting naturally wavy hair:

  • Don't cut too much at once â it's better to make small cuts and work your way up.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it may appear longer than when it's dry.
  • Be patient and take your time â cutting your own hair can be a slow process.
  • If you're unsure, it's better to leave it to the professionals.

FAQs About Cutting Naturally Wavy Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting naturally wavy hair:

Q: Can I cut my naturally wavy hair when it's wet?
A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as it may appear longer when it's wet. Q: How often should I cut my naturally wavy hair?
A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and the style you want. On average, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks. Q: Should I use clippers to cut my naturally wavy hair?
A: Clippers can be useful for trimming the hair around your ears and neckline, but they're not necessary for cutting wavy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Naturally Wavy Hair

After cutting your naturally wavy hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and frizz-free.
  • Get regular trims to avoid split ends and maintain your haircut.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own naturally wavy hair and save money on salon visits. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment and try new things!
