Difference Between Low Fade And Drop Fade

Written by Petter Jun 13, 2023 · 8 min read

In this article, we will discuss the difference between low fade and drop fade haircuts. Both styles are popular among men, and it's essential to know the difference before choosing which one to get. We'll cover the basics of each style, how to achieve them, and tips for maintaining your new look.

What is a Low Fade?

A low fade is a popular haircut that involves cutting the hair shorter as it gets closer to the neckline. The hair gradually fades from longer on top to shorter on the sides and back. The fade can be subtle or more dramatic, depending on personal preference. Low fades can be achieved with clippers or scissors, and they are a versatile style that works with many different hair types.

What is a Drop Fade?

A drop fade, also known as a "drop" or "drop temple," is a more dramatic version of the low fade. The hair is cut even shorter at the temples, creating a more pronounced arch that drops down towards the ear. This style is often paired with a buzz cut or crew cut on top, creating a sharp contrast between the longer hair on top and the very short sides.

What's the Difference?

The main difference between a low fade and a drop fade is the level of fade. A low fade gradually tapers from longer hair on top to shorter hair on the sides and back. A drop fade has a more dramatic arch around the temples, creating a sharper contrast between the longer hair on top and the very short sides. Drop fades are typically more noticeable and eye-catching than low fades.

How to Achieve a Low or Drop Fade

To achieve a low or drop fade, you'll need to enlist the help of a professional barber or hairstylist. Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a low or drop fade:

Step 1: Decide on the Length

Before your barber starts cutting, you'll need to decide on the length of your hair. Let them know if you want a short, medium, or long cut on top, and whether you want a subtle or dramatic fade.

Step 2: Cut the Top

Next, your barber will cut the hair on top of your head to your desired length. They may use scissors or clippers, depending on your hair type and the style you're going for.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Using clippers, your barber will gradually cut the hair shorter as they move towards the neckline. They may use different guard sizes to create a subtle or more dramatic fade. For a drop fade, they'll also create a sharp arch around the temples.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Finally, your barber will blend the hair on the sides and back with the longer hair on top. This creates a seamless, natural-looking fade that transitions from longer to shorter hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade

To keep your fade looking fresh, you'll need to visit your barber regularly for touch-ups. Depending on how quickly your hair grows, you may need to get a trim every 2-4 weeks. You can also use styling products like hair wax or pomade to create different looks with your faded haircut.

FAQ

Q: Can I get a low or drop fade with curly hair?

A: Yes! Low and drop fades work with many different hair types, including curly hair. Your barber may need to use different techniques to achieve the desired look, but it's definitely possible.

Q: Do I need to use special hair products for a fade?

A: Not necessarily, but using a styling product can help you achieve different looks with your faded haircut. Hair wax or pomade can help you create a sleek, polished look, while a texturizing spray can add volume and texture to your hair.

Hair Care for Faded Haircuts

To keep your faded haircut looking its best, you'll need to take good care of your hair. This includes washing it regularly with a gentle shampoo, using conditioner to keep it soft and moisturized, and avoiding heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

In conclusion, both low fades and drop fades are popular men's haircuts that offer a stylish, versatile look. The main difference between the two styles is the level of fade, with drop fades being more dramatic and eye-catching. To achieve either style, you'll need to visit a professional barber or hairstylist, and to maintain your new look, you'll need to visit them regularly for touch-ups and take good care of your hair at home.
