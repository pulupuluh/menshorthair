Things To Eat To Stop Hair Fall A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Mar 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of seeing more hair on your comb than on your head? Hair fall can be a frustrating experience, but did you know that your diet can play a significant role in preventing it? In this article, we will look at some of the best things to eat to stop hair fall.

Table of Contents

  1. Protein
  2. Omega-3 Fatty Acids
  3. Iron
  4. Vitamin C
  5. Biotin
  6. Zinc
  7. FAQs
  8. Hair Care Tips

Protein

Protein is a crucial nutrient for healthy hair growth. Our hair is made up of a protein called keratin, so it's essential to consume enough of it to maintain strong and healthy hair. Foods that are high in protein include meat, fish, eggs, nuts, and legumes. If you're a vegetarian or vegan, make sure to include plant-based protein sources like quinoa, tofu, and lentils in your diet.


Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats that our body cannot produce on its own. These fats are vital for healthy hair growth and can be found in foods like fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines), chia seeds, flaxseeds, and walnuts. Omega-3s help to nourish hair follicles, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy hair growth.


Iron

Iron is essential for healthy hair growth as it helps to carry oxygen to the hair follicles. If you don't consume enough iron, your hair can become weak and brittle, leading to hair fall. Foods that are high in iron include red meat, chicken, fish, spinach, and lentils. If you're iron deficient, consider taking an iron supplement after consulting with your doctor.


Vitamin C

Vitamin C is a powerful antioxidant that helps to protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals. It also helps the body to absorb iron, which is crucial for healthy hair growth. Foods that are high in vitamin C include citrus fruits, strawberries, kiwi, bell peppers, and broccoli.


Biotin

Biotin, also known as vitamin B7, is a water-soluble vitamin that plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy hair. Biotin helps to produce keratin, the protein that makes up our hair. Foods that are high in biotin include eggs, almonds, sweet potatoes, spinach, and broccoli.


Zinc

Zinc is a mineral that helps to keep our hair and scalp healthy. It plays a crucial role in cell growth and repair and helps to keep the oil glands around hair follicles functioning correctly. Foods that are high in zinc include oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds, and lentils.


FAQs

How long does it take for dietary changes to show an effect on hair fall?

It can take up to three months for dietary changes to have a visible effect on hair fall. Be patient and consistent with your diet changes, and you'll start to see results.

Can supplements help with hair fall?

Supplements can help with hair fall, but it's essential to consult with your doctor before taking any. Overdosing on supplements can be harmful and can lead to hair fall.

Can stress cause hair fall?

Yes, stress can cause hair fall. When we're stressed, our body produces hormones that can lead to hair fall. It's essential to manage stress levels to prevent hair fall.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to incorporating the right foods into your diet, it's essential to take care of your hair to prevent hair fall. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners and curling irons as they can damage hair follicles.
  • Avoid tying your hair too tightly as it can lead to hair breakage.
  • Don't comb or brush your hair when it's wet as it can lead to hair fall.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood circulation and promote healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, eating the right foods and taking care of your hair can help prevent hair fall. Incorporate the foods mentioned in this article into your diet and follow the hair care tips to maintain healthy and strong hair.
