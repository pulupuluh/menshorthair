Which Hair Colour Is Allowed In Islam

Written by Bardi Mar 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are a Muslim and want to dye your hair, it is essential to know which hair colour is allowed in Islam. According to Islamic teachings, it is permissible to change the colour of your hair, but there are certain guidelines that you need to follow. In this article, we will discuss which hair colour is allowed in Islam and some related topics.

Description

The colour of your hair is a personal choice, and Islam does not restrict you from changing it. However, there are some guidelines that you need to follow when dyeing your hair. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) encouraged Muslims to change their hair colour and said that it was a way to beautify oneself.

How to Dye Your Hair in Islam

When dyeing your hair, it is essential to use natural hair dyes that are made from henna or other natural substances. Chemical hair dyes are not allowed in Islam as they can harm your hair and scalp. It is also essential to avoid dyeing your hair black, as black is considered the colour of the devil in Islamic teachings.

Step by Step Guide to Dye Your Hair in Islam

Step 1: Choose a Natural Hair Dye

When choosing a hair dye, make sure it is made from natural substances such as henna, indigo, or chamomile. These natural hair dyes are safe for your hair and scalp and also provide a beautiful colour that lasts for a long time.

Step 2: Prepare the Hair Dye

Follow the instructions on the package to prepare the hair dye. Most natural hair dyes require mixing with water or other natural substances to create a paste.

Step 3: Apply the Hair Dye

Apply the hair dye to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to cover all your hair evenly with the dye. Leave the dye on your hair for the recommended time mentioned on the package.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair

After the recommended time has passed, rinse your hair with warm water until the water runs clear. Avoid using shampoo or conditioner for at least 24 hours after dyeing your hair.

Tips for Dyeing Your Hair in Islam

Here are some tips that you can follow when dyeing your hair in Islam:

  • Choose a natural hair dye made from henna or other natural substances.
  • Avoid using chemical hair dyes that can harm your hair and scalp.
  • Do not dye your hair black, as it is considered the colour of the devil in Islamic teachings.
  • Test the hair dye on a small patch of skin before applying it to your hair to avoid any allergic reactions.
  • Follow the instructions on the package carefully when preparing and applying the hair dye.

FAQ

Q. Can I dye my hair any colour in Islam?

A. No, you cannot dye your hair black as it is considered the colour of the devil in Islam. It is also recommended to avoid bright or unnatural colours.

Q. Can I dye my hair with chemical hair dyes in Islam?

A. No, it is not permissible to use chemical hair dyes in Islam as they can harm your hair and scalp.

Q. Can I dye my hair with henna in Islam?

A. Yes, you can dye your hair with henna in Islam as it is a natural hair dye that is safe for your hair and scalp.

Hair Care in Islam

Islam encourages Muslims to take care of their hair and keep it clean and healthy. It is recommended to wash your hair regularly and use natural products that are free from harmful chemicals. You can also use natural hair oils such as olive oil or coconut oil to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.

Conclusion

In conclusion, it is permissible to dye your hair in Islam, but you need to follow certain guidelines. Use natural hair dyes made from henna or other natural substances and avoid using chemical hair dyes. Do not dye your hair black, and follow the instructions carefully when dyeing your hair. Take care of your hair and keep it clean and healthy using natural products.
