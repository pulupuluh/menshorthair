Which Hair Colour Is Best For My Skin Tone A Guide For 2023

Written by Daniel Mar 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking about changing your hair colour? With so many options to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which shade will look best on you. One of the most important factors to consider is your skin tone. In this guide, we will explore which hair colours are best suited for different skin tones, as well as provide tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair
  • FAQs about Hair Colour and Skin Tone
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Colour

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair colour is to determine your skin tone. There are three main categories: warm, cool, and neutral. Here are some tips to help you figure out which category you fall into:

Warm Skin Tone

If you have a warm skin tone, you likely have golden or yellow undertones. Your skin may also have a peachy or golden glow. Here are some indicators that you have a warm skin tone:

  • Your veins appear greenish
  • You look best in gold jewelry
  • Your skin tans easily

Cool Skin Tone

If you have a cool skin tone, you likely have pink or blue undertones. Your skin may also have a rosy or reddish glow. Here are some indicators that you have a cool skin tone:

  • Your veins appear bluish
  • You look best in silver jewelry
  • Your skin burns easily

Neutral Skin Tone

If you have a neutral skin tone, you have a balance of both warm and cool undertones. Your skin may appear to have no obvious undertones. Here are some indicators that you have a neutral skin tone:

  • Your veins appear blue-green
  • You look good in both gold and silver jewelry
  • Your skin tans and burns equally

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Once you have determined your skin tone, you can start thinking about which hair colours will complement your complexion. Here are some general tips to keep in mind:

  • For warm skin tones, opt for hair colours with golden or honey tones, such as caramel or warm brown.
  • For cool skin tones, opt for hair colours with ashy or cool undertones, such as platinum blonde or cool brown.
  • For neutral skin tones, you can experiment with both warm and cool hair colours.
  • Consider your natural hair colour when choosing a new shade. If you have naturally dark hair, it may be easier to transition to a darker shade with warm undertones.

Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair

If you have never coloured your hair before, it can be helpful to follow a step-by-step guide to ensure that you achieve your desired results. Here are some basic steps to follow:

  • Choose a hair colour that complements your skin tone and natural hair colour.
  • Read the instructions carefully and do a patch test to check for any allergic reactions.
  • Apply the hair colour to dry hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  • Leave the colour on for the recommended amount of time, as stated in the instructions.
  • Rinse out the hair colour thoroughly and apply the provided conditioner.
  • Style your hair as desired.

FAQs about Hair Colour and Skin Tone

Q: Can I dye my hair a drastically different colour from my natural shade?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair a drastically different colour from your natural shade. However, keep in mind that it may require more maintenance and upkeep, especially if you are going from a darker to a lighter shade.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how noticeable your roots are. As a general rule, touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain a consistent colour.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have previously chemically treated hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair if you have previously chemically treated hair. However, it is important to wait at least 2-4 weeks between chemical treatments to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Colour

Once you have coloured your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain the vibrancy and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as it can strip the colour.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as they can also damage your hair and cause the colour to fade.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you have a better understanding of which hair colour is best for your skin tone, you can confidently choose a shade that will enhance your natural features. Remember to follow the instructions carefully and take care of your hair to maintain a beautiful and healthy mane.
