How To Cut Your Own Hair With Clippers Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to save some money on haircuts, or just want to try something new, cutting your own hair with clippers fade may be the way to go. It can be intimidating at first, but with the right tools and a little practice, you can achieve a professional-looking fade right at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair with clippers fade, from start to finish.

Description

The fade is a popular hairstyle that involves a gradual transition from short hair to longer hair. It's a versatile style that can be worn by people of all ages and hair types. With clippers, you can achieve a smooth, even fade without having to go to the barber.

How to Cut Your Own Hair with Clippers Fade

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

The first step in cutting your own hair with clippers fade is to choose the right clippers. Look for clippers that are designed for home use, with adjustable blades and different guard sizes. Wahl, Andis, and Oster are all popular brands that make quality clippers.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and ensure a more even cut.

Step 3: Choose the Right Guard Size

The guard size you choose will determine how short your hair will be. Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller size as you go. A good starting point is a #4 guard for the top and a #2 guard for the sides.

Step 4: Start with the Sides

Begin by trimming the sides of your hair. Start with the largest guard size and work your way down, using a comb to lift the hair as you go. Use an upward motion to create a fade effect, gradually blending the shorter hair into the longer hair on top.

Step 5: Move to the Top

Once you've finished the sides, move on to the top of your hair. Use the same guard size you used on the sides, and trim the hair evenly across the top of your head.

Step 6: Blend the Top and Sides

Using a smaller guard size, blend the top and sides of your hair together. Use a comb to lift the hair and create a smooth transition between the shorter sides and the longer top.

Step 7: Trim the Neckline and Sideburns

Finish off your cut by trimming the neckline and sideburns. Use a smaller guard size or no guard at all for a clean, tapered look.

Tips

  • Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller size as you go.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and create a smooth transition between the shorter sides and the longer top.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to take small, gradual steps than to make big, drastic cuts.
  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be discouraged if your first attempt isn't perfect. Keep practicing and you'll get better with time.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Clippers are designed specifically for cutting hair and will give you a more even, professional-looking cut.

What if I mess up my cut?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! It's just hair and it will grow back. You can always go to a barber or stylist to fix any mistakes.

How often should I cut my hair?

How often you cut your hair will depend on your personal preference and hair type. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair with clippers fade, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. You can also use hair styling products, like pomade or gel, to create different looks and add texture to your hair.

Cutting your own hair with clippers fade can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little practice, you can achieve a professional-looking fade right at home. Follow these tips and steps to master the art of cutting your own hair with clippers fade.
