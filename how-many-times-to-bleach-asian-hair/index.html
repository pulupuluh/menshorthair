How Many Times To Bleach Asian Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Aug 17, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're an Asian with dark hair and want to achieve a lighter shade, bleaching is the best option. However, bleaching Asian hair requires a careful approach, as it can be more delicate than other hair types. In this article, we'll discuss how many times to bleach Asian hair and provide tips for successful bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • How to Bleach Asian Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Successful Bleaching
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Bleaching

How to Bleach Asian Hair

Bleaching Asian hair requires a strategic approach to avoid damaging the hair. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Determine the Level of Lightening Needed

Before bleaching, determine the level of lightening needed to achieve your desired hair color. If your hair is dark, you may need to bleach it multiple times to reach the desired level. It's essential to be patient and avoid over-processing the hair.

Step 2: Choose the Right Bleaching Product

When choosing a bleaching product, look for one that is specifically formulated for Asian hair. These products are gentler and have a lower concentration of peroxide. Avoid using a product with a high concentration of peroxide, as it can damage your hair.

Step 3: Protect Your Scalp and Hairline

Before applying the bleach, protect your scalp and hairline with petroleum jelly or a protective cream. This will prevent the bleach from getting on your skin and causing irritation.

Step 4: Apply the Bleach

Follow the instructions on the bleaching product, and apply it to your hair. Begin at the ends of your hair, and work your way up to the roots. Avoid overlapping the bleach on already processed hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to bleach Asian hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup. Dry your hair completely.

Step 2: Mix the Bleach

Follow the instructions on the bleaching product to mix the bleach. Use a non-metallic bowl and brush to mix the bleach.

Step 3: Apply the Bleach

Divide your hair into sections and apply the bleach using the brush. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. Avoid applying the bleach to already processed hair.

Step 4: Check the Lightening Process

Check your hair every 10 minutes to monitor the lightening process. Avoid leaving the bleach on your hair for too long, as it can damage your hair.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

Rinse your hair with warm water and apply a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.

Tips for Successful Bleaching

Here are some tips for successful bleaching:

  • Do a strand test to determine how long you need to leave the bleach on your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 24-48 hours before bleaching to protect your scalp.
  • Use a toner after bleaching to tone down any brassy tones.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools after bleaching, as your hair is more delicate and prone to damage.

FAQs

How many times should I bleach my Asian hair?

The number of times you should bleach your Asian hair depends on the level of lightening you need to achieve your desired hair color. Generally, it's best to bleach your hair in stages to avoid damaging your hair.

Can I bleach my Asian hair at home?

Yes, you can bleach your Asian hair at home, but it's essential to follow the instructions on the product and take precautions to protect your hair and scalp.

Will bleaching my Asian hair damage it?

Bleaching can damage your Asian hair if not done correctly. It's essential to follow the instructions on the product and avoid over-processing your hair.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your Asian hair, it's essential to take care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as your hair is more delicate and prone to damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how many times to bleach Asian hair and how to do it correctly, you can achieve your desired hair color without damage. Remember to take precautions and take care of your hair after bleaching to maintain its health and shine.
