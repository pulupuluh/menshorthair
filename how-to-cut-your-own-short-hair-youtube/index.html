How To Cut Your Own Short Hair Youtube A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the help of YouTube, it's easier than ever to achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own short hair using YouTube tutorials. From selecting the right tools to perfecting your technique, we've got you covered.

Description

Whether you're looking to save money on salon visits or simply prefer the convenience of cutting your own hair, YouTube is a valuable resource for DIY haircuts. With thousands of tutorials available, it's easy to find a style that suits your needs and skill level. However, cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially when it comes to short styles. That's why we've put together this comprehensive guide to help you get the results you want.

How to Cut Your Own Short Hair YouTube: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the right tools. This includes a pair of sharp scissors, hair clippers (if desired), a comb, and hair ties. You may also want to invest in a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head.

Step 2: Choose Your Style

Once you have your tools, it's time to choose your desired style. YouTube is a great place to find inspiration, whether you're looking for a classic bob or a trendy pixie cut. Make sure to choose a style that is realistic for your hair type and skill level.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wash and dry your hair. This will help you get a more accurate cut and prevent any tangles or knots.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into manageable pieces. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, start cutting your hair in small sections. Make sure to use a comb to guide your cuts and keep your hair even. If you're using clippers, make sure to choose the right guard and follow the same technique.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

As you cut, make sure to check your progress in the mirror. This will help you make any necessary adjustments and ensure that your cut is even all around.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your cut, it's time to style your hair. You can use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to achieve your desired look. YouTube is also a great resource for styling tutorials.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Short Hair

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

  • Start slow and only cut a little at a time.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and ensure that your cut is even.
  • Invest in quality tools, including sharp scissors and hair clippers.
  • Choose a style that is realistic for your hair type and skill level.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Can I really cut my own hair?

Yes, with the right tools and technique, anyone can cut their own hair. It may take some practice, but it's a great way to save money and achieve a personalized look.

What if I mess up?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a salon to have your hair fixed or try to fix it yourself. Remember to start slow and only cut a little bit at a time.

How often should I cut my own hair?

This depends on your hair type and desired style. Generally, short haircuts require more frequent maintenance, so you may need to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

Cutting your own hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. Make sure to take care of your hair by washing and conditioning regularly, using heat protectant products, and avoiding harsh chemicals and treatments.

Cutting your own short hair using YouTube tutorials can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, technique, and inspiration, you can achieve a look that is personalized and unique to you. Remember to start slow, practice on a mannequin head or willing friend, and have fun!
