How To Stop Your Short Haired Dog From Shedding

Written by Christine Jul 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a pet owner, you know how frustrating it can be to constantly clean up after your furry friend. If you have a short haired dog that seems to shed more than usual, you're not alone. Shedding is a natural process for dogs, but there are ways to minimize it. In this article, we'll show you how to get a short haired dog to stop shedding.

How to

There are several methods you can use to stop your short haired dog from shedding. Here are some of the most effective ones:

1. Brush Your Dog Regularly

One of the most effective ways to reduce shedding is to brush your dog regularly. Brushing helps to remove loose hair and dirt from your dog's coat. You should use a brush that's appropriate for your dog's coat type. For short haired dogs, a slicker brush or a rubber brush is ideal.

2. Bathe Your Dog

Bathing your dog on a regular basis can also help to reduce shedding. Make sure that you use a shampoo that's designed for dogs and that you rinse your dog thoroughly. You should also avoid bathing your dog too often, as this can dry out their skin and cause more shedding.

3. Feed Your Dog a Healthy Diet

Your dog's diet can also affect their shedding. Make sure that you're feeding your dog a high-quality diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals. A diet that's high in protein can also help to reduce shedding.

4. Provide Your Dog with Plenty of Water

Make sure that your dog has access to plenty of water. Dehydration can cause your dog's skin to become dry and flaky, which can lead to more shedding.

5. Keep Your Dog's Environment Clean

Keeping your dog's environment clean can also help to reduce shedding. Vacuum your floors and furniture regularly, and wash your dog's bedding on a weekly basis.

Step by Step

Step 1: Brush your dog regularly

Use a slicker brush or a rubber brush to remove loose hair and dirt from your dog's coat. Make sure that you brush your dog's entire body, including their legs and belly.

Step 2: Bathe your dog

Bathe your dog on a regular basis using a shampoo that's designed for dogs. Rinse your dog thoroughly to remove all of the shampoo.

Step 3: Feed your dog a healthy diet

Make sure that you're feeding your dog a high-quality diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals.

Step 4: Provide your dog with plenty of water

Make sure that your dog has access to plenty of water to prevent dehydration.

Step 5: Keep your dog's environment clean

Regularly vacuum your floors and furniture, and wash your dog's bedding on a weekly basis.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help reduce shedding:

  • Use a shedding blade to remove excess hair from your dog's coat
  • Give your dog a fish oil supplement to promote healthy skin and coat
  • Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals on your dog's coat

FAQ

How often should I brush my dog?

You should brush your dog at least once a week, but daily brushing is ideal for short haired dogs.

What kind of brush should I use?

For short haired dogs, a slicker brush or a rubber brush is ideal.

How often should I bathe my dog?

You should bathe your dog every 4-6 weeks, or as needed.

Hair Care

Taking care of your dog's hair is important for reducing shedding. Make sure that you're using a brush that's appropriate for your dog's coat type, and that you're using a high-quality shampoo and conditioner. You should also avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals, as these can dry out your dog's skin and cause more shedding.

By following these tips, you can help to reduce shedding in your short haired dog. Remember to be patient, as it may take some time to see results. With a little bit of effort, you can keep your home clean and your dog happy and healthy.
