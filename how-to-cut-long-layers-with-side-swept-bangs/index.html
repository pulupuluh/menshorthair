How To Cut Long Layers With Side Swept Bangs

If you're looking for a new hairstyle to update your look, long layers with side swept bangs can be a great option. This hairstyle is versatile, easy to style, and looks great on all hair types. In this article, we'll go over how to cut long layers with side swept bangs, as well as hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

How to Cut Long Layers with Side Swept Bangs

Cutting long layers with side swept bangs can be done at home, but it's important to have the right tools and technique to achieve the desired look. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Sectioning clips
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape

Before beginning the haircut, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to wear a haircutting cape or an old shirt to protect your clothing. Here's how to cut long layers with side swept bangs:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Section off the bangs. Using a comb, part your hair down the middle and section off the front section of your hair from ear to ear. This will be your bangs section.
  2. Trim the bangs. Comb your bangs straight down and trim them to your desired length. You can either cut them straight across or at an angle for a softer look.
  3. Create the guide layer. Take a section of hair from the top of the head and comb it straight up. This will be your guide layer for the rest of the haircut.
  4. Cut the guide layer. Using your scissors, cut the guide layer to your desired length. This will determine the length of the rest of your layers.
  5. Create the layers. Take another section of hair from the top of the head, comb it straight up, and line it up with the guide layer. Cut the hair to the same length as the guide layer. Repeat this step for the rest of the hair on the top of the head.
  6. Move to the sides. Take a section of hair from the sides of the head, comb it straight up, and line it up with the guide layer. Cut the hair to the same length as the guide layer. Repeat this step for the rest of the hair on the sides of the head.
  7. Blend the layers. Comb the hair down and check for any unevenness. Use your scissors to blend any uneven areas.
  8. Texturize the ends. Use your scissors to create texture at the ends of the hair for a softer, more natural look.
  9. Blend the bangs. Comb the bangs to the side and blend them into the rest of the layers.
  10. Style as desired. Use a flat iron or curling iron to add waves or straighten the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers with Side Swept Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting long layers with side swept bangs:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much.
  • Use a point-cutting technique to create soft, natural-looking layers.
  • Check your work in a mirror to ensure evenness.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider visiting a professional stylist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long layers with side swept bangs:

Can I cut long layers with side swept bangs on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut long layers with side swept bangs on curly hair. However, it's important to cut the hair when it's dry and in its natural state to avoid any surprises.

How often should I trim my long layers with side swept bangs?

It's recommended to trim your long layers with side swept bangs every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Can I style my long layers with side swept bangs differently?

Yes, you can style your long layers with side swept bangs in a variety of ways. You can wear them straight, wavy, or curled, and experiment with different partings.

Hair Care Tips for Long Layers with Side Swept Bangs

To keep your long layers with side swept bangs looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent breakage and split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful long layers with side swept bangs at home. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
