How To Remove Permanent Red Hair Color

Written by Robby Jul 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your permanent red hair color? Do you want to try a new hair color but don't know how to remove the red color? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll discuss how to remove permanent red hair color and give you some tips on how to take care of your hair after the process.

Description

Permanent red hair color can be difficult to remove, but it's not impossible. There are many methods you can try, but you should be prepared for the process to take time and effort. Removing permanent hair color can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to take care of your hair before and after the process.

How to

There are several methods you can try to remove permanent red hair color, including:

1. Hair Color Remover

Hair color removers are specifically designed to remove hair dye from your hair. They work by breaking down the hair dye molecules and making them easier to wash out. You can buy hair color removers at most drugstores and beauty supply stores.

2. Bleach

Bleaching your hair can be an effective way to remove permanent hair color, but it can also be damaging to your hair. Bleaching strips your hair of its natural oils and can leave it dry and brittle. If you decide to bleach your hair, make sure to use a deep conditioning treatment afterwards to help restore your hair's moisture.

3. Vinegar

Vinegar is a natural way to remove hair color from your hair. Mix equal parts vinegar and water and apply the mixture to your hair. Let it sit for about 15 minutes and then rinse it out.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to remove permanent red hair color using a hair color remover:

Step 1: Choose a hair color remover

Choose a hair color remover that's specifically designed for permanent hair dye. Follow the instructions on the package carefully.

Step 2: Test the product

Before applying the product to your entire head of hair, do a patch test to make sure you're not allergic to the product.

Step 3: Apply the product

Apply the product to your hair according to the instructions on the package. Make sure to wear gloves and apply the product evenly.

Step 4: Rinse and shampoo

After the recommended time, rinse the product out of your hair thoroughly with warm water. Shampoo your hair to remove any remaining product.

Step 5: Condition

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to help restore its moisture. Leave the treatment on for 5-10 minutes and then rinse it out.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you remove permanent red hair color:

  • Be patient. Removing permanent hair color can take time and multiple treatments.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to help remove hair dye from your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair after removing hair color.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to help restore your hair's moisture.

FAQ

Q: Is it possible to remove permanent hair color without damaging my hair?

A: Removing permanent hair color can be damaging to your hair, but there are steps you can take to minimize the damage. Use a deep conditioning treatment after the process and avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.

Q: Can I dye my hair after removing permanent hair color?

A: Yes, but it's important to wait a few weeks before dyeing your hair again to give your hair time to recover.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair after removing permanent hair color is important to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore your hair's moisture.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Removing permanent red hair color can be a long and difficult process, but it's not impossible. With these tips and methods, you can successfully remove the red color and take care of your hair afterwards.
