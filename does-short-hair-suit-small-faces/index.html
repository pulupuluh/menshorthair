Does Short Hair Suit Small Faces

Written by Eveline May 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short hair has been a trend for a while now and it's not surprising why. It's chic, low-maintenance, and perfect for those who want to switch up their look. But for those with small faces, the question remains: does short hair suit us? In this article, we'll explore the answer to that question and provide some tips on how to pull off short hair with a small face.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Short hair can be a great option for those with small faces, but it's important to choose the right style. A good haircut can help balance out your features and make your face appear more proportionate. However, a bad haircut can make your face look even smaller and emphasize certain features that you may not want to highlight.

How To

When choosing a short haircut, consider your face shape and features. A good rule of thumb for small faces is to choose a style that adds volume to the top of the head and creates the illusion of a longer face. Pixie cuts, bobs, and layered styles can all work well for small faces.

1. Pixie Cuts

Pixie cuts are short, cropped haircuts that can be styled in a variety of ways. They're a great choice for small faces because they add volume to the top of the head, which can help balance out the face. Pixie cuts can be worn with bangs or without, and can be styled straight or curly.

2. Bobs

Bobs are another great option for small faces. A bob that hits just above the shoulders can help elongate the face and make it appear more proportionate. Bobs can be styled straight or curly, and can be worn with or without bangs.

3. Layered Styles

Layered styles can also work well for small faces. Layers can add volume and texture to the hair, which can help create the illusion of a longer face. Layered styles can be worn at any length, from short to long.

Step By Step

When getting a short haircut, it's important to communicate with your stylist to ensure that you get the right style for your face. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Research

Before visiting the stylist, research different short haircuts that might work well with your face shape and features. This will give you an idea of what you're looking for and help you communicate your preferences to your stylist.

2. Consultation

During your consultation, discuss your preferences with your stylist and ask for their recommendations. They may suggest a style that you hadn't considered before. Be open to their suggestions, but also be clear about what you want.

3. Maintenance

Short haircuts require more frequent maintenance than longer styles. Be prepared to visit the salon every 4-6 weeks for a trim to keep your style looking fresh.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for pulling off short hair with a small face:

  • Choose a style that adds volume to the top of the head
  • Avoid styles that emphasize the jawline
  • Consider adding layers to create the illusion of a longer face
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair

FAQ

1. Will a pixie cut make my face look smaller?

A pixie cut can actually help balance out your features and make your face appear more proportionate. However, it's important to choose the right style for your face shape and features.

2. Can I wear bangs with a short haircut?

Yes, bangs can be a great addition to a short haircut. However, make sure to choose a style that works well with your face shape and features.

3. How often should I get a trim for my short haircut?

Short haircuts require more frequent maintenance than longer styles. Plan on visiting the salon every 4-6 weeks for a trim to keep your style looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your short haircut looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray

In conclusion, short hair can definitely suit small faces, but it's important to choose the right style. Consider your face shape and features when choosing a haircut, and be sure to communicate your preferences with your stylist. With the right haircut and styling, you can look and feel your best with short hair.
