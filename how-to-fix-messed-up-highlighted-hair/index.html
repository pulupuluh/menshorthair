How To Fix Messed Up Highlighted Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Highlighted hair is a popular hairstyle trend that can add dimension and depth to your hair color. However, sometimes things can go wrong, and your highlighted hair can turn out to be a disaster. Don't worry; we've got you covered! In this article, we will share some tips and tricks on how to fix messed up highlighted hair.

Description

Highlighted hair is a process of adding lighter or brighter colors to your hair. It is done by bleaching or lightening specific sections of your hair, creating a contrast between your natural base color and the highlighted sections. Highlighted hair can look stunning when done correctly, but it can also turn out to be a disaster if not done well.

If you have messed up your highlighted hair, don't worry. There are various ways to fix it, depending on the severity of the damage. In this article, we will discuss the steps you can take to fix your highlighted hair and restore it to its former glory.

How to Fix Messed Up Highlighted Hair

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing your highlighted hair is to assess the damage. Look at your hair and identify the areas that need fixing. Determine the severity of the damage and decide on the best course of action.

Step 2: Seek Professional Help

If you have severely damaged your highlighted hair, it is best to seek professional help. A hairstylist can assess the damage and recommend the best course of action. They can also help you fix the problem without causing further damage to your hair.

Step 3: Use a Color Correcting Shampoo

If your highlighted hair has a brassy or yellow tone, you can use a color-correcting shampoo to fix it. These shampoos contain purple or blue pigments that neutralize the yellow or orange tones in your hair. Use the shampoo once or twice a week to maintain the color.

Step 4: Try a Hair Mask

If your highlighted hair is dry and damaged, you can try a hair mask to restore moisture and shine. Look for a hair mask that contains ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter. Apply the mask once a week and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it off.

Step 5: Add More Highlights

If your highlighted hair looks dull or flat, you can add more highlights to create depth and dimension. However, it is best to leave this step to the professionals to avoid further damage to your hair.

Tips

  • Always use a heat protectant when using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like bleach or ammonia.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to maintain the color and health of your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

FAQ

Can I fix my highlighted hair at home?

If the damage is not severe, you can fix your highlighted hair at home. However, it is best to seek professional help if you are unsure or have severely damaged your hair.

How often should I use a color-correcting shampoo?

You can use a color-correcting shampoo once or twice a week to maintain the color of your highlighted hair.

Can I add more highlights to my damaged hair?

If your hair is severely damaged, it is best to avoid adding more highlights. Seek professional help to fix the damage before adding more color to your hair.

Hair Care

Highlighted hair requires extra care and attention to maintain its color and health. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Apply a hair mask once a week to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant.

Now you know how to fix messed up highlighted hair. Remember to assess the damage, seek professional help if necessary, and take care of your hair to maintain its color and health. Follow these tips and tricks to keep your highlighted hair looking its best.
