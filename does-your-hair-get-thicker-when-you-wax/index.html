Does Your Hair Get Thicker When You Wax

Written by Bardi Mar 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Many people wonder if their hair will get thicker if they wax. There are different opinions on this matter, but the truth is that waxing does not make your hair grow thicker. In this article, we will explain why and give you some tips on how to take care of your hair after waxing.

Table of Contents

  • Why Waxing Doesn't Make Your Hair Thicker
  • How to Wax Properly
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Waxing

Why Waxing Doesn't Make Your Hair Thicker

One of the biggest misconceptions about waxing is that it makes your hair grow thicker. This is not true. When you wax, you remove the hair from the root. This means that the hair will take longer to grow back, but it will not grow back thicker. In fact, waxing can actually make your hair grow back thinner over time. When you wax regularly, the hair follicles become weaker and produce thinner hair.

Another reason why people think that waxing makes their hair thicker is that the hair appears thicker when it starts to grow back. This is because the hair is growing back from the root and has not been exposed to the elements. However, after a few days, the hair will become thinner again.

How to Wax Properly

Waxing is a great way to remove unwanted hair from your body. However, it is important to do it properly to avoid any complications. Here are some tips on how to wax properly:

  • Choose the right type of wax for your skin type.
  • Make sure that your skin is clean and dry before waxing.
  • Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth.
  • Remove the wax in the opposite direction of hair growth.
  • Hold your skin taut while waxing to reduce pain.
  • Apply a soothing lotion or cream after waxing.

Step-by-Step Guide to Waxing

If you are new to waxing, here is a step-by-step guide to help you:

  1. Cleanse your skin with soap and water.
  2. Dry the area thoroughly.
  3. Apply a thin layer of wax in the direction of hair growth.
  4. Place a strip of cloth or paper over the wax and press down firmly.
  5. Hold your skin taut with one hand and use the other hand to pull off the strip in the opposite direction of hair growth.
  6. Repeat until all the unwanted hair is removed.
  7. Apply a soothing lotion or cream to the waxed area.

Tips for After Waxing

After waxing, it is important to take care of your skin to prevent any complications. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Avoid hot showers or baths for at least 24 hours.
  • Avoid tight clothing that can irritate the skin.
  • Avoid sun exposure for at least 24 hours.
  • Avoid using any harsh soaps or scrubs on the waxed area.
  • Apply a soothing lotion or cream to the waxed area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about waxing:

Does waxing hurt?

Waxing can be uncomfortable, but the pain is usually tolerable. You can take over-the-counter pain medication before waxing to reduce pain.

How often should I wax?

It depends on how fast your hair grows. Generally, you should wax every 4-6 weeks.

Can I wax at home?

Yes, you can wax at home, but it is important to follow the instructions carefully to avoid any complications.

Hair Care After Waxing

After waxing, it is important to take care of your hair to prevent any damage. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Avoid using any harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair for at least 24 hours.
  • Avoid tying your hair up tightly.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy.

In conclusion, waxing does not make your hair grow thicker. In fact, it can make your hair grow back thinner over time. It is important to take care of your skin after waxing to prevent any complications. Follow the tips in this article to ensure a successful waxing experience.
