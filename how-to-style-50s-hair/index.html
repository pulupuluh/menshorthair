How To Style 50S Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 10, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to achieve that classic 50s hairstyle? Whether you’re going for a Marilyn Monroe-inspired look or a more subdued style, the 50s era is known for its iconic hairdos. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps to achieve a classic 50s hairstyle.

Description

The 50s era is known for its glamorous and feminine hairstyles. From voluminous curls to sleek updos, there are a variety of looks to choose from. To achieve a classic 50s hairstyle, you’ll need some basic hair tools such as a curling iron, hairspray, and bobby pins.

How to Style 50s Hair

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin styling, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Apply a small amount of heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from heat styling tools.

Step 2: Create a deep side part

To achieve a classic 50s look, create a deep side part on whichever side you prefer. Use a comb to create a clean, defined part.

Step 3: Curl your hair

Using a curling iron, curl small sections of your hair away from your face. For a more voluminous look, use a larger curling iron barrel. Once you’ve curled all sections of your hair, use your fingers to loosen the curls and create a more natural-looking wave.

Step 4: Pin back one side of your hair

Using bobby pins, pin back one side of your hair. This will create a classic, vintage look that was popular in the 50s era. Make sure to leave some hair loose in the front for a more natural look.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

To ensure your 50s hairstyle stays in place, finish with a generous amount of hairspray. This will help hold your curls and keep your hair looking voluminous all day long.

Tips for Styling 50s Hair

Tip 1: Use hot rollers for a more voluminous look

If you’re looking for a more voluminous 50s hairstyle, consider using hot rollers instead of a curling iron. Hot rollers will help create more defined curls and volume, giving you that classic 50s look.

Tip 2: Don’t be afraid to tease your hair

Teasing your hair can help create more volume and texture, which is perfect for achieving a 50s hairstyle. Use a teasing comb to gently backcomb the roots of your hair, then smooth over the top layer for a more polished finish.

Tip 3: Experiment with different hair accessories

The 50s era was known for its hair accessories, so don’t be afraid to add some to your 50s hairstyle. From headbands to hair scarves, there are plenty of options to choose from.

FAQ

What products do I need to style 50s hair?

To style 50s hair, you’ll need a curling iron, hairspray, bobby pins, and a comb. You may also want to use hot rollers for a more voluminous look.

What if I have short hair?

If you have short hair, you can still achieve a 50s-inspired hairstyle. Consider using a smaller curling iron or hot rollers to create defined curls, and use bobby pins to pin back any sections of hair that are too short to curl.

How can I make my 50s hairstyle last all day?

To make your 50s hairstyle last all day, finish with a generous amount of hairspray. You may also want to consider using a hair primer or setting spray to help hold your curls in place.

Hair Care for 50s Hairstyles

To keep your hair healthy while styling 50s hairstyles, it’s important to use heat protectant spray before using hot tools. You may also want to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and moisturized. Avoid using too much hairspray or other styling products, as this can lead to buildup and damage over time.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to achieve a classic 50s hairstyle in no time. Whether you’re going for a retro-inspired look or just want to switch up your hairstyle, the 50s era is a great source of inspiration.
