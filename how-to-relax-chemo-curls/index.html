How To Relax Chemo Curls A Complete Guide

Written by William Jun 13, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're struggling with chemo curls, you're not alone. Many cancer patients experience this side effect, which can be frustrating and difficult to manage. However, there are ways to relax chemo curls and achieve the look you want. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide to relaxing chemo curls, along with tips and advice for caring for your hair.

Table of Contents:

  • What are Chemo Curls?
  • How to Relax Chemo Curls
  • Step-by-Step Guide

What are Chemo Curls?

Chemo curls are a side effect of chemotherapy. When hair grows back after treatment, it can come in curly or wavy, even if it was straight before. This is because chemotherapy can damage the hair follicles, causing them to produce different types of hair than before. While some people love their chemo curls, others find them difficult to manage or don't like how they look.

How to Relax Chemo Curls

If you're looking to relax your chemo curls, there are a few options to consider:

  • Chemical relaxers
  • Flat ironing
  • Blow drying

Each of these methods has its pros and cons, so it's important to choose the one that works best for your hair type and personal preferences.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to relaxing chemo curls with a chemical relaxer:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or product residues.
  2. Apply a base cream to your scalp to protect it from the relaxer.
  3. Section your hair into four parts and apply the relaxer to each section, starting at the back.
  4. Leave the relaxer on for the recommended time (usually 10-20 minutes).
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  6. Apply a neutralizing shampoo to stop the relaxing process.
  7. Deep condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent damage.
  8. Style your hair as desired.

Note: Chemical relaxers can be damaging to hair and should be used with caution. Consider consulting a professional stylist for assistance.

Tips for Caring for Relaxed Hair

Once you've relaxed your chemo curls, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs About Relaxing Chemo Curls

Is it safe to relax chemo curls?

Chemical relaxers can be damaging to hair, so it's important to use them with caution. Consider consulting a professional stylist for assistance and to ensure that your hair is strong enough for the process.

How long does a chemical relaxer last?

Chemical relaxers typically last for 6-8 weeks, depending on your hair type and how quickly it grows.

Can I relax my chemo curls at home?

While it's possible to relax your chemo curls at home, it's important to do so safely and with caution. Consider consulting a professional stylist for assistance.

Relaxing chemo curls can be a great way to achieve the look you want and feel more confident in your appearance. By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can keep it healthy and looking its best. If you have any questions or concerns about relaxing your chemo curls, consider consulting a professional stylist or your healthcare provider.
