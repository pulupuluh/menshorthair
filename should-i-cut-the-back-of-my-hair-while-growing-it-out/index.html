Should I Cut The Back Of My Hair While Growing It Out

Written by Petter Jun 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When you decide to grow out your hair, it can be a challenging task. However, one of the most common questions that arise when growing out hair is whether or not you should cut the back of your hair. The answer to this question is not always clear cut, and it depends on various factors. In this article, we will explore the different reasons why you may or may not want to cut the back of your hair while growing it out.

Table of Content:

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

The decision to cut the back of your hair while growing it out depends on the style you are aiming for. If you want your hair to be all one length or a blunt cut, then it is best not to cut the back of your hair. However, if you want a layered look, then cutting the back of your hair can help you achieve this effect. It is important to keep in mind that cutting the back of your hair may slow down the growth process, but it can also improve the overall appearance of your hair.

How to

If you decide to cut the back of your hair while growing it out, it is essential to do it properly. The following steps will guide you on how to cut the back of your hair:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair into three parts, the top, middle, and bottom.
  2. Using a comb, comb through the hair in the back section to ensure that it is tangle-free.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Using a pair of hair-cutting scissors, cut the hair to the desired length.
  5. Repeat the process until you have cut all the hair in the back section.
  6. Blend the hair in the back with the hair in the middle and top sections.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions to help you grow out your hair while keeping it healthy:

  1. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  3. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  4. Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  5. Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair health.
  6. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you grow out your hair:

  • Be patient. Hair growth takes time, and it can be frustrating waiting for your hair to grow.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to keep your hair looking fresh and stylish as it grows.
  • Consider taking hair growth supplements to help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing out hair:

Q: Will cutting the back of my hair make it grow faster?

A: No, cutting the back of your hair will not make it grow faster. Hair growth is determined by genetics and various other factors, but cutting your hair can help keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: How long does it take to grow out hair?

A: The time it takes to grow out hair varies from person to person. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential when growing out your hair. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair health.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, whether you should cut the back of your hair while growing it out depends on the style you want to achieve. If you want a layered look, then cutting the back of your hair can help you achieve this effect. However, if you want your hair to be all one length or a blunt cut, then it is best not to cut the back of your hair. Remember to take care of your hair properly by washing it regularly, avoiding heat styling tools, protecting it from the sun and wind, eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and getting regular trims.
