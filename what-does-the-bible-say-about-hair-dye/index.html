What Does The Bible Say About Hair Dye

Written by Aprilia Jul 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read
What Does The Bible Say About Hair Dye | Hair Care Tips

Hair dye is a popular beauty practice that many people enjoy. But, what does the Bible say about it? As a believer, it's important to understand what the Bible says about our appearance and whether or not hair dye is acceptable. In this article, we'll explore what the Bible says about hair dye and provide some tips and guidance for those who are considering using it.

What Does The Bible Say About Appearance?

The Bible doesn't provide specific instructions on how we should look or what we should wear. However, it does mention our appearance in a few verses. One of the most well-known verses is 1 Samuel 16:7 which says, "The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart." This verse reminds us that God cares more about our heart and character than our outward appearance.

Is Hair Dye Acceptable?

The Bible doesn't specifically mention hair dye, so it's up to personal interpretation. However, there are a few verses that mention natural hair color. In 1 Corinthians 11:14-15, it says, "Does not the very nature of things teach you that if a man has long hair, it is a disgrace to him, but that if a woman has long hair, it is her glory? For long hair is given to her as a covering." This verse suggests that we should embrace our natural hair color and not try to change it.

How To Use Hair Dye In A Godly Way?

If you choose to dye your hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, consider why you want to dye your hair. Is it to fit in with society's standards or to feel more confident in yourself? If it's the latter, then there's nothing wrong with that. Second, choose a hair dye that is safe and doesn't damage your hair or scalp. Finally, remember that your appearance isn't the most important thing. Embrace your natural beauty and focus on cultivating a heart that is pleasing to God.

Step By Step Guide To Dye Hair In A Safe Way?

If you're new to hair dyeing, here's a step-by-step guide to help you do it safely:

  1. Choose a hair dye that is safe and doesn't contain harsh chemicals.
  2. Do a patch test on your skin to make sure you're not allergic to the dye.
  3. Protect your skin by applying petroleum jelly around your hairline and ears.
  4. Follow the instructions on the dye package carefully.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply a conditioner to keep it moisturized.

Tips To Maintain Colored Hair?

If you want to maintain your colored hair, here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair as it can strip the color.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling as it can damage your hair and cause the color to fade.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Is It A Sin To Dye Your Hair?

No, it's not a sin to dye your hair. However, it's important to consider why you want to dye your hair and if it aligns with what God wants for you.

What Hair Colors Are Acceptable According To The Bible?

The Bible doesn't mention specific hair colors that are acceptable or unacceptable. It's up to personal interpretation.

Hair Care

Regardless of whether or not you choose to dye your hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Apply conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling as it can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in nutrients that promote hair health.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Bible doesn't provide specific instructions on whether or not hair dye is acceptable. However, it's important to consider why you want to dye your hair and if it aligns with what God wants for you. If you choose to dye your hair, make sure to use a safe product and take care of your hair to maintain its health. Remember that your appearance isn't the most important thing, and focus on cultivating a heart that is pleasing to God.
