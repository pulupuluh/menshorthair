How To Cut The Perfect Wispy Bangs Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 11, 2023 · 11 min read

Looking for a way to update your hairstyle without drastic changes? Wispy bangs are a perfect choice! They add a touch of softness and playfulness to your look, and they’re easy to maintain. However, cutting wispy bangs isn’t as simple as it seems. You need to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut the perfect wispy bangs at home, without risking a hair disaster. Read on to learn more!

Table of Contents:

  • Part 1: How to Prepare for Cutting Wispy Bangs
  • Part 2: How to Cut Wispy Bangs: Step-by-Step Guide
  • Part 3: Tips and Tricks for Cutting Wispy Bangs
  • Part 4: FAQ About Wispy Bangs
  • Part 5: Hair Care Tips for Wispy Bangs

Part 1: How to Prepare for Cutting Wispy Bangs

Before you start cutting, it’s essential to prepare your hair and tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • A sharp pair of hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A mirror or two (one in front of you, one behind you)
  • Hair clips or elastics to section your hair
  • A spray bottle with water

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start. It’s easier to see the natural fall of your hair when it’s not wet. Also, avoid cutting your hair when you’re in a hurry or stressed. Cutting bangs requires patience and attention to detail, so pick a time when you’re relaxed and focused.

Part 2: How to Cut Wispy Bangs: Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let’s get to the cutting part! Follow these steps to achieve the perfect wispy bangs:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Take a fine-tooth comb and part your hair down the middle. Then, section the front part of your hair, which will become your bangs. Clip the rest of your hair away, so it doesn’t get in the way.

Step 2: Determine Your Bangs’ Length

Decide how long you want your bangs to be. Remember that wispy bangs should be longer on the sides and shorter in the middle. To find the perfect length, hold your hair in front of your face and measure where you want your bangs to end. Then, add an extra inch or so for mistakes.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Section

Take the middle section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and cut the hair at the desired length. Don’t cut straight across, as it will result in blunt edges. Instead, point cut the hair by snipping into the ends at a slight angle. This technique creates natural-looking, wispy ends.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Now, it’s time to cut the sides of your bangs. Comb the hair on one side of your middle section and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at the same length as the middle section, but angle your scissors slightly towards the middle. This technique will create layers and texture. Repeat the same process on the other side.

Step 5: Check the Length

Release your hair and check the length of your bangs. If they’re too long, you can trim them a bit more. If they’re too short, you’ll need to wait for them to grow out. Don’t panic if they’re not perfect – remember that wispy bangs are supposed to be imperfect and natural-looking.

Step 6: Add Texture

If your bangs look too dense or heavy, you can add some texture by point cutting them. Hold your scissors vertically and snip into the ends of your bangs, creating small notches. This technique thins out the hair and creates a wispy effect.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Now that you’ve cut your bangs, it’s time to style them. You can blow-dry them with a round brush to add volume or air-dry them for a natural look. You can also use a straightening iron or a curling wand to create different looks. Experiment with different styles until you find the one that suits you the best.

Part 3: Tips and Tricks for Cutting Wispy Bangs

Cutting wispy bangs requires some practice and patience. Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect look:

  • Start with small snips – you can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back once it’s gone.
  • Don’t cut your bangs when they’re wet – your hair will shrink when it dries, and you might end up with shorter bangs than you intended.
  • Don’t cut your bangs straight across – it creates a harsh line and looks unnatural.
  • Point cut your bangs to create texture and softness.
  • Use a razor comb to feather the ends of your bangs.
  • Don’t use regular scissors – they can damage your hair and create split ends.

Part 4: FAQ About Wispy Bangs

Here are some common questions about wispy bangs:

What face shapes suit wispy bangs?

Wispy bangs suit almost every face shape, but they’re especially flattering for round and heart-shaped faces. They soften the angles and create a more feminine look.

Can I cut wispy bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can! However, make sure to cut your bangs when your hair is dry and in its natural state. Curly hair tends to bounce up when it’s dry, so if you cut it when it’s wet, you might end up with shorter bangs than you intended.

How often do I need to trim my wispy bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs get too long. However, as a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and neat.

Part 5: Hair Care Tips for Wispy Bangs

Once you’ve cut your wispy bangs, you need to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your bangs frequently – they tend to get oily faster than the rest of your hair.
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to add volume and bounce.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products – they can weigh down your bangs and make them look greasy.
  • Don’t touch your bangs too much – the oils from your fingers can make them dirty and flat.
  • If you have bangs and wear glasses, make sure they’re not too long – they can get caught in your frames and cause discomfort.

Cutting wispy bangs is a fun and easy way to update your hairstyle. With this guide, you’ll be able to cut your bangs at home like a pro. Remember to take your time, follow the steps carefully, and have fun!
