How To Cut Medium Length Layered Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle and want to save some money, cutting your own hair at home can be a great option. However, cutting medium length layered hair can be a bit tricky. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut medium length layered hair at home, along with some tips and tricks to make it easier.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Cut Medium Length Layered Hair at Home
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips and Tricks
  5. FAQ

Description

Medium length layered hair is a popular hairstyle that can give you a chic and stylish look. Layers can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic and interesting. However, getting a haircut at a salon can be expensive and time-consuming, especially if you want to maintain your layers regularly. By learning how to cut your own hair at home, you can save money and time, while still getting the look you want.

How to Cut Medium Length Layered Hair at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need some basic tools. Here are the things you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle with water

Once you have all the necessary tools, you can start cutting your hair. Here are the general steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, left, right, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.
  3. Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and trim it to the desired length. Use this section as a guide for the rest of the hair in the back section. Cut the hair in a straight line, or at an angle if you want a more layered look.
  4. Move to the left section and repeat the same process. Use the hair in the back section as a guide.
  5. Repeat the same process on the right section.
  6. Finally, work on the top section. Take a small section of hair from the front and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair and moving towards the ends. This will create layers that frame your face.
  7. Check your hair in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments. Use the comb and scissors to even out any uneven parts.
  8. Once you're satisfied with the length and layers, wash and style your hair as usual.

Tips and Tricks

Cutting your own hair can be a bit intimidating, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Here are some tips and tricks to make the process easier:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to cut more hair later than to fix a mistake.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can look longer than it actually is, and you may end up cutting too much.
  • Use a mirror to check your hair from different angles. Make sure the layers are even on both sides.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so don't rush it.
  • If you're not confident in your skills, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Hair scissors are designed to cut hair smoothly and accurately, while regular scissors can damage the hair and create uneven lines.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim a bit more hair to fix the mistake. If it's a major mistake, you can always visit a salon to have it fixed.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy and beautiful hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. If you do use them, apply a heat protectant spray first.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a balanced diet and drink plenty of water to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

By following these tips, you can maintain your medium length layered hair and keep it looking great between haircuts.

With this step-by-step guide, cutting your own medium length layered hair at home is easier than you think. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the tips and tricks we've provided. Good luck!
