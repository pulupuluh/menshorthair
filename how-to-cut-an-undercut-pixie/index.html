How To Cut An Undercut Pixie A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and edgy haircut, an undercut pixie might be the perfect choice for you. This style is all about shaved sides and back, with longer hair on top. It's a great way to show off your personality and individuality, and it's perfect for those who want a low-maintenance haircut. In this article, we'll show you how to cut an undercut pixie in relaxed English language, step-by-step.

Description: What is an Undercut Pixie?

An undercut pixie is a type of haircut that features short hair on the sides and back, with longer hair on top. The sides and back are usually shaved or cut very short, while the top is left longer and can be styled in a variety of ways. This style is popular among women who want a bold and edgy look, and it's also a great choice for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle.

How to Cut an Undercut Pixie: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather your tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, a comb, hairdressing scissors, and hair clips.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, back, and nape. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Sides and Back

Using your clippers, start cutting the sides and back of your hair. Use a shorter guard to create a close shave, or leave a bit more length if you prefer. Be sure to blend the sides and back into the top for a seamless look.

Step 4: Cut the Nape

Next, use your clippers to cut the hair at the nape of your neck. This area should be cut short, but not shaved.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Using your scissors, start cutting the hair on the top of your head. This area should be left longer than the sides and back, so be sure to cut it to the desired length. You can also add layers or texture to the top for a more dynamic look.

Step 6: Blend the Top and Sides

Use your scissors to blend the top and sides of your hair. This will create a seamless transition between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 7: Style as Desired

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. You can add texture, volume, or sleekness depending on your personal preference.

Tips for Cutting an Undercut Pixie

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting an undercut pixie:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter. You can always cut more hair, but you can't add it back once it's gone.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting hair can be tricky, so don't rush the process.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting skills, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQs About Undercut Pixie Haircuts

1. Who is an undercut pixie haircut suitable for?

An undercut pixie is suitable for anyone who wants a bold and edgy look. It's a great choice for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle, and it's perfect for those with thick hair.

2. How often do I need to cut my undercut pixie?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Generally, you'll need to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style.

3. Can I color my undercut pixie?

Yes, you can color your undercut pixie. However, keep in mind that the shaved sides and back will show the color more prominently than the longer hair on top.

Hair Care for Undercut Pixie Haircuts

To keep your undercut pixie looking its best, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too dry or greasy.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.
  • Consider using a styling product such as wax or pomade to add texture and hold to your hair.

Now that you know how to cut an undercut pixie, it's time to try it out for yourself! Remember to follow these steps carefully, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With a little practice, you'll be rocking a bold and edgy hairstyle that's sure to turn heads.
