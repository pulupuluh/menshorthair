Haircuts For Thin Hair Over 60 Tips And Styles To Consider

Written by Daniel Apr 10, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you struggling with thin hair over 60? Don’t worry, you’re not alone. Many women face the same issue as they age. But that doesn’t mean you can’t have stylish haircuts that suit your hair type and age. In this article, we’ll discuss some of the best haircuts for thin hair over 60, along with tips on how to maintain and care for your hair.

How to Choose the Right Haircut for Thin Hair over 60

Choosing the right haircut is crucial when you have thin hair, as it can make a huge difference in appearance. Here are some tips to keep in mind when selecting a haircut:

1. Consider Your Face Shape

Your face shape should be the first thing you consider when choosing a haircut. For instance, if you have a round face, you may want to opt for a layered bob that adds volume to the crown area. On the other hand, if you have an oval face, you can go for a short pixie cut that adds texture and dimension to your hair.

2. Go for Shorter Haircuts

Shorter haircuts are ideal for thin hair as they make your hair look fuller and voluminous. A pixie cut, a bob or a layered haircut are some of the best options for thin hair over 60.

3. Add Layers

Adding layers to your haircut can add volume and texture to your thin hair. However, make sure the layers are not too short as they can make your hair look even thinner.

4. Avoid Heavy Bangs

Heavy bangs can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Instead, opt for a side-swept bang that adds volume and frames your face.

Step by Step Guide to Haircuts for Thin Hair over 60

Now that you know what to consider when choosing a haircut, here’s a step-by-step guide on how to get a haircut for thin hair over 60:

1. Consult with Your Stylist

Before getting a haircut, it’s important to consult with your stylist. They can recommend the best haircut for your hair type and face shape. They can also give you tips on how to style and maintain your hair.

2. Wash Your Hair

Make sure to wash your hair before getting a haircut. This will give your stylist a clear view of your hair and make it easier to cut.

3. Section Your Hair

Your stylist will likely section your hair before cutting it. This helps them to create an even cut and avoid any mistakes.

4. Cut Your Hair

Your stylist will cut your hair according to the selected haircut. They may add layers, texture or volume to your hair to make it look fuller.

5. Style Your Hair

Once your haircut is done, your stylist will style your hair according to your preference. They may use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to create the desired look.

Tips for Maintaining and Caring for Thin Hair over 60

Here are some tips to keep in mind to maintain and care for your thin hair over 60:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

A volumizing shampoo and conditioner can add volume and texture to your thin hair. Look for products that contain protein and collagen, as they can strengthen your hair and make it look fuller.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair as they can damage your hair and make it look thin and brittle. If you must use heat styling, make sure to use a heat protectant spray.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look thin and unhealthy.

4. Consider Hair Extensions

If you want to add extra volume and length to your hair, consider getting hair extensions. They can be a great way to add fullness and volume to thin hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I dye my thin hair?

Yes, you can dye your thin hair. However, make sure to use a hair dye that is gentle and doesn’t damage your hair. Also, avoid dyeing your hair too often as it can make your hair look thin and unhealthy.

2. Can I get a perm on thin hair over 60?

Yes, you can get a perm on thin hair over 60. However, make sure to consult with your stylist before getting a perm as it can damage your hair if not done properly.

3. How often should I wash my thin hair?

You can wash your thin hair every other day or every two days. Washing your hair too often can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look thin and dry.

Conclusion

Having thin hair over 60 doesn’t mean you can’t have stylish haircuts. With the right haircut, products and care, you can make your hair look fuller and voluminous. Make sure to consult with your stylist, use the right products, and avoid damaging your hair with heat styling or harsh chemicals. Follow these tips and you’ll have beautiful hair that enhances your natural beauty.
