Which Hair Is Best For Butterfly Locs

Written by Christine Jun 22, 2023 繚 7 min read

Butterfly locs are a popular hairstyle that has been gaining popularity in recent years. This style is characterized by its loose, wavy texture and unique butterfly-shaped parting. One of the most important aspects of achieving this hairstyle is choosing the right type of hair. In this blog post, we will discuss which hair is best for butterfly locs.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Achieve Butterfly Locs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Butterfly Locs
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Butterfly Locs

Description

Butterfly locs are a protective hairstyle that can be achieved using a variety of different types of hair. The style involves parting the hair in a butterfly shape and then wrapping the hair around itself to create a wavy, textured look. The hair used for butterfly locs can be synthetic or human hair, but it is important to choose high-quality hair that will not easily tangle or shed.

How to Achieve Butterfly Locs

Before discussing which hair is best for butterfly locs, it is important to understand the basic process for achieving this hairstyle. Here is a quick overview:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair.
  2. Section your hair into small, butterfly-shaped parts.
  3. Wrap each section of hair around itself to create a twisted, loc-like effect.
  4. Continue this process until all of your hair is twisted into locs.
  5. Style your locs as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide for achieving butterfly locs:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt or buildup from your hair and prepare it for styling.
  2. Section your hair into small, butterfly-shaped parts. Use a comb to create a part down the center of your head, and then create two more diagonal parts on either side of the center part. This should create a butterfly-shaped parting.
  3. Take a small section of hair from one of the butterfly-shaped parts and begin twisting it around itself. You can wrap the hair tightly for a more uniform look, or more loosely for a more textured, wavy look.
  4. Continue twisting the hair until you reach the end, and then secure it with a hair tie or rubber band.
  5. Repeat this process for all of the sections of hair, creating twisted locs all over your head.
  6. Style your locs as desired. You can leave them loose and wavy, or you can pull them back into a ponytail or bun.

Tips for Maintaining Butterfly Locs

Once you have achieved butterfly locs, it is important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heavy products, as they can weigh down your locs and make them look limp. Instead, opt for lightweight oils or serums to add shine and moisture.
  • Wrap your locs at night to prevent them from tangling or becoming frizzy.
  • Be gentle when washing your locs, as rough handling can cause them to unravel or become damaged.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your locs, as this can cause them to dry out and become brittle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about butterfly locs:

What type of hair is best for butterfly locs?

Synthetic hair is often used for butterfly locs, as it is affordable and easy to find. However, human hair can also be used for a more natural look and feel.

How long do butterfly locs last?

Butterfly locs can last anywhere from 6-8 weeks, depending on how well they are maintained.

Can I do butterfly locs on my own?

Yes, butterfly locs can be done at home with the right tools and techniques. However, it may be easier to have a professional stylist do them for you.

Hair Care for Butterfly Locs

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful butterfly locs. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your locs every 1-2 weeks, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy products, as they can weigh down your locs and make them look limp.
  • Wrap your locs at night to prevent them from tangling or becoming frizzy.
  • Be gentle when washing your locs, as rough handling can cause them to unravel or become damaged.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your locs, as this can cause them to dry out and become brittle.

By following these tips and choosing the right type of hair, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting butterfly locs that will turn heads and make you feel confident and stylish.
