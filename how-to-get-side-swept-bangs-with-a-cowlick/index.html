How To Get Side Swept Bangs With A Cowlick A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 02, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have a cowlick, you know how frustrating it can be to style your hair, especially when it comes to bangs. But fear not, because in this article, we’ll show you how to get side swept bangs even with a pesky cowlick.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Side Swept Bangs with a Cowlick
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Bangs with a Cowlick
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Side Swept Bangs

How to Get Side Swept Bangs with a Cowlick

The first step to getting side swept bangs with a cowlick is choosing the right length for your face shape. Generally, longer bangs work better for those with cowlicks, as they have more weight to hold the hair down.

Next, you’ll want to blow dry your bangs in the direction you want them to go. Use a round brush and blow dry your hair in small sections, pulling the brush down and outwards to create a sweeping motion. If your cowlick is particularly stubborn, you may need to use a bit of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add some lift to your roots.


2. While your hair is still damp, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from heat styling tools.


3. Blow dry your hair using a round brush, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Focus on your bangs, pulling the brush down and outwards to create a sweeping motion.


4. If your cowlick is particularly stubborn, use a bit of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.


5. Finish off your look with a spritz of hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Styling Bangs with a Cowlick

- Choose longer bangs to add weight and hold down your cowlick.


- Use a round brush and blow dry your hair in small sections, pulling the brush down and outwards to create a sweeping motion.


- Use a bit of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.


- If your cowlick is particularly stubborn, try blow drying your hair in the opposite direction to your cowlick, then switching back to your desired direction.


- Consider using a straightening iron to smooth out any unruly hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I still get side swept bangs if I have a cowlick?

A: Absolutely! With the right techniques and tools, you can achieve the perfect side swept bangs, even with a cowlick.

Q: How do I choose the right length for my bangs?

A: It’s best to consult with a hairstylist, as they can help you choose the best length for your face shape and hair type.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Side Swept Bangs

- Schedule regular trims to keep your bangs looking fresh.


- Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.


- Avoid touching your bangs too much, as oils from your hands can cause them to become greasy.


- Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.


- Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to achieve the perfect side swept bangs, even with a pesky cowlick. Happy styling!
