How to Make Dreadlocks with Curly Hair

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that involves twisting and knotting the hair to create long, matted strands. If you have curly hair, you can still achieve this look. However, it may take a little more effort and patience due to the texture of your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here are the steps to make dreadlocks with curly hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair to remove any product buildup and oils.
  2. Section your hair. Use a comb to create sections of hair that are about the size of the dreadlocks you want to create.
  3. Apply a locking gel or wax. This will help the hair hold its shape and keep the strands together.
  4. Twist the hair. Take each section and twist it tightly from the root to the end. Use a comb to help keep the twist tight.
  5. Palm roll the hair. Roll the twisted section between your palms to smooth out any bumps and to help the hair lock together.
  6. Repeat. Continue twisting and palm rolling each section until all of your hair has been twisted.
  7. Keep the hair in place. Use rubber bands or clips to secure the ends of each section. This will help the hair stay twisted and prevent unraveling.
  8. Let the hair dry. Allow the hair to air dry completely. This can take several hours or overnight.
  9. Remove the rubber bands or clips. Once the hair is dry, remove the rubber bands or clips from the ends of each section.
  10. Separate the sections. Use your fingers or a comb to separate the sections into individual dreadlocks.

Tips for Making Dreadlocks with Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when making dreadlocks with curly hair:

  • Use a locking gel or wax specifically designed for curly hair. This will help the hair hold its shape and prevent frizz.
  • Be patient. It may take longer to create dreadlocks with curly hair due to the texture of the hair.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase. This will help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid using a hairbrush. Brushing curly hair can cause frizz and breakage, which can make it more difficult to create dreadlocks.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making dreadlocks with curly hair:

Q: Can I make dreadlocks with short curly hair?

A: Yes, you can make dreadlocks with short curly hair. However, the length of your hair will determine the size of the dreadlocks you can create.

Q: How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

A: It is recommended to wash your dreadlocks once every 1-2 weeks. Over-washing can cause the hair to become dry and brittle.

Q: How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

A: It can take several months for dreadlocks to fully form, depending on the texture and length of your hair. However, you will start to notice the hair locking together within the first few weeks.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Maintaining dreadlocks requires a different hair care routine than other hairstyles. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products that can cause buildup and prevent the hair from locking together.
  • Use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to dry your hair. Avoid air drying, as this can cause mildew and odors.
  • Retwist your dreadlocks as needed to maintain their shape and prevent unraveling.
  • Protect your hair when swimming by wearing a swim cap or tying your hair up in a bun.

Follow these tips and your dreadlocks will look great for years to come.
