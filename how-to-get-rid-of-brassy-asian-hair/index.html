How To Get Rid Of Brassy Asian Hair A Complete Guide

Written by William Sep 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of having brassy hair that just won't go away? If you're an Asian woman with relaxed hair, getting rid of brassy tones can be a challenge. But fear not, because in this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy Asian hair. We'll cover everything from how to identify brassy tones to the best products and techniques to use. Keep reading to discover our top tips!

Description: What is Brassy Asian Hair?

Brassy hair is a common problem among Asian women with relaxed hair. It's a term used to describe hair that has yellow or orange tones instead of the desired cool or neutral tones. Brassy hair is caused by several factors, including exposure to sunlight, chlorine, and hard water. It can also be a result of using the wrong hair products or not using enough toner to neutralize yellow or orange pigments.

How to Identify Brassy Tones

Before you can get rid of brassy Asian hair, you need to identify the brassy tones in your hair. The easiest way to do this is by looking at your hair in natural light. If you notice that your hair has a yellow or orange cast, then it's brassy. Another way to check is by using a white towel or cloth. Rub the towel against your hair, and if it turns yellow or orange, then your hair is brassy.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Get Rid of Brassy Asian Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Shampoo

The first step to getting rid of brassy Asian hair is to choose the right shampoo. Look for a shampoo that is specifically designed for color-treated hair and contains purple or blue pigments. These pigments help to neutralize yellow or orange tones in your hair. We recommend using the Fanola No Yellow Shampoo or the Joico Color Balance Purple Shampoo.

Step 2: Use a Toner

After shampooing, use a toner to further neutralize brassy tones. Toner is a semi-permanent hair color that helps to correct unwanted tones in your hair. Apply the toner to your hair, following the instructions on the packet. We recommend using the Wella Color Charm Toner in T18 or T14 shades.

Step 3: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair is essential to maintaining healthy hair and preventing breakage. After using the toner, apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 20 minutes. We recommend using the Olaplex No. 3 Hair Perfector or the Shea Moisture Manuka Honey & Yogurt Hydrate + Repair Protein Power Treatment.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair from Sunlight and Chlorine

Exposure to sunlight and chlorine can cause your hair to become brassy. Protect your hair by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside, and apply a leave-in conditioner that contains UV filters. When swimming, wear a swim cap, or apply a leave-in conditioner that is specifically designed to protect your hair from chlorine. We recommend using the Klorane Sun Radiance Protective Oil or the Shea Moisture Coconut & Hibiscus Hold & Shine Moisture Mist.

Tips for Maintaining Non-Brassy Hair

Once you've gotten rid of brassy tones in your hair, it's essential to maintain your hair's color. Here are some tips for maintaining non-brassy hair:

  • Use a purple or blue shampoo once a week to neutralize any unwanted tones.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to too much sunlight or chlorine.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and strong.
  • Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools.

FAQs: Frequently Asked Questions About Brassy Asian Hair

Q: Can I dye my hair to get rid of brassy tones?

A: Dyeing your hair is not the best solution for getting rid of brassy tones. Dyeing your hair can cause further damage and potentially worsen the brassy tones in your hair. Instead, use toner and purple or blue shampoo to neutralize unwanted tones.

Q: How often should I use toner?

A: We recommend using toner once a month or as needed. Overuse of toner can lead to dry and brittle hair.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple or blue shampoo?

A: Regular shampoo is not effective in neutralizing brassy tones. Purple or blue shampoo contains pigments that help to neutralize unwanted tones in your hair.

Hair Care: Final Thoughts

Brassy hair can be frustrating, but it's not impossible to get rid of. By following our step-by-step guide and tips, you'll be well on your way to maintaining non-brassy hair. Remember to choose the right products for your hair, protect your hair from sunlight and chlorine, and deep condition your hair regularly. With a little bit of effort and patience, you can say goodbye to brassy Asian hair once and for all!
