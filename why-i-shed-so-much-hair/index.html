Why Do I Shed So Much Hair Understanding The Causes And Solutions

Written by Petter Feb 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you worried about the amount of hair you're shedding every day? You're not alone. Many people experience hair loss or excessive shedding at some point in their lives. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why you might be shedding so much hair and provide you with some tips on how to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prevent Hair Shedding
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Reducing Hair Shedding

Causes of Hair Shedding

There are many reasons why you might be shedding more hair than normal. Some of the most common causes include:

  • Genetics: Hair loss can be hereditary, so if your parents or grandparents experienced hair loss, you may be more likely to as well.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes, such as those that occur during pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid imbalances, can cause hair shedding.
  • Nutritional deficiencies: A lack of certain nutrients in your diet, such as iron or biotin, can lead to hair loss.
  • Stress: Stressful events, such as a divorce or the death of a loved one, can cause temporary hair loss.
  • Medical conditions: Certain medical conditions, such as alopecia areata or lupus, can cause hair loss.
  • Medications: Some medications, such as chemotherapy drugs or blood thinners, can cause hair loss as a side effect.

How to Prevent Hair Shedding

While you may not be able to prevent all hair shedding, there are some steps you can take to minimize it:

  • Eat a balanced diet: Make sure you're getting enough nutrients in your diet, such as iron, biotin, and protein.
  • Manage stress: Practice stress-reducing activities, such as yoga or meditation, to minimize the impact of stress on your body.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Avoid pulling your hair back tightly into ponytails or braids, as this can cause hair breakage and shedding.
  • Use a gentle shampoo: Use a mild shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling: Limit your use of heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause shedding.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as perms or relaxers, as they can damage your hair and cause shedding.

Step-by-Step Guide to Reducing Hair Shedding

Follow these steps to reduce hair shedding:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo.
  2. Apply a conditioner to your hair and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out.
  3. Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too vigorously.
  4. Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangler to your hair to help prevent breakage and shedding.
  5. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  6. Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly.
  7. Avoid pulling your hair back tightly into ponytails or braids.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can lead to hair breakage and shedding.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun's damaging rays.
  • Don't over-wash your hair: Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage.
  • Get enough sleep: Lack of sleep can lead to stress, which can cause hair shedding.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair shedding:

  • Is hair shedding normal? Yes, it's normal to shed some hair every day. On average, people shed around 50 to 100 hairs per day.
  • When should I be concerned about hair shedding? If you're shedding more hair than usual or notice bald patches on your scalp, you should speak to a doctor.
  • Can stress cause hair shedding? Yes, stress can cause temporary hair shedding.
  • Can hair shedding be reversed? Depending on the cause of your hair shedding, it may be reversible. Speak to a doctor to determine the underlying cause.

Hair Care Products for Reducing Hair Shedding

Here are some hair care products that can help reduce hair shedding:

  • Leave-in conditioner: Leave-in conditioner can help prevent breakage and shedding by providing your hair with extra moisture and protection.
  • Scalp treatment: A scalp treatment can help improve the health of your scalp, which can in turn improve the health of your hair.
  • Biotin supplement: Taking a biotin supplement can help improve the strength and thickness of your hair.
  • Volumizing shampoo: A volumizing shampoo can help give your hair more body and thickness, which can help prevent breakage and shedding.

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can reduce hair shedding and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
