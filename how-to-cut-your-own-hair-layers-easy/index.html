How To Cut Your Own Hair Layers Easy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 20, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking to add some depth and volume to your hair, adding layers can be a great option. But, if you're not looking to spend a fortune at the salon, cutting your own hair layers can be a budget-friendly solution. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own hair layers easily from the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Layers
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair Layers

How to Cut Your Own Hair Layers

Cutting your own hair layers can seem like a daunting task, but with a bit of preparation and patience, it can be done easily. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry completely. This will make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections. Use a comb to create a middle part and then part your hair from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair away from the bottom using hair clips.

Step 3: Start cutting the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers at a 45-degree angle. Use scissors to cut the hair at the angle, starting from the middle of the section and working your way down to the end. Repeat this process for the entire bottom section of your hair.

Step 4: Release the top section of your hair and repeat the process. This time, take a small section of hair from the back and hold it at a 90-degree angle. Again, cut the hair at the angle, starting from the middle of the section and working your way down to the end. Repeat for the entire top section of your hair.

Step 5: Blend the layers. Take a small section of hair from the front and hold it up at a 45-degree angle. Cut the hair at the angle, working your way towards the back of your head. Do this on both sides of your head until the layers blend together.

Step 6: Check for any unevenness and make any necessary adjustments. Once you're happy with the length and shape of your hair, you're done!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Layers

Tip 1: Invest in a good pair of scissors. Using the right tools will make the process easier and help you achieve better results.

Tip 2: Take your time. Cutting your own hair layers can be time-consuming, but rushing through it can lead to mistakes.

Tip 3: Start small. If you're new to cutting your own hair, start with small trims and work your way up to cutting layers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cut my own hair layers without experience?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to follow the steps carefully and take your time.

Q: Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style.

Q: How often should I cut my own hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair Layers

After cutting your own hair layers, it's important to take proper care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.

Tip 2: Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.

Tip 3: Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your hair layers and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these easy steps and tips, you'll be able to achieve great results from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
