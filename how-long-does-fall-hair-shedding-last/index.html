How Long Does Fall Hair Shedding Last

Written by Petter Jun 08, 2023 · 8 min read

As the leaves start to change colors and the weather gets cooler, many people notice an increase in hair shedding. This is known as fall hair shedding, and it is a normal part of the hair growth cycle. In this article, we will discuss how long fall hair shedding lasts and provide tips on how to minimize its impact on your hair.

Why Does Hair Shed More in the Fall?

Many people experience increased hair shedding in the fall, and there are a few reasons why this happens. One of the main reasons is that hair growth is tied to the seasons. During the summer months, our hair grows faster due to increased exposure to sunlight and warmth. However, as the weather gets cooler in the fall, our hair growth slows down, and we start to shed more hair.

Another reason why we shed more hair in the fall is that the hair growth cycle naturally goes through a shedding phase. This is when old hair falls out to make way for new hair growth. This shedding phase tends to happen more frequently in the fall, which is why many people notice an increase in hair shedding during this time.

How Long Does Fall Hair Shedding Last?

Fall hair shedding typically lasts for a few weeks to a few months, depending on the individual. Some people may notice an increase in hair shedding for just a few weeks, while others may experience it for several months. The duration of fall hair shedding can also depend on factors such as genetics, diet, and stress levels.

How to Minimize Fall Hair Shedding

While fall hair shedding is a natural process, there are things you can do to minimize its impact on your hair. Here are a few tips:

Step-by-Step Guide to Minimizing Fall Hair Shedding

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals, as these can damage your hair and lead to increased shedding.


2. Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.


3. Avoid using heat styling tools such as hair dryers and flat irons, as these can damage your hair and lead to increased shedding.


4. Eat a healthy, balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals. This will help to nourish your hair and promote healthy growth.


5. Manage your stress levels through activities such as yoga, meditation, or exercise. High levels of stress can lead to increased hair shedding.

Tips for Preventing Fall Hair Shedding

Here are a few additional tips for preventing fall hair shedding:


- Keep your hair moisturized by using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.


- Avoid wearing tight hairstyles such as braids or ponytails, as these can pull on your hair and lead to increased shedding.


- Protect your hair from the elements by wearing a hat or scarf when you are outside.

FAQs about Fall Hair Shedding

Q: Is fall hair shedding normal?

A: Yes, fall hair shedding is a normal part of the hair growth cycle.


Q: How much hair shedding is normal?

A: It is normal to shed between 50 and 100 hairs per day.


Q: Will fall hair shedding lead to hair loss?

A: No, fall hair shedding is a natural process and should not lead to hair loss unless there is an underlying medical condition.

Hair Care Tips for Fall

As the weather gets cooler, it is important to adjust your hair care routine to keep your hair healthy and strong. Here are a few tips:


- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.


- Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.


- Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools.


- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, fall hair shedding is a normal part of the hair growth cycle, and it typically lasts for a few weeks to a few months. While you can't completely prevent fall hair shedding, you can minimize its impact on your hair by following the tips and guidelines provided in this article. By taking good care of your hair, you can ensure that it stays healthy and strong throughout the fall and beyond.
