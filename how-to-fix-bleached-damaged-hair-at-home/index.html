How To Fix Bleached Damaged Hair At Home Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 29, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of having dry, brittle, and damaged hair after bleaching it? Don't worry! There are ways to fix this problem at home without having to spend a lot of money at the salon. In this article, we will share with you some tips and tricks on how to fix bleached damaged hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Bleached Damaged Hair at Home
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Damage
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Bleaching

Description

Bleaching hair is a common hair coloring technique that can damage hair if not done properly. It involves removing the natural pigment from the hair, which can lead to dryness, breakage, and split ends. If you have bleached your hair and are experiencing these problems, there are ways to repair it at home.

How to Fix Bleached Damaged Hair at Home

Here are some tips on how to fix bleached damaged hair at home:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Mask

A deep conditioning mask can help restore moisture to your hair and repair the damage caused by bleaching. Look for a mask that contains natural ingredients like avocado, coconut oil, or honey.

2. Apply Coconut Oil

Coconut oil is a natural moisturizer that can help repair damaged hair. Apply it to your hair and leave it on for at least an hour before washing it out with shampoo.

3. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for a treatment that contains keratin or amino acids.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can further damage your hair. Avoid using them or use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to fix bleached damaged hair at home:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply a Deep Conditioning Mask

Apply a deep conditioning mask to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

Step 3: Apply Coconut Oil

Apply coconut oil to your hair and leave it on for at least an hour. Cover your hair with a shower cap or towel to keep the oil from dripping.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair with cool water and shampoo it as usual.

Step 5: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended time. Rinse it out with cool water.

Step 6: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools or use them sparingly. Always use a heat protectant spray.

Tips to Prevent Hair Damage

Here are some tips to prevent hair damage:

1. Avoid Over-Processing

Avoid over-processing your hair with chemical treatments. Wait at least six weeks between treatments.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Q: Can I fix bleached damaged hair at home?

A: Yes, you can fix bleached damaged hair at home with the right products and techniques.

Q: How long does it take to fix bleached damaged hair?

A: It can take several weeks or months to fully repair bleached damaged hair.

Q: Can I bleach my hair again after fixing the damage?

A: It's best to wait at least a month before bleaching your hair again to prevent further damage.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of sulfates and harsh chemicals.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools or use them sparingly. Always use a heat protectant spray.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, fixing bleached damaged hair at home is possible with the right products and techniques. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine to see results. Follow these tips and tricks to restore your hair's health and shine.
