Low Fade Haircut Long Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your long hair getting in the way or looking too messy? A low fade haircut might just be the solution you need. This style is perfect for those who want to keep their length but still want a clean and stylish look. In this guide, we will go over everything you need to know about the low fade haircut for long hair, from how to get it to how to take care of it.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Get the Low Fade Haircut for Long Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Maintaining Your Low Fade Haircut
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care Tips for Long Hair

Description

The low fade haircut for long hair is a modern take on the classic fade. It involves trimming the hair on the sides and back of the head gradually shorter, while leaving the hair on top long. This creates a clean and polished look without sacrificing length or volume. The low fade is a versatile style that can be customized to suit your individual preferences, whether you prefer a subtle fade or a more dramatic contrast.

How to Get the Low Fade Haircut for Long Hair

The low fade haircut is best achieved by going to a professional hairstylist who has experience with this style. They will be able to create a fade that is tailored to your hair type and face shape. When getting a low fade, it is important to communicate with your stylist about the length you want to keep on top and the level of fade you desire.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue.

2. Towel dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles.

3. Section off the top of your hair and clip it away to keep it out of the way.

4. Use clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head, starting with a longer guard and gradually moving to a shorter guard to create the fade.

5. Blend the fade into the longer hair on top using scissors or clippers.

6. Style the hair on top as desired using a blow dryer and styling products.

Tips for Maintaining Your Low Fade Haircut

1. Get regular trims to maintain the faded look and prevent split ends.

2. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

3. Use a styling product to keep your hair in place and add texture.

4. Consider using a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.

FAQ

Q: Will the low fade haircut work for all hair types?

A: Yes, the low fade can be customized to suit any hair type, from thin and straight to thick and curly.

Q: How often should I get a trim?

A: It is recommended to get a trim every 4 to 6 weeks to maintain the faded look.

Q: Can I style my hair differently with a low fade?

A: Yes, the beauty of the low fade is that it can be styled in a variety of ways, from a slicked-back look to a messy textured style.

Hair Care Tips for Long Hair

1. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

2. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

4. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

Now that you know everything there is to know about the low fade haircut for long hair, you can confidently go to your stylist and get the look you've been dreaming of. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
