How To Cut Your Own Shaggy Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance haircut that adds volume and texture to your hair, shaggy layers might be the perfect choice for you. Not only are they trendy and stylish, but they can also flatter any face shape and hair texture. However, going to a salon every few weeks can be expensive and time-consuming, especially if you have a busy schedule. Luckily, cutting your own shaggy layers at home is easier than you might think. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own shaggy layers in a relaxed and straightforward way.

Description

Shaggy layers are a type of haircut that involves cutting the hair into different lengths to create a messy, undone look. Shaggy layers are particularly popular among women with thin or fine hair, as they can add volume and make the hair appear thicker. Shaggy layers are also great for those who want a low-maintenance haircut that doesn't require frequent trims.

How to Cut Your Own Shaggy Layers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own shaggy layers is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips or bobby pins to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll need to section off your hair. Start by dividing your hair into two sections, from ear to ear, and clip the top section away. Then, divide the bottom section into two more sections, from the nape of your neck to the top of your ears, and clip them away as well.

Step 3: Cut Your Layers

Now it's time to start cutting your layers. Begin with the bottom section of hair, and take a small section of hair from the back. Hold the hair between two fingers and slide your fingers down to the desired length. Use your scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Repeat this process, working your way up the back of your head.

Once you've finished cutting the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head, and hold it between two fingers. Slide your fingers down to the desired length, and cut the hair in a straight line. Repeat this process, working your way back towards the crown of your head.

Step 4: Blend Your Layers

After you've cut all of your layers, it's time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down, and use your scissors to make small, upward-pointing cuts into the ends of your hair. This will help to blend your layers and create a more natural, shaggy look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting and blending your layers, it's time to style your hair. You can use a hairdryer and a round brush to add volume and shape to your hair. Alternatively, you can let your hair air dry and use a texturizing spray to create a more tousled, undone look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Shaggy Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more if necessary.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as this can cause it to shrink and appear shorter than you intended.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be challenging, but it's worth it if you want to save time and money.

FAQ

Can I cut my own shaggy layers if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Shaggy layers can work well on all hair types, including curly hair. Just be sure to cut your hair when it's dry, and take into account the way your curls will fall.

How often should I trim my shaggy layers?

Shaggy layers are meant to be low-maintenance, so you can go longer between trims than with other haircuts. However, if you notice your hair starting to look too long or shapeless, it's a good idea to trim it every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Shaggy Layers

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Brush your hair regularly to distribute natural oils and prevent tangles.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or using hot tools.

By following these simple steps and tips, you can easily cut your own shaggy layers at home. Not only will you save time and money, but you'll also have a stylish and trendy haircut that flatters your face and hair type. Happy cutting!
