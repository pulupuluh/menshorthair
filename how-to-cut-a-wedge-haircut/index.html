How To Cut A Wedge Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut a wedge haircut! If you're looking for a stylish and easy-to-maintain haircut, the wedge might just be the perfect choice for you. This iconic haircut has been popularized by celebrities like Dorothy Hamill and Victoria Beckham, and it's a great option for both short and medium-length hair.

Description

The wedge haircut is characterized by a short and layered back, with longer layers in the front that gradually taper towards the face. This creates a triangular shape that frames the face and accentuates the cheekbones.

The wedge haircut can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to add more layers and volume to the top of the haircut to elongate your face. If you have thin hair, you can add some texture and dimension with layers and highlights.

How to Cut a Wedge Haircut

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut a wedge haircut:

  1. Start by washing and blow-drying the hair. Comb it out to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Section the hair into three parts: the top, sides, and back. Clip the top section away for now.
  3. Starting at the nape of the neck, use a razor or scissors to cut the hair into a graduated bob. This means cutting the hair shorter in the back and gradually longer towards the front.
  4. Once you've finished cutting the back section, move on to the sides. Cut them at a slight angle, following the shape of the head.
  5. Now it's time to work on the top section. Comb it straight up and cut it at a 45-degree angle, following the shape of the head. This will create the signature wedge shape.
  6. Blend the layers together by using thinning shears or a razor to create texture and movement.
  7. Check the haircut for symmetry and adjust any uneven parts.
  8. Style the hair using a round brush and a blow dryer to add volume and shape. You can also use a flat iron or curling iron to add more texture and dimension.

Tips for Cutting a Wedge Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a wedge haircut:

  • Use sharp scissors or a razor to ensure clean and precise cuts.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small sections and work slowly to avoid mistakes.
  • Take into account the natural texture and movement of the hair when cutting. This will help you create a more natural and effortless look.
  • Always cut the hair when it's dry. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer, which can lead to uneven cuts.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about the wedge haircut:

Q: Is the wedge haircut easy to maintain?

A: Yes, the wedge haircut is relatively easy to maintain. Since it's a shorter haircut, it requires less styling time and product. However, you may need to trim it every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Q: Can the wedge haircut be adapted to different hair types?

A: Yes, the wedge haircut can be adapted to different hair types. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to add more layers and volume to prevent the hair from looking too heavy. If you have straight hair, you can add some texture and movement with layers and highlights.

Hair Care Tips for a Wedge Haircut

Here are some tips to keep your wedge haircut looking great:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent the hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using too much heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Protect the hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

That's it for our guide on how to cut a wedge haircut! We hope you found it helpful and informative. Remember to take your time and work slowly to achieve the best results. Good luck!
