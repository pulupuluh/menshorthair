Would A Lob Look Good On Me Everything You Need To Know

Written by Aprilia Mar 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you considering getting a lob haircut but wondering if it would suit your face shape and hair texture? If so, you're not alone! A lob, or long bob, is a versatile haircut that has been popular for several years now. It's a perfect length that sits between a bob and medium-length hair and can be styled in various ways. In this article, we'll answer all your questions about whether a lob would look good on you, from how to choose the right length to tips for taking care of your new cut.

Description of a Lob Haircut

A lob haircut is a style that falls between a bob and longer hair. It's typically cut to sit just above the shoulders, but there are variations in length. This haircut can be blunt, layered, or textured, and it can be styled straight or wavy. The lob is a low-maintenance haircut that looks chic and stylish, making it a popular choice among many women.

How to Choose the Right Length for Your Lob

The first thing to consider when deciding on a lob haircut is the length that will suit you best. The ideal length will depend on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Here are some tips to help you choose the right length for your lob:

  • If you have a round face, a lob that falls just below the chin will help elongate your face.
  • If you have a square face, a lob that falls just above the shoulders will soften your features.
  • If you have an oval face, you can pull off any length of lob.
  • If you have fine hair, a shorter lob will give your hair more volume and body.
  • If you have thick hair, a longer lob will help weigh down your hair and make it more manageable.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Lob Haircut

If you've decided to go for a lob, here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect cut:

  1. Research different lob styles and lengths to find inspiration.
  2. Book an appointment with a trusted hairstylist who has experience cutting lobs.
  3. Discuss your desired length and style with your hairstylist.
  4. Wash your hair before your appointment and don't use any styling products.
  5. Bring a photo of your desired lob to show your hairstylist.
  6. Trust your hairstylist's advice on the best length and style for your face shape and hair texture.
  7. After your haircut, ask your hairstylist for tips on how to style and maintain your new lob.

Tips for Styling Your Lob Haircut

Once you've got your lob haircut, here are some tips for styling it:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • To add volume and texture, use a sea salt spray on damp hair and scrunch with your hands.
  • To create loose waves, use a curling wand and curl away from your face.
  • To straighten your hair, use a flat iron and run it through small sections of your hair.
  • To create a sleek and polished look, use a hair serum to tame any frizz.

FAQ About Lob Haircuts

1. Can I still wear my hair up with a lob?

Yes, a lob can be styled into an updo or ponytail. You may need to use hair clips or bobby pins to secure shorter layers.

2. Will a lob suit my face shape?

A lob can suit any face shape, but the length and style may need to be adjusted to flatter your features.

3. How often do I need to get my lob trimmed?

You should get your lob trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

4. Can I still color my hair with a lob?

Yes, a lob can be colored just like any other haircut. Talk to your hairstylist about the best color options for your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Your Lob

To keep your lob looking healthy and shiny, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools every day to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Now that you know everything about getting a lob haircut, it's time to book your appointment and enjoy your new look!
