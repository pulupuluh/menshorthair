How To Cut Hair At Home For Boys With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you are a beginner. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be a great way to save money and give your child a fresh new look. In this article, we will show you how to cut hair at home for boys with scissors in a relaxed English language. We will cover everything from how to prepare your child's hair to the final finishing touches. So, let's get started!

Description

Before we begin, it's important to note that cutting hair at home requires some patience and practice. You may not get it right the first time, but don't worry â with time and practice, you'll become a pro. Also, make sure to use sharp scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage the hair and make it harder to cut.

How to Cut Hair at Home for Boys with Scissors

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the supplies you'll need. These include:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clippers (optional)
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Clip or hair tie

Having all these supplies handy will make the process easier, and you won't have to stop midway to search for something.

Step 2: Prepare Your Child's Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your child's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair is harder to cut, and you may end up cutting more than you intended. Use the comb to detangle the hair and section it off using clips or a hair tie. Make sure you have a clear view of the area you want to cut.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start by cutting small sections of hair at a time. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. It's essential to make small cuts rather than big ones. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't undo a big cut.

Step 4: Trim the Sides and Back

When cutting the sides and back, use the clipper to trim the hair to the desired length. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to the shortest. Use the scissors to blend the hair on the sides and back with the top.

Step 5: Cut the Top

When cutting the top, make sure to keep it longer than the sides and back. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim it at an angle. Make small cuts to avoid cutting too much hair. Blend the top with the sides and back using the scissors.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, use the comb to check for any uneven spots. Make small adjustments as needed.

Step 7: Finish with Styling

Finally, style your child's hair as desired. You can use a hair gel or pomade to keep it in place.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if needed
  • Check for uneven spots regularly
  • Take your time and don't rush
  • Watch tutorial videos for more guidance

FAQ

Can I cut my child's hair if they have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, cutting curly hair requires a different technique. Make sure to research and practice before cutting.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows and the style you want. On average, cutting every 4-6 weeks is recommended.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry â mistakes happen. If you cut too much, you can always wait for it to grow back or take your child to a professional stylist for a fix.

Hair Care

Cutting hair at home is just one part of hair care. To keep your child's hair healthy, make sure to wash it regularly, use a conditioner, and avoid using too much heat or chemicals. Also, make sure to trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut hair at home for boys with scissors, it's time to put it into practice. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll soon become an expert.
