How To Fade Your Own Hair Color A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've ever dyed your hair and later regretted it, you know how frustrating it can be to wait for the color to fade on its own. Luckily, there are a few ways you can speed up the process and fade your own hair color at home. In this article, we'll walk you through each step of the process and provide tips for maintaining healthy hair along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fade Your Own Hair Color
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Fading Hair Color
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Fading

How to Fade Your Own Hair Color

Before we get started, it's important to note that the effectiveness of these methods may vary depending on your hair type, the dye used, and how long ago you colored your hair. With that said, here are a few ways to fade your own hair color:

Baking Soda

Baking soda is a natural exfoliant that can help remove buildup from your hair. To use it to fade your hair color, mix 1/4 cup of baking soda with enough water to make a paste. Apply the paste to your hair, focusing on the areas with the most color, and let it sit for 15-20 minutes. Rinse with warm water and shampoo as usual. Repeat every few days until desired results are achieved.

Anti-Dandruff Shampoo

Anti-dandruff shampoos contain harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of color. To use this method, wet your hair and apply the shampoo, focusing on the areas with the most color. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing with warm water. Repeat every few days until desired results are achieved.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is an antioxidant that can help fade hair color. Crush up 10-15 tablets of vitamin C and mix with enough water to make a paste. Apply the paste to your hair and let it sit for 30-60 minutes before rinsing with warm water. Repeat every few days until desired results are achieved.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know a few methods for fading hair color, here's a step-by-step guide to help you through the process:

  1. Choose a method that works best for you.
  2. Gather all necessary supplies.
  3. Protect your clothing and surfaces with towels.
  4. Apply the chosen method to your hair, focusing on the areas with the most color.
  5. Let the product sit for the recommended amount of time.
  6. Rinse with warm water and shampoo as usual.
  7. Repeat every few days until desired results are achieved.

Tips for Fading Hair Color

While these methods can be effective, they can also be harsh on your hair. Here are a few tips to help you maintain healthy hair while fading your hair color:

  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Minimize washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos or conditioners.
  • Consider using a hair mask to help repair any damage.

FAQs

Can I use these methods on any hair color?

Yes, these methods can be used on any hair color. However, they may be more effective on lighter colors.

How long will it take to fade my hair color?

The length of time it takes to fade your hair color depends on a variety of factors, including the method used and your hair type. It may take several days or weeks to achieve desired results.

Will these methods damage my hair?

These methods can be harsh on your hair and may cause damage if used too frequently or for too long. It's important to maintain healthy hair habits to minimize damage.

Hair Care After Fading

Once you've successfully faded your hair color, it's important to continue taking care of your hair to maintain its health. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools whenever possible.
  • Consider getting a trim to remove any damaged ends.

With these tips and methods, you should be able to successfully fade your own hair color at home. Remember to take care of your hair throughout the process to minimize damage and maintain its health.
