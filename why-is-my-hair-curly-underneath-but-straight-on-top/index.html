Why Is My Hair Curly Underneath But Straight On Top

Written by Christine Jul 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Do you ever wonder why your hair is curly underneath but straight on top? It may seem like a mystery, but there's a scientific explanation for this phenomenon. In this blog post, we'll explore the reasons behind this common hair pattern and provide some tips on how to care for your curly locks.

Description

The hair pattern of being curly underneath but straight on top is quite common, and it often leaves people feeling frustrated or confused. This hair type is also known as type 2 hair, which is characterized by waves or loose curls. The hair on top of your head is straight because it is exposed to more sunlight and air, which can make it drier and less likely to curl. The hair underneath, on the other hand, is protected from the elements and retains more moisture, which can lead to more defined curls.

How to

If you have curly hair underneath and straight hair on top, there are some things you can do to enhance your natural texture and keep your hair healthy. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Hair Products

When it comes to curly hair, using the right hair products can make all the difference. Look for products that are designed for curly hair, such as sulfate-free shampoos, curl-enhancing conditioners, and styling gels. These products can help define your curls and keep them looking healthy and shiny.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair, so it's best to avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural curls.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make curly hair look frizzy and undefined. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

Step by step

If you're not sure how to care for your curly hair, here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp and roots, and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

2. Condition Your Hair

Apply a curl-enhancing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Apply Styling Products

Apply a curl-enhancing gel or mousse to your hair, working it through from root to tip. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair, or air-dry it for a more natural look.

4. Maintain Your Curls

Throughout the day, you may need to touch up your curls with a bit of styling product. Use a small amount of gel or mousse to redefine your curls and keep them looking their best.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for curly hair:

1. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage, as it reduces friction between your hair and the pillowcase. This can help your curls stay defined and healthy-looking.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz and breakage. To protect your hair, wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray before going outside.

3. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Curly hair is delicate, so it's important to be gentle when styling and detangling it. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it's dry.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curly hair:

1. Can Curly Hair Become Straight?

While it's not possible to permanently change the texture of your hair, heat styling and chemical treatments can temporarily make curly hair look straight. However, these methods can also damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

2. How Can I Make My Curls More Defined?

To make your curls more defined, use curl-enhancing products like gels, mousses, and creams. You can also try diffusing your hair with a hair dryer, or using a curling iron to define individual curls.

3. How Often Should I Wash My Curly Hair?

How often you should wash your curly hair depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash it every day, while others only wash it once a week. Experiment to find a washing routine that works best for your hair.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid heat styling, and use products that are designed for curly hair. With a little bit of TLC, you can embrace your natural texture and feel confident in your curls.
