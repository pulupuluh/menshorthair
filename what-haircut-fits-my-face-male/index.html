What Haircut Fits My Face Male A Guide To Finding The Perfect Hairstyle

Written by Robby May 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a male looking for a new haircut, it's important to consider what haircut fits your face. Different face shapes require different hairstyles to bring out the best features and create a balanced look. In this guide, we'll be discussing the different face shapes and what haircut fits them best.

What is My Face Shape?

Before choosing a hairstyle, it's important to determine your face shape. To do this, stand in front of a mirror and trace the outline of your face with a washable marker or lipstick. Then, step back and look at the shape you've drawn. There are six main face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Triangle

Once you've determined your face shape, you can choose a haircut that will complement your features and create balance.

What Haircut Fits My Face Male?

Round Face

A round face is wider at the cheeks and has a softer jawline. To elongate the face, a short back and sides with longer hair on top is a great option. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides or emphasize the roundness of the face.

Oval Face

An oval face is considered the most versatile face shape and can pull off most hairstyles. A classic short back and sides or a longer, textured style both work well for an oval face.

Square Face

A square face has a strong jawline and forehead, and a haircut that softens these features is ideal. A textured crop or a side-swept fringe can balance out the face and create a flattering look. Avoid blunt cuts or hairstyles that add volume to the sides.

Heart Face

A heart-shaped face has a wider forehead and a pointed chin. A hairstyle that adds volume to the top and sides of the head can balance out the face shape. A messy quiff or a layered cut with textured fringe are great options. Avoid hairstyles that are too short or slicked back.

Diamond Face

A diamond face has a narrow forehead and chin with wider cheekbones. A fringe or textured crop can soften the angles of the face and create balance. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides or emphasize the narrowness of the forehead and chin.

Triangle Face

A triangle face has a wider jawline and a narrower forehead. A hairstyle that adds volume to the top and sides of the head can balance out the face shape. A textured crop or a messy quiff are great options. Avoid hairstyles that are too short or emphasize the width of the jawline.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

When choosing a haircut, it's important to consider your hair type and lifestyle. If you have thick, curly hair, a longer style with layers can help manage the volume. If you have thin hair, a shorter style with texture can create the illusion of fullness. It's also important to consider how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day. A low-maintenance style may be better suited for a busy lifestyle.

FAQ

What is the most flattering hairstyle for men?

The most flattering hairstyle for men depends on their face shape and hair type. A short back and sides with longer hair on top is a classic style that works well for most face shapes.

How often should men get a haircut?

Men should typically get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style of their haircut.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your haircut looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools too often. A regular trim can also help maintain the shape of your haircut.

Choosing the right haircut can make a big difference in your overall appearance. By considering your face shape and hair type, you can choose a style that brings out your best features and creates balance. Remember to take care of your hair to keep your haircut looking its best.
