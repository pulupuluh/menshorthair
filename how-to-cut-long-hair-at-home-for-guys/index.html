How To Cut Long Hair At Home For Guys Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the barbershop every few weeks? Or maybe you just prefer the convenience of cutting your own hair at home. Whatever your reason may be, cutting long hair at home can seem like a daunting task, especially for guys. But don't worry, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut right in your own bathroom. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut long hair at home for guys.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Prepping Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Cleaning Up
  6. Finishing Touches
  7. Tips for Cutting Long Hair at Home for Guys
  8. FAQ
  9. Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Here are the essentials:

  • Scissors - preferably hair cutting scissors, but regular scissors will do in a pinch
  • Comb
  • Clippers - optional, but helpful for trimming the back of your head
  • Handheld mirror - to see the back of your head
  • Hair ties or clips - to section off your hair
  • Towel or cape - to catch any hair clippings

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can shrink and make it harder to get an accurate cut. Comb through your hair to detangle any knots and make sure it's all going in the same direction.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any spots. Start by dividing your hair into four even sections - two in the front and two in the back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting. Start with the back of your head and work your way forward. Use the handheld mirror to check your progress as you go. If you're using clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter length. If you're using scissors, use the comb to lift a section of hair and cut straight across. Take your time and don't cut too much off at once.

Cleaning Up

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to clean up. Shake out your towel or cape outside to get rid of any hair clippings. Use a vacuum or broom to clean up any hair on the floor. Take a shower to get rid of any loose hair on your body.

Finishing Touches

After you've cleaned up, take a closer look at your haircut. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven spots. If you want a more polished look, use the clippers to trim the hair around your ears and neckline.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair at Home for Guys

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down - you can always cut more off, but you can't put it back on
  • Take breaks if you need to - cutting your own hair can be tiring and it's important to take breaks to avoid mistakes
  • Be patient - cutting your own hair takes time and practice
  • Watch tutorials - there are plenty of tutorials online that can help you with specific techniques or styles

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my hair wet?
A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry to get a more accurate cut.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style you're going for. As a general rule, you should aim to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to successfully cut long hair at home for guys. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
