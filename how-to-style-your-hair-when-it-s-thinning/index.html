How To Style Your Hair When It s Thinning Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Mar 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Thinning hair can be a frustrating and stressful experience. But, it doesn't have to mean the end of your good hair days. In this article, we'll provide you with some tips and tricks on how to style your hair when it's thinning. We'll cover everything from the best haircuts for thinning hair to products that can help add volume and thickness to your locks.

Table of Contents

  1. Best Haircuts for Thinning Hair
  2. Products for Thinning Hair
  3. Styling Tips for Thinning Hair
  4. Hair Care for Thinning Hair
  5. FAQs

Best Haircuts for Thinning Hair

When it comes to thinning hair, the right haircut can make all the difference. Here are a few haircuts that can help make your hair appear thicker:

Short Haircuts

Short haircuts can make thinning hair look thicker and fuller by creating the appearance of more volume. Pixie cuts, bobs, and shags are all great options for those with thinning hair. These haircuts work best when they're styled with layers and texture to add dimension to your locks.

Long Haircuts

If you prefer longer hair, consider a layered haircut. Layers can make your hair appear fuller while adding texture and movement to your locks. Long haircuts with blunt ends can also help make your hair look thicker.

Products for Thinning Hair

Using the right hair products can also help make your hair appear thicker and fuller. Here are a few products to consider:

Thickening Shampoo and Conditioner

Thickening shampoo and conditioner can help add volume to your hair while also cleaning and nourishing your locks. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and wheat protein.

Volumizing Mousse

Volumizing mousse can help add volume to your hair by lifting your roots and giving your locks a fuller appearance. Apply the mousse to damp hair before blow-drying for best results.

Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can help add volume and texture to your hair while also absorbing excess oil. This can be particularly helpful for those with thinning hair, as oily hair can make your locks appear even thinner.

Styling Tips for Thinning Hair

In addition to the right haircut and products, there are a few styling tips that can help make your hair appear thicker:

Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can help add volume to your locks by lifting your roots. To do this, flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair in sections.

Use a Round Brush

Using a round brush while blow-drying can help add volume and texture to your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down, rolling the brush as you go.

Avoid Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy products like oils and serums, as these can weigh your hair down and make it appear thinner. Instead, opt for lightweight products like mousses and sprays.

Hair Care for Thinning Hair

In addition to the right haircut, products, and styling tips, taking care of your hair is key when it comes to thinning locks. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help keep your locks clean and healthy. However, be careful not to over-wash, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it appear even thinner.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it appear thinner. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using high heat settings.

Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Be sure to eat foods that are high in protein, iron, and vitamin D.

FAQs

Q: Can coloring my hair make it appear thicker?

A: Yes, coloring your hair can make it appear thicker by adding dimension and texture to your locks. However, be sure to avoid harsh chemicals and opt for a professional coloring job.

Q: Will using hair extensions help make my hair appear thicker?

A: Yes, hair extensions can help make your hair appear thicker by adding volume and length to your locks. However, be sure to choose extensions that are made from high-quality hair and that are applied by a professional.

Q: Can stress cause thinning hair?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss and thinning. If you're experiencing hair loss, be sure to talk to your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Thinning hair doesn't have to mean the end of good hair days. By following these tips and tricks, you can style your hair to look fuller and more voluminous.
