How To Control Frizzy Curly Short Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 06, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Frizzy curly short hair can be a challenge to manage, especially if you don't know how to tame it properly. Many people with this hair type struggle to keep their hair under control, which can lead to frustration and even embarrassment. However, with the right techniques and products, you can learn how to control frizzy curly short hair and love your locks again. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about managing your frizzy curly short hair, from how to style it to the best hair care practices.

How to Control Frizzy Curly Short Hair

The first step to controlling frizzy curly short hair is to understand what causes the frizz in the first place. Frizz occurs when the hair cuticle is raised, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft and create a puffy, frizzy appearance. This can happen for a variety of reasons, including humidity, heat damage, and using the wrong hair products.

To control frizzy curly short hair, you need to focus on smoothing the hair cuticle and keeping moisture out. This can be achieved through a combination of styling techniques and hair care practices.

Choose the Right Hair Products

The right hair products can make all the difference when it comes to controlling frizzy curly short hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, as these will contain ingredients that help to smooth the hair cuticle and reduce frizz. Avoid products that contain sulfates, as these can be drying and strip the hair of its natural oils.

Some of the best hair products for controlling frizzy curly short hair include:

  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Curl-defining cream
  • Gel
  • Hair oil

Experiment with different products to see what works best for your hair type and styling preferences.

Use Heat Protection

If you use heat styling tools on your hair, it's important to use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent heat damage. Heat damage can cause the hair cuticle to become raised, leading to frizz and breakage.

Blow Dry Your Hair with a Diffuser

When blow drying your frizzy curly short hair, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. This will help to distribute the heat evenly and prevent the hair from becoming too frizzy. Start by using a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser until it's dry.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Frizzy Curly Short Hair

Follow these steps to tame your frizzy curly short hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Use a curl-defining cream or gel to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  4. Blow dry your hair with a diffuser attachment on low heat.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a hair oil to smooth any remaining frizz and add shine.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Curly Short Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing frizzy curly short hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils from your hands to your hair and contribute to frizz.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your hair type and face shape.

FAQs About Frizzy Curly Short Hair

Q: Can you straighten frizzy curly short hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten frizzy curly short hair using a flat iron or other heat styling tools. However, it's important to use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent heat damage.

Q: How often should you wash frizzy curly short hair?

A: It's best to wash frizzy curly short hair no more than two to three times per week, as over-washing can strip the hair of its natural oils and contribute to frizz.

Q: How do you sleep with frizzy curly short hair?

A: To prevent frizz while you sleep, consider sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase. You can also loosely braid your hair before bed to keep it from becoming too tangled.

Best Hair Care Practices for Frizzy Curly Short Hair

Here are some additional hair care practices to keep your frizzy curly short hair healthy and manageable:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too frequently, as this can cause heat damage and contribute to frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can be drying and contribute to frizz.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel when it's wet, as this can cause frizz. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

With these tips and techniques, you can learn how to control frizzy curly short hair and enjoy beautiful, manageable locks. Remember, it may take some experimentation to find the right products and techniques for your hair type, so don't be afraid to try new things and see what works best for you.
