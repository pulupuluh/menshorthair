How to Say Trim Hair in Spanish

The most common way to say "trim hair" in Spanish is "cortar el pelo". This phrase is easy to remember and widely used in Spanish-speaking countries. However, there are other ways to say it depending on the region or the style you want. Here are some variations:

  • "Recortar el pelo": This is a more precise way to say "trim hair". It's often used when you want to trim just a little bit of hair, or when you want to give your hair a neater look.
  • "Podar el cabello": This phrase is commonly used in Mexico and Central America. It literally means "prune hair" and is often used when you want to get rid of split ends or damaged hair.
  • "Cortar las puntas": If you only want to trim the ends of your hair, you can use this phrase. It means "cut the tips" and is a common request in Spanish-speaking salons.

Remember to use the appropriate verb form depending on who is doing the trimming. For example, if you want to say "I want to trim my hair" you would say "Quiero cortarme el pelo". If you want to say "She is trimming my hair", you would say "Ella me estÃ¡ cortando el pelo".
