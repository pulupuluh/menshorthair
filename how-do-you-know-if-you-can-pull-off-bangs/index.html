How Do You Know If You Can Pull Off Bangs

Written by William Sep 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you thinking of getting bangs but not sure if they'll suit you? Bangs can be a great way to switch up your look and add some edge to your hairstyle. However, not everyone can pull off bangs, and it's essential to know if they'll work for you before you make the chop. In this article, we'll go over how you can determine if you can pull off bangs and some tips for styling and caring for them.

Description

Bangs, also known as fringe, are a popular hairstyle that involves cutting the hair in front of your forehead. They can be cut straight across, angled, or parted in the middle or on the side. Bangs can frame your face, make your eyes pop, and add some personality to your hairstyle. However, not all face shapes and hair types are suited for bangs.

How To Determine If You Can Pull Off Bangs

Here are some factors to consider when deciding if you can pull off bangs:

1. Face Shape

Your face shape plays a significant role in whether or not bangs will suit you. Generally, bangs look best on oval, heart-shaped, and round faces. If you have a square or long face, bangs may make your face appear even more angular or elongate it.

2. Hair Type

Your hair type is another factor to consider. If you have fine or thin hair, bangs can add volume and texture to your hairstyle. However, if you have thick or curly hair, bangs can be challenging to manage and may require more styling time.

3. Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is also an essential factor to consider when deciding if you can pull off bangs. If you're someone who likes to hit the gym every day or doesn't have much time to style your hair, bangs may not be the best option for you. Bangs require regular trimming and styling to maintain their shape and length.

Step By Step Guide

If you've decided that bangs are right for you, here are some tips for getting and styling them:

1. Find Inspiration

Before you get bangs, it's essential to find some inspiration for the type of bangs you want. Look online or in magazines for pictures of bangs that suit your face shape and hair type.

2. Consult With Your Hairstylist

Your hairstylist can help you determine the best type of bangs for your face shape and hair type. They can also give you tips on how to style and care for your bangs.

3. Choose Your Bangs

Once you've consulted with your hairstylist, it's time to choose your bangs. Consider the length, thickness, and shape of your bangs. Straight bangs are the most common, but angled or parted bangs can also look great.

4. Get Them Cut

Once you've chosen your bangs, it's time to get them cut. Make sure to communicate with your hairstylist about the length and thickness of your bangs.

5. Style Them

Styling your bangs can take some practice, but it's essential to maintain their shape and length. Use a blow dryer and round brush to style your bangs straight or to the side. You can also use a flat iron or curling iron to add some texture.

Tips For Caring For Your Bangs

Here are some tips for caring for your bangs:

1. Trim Them Regularly

Bangs require regular trimming to maintain their shape and length. Trim them every 4-6 weeks to prevent them from becoming too long or uneven.

2. Shampoo Them Often

Bangs can become greasy quickly, especially if you have oily skin. Shampoo them often to keep them clean and free of oil buildup.

3. Use Hair Products

Use hair products such as hairspray or hair wax to keep your bangs in place and add some texture.

FAQ

1. Can anyone pull off bangs?

No, not everyone can pull off bangs. Your face shape and hair type are essential factors to consider when deciding if bangs will suit you.

2. Are bangs high maintenance?

Yes, bangs require regular trimming and styling to maintain their shape and length. They also need to be washed and shampooed more often than the rest of your hair.

Hair Care

When getting bangs, it's essential to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain its health. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use A Heat Protectant

When styling your bangs with heat tools, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage and breakage.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair in top condition.

3. Protect Your Hair From The Sun

The sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to shield your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

In conclusion, bangs can be a great way to switch up your look and add some edge to your hairstyle. However, not everyone can pull off bangs, and it's essential to consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle before making the chop. If you've decided to go for it, consult with your hairstylist and use these tips for styling and caring for your bangs.
