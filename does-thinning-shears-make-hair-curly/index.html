Does Thinning Shears Make Hair Curly

Written by Frank Jul 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your hair, you may have come across thinning shears. These specialized scissors are designed to remove bulk from your hair without sacrificing length, resulting in a more layered and textured look.

However, you may be wondering if using thinning shears can make your hair curly. In this blog post, we'll explore the answer to this question and more.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Use Thinning Shears
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Using Thinning Shears
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips

Description

Thinning shears are not designed to make your hair curly, but they can contribute to a more textured and layered look. By removing bulk from your hair, thinning shears can help create volume and movement.

If you have naturally curly hair, using thinning shears can help enhance your curls by removing excess weight and allowing your curls to bounce back. However, if you have straight hair, using thinning shears alone won't make your hair curly.

How to Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears should not be used on wet hair, as this can result in uneven cutting and damage to your hair. Instead, use them on dry hair, and only cut small sections at a time.

When using thinning shears, it's important to hold them at an angle, rather than straight up and down. This will create a more natural, blended look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for using thinning shears:

  1. Start with dry hair that has been detangled and combed through.
  2. Section off a small piece of hair, about 1-2 inches wide.
  3. Hold the thinning shears at an angle, and make small snips along the length of your hair.
  4. Repeat this process with each section of hair, taking care not to cut too much at once.
  5. Once you've finished, style your hair as desired.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some additional tips for using thinning shears:

  • Don't cut too close to the roots, as this can create uneven layers.
  • Use a comb to guide your thinning shears and ensure even cutting.
  • Consider using thinning shears on the bottom layer of your hair to add volume and movement.
  • Regularly clean and sharpen your thinning shears to ensure they work properly and don't damage your hair.

FAQs

Can thinning shears damage your hair?

Thinning shears can damage your hair if not used properly. It's important to only cut small sections at a time, and to avoid cutting too close to the roots or ends of your hair. Additionally, using dull or dirty thinning shears can cause damage and breakage.

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but may not be suitable for very fine or thin hair. If you have curly or wavy hair, using thinning shears can help enhance your natural texture and create a more layered look.

Hair Care Tips

Using thinning shears is just one way to add texture and movement to your hair. To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and using thinning shears properly, you can achieve a more textured and layered look without sacrificing the health of your hair.
