How To Cut Short Hair Yourself At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to save money on haircuts or simply want to try something new, cutting your short hair at home can be a great option. While it may seem intimidating at first, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without ever leaving your house. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting short hair at home, answer some frequently asked questions, and provide tips for maintaining your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather some basic tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and intended for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts. Clippers are optional, but can be helpful for achieving an even length or creating a fade.

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is. Comb your hair to remove any tangles, and part it in the way you normally wear it.

Sectioning Your Hair

Next, section your hair to make it easier to cut. Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use a clip or hair tie to secure each section.

Starting with one of the front sections, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use your fingers as a guide and cut the hair straight across. Repeat on the other front section.

Cutting Your Hair

For the back sections, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers, just like you did with the front sections. Use your fingers as a guide and cut the hair straight across. Be sure to check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

If you want to create a layered look, you can use the point-cutting technique. Hold the scissors vertically and make small cuts into the ends of your hair, working your way up the section. This will create texture and movement.

If you're using clippers, start with the longest guard and work your way down to achieve your desired length. Use the clippers to create a fade by gradually blending the hair from short to long. Be sure to follow the natural shape of your head.

Finishing Touches

Once you're finished cutting, remove the clips or hair ties and shake out your hair. Use a comb to make any adjustments and check for any uneven spots. If you're happy with your cut, you're done! If not, make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair at Home

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. It's easier to take more off than to put it back on!
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes! You can cut curly hair just like straight hair. Keep in mind that curly hair may bounce up after it's cut, so be conservative with the amount you cut off.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, short hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry! Hair grows back. If you make a mistake, try to even it out as best you can and wait for it to grow out. You can always go to a professional stylist for a fix if necessary.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible and use a heat protectant when you do. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.

Cutting short hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, techniques, and a little patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without ever leaving your house. Follow our step-by-step guide, and don't be afraid to experiment!
