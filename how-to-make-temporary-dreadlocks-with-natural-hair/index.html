How To Make Temporary Dreadlocks With Natural Hair

Written by Frank Jun 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our step-by-step guide on how to make temporary dreadlocks with natural hair. If you're looking for a new hairstyle that is easy to maintain and perfect for the summer, then temporary dreadlocks may be just what you need. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about creating temporary dreadlocks with natural hair, including how to do it, tips for maintaining your new hairstyle, and answers to frequently asked questions. Let's get started!

How To

Making temporary dreadlocks with natural hair is relatively simple, but it does require some time and effort. Here are the basic steps you need to follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair thoroughly. This will help to remove any product buildup or dirt that may be in your hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to ensure that your hair is clean and free of any residue.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll need to section your hair. Use a rat-tail comb to create even sections of hair that are about the same size. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip to keep it separate from the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Once you have your hair sectioned, it's time to start twisting each section. Take a section of hair and twist it tightly, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Use your fingers to hold the twist in place as you work your way down the section of hair.

Step 4: Repeat

Continue twisting each section of hair until your entire head is covered in twists. Be sure to twist each section in the same direction, either clockwise or counterclockwise, to ensure that your dreadlocks are uniform.

Step 5: Set Your Twists

Once you have twisted your entire head of hair, it's time to set your twists. You can do this by using a hair dryer on low heat to dry your hair or by allowing your hair to air dry. Be sure to leave your twists in for at least 24 hours to allow them to set properly.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your temporary dreadlocks:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Wash your hair once a week with a clarifying shampoo to keep your scalp clean and your hair healthy.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water and a little bit of leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about temporary dreadlocks:

Q: How long do temporary dreadlocks last?

A: Temporary dreadlocks can last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks, depending on how well you maintain them.

Q: Can I undo my temporary dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can undo your temporary dreadlocks by simply untwisting the sections of hair. However, be aware that your hair may be slightly tangled or knotted after you remove the twists.

Q: Can I swim with temporary dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can swim with temporary dreadlocks, but be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly with fresh water afterward to remove any chlorine or saltwater.

Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your hair while you have temporary dreadlocks:

  • Be gentle when detangling your hair.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties or elastics that can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

That's it! With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create temporary dreadlocks with natural hair and keep them looking great for as long as you want. Have fun experimenting with different styles and colors, and don't be afraid to try new things. Happy styling!
