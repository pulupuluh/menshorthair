How To Wear A Headband With Straight Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyles with your straight hair? Why not try adding a headband to your look? Headbands are a simple yet stylish accessory that can elevate any hairstyle. In this article, we will guide you on how to wear a headband with straight hair, from choosing the right headband to styling your hair around it.

Table of Contents

  • Choosing the Right Headband
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Placing the Headband
  • Styling Your Hair
  • Tips for Wearing a Headband with Straight Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Choosing the Right Headband

When it comes to headbands, there are many different styles to choose from. Here are some tips to help you pick the perfect headband:

Consider the Occasion

Think about where you will be wearing the headband. Is it for a casual day out or a formal event? This will help determine the style and color of the headband you should choose.

Choose the Right Width

The width of the headband can also make a difference in how it looks with your straight hair. If you have a smaller head, opt for a thinner headband. If you have a larger head, a wider headband may look more proportional.

Pick the Right Material

Headbands come in many different materials, including fabric, metal, and plastic. Consider the material of the headband and how it will feel on your head. Metal headbands may be uncomfortable for extended wear, while plastic headbands may not be as durable.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you put on your headband, you need to prepare your hair. Here are some tips:

Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help the headband stay in place and prevent your hair from slipping out.

Apply Products

If you have fine or thin hair, apply a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and hold. For thicker hair, use a smoothing serum to tame any frizz or flyaways.

Placing the Headband

Now that your hair is prepped, it's time to put on the headband. Here's how:

Start at the Top of Your Head

Place the headband at the top of your head, just behind your hairline. Make sure it's centered.

Secure the Headband

Slide the headband down towards the nape of your neck, securing it in place. You can adjust the headband as needed to make sure it's snug but not too tight.

Styling Your Hair

Now that the headband is in place, you can style your hair around it. Here are some ideas:

Leave Your Hair Down

You can wear your hair down and straight, letting the headband add some interest to your look.

Create a Half-Up Style

Pull the top half of your hair back and secure it with a clip or bobby pins. This creates a half-up, half-down look that's perfect for a casual day out.

Create a Top Knot

Gather your hair into a high ponytail, then twist it into a bun and secure with a hair tie. This creates a chic and effortless look.

Tips for Wearing a Headband with Straight Hair

Here are some additional tips for wearing a headband with straight hair:

  • Experiment with different headband styles to find what works best for you.
  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose sections of hair around the headband.
  • Try adding some curls or waves to your hair for added texture.
  • Accessorize with earrings or a necklace that complement your headband.

FAQs

Can I wear a headband with bangs?

Yes! Headbands can look great with bangs. Just make sure to place the headband behind your bangs, not over them.

Can I wear a headband with short hair?

Absolutely! Just make sure to choose a headband that's the right size for your head and hair length.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your straight hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to wear a headband with straight hair, it's time to try it out for yourself! Whether you're going for a casual or formal look, a headband can add some flair and personality to your hairstyle.
