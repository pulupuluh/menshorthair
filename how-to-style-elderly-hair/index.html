How To Style Elderly Hair Tips Tricks And Techniques

Written by Luffy May 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As we age, our hair changes too. It can become thinner, drier, and more prone to breakage. However, that doesn't mean you have to give up on styling your hair altogether. With the right techniques and products, you can still achieve beautiful, stylish hair even in your golden years. In this article, we'll share some tips, tricks, and techniques for styling elderly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Elderly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Elderly Hair
  • Tips for Styling Elderly Hair
  • FAQs about Styling Elderly Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Elderly Hair

How to Style Elderly Hair

The first step to styling elderly hair is to choose the right hairstyle. You want something that flatters your face shape and works with your hair type. A short, layered bob is a great option for thinning hair, while a longer, layered cut can add volume and movement to thicker hair.

Once you have the right haircut, it's time to think about styling. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add body to thinning hair.
  • Blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and lift at the roots.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and tame frizz.
  • Experiment with different styles, such as updos, braids, or half-up, half-down styles.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Elderly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling elderly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with products designed for your hair type.
  2. Towel dry your hair to remove excess water.
  3. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots, focusing on the crown of your head.
  4. Blow dry your hair using a round brush, lifting at the roots to add volume.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and tame frizz.
  6. Experiment with different styles, such as a messy bun, a braided crown, or a half-up, half-down style.
  7. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips for Styling Elderly Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling elderly hair:

  • Invest in a good quality, lightweight hair dryer that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your hair.
  • If you have thinning hair, consider using a scalp treatment to stimulate hair growth.
  • If you have gray hair, use a purple shampoo to prevent yellowing and keep your hair looking bright and vibrant.
  • Don't be afraid to try new hairstyles or hair accessories, such as headbands, scarves, or hair clips.

FAQs about Styling Elderly Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling elderly hair:

Q: Can I still color my hair as I age?

A: Yes, you can still color your hair as you age. However, it's important to use gentle, ammonia-free formulas that won't damage your hair. Consider visiting a professional stylist for best results.

Q: What can I do about thinning hair?

A: There are several options for thinning hair, including volumizing products, hair extensions, and hairpieces. Talk to your stylist about what options might work best for you.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of natural oils.

Hair Care Tips for Elderly Hair

Finally, here are some general hair care tips for elderly hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as sulfates and parabens, which can dry out and damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and maintenance.

By following these tips, tricks, and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, stylish hair at any age. Don't be afraid to experiment and try new styles â after all, age is just a number!
