Why Is My Pubic Hair Thicker After Shaving

Written by Petter Aug 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have ever shaved your pubic hair, you might have noticed that the hair seems to grow back thicker and coarser. This can be frustrating, especially if you are trying to maintain a neat and tidy appearance down there. In this blog post, we will discuss why your pubic hair may seem thicker after shaving, and what you can do to prevent it.

Description

Pubic hair is coarser and thicker than the hair on other parts of the body, which is why it can be difficult to shave. When you shave your pubic hair, you are cutting the hair off at the surface of the skin. This means that the hair shaft is wider and more visible, which can give the appearance of thicker hair.

Additionally, shaving can cause the hair to grow back at a different angle, which can make it appear thicker and more noticeable. This is because the hair follicles are no longer growing in the same direction, which can create a more noticeable shadow.

How to Shave Your Pubic Hair

Before we dive into why your pubic hair may be thicker after shaving, let's first discuss the proper way to shave your pubic hair. Here are some steps you can follow to ensure a clean and safe shave:

Step 1: Trim the Hair

Before you start shaving, it's important to trim your pubic hair down to a manageable length. You can use scissors or clippers to do this, but be careful not to cut yourself.

Step 2: Exfoliate

Next, exfoliate the area with a gentle scrub or loofah to remove any dead skin cells and help prevent ingrown hairs.

Step 3: Apply Shaving Cream

Apply a generous amount of shaving cream or gel to the area you want to shave. This will help lubricate the skin and prevent nicks and cuts.

Step 4: Shave

Using a clean and sharp razor, shave in the direction of hair growth. Take your time and be careful not to apply too much pressure, as this can cause razor burn or cuts.

Step 5: Rinse and Moisturize

Once you have finished shaving, rinse the area with warm water and apply a moisturizing lotion or oil to soothe the skin.

Tips to Prevent Thicker Hair Growth

Now that you know how to properly shave your pubic hair, let's discuss some tips to prevent thicker hair growth:

Tip 1: Use a Sharp Razor

A dull razor can cause irritation and can actually make your hair appear thicker. Make sure you use a sharp and clean razor every time you shave.

Tip 2: Don't Shave Too Often

Shaving too often can cause irritation and can actually make your hair grow back thicker. Try to wait at least a few days in between shaves.

Tip 3: Consider Waxing or Laser Hair Removal

If you are tired of dealing with thick hair growth after shaving, you may want to consider waxing or laser hair removal. These methods can provide longer-lasting results and can actually reduce hair growth over time.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for pubic hair to be thicker?

A: Yes, pubic hair is naturally thicker and coarser than other types of hair on the body.

Q: Why does my pubic hair itch after shaving?

A: Itchiness after shaving is common and is usually caused by irritation or ingrown hairs. Make sure you exfoliate before shaving and use a moisturizing lotion afterward.

Q: Can shaving pubic hair cause infections?

A: Shaving can increase the risk of infection if you nick yourself or if bacteria gets trapped in hair follicles. Make sure you use a clean razor and avoid shaving over any cuts or irritation.

Hair Care

Overall, shaving your pubic hair can be a tricky and frustrating process. However, by following the proper steps and taking care of your skin, you can prevent thicker hair growth and maintain a neat and tidy appearance down there. Remember to always use a clean and sharp razor, exfoliate regularly, and moisturize after shaving to keep your skin smooth and healthy.
