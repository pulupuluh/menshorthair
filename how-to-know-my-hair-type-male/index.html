How To Know My Hair Type Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline May 17, 2023 · 9 min read

Knowing your hair type is crucial when it comes to taking care of your hair. Every hair type has specific needs, and understanding your hair type will help you choose the right products and styling techniques. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about how to know your hair type male.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Know My Hair Type Male
  3. Step by Step Guide
  4. Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips for Different Hair Types

Description

Hair typing is a system that categorizes hair based on its texture, curl pattern, and thickness. There are different hair typing systems, but the most common one is the Andre Walker Hair Typing System. This system categorizes hair into four types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each type is further divided into three subcategories based on the hair's texture and curl pattern.

How to Know My Hair Type Male

The first step in identifying your hair type is to wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using any styling products or heat styling tools as they can alter your hair's natural texture. Once your hair is clean, let it air dry without combing or brushing it.

Texture

The texture of your hair is determined by the size of the individual hair strands. There are three textures: fine, medium, and thick. To determine your hair's texture, take a single strand of hair and roll it between your fingers. If you can barely feel the strand, you have fine hair. If you can feel it but not too much, you have medium hair. If you can feel a thick strand, you have thick hair.

Curl Pattern

The curl pattern of your hair is determined by the shape of your hair follicle. There are four curl patterns: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. To determine your curl pattern, look at your hair when it's dry and without any product. If your hair falls straight, you have straight hair. If your hair has a slight bend or wave, you have wavy hair. If your hair forms loose or tight curls, you have curly hair. If your hair forms tight coils or spirals, you have coily hair.

Density

The density of your hair is determined by the number of hair strands on your head. There are three densities: low, medium, and high. To determine your hair density, tie your hair in a ponytail and measure the circumference of the ponytail. If your ponytail is less than two inches, you have low density hair. If your ponytail is between two and four inches, you have medium density hair. If your ponytail is more than four inches, you have high density hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to know your hair type male:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Let your hair air dry without combing or brushing it.
  3. Take a single strand of hair and roll it between your fingers to determine your hair's texture.
  4. Look at your hair when it's dry and without any product to determine your curl pattern.
  5. Tie your hair in a ponytail and measure the circumference of the ponytail to determine your hair's density.

Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type

Identifying your hair type can be tricky, especially if you have a mix of textures and curl patterns. Here are some tips to help you determine your hair type:

  • Ask a hair stylist to help you identify your hair type.
  • Look at pictures of different hair types and compare them to your hair.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques and products to see what works best for your hair.

FAQs

1. What is the best way to determine my hair type?

The best way to determine your hair type is to wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, let it air dry, and then examine it for texture, curl pattern, and density.

2. Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change over time due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

3. Can I have a mix of different hair types?

Yes, it's common to have a mix of different hair types on your head.

Hair Care Tips for Different Hair Types

Straight Hair

Straight hair tends to be oily and requires frequent washing. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup and a lightweight conditioner to prevent weighing down your hair. Avoid heavy products and heat styling tools.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair tends to be frizzy and requires moisture. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your waves.

Curly Hair

Curly hair tends to be dry and requires a lot of moisture. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a deep conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Apply a curl-defining cream or gel to enhance your curls and avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry.

Coily Hair

Coily hair tends to be fragile and requires gentle handling. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a deep conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. Apply a leave-in conditioner and a natural oil to seal in moisture. Avoid heat styling tools and tight hairstyles.

Knowing your hair type and taking care of it accordingly is the key to healthy and beautiful hair. Follow the tips and advice in this article to identify your hair type and choose the right products and styling techniques. Remember, your hair is unique, and what works for someone else may not work for you, so experiment and find what works best for your hair.
