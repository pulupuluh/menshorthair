When Does My Hair Stop Falling Out After Pregnancy

Written by Oliver May 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our blog post where we answer the question, "When does my hair stop falling out after pregnancy?" If you're a new mom, you may have noticed that your hair is falling out more than usual. Don't worry; this is perfectly normal. In this article, we'll explain why this happens and give you tips on how to care for your hair during this time.

Table of Contents

  • Why Does Hair Loss Happen After Pregnancy?
  • When Does Hair Loss Stop After Pregnancy?
  • How Long Does Hair Loss Last After Pregnancy?
  • Tips for Caring for Your Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss
  • FAQs
  • Final Thoughts

Why Does Hair Loss Happen After Pregnancy?

During pregnancy, your hormones change, and you may have noticed that your hair looked thicker and more luscious than ever. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels return to normal, and your hair may start to shed. This is because during pregnancy, your hair went into a resting phase, and after giving birth, it enters a shedding phase.

Additionally, many new moms experience stress, lack of sleep, and poor nutrition, which can also contribute to hair loss. It's essential to take care of yourself during this time to help your hair grow back healthy and strong.

When Does Hair Loss Stop After Pregnancy?

Generally, hair loss after pregnancy peaks at around three to four months postpartum. However, every woman's body is different, and some may experience hair loss for up to a year after giving birth.

The good news is that hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually grow back. You may notice new hair growth around six months after giving birth, and your hair should return to its pre-pregnancy state within a year or two.

How Long Does Hair Loss Last After Pregnancy?

As we mentioned earlier, hair loss after pregnancy is temporary and should resolve itself within a year or two. However, there are things you can do to help promote healthy hair growth and speed up the process.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some tips to help you care for your hair and promote healthy hair growth during postpartum hair loss:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals is essential for healthy hair growth. Foods that are particularly good for your hair include eggs, fish, nuts, and leafy green vegetables.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight ponytails, braids, or buns. Opt for loose hairstyles instead, such as a messy bun or a loose braid.

Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle, sulfate-free hair products that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Additionally, avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make hair loss worse.

Take Care of Your Scalp

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean your scalp and hair. Additionally, massaging your scalp can help promote healthy hair growth by increasing blood flow to your hair follicles.

FAQs

Is hair loss after pregnancy permanent?

No, hair loss after pregnancy is temporary and should resolve itself within a year or two.

Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

Yes, breastfeeding can cause hair loss due to hormonal changes in the body.

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented entirely. However, taking care of yourself by eating a healthy diet, getting enough sleep, and reducing stress can help promote healthy hair growth.

Final Thoughts

If you're experiencing hair loss after pregnancy, don't worry; this is perfectly normal. Remember that this is temporary, and with proper care, your hair will grow back healthy and strong. Follow our tips for caring for your hair during postpartum hair loss, and don't hesitate to reach out to your doctor if you have any concerns.
