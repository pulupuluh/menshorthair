What Does Thinning Out Your Hair Do

Written by Daniel Apr 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning out your hair is a common practice that many individuals use to achieve a certain look or to make their hair more manageable. However, there are many misconceptions about what thinning out your hair actually does. In this blog post, we will explore what thinning out your hair does, how to do it, tips, frequently asked questions, and how to care for your hair after thinning it out.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Thin Out Your Hair
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Thinning Out Your Hair

Description

Thinning out your hair is a process that involves removing some of the bulk from your hair strands. This can be done using thinning shears or a razor comb, which are designed to remove some of the hair while leaving the rest intact. Many people choose to thin out their hair to make it more manageable, especially if they have thick or curly hair. Thinning out your hair can also give it a more textured, layered look, which is popular in many hairstyles.

How To Thin Out Your Hair

Thinning out your hair can be done at home or in a salon. If you are doing it at home, it is important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your hair. It is also important to know how much hair to remove, as thinning out your hair too much can lead to uneven patches or thinning spots.

Step By Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out your hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Separate your hair into sections using clips.
  3. Take one section at a time and comb through it to remove any tangles.
  4. Hold the section of hair straight up in the air.
  5. Take your thinning shears or razor comb and start at the bottom of the hair section.
  6. Using the shears or comb, start cutting small sections of hair at a time, while keeping the shears or comb at a slight angle.
  7. Continue to cut small sections of hair until you have achieved the desired level of thinning.
  8. Repeat on all hair sections.
  9. Style as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start by thinning out a small amount of hair at a time, and gradually increase the amount as needed.
  • Be sure to use sharp thinning shears or a razor comb to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Consult with a hair stylist if you are unsure of how much hair to remove.
  • Keep in mind that thinning out your hair can make it more prone to breakage, so be sure to use a deep conditioner regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out your hair:

Does thinning out your hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning out your hair does not make it grow back thicker. Thinning out your hair only removes some of the bulk, and does not affect the actual hair follicle.

Does thinning out your hair damage it?

Thinning out your hair can damage it if done improperly. It is important to use the right tools and techniques, and to not remove too much hair at once.

How often should I thin out my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. It is generally recommended to thin out your hair every 2-3 months.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Your Hair

After thinning out your hair, it is important to take good care of it to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after thinning it out:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Do not use heat styling tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to achieve a more manageable, textured look. However, it is important to use the right tools and techniques, and to take good care of your hair afterwards to prevent damage and breakage. By following the tips and guidelines in this blog post, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy-looking head of hair.
