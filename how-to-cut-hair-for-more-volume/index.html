How to Cut Hair for More Volume: Step-by-Step Guide in 2023

If you have fine or thin hair, you may be looking for ways to add more volume to your locks. One way to achieve this is by getting a haircut that is specifically designed to add volume to your hair. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair for more volume. We will also share some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Consultation
  • Step 2: Sectioning
  • Step 3: Cutting
  • Step 4: Styling
  • Tips for Adding Volume to Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Voluminous Hair

Step 1: Consultation

The first step in cutting hair for more volume is to have a consultation with your stylist. During the consultation, you should discuss the type of haircut you want and your desired outcome. Your stylist will also consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle when determining the best cut for you.

It's important to be honest with your stylist about your hair concerns and what you want to achieve. This will help them create a cut that works for you and your hair type.

Step 2: Sectioning

Once you and your stylist have decided on the best cut for you, the next step is sectioning your hair. Your stylist will use clips to separate your hair into sections, starting at the nape of your neck and working their way up to the crown of your head.

Sectioning your hair ensures that your stylist can cut your hair evenly and achieve the desired volume. It also helps them to see any areas that may need extra attention.

Step 3: Cutting

Now it's time for the actual cutting. Your stylist will use scissors or a razor to create texture and movement in your hair. They may use thinning shears to remove bulk and add more layers to your hair.

The key to cutting hair for more volume is to create layers that are shorter on top and longer on the bottom. This creates the illusion of more volume and movement in your hair. Your stylist may also add some face-framing layers to accentuate your features.

Step 4: Styling

Once your hair is cut, it's time to style it. Your stylist may blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create more volume. They may also use styling products like mousse or volumizing spray to add even more lift and body to your hair.

If you prefer to style your hair at home, be sure to use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help add volume to your hair. You can use a round brush and blow-dryer to create volume, or try using a volumizing mousse or spray.

Tips for Adding Volume to Your Hair

Here are some tips for adding more volume to your hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to create more lift
  • Try a volumizing mousse or spray
  • Add some highlights or lowlights to create depth and dimension in your hair
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh your hair down

FAQs

Q: How often should I get my hair cut for more volume?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your hairstyle and keep your hair healthy.

Q: Can I still get a voluminous haircut if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, absolutely! Your stylist can create layers and texture in your hair to add volume, regardless of your hair texture.

Hair Care Tips for Voluminous Hair

Here are some tips to care for your voluminous hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to prevent tangles and breakage
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can achieve the voluminous hair of your dreams. Remember to consult with your stylist and be honest about your hair concerns to achieve the best results.
