Face Shapes Ranked A Guide To Finding Your Perfect Hairstyle

Written by Frank Aug 15, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you tired of trying different hairstyles that just don't seem to work for you? It could be because you're not considering your face shape. The right hairstyle can enhance your features and make you look your best. In this guide, we'll rank the different face shapes and give you tips on which hairstyles work best for each one. Let's get started!

Round Face Shape

Round faces have soft, curved lines and a width and length that are roughly the same. The best hairstyles for round faces are those that add volume and height to the crown, elongating the face. Soft, layered cuts that fall at or below the chin work well. Side-swept bangs also help to create angles.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for round faces are:

  • Long, layered cuts
  • Angled bobs
  • Side-swept bangs
  • High ponytails

On the other hand, styles that should be avoided include chin-length bobs, blunt bangs, and center parts.

Oval Face Shape

Oval faces are longer than they are wide, with a gently rounded hairline and jaw. This face shape is considered the most versatile, as almost any hairstyle works well. The goal is to keep the balance and not add too much volume to the crown or too much width to the sides.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for oval faces are:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Bob cuts
  • Pixie cuts

Styles to avoid for oval faces include heavy, blunt bangs and styles that add too much volume to the crown.

Square Face Shape

Square faces have a strong jawline and forehead, with a width and length that are roughly the same. The goal is to soften the angles and create a more oval shape. Soft, layered cuts that fall below the jawline work well, as do styles with wispy bangs.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for square faces are:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Soft, layered bobs
  • Loose, messy updos

Avoid styles that are too blunt or angular, as they can make the face look boxy.

Heart Face Shape

Heart-shaped faces have a wider forehead and a narrower chin, with a pointy jawline. The goal is to balance the face by adding width at the chin and softening the forehead. Styles with layers and volume at the chin work well, as do side-swept bangs.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for heart-shaped faces are:

  • Long layers with volume at the chin
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Medium-length bobs
  • Loose waves

Avoid styles that add volume at the crown or are too short, as they can make the forehead appear wider.

Diamond Face Shape

Diamond faces have a narrow forehead and chin, with wide cheekbones. The goal is to balance the face by adding width at the forehead and chin. Soft, layered cuts work well, as do styles with volume at the crown and chin.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for diamond-shaped faces are:

  • Soft, layered cuts
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Volume at the crown
  • Chin-length bobs

Avoid styles that add too much volume at the sides, as they can make the face appear wider.

Oblong Face Shape

Oblong faces are longer than they are wide, with a straight hairline and jaw. The goal is to add width to the face and create a more oval shape. Styles with volume at the sides work well, as do chin-length bobs and side-swept bangs.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for oblong-shaped faces are:

  • Chin-length bobs
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Loose waves
  • Styles with volume at the sides

Avoid styles that are too long or flat, as they can elongate the face even more.

Pear Face Shape

Pear-shaped faces have a wider jawline and a narrower forehead. The goal is to balance the face by adding width at the forehead and softening the jawline. Styles with volume at the crown and chin work well, as do side-swept bangs.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for pear-shaped faces are:

  • Volume at the crown and chin
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Medium-length bobs
  • Loose waves

Avoid styles that add too much volume at the sides, as they can make the jawline appear wider.

Triangle Face Shape

Triangle faces have a wider jawline and a narrower forehead. The goal is to balance the face by adding width at the forehead and softening the jawline. Styles with volume at the crown and chin work well, as do side-swept bangs.

Some examples of hairstyles that work well for triangle-shaped faces are:

  • Volume at the crown and chin
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Medium-length bobs
  • Loose waves

Avoid styles that add too much volume at the sides, as they can make the jawline appear wider.

Hairstyle Tips for Any Face Shape

No matter what your face shape is, there are a few general tips that can help you choose the right hairstyle:

  • Consider your hair texture and thickness
  • Think about your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair
  • Consult with a stylist who can recommend styles that work well for your face shape and hair type
  • Experiment with different styles and don't be afraid to try something new!

FAQs

What's the best way to determine my face shape?

The easiest way to determine your face shape is to take a picture of yourself facing the camera with your hair pulled back. Use a straight edge or ruler to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin. Once you have these measurements, you can compare them to the different face shapes to determine which one you have.

Can I still wear a style that's not recommended for my face shape?

Of course! These guidelines are just suggestions, and ultimately you should wear whatever hairstyle makes you feel confident and beautiful. However, keep in mind that certain styles may not be as flattering as others, and it's always good to experiment and try new things.

How often should I trim my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What's the best way to keep my hair healthy?

Some tips for keeping your hair healthy include:

  • Washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
