What Determines The Texture Of The Hair

Written by Petter Jul 22, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you curious about what determines the texture of your hair? Understanding what causes your hair to be curly, straight, wavy, or kinky can help you choose the best hair care products and styling techniques for your hair type. In this article, we’ll explore the factors that determine your hair’s texture and how to care for your hair accordingly.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Shaft Shape
  • Hair Follicle Shape
  • Humidity
  • Heat Styling
  • Chemical Treatments

  • FAQ

Genetics

Genetics is the primary factor that determines your hair’s texture. Your hair type is determined by the shape of your hair follicles, which is determined by your DNA. If your parents have curly hair, you’re more likely to have curly hair too. If both of your parents have straight hair, you’re more likely to have straight hair as well.

However, it’s important to note that genetics is not the only factor that determines your hair’s texture. Other factors can influence the shape of your hair follicles and the texture of your hair.

Hair Shaft Shape

The shape of your hair shaft also plays a role in determining your hair’s texture. Your hair shaft can be round, oval, or flat. If your hair shaft is round, your hair is likely to be straight. If it’s oval, your hair is more likely to be wavy. If it’s flat, your hair is more likely to be curly or kinky.

Hair Follicle Shape

The shape of your hair follicles can also influence your hair’s texture. If your hair follicles are curved, your hair will grow out at an angle, which can create a curly or wavy texture. If your hair follicles are straight, your hair will grow straight out of your scalp, creating a straight texture.

Humidity

Humidity can also affect the texture of your hair. When there’s a lot of moisture in the air, your hair can become frizzy and curly. This is because the moisture in the air causes your hair to absorb water, which can disrupt the hydrogen bonds that hold your hair in its natural shape.

Heat Styling

Heat styling can also change the texture of your hair. When you apply heat to your hair, it can break down the hydrogen bonds that hold your hair in its natural shape. This can create a straighter texture, even if your hair is naturally curly or wavy.

Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments, such as relaxers or perms, can also change the texture of your hair. Relaxers break down the protein bonds in your hair, which can create a straighter texture. Perms use chemicals to break down and reform the protein bonds in your hair, which can create a curly or wavy texture.

Hair Care Tips

The best way to care for your hair depends on your hair’s texture. Here are some general tips:

Curly or Kinky Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling, as it can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to help moisturize your hair and reduce frizz.

Straight or Wavy Hair

  • Use a lightweight shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you plan to heat style your hair.
  • Use a boar bristle brush to distribute natural oils throughout your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as it can strip your hair of natural oils.

FAQ

Q: Can I change the texture of my hair?

A: Yes, you can change the texture of your hair with heat styling or chemical treatments. However, it’s important to be aware of the potential damage these treatments can cause to your hair.

Q: Is it possible to have more than one hair texture?

A: Yes, it’s possible to have multiple textures on your head. For example, you may have straight hair at the roots and curly hair at the ends.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: The frequency of hair washing depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it’s best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.

Understanding what determines the texture of your hair can help you choose the best hair care products and styling techniques for your hair type. By following these tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and looking its best.
