How To Do Dreads With Short Hair At Home

Written by Luffy May 05, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking to get dreads but have short hair, you might be wondering if it's even possible. The good news is that it is! In this article, we'll walk you through how to do dreads with short hair at home. From preparation to maintenance, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Preparation
  2. Sectioning Your Hair
  3. Creating the Dreads
  4. Maintenance
  5. Tips for Success
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care for Dreads

Preparation

Before getting started, there are a few things you'll need to do to prepare your hair. First, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. This will make it easier to section and dread. Next, gather the following tools:

  • Comb
  • Hair elastics or rubber bands
  • Dread wax or locking gel

Once you have these items, you're ready to begin.

Sectioning Your Hair

The first step in creating dreads with short hair is to section your hair into small, even sections. You can do this by using a comb to create partings from front to back and side to side. Each section should be about the size of a pencil or slightly larger. Secure each section with a hair elastic or rubber band.

Creating the Dreads

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start creating the dreads. Apply a small amount of dread wax or locking gel to each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Use your fingers to twist each section in a clockwise direction until it begins to tighten and form a dread. Repeat this process for each section until your entire head is covered in dreads.

Maintenance

Once your dreads are created, it's important to maintain them properly to keep them looking clean and neat. Here are a few maintenance tips:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Retwist any loose sections as needed to keep the dreads tight.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol or other drying ingredients.

Tips for Success

Creating dreads with short hair can be a bit tricky, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

  • Start with clean, dry hair to make the process easier.
  • Use a small amount of product to avoid buildup and residue.
  • Be patient – creating dreads takes time and practice.

FAQs

Can I create dreads with hair that's been chemically treated?

Yes, but keep in mind that chemically treated hair may be weaker and more prone to breakage. Be gentle when creating and maintaining your dreads.

Can I create dreads without using product?

While it's possible to create dreads without using product, it may be more difficult to get them to stay in place. Dread wax or locking gel helps to hold the dreads together and prevent them from unraveling.

How long does it take to create dreads with short hair?

The amount of time it takes to create dreads with short hair depends on the length and thickness of your hair. On average, it can take several hours to create a full head of dreads.

Hair Care for Dreads

When you have dreads, it's important to take extra care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Wash your dreads regularly to keep them clean.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create and maintain dreads with short hair at home. Happy dreading!
