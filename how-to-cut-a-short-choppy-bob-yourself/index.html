How To Cut A Short Choppy Bob Yourself

Written by William Aug 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your long hair and ready for a change? A short choppy bob may be just the thing you need! Not only is it trendy and stylish, but it's also low maintenance and easy to style. In this article, we'll guide you through the step-by-step process of cutting a short choppy bob yourself, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut a Short Choppy Bob Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting a Short Choppy Bob
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Short Choppy Bobs

How to Cut a Short Choppy Bob Yourself

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror behind you so you can see the back of your head.

Now that you have everything you need, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb through it to make sure there are no tangles.

Step 2: Section off the hair at the nape of your neck and clip the rest of your hair up and out of the way.

Step 3: Decide on the length you want your bob to be and cut the hair at the nape of your neck. Make sure to cut straight across.

Step 4: Release the next section of hair and use the previous section as a guide to cut the next section. Again, cut straight across.

Step 5: Continue to release sections of hair and cut straight across, using the previous section as a guide. Make sure to cut the hair slightly shorter towards the back of your head and longer towards the front.

Step 6: Once you've cut all the sections, check your hair in the mirror to make sure it's even. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Use your scissors to create choppy layers throughout your hair. Start by taking small sections of hair and cutting into them at different angles. This will give your hair a textured, choppy look.

Step 8: Style your hair as desired. You can use a straightener or curling iron to add some waves or curls.

Tips for Cutting a Short Choppy Bob

- Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut

- Start by cutting your hair longer than you think you want it. You can always go shorter, but you can't add length back once it's been cut.

- Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time. It's better to take your time and do it right than rush and make mistakes.

- Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member. It can be difficult to cut the back of your hair yourself.

FAQs

Q: How short should I go?

A: The length of your bob is up to you, but it's important to consider your face shape and hair type when deciding on the length.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries, so cutting it when it's wet can result in a shorter cut than intended.

Q: How often should I trim my short choppy bob?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your bob.

Hair Care Tips for Short Choppy Bobs

- Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair

- Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair

- Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes

- Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your bob

With these tips and steps, you'll be able to cut a short choppy bob yourself and have a stylish new look in no time!
