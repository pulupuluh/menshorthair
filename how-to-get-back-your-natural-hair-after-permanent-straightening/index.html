How To Get Back Your Natural Hair After Permanent Straightening

Written by Frank Jul 13, 2023 ¬∑ 6 min read

Are you tired of your straightened hair and want to return to your natural curls? Permanent straightening may have seemed like a good idea at the time, but it can be difficult to get your natural hair back. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to get back your natural hair after permanent straightening.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Back Your Natural Hair After Permanent Straightening
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Follow
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Description

Permanent straightening is a chemical process that breaks down the bonds in your hair, making it straight. The process is irreversible, so once you've done it, you can't simply wash it out. However, there are ways to get your natural hair back by growing out your hair and using certain hair care techniques.

How to Get Back Your Natural Hair After Permanent Straightening

The first step to getting your natural hair back is to stop using heat styling tools. This includes blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons. Heat can damage your hair and prevent it from growing out healthy. Instead, allow your hair to air dry and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it.

Next, you'll need to be patient. Growing out your hair can take time, but it's worth it to get your natural curls back. As your hair grows, trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Step by Step Guide

1. Stop using heat styling tools
2. Be patient and allow your hair to grow out
3. Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage
4. Use hair care products specifically designed for curly hair
5. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair
6. Consider getting a hair cut that will help your hair grow out evenly
7. Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals

Tips to Follow

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness
- Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz
- Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
- Experiment with different hairstyles to make the growing-out process more fun

FAQs

Can I color my hair while growing it out?

Yes, you can color your hair while growing it out. However, it's important to use hair color that's gentle on your hair and avoid over-processing it.

How long will it take for my hair to grow out?

The length of time it takes for your hair to grow out depends on how long you want it to be. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it could take several months to a year or more to grow out your hair.

Hair Care

When it comes to hair care, it's important to use products that are gentle on your hair and won't cause damage. Look for hair care products specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. Be patient and take care of your hair, and you'll soon have your natural curls back!

In conclusion, getting back your natural hair after permanent straightening can be a long process, but it's worth it to have healthy, beautiful curls. Follow these tips and tricks, be patient, and take care of your hair, and you'll soon have the hair of your dreams.
