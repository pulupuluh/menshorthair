How To Cut Your Own Women s Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 15, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon? Maybe you want to try something new or are simply looking for a way to save money. Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair with clippers can be a great way to achieve a fresh new look without breaking the bank. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own women's hair with clippers, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Women's Hair with Clippers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut Your Own Women's Hair with Clippers

Before you get started, it's important to have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with multiple guard sizes
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape

Once you have your tools, it's time to get started. Here's a quick overview of the process:

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Section your hair
  3. Choose the right guard size
  4. Cut the hair with clippers
  5. Blend the hair with scissors
  6. Trim the bangs (if desired)

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Wash and dry your hair: Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a more even cut.

2. Section your hair: Use a comb to separate your hair into sections. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. Use a clip or hair tie to keep each section in place.

3. Choose the right guard size: Clippers come with different guard sizes, which determine the length of the hair. Choose the guard size based on how short you want your hair to be.

4. Cut the hair with clippers: Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up. Use the clippers to trim the hair to the desired length. Follow the natural shape of your head and move the clippers in an upward motion.

5. Blend the hair with scissors: Once you've finished using the clippers, it's time to blend the hair with scissors. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut any uneven sections. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.

6. Trim the bangs (if desired): If you have bangs, use scissors to trim them to the desired length. Start by cutting them longer than you think you want them, then gradually trim them shorter until you achieve the desired length.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

1. Start with longer guard sizes: It's always better to start with a longer guard size and work your way down. This will give you more control and prevent you from cutting your hair too short.

2. Take breaks: Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming. Take breaks as needed to rest your arms and give yourself a break.

3. Use a mirror: Make sure you have a mirror in front of you and behind you so you can see all angles of your head.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry. This will ensure a more even cut and prevent any surprises once your hair dries.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you like to keep it. On average, most people need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After the Cut

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair looking healthy
  • Visit a professional stylist if you need a trim or want to try a new style

With a little practice and patience, cutting your own hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. Follow these tips and tricks, and you'll be on your way to a great new haircut in no time.
