How To Cover Up A Bald Spot On Your Head

Written by Eveline Feb 22, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you self-conscious about a bald spot on your head? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience hair loss, and it can be frustrating and embarrassing. But there are ways to cover up a bald spot and boost your confidence. Keep reading for our guide on how to cover up a bald spot on your head.

Description: What Causes Bald Spots?

Bald spots can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, hormones, stress, and medical conditions. If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to talk to your doctor to rule out any underlying health issues. Once you know the cause, you can explore treatment options and ways to cover up the bald spot.

How to Cover Up a Bald Spot on Your Head

There are several ways to cover up a bald spot, depending on your preference and budget. Here are some options:

1. Hairstyles and Haircuts

One of the easiest ways to cover up a bald spot is with a strategic hairstyle or haircut. For example, you can try a comb-over, where you comb your hair over the bald spot to hide it. Or you can try a buzz cut or shaved head, which can create a more uniform look and draw attention away from the bald spot.

2. Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a temporary solution to cover up a bald spot. They're made of keratin fibers that blend in with your natural hair and attach to your scalp with static electricity. Simply sprinkle the fibers onto the bald spot and pat them down to blend in. Hair fibers are water-resistant and can last until your next shampoo.

3. Wigs and Hairpieces

Wigs and hairpieces are a more permanent solution to cover up a bald spot. There are many types of wigs and hairpieces available, from synthetic to human hair. You can choose a wig that matches your natural hair color and style, or you can try a new look altogether. Hairpieces, such as toupees or hair toppers, are smaller and can be attached to the bald spot with clips or adhesive.

Step by Step: How to Apply Hair Fibers

If you choose to use hair fibers to cover up your bald spot, follow these steps:

Step 1: Clean and Dry

Make sure your hair and scalp are clean and dry before applying hair fibers. You can use a dry shampoo to absorb any oil or sweat.

Step 2: Sprinkle

Hold the hair fibers bottle about six inches away from your head and sprinkle the fibers onto the bald spot. Start with a small amount and build up as needed.

Step 3: Pat Down

Use your fingers to pat down the fibers and blend them in with your natural hair. Don't rub or brush, as this can cause the fibers to fall out.

Step 4: Style

Style your hair as desired. You can use hairspray to lock in the fibers and create a more natural look.

Tips for Covering Up a Bald Spot

Here are some additional tips to help you cover up a bald spot:

  • Choose a hairstyle or hairpiece that makes you feel confident and comfortable.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to create the illusion of thicker hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make it look thinner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing hats or tight headbands, as they can pull on your hair and cause more hair loss.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair grow back on a bald spot?

It depends on the cause of the bald spot. If it's caused by genetics or age, it's unlikely that hair will grow back. However, if it's caused by a medical condition, such as alopecia, hair may grow back with treatment.

Are hair fibers safe to use?

Yes, hair fibers are safe to use and won't damage your hair or scalp. They're also easy to remove with shampoo.

How long do wigs and hairpieces last?

It depends on the quality and care of the wig or hairpiece. Synthetic wigs and hairpieces can last up to six months with proper care, while human hair wigs and hairpieces can last up to a year or more.

Hair Care for a Bald Spot

Even if you're covering up a bald spot, it's important to take care of your remaining hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and further hair loss.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cover up a bald spot and feel good about your appearance. Remember to talk to your doctor about any concerns regarding hair loss, and explore treatment options that may work for you.
