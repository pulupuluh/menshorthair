How To Fake A Pixie Cut With Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you itching for a change but don't want to commit to a pixie cut? Maybe you're curious about how you'd look with short hair, but you're not ready to take the plunge just yet. Luckily, there's a way to fake a pixie cut with short hair! In this article, we'll show you how to do it in just a few simple steps.

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that's usually cropped close to the head, with longer layers on top. It's a bold and daring look that can be both edgy and feminine. However, not everyone is ready to chop off their locks to achieve this style. That's where faking a pixie cut comes in. With a few clever styling tricks, you can create the illusion of a shorter cut without actually cutting your hair.

How to Fake a Pixie Cut with Short Hair

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help your styling products hold better and prevent any unwanted frizz or flyaways. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, consider straightening it before you begin.

Step 2: Apply a texturizing spray

To create the illusion of shorter hair, you'll need to add some texture and volume. Apply a texturizing spray to your roots and throughout your hair, scrunching it with your fingers to create a tousled, messy look.

Step 3: Pin the sides of your hair back

Take a section of hair from each side of your head and pin it back, securing it with bobby pins. This will create the appearance of a shorter, cropped cut on the sides, while leaving the longer layers on top.

Step 4: Use a hair tie to create a faux-hawk

Gather the hair on top of your head and use a hair tie to create a small ponytail. Tease the hair in the ponytail to add volume, then use bobby pins to secure it in place, creating a faux-hawk. You can also twist the hair in the ponytail to create a messy, textured look.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

To ensure your faux pixie cut stays in place all day, finish with a spritz of hairspray. This will also help to tame any flyaways and add extra hold to your style.

Tips for Faking a Pixie Cut

  • Experiment with different texturizing sprays until you find one that works best for your hair type.
  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to create a seamless look.
  • Try adding a hair accessory, such as a headband or scarf, to enhance your faux pixie cut.
  • Consider using a hair gel or pomade to add extra hold and texture to your style.

FAQ

Can I fake a pixie cut with any hair length?

The shorter your hair, the easier it will be to fake a pixie cut. However, if you have hair that's at least chin-length, you can still create a similar look with the right styling techniques.

Will faking a pixie cut damage my hair?

No, faking a pixie cut shouldn't damage your hair. However, using too much product or styling your hair too tightly can cause breakage or split ends over time. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray if you're using heat tools to style your hair.

How long will my faux pixie cut last?

Your faux pixie cut should last all day, as long as you use enough hairspray and bobby pins to secure it in place. However, if you're planning on dancing or doing any other strenuous activities, you may need to touch up your style throughout the day.

Hair Care for Short Hair

If you're considering a pixie cut or fake pixie cut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can easily fake a pixie cut with short hair. Whether you're testing out a new look or just want to switch things up, this style is a fun and easy way to experiment with shorter hair. Give it a try and see how you like it!
