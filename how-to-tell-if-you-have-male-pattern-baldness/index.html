How To Tell If You Have Male Pattern Baldness

Written by Daniel Jul 07, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re experiencing hair thinning or hair loss, you may be wondering if you have male pattern baldness. This condition affects millions of men worldwide and can lead to complete baldness. In this article, we’ll discuss how to tell if you have male pattern baldness and what you can do about it.

Description of Male Pattern Baldness

Male pattern baldness is a genetic condition that affects men as they age. It’s caused by a hormone called dihydrotestosterone (DHT), which is produced by the male hormone testosterone. DHT causes hair follicles to shrink and eventually stop producing hair. Male pattern baldness typically starts at the hairline and gradually moves back towards the crown of the head.

How to Tell If You Have Male Pattern Baldness

The most obvious sign of male pattern baldness is a receding hairline. This usually starts at the temples and moves back towards the crown of the head. You may notice that your hair is thinner at the top of your head than it used to be, or that you’re losing hair in a circular pattern at the crown of your head.

Another sign of male pattern baldness is a more prominent part. If you’re used to having a center part in your hair, you may notice that it’s becoming wider or that you can see more of your scalp when you part your hair.

You may also notice that your hair is falling out more easily than it used to. If you’re finding more hair on your pillow in the morning or in your brush or comb, this could be a sign of male pattern baldness.

Step by Step Guide to Check for Male Pattern Baldness

To check for male pattern baldness, follow these steps:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror with good lighting.
  2. Take a close look at your hairline. Is it receding, particularly at the temples?
  3. Check the crown of your head. Is there a circular pattern of hair loss?
  4. Part your hair and check to see if your part is wider than it used to be.
  5. Check your pillow, brush, and comb for hair loss.

Tips for Dealing with Male Pattern Baldness

If you think you have male pattern baldness, there are a few things you can do to manage it:

  • Speak to your doctor or a dermatologist about your hair loss. They can help you determine the cause and recommend treatments.
  • Consider using hair loss products like minoxidil or finasteride. These can help slow down or even reverse hair loss in some cases.
  • Try changing your hairstyle. A shorter haircut or a different part can help conceal hair loss.
  • Wear a hat or cap to protect your scalp from the sun and hide any bald spots.

FAQ About Male Pattern Baldness

Q: Can women get male pattern baldness?

A: Yes, women can also experience male pattern baldness. It’s less common than in men, but it can still happen.

Q: Is male pattern baldness reversible?

A: In some cases, male pattern baldness can be reversed with treatment. However, it’s important to speak to your doctor before starting any treatments.

Q: Does stress cause male pattern baldness?

A: While stress can cause hair loss, it’s not the primary cause of male pattern baldness. Genetics and hormones are the main factors.

Hair Care Tips for Men with Male Pattern Baldness

If you have male pattern baldness, it’s important to take care of your remaining hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a soft-bristled brush to avoid pulling out hair.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun with a hat or sunscreen.

If you suspect that you have male pattern baldness, don’t panic. There are many treatments and management strategies available to help you deal with hair loss. Speak to your doctor or a dermatologist to learn more.
