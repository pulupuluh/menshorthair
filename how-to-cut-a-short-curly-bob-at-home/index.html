How To Cut A Short Curly Bob At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Apr 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of shelling out money at the salon for a fresh cut every few weeks? Do you want to try something new with your short curly hair? Cutting a short curly bob at home might seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut a short curly bob at home, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage to your hair.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual. Once you've towel-dried it, comb through any tangles and part your hair where you prefer. Then, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

Clip or tie back the sections you're not currently working on to keep them out of the way. Spray the section you're starting with with water to dampen it slightly. This will make it easier to cut and give you more control.

Cutting Your Hair

Begin with the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the strand between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Then, using your scissors, cut the hair in a straight line.

Repeat this process, working your way through each section. When you get to the back sections, you may need to use a mirror to see what you're doing.

Remember to cut little by little â you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut. It's better to err on the side of caution and take your time.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror to make sure everything looks even. If you notice any areas that need touching up, take small sections of hair and trim them as needed.

Finally, use your comb to create layers or texture as desired. You can also use your fingers to scrunch your curls and create more volume.

Tips for Success

  • Don't rush â take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress.
  • If you're not sure about a length or cut, start with a longer length and work your way down.
  • When in doubt, take less off â you can always cut more later.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

It's generally easier to cut curly hair when it's wet because it's easier to control. However, if you prefer to cut it dry, make sure to dampen it with a spray bottle first.

How often should I cut my hair?

This can vary depending on how fast your hair grows and the style you're going for. As a general rule, aim to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to prevent split ends.

Hair Care After the Cut

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair to keep your curls moisturized and defined. Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can cause damage to your hair. Instead, embrace your natural curls and let them air dry whenever possible.

Cutting a short curly bob at home might seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Just remember to take your time, cut little by little, and use a mirror to check your progress. With a little practice, you might just become your own personal hairstylist!
