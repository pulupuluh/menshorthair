How To Style Edges For White Girls A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Sep 01, 2023 ¬∑ 8 min read

If you're a white girl looking to style your edges, you've come to the right place. Edges are the small hairs at the front of your hairline that can sometimes be difficult to tame. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to style your edges and keep them looking great. We'll also answer some frequently asked questions and provide some tips and tricks to help you achieve the look you want. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Style Edges for White Girls

Styling your edges can be a challenge, but it doesn't have to be. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the look you want. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Cleanse and Moisturize

Before you start styling your edges, it's important to make sure your hair is clean and moisturized. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse your hair, and then apply a moisturizing leave-in conditioner to your edges.

Step 2: Brush Your Edges

Use a soft-bristle brush to brush your edges forward. This will help to smooth them down and prepare them for styling.

Step 3: Apply Edge Control

Edge control is a product specifically designed to help you style your edges. Apply a small amount of edge control to your edges using your fingertips or a small brush.

Step 4: Style Your Edges

Use your fingertips or a brush to style your edges into the desired shape. You can create a sleek look by brushing your edges straight down, or you can create a more natural look by brushing them to the side.

Step 5: Set Your Edges

Once you've styled your edges, use a scarf or a headband to set them in place. Leave the scarf or headband on for at least 30 minutes to allow the edge control to dry and set your edges.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you style your edges:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any dirt and oil from your hair and prepare it for styling.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a moisturizing leave-in conditioner to your hair, paying special attention to your edges. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

Step 3: Brush Your Edges

Use a soft-bristle brush to brush your edges forward. This will help to smooth them down and prepare them for styling.

Step 4: Apply Edge Control

Apply a small amount of edge control to your edges using your fingertips or a small brush. Be sure to apply it evenly to avoid clumping.

Step 5: Style Your Edges

Use your fingertips or a brush to style your edges into the desired shape. You can create a sleek look by brushing your edges straight down, or you can create a more natural look by brushing them to the side.

Step 6: Set Your Edges

Once you've styled your edges, use a scarf or a headband to set them in place. Leave the scarf or headband on for at least 30 minutes to allow the edge control to dry and set your edges.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect edge style:

  • Use a soft-bristle brush to brush your edges. This will help to prevent breakage.
  • Apply edge control sparingly. Too much product can cause build-up and make your edges look greasy.
  • Use a scarf or a headband to set your edges. This will help to keep them in place while the edge control dries.
  • Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I style my edges without edge control?

Yes, you can. You can use a small amount of gel or pomade to style your edges if you don't have edge control.

How often should I style my edges?

You should only style your edges when necessary. Over-styling can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Can I use a hair dryer to set my edges?

Yes, you can. Use a cool setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking great, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a moisturizing leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve the perfect edge style and keep your hair looking great. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid over-styling to prevent damage and breakage. Happy styling!
