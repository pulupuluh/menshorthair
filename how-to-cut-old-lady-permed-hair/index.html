How To Cut Old Lady Permed Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you a hairstylist who needs help cutting old lady permed hair? Look no further! In this article, we'll go over the steps to cutting permed hair and share some tips to ensure a successful cut. So, let's get started!

Description

Cutting old lady permed hair can be a challenge, but with the right technique, it can be done with ease. Permed hair is chemically treated to create curls or waves, and the hair's texture can make it difficult to cut. However, with the right tools and a few tips, you can give your client a fresh new look.

How to Cut Old Lady Permed Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp shears, a comb, and sectioning clips. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle with water handy to keep the hair damp while you cut.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into small sections. Use the sectioning clips to keep the hair in place as you work. This will make it easier to cut the hair evenly.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the hair, starting at the back and working your way to the front. Use the comb to lift the hair and the shears to trim the ends. Be sure to cut the hair at an angle to create layers and texture.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

As you cut, check for evenness. The hair should be the same length on both sides. If you notice unevenness, go back and trim the hair until it's even.

Step 5: Finish the Cut

Once you've finished cutting the hair, use the comb to blend the layers together. This will create a natural look and prevent any harsh lines. You can also add some texture by using the shears to point cut the ends.

Tips for Cutting Old Lady Permed Hair

Tip 1: Don't Cut Too Much

Permed hair is delicate, and cutting too much can damage it. Only trim the ends and avoid using thinning shears.

Tip 2: Cut in Small Sections

Cutting in small sections will help you achieve an even cut and prevent any mistakes.

Tip 3: Use the Right Shears

Use sharp shears to ensure a clean cut. Dull shears can damage the hair and create split ends.

Tip 4: Keep the Hair Damp

Keeping the hair damp will make it easier to cut and prevent any flyaways.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut permed hair with regular shears?

A: Yes, you can use regular shears to cut permed hair. Just make sure they're sharp.

Q: Can I use thinning shears on permed hair?

A: It's best to avoid using thinning shears on permed hair. They can damage the hair and create split ends.

Q: How often should I cut permed hair?

A: Permed hair should be trimmed every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Permed hair requires special care to keep it healthy and maintain its shape. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to the hair to keep it hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing the hair when it's wet, as it can cause breakage.

By following these tips and using the right cutting technique, you can give your client the perfect cut for their permed hair.
