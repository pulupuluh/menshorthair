Why Does My Hair Fall Off When I Dye It

Written by Aprilia Jun 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've ever dyed your hair before, you may have noticed that your hair falls out more than usual. This can be a scary experience, and many people wonder why this happens. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair falls off when you dye it and provide tips on how to prevent it.

Description

Hair dye contains chemicals that can damage your hair. These chemicals can cause your hair to become brittle and break easily. When you dye your hair, these chemicals penetrate the hair shaft and weaken the hair from the inside out. This can cause your hair to fall off when you brush or comb it.

How to

If you're planning to dye your hair, there are a few things you can do to minimize the risk of hair loss. Here are some tips:

1. Choose a High-Quality Hair Dye

Choose a high-quality hair dye that contains natural ingredients. Avoid using cheap hair dye that contains harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Before you dye your hair, deep condition it to help strengthen it. This will help to prevent breakage and hair loss.

3. Don't Wash Your Hair Before Dyeing It

Don't wash your hair before dyeing it. This will help to protect your hair from the chemicals in the hair dye.

4. Follow the Instructions on the Hair Dye

Follow the instructions on the hair dye carefully. Don't leave the dye on your hair for too long or too short. This can cause your hair to become damaged and fall off.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to dye your hair without causing hair loss:

1. Choose a High-Quality Hair Dye

Choose a hair dye that contains natural ingredients and is free of harsh chemicals.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep condition your hair before dyeing it to help strengthen it.

3. Protect Your Skin

Protect your skin by applying petroleum jelly or a barrier cream around your hairline and ears. This will help to prevent the dye from staining your skin.

4. Mix the Dye

Follow the instructions on the hair dye to mix it properly.

5. Apply the Dye

Apply the dye to your hair carefully, making sure to cover all areas evenly.

6. Leave the Dye on for the Recommended Time

Leave the dye on for the recommended time. Don't leave it on for too long or too short.

7. Rinse Your Hair Thoroughly

Rinse your hair thoroughly with water to remove all the dye.

8. Use a Deep Conditioner

Use a deep conditioner after rinsing your hair to help restore its moisture.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss when dyeing your hair:

1. Don't Dye Your Hair Too Often

Don't dye your hair too often. This can cause your hair to become damaged and fall off.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals to help protect your hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools such as blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. These tools can damage your hair and cause it to fall off.

FAQ

Q. Can hair loss from dyeing be permanent?

A. Hair loss from dyeing is usually not permanent. However, if the hair follicle is damaged, it may result in permanent hair loss.

Q. Can dyeing your hair cause scalp irritation?

A. Yes, dyeing your hair can cause scalp irritation. This is because the chemicals in the hair dye can be harsh and irritating to the skin.

Q. How long after dyeing my hair can I wash it?

A. Wait at least 48 hours after dyeing your hair before washing it. This will help to protect your hair from the chemicals in the hair dye.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintain healthy hair. Here are some tips:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals to help promote healthy hair.

2. Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and perm solutions.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet. This will help to prevent breakage and hair loss.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

5. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains SPF.

By following these tips, you can help prevent hair loss when dyeing your hair. Remember to choose a high-quality hair dye, deep condition your hair, and follow the instructions carefully.
