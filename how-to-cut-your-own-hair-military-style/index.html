How To Cut Your Own Hair Military Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts or waiting in long lines at the barbershop? Do you want to achieve a clean and tidy military-style haircut from the comfort of your own home? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair military style.

Description

A military-style haircut is a short, buzzed cut that is low maintenance and easy to maintain. It is a popular choice among men who prefer a clean and professional look. This style is also commonly known as a "high and tight" or "buzz cut." Cutting your own hair military style can be a challenging task, but it is possible with the right tools and techniques.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Military Style

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of hair clippers, hair cutting scissors, a mirror, a comb, and a towel or cape to protect your clothing from hair clippings.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clipper Guard Size

Clipper guards come in different sizes, and each size determines the length of your hair. For a military-style haircut, we recommend using a #1 or #2 guard for a shorter cut.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Begin by using the clippers to trim the sides of your head. Start at the base of your head and work your way up to the top, using the comb to guide the clippers. Be sure to use even pressure and avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 4: Trim the Top

Next, use the scissors to trim the hair on top of your head. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim it evenly. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as it can be difficult to fix mistakes.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair on the sides and top using the clippers and scissors. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim it evenly, making sure to blend the two sections seamlessly.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline

Use the clippers to clean up the hair on your neckline. Start at the center and work your way outwards. Be sure to use even pressure and avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 7: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Use the mirror to check for any uneven spots. If you notice any, use the clippers or scissors to even them out.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you have finished cutting your hair, use a brush or towel to remove any loose hair clippings. Take a shower to wash off any remaining hair clippings.

Tips

- Use the mirror to check for any uneven spots throughout the haircut.
- Take your time and go slowly to avoid mistakes.
- Use even pressure and avoid cutting too much hair at once.
- If you are unsure about cutting your own hair, consider asking a friend or family member for help.
- Practice makes perfect! Don't be discouraged if your first haircut isn't perfect.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my hair with just scissors?

A: While it is possible to cut your hair with just scissors, it can be difficult to achieve a clean and even military-style cut. We recommend using clippers to achieve the best results.

Q: How often should I cut my hair in a military style?

A: Military-style haircuts are low maintenance and can last for several weeks before needing a touch-up. However, the frequency of haircuts depends on personal preference and hair growth rate.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, be sure to take care of it properly. Shampoo and condition your hair regularly, and use a hair oil or serum to keep it moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep it healthy and strong.
