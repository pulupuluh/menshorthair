How To Even Up A Bad Haircut Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia May 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever walked out of the salon with a bad haircut? It can be frustrating and upsetting, but don't worry, you can fix it! In this blog post, we'll give you some tips and tricks on how to even up a bad haircut.

How to Even Up a Bad Haircut

If you've received a bad haircut, the first thing you should do is go back to the salon and ask the stylist to fix it. However, if you don't want to go back to the salon or if you're too busy, you can follow these steps to even up your hair at home.

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Before you start cutting your hair, take a good look at it and assess the damage. Determine which parts of your hair are uneven and where you need to make adjustments.

Step 2: Invest in Good Hair Cutting Scissors

If you're going to cut your own hair, it's important to invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Don't use regular scissors because they can cause split ends and damage your hair.

Step 3: Start with Small Snips

When you're ready to start cutting, begin with small snips. Don't cut off too much at once because you might end up making the problem worse.

Step 4: Use a Comb to Guide Your Cuts

To ensure that your cuts are even, use a comb to guide your cuts. Comb your hair straight and hold the ends between your fingers. Use the comb as a guide and cut along the comb.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

After you've made a few snips, check to see if your hair is even. If it's still uneven, make more small snips until it's even.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've evened up your hair, style it as you normally would. You can also use a hair straightener or curling iron to give it some extra style.

Tips for Even Up a Bad Haircut

Here are some additional tips to help you even up a bad haircut:

  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet because it can make it look shorter when it dries.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, but with practice, you'll get better.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member. They can help you see the back of your head and make sure your cuts are even.
  • If you're nervous about cutting your own hair, start with small snips and work your way up.

FAQ

Can I fix a bad haircut at home?

Yes, you can fix a bad haircut at home. Just follow the steps in this blog post to even up your hair.

What should I do if I'm not confident about cutting my own hair?

If you're not confident about cutting your own hair, it's best to go back to the salon and ask the stylist to fix it.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking healthy and strong, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often because they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of protein and vitamins to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can even up a bad haircut and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and cuts, and remember to take good care of your hair!
