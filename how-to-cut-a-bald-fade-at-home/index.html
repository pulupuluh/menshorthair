How To Cut A Bald Fade At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 29, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts? Do you want to try cutting your own hair? If yes, then you're in the right place. Today, we'll be discussing how to cut a bald fade at home. A bald fade is a popular hairstyle among men, and it's not as complicated as it sounds. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about cutting a bald fade at home, including tools you'll need, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care.

Tools You'll Need

Before we start, let's make sure you have all the tools you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Trimmer
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Clipper guards (1, 2, 3, 4)
  • Mirror

Make sure your clippers and trimmer are sharp and oiled. You don't want to use dull blades on your hair. Also, choose a place with good lighting and a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head.

How to Cut a Bald Fade at Home

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair and Tools

Before you start cutting, wash your hair and dry it completely. Wet hair can be harder to cut, and you don't want to slip with your clippers. Put on your haircutting cape or towel to catch the hair that falls.

Next, attach the #1 guard to your clippers and start buzzing the sides of your head. Go against the grain, from the bottom to the top, until you have a uniform length all around. Don't worry about the top for now.

Step 2: Buzz the Sides

Now, switch to the #2 guard and start creating the fade. Start below the temple and go halfway up the side of your head. Use an upward motion to blend the shorter hair with the longer hair on top. Repeat on the other side.

Switch to the #3 guard and repeat the process, starting lower than before and going up to the temple. Again, use an upward motion to blend the hair. Repeat on the other side.

Finally, switch to the #4 guard and go just above the ear, using the same upward motion to blend the hair. Repeat on the other side.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Now that you have the fade on the sides, it's time to blend it with the top. Take off the guard from your clippers and use the bare blades to create a seamless transition between the sides and the top. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it with the scissors. Take your time and make sure it looks even.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

Next, use your trimmer to clean up the hairline around your ears and neck. Use the longest guard to avoid cutting too much hair. Then, go over the sides and back of your head with the trimmer to make sure it's all even.

Step 5: Clean Up

Finally, take a shower and wash your hair to get rid of any loose hair. Use a towel to dry your hair and style it as you like. Congratulations, you've just cut a bald fade at home!

Tips for Cutting a Bald Fade at Home

  • Take your time and don't rush. It's better to take longer and get it right than to make a mistake.
  • Use the mirror to check your work from different angles.
  • If you're not sure about a certain step, watch a tutorial video or ask someone for help.
  • Experiment with different guard sizes and techniques to find what works best for your hair.
  • Don't forget to oil and clean your clippers and trimmer after use.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut a bald fade on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can. Just make sure you're using the right guard sizes and techniques to avoid cutting too much hair.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your hair to be. As a general rule, you should cut your hair every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my own hair without a mirror?

A: It's not recommended, as you won't be able to see the back of your head and may end up with an uneven cut.

Hair Care After Cutting a Bald Fade

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking good. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling or breaking it.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your hairstyle and avoid split ends.
  • Protect your hair from sun damage by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

We hope this article has been helpful in teaching you how to cut a bald fade at home. Remember, practice makes perfect, and with time, you'll be able to master this hairstyle. Good luck!
