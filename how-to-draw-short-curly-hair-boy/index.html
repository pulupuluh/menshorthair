How to Draw Short Curly Hair Boy

Drawing short curly hair on a boy can be challenging, but with these simple steps, you can create a realistic and detailed drawing:

Step 1: Draw the Face

Start by drawing the shape of the boy's face. Make sure to add details such as the eyes, nose, and mouth. This will serve as your guide for the hair.

Step 2: Draw the Outline of the Hair

Using light pencil strokes, draw the outline of the hair. Curly hair tends to be voluminous and messy, so keep that in mind when drawing the outline.

Step 3: Add Texture to the Hair

Using small, circular strokes, add texture to the hair. This will give it a more realistic and detailed look.

Step 4: Define the Curls

Define the curls by drawing small, tight spirals. Keep in mind that curly hair tends to have more volume at the top, so make sure to add more curls in that area.

Step 5: Shade the Hair

Using a darker pencil, shade the hair. Start by shading the areas where the hair is the darkest, such as the roots and the areas where the curls are the tightest.

Step 6: Add Highlights

Using a white pencil, add highlights to the hair. This will give it a more dynamic and three-dimensional look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Let's break down the steps further:

Step 1: Draw the Face

Start by drawing a circle for the head. Draw a vertical line down the center and a horizontal line across the center. These lines will serve as your guide for the facial features. Add the eyes, nose, and mouth.

Step 2: Draw the Outline of the Hair

Using light pencil strokes, draw the outline of the hair. Keep in mind the shape of the boy's head and the direction in which the hair is flowing.

Step 3: Add Texture to the Hair

Using small, circular strokes, add texture to the hair. This will give it a more realistic and detailed look.

Step 4: Define the Curls

Define the curls by drawing small, tight spirals. Start by adding more curls at the top of the head and gradually decrease the number of curls as you move towards the bottom.

Step 5: Shade the Hair

Using a darker pencil, shade the areas where the hair is the darkest, such as the roots and the areas where the curls are the tightest.

Step 6: Add Highlights

Using a white pencil, add highlights to the hair. This will give it a more dynamic and three-dimensional look.

Tips for Drawing Short Curly Hair Boy

Here are some tips that will help you draw short curly hair boy more effectively:

  • Observe the hair carefully and take note of the direction in which it flows
  • Start with light pencil strokes and gradually add more detail as you go
  • Use small, circular strokes to add texture to the hair
  • Define the curls by drawing small, tight spirals
  • Use a darker pencil to shade the hair and a white pencil to add highlights

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I draw short curly hair boy if I can't draw curls?

A: Start by drawing loose, wavy lines. Gradually add more detail by drawing smaller, tighter spirals. Practice is key!

Q: How do I make the hair look more voluminous?

A: Add more curls at the top of the head and gradually decrease the number of curls as you move towards the bottom.

Q: How do I shade the hair?

A: Start by shading the areas where the hair is the darkest, such as the roots and the areas where the curls are the tightest. Use a darker pencil for this. Gradually add more shading as you move towards the lighter areas of the hair.

Hair Care Tips

Short curly hair requires special care to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair
  • Use a microfiber towel to dry the hair, as regular towels can cause frizz
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the curls
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of the hair

And there you have it! A step-by-step guide on how to draw short curly hair boy. With practice and patience, you can create a realistic and detailed drawing. Don't forget to take care of your own curly hair, too!
