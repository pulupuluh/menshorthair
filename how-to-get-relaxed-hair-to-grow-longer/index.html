How To Get Relaxed Hair To Grow Longer Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to grow your relaxed hair longer, it can be a bit challenging. Relaxed hair is more fragile and prone to breakage than natural hair, so it requires extra care and attention. However, with the right hair care routine and a few tips and tricks, you can get your relaxed hair to grow longer and healthier. In this post, we'll share some of the best ways to promote hair growth and keep your relaxed hair looking its best.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Get Relaxed Hair to Grow Longer

How to Get Relaxed Hair to Grow Longer

Here are some tips and tricks to help you get your relaxed hair to grow longer:

1. Keep Your Hair Moisturized

Relaxed hair is more prone to dryness, which can lead to breakage and damage. To keep your hair moisturized, make sure to use a leave-in conditioner or moisturizer after washing your hair. You can also use oils like coconut oil or argan oil to seal in moisture and keep your hair hydrated throughout the day.

2. Avoid Overprocessing Your Hair

Overprocessing your hair can lead to damage and breakage, which can prevent your hair from growing longer. Try to limit the amount of heat and chemicals you use on your hair, and make sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum before styling with heat tools.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help prevent breakage and damage, as brushes can be too harsh on relaxed hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently detangling any knots or tangles as you go.

4. Protect Your Hair at Night

When you sleep, your hair can become tangled and frizzy, which can lead to breakage and damage. To protect your hair at night, wrap it in a satin or silk scarf or bonnet. This will help keep your hair smooth and prevent it from rubbing against your pillowcase.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

Healthy hair starts from the inside out, so make sure to eat a well-balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. Foods like eggs, nuts, and leafy greens are great for promoting hair growth and keeping your hair healthy and strong.

Hair Care Tips for Relaxed Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips that can help promote hair growth and keep your relaxed hair looking its best:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on relaxed hair, so look for a sulfate-free shampoo that will gently cleanse your hair without stripping it of its natural oils.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair Weekly

Deep conditioning can help nourish and strengthen your hair, preventing breakage and damage. Try to deep condition your hair once a week, using a moisturizing conditioner or hair mask.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can limit hair growth. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, removing any damaged or split ends.

4. Limit Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to relaxed hair, so try to limit the amount of heat you use on your hair. If you do use heat tools, make sure to use a heat protectant and keep the temperature on the lowest setting possible.

FAQs About Growing Relaxed Hair

1. How Long Does It Take for Relaxed Hair to Grow?

On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. However, the rate of hair growth can vary depending on factors like genetics, age, and overall health.

2. Can Relaxed Hair Grow Past Shoulder Length?

Yes, relaxed hair can grow past shoulder length with the right care and attention. However, it may take longer to grow longer than it would for natural hair.

3. Can Relaxed Hair Grow Back After Being Damaged?

Yes, relaxed hair can grow back after being damaged. However, it may take time and patience to see results. To promote hair growth, make sure to keep your hair healthy and nourished with a healthy diet and proper hair care.

With these tips and tricks, you can get your relaxed hair to grow longer and healthier. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.
