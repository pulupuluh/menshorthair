How To Make Back Hair Straight Tips And Tricks

If you have back hair and you're wondering how to make it straight, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks to help you get the straight hair you're looking for. Here's what we'll cover:

How to Make Back Hair Straight

There are several ways to make back hair straight. Some people prefer to use heat styling tools like flat irons or blow dryers, while others opt for chemical treatments like relaxers or keratin treatments. Here are some popular methods:

1. Flat Iron

A flat iron is the most popular way to straighten hair. You can use it to straighten your back hair as well. All you need to do is apply a heat protectant spray to your hair, then use the flat iron to straighten small sections of your hair. Be careful not to burn yourself, and don't use too much heat as it can damage your hair.

2. Blow Dryer

A blow dryer can also be used to straighten back hair. You'll need a round brush to help you straighten your hair as you blow dry it. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair, then use the round brush to pull your hair straight as you blow dry it. Make sure you use the cool setting on your blow dryer to avoid damaging your hair.

3. Chemical Treatments

If you want a more permanent solution for straightening your back hair, you can try chemical treatments like relaxers or keratin treatments. Relaxers use chemicals to break down the protein bonds in your hair, making it straighter. Keratin treatments use a protein called keratin to smooth out your hair and make it straighter. These treatments should only be done by a professional, as they can be dangerous if not done correctly.

Step-by-step Guide

If you're using a flat iron or blow dryer to straighten your back hair, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for straight hair. This will help to remove any product buildup and make your hair easier to straighten.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant Spray

Spray a heat protectant spray onto your hair to protect it from the heat of the flat iron or blow dryer. Make sure you apply the spray evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

If you're using a blow dryer, use a round brush to pull your hair straight as you blow dry it. Make sure you use the cool setting on your blow dryer to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

If you're using a flat iron, start by straightening the bottom sections of your hair and work your way up. Make sure you don't use too much heat, as this can damage your hair. Use a comb to help you straighten each section of hair.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish off by applying a light hairspray to hold your hair in place. This will help to keep your hair straight throughout the day.

Tips for Making Back Hair Straight

Here are some tips to help you make your back hair straight:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Don't use too much heat when straightening your hair, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a comb to help you straighten each section of hair.
  • Use a round brush to help you straighten your hair as you blow dry it.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends.

FAQs

1. Can I use a flat iron on my back hair?

Yes, you can use a flat iron to straighten your back hair. Just be careful not to burn yourself, and don't use too much heat as it can damage your hair.

2. How often should I straighten my back hair?

You should only straighten your hair once or twice a week to avoid damaging it. Make sure you use a heat protectant spray and don't use too much heat when straightening your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Straight Hair

If you have straight hair, here are some hair care tips to keep it healthy and shiny:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for straight hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from getting tangled while you sleep.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your back hair straight and keep it healthy and shiny. Whether you prefer to use a flat iron, blow dryer, or chemical treatments, make sure you take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
