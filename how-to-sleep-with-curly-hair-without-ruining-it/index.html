How To Sleep With Curly Hair Without Ruining It

Written by Bardi Jul 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Having curly hair is a blessing and a curse. The lush, bouncy, and voluminous locks come with an extra set of challenges, especially when it comes to sleeping. Waking up with frizz, tangles, and flattened curls is a common nightmare for curly-haired people, but it doesn't have to be that way. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to sleep with curly hair without ruining it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Sleep with Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Sleep with Curly Hair

The first rule of sleeping with curly hair is to avoid sleeping with wet hair. Wet hair is more vulnerable to breakage, and the friction caused by rubbing against the pillow can lead to frizz and tangles. Dry your hair thoroughly before going to bed, or use a diffuser to speed up the process without damaging your curls.

The second rule is to protect your curls from friction. Friction can happen between your hair and the pillowcase, or between different sections of your hair. To minimize friction, switch to a satin or silk pillowcase, which is smoother and less likely to cause tangles and frizz. You can also wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet to keep your curls in place and prevent them from rubbing against each other.

The third rule is to avoid sleeping in one position all night. Sleeping in the same position can flatten your curls or create dents and creases. Try to alternate between sleeping on your back, side, and stomach to distribute the pressure and maintain the shape of your curls. You can also use a silk or satin scrunchie to loosely tie your hair up in a pineapple or bun to keep it out of the way and prevent it from getting squished.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to sleep with curly hair without ruining it:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and add moisture.
  3. Dry your hair with a diffuser or air-dry it until it's about 80% dry.
  4. Brush your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle it.
  5. Part your hair into sections and twist each section loosely to keep it separated.
  6. Wrap your head with a silk scarf or bonnet, or switch to a silk or satin pillowcase.
  7. Change your sleeping position throughout the night to prevent flattening and creasing.
  8. In the morning, remove the scarf or bonnet and gently shake out your curls.
  9. Refresh your curls with a spray bottle of water or a curl refresher product.
  10. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your bouncy, defined curls!

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you sleep with curly hair:

  • Avoid using cotton pillowcases or towels, as they can absorb moisture and cause frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton T-shirt to dry your hair, as they are gentler and less likely to cause breakage.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends and add shine.
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase or scarf, as it can also benefit your skin and prevent wrinkles.
  • Consider sleeping on a silk or satin sheet or blanket, as it can provide even more protection and comfort.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about sleeping with curly hair:

Q: Should I sleep with my hair up or down?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Sleeping with your hair up in a pineapple or bun can help maintain volume and prevent tangling, but it can also create creases and dents. Sleeping with your hair down or loosely tied up can prevent creasing, but it can also lead to more frizz and tangling.

Q: Can I sleep with wet hair if I don't have time to dry it?

A: It's not recommended, as wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage. If you have to sleep with wet hair, use a microfiber towel or cotton T-shirt to gently squeeze out the excess moisture, and avoid rubbing or pulling your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people with curly hair can go several days or even a week without washing, while others prefer to wash their hair every other day or every few days. Experiment with different schedules and see what works best for your hair and scalp.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Finally, here are some general tips for taking care of curly hair:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and sulfates, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioner or hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or blow dryers on high heat, as they can damage your curls and cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid using brushes or fine-tooth combs.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf, or using a leave-in conditioner with UV protection.

By following these tips and tricks, you can sleep with curly hair without ruining it and wake up to beautiful, defined curls every day. Remember to be gentle, patient, and experiment with different techniques until you find the perfect routine for your hair type and lifestyle.
