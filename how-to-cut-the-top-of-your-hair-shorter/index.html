How To Cut The Top Of Your Hair Shorter A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your long locks and looking for a change? Cutting the top of your hair shorter can give you a fresh, new look. But if you're not a professional hairstylist, cutting your own hair can be intimidating. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut the top of your hair shorter in easy-to-follow steps.

Description

Cutting the top of your hair shorter can be a great way to change up your look without making a drastic change. This technique works well for both men and women and can be done at home with the right tools and a little bit of patience. By following our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve a stylish, shorter haircut in no time.

How To Cut the Top of Your Hair Shorter

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties (optional)

Once you have everything you need, find a well-lit area to work in and a comfortable chair to sit in. It's also a good idea to put a towel or cape around your shoulders to catch any loose hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Before you start cutting, decide on the length you want your hair to be. It's a good idea to start with a longer length and work your way down if you're not sure. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to cut. Use a comb to create a part in the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, create a horizontal part from ear to ear. This will create four sections of hair.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with one of the front sections, comb your hair straight down and cut it to the desired length. Make sure to cut straight across and not at an angle. Work your way around your head, cutting each section to the same length.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

If you want a more natural look, use clippers to blend the sides of your hair with the top. Start with a longer guard and work your way down until the sides are the same length as the top. Remember to blend in a downward motion to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for evenness. Use a mirror to look at the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments. If you notice any uneven spots, trim them carefully with scissors.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down if you're not sure.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, not wet.
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors.
  • Take breaks if you need to rest your arms or hands.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with just scissors?

Yes, you can cut your hair with just scissors. However, using clippers can make the process easier and help you achieve a more even cut.

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so you may want to cut it a little longer than you actually want it to be.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Try to even out the sides as best you can and wait for your hair to grow back. If the mistake is really noticeable, you may want to go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. You should also get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting the top of your hair shorter can be a fun and easy way to change up your look. By following our step-by-step guide and using the right tools, you can achieve a stylish, shorter haircut at home. Remember to take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
