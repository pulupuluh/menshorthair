How To Stop Relaxed Hair Breakage Tips And Tricks

Description

Relaxing your hair can be a great way to achieve that sleek, straight look you've always wanted. However, it can be a double-edged sword as it can also lead to hair breakage. Hair breakage is a common problem among people with relaxed hair, and it can be caused by a variety of factors such as chemical damage, heat styling, and lack of proper hair care. If you're struggling with relaxed hair breakage, don't panic! There are several things you can do to prevent it and keep your hair healthy and strong.

How to Stop Relaxed Hair Breakage

Preventing relaxed hair breakage starts with understanding the causes. Chemical damage from relaxers, heat styling, and lack of proper hair care can all contribute to hair breakage. Here are some tips to help you stop relaxed hair breakage:

1. Avoid Chemical Damage

One of the main causes of hair breakage is chemical damage from relaxers. If you're using a relaxer, make sure to follow the instructions carefully and don't leave it on for too long. Overprocessing your hair can cause it to become weak and break. Limit the number of times you relax your hair, and consider using a milder relaxer or switching to a different hair straightening method.

2. Reduce Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling wands can also contribute to hair breakage. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and don't use them too often. Try to air dry your hair as much as possible and use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to reduce heat damage.

3. Practice Proper Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for preventing hair breakage. Make sure to moisturize your hair regularly with a deep conditioning treatment, and avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils. Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and don't brush your hair when it's wet as it's more prone to breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you prevent relaxed hair breakage:

Step 1: Limit the Use of Relaxers

Only use relaxers when necessary and follow the instructions carefully. Don't leave the relaxer on for too long, and don't process your hair too often.

Step 2: Use Heat Styling Tools Sparingly

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and make sure to use a heat protectant spray when you do. Try to air dry your hair as much as possible, and use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to reduce heat damage.

Step 3: Moisturize Your Hair Regularly

Moisturize your hair regularly with a deep conditioning treatment. Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils, and use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you stop relaxed hair breakage:

1. Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent your hair from drying out and breaking while you sleep. You can also wrap your hair in a satin or silk scarf before bed.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Opt for looser styles such as buns or braids.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote hair growth and prevent breakage. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about relaxed hair breakage:

1. Can I still relax my hair if I have breakage?

If you have severe breakage, it's best to avoid relaxing your hair until it's fully recovered. Once your hair is healthy again, you can resume relaxing it.

2. How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you prevent relaxed hair breakage:

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day.

2. Trim Your Ends Regularly

Trim your ends every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

3. Avoid Overprocessing Your Hair

Avoid overprocessing your hair with too many chemicals or heat styling tools.

By following these tips and tricks, you can prevent relaxed hair breakage and keep your hair healthy and strong. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to seek professional help if you're struggling with severe breakage.
