How Can Thyroid Cause Hair Loss

Written by Daniel Feb 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thyroid problems are one of the leading causes of hair loss. Hair loss is a common symptom of thyroid disorders, and it can be alarming for many people. If you're experiencing hair loss and are concerned that your thyroid may be to blame, this article will help you understand the connection between the two.

What is the Thyroid?

The thyroid is a butterfly-shaped gland located in the neck. It produces hormones that regulate the body's metabolism, which affects everything from heart rate to body temperature to hair growth. When the thyroid produces too much or too little of these hormones, it can cause a range of problems, including hair loss.

How Does Thyroid Cause Hair Loss?

Thyroid hormones play a crucial role in hair growth. When the thyroid produces too much or too little of these hormones, it can disrupt the normal growth cycle of hair follicles. This can cause hair to fall out prematurely or prevent new hair from growing, leading to thinning or bald patches.

Hypothyroidism and Hair Loss

Hypothyroidism, or an underactive thyroid, occurs when the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormones. This can cause a range of symptoms, including fatigue, weight gain, and hair loss. In hypothyroidism, hair loss is usually diffuse and affects the entire scalp rather than specific areas.

Hyperthyroidism and Hair Loss

Hyperthyroidism, or an overactive thyroid, occurs when the thyroid gland produces too much thyroid hormone. This can cause symptoms such as weight loss, anxiety, and hair loss. In hyperthyroidism, hair loss is usually patchy and affects specific areas of the scalp rather than the entire scalp.

How Can I Treat Thyroid-Related Hair Loss?

The first step in treating thyroid-related hair loss is to get your thyroid levels checked by a doctor. If your thyroid is not functioning properly, your doctor may prescribe medication to regulate your thyroid hormones. This can help to restore normal hair growth.

In addition to medication, there are several things you can do to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. These include:

Eating a Balanced Diet

A healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough iron, zinc, and biotin, which are all important for hair health.

Reducing Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to find ways to manage stress in your life. This could include practicing relaxation techniques such as meditation or yoga, or simply taking time to do things you enjoy.

Taking Care of Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

FAQ About Thyroid and Hair Loss

Can Thyroid Medication Cause Hair Loss?

While thyroid medication can help to restore normal hair growth in people with thyroid problems, it can also cause hair loss in some cases. This is usually temporary and will resolve once your body adjusts to the medication.

Can Thyroid Hair Loss Be Reversed?

If your hair loss is due to a thyroid problem, it can often be reversed with proper treatment. Once your thyroid levels are back to normal, your hair should begin to grow back.

Is There a Connection Between Thyroid and Female Pattern Hair Loss?

Female pattern hair loss is a genetic condition that affects many women. While thyroid problems can contribute to hair loss in women, they are not the primary cause of female pattern hair loss.

Conclusion

Thyroid problems can cause hair loss, but with proper treatment, this can often be reversed. If you're experiencing hair loss and are concerned that your thyroid may be to blame, talk to your doctor about getting your thyroid levels checked. With the right treatment and a healthy lifestyle, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
