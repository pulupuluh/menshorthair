Mid Length Haircuts With Long Bangs The Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of your long hair but not ready to go short? A mid length haircut with long bangs might just be the perfect solution for you! This versatile style can be dressed up or down, and the long bangs add a touch of playfulness and femininity to any look. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about mid length haircuts with long bangs, including how to get the perfect cut, styling tips, and hair care advice.

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Mid Length Haircuts with Long Bangs

How to Get the Perfect Mid Length Haircut with Long Bangs

The first step to getting a mid length haircut with long bangs is to find a stylist who specializes in this type of cut. Look for someone with experience and a portfolio of similar cuts that you like. When you sit down for your consultation, be clear about the length you want for both your hair and your bangs. It's also a good idea to bring in pictures of haircuts you like to give your stylist a better idea of what you're looking for.

Once you and your stylist have agreed on the cut, it's time to get to work! Your stylist will likely start by washing and conditioning your hair to make sure it's clean and smooth. Then, they'll section your hair and start cutting. The length of your hair and bangs will depend on your personal preference, but a good rule of thumb is to have the bangs hit just above your eyebrows and the length of the hair hit at or just below your collarbone.

After the cut is finished, your stylist may blow dry and style your hair to give you an idea of what the final look will be. If you're happy with the cut, you're good to go!

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Mid Length Haircuts with Long Bangs

Styling mid length haircuts with long bangs is easy and fun! Here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect look:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type. If you have fine hair, look for volumizing products. If you have curly hair, look for products that enhance your curls.
  2. Towel dry your hair to remove excess water.
  3. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from hot tools.
  4. Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and smoothness.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or straightener to create loose waves or curls. You can also leave your hair straight if you prefer.
  6. Next, style your bangs. You can either leave them straight and sleek or use a curling iron to add a gentle curve.
  7. Finish off your look with a spritz of hairspray to hold everything in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Mid Length Haircut with Long Bangs

Here are some tips to keep your mid length haircut with long bangs looking great:

  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day to prevent damage.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair type.

FAQs About Mid Length Haircuts with Long Bangs

1. Will a mid length haircut with long bangs work for my face shape?

Mid length haircuts with long bangs can be flattering on a variety of face shapes, including oval, round, and heart-shaped. Talk to your stylist about the best length and style for your face shape.

2. How often should I get my mid length haircut with long bangs trimmed?

It's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

3. Can I still wear my hair up with a mid length haircut with long bangs?

Absolutely! You can wear your hair up in a ponytail, bun, or braid with a mid length haircut with long bangs. Just make sure to leave your bangs out for a playful touch.

Hair Care Tips for Mid Length Haircuts with Long Bangs

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your mid length haircut with long bangs looking its best:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent stripping your scalp of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, which can cause damage and dryness.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock a mid length haircut with long bangs with confidence and style. Whether you're headed to work or out for a night on the town, this versatile look is sure to turn heads!
