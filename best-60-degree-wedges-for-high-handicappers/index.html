If you're a high handicapper looking to improve your short game, investing in a good 60 degree wedge is a great place to start. What is a 60 Degree Wedge?

A 60 degree wedge is a type of club used in golf for short approach shots and shots around the green. It has a loft angle of 60 degrees, making it one of the highest lofted clubs in a golfer's bag. This high loft angle allows the ball to get up in the air quickly and land softly, making it ideal for shots that need to clear obstacles or stop quickly on the green.

How to Choose the Best 60 Degree Wedge for High Handicappers

When choosing a 60 degree wedge, there are several factors to consider, including:

1. Loft Angle

The loft angle of your wedge will determine how high the ball will go and how quickly it will stop on the green. For high handicappers, a 60 degree wedge is a good choice as it provides plenty of loft for getting the ball up in the air quickly.

2. Shaft Length

The length of the shaft can affect the feel and control of the club. A shorter shaft can provide more control, but may sacrifice some distance. High handicappers may benefit from a slightly longer shaft for added distance and forgiveness.

3. Weight and Balance

The weight and balance of the club can also affect its feel and performance. A club that is too heavy or poorly balanced can be difficult to control, while a well-balanced club can provide better accuracy and consistency.

Top 5 Best 60 Degree Wedges for High Handicappers

1. Cleveland CBX 2 Wedge

The Cleveland CBX 2 Wedge is a great option for high handicappers looking for forgiveness and versatility. It features a wide sole design and a hollow cavity back, which helps to redistribute weight and improve forgiveness on off-center hits. The club also features a Rotex face, which provides plenty of spin and control on short shots around the green.

2. Callaway Mack Daddy CB Wedge

The Callaway Mack Daddy CB Wedge is another great option for high handicappers. It features a cavity back design and a larger sweet spot, which helps to improve forgiveness and accuracy on off-center hits. The club also features Callaway's proprietary JAWS grooves, which provide plenty of spin and control on short shots.

3. Ping Glide 3.0 Wedge

The Ping Glide 3.0 Wedge is a versatile club that can be customized to fit a player's specific needs. It features a multi-material construction and a precision-milled face, which provides plenty of spin and control on short shots. The club also features Ping's customizable sole grinds, which allow players to fine-tune the club's performance based on their swing and playing conditions.

4. TaylorMade Hi-Toe Big Foot Wedge

The TaylorMade Hi-Toe Big Foot Wedge is a unique club that features an oversized sole and a high toe design. The club also features TaylorMade's ZTP-17 grooves, which provide plenty of spin and control on short shots.

5. Mizuno T20 Wedge

The Mizuno T20 Wedge is a high-quality club that offers plenty of spin and control on short shots. The club also features Mizuno's Grain Flow Forged construction, which provides a soft and responsive feel at impact.

Tips for Using a 60 Degree Wedge

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when using a 60 degree wedge:

1. Practice Your Technique

A 60 degree wedge requires a slightly different technique than other clubs in your bag. Practice hitting different types of shots with your wedge, such as pitch shots, chip shots, and bunker shots, to improve your technique and confidence.

2. Focus on Your Setup

Make sure your setup is consistent and comfortable. This will help you make solid contact with the ball and improve your accuracy and distance control.

3. Choose the Right Shot

When using a 60 degree wedge, it's important to choose the right shot for the situation. Consider factors such as the lie of the ball, the distance to the green, and any hazards or obstacles in your way.

FAQs

What is the difference between a 56 degree wedge and a 60 degree wedge?

A 56 degree wedge has a lower loft angle than a 60 degree wedge, typically around 10 degrees lower. This makes it better suited for longer shots that require more distance and less loft.

Can a high handicapper benefit from using a 60 degree wedge?

Absolutely! A 60 degree wedge can be a great tool for high handicappers looking to improve their short game. The high loft angle provides plenty of height and spin, making it easier to get the ball up in the air and stop it quickly on the green.

Final Thoughts

Investing in a good 60 degree wedge can be a game-changer for high handicappers looking to improve their short game. Consider factors such as loft angle, shaft length, and weight and balance when choosing a wedge that's right for you. And don't forget to practice your technique and choose the right shot for the situation!
