How To Get Rid Of Curly Hair Frizz Tips And Tricks

Written by William Mar 03, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that frizz can be a big problem. Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is raised, allowing moisture to enter and cause the hair to swell. This can be caused by a number of factors, including humidity, dryness, and damage. But don’t worry, there are plenty of ways to tame your frizz and keep your curls looking smooth and defined. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks for how to get rid of curly hair frizz.

How to Get Rid of Curly Hair Frizz

Step 1: Start with a Good Shampoo and Conditioner

The foundation of good hair care is a good shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and that contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil. Avoid products that contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-in Conditioner

After you’ve washed and conditioned your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like jojoba oil, argan oil, or glycerin, which can help smooth and hydrate your curls.

Step 3: Use a Microfiber Towel or T-shirt to Dry Your Hair

When it comes to drying your hair, avoid using a regular towel, which can create friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the water out of your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Product

To help define your curls and prevent frizz, apply a styling product like a curl cream, gel, or mousse. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or aloe vera.

Step 5: Avoid Touching Your Hair

Once you’ve styled your hair, avoid touching it as much as possible. The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to frizz. If you need to adjust your curls, use your fingers to gently reshape them.

Tips for Preventing Curly Hair Frizz

In addition to the steps above, there are a few other things you can do to prevent curly hair frizz:

Tip 1: Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can create friction and lead to frizz. To prevent this, consider sleeping on a satin pillowcase, which is gentler on your hair.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

Tip 3: Use a Diffuser Attachment

If you’re using a blow dryer to dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat more evenly and prevent frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can I use hair oil to prevent frizz?

A: Yes, hair oil can be a great way to prevent frizz. Look for oils that are lightweight and won’t weigh down your hair, like argan oil, jojoba oil, or grapeseed oil.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. Generally, it’s best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to the tips above, there are a few other things you can do to keep your curly hair healthy and beautiful:

Tip 1: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Tip 2: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails can put stress on your hair and lead to breakage. If you do wear your hair in a tight style, make sure to give your hair a break every once in a while.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Just like your skin, your hair can be damaged by the sun’s UV rays. To protect your hair, wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

With these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to curly hair frizz and hello to beautiful, defined curls. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.
