How To Thin Out A Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by William May 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to thin out your pixie cut? Thinning out a pixie cut can help give it more texture and movement, and make it easier to style. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to thin out your pixie cut, as well as provide tips and hair care advice to keep your new look looking great.

How to Thin Out a Pixie Cut

Thinning out a pixie cut can be done at home or at a salon. Here are the steps to thin out your pixie cut:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start thinning out your pixie cut, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin out your hair, and prevent any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips or elastics. This will make it easier to thin out your hair evenly.

Step 3: Thin out your hair

Use thinning scissors or a razor to thin out your hair. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, taking small sections of hair at a time.

Thin out your hair by cutting into it at an angle. This will create texture and movement in your hair. Be careful not to thin out your hair too much, as this can make it look stringy and damaged.

Step 4: Check your work

After you’ve thinned out your hair, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, and continue thinning out your hair until you achieve your desired look.

Step 5: Style your hair

Once you’ve thinned out your hair, style it as desired. Use a texturizing spray or pomade to give your hair more texture and movement.

Tips for Thinning Out a Pixie Cut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out a pixie cut:

  • Start with a little hair and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair, but you can’t put it back once it’s cut!
  • Be careful not to thin out your hair too much, as this can make it look stringy and damaged.
  • Use thinning scissors or a razor, as regular scissors can create blunt lines in your hair.
  • Always cut into your hair at an angle, rather than straight across.

FAQ: Thinning Out a Pixie Cut

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out a pixie cut:

Can I thin out my pixie cut at home?

Yes, you can thin out your pixie cut at home. However, it’s important to use the right tools and technique to avoid damaging your hair.

What kind of scissors should I use to thin out my hair?

You should use thinning scissors or a razor to thin out your hair. Regular scissors can create blunt lines in your hair.

How much hair should I thin out?

Start with a little hair and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair, but you can’t put it back once it’s cut!

Hair Care Tips for a Thinned Out Pixie Cut

Here are some hair care tips to keep your thinned out pixie cut looking great:

  • Use a texturizing spray or pomade to give your hair more texture and movement.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools.

Conclusion

Thinning out a pixie cut can give it more texture and movement, and make it easier to style. By following these steps and hair care tips, you can achieve a thinned out pixie cut that looks great and is easy to maintain.
