How to Style My Short Natural Black Hair

Styling short natural black hair can be a challenge, but it is not impossible. There are many styles you can try, from twist-outs to braids. Here are some popular styles:

  • Twist-outs
  • Braid-outs
  • Finger coils
  • Bantu knots
  • Afro

Each of these styles can be achieved with the right technique and products. Keep reading for a step by step guide on how to achieve each style.

Step by Step Guide

Twist-outs

Twist-outs are a popular style for short natural black hair. Here's how to achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly.
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  6. Untwist each section of hair and fluff to create volume.
  7. Use a hair pick to shape your hair.

Braid-outs

Braid-outs are another popular style for short natural black hair. Here's how to achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Braid each section of hair tightly.
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  6. Unbraid each section of hair and fluff to create volume.
  7. Use a hair pick to shape your hair.

Finger Coils

Finger coils are a great style for short natural black hair. Here's how to achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly around your finger.
  5. Continue this process until all of your hair is twisted.
  6. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  7. Untwist each section of hair and fluff to create volume.
  8. Use a hair pick to shape your hair.

Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a fun and stylish way to wear short natural black hair. Here's how to achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly.
  5. Wrap the twisted hair around itself to create a knot.
  6. Secure the knot with a hair tie or bobby pin.
  7. Repeat this process until all of your hair is in knots.
  8. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  9. Untie each knot and fluff to create volume.
  10. Use a hair pick to shape your hair.

Afro

An afro is a classic style for short natural black hair. Here's how to achieve this look:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Use a hair pick to shape your hair into an afro.

Tips for Styling Short Natural Black Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect style for your short natural black hair:

  • Use products that are specifically designed for natural hair.
  • Don't use too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my short natural black hair?

You should wash your hair at least once a week, but you can wash it more often if you feel that it is necessary.

Can I use heat on my short natural black hair?

Yes, you can use heat on your hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant and don't use too much heat, as this can cause damage to your hair.

What should I do if my hair is dry and brittle?

You should moisturize your hair regularly and avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.

Hair Care for Short Natural Black Hair

Here are some tips for keeping your short natural black hair healthy:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Wear protective styles to protect your hair from damage.

Now that you know how to style your short natural black hair, it's time to try out some new looks. Whether you're going for a twist-out, braid-out, or afro, we hope these tips and tricks help you achieve the perfect style for your hair.
