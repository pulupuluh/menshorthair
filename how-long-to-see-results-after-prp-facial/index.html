How Long To See Results After Prp Facial

Written by Frank Jul 04, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're considering getting a PRP facial, you might be wondering how long it takes to see results. PRP, or platelet-rich plasma, facials are a popular cosmetic treatment that uses your own blood plasma to rejuvenate your skin. It can help reduce the appearance of fine lines, wrinkles, and acne scars, and give you a more youthful, glowing complexion.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

A PRP facial involves drawing a small amount of blood from your arm and spinning it in a centrifuge to separate the plasma from the red blood cells. The plasma is then injected into your face using tiny needles, where it stimulates collagen production and promotes cell renewal. This process can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the size of the treatment area.

After the treatment, you might experience some redness and swelling, but this should subside within a few hours to a few days. You should also avoid sun exposure and strenuous exercise for a few days after the treatment to allow your skin to heal.

How to

If you're interested in getting a PRP facial, the first step is to find a reputable provider in your area. Look for a provider who has experience with PRP facials and who uses sterile equipment and techniques to ensure your safety.

Once you've found a provider, you'll schedule a consultation to discuss your goals and expectations for the treatment. Your provider will also evaluate your skin and determine whether you're a good candidate for the treatment.

If you decide to move forward with the treatment, you'll schedule an appointment for the procedure. On the day of the procedure, you'll arrive at the provider's office and the procedure will begin.

Step by Step

The PRP facial procedure typically involves the following steps:

  1. A small amount of blood is drawn from your arm.
  2. The blood is placed in a centrifuge to separate the plasma from the red blood cells.
  3. The plasma is injected into your face using tiny needles.
  4. The procedure is repeated in different areas of your face as needed.
  5. You'll rest for a few minutes to allow the plasma to absorb into your skin.
  6. You'll be given aftercare instructions and any necessary medications.

Tips

To get the best results from your PRP facial, it's important to follow your provider's aftercare instructions carefully. This may include:

  • Avoiding sun exposure for a few days
  • Avoiding strenuous exercise for a few days
  • Using gentle skincare products that won't irritate your skin
  • Drinking plenty of water to keep your skin hydrated
  • Avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, which can damage your skin

FAQ

How long does it take to see results after a PRP facial?

Most people start to see results within a few weeks of the treatment, but it can take up to three months to see the full effects.

How long do the results of a PRP facial last?

The results of a PRP facial can last up to a year, but this can vary depending on your age, skin type, and lifestyle factors.

Is a PRP facial painful?

Most people experience mild discomfort during the procedure, but it's usually well-tolerated. Your provider may apply a numbing cream to your face before the procedure to reduce any discomfort.

Hair Care

PRP treatments aren't just for the face – they can also be used to stimulate hair growth. If you're experiencing hair loss or thinning, a PRP hair treatment might be a good option for you. The treatment involves injecting PRP into the scalp, where it promotes hair growth and strengthens hair follicles.

Like with the facial treatment, it can take a few months to see results from a PRP hair treatment. You may need several treatments spaced a few months apart to get the best results.

Overall, PRP facials and hair treatments are safe and effective ways to rejuvenate your skin and hair. If you're interested in these treatments, talk to a reputable provider in your area to learn more.
