How To Style Your Hair Like 60S

Written by Oliver May 13, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you a big fan of the retro look? Do you want to add some vintage style to your hair? Well, you’re in luck because we’re here to help you achieve that 60s-inspired hairdo. Whether you’re looking to create a bouffant or a beehive, we’ve got you covered. In this post, we’ll take you through the steps to achieve that iconic 60s look.

Description

The 60s was a time of revolution, a time when people broke free from the traditional norms and embraced a new way of living. The fashion and beauty industry also saw a significant shift during this time, with big hair becoming a popular trend. Women styled their hair in bouffants, beehives, and even pixie cuts. The 60s hairdos were all about volume and texture, and they have remained timeless to this day.

How to

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

The first step is to ensure that your hair is clean and dry. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to create a good base for your style. Towel dry your hair and use a blow dryer to create volume at the roots.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair into three parts: the crown, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips to secure the sections out of the way.

Step 3: Tease the crown

Take the crown section and backcomb it to create volume. Use a fine-toothed comb and gently tease the hair at the roots. Spray some hairspray to hold the volume in place.

Step 4: Create the beehive

To create a beehive, take the teased crown section and smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section. Pin the hair in place with bobby pins. Then, take the sides of your hair and sweep them towards the back, securing them with bobby pins. Use some more hairspray to keep the style in place.

Step 5: Add some curls

The 60s hairstyle was all about curls, so use a curling wand to add some curls to the rest of your hair. Curl the hair away from your face, and make sure to leave the ends straight for a more natural look.

Tips

- Use a volumizing mousse to add extra volume to your hair
- Don’t over-tease your hair; you want to create volume, not damage your hair
- Use bobby pins that match your hair color to hide them in your hair
- Use a hairspray with a strong hold to keep your style in place all day long

FAQ

Q: Can I achieve the 60s look with short hair?

A: Absolutely! The 60s was all about experimenting with different styles, so even if you have short hair, you can still achieve a vintage look. Try a pixie cut with a side-swept fringe for a 60s-inspired look.

Q: Do I need to use heat styling tools to achieve the 60s look?

A: While using a curling wand can help you achieve those iconic 60s curls, you can also achieve the look without heat styling tools. Try setting your hair in rollers overnight for a more natural look.

Hair Care

When styling your hair, it’s important to take care of it to prevent damage. Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools, and try not to use them too often. Use a hydrating hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.

Now that you know how to achieve the 60s hairdo, you’re ready to rock that retro look. Remember to have fun and experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
