How To Cut Long Thin Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have long thin curly hair, you may be wondering how to cut it properly without damaging your curls. Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut that complements your natural curls. In this guide, we will show you how to cut long thin curly hair step-by-step, as well as offer tips and advice to ensure your curls look their best.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need to make sure you have the right tools. Here are the essential tools you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Comb or brush

You may also want to invest in a pair of hair-cutting shears, which are specifically designed for cutting hair and will give you a cleaner cut.

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, so it's best to cut your hair when it's dry.

Once your hair is dry, comb or brush it to remove any tangles or knots. You may also want to style your hair in its natural curl pattern so you can see how the curls fall.

Sectioning Your Hair

To make cutting your hair easier, it's best to section it off into smaller, manageable sections. Use hair clips or ties to separate your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back.

Starting at the back, take one section of hair and twist it tightly. This will help you see the length of the hair and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much off.

Cutting Your Hair

Using your sharp scissors or hair-cutting shears, start cutting your hair in small sections. It's best to start with small sections and gradually work your way up to larger sections.

When cutting your hair, it's important to cut in a downward motion rather than across. This will help you maintain the natural shape of your curls and prevent them from frizzing.

When you reach the front sections of your hair, be sure to leave some length around your face. This will help frame your face and give you a more flattering look.

Finishing Touches

Once you have finished cutting your hair, remove the clips or ties and shake your head to see how your curls fall. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them carefully.

If you want to add some layers to your hair, you can do so by taking small sections and cutting them at an angle. This will create a natural layered effect and give your hair more volume.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair can be challenging, but these tips can help make the process easier:

  • Start with small sections and work your way up to larger sections
  • Cut in a downward motion, rather than across
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors
  • Leave some length around your face for a flattering look
  • Don't cut your hair too short, as it may shrink up when it dries

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your curls looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

To keep your curly hair looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz

With these tips and techniques, you can cut your long thin curly hair with confidence and achieve a great haircut that complements your natural curls.
