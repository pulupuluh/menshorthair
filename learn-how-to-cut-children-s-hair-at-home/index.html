Learn How To Cut Children s Hair At Home

Written by Bardi Apr 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a parent, you know how expensive it can be to take your child to a hair salon every few weeks. But with a little bit of practice and patience, you can learn how to cut children's hair at home. Not only will you save money, but you'll also have the satisfaction of knowing that you did it yourself. In this article, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut your child's hair, along with some tips and frequently asked questions.

How to Cut Children's Hair at Home

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clipper with different guard sizes
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Towels

Follow these steps to cut your child's hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing your child's hair and letting it dry completely. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the hair is clean and free of any tangles.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Using the comb, divide the hair into sections. Start at the back of the head and work your way forward. Use a clip to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair at the back of the head, using the clipper with the appropriate guard size. Work your way up the head, using the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly. Be sure to trim the hair around the ears and neckline carefully.

Step 4: Trim the Top of the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the sides and back of the head, move on to the top of the head. Use scissors to trim the hair evenly, cutting it at a slight angle to create layers.

Step 5: Finish with the Bangs

Finally, trim the bangs. Use scissors to cut them straight across or at an angle, depending on the desired look.

Tips for Cutting Children's Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting your child's hair:

  • Work slowly and carefully, making small snips rather than big cuts.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it at an even length.
  • Keep the hair wet while cutting, as it's easier to manage and cut evenly.
  • Use a towel around the child's neck to catch any loose hair.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes restless or uncomfortable.

FAQ about Cutting Children's Hair at Home

How often should I cut my child's hair?

Most children need a haircut every 4-6 weeks, depending on how fast their hair grows.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. It's just hair, and it will grow back. You can always take your child to a salon to have it fixed.

What if my child won't sit still?

Try distracting your child with a favorite toy or video. You can also cut their hair while they're watching TV or a movie.

Hair Care for Children

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling. Trim the hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy.

Learning how to cut children's hair at home is a great way to save money and bond with your child. With a little bit of practice and patience, you can become an expert hair cutter in no time!
