How To Cut Your Hair Straight Across Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 11, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of shelling out money for haircuts every few weeks? With a little patience and practice, you can learn how to cut your hair straight across yourself at home. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know, from how to prepare your hair to the best tools and techniques for achieving a professional-looking cut.

  • How to Cut Your Hair Straight Across Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Your Hair Straight Across Yourself

Cutting your hair straight across yourself may seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it’s a relatively simple process. Here are the basic steps:

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Before you begin cutting, make sure your hair is clean and free of any styling products. It’s also best to cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in an uneven cut.

2. Section your hair.

Use clips to separate your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

3. Decide on your length.

Determine how much length you want to take off and mark it with a comb or your fingers. Remember to start conservatively – you can always cut more later.

4. Cut straight across.

Using sharp scissors, cut straight across each section of hair, starting in the back and working your way forward. Make sure to keep the sections of hair even and parallel to the ground.

5. Check for evenness.

Once you’ve finished cutting, check your hair in the mirror to ensure it’s even. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them until your hair is perfectly straight across.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let’s dive into a more detailed, step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary supplies on hand. You’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clips
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin cutting. Wet hair can appear longer and may result in an uneven cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use clips to separate your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. This will make it easier to cut your hair straight across.

Step 4: Determine Your Length

Decide how much length you want to take off and mark it with a comb or your fingers. Remember to start conservatively – you can always cut more later.

Step 5: Cut Straight Across

Using sharp scissors, cut straight across each section of hair, starting in the back and working your way forward. Make sure to keep the sections of hair even and parallel to the ground.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you’ve finished cutting, check your hair in the mirror to ensure it’s even. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them until your hair is perfectly straight across.

Tips for Achieving a Great Cut

Here are a few tips to help you achieve a professional-looking cut:

  • Invest in quality scissors – dull scissors can result in a jagged cut.
  • Start with a conservative length and work your way up – it’s easier to cut more hair than to add it back.
  • Don’t rush – take your time and make sure each section of hair is even.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your hair straight across yourself:

Is it hard to cut your hair straight across yourself?

While it may seem daunting at first, cutting your hair straight across yourself is a relatively simple process with the right tools and technique.

What scissors are best for cutting hair?

Sharp, high-quality scissors are best for cutting hair. Avoid using dull scissors, as they can result in a jagged cut.

How can I make sure my hair is even?

Use clips to separate your hair into sections, and cut each section straight across, making sure to keep the sections even and parallel to the ground. Check your hair in the mirror to ensure it’s even.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair straight across yourself, make sure to care for your hair properly to maintain its health and appearance. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a heat protectant when using hot styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape.

Conclusion

Cutting your hair straight across yourself can save you time and money in the long run. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to invest in quality tools, take your time, and start conservatively – you can always cut more later.
