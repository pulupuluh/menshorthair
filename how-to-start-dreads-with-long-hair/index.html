Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are created by twisting and knotting hair until it forms long, ropelike strands. While many people start dreads with short hair, it is possible to start dreads with long hair as well. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with long hair and offer tips on how to care for them.

How to Start Dreads with Long Hair

Starting dreads with long hair can be a bit more challenging than with short hair, but it is still possible. The process involves sectioning the hair and twisting it until it forms knots. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before starting the dreadlocks process, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and a deep conditioner to moisturize your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a rat-tail comb, section your hair into even parts. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be. For thicker dreads, section your hair into larger parts. For thinner dreads, section your hair into smaller parts.

Step 3: Twist and Knot Your Hair

Take one section of hair and twist it until it forms a tight rope. Then, knot the twisted hair to create a dreadlock. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreadlocks.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with long hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Condition your hair with a deep conditioner to moisturize it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a rat-tail comb, section your hair into even parts. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Apply Locking Gel

Apply locking gel to each section of hair. This will help hold the twists in place and encourage the hair to knot.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

Take one section of hair and twist it until it forms a tight rope. Use your fingers to smooth out any bumps or lumps in the twist.

Step 5: Knot Your Hair

Once the hair is twisted, knot it to create a dreadlock. To do this, wrap the twisted hair around itself until it forms a knot. Use a small rubber band or hair tie to secure the knot in place.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Repeat the twisting and knotting process with each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreadlocks.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Long Hair

Starting dreads with long hair can be a time-consuming process, but it is worth it in the end. Here are some tips to help make the process a bit easier: - Be patient. It can take several months for your dreads to fully form. - Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin bonnet or scarf. - Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair and cause buildup. - Use a crochet hook to maintain your dreadlocks and keep them looking neat. - Don't worry if your dreads look messy or uneven at first. They will eventually even out and look more uniform with time.

FAQs

Can you start dreads with long hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with long hair. The process involves sectioning the hair and twisting it until it forms knots.

How long does it take to start dreads with long hair?

It can take several months for your dreads to fully form, depending on the length and texture of your hair.

What products do I need to start dreads with long hair?

You will need a clarifying shampoo, a deep conditioner, locking gel, and a small rubber band or hair tie.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once your dreads are formed, it is important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy dreadlocks: - Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo. - Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your dreads moisturized. - Avoid using heavy products that can cause buildup. - Use a crochet hook to maintain your dreads and keep them looking neat. - Protect your dreads at night by wearing a silk or satin bonnet or scarf. Starting dreads with long hair may seem daunting at first, but with patience and persistence, you can achieve the look you want. Follow these steps and tips to start your journey to beautiful, healthy dreadlocks.
