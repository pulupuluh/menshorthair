How To Cut Your Own Hair Man Bun A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 23, 2023 · 11 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new look, you might be considering a man bun. But before you can rock this stylish hairstyle, you'll need to learn how to cut your own hair man bun. Don't worry, it's easier than you might think! Follow this step-by-step guide and you'll be sporting a trendy man bun in no time.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Man Bun

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're trying to achieve a specific style like a man bun. But with the right tools and techniques, you can do it yourself at home. Here are the basic steps:

1. Gather Your Tools

The first step is to gather all the tools you'll need. You'll need a pair of hair clippers, a pair of scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Make sure your clippers have a variety of guard sizes so you can achieve the length you want.

2. Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to work with and ensure you get a more accurate cut.

3. Decide on Your Style

Before you start cutting, decide on the style you want. Do you want a high or low man bun? Do you want a fade or a more even cut? Look at pictures for inspiration and decide on the length you want for the top and sides of your hair.

4. Start with the Sides

Start by using your clippers with the appropriate guard size to trim the sides of your hair. Use the longest guard size first and work your way down until you achieve the length you want. Remember, you can always take more off, but you can't put it back on!

5. Cut the Top

Next, use your scissors to trim the top of your hair to the desired length. Start with small cuts and take your time to ensure you get an even cut. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

6. Blend the Sides and Top

Use your clippers without a guard to blend the sides and top of your hair. This will create a seamless transition between the two lengths.

7. Cut the Back

Use your mirror to trim the hair at the back of your head. This can be tricky, so take your time and use small cuts until you achieve the desired length.

8. Style Your Man Bun

Once you've achieved the desired length and shape, it's time to style your man bun. Use your comb to gather your hair at the crown of your head and tie it into a bun. Use a hair tie to secure it in place.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect man bun:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use small cuts and take your time to ensure an even cut.
  • Don't cut too much off the top at once. It's better to take small cuts and check your progress frequently.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure it's even.
  • Invest in quality tools to make the process easier.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair man bun without clippers?

A: It's possible, but it will be more difficult to achieve an even cut. Clippers make the process easier and more accurate.

Q: How often should I cut my own hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your style. Most people cut their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Should I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: It's generally easier to cut wet hair, but make sure it's completely dry before you start styling your man bun.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Once you've cut your hair and styled your man bun, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain your new look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools like flat irons or blow dryers, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain your style.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair man bun, you can rock this stylish look with confidence. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these simple steps. Happy cutting!
