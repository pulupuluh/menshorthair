How To Cut A Comb Over Fade Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 26, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re looking to save some money and cut your own hair, a comb over fade is a popular and stylish choice. It’s a versatile haircut that works for many face shapes and hair types. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps to achieve a professional-looking comb over fade at home.

Description

A comb over fade is a classic hairstyle that has been around for decades. It’s a perfect blend of a traditional comb over and a fade. The style features short hair on the sides that gradually fades into longer hair on top. The hair on top is then combed over to one side.

How to Cut a Comb Over Fade Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you’ll need to gather your tools. You’ll need a good pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to invest in a handheld mirror to help you see the back of your head.

Step 2: Start with the Sides

Begin by using the clippers to trim the hair on the sides of your head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to achieve a fade effect. Use the comb to blend the hair between the different guard lengths. Be sure to take your time and make small, even strokes.

Step 3: Trim the Top

Next, use the scissors to trim the hair on top of your head. Start by cutting the hair to the desired length. You can then use the comb to lift the hair and trim any uneven sections.

Step 4: Create the Part

Decide which side you want to comb your hair over to and use the comb to create a part. The part should be about an inch above your temple and run straight back to the crown of your head.

Step 5: Comb Over the Hair

Use the comb to comb the longer hair on top of your head over to one side. You may want to use a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to help hold the hair in place.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Use the scissors to blend the hair between the top and sides of your head. Make sure to take your time and make small, even cuts. You want to achieve a smooth and natural blend.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use the clippers to clean up the edges around your ears and the back of your neck. You can also use the scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Tips

- Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard when cutting the sides to achieve a fade effect.
- Take your time and make small, even cuts.
- Use a handheld mirror to help you see the back of your head.
- Invest in a good pair of clippers and scissors.
- Use a styling product to help hold the hair in place.

FAQ

Q: Can I achieve a comb over fade with curly hair?
A: Yes, a comb over fade works with curly hair. However, it may require more maintenance to keep the curls in place.

Q: How often should I trim my comb over fade?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows. Generally, you should trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style.

Hair Care

To keep your comb over fade looking great, you should wash it regularly with a good shampoo and conditioner. Use a styling product to hold the hair in place and avoid over-styling. You should also trim your hair regularly to maintain the style.

With these simple steps, you can achieve a professional-looking comb over fade at home. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the tips for best results.
