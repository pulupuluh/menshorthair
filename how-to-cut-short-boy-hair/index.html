How To Cut Short Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 05, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you a parent looking to save money on haircuts for your son? Or are you a hairstylist looking to learn how to cut short boy hair? Whatever the case may be, this step-by-step guide will teach you everything you need to know about cutting short boy hair.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Short Boy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Boy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Boy Hair

How to Cut Short Boy Hair

Cutting short boy hair may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, anyone can do it. Here are the basic steps to follow:

1. Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting, you'll need to gather the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel
  • Spray bottle

2. Wash and Dry the Hair

Wash your child's hair with shampoo and conditioner, then towel dry it. Make sure the hair is completely dry before cutting.


3. Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut the hair. You can use clippers for a uniform length, or scissors for a more textured look.


4. Start Cutting

Starting at the nape of the neck, use clippers to cut the hair to the desired length. Work your way up the head, using the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to cut it.


5. Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair between the shorter and longer sections. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a natural-looking blend.


6. Trim the Sides and Back

Using scissors, trim the sides and back to the desired length. Keep the hair slightly longer on top for a more modern look.


7. Finish with Detailing

Use clippers to trim around the ears and neck, and use scissors to trim any stray hairs.


Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed, step-by-step guide for cutting short boy hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Wash the hair with shampoo and conditioner, then towel dry it. Make sure the hair is completely dry before cutting.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut the hair. You can use clippers for a uniform length, or scissors for a more textured look.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Section the hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use clips or rubber bands to separate the sections.

Step 4: Start Cutting the Back

Starting at the nape of the neck, use clippers to cut the hair to the desired length. Work your way up the head, using the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to cut it.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair between the shorter and longer sections. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a natural-looking blend.

Step 6: Trim the Sides

Using scissors, trim the sides to the desired length. Keep the hair slightly longer on top for a more modern look.

Step 7: Trim the Top

Using scissors, trim the top to the desired length. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a natural-looking blend.

Step 8: Finish with Detailing

Use clippers to trim around the ears and neck, and use scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Short Boy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short boy hair:

  • Use a cape or towel to protect clothing from hair clippings.
  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp while cutting.
  • Use scissors to trim the hair around the ears and neck for a more polished look.
  • Take breaks if necessary to avoid fatigue or frustration.

FAQs

How often should I cut my son's hair?

It depends on how fast his hair grows and the style you're going for. Generally, a trim every 4-6 weeks is recommended to maintain the desired length and shape.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

Don't panic! Mistakes can be fixed with a little creativity. If you accidentally cut too much hair, try blending it with the longer sections. If the hair looks uneven, trim the longer sections to match the shorter ones.


Hair Care for Short Boy Hair

Proper hair care can help maintain the style and health of short boy hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for children's hair.
  • Brush or comb the hair daily to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the hair.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the desired length and shape.

Now that you know how to cut short boy hair, you can save money and time by doing it at home. Whether you're a parent or a hairstylist, these tips and techniques will help you achieve a professional-looking haircut every time.
