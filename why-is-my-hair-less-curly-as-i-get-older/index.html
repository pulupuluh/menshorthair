Why Is My Hair Less Curly As I Get Older

Written by Robby Jun 08, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're someone who has always had curly hair, you may have noticed that as you've gotten older, your hair has started to become less curly. This can be a frustrating experience, especially if you've always loved having curly hair. But why does this happen?

Description

As we age, our hair goes through a number of changes. One of these changes is a loss of elasticity, which can cause our hair to become less curly. Additionally, as we get older, our hair follicles produce less sebum, which is the natural oil that helps to keep our hair moisturized and healthy. This can lead to dryness and brittleness, which can also contribute to a loss of curl.

How to

If you're someone who is experiencing a loss of curl in your hair, there are a few things you can do to help encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern.

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

One of the most important things you can do for your hair is to keep it moisturized. This is especially important if you have curly hair, as curly hair tends to be naturally drier than other hair types. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for curly hair, as these products will contain ingredients that can help to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

In addition to using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, you may also want to consider using a leave-in conditioner. This can help to provide your hair with an extra boost of moisture, which can help to encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling, such as using a flat iron or curling iron, can be very damaging to your hair, and can cause your hair to become less curly over time. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray, and try to limit your use of these tools as much as possible.

Step by Step

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to choose products that are specifically designed for curly hair.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After you've washed and conditioned your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. This will help to provide your hair with an extra boost of moisture, which can help to encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern.

Step 3: Air Dry Your Hair

Avoid using heat styling tools, and instead, allow your hair to air dry naturally. This can help to prevent damage to your hair, and can also help to encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips that can help to encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this can help to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or straightening treatments.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant hair product.

FAQ

Q: Can I get my curls back?

A: While you may not be able to get your curls back to exactly how they were when you were younger, you can take steps to encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern.

Q: Can stress cause a loss of curl?

A: Yes, stress can be a contributing factor to a loss of curl in your hair. Try to find ways to manage your stress, such as exercise, meditation, or yoga.

Hair Care

If you're someone who is experiencing a loss of curl in your hair, it's important to take steps to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. This can help to encourage your hair to maintain its natural curl pattern, and can also help to prevent damage to your hair. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can help to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful, no matter your age.
