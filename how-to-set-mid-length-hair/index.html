How To Set Mid Length Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have mid-length hair, you may find it challenging to style it. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to set mid-length hair. We will also provide tips and answer frequently asked questions to help you achieve the perfect hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • How to Set Mid Length Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Setting Mid Length Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Set Mid Length Hair

Before starting with the steps, you need to prepare your hair and the tools you will need. Here's what you need to do:

  1. Wash your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Dry your hair thoroughly with a towel.
  2. Apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will prevent damage from heat styling tools.
  3. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  4. Prepare the styling tools you will need, such as a hair dryer, curling iron, or straightener.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Blow Dry Your Hair

Start by blow-drying your hair. Use a round brush to lift the roots and create volume. Blow dry your hair in sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use a concentrator nozzle to direct the airflow and prevent frizz.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into three parts - the top, middle, and bottom. Clip the top and middle sections up and out of the way.

Step 3: Curl the Bottom Section

Take the bottom section of your hair and curl it using a curling iron. Start from the roots and work your way down to the ends. Hold the curling iron for a few seconds before releasing the curl. Repeat this step until you've curled all the hair in the bottom section.

Step 4: Straighten the Middle Section

Take the middle section of your hair and straighten it using a straightener. Start from the roots and work your way down to the ends. Hold the straightener for a few seconds before releasing the hair. Repeat this step until you've straightened all the hair in the middle section.

Step 5: Curl the Top Section

Take the top section of your hair and curl it using a curling iron. Start from the roots and work your way down to the ends. Hold the curling iron for a few seconds before releasing the curl. Repeat this step until you've curled all the hair in the top section.

Step 6: Set Your Hair

Once you've finished curling and straightening your hair, it's time to set it. Use a hairspray or a hair setting spray to keep your hair in place. Spray it lightly all over your hair, focusing on the curls and the ends.

Tips for Setting Mid Length Hair

  • Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools.
  • Use a round brush when blow drying your hair to create volume.
  • Clip the top and middle sections of your hair out of the way when styling the bottom section.
  • Use a curling iron with a smaller barrel size for tighter curls, and a larger barrel size for loose waves.
  • Use a straightener with ceramic plates to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a hairspray or a hair setting spray to keep your hair in place.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it every other day or every three days. If you exercise regularly, you may need to wash your hair more often.

Q: Can I use a hair dryer without a concentrator nozzle?

A: Yes, you can, but it may cause frizz and damage to your hair. A concentrator nozzle directs the airflow and helps you to achieve a smooth finish.

Q: How long should I hold the curling iron or straightener?

A: It depends on your hair type and the heat setting of your styling tool. As a general rule, hold the curling iron or straightener for 5-10 seconds.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as it's more prone to breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as it can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to keep your hair healthy.

Setting mid-length hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful hairstyle. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips, and don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny.
