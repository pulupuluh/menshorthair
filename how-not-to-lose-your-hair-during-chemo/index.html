How Not To Lose Your Hair During Chemo Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Mar 20, 2023 · 6 min read

Chemotherapy is a common cancer treatment that can have many side effects, including hair loss. Losing your hair can be a stressful and emotional experience, but there are ways to minimize or prevent it from happening. In this blog post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how not to lose your hair during chemo.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Prepare for Chemo
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss
  3. Tips for Hair Care During Chemo
  4. FAQs About Chemo and Hair Loss

How to Prepare for Chemo

Before starting chemotherapy, it's essential to prepare your body and mind for the treatment. Here are some things you can do:

  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and fluids.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Get plenty of rest and sleep to help your body recover.
  • Talk to your doctor about medications or supplements that may help reduce hair loss.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemo, there are several steps you can take to minimize it:

  1. Use a scalp cooling system. This device can help reduce blood flow to the scalp, which can minimize hair loss.
  2. Try gentle hair care products that are free of harsh chemicals and fragrances.
  3. Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons.
  4. Use a soft brush to comb your hair gently, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  5. Wash your hair less frequently and use lukewarm water instead of hot water.
  6. Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns that can pull on your hair.
  7. Consider wearing a wig, hat, or scarf to cover your head if you do experience hair loss.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemo

If you do experience hair loss during chemo, here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that are gentle on your scalp.
  • Pat your hair dry gently with a towel instead of rubbing it vigorously.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid using hair dyes or chemical treatments on your hair until after chemo is complete.

FAQs About Chemo and Hair Loss

Q: Will I definitely lose my hair during chemo?

A: Not everyone who undergoes chemotherapy experiences hair loss, but it is a common side effect. The severity of hair loss can vary from person to person.

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemo?

A: Yes, most people's hair will grow back after chemo. However, it may be thinner or a different texture than before.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

A: Hair usually starts to grow back a few weeks after chemo is complete, but it can take several months to a year for it to fully grow back.

Q: Can I dye my hair after chemo?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully grown back and your scalp has healed before using hair dyes or other chemical treatments.

Q: What can I do to boost hair growth after chemo?

A: Eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and taking vitamins and supplements that support hair growth can be helpful. You can also talk to your doctor about medications or topical treatments that may help.

While hair loss during chemo can be a challenging side effect, there are steps you can take to minimize it and care for your hair during and after treatment. Talk to your doctor about your options and don't be afraid to reach out to support groups or loved ones for emotional support.
