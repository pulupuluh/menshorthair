How To Cut Small Boy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 25, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting a small boy's hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily at home. In this article, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut small boy hair at home, with tips and tricks to ensure a successful haircut every time.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Small Boy Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after the Haircut

How to Cut Small Boy Hair at Home

Before starting, make sure you have the right tools – a pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clippers if you plan to cut the hair short.

It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to keep the hair off your child's clothes, and a spray bottle with water to help dampen the hair before cutting.

Once you have all the necessary tools, it's time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and combing your child's hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Decide on the length and style you want to cut, and use hair clips to section off the hair you want to cut.
  3. Use hair clippers or scissors to cut the hair, following the natural shape of the head.
  4. Work around the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time.
  5. For longer hair, use scissors to add layers or texture.
  6. Use a comb to blend any harsh lines or uneven spots.
  7. Check the hair from different angles to make sure it's even, and make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Once you're happy with the haircut, use a spray bottle to dampen the hair and comb it into place.
  9. Trim any stray hairs or bangs as needed.
  10. Finally, remove the hair clips and use a towel or cape to remove any loose hairs.

Tips and Tricks

  • If your child is wiggly, consider giving them a toy or snack to keep them occupied during the haircut.
  • Start with longer sections of hair, and work your way to shorter sections.
  • Use the comb and scissors to create a natural-looking, textured haircut.
  • For a more professional look, consider taking a tutorial online or watching YouTube videos.
  • Be patient and take breaks if needed. It's important to keep your child calm and comfortable during the haircut.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my child's hair?

A: It depends on the length and style of the haircut, but generally every 4-8 weeks is recommended to maintain the shape and style.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting the hair?

A: Don't panic – mistakes can be fixed! Use the comb and scissors to blend any uneven spots, and if needed, take your child to a professional stylist for a touch-up.

Hair Care after the Haircut

After the haircut, it's important to maintain your child's hair with regular washing and conditioning. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much product or heat styling tools.

If your child has longer hair, consider using hair ties or clips to keep it out of their face during playtime or school.

With the right tools and techniques, cutting your child's hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. Just remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help or advice if needed.
