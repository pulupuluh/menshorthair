How To Cut Men s Hair With Thinning Scissors

Written by Bardi Mar 06, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you looking for a way to add some texture to your hair without taking off too much length? Thinning scissors might be the answer you're looking for. These specialized scissors can help you achieve a natural, layered look that is perfect for men's haircuts. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut men's hair with thinning scissors step by step.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Cut Men's Hair with Thinning Scissors
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Using Thinning Scissors
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care after Thinning Scissors

Step by Step Guide

Before you start cutting, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of thinning scissors, regular scissors, a comb, and some hair clips. Here's how to cut men's hair with thinning scissors:

  1. Start by washing and drying the hair. This will make it easier to work with.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut. Use hair clips to keep the rest of the hair out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it with the regular scissors to the desired length.
  4. Using the thinning scissors, make small cuts into the hair, starting at the ends and working your way up towards the roots. This will create a layered effect and add texture to the hair.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair with the regular scissors and then using the thinning scissors until you've cut all the sections you want to.
  6. Comb the hair to check for any uneven areas. Make any necessary adjustments with the scissors.

Remember to take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time. This will help you achieve a more natural look.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors:

  • Use the thinning scissors sparingly. Overuse can lead to uneven or choppy hair.
  • Keep the scissors at an angle when cutting. This will create a more natural, layered look.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on wet hair. This can lead to uneven results.
  • Clean your scissors regularly to keep them in good condition.

FAQs

How often should I use thinning scissors?

It depends on the length and thickness of your hair. As a general rule, you should use thinning scissors every 6 to 8 weeks to maintain the layered look.

Can I use thinning scissors on curly hair?

Yes, but you'll need to be careful not to cut too much hair or create an uneven look. It's best to work with small sections of hair at a time and take your time.

Can I use thinning scissors on my own hair?

It's possible, but it's recommended that you have someone else do it for you. It's difficult to see the back of your head and it's easy to make mistakes.

Hair Care after Thinning Scissors

After using thinning scissors, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish the hair.
  • Trim the ends of your hair every 2 to 3 months to prevent split ends.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking great and maintain the layered look created by thinning scissors.

Now that you know how to cut men's hair with thinning scissors, you can experiment with different styles and create a natural, textured look that's perfect for you. Remember to take your time, use small sections of hair, and follow our tips for the best results.
