How To Cut Short Layered Haircuts A Step By Step Guide

Are you looking for a fresh and modern haircut that's easy to maintain? Short layered haircuts might be the perfect choice for you! Not only do they add texture and dimension to your hair, but they also frame your face beautifully. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting short layered haircuts at home. Let's get started!

How to Cut Short Layered Haircuts

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, it's important to understand the basic principles of cutting layered hair. Layered haircuts involve cutting different lengths of hair throughout the head, creating a textured and voluminous look. Short layered haircuts can be customized to suit your face shape and hair texture, so it's important to consider these factors before you start cutting.

What You'll Need

Here are the essential tools you'll need to cut short layered haircuts:

  • Sharp hairdressing scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Large mirror
  • Small mirror (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools, let's get into the step-by-step process of cutting short layered haircuts:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair, making it easier to cut. If you have curly or wavy hair, let it dry naturally or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use the comb to create a horizontal parting across the back of your head, dividing it into two sections. Then, create a vertical parting down the middle of your head, dividing the back into four sections. Clip each section out of the way.

Next, create a parting from the top of one ear to the top of the other ear. This will separate the top section from the sides. Clip the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start by cutting the back sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut the hair to the desired length. Repeat this process with each section, working your way up the back of your head.

Once you've finished cutting the back sections, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from the front of one of the sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length, using the hair on the back of your head as a guide.

Repeat this process with each section on the sides, making sure to cut the hair at an angle to create layers. Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure precision.

Step 4: Cut the Top Section

Finally, unclip the top section of your hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length, using the hair on the sides and back as a guide. Make sure to cut the hair at an angle to create layers and texture.

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the small mirror to check the back and sides for any unevenness. Make any necessary adjustments before moving on to the next step.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it! Use a blow dryer and a round brush to create volume and movement. You can also use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and waves.

Tips for Cutting Short Layered Haircuts

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short layered haircuts:

  • Start with clean, dry hair to make it easier to cut.
  • Use sharp hairdressing scissors to ensure precision.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use the hair on the back and sides as a guide when cutting the top section.
  • Check the back and sides for any unevenness using a small mirror.

FAQ

Can I cut short layered haircuts on my own?

Yes, you can! Just make sure you have the necessary tools and follow the step-by-step guide carefully.

How often should I get my short layered haircut trimmed?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your haircut looking fresh.

Hair Care Tips for Short Layered Haircuts

Here are some hair care tips to keep your short layered haircut looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut short layered haircuts like a pro! Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll end up with a beautiful and modern haircut that suits your face shape and hair texture.
