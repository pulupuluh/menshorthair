What Can I Do If My Perm Is Too Curly Tips And Tricks For Managing Your Curls

Written by Bardi Feb 25, 2023 · 7 min read

Perms are a great way to add some curl and volume to your hair, but sometimes the results can be a bit too curly. If you're struggling with an overly curly perm, don't worry – there are plenty of things you can do to manage your curls and get the look you want. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for dealing with a too-curly perm.

Table of Contents

  • How to Relax Your Perm
  • Styling Tips for Curly Perms
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Perms
  • FAQ

How to Relax Your Perm

If your perm is too curly, one way to relax it is to use a straightening iron. This will help to loosen the curls and give you a more relaxed, wavy look. You can also try using a flat iron to straighten your hair after washing it. Another option is to use a hair relaxer or straightening treatment, which can help to permanently loosen the curls.

However, it's important to be careful when using these methods, as they can damage your hair if not done properly. Always use a heat protectant spray before using a straightening iron, and follow the instructions carefully when using a hair relaxer or straightening treatment.

Step-by-Step Guide: Using a Straightening Iron to Relax Your Perm

  1. Wash your hair and apply a heat protectant spray.
  2. Separate your hair into sections.
  3. Starting at the roots, use a straightening iron to gently pull your hair straight.
  4. Continue working your way down each section, pulling the hair straight as you go.
  5. Once you've straightened all of your hair, apply a styling product to help hold the look.

Remember to always use a heat protectant spray before using a straightening iron, and to be gentle with your hair to avoid damage.

Styling Tips for Curly Perms

If you're happy with the overall look of your perm, but just want to manage the curls a bit better, there are a few styling tips you can try:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to gently separate and define your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as this can create frizz and break up your curls.
  • Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help define your curls without creating frizz.
  • Apply a curl-defining product, such as mousse or gel, to help hold your curls in place.

Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for your hair. Remember to be gentle with your curls to avoid breakage and frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Perms

Proper hair care is essential for managing a curly perm. Here are some tips to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and dry out your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your curls soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make your curls frizzy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid tugging or pulling on your curls. With proper care and maintenance, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful.

FAQ

1. Can I undo a perm if I don't like the results?

Yes, you can undo a perm by using a perm solution neutralizer or getting a perm reversal treatment. However, keep in mind that these treatments can be harsh on your hair and may cause damage.

2. Will using a hair relaxer damage my hair?

Hair relaxers can be damaging if not used properly. It's important to follow the instructions carefully and use a deep conditioning treatment after the relaxer to help repair any damage.

3. How often should I wash my curly perm?

It's best to wash your curly perm no more than once or twice a week to avoid stripping away natural oils and drying out your curls.
