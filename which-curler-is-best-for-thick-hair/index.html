Which Curler Is Best For Thick Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to find the right curling iron that will give you the look you want without damaging your hair. With so many different types of curling irons on the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the best one for your hair type. In this article, we will discuss the best curling iron for thick hair and provide some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect curl.

How to Choose the Best Curling Iron for Thick Hair

When it comes to thick hair, there are several factors to consider when choosing a curling iron. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Barrel Size

The size of the barrel is important when it comes to curling thick hair. For tighter curls, a smaller barrel size is best. For looser waves, a larger barrel size is ideal. A 1-inch barrel is a good size for most hair types, but if you have very thick hair, you may want to consider a 1.5-inch barrel.

Heat Settings

Thick hair requires higher heat settings to achieve a lasting curl. Look for a curling iron that can reach at least 400 degrees Fahrenheit. However, it's important to note that high heat can also cause damage to your hair, so be sure to use a heat protectant spray before curling.

Material

The material of the curling iron can also affect how well it works on thick hair. Ceramic or tourmaline curling irons are best for thick hair as they distribute heat evenly and prevent hot spots that can damage the hair. Titanium curling irons are also a good option as they heat up quickly and retain heat well.

Step-by-Step Guide to Curling Thick Hair

Now that you know what to look for in a curling iron for thick hair, let's go over the steps to achieve the perfect curl:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start curling, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the high heat of the curling iron.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections. The smaller the section, the tighter the curl will be. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Starting at the roots, wrap a section of hair around the curling iron barrel. Hold for a few seconds and release. Continue curling all the sections of hair.

Step 4: Finish Your Look

Once you've curled all of your hair, use a texturizing spray or hairspray to hold the curls in place. You can also use your fingers to separate the curls for a more natural look.

Tips for Curling Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to make curling thick hair easier:

  • Use a larger barrel size for looser waves
  • Wrap the hair around the curling iron away from your face for a more natural look
  • Don't hold the curling iron on your hair for too long to avoid damage
  • Use a heat protectant spray before curling to prevent damage

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people ask about curling thick hair:

What temperature should I use to curl thick hair?

Thick hair requires higher heat settings to achieve a lasting curl. Look for a curling iron that can reach at least 400 degrees Fahrenheit.

What size barrel should I use to curl thick hair?

For tighter curls, a smaller barrel size is best. For looser waves, a larger barrel size is ideal. A 1-inch barrel is a good size for most hair types, but if you have very thick hair, you may want to consider a 1.5-inch barrel.

How do I prevent damage to my hair when curling?

Use a heat protectant spray before curling to prevent damage. Don't hold the curling iron on your hair for too long, and don't use a heat setting that is too high for your hair type.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for thick hair:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep hair hydrated
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh hair down

Curling thick hair can be a challenge, but with the right curling iron and some practice, you can achieve the perfect curl. Remember to choose a curling iron with a larger barrel size, high heat settings, and a material that distributes heat evenly. Prep your hair with a heat protectant spray, section your hair, and curl away. Finish your look with a texturizing spray or hairspray and enjoy your beautiful curls!
