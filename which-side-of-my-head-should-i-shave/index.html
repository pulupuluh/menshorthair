Which Side Of My Head Should I Shave

Written by Daniel Feb 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Shaving your head can be a bold and empowering move. Whether you're doing it for style, comfort, or to embrace your baldness, it's important to know which side of your head to shave. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about which side of your head to shave, from what to consider before making the cut to tips on hair care post-shave.

Table of Contents

  1. Considerations Before Shaving
  2. How to Decide Which Side to Shave
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Shaving Your Head
  4. Tips for a Smooth Shave
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After Shaving

Considerations Before Shaving

Before you shave your head, there are a few things to consider. First, think about your comfort level. Some people prefer to shave one side of their head and leave the other side long or buzzed. Others prefer a clean, all-over shave. Consider your personal style and how you want to present yourself.

Another factor to consider is your face shape. Shaving one side of your head can accentuate certain features, while leaving one side longer can balance out your face. Think about what features you want to highlight and which ones you'd like to downplay.

How to Decide Which Side to Shave

Once you've considered your personal style and face shape, it's time to decide which side of your head to shave. There's no hard and fast rule, but there are a few guidelines you can follow:

  • If you have a prominent jawline or cheekbones, consider shaving the side of your head that will show them off.
  • If you have a round face, consider leaving the sides a bit longer to balance out your features.
  • If you have a long face, consider shaving the sides closer to your scalp to create the illusion of width.
  • Consider the direction your hair grows. If your hair grows in a certain direction on one side of your head, it may be easier to shave that side.

Step-by-Step Guide to Shaving Your Head

Now that you've decided which side of your head to shave, it's time to get to work. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by trimming your hair as short as possible with clippers or scissors.
  2. Take a warm shower or wrap a warm towel around your head to soften your hair and open up your pores.
  3. Apply shaving cream or gel to the side of your head you're going to shave.
  4. Using a razor, shave in the direction your hair grows. Be careful not to apply too much pressure or go over the same area too many times, as this can cause irritation and razor burn.
  5. Rinse your head with cool water to close your pores and prevent irritation.
  6. Apply a soothing aftershave or moisturizer to prevent dryness and irritation.
  7. Repeat the process on the other side of your head if desired.

Tips for a Smooth Shave

Shaving your head can be a bit tricky, especially if you're new to it. Here are a few tips to make the process smoother:

  • Invest in a high-quality razor with multiple blades to get a closer shave.
  • Use a shaving cream or gel specifically designed for sensitive skin to prevent irritation.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Shave in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.
  • Use a mirror to check for any missed spots.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about shaving your head:

Q: Will shaving my head make my hair grow back thicker?

A: No, this is a common myth. Shaving your head does not affect the rate or texture of hair growth.

Q: Can I shave my head with an electric razor?

A: Yes, you can use an electric razor to shave your head. However, it may not give you as close of a shave as a traditional razor.

Q: How often do I need to shave my head?

A: This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain a shaved look. Some people shave their heads once a week, while others do it every few days.

Hair Care After Shaving

After you've shaved your head, it's important to take care of your skin and hair to prevent irritation and promote healthy growth. Here are a few tips:

  • Apply a moisturizer or aftershave to prevent dryness and soothe any irritation.
  • Avoid exposing your head to direct sunlight, as this can cause sunburn and damage.
  • Wash your scalp regularly with a gentle shampoo to remove any buildup.
  • Consider using a scalp oil or serum to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and guidelines, you should be able to confidently decide which side of your head to shave and perform a smooth and safe shave. Remember to take care of your skin and hair after the shave to promote healthy growth and maintain a polished look.
