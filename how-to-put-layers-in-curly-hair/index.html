How To Put Layers In Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to style it. One way to add depth and volume to your curls is by adding layers. Not only do layers add movement and texture to your curls, but they can also help reduce bulk and manageability. In this guide, we'll show you how to put layers in curly hair, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Section 1: How to Prepare Your Hair
  • Section 2: How to Cut Layers in Curly Hair
  • Section 3: Tips for Maintaining Layered Curly Hair
  • Section 4: Frequently Asked Questions About Layered Curly Hair
  • Section 5: Essential Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

Section 1: How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting layers in your curly hair, you need to prepare your hair properly. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair using products that are specifically designed for curly hair. This will help hydrate your curls and make them more manageable.

Step 2: Detangle Your Curls

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair. Make sure to start at the ends and work your way up to avoid damaging your curls.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Blot your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls gently.

Section 2: How to Cut Layers in Curly Hair

Now that your hair is prepared, it's time to cut your layers. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips or elastics. This will help you focus on one section at a time and ensure that you cut all the layers evenly.

Step 2: Start with the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair and cut this layer to your desired length. Make sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair tends to shrink when it dries.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've cut the bottom layer, release the next section of hair and cut it to the same length as the bottom layer. Use the bottom layer as a guide to ensure that you cut the middle layer evenly.

Step 4: Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, release the top layer of your hair and use the previous layers as a guide to cut this layer to the same length. This will create the layered effect and add volume and movement to your curls.

Section 3: Tips for Maintaining Layered Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you maintain your layered curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

Section 4: Frequently Asked Questions About Layered Curly Hair

Q: How often should I cut my layered curly hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your curls.

Q: Can I cut my own layered curly hair?

A: While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's recommended to get a professional haircut to ensure that your layers are even and well-shaped.

Q: Will layers make my curly hair look thinner?

A: Layers can actually make your hair look thicker by adding volume and movement to your curls.

Section 5: Essential Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

Here are some essential hair care tips for layered curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define and hydrate your curls.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your curls.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective hair product.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the health of your curls.

Now that you know how to put layers in curly hair, you can experiment with different styles and enjoy the added volume and movement in your curls. Remember to take good care of your hair and get regular trims to keep your curls looking their best.
