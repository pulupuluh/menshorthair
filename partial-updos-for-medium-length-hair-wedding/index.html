Partial Updos For Medium Length Hair Wedding

Written by Aprilia Apr 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Welcome to our blog post about partial updos for medium length hair wedding. If you're getting married soon and looking for a hairstyle that's both elegant and practical, then a partial updo might be the perfect choice for you. In this article, we'll show you how to create a beautiful partial updo that works well with medium length hair. So, let's get started!

Description

A partial updo is a hairstyle that combines both an updo and loose hair. It's perfect for those who want to keep some hair down but still want a sophisticated look. Partial updos work well with medium length hair as they allow you to show off your natural hair texture and length while still keeping your hair out of your face.

How to

Here's how to create a partial updo for your medium length hair wedding:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair and apply a heat protection spray to prevent any damage from styling tools. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume and texture.

Step 2: Section your hair

Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and tease it with a fine-toothed comb. This will create volume and height for your partial updo. Repeat this process with the rest of your hair, taking small sections and teasing them at the roots.

Step 3: Create the updo

Gather the teased hair at the crown of your head and pin it in place using bobby pins. Leave some hair loose around your face to frame it. Take the remaining teased hair and twist it into a loose bun at the nape of your neck. Secure it with bobby pins.

Step 4: Finish the look

Spray your partial updo with a medium hold hairspray to keep it in place. You can also add some accessories, such as a headband or flowers, to complete the look.

Tips

Here are some tips to make your partial updo look even better:

  • Use a curling iron to add some waves to your loose hair for a romantic look.
  • Use a texturizing spray to add more volume and texture to your hair.
  • Choose bobby pins that match your hair color to make them less noticeable.

FAQ

Q: Can I create a partial updo with short hair?

A: Partial updos work best with medium length hair, but you can create a similar look with short hair by using bobby pins to pin back some of your hair and leaving the rest loose.

Q: Can I create a partial updo myself?

A: Yes, you can create a partial updo yourself, but it might be easier to have a friend or hairstylist help you.

Hair Care

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

We hope you found this article helpful in creating a beautiful partial updo for your medium length hair wedding. Remember to take care of your hair before and after the big day to keep it healthy and looking its best.
