How To Cut Face Framing Layers For Fine Hair

Written by Petter Jul 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having limp and lifeless hair? Cutting face framing layers can give your fine hair more volume and movement. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut face framing layers for fine hair.

Description

Face framing layers are a popular haircut style that can add depth and dimension to your hair. This haircut style is perfect for those with fine hair as it can make it look thicker and fuller. By cutting layers around the face, you can create movement and texture that will give your hair a more voluminous look.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers for Fine Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You will also need a pair of sharp scissors and a comb. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Part your hair

Part your hair down the middle or on the side, depending on your preference. This will help you to see where to cut the layers.

Step 2: Section your hair

Take a section of hair from the top of your head to the top of your ears. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the first layer

Hold the section of hair between your fingers and point cut into the ends. This means you will cut into the hair at an angle, rather than straight across. This will create a softer, more natural look.

Step 4: Cut the second layer

Take another section of hair from just below the first layer and repeat the same process. Make sure you are cutting the hair at an angle to create a layered effect.

Step 5: Blend the layers

Use the comb to blend the two layers together by combing the hair downwards. This will help to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 6: Repeat on the other side

Repeat the same process on the other side of your head until you have achieved the desired amount of layers.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting face framing layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a point cutting technique for a softer, more natural look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and you don't want to make any mistakes due to fatigue.

FAQ

1. Can I cut face framing layers on my own?

Yes, you can cut face framing layers on your own. However, it is recommended that you have some experience with cutting hair before attempting this style.

2. How often should I cut my face framing layers?

You should cut your face framing layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

3. Will face framing layers work for all face shapes?

Yes, face framing layers can work for all face shapes. The key is to adjust the length and angle of the layers to flatter your face shape.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is important to maintain the health and appearance of your face framing layers. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add more body to your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using styling tools like a curling iron or straightener.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut face framing layers for fine hair, you can give your hair a new and refreshed look. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully for the best results.
