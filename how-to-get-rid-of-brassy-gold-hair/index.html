How To Get Rid Of Brassy Gold Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Aprilia Feb 28, 2023 ¬∑ 8 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking brassy and gold? That orange-yellow tint may be due to the buildup of minerals and chemicals in your hair, or it may be due to the natural color of your hair. Whatever the cause, you can get rid of brassy gold hair with the right tools and techniques. In this article, we'll show you how to get rid of brassy gold hair, step-by-step. We'll also provide some tips and tricks to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Gold Hair

The first step in getting rid of brassy gold hair is to understand what causes it. Brassy hair is usually caused by a buildup of minerals and chemicals in your hair. These minerals and chemicals come from hard water, chlorine, sun exposure, and hair products. To get rid of brassy hair, you need to remove these minerals and chemicals from your hair.

One of the easiest ways to remove these minerals and chemicals is to use a clarifying shampoo. Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove buildup from your hair. Look for a clarifying shampoo that contains sulfates. Sulfates are powerful cleansers that can help remove buildup from your hair.

If you're not a fan of sulfates, you can also use a purple shampoo. Purple shampoos are designed to neutralize brassy tones in blonde and silver hair. They work by depositing a cool, violet pigment onto your hair, which cancels out the warm, yellow tones. However, purple shampoos can be drying, so be sure to use a conditioner afterwards.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy gold hair:

  1. Wet your hair thoroughly.
  2. Apply a clarifying shampoo or a purple shampoo.
  3. Massage the shampoo into your scalp and hair for about 2 minutes.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly.
  5. If you used a purple shampoo, use a conditioner afterwards.
  6. Repeat this process once a week, or as needed.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you get rid of brassy gold hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to brassiness.
  • Avoid washing your hair with hot water, as it can open up the cuticle and make your hair more porous. This can make it more prone to brassiness.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more prone to brassiness.
  • Consider getting a toner or a gloss treatment at a salon to help neutralize brassy tones in your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of brassy gold hair:

Q: How often should I use a clarifying shampoo?

A: You should use a clarifying shampoo once a week, or as needed. Using it too often can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use a purple shampoo on brown hair?

A: Purple shampoo is designed for blonde and silver hair, but it can also be used on brown hair with highlights. If you have dark brown hair, a blue shampoo may be more effective at neutralizing brassiness.

Q: Can I use lemon juice to get rid of brassy hair?

A: Lemon juice can help lighten your hair, but it can also dry it out and make it more prone to breakage. It's best to use a clarifying shampoo or a purple shampoo instead.

Hair Care

Getting rid of brassy gold hair is just the first step in achieving healthy, shiny hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip it of its natural oils.

With these tips and tricks, you can get rid of brassy gold hair and keep your hair looking healthy and shiny. Remember to use a clarifying shampoo or a purple shampoo once a week, and to take care of your hair with a healthy diet and regular trims.
