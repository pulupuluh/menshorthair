How To Cut Your Own Hair Layers And Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim or a new style? With the right tools and techniques, you can learn how to cut your own hair layers and bangs at home. Not only will you save money, but you'll also have more control over your own hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting layers and bangs, provide tips and tricks for a successful cut, and answer some frequently asked questions about hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Cutting Layers
  • Cutting Bangs
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your own hair, you'll need to gather some basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water

It's important to use sharp scissors that are designed for cutting hair, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts. You can find hair shears at beauty supply stores or online.

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly, and you don't want to accidentally cut off more than you intended.

Next, decide on the length and style you want for your layers and bangs. It's a good idea to start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length, as you can always trim more later.

Cutting Layers

To cut layers, start by separating your hair into sections. You can do this by parting your hair down the middle and then splitting it into two sections, or by creating a zigzag part and separating your hair into four or more sections. Use hair clips or ties to keep each section separate.

Starting with one section at a time, comb your hair straight down and hold the ends between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut into the ends at a slight angle, making small snips as you work your way up. Be sure to cut at an angle rather than straight across, as this will create a softer, more natural look.

Repeat this process for each section of your hair, taking care to blend the layers so that there are no harsh lines.

Cutting Bangs

To cut bangs, start by separating the front section of your hair from the rest of your hair. This section should be about as wide as the distance between your eyebrows.

Comb your hair straight down and decide on the length you want for your bangs. Hold the ends between your fingers and cut straight across, making small snips to avoid cutting off too much at once.

If you want side-swept bangs, angle your scissors slightly and cut from the middle of your bangs toward the side. Be sure to cut in small increments, as it's easy to cut off more than you intended.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to wet your hair if it starts to dry out while you're cutting.
  • Take breaks if your arms or hands start to cramp up.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, practice on a wig or a friend's hair first.

FAQs

Is it easy to cut your own hair?

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you're trying a new style or technique. However, with practice and the right tools, it is possible to achieve a salon-worthy cut at home.

How often should I cut my hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and style. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After you've cut your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain your new style. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you're ready to cut your own hair layers and bangs at home. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and practice until you feel confident with the process.
