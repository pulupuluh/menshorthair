How To Use Hair Thinning Scissors On Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Feb 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to thin out your fringe without losing too much length, hair thinning scissors are a great tool to have in your arsenal. With these specialized scissors, you can easily create a softer, more natural-looking fringe that's perfect for any hair type. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of using hair thinning scissors on your fringe, including tips and tricks for achieving the best results.

How to Use Hair Thinning Scissors on Fringe

Before we get started, it's important to note that hair thinning scissors should only be used on dry hair. Wet hair can be deceiving, making it difficult to achieve the desired look. Once your hair is dry, you can follow these steps:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Before you begin, you'll want to section off your fringe. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't accidentally cut too much hair. Use hair clips to divide your fringe into smaller sections, making sure that each section is no wider than the length of your thinning scissors.

Step 2: Hold the Scissors Correctly

When using hair thinning scissors, it's important to hold them correctly. Hold the scissors with your thumb and middle finger, leaving your index finger free to guide the scissors. Make sure that the blades are facing upwards and that the teeth are pointing towards the ceiling.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Using your free hand, gently pull the section of hair taut. Then, starting at the top of the section, use the thinning scissors to snip into the hair, taking small, even cuts. Be sure to keep the scissors moving in a zigzag pattern, rather than cutting straight across. This will help to create a more natural look.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your fringe with the thinning scissors, use a regular pair of scissors to blend any harsh edges. This will help to create a more seamless transition between your fringe and the rest of your hair.

Tips for Using Hair Thinning Scissors on Fringe

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using hair thinning scissors on your fringe:

  • Start with small cuts, as it's always easier to take more hair off than to put it back on.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to make several small cuts than one big one.
  • Always cut in a zigzag pattern to create a softer look.
  • Use a regular pair of scissors to blend any harsh edges once you're finished.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not comfortable doing it on your own.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using hair thinning scissors on fringe:

Can I use hair thinning scissors on wet hair?

No, it's best to use hair thinning scissors on dry hair. Wet hair can be deceiving, making it difficult to achieve the desired look.

Can I use hair thinning scissors on any hair type?

Yes, hair thinning scissors can be used on any hair type. However, it's important to use the right technique to achieve the best results.

Do I need to use a regular pair of scissors as well?

Yes, it's a good idea to use a regular pair of scissors to blend any harsh edges once you're finished using the thinning scissors.

Hair Care After Using Hair Thinning Scissors on Fringe

After using hair thinning scissors on your fringe, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.

By following these tips and using hair thinning scissors correctly, you can achieve a beautiful, natural-looking fringe that's perfect for any hair type. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
