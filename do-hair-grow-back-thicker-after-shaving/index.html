Do Hair Grow Back Thicker After Shaving The Truth Revealed

Written by Frank Aug 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you one of those who are wondering if hair grows back thicker after shaving? You're not alone! A lot of people believe that shaving hair will make it grow back thicker and darker. But is this really true? Let's find out!

What Happens When You Shave Your Hair?

When you shave your hair, you cut it at the surface of your skin. This means that the hair follicle is still intact and will continue to grow hair. However, the hair that grows back is often blunter and thicker at the base, which makes it appear to be darker and coarser.

This is because when hair is cut, the thickest part of the hair shaft is exposed, which makes it appear thicker. The hair also hasn't been exposed to environmental factors like sunlight and pollution, which can make it appear lighter and finer.

Does Hair Really Grow Back Thicker After Shaving?

The short answer is no. Hair does not actually grow back thicker after shaving. The hair that grows back may appear to be thicker and darker, but this is simply an illusion caused by the bluntness of the hair shaft and the fact that it hasn't been exposed to environmental factors.

When you shave your hair, you're simply cutting off the hair at the surface of your skin. This means that the hair follicle is still intact and will continue to grow hair at the same rate and thickness as before.

How to Shave Your Hair

If you're planning to shave your hair, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  1. Start by trimming your hair with scissors or clippers to make it easier to shave.
  2. Use a shaving cream or gel to lubricate your skin and reduce the risk of razor burn.
  3. Use a sharp razor to avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.
  4. Shave in the direction of hair growth to avoid ingrown hairs.
  5. Rinse your skin with cool water and apply a moisturizer to soothe your skin.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about shaving and hair growth:

Q: Does shaving cause hair to grow back faster?

A: No, shaving does not cause hair to grow back faster. The rate of hair growth is determined by genetics and other factors, not by shaving.

Q: Does shaving cause ingrown hairs?

A: Shaving can increase the risk of ingrown hairs, especially if you shave against the direction of hair growth or use a dull razor.

Q: Can shaving cause razor burn?

A: Yes, shaving can cause razor burn if you use a dull razor or don't use a shaving cream or gel to lubricate your skin.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or flat irons, too often.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

Conclusion

So, does hair really grow back thicker after shaving? The answer is no. While the hair may appear to be thicker and darker, this is simply an illusion caused by the bluntness of the hair shaft and the fact that it hasn't been exposed to environmental factors.

If you're planning to shave your hair, make sure to use a sharp razor, shaving cream or gel, and shave in the direction of hair growth to avoid ingrown hairs and razor burn. And don't forget to follow a healthy hair care routine to keep your hair strong and healthy!
