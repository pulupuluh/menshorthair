Why Would A Man Lose His Underarm Hair

If you've noticed that your underarm hair is disappearing, you might be wondering what's going on. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why men might lose their underarm hair and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents

  • Hormonal Changes
  • Aging
  • Medical Conditions
  • Genetics
  • Hair Removal
  • Tips for Underarm Hair Care
  • FAQs

Hormonal Changes

One of the most common reasons why men might lose their underarm hair is due to hormonal changes. Testosterone levels naturally decline as men age, and this can lead to a decrease in body hair, including underarm hair.

Additionally, some medical conditions or medications can disrupt hormonal balance and cause hair loss. For example, men with hypothyroidism (an underactive thyroid) may experience hair loss as a result of low levels of thyroid hormones.

Aging

As mentioned, aging can play a role in underarm hair loss. As men get older, hair growth slows down and hair follicles may become less active. This can result in thinner, sparser hair or even complete hair loss in some areas, including the underarms.

Medical Conditions

There are several medical conditions that can cause underarm hair loss in men. Alopecia areata, an autoimmune disorder that causes patchy hair loss, can affect any part of the body, including the underarms. Other conditions that can cause hair loss include lupus, psoriasis, and certain fungal infections.

Genetics

Genetics can also play a role in underarm hair loss. Some men may simply be predisposed to thinner or sparser hair, while others may have a genetic condition that causes hair loss, such as male pattern baldness.

Hair Removal

Finally, hair removal can also contribute to underarm hair loss in men. Shaving, waxing, and other hair removal methods can damage hair follicles and lead to thinner, sparser hair over time. Additionally, some men may develop ingrown hairs or infections as a result of hair removal, which can cause hair loss in the affected area.

Tips for Underarm Hair Care

If you're experiencing underarm hair loss, there are a few things you can do to care for your remaining hair:

  • Gently wash your underarms with a mild soap or cleanser
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on your underarms
  • Avoid excessive sweating or friction in the underarm area
  • Consider using a moisturizing lotion or oil to keep your skin and remaining hair healthy

FAQs

Is underarm hair loss common in men?

Yes, underarm hair loss is relatively common in men, especially as they age or experience hormonal changes.

Can underarm hair loss be reversed?

In some cases, underarm hair loss may be reversible if it is caused by a medical condition or medication. However, hair loss due to aging or genetics may be more difficult to reverse.

What should I do if I'm experiencing underarm hair loss?

If you're concerned about your underarm hair loss, it's a good idea to talk to your doctor. They can help determine the underlying cause and recommend appropriate treatment options.


Overall, underarm hair loss in men can be caused by a variety of factors, including hormonal changes, aging, medical conditions, genetics, and hair removal methods. By taking care of your remaining hair and addressing any underlying medical issues, you can help keep your underarms healthy and hair-covered.
