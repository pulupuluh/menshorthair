How To Style Long Hair But Short Sides A Complete Guide

Long hair with short sides is a popular hairstyle that has been trending for a while now. It's a versatile look that can be styled in many different ways, from edgy to sophisticated. In this article, we will discuss how to style long hair but short sides, step by step. We'll also provide tips on how to maintain this look and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

How to Style Long Hair but Short Sides

Styling long hair with short sides can seem intimidating at first, but it's actually quite simple. The key is to find a style that complements your face shape and hair texture. Here are some popular styles to consider:

  • Undercut: This style features shaved sides and a longer top. The length of the top can vary, depending on your preference.
  • Side Part: This classic style is perfect for those who want a more sophisticated look. Simply comb your longer hair to one side and keep the sides short.
  • Man Bun: If you have long, thick hair, you can opt for a man bun. Keep the sides short and tie your hair up in a bun at the back of your head.
  • Cornrows: If you have textured hair, you can try cornrows on the sides and keep the top long.

Choose a style that suits your personality and lifestyle. Once you've decided on a style, it's time to learn how to style it.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to style long hair but short sides:

  1. Wash and towel-dry your hair.
  2. Apply a styling product to the longer hair on top. This could be a pomade, wax, or gel, depending on your hair type and the style you want to achieve.
  3. Blow-dry your hair, using a round brush to create volume and texture.
  4. If you're going for a sleek look, use a flat iron to straighten your hair.
  5. Comb your hair to one side (if you're doing a side part) or tie it up in a bun (if you're doing a man bun).
  6. If you're doing an undercut, use clippers to trim the sides and back of your head.
  7. Style the longer hair on top to your desired look, using your fingers or a comb.
  8. Finish with a hairspray or other finishing product to hold the style in place.

Remember, practice makes perfect. It may take a few tries to get the style just right, but don't give up!

Tips for Maintaining the Look

Once you've styled your hair, you'll want to keep it looking great. Here are some tips for maintaining the long hair with short sides look:

  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims. This will keep the sides looking neat and tidy.
  • Invest in quality hair products. Cheap products can damage your hair and make it harder to style.
  • Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and style.
  • Don't be afraid to try new styles. The beauty of this look is its versatility.

FAQs

Q: Will long hair with short sides suit me?

A: Long hair with short sides can suit most face shapes and hair types. Consult with your stylist to see what style would work best for you.

Q: Can I style my hair without using heat?

A: Yes! There are many heat-free ways to style your hair, such as braids, twists, and updos.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or exercise regularly, you may need to wash your hair more often. If you have dry hair, you can wash it less frequently.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know how to style long hair but short sides, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.
  • Get enough sleep and manage stress levels, as these can affect the health of your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style and care for your long hair with short sides like a pro.
