How To Cut Curly Boy Hair With Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Mar 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As a parent, one of the most important tasks you have is taking care of your child's hair. If you're the parent of a curly-haired boy, you know how challenging it can be to keep their hair looking neat and tidy. Cutting curly boy hair with scissors may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and efficiently. In this article, we'll walk you through step-by-step on how to cut curly boy hair with scissors.

How to Cut Curly Boy Hair with Scissors

Cutting curly boy hair with scissors requires a bit of skill and patience, but with the right technique, you can achieve a great-looking haircut. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting curly boy hair with scissors is to gather all of the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and damage to the hair.

Step 2: Wash and Condition the Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wash and condition the hair. This will make it easier to comb and cut. After washing and conditioning, towel-dry the hair and comb it through to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Next, section the hair using clips or elastics. Start at the back of the head and work your way forward, dividing the hair into manageable sections. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Once the hair is sectioned, start cutting. Use the comb to guide the hair and make sure you're cutting evenly. Start at the bottom of each section and work your way up, cutting small sections at a time. It's important to cut slowly and carefully to avoid any mistakes.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After you've cut all of the sections, it's time to blend the hair. This means using the scissors to trim any uneven or longer pieces to create a seamless look. Make sure you're blending evenly and not cutting too much hair.

Step 6: Check for Any Unevenness

Once you've finished cutting and blending, check for any unevenness. Use the comb to comb through the hair and make sure everything looks even. If you notice any areas that need to be trimmed, do so carefully.

Step 7: Style the Hair

After you've finished cutting and blending, it's time to style the hair. Use a styling product, such as a gel or mousse, to define the curls and keep them in place. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry the hair without disrupting the curls.

Tips for Cutting Curly Boy Hair with Scissors

Cutting curly boy hair with scissors can be challenging, but with these tips, you can make the process easier:

  • Start with dry hair to get a better idea of how the hair will look after it's cut.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp as you cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time to avoid mistakes.
  • Blend the hair carefully to avoid any choppy or uneven sections.
  • Use a styling product to define the curls and keep them in place.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly boy hair with scissors:

Can I cut curly boy hair with clippers?

While it's possible to cut curly boy hair with clippers, it's not recommended. Clippers can cause damage to the hair and make it look uneven.

How often should I cut my child's curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake while cutting, don't panic. You can always trim the hair carefully to even it out.

Hair Care for Curly Boys

After cutting your child's curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care for curly boys:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid brushing the hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage the hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish the hair.

Cutting curly boy hair with scissors may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and efficiently. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a great-looking haircut for your curly-haired boy.
