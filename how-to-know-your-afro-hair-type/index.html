How To Know Your Afro Hair Type

Written by Luffy Mar 30, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As someone with Afro hair, it's important to understand your hair type so you can properly care for it. Knowing your hair type can help you determine the right products and styling techniques that work best for your unique hair needs. In this article, we will go over how to determine your Afro hair type and provide tips on how to care for each type.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Determine Your Hair Type
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Different Hair Types

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are several factors to consider when determining your hair type, including curl pattern, density, and porosity. The most common way to determine your hair type is by examining your curl pattern. Afro hair can be classified into four main types:

Type 1: Straight Hair

Straight hair is often classified as Type 1. This hair type is characterized by a lack of curl or wave pattern. Straight hair is typically shiny and smooth and can be fine, medium, or thick in texture.

Type 2: Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is classified as Type 2. This hair type has a slight curl pattern and is often described as having an "S" shape. Wavy hair can be fine, medium, or thick in texture.

Type 3: Curly Hair

Curly hair is classified as Type 3. This hair type has a distinct curl pattern and is often described as having a spiral shape. Curly hair can be fine, medium, or thick in texture.

Type 4: Coily/Kinky Hair

Coily or kinky hair is classified as Type 4. This hair type has a tight curl pattern and is often described as having a "Z" or "S" shape. Coily/kinky hair can be fine, medium, or thick in texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to determine your hair type:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on your blow dryer to dry your hair.
  3. Take a small section of hair and examine the curl pattern. Determine whether your hair is straight, wavy, curly, or coily/kinky.
  4. Determine your hair's texture by examining the thickness of each strand. Determine whether your hair is fine, medium, or thick.
  5. Examine your hair's porosity by running your fingers up and down a strand of hair. Determine whether your hair is low, medium, or high porosity.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Now that you know your hair type, it's important to care for it properly. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling to prevent damage.
  • Use a lightweight, silicone-free serum to add shine.

Wavy Hair

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent frizz.
  • Use a curl-defining cream to enhance your natural wave pattern.

Curly Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid towel-drying your hair to prevent frizz.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner and curl-enhancing gel to define your curls.

Coily/Kinky Hair

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using a brush or comb on dry hair to prevent breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner and oil to seal in moisture.

FAQs

What is hair porosity?

Hair porosity refers to how easily your hair can absorb and retain moisture. Low porosity hair has a hard time absorbing moisture, while high porosity hair absorbs moisture easily but has a hard time retaining it.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Straight hair can be washed every 2-3 days, while curly and coily/kinky hair may only need to be washed once a week.

What is a good hair care routine for Afro hair?

A good hair care routine for Afro hair includes washing and conditioning regularly, using a leave-in conditioner, and using oils to seal in moisture. It's also important to avoid excessive heat styling and to protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin bonnet.

Hair Care for Different Hair Types

Each hair type requires different care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is relatively low maintenance but still requires proper care to keep it healthy. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair, and avoid excessive heat styling to prevent damage. Use a lightweight, silicone-free serum to add shine.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by a slight curl pattern and requires a bit more maintenance than straight hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated, and avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent frizz. Use a curl-defining cream to enhance your natural wave pattern.

Curly Hair

Curly hair is characterized by a distinct curl pattern and requires a lot of moisture to keep it healthy. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness, and avoid towel-drying your hair to prevent frizz. Use a leave-in conditioner and curl-enhancing gel to define your curls.

Coily/Kinky Hair

Coily or kinky hair is characterized by a tight curl pattern and requires a lot of moisture to prevent breakage. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness, and avoid using a brush or comb on dry hair. Use a leave-in conditioner and oil to seal in moisture.

By understanding your hair type and following the proper care routine, you can keep your Afro hair healthy and looking its best.
