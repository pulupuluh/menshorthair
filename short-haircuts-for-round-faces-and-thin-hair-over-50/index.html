Short Haircuts For Round Faces And Thin Hair Over 50

Written by Bardi Jul 29, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of your long, thin hair? Do you want to try a new hairstyle that flatters your round face? If you answered yes to both questions, then you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll give you tips on the best short haircuts for round faces and thin hair over 50. Let’s get started.

Description

As we age, our hair becomes thinner and less voluminous. This can be a challenge, especially if you have a round face. A round face has softer, fuller cheeks and a less prominent jawline. If you have thin hair, it can make your face look even rounder. That’s why it’s important to choose a haircut that complements your face shape and adds volume to your hair.

How To

Before we dive into the different short haircuts for round faces and thin hair over 50, let’s first talk about the factors you need to consider when choosing a hairstyle:

Face shape

As we mentioned earlier, a round face has softer, fuller cheeks and a less prominent jawline. Your face shape will determine the type of haircut that will flatter you the most. If you have a round face, you should avoid hairstyles that add width to your face, such as blunt bangs.

Hair texture

Your hair texture will also affect the hairstyle you choose. If you have thin hair, you should go for a haircut that adds volume and texture to your hair. If you have fine hair, you should avoid haircuts that are too layered, as it can make your hair look flat.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also be considered when choosing a haircut. If you’re always on the go, you should opt for a low-maintenance haircut that doesn’t require too much styling.

Step by Step

Now that you know the factors to consider when choosing a hairstyle, let’s move on to the short haircuts for round faces and thin hair over 50:

1. Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short haircut that works well for round faces and thin hair. It adds volume to your hair and highlights your facial features. This haircut is low-maintenance and perfect for women who are always on the go.

2. Bob Cut

The bob cut is another classic short haircut that flatters round faces and adds volume to thin hair. It’s a versatile haircut that can be styled in different ways, such as a straight bob or a layered bob.

3. Shag Cut

The shag cut is a layered haircut that adds texture and volume to your hair. It works well for round faces and thin hair, as it adds movement and dimension to your hair.

4. Asymmetrical Cut

The asymmetrical cut is a modern haircut that adds an edgy vibe to your look. It works well for round faces and thin hair, as it adds volume and texture to your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your short haircut:

1. Use the right products

Use products that are specifically designed for your hair type. If you have thin hair, use volumizing products to add body and texture to your hair.

2. Get regular trims

Regular trims will keep your haircut looking fresh and prevent split ends. Depending on your hair growth, you should get a trim every 4-6 weeks.

3. Protect your hair from heat

Heat styling can damage your hair, especially if you have thin hair. Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, such as a blow dryer or flat iron.

FAQ

Q: Can I still have bangs with a round face and thin hair?

A: Yes, you can still have bangs with a round face and thin hair. However, you should avoid blunt bangs, as it can make your face look wider. Opt for side-swept bangs or wispy bangs instead.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have thin hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair if you have thin hair. However, you should avoid using harsh chemicals and opt for gentle hair dyes instead.

Q: How do I add volume to my thin hair?

A: You can add volume to your thin hair by using volumizing products, such as mousse or dry shampoo. You can also blow dry your hair upside down to add volume.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips to keep your short haircut looking healthy and shiny:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt and oil buildup. However, avoid washing your hair too often, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Use a conditioner

Use a conditioner after shampooing to nourish your hair and prevent tangles. Apply the conditioner from mid-length to the ends of your hair.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray. The sun can damage your hair and make it look dull and brittle.

Conclusion

Choosing the right haircut for your round face and thin hair can be a challenge, but it’s not impossible. By considering your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can choose a short haircut that flatters you the most. Remember to use the right products and maintain your haircut regularly to keep it looking fresh and healthy.
