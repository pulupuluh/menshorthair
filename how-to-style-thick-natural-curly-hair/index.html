How To Style Thick Natural Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thick natural curly hair, you know that styling it can be a challenge. You want to embrace the natural texture and volume of your curls, but you also want to avoid frizz and maintain definition. In this article, we'll provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to style thick natural curly hair. From how to prep your hair to the best products to use, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Choosing the Right Products
  • Styling Your Hair
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Prepping Your Hair

The key to styling your thick natural curly hair is to start with a good foundation. This means prepping your hair properly before you style it. Here are some tips:

1. Shampoo and Condition

Start by shampooing and conditioning your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair to enhance your natural curls. Avoid products that contain sulfates and silicones as they can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.

2. Detangle

Next, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Don't use a brush as it can cause frizz and breakage. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

3. Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to provide moisture and enhance your natural curls. Be sure to distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

Choosing the Right Products

Choosing the right products is key to styling your thick natural curly hair. Here are some products that are perfect for curly hair:

1. Curl Cream

A curl cream is a must-have for curly hair. It helps to define curls, reduce frizz, and add moisture. Look for curl creams that are lightweight and non-greasy.

2. Gel

Gel is another great product for curly hair. It helps to define curls and hold them in place. Look for gels that are alcohol-free and don't leave a sticky residue.

3. Oil

An oil can provide moisture to your hair and help to reduce frizz. Look for oils that are lightweight and don't weigh down your hair.

Styling Your Hair

Now that you've prepped your hair and chosen the right products, it's time to style your hair. Here are some styles that work well for thick natural curly hair:

1. Wash and Go

A wash and go is a simple style that involves washing your hair, applying your products, and letting your hair air dry. This style works well for those who want to embrace their natural curls.

2. Pineapple

A pineapple is a style that involves gathering your hair on top of your head and securing it with a hair tie. This style is great for preserving your curls overnight and preventing frizz.

3. Half-Up Half-Down

A half-up half-down style involves gathering the top half of your hair and securing it with a hair tie. This style is great for those who want to show off their curls while keeping their hair out of their face.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you style your thick natural curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash

Overwashing your hair can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

2. Use a Microfiber Towel

A microfiber towel is gentler on your hair than a regular towel and helps to reduce frizz. Use it to gently pat your hair dry.

3. Don't Touch Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz. Try not to touch your hair too much throughout the day.

FAQs

1. How do I prevent frizz?

To prevent frizz, use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, don't overwash your hair, and avoid touching your hair too much.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

You should wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

3. Can I straighten my curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but be sure to use heat protectant and avoid straightening it too often to prevent damage.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some tips:

1. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly helps to prevent split ends and breakage.

2. Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Protect your hair while sleeping by using a satin pillowcase or wearing a satin bonnet.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling your hair too often to prevent damage. If you do use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your thick natural curly hair with ease. Remember to embrace your natural texture and have fun with your hair!
