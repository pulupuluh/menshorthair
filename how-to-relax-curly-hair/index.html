How To Relax Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 04, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you may have thought about relaxing it at some point. Relaxing your hair can make it easier to manage, reduce frizz, and give you more styling options. However, it's important to approach the process carefully to avoid damaging your hair. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to relax curly hair.

How to Relax Curly Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose the Right Relaxer

The first step in the process is to choose a relaxer that's right for your hair type. There are many types of relaxers available, so it's important to do your research and consult with a stylist if you're not sure which one to choose.

Some of the most popular types of relaxers include lye relaxers, no-lye relaxers, and mild relaxers. Lye relaxers are usually the strongest and most effective, but they can also be the most damaging to your hair. No-lye relaxers are gentler and less damaging, but they may not be as effective. Mild relaxers are a good option for people with fine or delicate hair.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the relaxer, you'll need to prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup or residue that could interfere with the relaxing process.

Next, you'll need to section your hair into four parts and apply a protective base to your scalp, ears, and hairline. This will help to protect your skin and prevent the relaxer from touching areas where it shouldn't.

Step 3: Apply the Relaxer

Once your hair is prepped and protected, it's time to apply the relaxer. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, applying the relaxer to each section of hair. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and avoid overlapping the relaxer onto previously relaxed hair.

After applying the relaxer, you'll need to let it sit for the recommended amount of time. This will vary depending on the type of relaxer you're using, so be sure to read the instructions carefully.

Step 4: Rinse and Neutralize

After the recommended time has passed, it's time to rinse out the relaxer. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until all of the relaxer has been removed.

Next, you'll need to neutralize your hair to stop the relaxing process. Apply the neutralizing shampoo that came with your relaxer kit and massage it into your hair and scalp. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water and repeat the neutralizing process if necessary.

Step 5: Condition and Style

Once your hair is neutralized, it's time to deep condition it. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and let it sit for 10-15 minutes. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to seal the cuticle and lock in moisture.

Finally, you're ready to style your newly relaxed hair! You can wear it straight, wavy, or curly, depending on your mood and the occasion. Just be sure to use heat protectant products and avoid excessive heat styling to prevent damage to your hair.

Tips for Relaxing Curly Hair

Relaxing curly hair can be a tricky process, so here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Choose a relaxer that's right for your hair type
  • Follow the instructions carefully
  • Protect your skin and scalp with a protective base
  • Don't overlap the relaxer onto previously relaxed hair
  • Rinse your hair thoroughly to remove all of the relaxer
  • Neutralize your hair to stop the relaxing process
  • Deep condition your hair to restore moisture
  • Use heat protectant products when heat styling

FAQ

Can I relax my hair at home?

Yes, you can relax your hair at home, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully and take your time. If you're not confident in your ability to relax your hair at home, consider consulting with a stylist.

How often should I relax my hair?

It's generally recommended to wait at least 6-8 weeks between relaxer treatments to avoid damaging your hair. However, the frequency of your treatments will depend on your hair type and the condition of your hair.

Can I color my hair after relaxing it?

Yes, you can color your hair after relaxing it, but it's important to wait at least 2 weeks after relaxing before coloring. This will give your hair time to recover and reduce the risk of damage.

Hair Care for Relaxed Hair

Once you've relaxed your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for relaxed hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid excessive heat styling
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction

With these tips and the step-by-step guide we've provided, you should be able to relax your curly hair safely and effectively. Remember to take your time, follow the instructions carefully, and take good care of your hair after the process is complete. Happy relaxing!
