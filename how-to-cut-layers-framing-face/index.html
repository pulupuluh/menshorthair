How To Cut Layers Framing Face Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 20, 2023 · 6 min read

If you’re looking for a way to update your hairstyle, cutting layers framing your face is a great way to do it. Layers can add volume and texture to your hair, and framing your face can highlight your best features. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll walk you through the process of cutting layers framing your face.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers Framing Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers Framing Face
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Layers Framing Face

Before you start cutting layers framing your face, there are a few things you’ll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle

Once you have everything you need, it’s time to get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It’s best to cut your hair when it’s clean and dry, as wet hair can be harder to cut accurately. If you need to wash your hair, make sure it’s completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Part your hair

Decide where you want your part to be, and use a comb to create a straight line. This will help you determine where to cut your layers.

Step 3: Section your hair

Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way. This will make it easier to cut the lower layers first.

Step 4: Cut the bottom layer

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle. Repeat this process, working your way around your head.

Step 5: Move on to the next layer

Unclip the top section of your hair, and comb out a small section of hair from the bottom layer. Use this as a guide to cut the next layer. Again, work your way around your head, cutting each section at an angle.

Step 6: Cut the layers framing your face

Once you’ve cut all the layers, it’s time to focus on the layers framing your face. Comb out a small section of hair on either side of your face, and hold it straight out in front of you. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from your chin and working your way down.

Tips for Cutting Layers Framing Face

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid mistakes.
  • Start with small sections of hair, and gradually work your way up to larger sections.
  • If you’re not sure how short to cut your layers, start with a longer length and work your way down.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my layers?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows. On average, you should cut your layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape.

Q: Can I cut my layers myself?

A: Yes, you can cut your layers yourself. However, if you’re not confident in your abilities, it’s best to go to a professional stylist.

Q: Will cutting layers damage my hair?

A: No, cutting layers will not damage your hair. In fact, regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals.

Cutting layers framing your face is a great way to update your hairstyle and highlight your best features. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a salon-quality cut at home.
