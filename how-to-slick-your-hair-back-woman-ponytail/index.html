How To Slick Your Hair Back Woman Ponytail

Description

Slicked back ponytails have been a popular hairstyle for decades. They are perfect for formal occasions, but can also be dressed down for a more casual look. Slicking your hair back into a ponytail can be a bit tricky, but with the right technique and products, it can be easily achieved.

How to Slick Your Hair Back Woman Ponytail

The first step in slicking your hair back into a ponytail is to start with clean, dry hair. Brush through your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to your hands and rub them together to distribute the product evenly.

1. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Use a comb to create a part from ear to ear, separating the top section from the bottom section. Clip the top section out of the way.

2. Smooth the Bottom Section

Apply the hair gel or pomade to the bottom section of your hair, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up to the part. Use a fine-tooth comb to smooth the hair down and remove any bumps or lumps.

3. Secure the Bottom Section

Gather the bottom section of your hair into a ponytail at the nape of your neck. Use a hair tie to secure it in place. Make sure the ponytail is tight and smooth.

4. Smooth the Top Section

Release the top section of your hair and apply hair gel or pomade to it, starting at the crown of your head and working your way down to the part. Use a fine-tooth comb to smooth the hair down and remove any bumps or lumps.

5. Secure the Top Section

Gather the top section of your hair into a ponytail and position it directly above the bottom ponytail. Use a hair tie to secure the top ponytail in place. Make sure the ponytail is tight and smooth.

6. Finish with Hairspray

Finish the look with a light mist of hairspray to keep everything in place. Use a clean toothbrush to smooth any flyaways or baby hairs down around your hairline.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you slick your hair back into a ponytail:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom
  3. Smooth the bottom section with hair gel or pomade
  4. Secure the bottom section with a hair tie
  5. Smooth the top section with hair gel or pomade
  6. Secure the top section with a hair tie directly above the bottom ponytail
  7. Finish with a light mist of hairspray

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect slicked back ponytail:

  • Use a fine-tooth comb to smooth your hair down
  • Apply hair gel or pomade to your hands and rub them together before applying to your hair
  • Use a hair tie that matches your hair color to avoid any visible bands
  • For a more polished look, use a bristle brush to smooth your hair down
  • Use a toothbrush to smooth down any flyaways or baby hairs around your hairline

FAQ

Can I slick my hair back into a ponytail if I have short hair?

Yes, you can still slick your hair back into a ponytail if you have short hair. Use hair gel or pomade to smooth your hair down and use bobby pins to secure any loose pieces.

What kind of hair gel or pomade should I use?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Look for a product that provides a strong hold and won't leave your hair looking greasy or oily.

How do I prevent my hair from frizzing?

To prevent frizz, make sure your hair is completely dry before slicking it back. You can also use a frizz-fighting serum or oil before applying the hair gel or pomade.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair, especially when using hair gel or pomade on a regular basis. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly to remove any buildup from hair products
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner

By following these tips and using the right technique and products, you can easily slick your hair back into a ponytail for a chic and polished look.
