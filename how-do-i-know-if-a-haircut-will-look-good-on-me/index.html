How Do I Know If A Haircut Will Look Good On Me

Written by Eveline Sep 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you considering getting a new haircut but worried that it may not suit you? It's a common concern, but there are ways to determine whether a haircut will look good on you. In this article, we'll share some tips on how to know if a haircut will suit your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, as well as some hair care tips to keep your new haircut looking great.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in determining if a haircut will suit you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each face shape has its own set of characteristics, and certain haircuts can enhance or detract from these features.

For example, if you have a round face, a short bob or pixie cut can help elongate your face and make it appear slimmer. On the other hand, if you have a square face, a long, layered cut can soften your features and add movement to your hair.

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and look at your face in a mirror. Use a tape measure to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, use these measurements to determine your face shape.

Oval Face Shape

An oval face shape is longer than it is wide, with a rounded hairline and jawline. This face shape is considered the most versatile, as it can pull off almost any haircut.

Round Face Shape

A round face shape has a rounded hairline and jawline, and is about as wide as it is long. Haircuts that add height to the crown of the head or elongate the face can help balance out round features.

Square Face Shape

A square face shape has a strong jawline, a square hairline, and is about as wide as it is long. Haircuts that soften the jawline and add movement to the hair can help balance out square features.

Heart Face Shape

A heart face shape has a wider forehead, a narrower jawline, and a pointed chin. Haircuts that add volume to the chin area can help balance out heart features.

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape is widest at the cheekbones and has a narrow forehead and jawline. Haircuts that add volume to the forehead and chin area can help balance out diamond features.

Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face shape is longer than it is wide, with a long, straight hairline and jawline. Haircuts that add volume to the sides of the face can help balance out oblong features.

Considering Your Hair Texture

In addition to your face shape, your hair texture can also determine whether a haircut will suit you. If you have fine, thin hair, a blunt cut may make your hair appear flat and lifeless. On the other hand, if you have thick, curly hair, a short, choppy cut may be difficult to manage.

Consider your hair's natural texture and how much time you're willing to spend styling it each day. Talk to your stylist about the best haircut for your hair texture and lifestyle.

Matching Your Lifestyle

When choosing a new haircut, it's important to consider your lifestyle. If you're active and frequently on the go, a low-maintenance cut that can be styled quickly and easily may be best. On the other hand, if you have a more formal job, a sleek, polished cut may be more appropriate.

Consider how much time and effort you're willing to invest in styling your hair each day. Talk to your stylist about cuts that will suit your lifestyle and hair care routine.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen a new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are some hair care tips to keep your new cut looking fresh:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent damage
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep hair soft and healthy
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I don't like my new haircut?

If you're unhappy with your new haircut, talk to your stylist about what you don't like and why. They may be able to make adjustments to the cut to better suit your needs.

How often should I get my hair cut?

It's recommended that you get your hair cut every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

What should I do if my hair is damaged?

If your hair is damaged, it's important to take steps to repair it. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By considering your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can determine whether a new haircut will suit you. Talk to your stylist about the best cut for your needs, and take care of your hair to keep it looking great. With these tips, you can confidently choose a haircut that will enhance your features and flatter your face.
