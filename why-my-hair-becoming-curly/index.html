Why Is My Hair Becoming Curly

Written by Christine Feb 24, 2023 · 9 min read

In recent years, many people have noticed a change in their hair texture. Some individuals with naturally straight hair have noticed that their hair has become curly or wavy over time. This can be confusing and frustrating for those who have always had a certain hair texture. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair may be becoming curly and provide some tips on how to care for your new hair texture.

Genetics

One of the most common reasons why your hair may be becoming curly is genetics. It is possible that you have inherited the curly hair gene from one or both of your parents. This means that your hair may have always had the potential to be curly, but it was not expressed until later in life. If you have siblings or other family members with curly hair, it is more likely that genetics is the cause of your hair texture change.

Hormones

Hormones can also play a role in hair texture changes. As we age, our hormone levels fluctuate, which can impact the shape and texture of our hair. Women may notice that their hair becomes curlier during pregnancy or menopause, when hormone levels are particularly high or low. Hormonal imbalances can also be caused by stress, medication, or certain medical conditions, which can affect hair texture.

Heat Damage

Heat damage from styling tools like flat irons and blow dryers can cause hair to become curly or wavy. When hair is exposed to high heat, the bonds that hold the hair strands together can be weakened or broken, causing the hair to take on a new shape. This type of heat damage is often irreversible, so it is important to use heat styling tools sparingly and always use a heat protectant product.

Product Build-Up

Product build-up can also cause hair to become curly or wavy. When hair is weighed down by too much product, it can lose its natural shape and texture. This can be particularly true for individuals who use heavy oils, gels, or creams on their hair. To avoid product build-up, use products sparingly and choose lightweight, water-based products that won't weigh down your hair.

How to Care for Curly Hair

If you have noticed that your hair is becoming curly, it is important to adjust your hair care routine accordingly. Curly hair requires different care than straight hair, so it is important to educate yourself on the best practices for curly hair care.

Step-by-Step Guide to Curly Hair Care

Step 1: Use a gentle shampoo. Curly hair is often drier than straight hair, so it is important to use a shampoo that is gentle and moisturizing. Look for shampoos that are sulfate-free and contain natural oils like coconut or argan oil.

Step 2: Condition regularly. Conditioning is essential for curly hair, as it helps to detangle and moisturize the hair. Use a conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair and leave it on for at least 5 minutes before rinsing.

Step 3: Use a leave-in conditioner. Leave-in conditioner can help to keep curly hair moisturized throughout the day. Apply a small amount to damp hair and distribute evenly with a wide-tooth comb.

Step 4: Avoid brushing. Brushing curly hair can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Step 5: Avoid heat styling. Heat styling tools can cause damage to curly hair, so it is best to avoid them whenever possible. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant product and keep the temperature low.

Tips for Curly Hair

Tip 1: Sleep on a silk pillowcase. Silk pillowcases are gentler on hair and can help to reduce frizz and breakage.

Tip 2: Use a diffuser. A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.

Tip 3: Embrace the natural texture. Don't fight your hair's natural texture. Embrace your curls and work with them to create a style that you love.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair stay curly?

A: It depends on the cause of your hair texture change. If genetics or hormones are the cause, your hair may stay curly. If heat damage or product build-up is the cause, your hair may return to its original texture with proper care.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten curly hair, but it is important to use heat protectant products and low heat settings to avoid damage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Tip 1: Deep condition regularly. Curly hair can benefit from deep conditioning treatments once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Tip 2: Avoid harsh chemicals. Chemical treatments like relaxers or perms can cause damage to curly hair. Avoid these treatments whenever possible.

Tip 3: Protect your hair from the sun. UV rays can damage hair, so it is important to protect your hair with a hat or scarf when spending time in the sun.

Conclusion

There are many reasons why your hair may be becoming curly, but with the right care, you can embrace your new texture and create beautiful styles. Whether it is genetics, hormones, heat damage, or product build-up that is causing your hair to change, following the tips and advice in this article can help you care for your curly hair and keep it healthy.
