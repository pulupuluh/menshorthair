How To Cut Yorkie Hair At Home With Scissors

Written by Petter Sep 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Yorkshire Terriers, also known as Yorkies, are small and adorable dogs that require regular grooming to keep their silky coat looking its best. Professional grooming can be expensive, but with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your Yorkie's hair at home with scissors. In this article, we will guide you through the process step-by-step, provide tips for success, and answer some frequently asked questions about Yorkie hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Yorkie Hair at Home with Scissors
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Success
  4. FAQs
  5. Yorkie Hair Care

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start cutting your Yorkie's hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clipper or trimmer (optional)
  • Comb or brush
  • Towel or table topper

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

  1. Start by brushing your Yorkie's hair to remove any tangles or mats. Use a comb or brush to gently work through any knots.
  2. Decide on the length you want to cut your Yorkie's hair. If you're unsure, start with a longer length and trim gradually.
  3. Use the scissors to cut the hair around your Yorkie's ears, being careful not to nick the skin. Hold the hair between your fingers and snip away the excess. Repeat on both ears.
  4. Next, trim the hair around your Yorkie's face. Use the comb to lift the hair up and away from the face, then use the scissors to trim the excess. Be careful not to get too close to the eyes or mouth.
  5. Move on to the body. Use the comb to lift the hair up and away from the skin, then use the scissors to trim the excess. Work in sections, starting at the back and moving towards the head.
  6. Be sure to trim the hair around your Yorkie's paws and nails. Use the scissors to trim the hair between the toes and around the paw pads. This will help prevent matting and keep your Yorkie's feet clean.
  7. Finally, trim the hair around your Yorkie's tail. Use the scissors to trim away any excess hair, being careful not to nick the skin.

Remember to take breaks and offer your Yorkie treats and praise throughout the grooming process. This will help keep them calm and relaxed.

Tips for Success

Here are some additional tips to help you successfully cut your Yorkie's hair at home:

  • Invest in high-quality scissors. Sharp scissors will make the process easier and reduce the risk of injury.
  • Work in a well-lit area to ensure you can see what you're doing.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair up and away from the skin before cutting. This will help prevent accidentally cutting the skin.
  • Take your time and work in small sections. Rushing can lead to uneven cuts or accidental injuries.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your Yorkie's hair, consider taking a class or watching online tutorials for additional guidance.

FAQs

How often should I cut my Yorkie's hair?

Yorkies should have their hair trimmed every 4-6 weeks to avoid matting and keep their coat looking healthy.

Can I use clippers or a trimmer instead of scissors?

Yes, clippers or trimmers can be used to cut a Yorkie's hair, but they may not provide the precision and control that scissors can offer.

What should I do if I accidentally cut my Yorkie's skin?

If you accidentally nick your Yorkie's skin, apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth to stop any bleeding. If the cut is deep or won't stop bleeding, seek veterinary care.

Yorkie Hair Care

Cutting your Yorkie's hair is just one component of their overall hair care routine. Here are a few additional tips to keep your Yorkie's coat looking and feeling its best:

  • Brush your Yorkie's hair daily to prevent matting and tangling.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner formulated for dogs to keep their coat clean and healthy.
  • Trim the hair around your Yorkie's eyes regularly to prevent irritation and infection.
  • Consider adding supplements or a diet rich in omega-3 fatty acids to promote healthy skin and coat.

Cutting your Yorkie's hair at home with scissors can be a cost-effective and rewarding way to keep your furry friend looking their best. With the right tools, techniques, and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home.
