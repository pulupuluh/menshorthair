What Are The Best Kind Of Hair Extensions In 2023

Written by Aprilia Apr 19, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you looking for a way to add length, volume, or color to your hair? Hair extensions are a great option! However, with so many different types of hair extensions available, it can be overwhelming to decide which one is right for you. In this article, we’ll break down what the best kind of hair extensions are and why.

Table of Contents

  • Clip-In Extensions
  • Tape-In Extensions
  • Sew-In Extensions
  • Micro-Link Extensions
  • Fusion Extensions
  • Halo Extensions
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

Clip-In Extensions

Clip-in extensions are a popular choice because they’re easy to use and don’t require any special tools or professional installation. They’re made with clips that attach to your natural hair, allowing you to easily add length or volume whenever you want. Clip-in extensions are also great for trying out different colors without committing to a permanent dye job.

One downside of clip-in extensions is that they can be heavy and uncomfortable to wear for long periods of time. They also require more maintenance than other types of extensions because you need to take them out and reapply them each day.

Tape-In Extensions

Tape-in extensions are a semi-permanent option that last for 6-8 weeks before needing to be reapplied. They’re attached to your natural hair with a special tape that’s designed to be gentle on your hair and scalp. Tape-in extensions are great for adding length and volume, and they’re less noticeable than clip-in extensions because they lay flat against your head.

One downside of tape-in extensions is that they can be difficult to remove without damaging your natural hair. They also require professional installation, which can be expensive.

Sew-In Extensions

Sew-in extensions, also known as weaves, are attached to your natural hair with a needle and thread. They’re a popular choice for people with thick, curly hair because they blend in well and can add a lot of volume. Sew-in extensions can last for several months with proper care.

One downside of sew-in extensions is that they can be heavy and uncomfortable to wear for long periods of time. They also require professional installation, which can be expensive.

Micro-Link Extensions

Micro-link extensions, also known as micro-bead extensions, are attached to your natural hair with small metal beads. They’re a semi-permanent option that can last for several months before needing to be reapplied. Micro-link extensions are great for adding length and volume, and they’re less noticeable than clip-in extensions because they lay flat against your head.

One downside of micro-link extensions is that they can be difficult to remove without damaging your natural hair. They also require professional installation, which can be expensive.

Fusion Extensions

Fusion extensions, also known as keratin extensions, are attached to your natural hair with a special adhesive that’s heated up and melted to bond the extension to your hair. They’re a semi-permanent option that can last for several months before needing to be reapplied. Fusion extensions are great for adding length and volume, and they’re less noticeable than clip-in extensions because they lay flat against your head.

One downside of fusion extensions is that they can be difficult to remove without damaging your natural hair. They also require professional installation, which can be expensive.

Halo Extensions

Halo extensions are a newer type of extension that are attached to a clear wire that sits around your head like a halo. They’re a temporary option that can be easily removed and reapplied whenever you want. Halo extensions are great for adding length and volume without the commitment of other types of extensions.

One downside of halo extensions is that they can be more noticeable than other types of extensions because the wire needs to be hidden under your natural hair. They also don’t work well for people with short hair.

FAQ

How much do hair extensions cost?

The cost of hair extensions varies depending on the type of extensions, the length and color of the hair, and where you live. Clip-in extensions are the most affordable option, with prices ranging from $50-$200. Sew-in extensions and fusion extensions are the most expensive, with prices ranging from $500-$2,000.

Can I color my hair extensions?

It depends on the type of extensions. Clip-in extensions can be colored with temporary or semi-permanent dyes, but you should avoid using permanent dyes because they can damage the extensions. Tape-in extensions, sew-in extensions, and fusion extensions can be colored, but you should always consult with a professional stylist to ensure that the extensions are not damaged.

How do I care for my hair extensions?

It’s important to follow the care instructions that come with your extensions. Generally, you should avoid using hot tools on your extensions, wash them gently with a sulfate-free shampoo, and avoid sleeping with wet hair. You should also brush your extensions regularly to prevent tangling.

Hair Care Tips

Whether you have hair extensions or not, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

Overall, the best kind of hair extensions for you will depend on your individual needs and preferences. Consider the level of maintenance you’re willing to commit to, your budget, and the look you’re trying to achieve. With the right extensions and proper care, you can have beautiful, long locks in no time!
