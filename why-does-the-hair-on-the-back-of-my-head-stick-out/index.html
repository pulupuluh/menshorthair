Why Does The Hair On The Back Of My Head Stick Out

Written by William Jun 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have hair that sticks out at the back of your head, regardless of how hard you try to tame it? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, but the good news is that there are steps you can take to address it.

Description

The hair at the back of your head is known as the occipital area. This area is prone to sticking out due to a few factors, such as hair texture, cowlicks, and how you style your hair. The hair in this area also tends to be coarser and more difficult to manage than other parts of your head, contributing to the problem.

How to

If you're wondering how to fix this issue, there are a few things you can try:

1. Blow-dry your hair

Using a round brush and a blow dryer, you can smooth out the hair at the back of your head. Make sure to point the nozzle of the blow dryer downwards to prevent frizz and flyaways.

2. Use a flat iron

If your hair is particularly unruly, you can use a flat iron to straighten it out. However, be careful not to use too much heat, as this can damage your hair.

3. Try a different haircut

If you have a lot of cowlicks or a particularly stubborn hair texture, you may benefit from a different haircut. Talk to your stylist about options that will help tame the hair at the back of your head.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to blow-drying your hair to smooth out the hair at the back of your head:

Step 1:

Wash your hair and towel dry it until it's damp.

Step 2:

Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent heat damage.

Step 3:

Section your hair and use a round brush to dry each section. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. Make sure to point the nozzle of the blow dryer downwards.

Step 4:

Once your hair is dry, use a bit of hair oil or serum to smooth out any remaining frizz or flyaways.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you manage the hair at the back of your head:

1. Use a satin pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent your hair from tangling while you sleep.

2. Avoid heat styling too often

Using heat styling tools too often can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz and flyaways. Try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair hydrated and reduce frizz.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the hair at the back of your head:

Why does the hair at the back of my head stick out?

The hair at the back of your head is prone to sticking out due to hair texture, cowlicks, and how you style your hair.

Can I fix hair that sticks out at the back of my head?

Yes, there are several steps you can take to address this issue, such as blow-drying your hair, using a flat iron, or trying a different haircut.

What haircut is best for taming the hair at the back of my head?

A layered haircut can help tame the hair at the back of your head by adding texture and movement.

Hair Care

It's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help prevent buildup and keep your scalp healthy.

2. Use a conditioner

Using a conditioner can help keep your hair hydrated and reduce frizz.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray can help prevent sun damage to your hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can tame the hair at the back of your head and achieve a smooth, polished look.
