Does Long Hair Make Your Face Look Fatter

If you have long hair and are worried about whether it makes your face look fatter, you've come to the right place. We'll explore this topic in-depth, answering your questions and giving you some tips on how to style your hair to complement your face shape.

Description

There's no simple answer to the question of whether long hair makes your face look fatter. It depends on your face shape and the style of your hair. In some cases, long hair can make your face look slimmer, while in others, it can make it appear wider.

There are some general rules to follow when it comes to choosing a hairstyle that flatters your face shape. Keep reading to learn more.

How to Determine if Long Hair Makes Your Face Look Fatter

The first step in determining whether long hair makes your face look fatter is to identify your face shape. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong.

Once you've identified your face shape, you can look for hairstyles that complement it. In general, if you have a round face, long hair can make your face look wider. On the other hand, if you have an oval face, long hair can actually make your face look slimmer.

It's important to note that these are just general guidelines. Everyone's face is unique, and what works for one person may not work for another. The best way to determine what hairstyle looks best on you is to experiment with different styles and get feedback from friends, family, and stylists.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair to Complement Your Face Shape

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you choose a hairstyle that flatters your face shape:

Step 1: Identify Your Face Shape

Use a tape measure to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin.

Here are the measurements you'll need:

  • Forehead: Measure across the widest part of your forehead, from one hairline to the other.
  • Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones, starting at the pointiest part below the outer corner of each eye.
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jawline begins to angle upward.
  • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Once you have these measurements, you can use them to identify your face shape:

  • Oval: Your face length is longer than the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Your forehead is slightly wider than your jawline, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face.
  • Round: Your face length is about the same as the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Your face is widest at your cheeks, and you have a rounded chin.
  • Square: Your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are about the same width, and your face length is similar to the width of your face. Your jawline is square and angular.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your jawline, and your face length is longer than the width of your face. Your chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are narrower. Your face is longer than it is wide, and your chin is pointed.
  • Oblong: Your face length is longer than the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Your forehead is about the same width as your jawline, and your chin is slightly pointed.

Step 2: Choose a Hairstyle That Complements Your Face Shape

Once you've identified your face shape, you can look for hairstyles that complement it. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Oval: You can wear almost any hairstyle, but long hair looks particularly good on oval faces. Avoid heavy bangs, which can shorten your face.
  • Round: Long hair can make your face look wider, so try a hairstyle that adds height and volume to the top of your head. A side part can also help elongate your face.
  • Square: Soft, layered styles work well on square faces. Avoid blunt cuts, which can make your face look even more angular.
  • Heart: Soft, side-swept bangs can balance out a pointed chin. Long, wavy styles also work well on heart-shaped faces.
  • Diamond: Soft, layered styles with volume at the crown can balance out a diamond-shaped face. Avoid hairstyles that are too wide at the sides.
  • Oblong: Long, layered styles with volume at the sides can add width to an oblong face. Avoid styles that are too long and straight, which can make your face look even longer.

Tips for Styling Your Hair to Make Your Face Look Thinner

If you're concerned about whether long hair makes your face look fatter, here are some tips to help you create a slimmer appearance:

  • Add volume to the top of your head to elongate your face.
  • Avoid heavy bangs, which can shorten your face.
  • Try a side part to create the illusion of a longer face.
  • Avoid styles that are too wide at the sides.
  • Consider adding layers to create movement and texture.

FAQs

Does long hair make your face look fatter?

It depends on your face shape and the style of your hair. In general, long hair can make your face look wider if you have a round face. However, if you have an oval face, long hair can actually make your face look slimmer.

What hairstyles look best on round faces?

Hairstyles that add height and volume to the top of the head can help elongate a round face. A side part can also create the illusion of a longer face.

What hairstyles look best on square faces?

Soft, layered styles work well on square faces. Blunt cuts can make the face look even more angular.

Hair Care Tips for Long Hair

If you have long hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid pulling your hair too tight when styling it, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, whether long hair makes your face look fatter depends on your face shape and the style of your hair. By identifying your face shape and choosing a hairstyle that complements it, you can create a look that flatters your features. And by taking good care of your long hair, you can keep it looking healthy and shiny.
