How To Work Out Curly Hair Type A Complete Guide

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can be tricky to manage if you don't know how to work out your hair type. There are different types of curly hair, and each type requires different care and styling techniques. In this article, we will guide you through the process of working out your curly hair type and give you some tips on how to care for your curls.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Work Out Your Curly Hair Type

The first step in caring for your curly hair is to determine your hair type. There are different systems for categorizing curly hair types, but the most widely used is the one developed by Andre Walker, Oprah Winfrey's hairstylist. In this system, there are four types of curly hair:

  • Type 1: Straight Hair
  • Type 2: Wavy Hair
  • Type 3: Curly Hair
  • Type 4: Coily Hair

To work out your hair type, you need to look at the shape of your curls and the texture of your hair. Here's how:

Step 1: Look at the Shape of Your Curls

The shape of your curls determines your hair type. Here's a breakdown of the different curl patterns:

  • Type 1: Straight hair with no curls or waves
  • Type 2: Wavy hair with loose, stretched out curls
  • Type 3: Curly hair with defined, springy curls
  • Type 4: Coily hair with tight, corkscrew curls

Step 2: Look at the Texture of Your Hair

The texture of your hair refers to the thickness of each individual strand. Here's a breakdown of the different hair textures:

  • Fine: Thin strands that are almost translucent when held up to the light
  • Medium: Strands that are neither too thick nor too thin
  • Coarse: Thick strands that are easy to see when held up to the light

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have worked out your curly hair type, here's a step-by-step guide on how to care for your curls:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Hair

Cleanse your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo or co-wash. Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils. Instead, use lukewarm or cool water to wash your hair.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Condition your hair with a moisturizing conditioner. Apply the conditioner from the mid-lengths to the ends of your hair, avoiding the roots. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and moisturize your curls. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair using a curl-enhancing product that is suitable for your hair type. You can use a gel, cream, or mousse to define your curls. Apply the product to damp hair, then scrunch your hair upwards to encourage your curls to form.

Step 5: Dry Your Hair

Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old T-shirt to gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair. You can also air dry your hair or use a diffuser on your hair dryer to dry your curls gently.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your curls. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my curly hair?

A: It's best to use a sulfate-free shampoo or co-wash on curly hair, as regular shampoos can be too harsh and strip your hair of its natural oils.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once a week. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash it more often.

Q: Should I use a diffuser when drying my curly hair?

A: Yes, a diffuser can help to dry your curls gently and prevent frizz. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your hair upwards with the diffuser to encourage your curls to form.

Q: How can I prevent my curls from becoming frizzy?

A: Use a moisturizing conditioner and leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated. Avoid using hot water and harsh shampoos, and use a microfiber towel or T-shirt to dry your hair gently. You can also use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and prevent frizz.

Hair Care

Working out your curly hair type can be a game-changer when it comes to hair care. Once you know your hair type, you can tailor your hair care routine to suit your specific needs. Remember to be patient with your curls, and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you. With the right care and attention, your curls will be healthy, bouncy, and beautiful.
