How To Cut Layers In Your Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 24, 2023 · 7 min read

Adding layers to your short hair is a great way to add volume, texture, and movement to your locks. However, going to a salon to have your hair professionally cut can be quite expensive. The good news is that cutting your own layers at home is possible, and with the right tools and technique, you can achieve salon-worthy results. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut layers in your own short hair, step by step.

Tools You’ll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need to gather some tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, it’s important to wash and dry it. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and ensure that you’re cutting it evenly. It’s also a good idea to detangle your hair with a comb.

If you have curly hair, it’s best to cut it while it’s dry. This will prevent any surprises with shrinkage after your hair dries.

Sectioning Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, it’s time to section it. Start by parting your hair down the middle and clipping one half up. Then, divide the bottom section in half and clip the top half up.

You should now have two sections of hair – the bottom section and the top section. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down. This will be your guide for cutting your layers.

Cutting Your Layers

Now that you’ve sectioned your hair, it’s time to start cutting your layers. Follow these steps:

  1. Hold the guide section of hair between your fingers and pull it straight up. This will be the length of your first layer.
  2. Using your scissors, cut the hair straight across. Make sure your scissors are at a slight angle to create a soft, blended effect.
  3. Release the clipped section of hair and comb it down. This will show you where your first layer falls.
  4. Take another section of hair from the bottom section and use the first layer as a guide. Hold the new section of hair between your fingers and cut it at the same length as the first layer.
  5. Continue this process, moving up the bottom section of hair, until you’ve cut all the layers you want.

Remember to take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time. You can always cut more hair, but you can’t put it back once it’s cut.

Blending Your Layers

Once you’ve cut your layers, it’s time to blend them. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and give your hair a natural, textured look.

Use your scissors to cut small sections of hair at a slight angle, blending the layers together. Be careful not to cut too much hair, as this can create choppy, uneven layers.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

  • Start with dry, detangled hair.
  • Use sharp scissors.
  • Section your hair carefully.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Take breaks if you need to.

FAQs

Can I cut layers in my own short hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in your own short hair. Just make sure you have the right tools and technique.

How do I know how many layers to cut?

This depends on the length, texture, and thickness of your hair. Start with one or two layers and add more if you want more volume and movement.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always cut more hair or visit a salon for a professional fix.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your layers, it’s important to take care of your hair. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and create split ends. And don’t forget to get regular trims to maintain your layers and keep your hair looking fresh.

With the right tools, technique, and care, you can cut layers in your own short hair and achieve salon-worthy results. Happy cutting!
