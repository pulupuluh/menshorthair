How Long Does It Take To Grow A Mullet From Bald

Written by Oliver May 31, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you thinking about growing a mullet but worried about how long it will take to grow one from bald? This is a common concern for many people who are looking to change up their hairstyle. In this article, we’ll discuss everything you need to know about growing a mullet from bald, including how long it takes, tips for faster growth, and how to care for your hair along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow a Mullet from Bald
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Faster Growth
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Grow a Mullet from Bald

First, it’s important to understand that hair growth is a slow process. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it can take several months to grow a full mullet from bald. However, there are some things you can do to speed up the process.

Tip #1: Eat a Healthy Diet

What you eat can affect the health of your hair. Make sure you’re getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet to promote hair growth. Foods like eggs, nuts, and leafy greens are great for hair health.

Tip #2: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can stimulate hair growth by improving blood flow to the hair follicles. You can do this with your fingers or a scalp massager.

Tip #3: Use Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products on the market that claim to speed up hair growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, caffeine, or minoxidil.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know some tips for faster hair growth, here’s a step-by-step guide on how to grow a mullet from bald:

Step 1: Let Your Hair Grow

The first step is to let your hair grow out. This can take several months, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Step 2: Trim the Sides

Once your hair has grown out a bit, you’ll want to start trimming the sides. This will help create the classic mullet shape.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Next, you’ll want to cut the back of your hair into the classic mullet shape. This involves leaving the hair on the top and sides longer while cutting the hair in the back shorter.

Step 4: Style Your Mullet

Once your mullet has grown out and been shaped, you can start styling it. Use a comb to create the classic mullet shape, and use hair products like gel or pomade to hold the style in place.

Tips for Faster Growth

Here are some additional tips for faster hair growth:

Tip #1: Take Care of Your Scalp

A healthy scalp is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you’re washing your hair regularly and using a gentle shampoo that won’t strip your scalp of its natural oils.

Tip #2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons can damage your hair and slow down hair growth. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible.

Tip #3: Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can slow down hair growth.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow a mullet from bald?

A: It can take several months to grow a full mullet from bald. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

Q: What can I do to speed up hair growth?

A: Eating a healthy diet, massaging your scalp, and using hair growth products can all help speed up hair growth.

Q: Can I grow a mullet if I have thinning hair?

A: It may be more difficult to grow a full mullet if you have thinning hair, but it’s still possible. Talk to your hairstylist about the best way to style your hair to create a mullet look.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind while growing out your mullet:

Tip #1: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is less likely to pull and damage your hair than a brush. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair after washing it.

Tip #2: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and healthy while you’re growing out your mullet.

Tip #3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun’s UV rays can damage your hair and slow down hair growth. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

In conclusion, growing a mullet from bald can take several months, but with the right tips and tricks, you can speed up the process. Remember to take care of your hair along the way to keep it healthy and looking its best.
