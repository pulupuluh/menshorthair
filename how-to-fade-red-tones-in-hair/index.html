How To Fade Red Tones In Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Jul 21, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've recently dyed your hair red and want to tone it down, you're not alone. Many people find that red tones can be too vibrant or difficult to maintain. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to fade red tones in your hair without damaging your locks. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of fading red tones in hair, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  • Why Do Red Tones Fade?
  • How to Fade Red Tones in Hair: Step by Step
  • Tips for Fading Red Tones in Hair
  • FAQs About Fading Red Tones in Hair
  • Hair Care After Fading Red Tones

Why Do Red Tones Fade?

Red hair dye is notorious for fading quickly, especially if you have fine or porous hair. When you dye your hair red, the color molecules penetrate the outer layer of your hair shaft and bind to the inner cortex. However, over time, the color molecules can break down and fade, especially if you wash your hair frequently, use hot tools, or expose your hair to the sun.

In addition, red hair dye is notoriously difficult to maintain because it can easily turn brassy or orange. This is because red hair dye contains a lot of warm tones, which can clash with your natural hair color or skin tone.

How to Fade Red Tones in Hair: Step by Step

If you want to fade red tones in your hair, there are several methods you can try. Here's a step-by-step guide to fading red tones in hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in fading red tones in hair is to wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove build-up and residue from your hair, which can help to strip away some of the red dye. Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp and hair, and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Apply a Color Remover

If you still have a lot of red tones in your hair after washing it, you may want to try a color remover. Color removers are designed to strip away hair dye without damaging your hair. Follow the instructions on the packaging carefully, and be sure to wear gloves and protect your clothing and skin.

Step 3: Use a Hair Toner

If you've successfully removed some of the red dye from your hair, you may notice that your hair looks brassy or yellow. This is because red dye often leaves warm tones behind. To neutralize these tones, you can use a hair toner. Hair toners come in many different shades, but if you want to neutralize warm tones, look for a toner with a blue or purple base.

Step 4: Wait and Repeat

After you've used a color remover and a toner, you may notice that your hair is less red and more neutral. However, if you still have some red tones left, you may need to repeat the process. It's important to wait at least a week between each treatment, to avoid damaging your hair.

Tips for Fading Red Tones in Hair

If you want to fade red tones in your hair, there are some tips you can follow to make the process easier and more effective. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, which can damage your hair and cause color to fade more quickly.
  • Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray when you're outside, to avoid sun damage.
  • Consider using a color-depositing shampoo or conditioner to maintain your new hair color.

FAQs About Fading Red Tones in Hair

Q: Can I fade red tones in my hair without using a color remover?

A: Yes, you can try washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo and using a hair toner to neutralize warm tones. However, if you have a lot of red dye in your hair, a color remover may be more effective.

Q: Will fading red tones damage my hair?

A: If you follow the instructions carefully and use gentle, sulfate-free products, fading red tones should not damage your hair. However, if you use harsh chemicals or don't follow the instructions, you could damage your hair.

Q: How long will it take to fade red tones in my hair?

A: It depends on how much red dye you have in your hair, how often you wash your hair, and how well you follow the instructions. It could take a few weeks or several months to completely fade red tones in your hair.

Hair Care After Fading Red Tones

After you've successfully faded red tones in your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can enjoy your new, faded hair color for a long time to come.
