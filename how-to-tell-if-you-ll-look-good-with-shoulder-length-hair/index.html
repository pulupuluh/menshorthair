How To Tell If You ll Look Good With Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Aprilia Jun 08, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you considering cutting your hair to a shoulder length? You're not alone. Many women are opting for this length as it's versatile, easy to maintain, and suits most face shapes. However, it's normal to have doubts about the right length that will look good on you. This blog post will guide you on how to tell if you'll look good with shoulder-length hair.

Determine Your Face Shape

Your face shape plays a significant role in determining the right haircut. Shoulder-length hair looks good on most face shapes, but some styles may be more flattering than others. Here are a few guidelines to follow:

  • Oval: Oval faces have balanced proportions, and most haircuts suit this face shape. You can choose any shoulder-length cut that you like.
  • Round: Round faces are widest at the cheeks, and a shoulder-length cut with layers that start at the jawline can slim down the face.
  • Square: Square faces have a strong jawline and forehead, so a shoulder-length cut with soft layers around the face can soften your features.
  • Heart: Heart faces have a wide forehead and narrow chin, so a shoulder-length bob with side-swept bangs can balance out the face.
  • Long: Long faces have a long, narrow shape, so a shoulder-length cut with layers and waves can add volume and width to the face.

Remember, these are just guidelines, and you should choose a haircut that you feel comfortable with and suits your personality.

Consider Your Hair Type

Another factor to consider is your hair type. Shoulder-length hair suits all hair types, but some styles may work better than others:

  • Straight: Shoulder-length hair can look sleek and polished on straight hair. Consider adding layers to add volume and movement to your hair.
  • Wavy: Shoulder-length hair can enhance natural waves and give them more definition. Consider getting a layered cut to add texture and volume.
  • Curly: Shoulder-length hair can look beautiful on curly hair. Consider getting a shoulder-length cut with layers to prevent your hair from looking too heavy.
  • Thick: Shoulder-length hair can manage thick hair and prevent it from looking too bulky. Consider getting a layered cut to add movement and texture to your hair.
  • Thin: Shoulder-length hair can add volume and body to thin hair. Consider getting a layered cut to add dimension to your hair.

Factor in Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is another important factor to consider when choosing a shoulder-length haircut. Here are a few questions to ask yourself:

  • Do you have time to style your hair? If you have a busy schedule, opt for a low-maintenance cut that's easy to style.
  • Will you be exercising regularly? If you're an active person, consider getting a shorter cut that's easy to manage and won't get in the way when you exercise.
  • Do you work in a professional environment? If you work in a corporate environment, opt for a sleek and polished shoulder-length cut that's appropriate for the workplace.
  • Do you want to experiment with different styles? If you're someone who likes to try new things, opt for a versatile shoulder-length cut that can be styled in different ways.

Measure Your Neck Length

Another factor to consider is your neck length. A short neck may not look good with a shoulder-length cut, while a longer neck can carry off this length effortlessly. Here's how to measure your neck length:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror with your hair pulled back.
  2. Place a ruler at the base of your neck, where it meets your shoulders.
  3. Measure the distance from the ruler to the bottom of your earlobe.

If the distance is less than 2.5 inches, consider getting a shorter cut that won't overwhelm your neck.

Try Wigs or Virtual Makeovers

If you're still unsure whether a shoulder-length cut will suit you, consider trying on wigs or using virtual makeover apps. These tools can give you a good idea of how you'll look with different hairstyles and colors.

Getting the Haircut

Once you've decided on the right length and style, it's time to get the haircut. Here are a few tips to ensure you get the best possible result:

  • Research your hairstylist: Look for a hairstylist who has experience cutting shoulder-length hair and has a good reputation.
  • Bring a picture: Bring a picture of the haircut you want to your hairstylist to ensure you're on the same page.
  • Communicate: Communicate with your hairstylist about your hair type, face shape, and lifestyle to ensure you get a cut that suits you.
  • Maintain the cut: Schedule regular trims every six to eight weeks to keep your shoulder-length cut looking fresh.

Styling Shoulder-Length Hair

Shoulder-length hair is versatile and easy to style. Here are a few styling tips:

  • Blow-dry: Blow-dry your hair with a round brush for a sleek and polished look.
  • Curl: Use a curling iron to add loose waves to your hair.
  • Braid: Braid your hair for a boho chic look.
  • Ponytail: Pull your hair back into a low ponytail for a classic look.

Hair Care Tips for Shoulder-Length Hair

Here are a few tips to keep your shoulder-length hair looking healthy and shiny:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Wash your hair every two to three days to keep it clean and fresh.
  • Use a good conditioner: Use a conditioner that suits your hair type to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid heat styling: Limit heat styling to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to prevent sun damage.
  • Get regular trims: Schedule regular trims to keep split ends at bay.

FAQs

Will shoulder-length hair suit me if I have a round face?

Yes, shoulder-length hair can look good on a round face. Opt for a layered cut that starts at the jawline to slim down your face.

Can I get a shoulder-length cut if I have thin hair?

Yes, shoulder-length hair can add volume and body to thin hair. Consider getting a layered cut to add dimension to your hair.

How often should I trim my shoulder-length hair?

Schedule regular trims every six to eight weeks to keep your shoulder-length cut looking fresh.

Conclusion

Choosing the right haircut can be daunting, but with these tips, you can tell if you'll look good with shoulder-length hair. Remember to factor in your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, and neck length when choosing a cut. Once you've gotten the haircut, maintain it with regular trims and proper hair care to keep it looking healthy and
