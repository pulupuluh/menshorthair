How To Match Natural Hair Color A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When it comes to coloring your hair, one of the most important things to consider is how to match your natural hair color. Whether you're touching up your roots or going for a completely new look, matching your natural hair color is crucial for achieving a seamless, natural-looking result. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of matching your natural hair color, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

How to Match Natural Hair Color: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Determine Your Natural Hair Color

The first step in matching your natural hair color is to determine what that color actually is. Take a close look at your hair in natural light - is it dark, medium, or light? Is it warm (with reddish or golden undertones) or cool (with ashy or blue undertones)? Once you've identified your natural hair color, you can use that as a starting point for selecting your hair dye.

Step 2: Choose a Shade

Once you know your natural hair color, it's time to choose a shade that matches it as closely as possible. Look for hair dye that's specifically labeled as "natural" or "neutral" - these shades are less likely to have strong undertones that could clash with your natural color. It's also a good idea to avoid shades that are more than two shades lighter or darker than your natural color, as these can be difficult to blend seamlessly.

Step 3: Do a Strand Test

Before you apply hair dye all over your head, it's important to do a strand test to make sure you've chosen the right shade. Cut a small section of hair from the back of your head and apply the hair dye according to the instructions on the package. Keep an eye on the color as it develops - if it's too light or too dark, you may need to choose a different shade.

Step 4: Apply the Hair Dye

Once you've chosen the right shade, it's time to apply the hair dye all over your head. Follow the instructions on the package carefully, making sure to apply the dye evenly and thoroughly. Use a comb to distribute the dye through your hair and avoid any patches or streaks.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

After the recommended processing time, rinse out the hair dye thoroughly and follow up with a conditioning treatment. This will help to lock in the color and keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Tips for Matching Natural Hair Color

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when matching your natural hair color:

  • Consider the season - your hair may look different in the summer sun than it does in winter light.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help - a professional stylist can help you choose the right shade and apply the dye evenly.
  • Be patient - it may take a few tries to get the color just right, so don't give up if your first attempt isn't perfect.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about matching natural hair color:

Can I dye my hair if I have highlights?

Yes, you can still dye your hair if you have highlights - just make sure to choose a shade that matches your natural hair color, rather than the color of your highlights.

Can I dye my hair if I have gray hair?

Yes, you can dye your hair if you have gray hair - just make sure to choose a hair dye that's specifically formulated for gray coverage.

How often should I touch up my roots?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how noticeable your roots are. As a general rule, you should touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain a seamless, natural-looking color.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking and feeling healthy after coloring, here are some tips to follow:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.
  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and moisturized.

By following these tips and using the right hair dye, you can easily match your natural hair color and achieve a beautiful, natural-looking result. Happy coloring!
