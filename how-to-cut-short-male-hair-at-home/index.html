How To Cut Short Male Hair At Home

Written by Robby Jun 17, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you’ve never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting short male hair at home can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hair. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of how to cut short male hair at home, step by step.

Description

Short hair is a popular choice for men, as it’s easy to maintain and style. However, regular trips to the barbershop can quickly add up in cost. By cutting your hair at home, you can save money and time. Plus, it’s a great skill to have in your back pocket.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need to gather the right tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Cape or towel
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in, and that your scissors are sharp.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it’s damp. Comb your hair in the direction it naturally falls, and clip any longer sections out of the way. Put on your cape or wrap a towel around your shoulders to catch any hair clippings.

Step 3: Choose Your Clipper Guard

Decide how short you want your hair to be, and choose a clipper guard accordingly. A #3 or #4 guard will leave your hair about 1/4 inch long, while a #1 or #2 guard will leave it closer to 1/8 inch. If you’re not sure, start with a longer guard and work your way down.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the back of your head, use the clippers to cut your hair in an upward motion against the direction of hair growth. Move the clippers up and out from your head, using a continuous motion. Work in small sections, and check your progress in the mirror frequently.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Top

Once you’ve finished the back of your head, move on to the sides and top of your head. Use the same technique, but blend the hair so that there are no harsh lines between the different sections. Use the comb to lift the hair and the scissors to trim any longer sections that the clippers missed.

Step 6: Trim the Neckline

Use the clippers or scissors to trim your neckline. Start by defining the hairline at the back of your neck, and work your way around to the sides. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers or scissors to trim any hair below the hairline.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, use the comb and scissors to clean up any stray hairs. Take a shower to wash away any hair clippings, and style your hair as desired.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you’re not sure how short you want your hair to be.
  • Work in small sections and check your progress frequently.
  • Use scissors to blend the different sections of your hair and create a natural look.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you with hard-to-reach areas.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair without clippers?

While clippers are the easiest and most efficient way to cut short hair, you can also use scissors to achieve a similar look. However, it may take longer and require more skill.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, short hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners too often.
  • Use a quality hair product, like a pomade or styling cream, to add texture and hold to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your desired length and shape.

Cutting short male hair at home can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut in the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to keep your hair looking its best.
