How To Fade Hair Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are looking to achieve a stylish and trendy haircut, a fade hair undercut could be the perfect choice for you. However, maintaining the perfect fade can be quite challenging, especially if you don't know how to do it right. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to fade hair undercut in a relaxed English language. So, let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fade Hair Undercut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Fading Hair Undercut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Fading Hair Undercut

Description

A fade hair undercut is a popular haircut where the hair on the sides and back of the head is shaved or cut very short, while the hair on top is left longer. The hair at the back and sides gradually fades into the longer hair on top, creating a smooth transition between the two lengths. This style is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance yet trendy look.

How to Fade Hair Undercut

The process of fading hair undercut involves gradually blending the hair on the sides and back into the longer hair on top. To achieve this, you'll need a good quality clipper, a comb, and a pair of scissors. Here are the steps:

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it.
  2. Use the clipper to shave the sides and back of your head, leaving about an inch of hair at the top.
  3. Choose the length of the clipper guard that you want to use for the fade. Start with a longer guard, such as a #4, and work your way down to a shorter guard, such as a #1 or #2, as you get closer to the hairline.
  4. Use the clipper without the guard to create a bald fade around the hairline and the nape of the neck.
  5. Blend the hair on the sides and back into the longer hair on top using the scissors and comb. Start by cutting the hair with the longer guard and work your way down to the shorter guard.
  6. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle to create a smooth transition between the different lengths.
  7. Repeat the process on the other side of your head.
  8. Check your work in a mirror to ensure that the fade is even on both sides.
  9. Use the clipper to trim the hair on top to the desired length.
  10. Finish by styling your hair as desired.

Tips for Fading Hair Undercut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one as you get closer to the hairline. This will help you achieve a more gradual and natural-looking fade.
  • Use the scissors and comb to blend the hair on the sides and back into the longer hair on top. Cutting at an angle can help create a smoother transition between the different lengths.
  • Take your time and be patient. Fading hair undercut requires precision and attention to detail. Rushing the process can result in an uneven or choppy fade.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a fade haircut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade starts to grow out. Typically, you should get a fade haircut every 2-4 weeks to maintain the perfect fade.

Q: What kind of hair works best for a fade undercut?

A: A fade undercut works best for hair that is thick and straight. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may need to straighten it or use a relaxer to achieve a smooth and even fade.

Q: Can I do a fade undercut at home?

A: Yes, you can do a fade undercut at home, but it requires some skill and practice. It's best to start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one as you get more comfortable with the process.

Hair Care After Fading Hair Undercut

After fading your hair undercut, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain the perfect fade. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as a blow dryer or flat iron, as they can damage your hair and cause it to break.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.

Congratulations, you now know how to fade hair undercut like a pro! By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and trendy look that is sure to turn heads. Happy fading!
