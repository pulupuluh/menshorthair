Are Braids Allowed In The Army

Description

The United States Army has strict grooming standards that all soldiers must adhere to. Hair is a significant part of the regulations. There are specific guidelines for how hair should be styled, cut, and maintained. Many women in the army prefer to wear braids as a protective style. However, the question arises, "Are braids allowed in the army?"

How to

When it comes to wearing braids in the army, there are specific guidelines that must be followed. The guidelines are as follows:

  • Braids must be in uniform size, shape, and have even tension.
  • Braids must be tucked in and not hang freely.
  • Braids must be styled to conform to the head.
  • Braids must not protrude from the head or create an unusual appearance.
  • Braids must not interfere with the proper wear of any military headgear.

Step by step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to style your hair in braids for the army:

  1. Part your hair into sections and comb through each section to detangle.
  2. Begin braiding each section of hair into uniform size, shape, and even tension.
  3. Tuck the ends of each braid in and secure with a bobby pin.
  4. Style the braids to conform to your head shape.
  5. Ensure that the braids do not protrude from your head or create an unusual appearance.
  6. Put on any military headgear you are required to wear and ensure that the braids do not interfere with the proper wear of the headgear.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when wearing braids in the army:

  • Wash your hair regularly and keep it clean to avoid any hygiene issues.
  • Avoid using hair products that can leave residue or build-up on your hair.
  • Ensure that your braids are not too tight, as this can cause hair breakage and damage over time.
  • If you have any concerns about the appearance of your braids or whether they meet the army's guidelines, speak to your commanding officer for clarification.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about braids in the army:

Q: Can I wear my braids in any style I want?

A: No, your braids must be styled in a way that conforms to the army's guidelines. They must be uniform in size and shape and have even tension. They cannot protrude from the head or create an unusual appearance.

Q: Can I wear extensions or add hair to my braids?

A: Yes, you can wear extensions or add hair to your braids as long as they meet the army's guidelines.

Q: Can I wear my braids in a bun?

A: No, braids must be tucked in and not hang freely. They must be styled to conform to the head and not interfere with the proper wear of any military headgear.

Hair Care

When wearing braids in the army, it is essential to take care of your hair to avoid any hygiene issues or damage. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing braids:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can leave residue or build-up on your hair.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized by applying a light oil to your hair and scalp regularly.
  • Take breaks between wearing braids to allow your hair to rest and recover.
  • Avoid tight braids, as they can cause hair breakage and damage over time.

In conclusion, braids are allowed in the army as long as they meet the specific guidelines outlined by the military. By following the guidelines and taking proper care of your hair, you can wear braids as a protective style while serving your country.
