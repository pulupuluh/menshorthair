How To Style Curly Hair While Growing It Out

Written by Oliver Jul 12, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you in the process of growing out your curly hair? It can be a challenging and frustrating task, especially when trying to style it during the awkward in-between stages. But fear not, we’ve got you covered with tips and tricks on how to style your curly hair while growing it out!

Table of Contents

  2. Step By Step Guide
  3. Tips for Styling Curly Hair
  4. FAQs

How to Style Curly Hair While Growing it Out

The key to styling your curly hair while growing it out is to embrace your natural curls and work with them. Here are some styling tips to get you through the awkward stages:

1. Use a Diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser to help enhance your curls and reduce frizz. This attachment disperses the heat and air evenly, which helps to prevent heat damage and maintain your natural curl pattern.

2. Embrace Protective Styles

Protective styles like braids, twists, and buns can help to protect your curly hair from damage while it’s growing out. These styles can also help to stretch your curls and make them more manageable.

3. Use Hair Accessories

Headbands, scarves, and hair clips can be great accessories for styling curly hair. They can help to keep your hair out of your face and add some extra flair to your look.

4. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage. Try to limit your hair washing to once or twice a week and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

5. Invest in a Good Leave-In Conditioner

A good leave-in conditioner can help to hydrate and define your curls while also providing some hold. Look for a leave-in conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair.

Step By Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to style your curly hair while growing it out:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Be sure to rinse it out completely.

Step 2: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a generous amount of leave-in conditioner to your damp hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair, scrunching it as you go to enhance your curls. Once your hair is dry, you can use hair accessories like headbands or clips to style it.

Tips for Styling Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling curly hair:

1. Use a Microfiber Towel

Ditch the rough towel and opt for a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair. These materials are gentler on your curls and help to reduce frizz.

2. Don’t Overdo It on Products

While it’s important to use products to hydrate and define your curls, it’s easy to go overboard. Too much product can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can slow down your hair growth progress. Aim to get a trim every 8-12 weeks.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to grow out curly hair?

A: The length of time it takes to grow out curly hair varies from person to person. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it could take anywhere from 6 months to a few years to grow out your curly hair.

Q: How can I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

A: To prevent frizz, avoid touching your hair too much, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer, and sleep with a silk or satin pillowcase.

Curly Hair Care

Caring for curly hair is essential to maintaining healthy, strong curls. Here are some tips for curly hair care:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it’s important to deep condition regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair From Heat Damage

When using heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low or medium.

4. Be Gentle When Detangling

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.

By following these tips and tricks, you can style your curly hair while growing it out with confidence. Remember, embrace your natural curls and work with them to achieve your desired look!
