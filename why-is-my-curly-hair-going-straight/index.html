Why Is My Curly Hair Going Straight

Written by Petter Apr 27, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have curly hair and notice that it’s going straight, you might be wondering what’s going on. There are a few reasons why this can happen, from changes in your hair care routine to hormonal shifts. Keep reading to find out what could be causing your curly hair to go straight, and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents

  • Cause #1: Heat Damage
  • Cause #2: Changes in Hormones
  • Cause #3: Overprocessing
  • Tips for Managing Straightening Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Straightening Hair

Cause #1: Heat Damage

One of the most common reasons why curly hair goes straight is heat damage. If you’re using hot styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers without heat protectant, you could be causing damage to your hair cuticles. This can lead to hair that’s more prone to breakage, frizz, and straightening.

To prevent heat damage, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum before using hot styling tools. You can also try air-drying your hair or using heat-free styling methods like twists, braids, or bantu knots.

Cause #2: Changes in Hormones

Another reason why curly hair might go straight is changes in hormones. Hormonal shifts like pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid problems can affect the texture of your hair. This is because hormones can affect the shape of your hair follicles, which can lead to changes in the curl pattern.

If you suspect that hormonal changes might be causing your curly hair to go straight, talk to your doctor. They can help you identify any underlying health issues and suggest treatments or lifestyle changes that could help.

Cause #3: Overprocessing

Overprocessing your hair with chemical treatments like relaxers, perms, or keratin treatments can also lead to straightening. These treatments work by breaking down the bonds in your hair, which can lead to hair that’s straighter and more fragile.

If you want to maintain your curly hair, avoid overprocessing with chemical treatments. Instead, try natural hair care methods like co-washing, deep conditioning, and using sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.

Tips for Managing Straightening Hair

If you’re dealing with straightening hair, there are a few tips you can use to manage your hair and keep it healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Try using a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair to help maintain your curl pattern.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep.

FAQs

Q: Can stress cause curly hair to go straight?

A: Stress can’t directly cause your hair to go straight, but it can lead to hair loss or breakage, which can affect the texture of your hair.

Q: Can using too much hairspray cause hair to go straight?

A: Using too much hairspray can cause buildup on your hair, which can make it look and feel stiff or flat. However, it’s unlikely to cause your hair to go straight on its own.

Hair Care for Straightening Hair

If you’re dealing with straightening hair, it’s important to take extra care to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you do.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that could cause tension or breakage, like tight ponytails or braids.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can help maintain healthy, beautiful hair – whether it’s curly or straight.
