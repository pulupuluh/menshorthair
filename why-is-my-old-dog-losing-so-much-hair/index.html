Why Is My Old Dog Losing So Much Hair

Written by Oliver Sep 12, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re a pet owner, you know that shedding is a natural part of having a furry companion. However, when your old dog starts losing more hair than usual, it can be concerning. In this article, we’ll discuss why your old dog may be losing so much hair and what you can do to help.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Loss in Old Dogs
  • Symptoms of Hair Loss in Old Dogs
  • Diagnosing Hair Loss in Old Dogs
  • Treatment for Hair Loss in Old Dogs
  • Preventing Hair Loss in Old Dogs
  • FAQs
  • Conclusion

Causes of Hair Loss in Old Dogs

There are many reasons why your old dog may be losing hair. Here are some of the most common causes:

1. Age

As dogs get older, their bodies go through a lot of changes. One of these changes is a decrease in the production of hormones that keep their coat healthy and thick. This can result in hair loss.

2. Allergies

Old dogs can develop allergies just like humans. These allergies can be caused by a variety of things, including food, pollen, and dust. Allergies can cause your dog to scratch and chew at their skin, resulting in hair loss.

3. Infections

Infections, such as ringworm and mange, can cause hair loss in dogs. These infections are often accompanied by other symptoms, such as itching and redness.

4. Stress

Stress can cause hair loss in dogs just like it can in humans. If your old dog has experienced a change in their environment or routine, it can cause them to become stressed and lose hair.

5. Poor Nutrition

If your old dog isn’t getting the proper nutrients in their diet, it can lead to hair loss. Make sure your dog is eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Symptoms of Hair Loss in Old Dogs

If your old dog is losing hair, you may notice some of the following symptoms:

  • Bald patches on their coat
  • Excessive shedding
  • Redness or inflammation of the skin
  • Scabs or sores on the skin
  • Itching or scratching

Diagnosing Hair Loss in Old Dogs

If you notice that your old dog is losing hair, it’s important to take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis. Your vet will likely perform a physical exam and may recommend blood tests or a skin biopsy to determine the cause of the hair loss.

Treatment for Hair Loss in Old Dogs

The treatment for hair loss in old dogs depends on the underlying cause. If your dog has an infection, they may need antibiotics or antifungal medication. If they have allergies, your vet may recommend allergy medication or a change in their diet. For age-related hair loss, there may not be much you can do to stop it.

Preventing Hair Loss in Old Dogs

While you may not be able to prevent age-related hair loss, there are things you can do to help keep your old dog’s coat healthy:

  • Feed them a balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals
  • Brush their coat regularly to remove loose hair
  • Bathe them regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Keep their environment clean and free of allergens

FAQs

1. Is hair loss in old dogs normal?

Some hair loss in old dogs is normal, but excessive hair loss may be a sign of an underlying issue.

2. Can stress cause hair loss in dogs?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss in dogs just like it can in humans.

3. How can I prevent hair loss in my old dog?

You can help prevent hair loss in your old dog by feeding them a balanced diet, brushing their coat regularly, bathing them with a gentle shampoo, and keeping their environment clean.

Conclusion

If you’re concerned about your old dog losing hair, it’s important to take them to the vet for a proper diagnosis. While some hair loss in old dogs is normal, excessive hair loss may be a sign of an underlying issue. By taking steps to keep your dog’s coat healthy, you can help prevent hair loss and keep your furry friend looking and feeling their best.
