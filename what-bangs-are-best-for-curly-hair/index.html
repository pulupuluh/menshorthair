What Bangs Are Best For Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On good days, it looks voluminous and bouncy. On bad days, it can be frizzy and unmanageable. Adding bangs to the mix can seem like a risky move, but with the right cut, they can actually enhance your curly locks. In this article, we will discuss what bangs are best for curly hair, including how to style them and care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Bangs for Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide to Cutting Curly Bangs
  • Tips for Styling Curly Bangs

  • Hair Care for Curly Hair with Bangs

Description

Bangs are a great way to switch up your look and add some personality to your curly hair. However, not all bangs are created equal. Straight across bangs, for example, can be difficult to style with curly hair, as they tend to separate and create unwanted gaps. Instead, curly hair looks best with side-swept or textured bangs that blend seamlessly with the rest of your hair.

How to Choose the Right Bangs for Curly Hair

When it comes to choosing bangs for curly hair, the key is to go for a cut that complements your unique curls. If you have tight ringlets, for example, consider longer, side-swept bangs that can blend in with your curls. On the other hand, if you have looser, more relaxed curls, try textured bangs that can add some movement and dimension to your hair.

It's also important to consider your face shape when choosing bangs. If you have a round face, go for longer, textured bangs that can elongate your face. If you have a square or angular face, consider softer, side-swept bangs that can soften your features.

Step by Step Guide to Cutting Curly Bangs

While it's always best to get your bangs cut by a professional stylist, you can also cut them yourself at home. Here's how:

  1. Start by sectioning off the front part of your hair that you want to cut into bangs.
  2. Use a spray bottle to dampen your hair.
  3. Comb your hair straight down and use hair cutting scissors to trim the ends of your hair. Start with small snips and work your way up, checking the length frequently.
  4. Once you've trimmed your hair to the desired length, use your fingers to separate and texturize the ends of your bangs.

Tips for Styling Curly Bangs

Once you've got your curly bangs, here are some tips for styling them:

  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your bangs without disrupting your curls.
  • Apply a curl-enhancing product, such as mousse or gel, to your bangs to help define your curls.
  • Use a curling iron or wand to touch up any curls that may have fallen flat.
  • Consider using bobby pins or hair clips to pin your bangs to the side on days when they just won't cooperate.

FAQs about Bangs and Curly Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about bangs and curly hair:

Can I straighten my curly bangs?

Yes, you can straighten your curly bangs, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging your hair.

What if I don't like my curly bangs?

If you're not happy with your curly bangs, don't panic. Bangs grow out relatively quickly, so you can always wait a few weeks and try a different style.

Hair Care for Curly Hair with Bangs

Maintaining healthy, bouncy curls can be a challenge, but with the right hair care routine, you can keep your curls looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can create frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your ends to help combat dryness and frizz.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With the right cut and styling techniques, bangs can be a fun and playful addition to your curly hair. Just remember to choose a style that complements your unique curls and follow a good hair care routine to keep your locks looking their best.
