How To Curl Hair 1940S Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

The 1940s were a decade of glamour and sophistication, and the hairstyles of the time reflected that. One of the most iconic looks of the era was the soft, voluminous curls that were often seen on Hollywood starlets. If you're looking to recreate this classic style for a special occasion or just for fun, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to curl hair 1940s style, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Curl Hair 1940s Style

The first step in achieving 1940s curls is to make sure your hair is clean and dry. You'll want to start with a good shampoo and conditioner, and then blow dry your hair until it's completely dry. Once your hair is dry, it's time to start curling.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • A curling iron with a 1-inch barrel
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hairspray
  • Sectioning clips

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to create 1940s curls:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair into small sections. Use the sectioning clips to keep each section in place.
  2. Spray each section with heat protectant spray. This will help protect your hair from damage and keep your curls looking shiny and healthy.
  3. Take one section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, starting at the root and working your way down to the ends. Hold the curling iron in place for about 10 seconds, or until the hair feels warm to the touch.
  4. Gently release the curl from the curling iron and use your fingers to shape the curl into the desired shape. Pin the curl in place with a bobby pin or clip and allow it to cool completely.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 with each section of hair, working your way around your head until all of your hair is curled and pinned in place.
  6. Once all of your hair is curled and pinned, spray it with hairspray. Be sure to hold the can about 12 inches away from your head and spray lightly, so as not to weigh down your curls.
  7. Allow your hair to cool completely. This will help set the curls and make them last longer.
  8. Once your hair is cool, remove the bobby pins or clips and gently brush through your curls with a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb. This will give your curls a softer, more natural look.
  9. Finish with a final spritz of hairspray to hold your curls in place.

Tips for Getting the Perfect 1940s Curls

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect 1940s curls:

  • Use a curling iron with a 1-inch barrel. This will give you soft, natural-looking curls that are typical of the era.
  • Don't curl your hair all the way to the ends. Instead, leave the ends straight for a more modern twist on the classic style.
  • For a more authentic look, part your hair on the side and sweep the curls over one shoulder.
  • If you have naturally straight hair, consider using hot rollers or a small-barrel curling iron to create curls that will hold all day.

FAQs

Q: How long will 1940s curls last?

A: With the right products and techniques, 1940s curls can last all day and into the evening.

Q: Can I create 1940s curls with a flat iron?

A: While it's possible to create curls with a flat iron, it's not the best tool for the job. A curling iron with a 1-inch barrel will give you much better results.

Q: Can I create 1940s curls on short hair?

A: Yes! While the look is often associated with long hair, you can create beautiful 1940s curls on short hair as well.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Curls

Now that you've got your 1940s curls, you'll want to make sure they last as long as possible. Here are some tips for maintaining your curls:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much. The oils from your hands can weigh down your curls and make them lose their shape.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase. This will help prevent frizz and keep your curls looking smooth and shiny.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often. This can strip your hair of its natural oils and make your curls dry and frizzy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to create beautiful 1940s curls that will turn heads and make you feel like a Hollywood starlet. Have fun experimenting with different styles and enjoy your new retro look!
