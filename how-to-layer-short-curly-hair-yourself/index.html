How To Layer Short Curly Hair Yourself

Written by Luffy Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have short curly hair, you might have noticed that it can be challenging to style it in a way that is both flattering and easy to maintain. One option that works well for many people is to layer their hair, which can add volume, shape, and definition to your curls. In this article, we'll go over how to layer short curly hair yourself, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Short Curly Hair Yourself
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Layer Short Curly Hair Yourself

The first step in layering your short curly hair is to decide what kind of layers you want. Some people prefer long layers that blend naturally into their hair, while others like shorter, more defined layers that add texture and volume. You can also choose to layer your hair all over or just in certain areas, like around your face or at the crown of your head.

Once you've decided on the type of layers you want, you'll need to gather some tools. You'll need a good pair of scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to help section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror set up so you can see the back of your head as you work.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for curly hair. This will help to keep your curls defined and moisturized, which will make them easier to work with.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. This will help to prevent breakage and make it easier to section off your hair for cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your hair clips to section off your hair into manageable sections. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, clipping up the top layers so that you can work on the bottom layers first.

Step 4: Cut Your Layers

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, use your scissors to cut the length of your hair to the desired length. Then, hold small sections of hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create the layers. Be sure to cut each section to the same length so that your layers are even.

Step 5: Move to the Next Section

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the next layer of hair and repeat the process until you've cut all of your layers.

Step 6: Check for Balance

Once you've finished cutting all of your layers, check to make sure that they are even and balanced. If you notice any areas that are longer or shorter than the rest, you can make small adjustments by cutting a little more off or blending the layers together.

Tips

  • When cutting your layers, it's better to start with less hair and work your way up to more. This will help you avoid cutting off too much hair at once.
  • If you're unsure about how to cut your layers, start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter layers. You can always cut more off later if you want to.
  • If you have naturally curly hair, it's a good idea to cut your hair while it's dry. This will help you see how your curls will fall and prevent any surprises when your hair dries.

FAQ

How often should I layer my short curly hair?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers start to lose their shape. Some people need to layer their hair every six weeks, while others can go three months or more between cuts.

Can I layer my hair if it's already been cut?

Yes, you can layer your hair at any time, even if it's already been cut. Just be sure to follow the same steps as above and take your time to ensure that your layers are even and balanced.

Hair Care

Once you've layered your short curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and health. Be sure to use products that are designed for curly hair, and avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons too often. You can also refresh your curls by using a leave-in conditioner or curl refresher spray throughout the day.

By following these steps, you can learn how to layer short curly hair yourself and achieve a look that is both stylish and easy to maintain. With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to create the perfect layers for your hair type and personal style.
