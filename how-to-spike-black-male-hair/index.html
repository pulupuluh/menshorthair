How To Spike Black Male Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 30, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you're looking to add some edge and personality to your hairstyle, spiking your hair is a great option. While this style can seem intimidating, it's actually quite easy to achieve with the right products and techniques. In this article, we'll break down how to spike black male hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Spike Black Male Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Spiking Black Male Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Spiked Hair

How to Spike Black Male Hair

Before we jump into the steps, it's important to understand that spiking your hair requires a certain length and texture. Ideally, your hair should be at least 2 inches long and have some natural texture or wave to it. If your hair is very straight or short, it may be difficult to achieve the desired look.

Now, let's get into the steps:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that may be weighing your hair down.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product that will help to give your hair hold and texture. There are a variety of products that work well for spiking, including pomades, waxes, and gels. Be sure to choose a product that is designed for your hair type and desired hold level.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer and a vented brush, blow dry your hair in the direction that you want it to spike. This will help to add volume and texture to your hair, making it easier to spike.

Step 4: Spike Your Hair

Now comes the fun part! Using your fingers or a comb, apply the styling product to your hair, focusing on the sections that you want to spike. Use your fingers to shape the spikes, or use a comb for a more precise look. Be sure to work quickly, as the product will begin to dry and harden.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finally, finish your look with a light mist of hairspray. This will help to hold your hair in place and prevent it from falling flat throughout the day.

Step-by-Step Guide

Let's break down the steps in more detail:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by wetting your hair and applying a quarter-sized amount of shampoo to your scalp. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, working it through your hair to the ends. Rinse thoroughly, then apply a quarter-sized amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner in your hair for 1-2 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

After towel-drying your hair, apply a pea-sized amount of styling product to your fingertips. Rub your hands together to distribute the product, then apply it to your hair, focusing on the sections that you want to spike. If you have thick or curly hair, you may need to use more product.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer and a vented brush, blow dry your hair in the direction that you want it to spike. Use the brush to lift your hair at the roots, creating volume and texture. If you have curly hair, you may want to use a diffuser attachment to help define your curls.

Step 4: Spike Your Hair

Now it's time to spike your hair! Use your fingers or a comb to apply the styling product to your hair, focusing on the sections that you want to spike. Work quickly, as the product will begin to dry and harden. Use your fingers to shape the spikes, or use a comb for a more precise look.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

To finish your look, spray a light mist of hairspray over your hair. This will help to hold your spikes in place and prevent them from falling flat throughout the day. Be sure to hold the hairspray can at least 6 inches away from your head to avoid a sticky, crunchy finish.

Tips for Spiking Black Male Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect spiked hairstyle:

  • Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and desired hold level.
  • Don't be afraid to use your fingers to shape your spikes - this will help to create a more natural, textured look.
  • If you have a cowlick or other unruly sections of hair, consider using a stronger hold product in those areas.
  • Be mindful of the weather when spiking your hair - humidity and rain can cause your spikes to fall flat.

FAQs

Is spiking my hair damaging?

When done in moderation, spiking your hair should not cause any damage. However, using excessive amounts of product or pulling your hair too tightly can lead to breakage and hair loss.

How long does spiked hair last?

Spiked hair can last anywhere from a few hours to a full day, depending on the strength of the product and the weather conditions. You may need to touch up your spikes throughout the day to keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care for Spiked Hair

While spiking your hair can be a fun and edgy style, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while spiking:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any product buildup.
  • Deep condition your hair once a month to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid pulling your hair too tightly or using excessive amounts of product.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to spike your black male hair with confidence and ease. Remember to choose the right products for your hair type and experiment with different techniques to find the look that works best for you. Happy spiking!
