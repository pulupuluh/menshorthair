How To Train Hair To Go Back Woman A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 30, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a woman looking to train your hair to go back, you're in the right place! This guide will take you through the steps needed to train your hair to go back, including tips and FAQs. Whether you're looking to switch up your hairstyle or need a new look for a special occasion, this guide will help you achieve the desired look.

How to Train Hair to Go Back Woman

Training your hair to go back is a process that takes time and patience. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you begin training your hair to go back, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo to remove any dirt or buildup.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to your hair while it's still damp. This will help hold your hair in place and make it easier to train.

Step 3: Brush Your Hair Back

Using a brush, start at the front of your hairline and brush your hair back. Keep brushing until your hair is in the desired position.

Step 4: Repeat Daily

Training your hair to go back requires consistency. Repeat this process daily until your hair starts to naturally fall back.

Tips for Training Hair to Go Back

Here are some tips to help you train your hair to go back:

Tip 1: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When brushing your hair back, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent any damage or breakage.

Tip 2: Don't Overuse Styling Products

While styling products can be helpful in training your hair to go back, don't overuse them. Too much product can weigh your hair down and make it harder to train.

Tip 3: Be Patient

Training your hair to go back takes time and patience. Don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away. Keep practicing and you'll get there.

FAQs

Q: How long will it take to train my hair to go back?

A: The time it takes to train your hair to go back varies for everyone. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months.

Q: Can I train my hair to go back if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can train your hair to go back if you have curly hair. However, it may take longer to train and require more styling products.

Q: Do I need to cut my hair to train it to go back?

A: No, you don't need to cut your hair to train it to go back. However, if you have long hair, you may want to consider getting a trim to make the process easier.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair while you're training it to go back. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly helps remove any dirt or buildup that can weigh your hair down.

Tip 2: Use a Conditioner

Using a conditioner after shampooing helps keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Tip 3: Limit Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it harder to train. Limit heat styling as much as possible and use a heat protectant when necessary.

With these tips and steps, you'll be on your way to training your hair to go back in no time. Remember to be patient and consistent, and your hair will start to naturally fall back in no time.
