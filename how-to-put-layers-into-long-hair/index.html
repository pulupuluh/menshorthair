How To Put Layers Into Long Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have long hair, adding layers can give it more volume, texture, and movement. However, getting layers can be intimidating, especially if you are not sure how they will look on you. In this guide, we will show you how to put layers into long hair, step-by-step. We will also give you tips on how to care for your hair after getting layers, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Layers into Long Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQs

How to Put Layers into Long Hair

When getting layers in long hair, it is important to consider the length of the layers and where they will be placed. The layers can be short, medium, or long, and can start at different points on your hair. Here are some ways to put layers into long hair:

1. Long Layers

Long layers are the most common and versatile type of layers. They add movement and texture to the hair without removing too much length. Long layers can start at the chin, shoulder, or even lower, depending on the desired look. They can be cut straight or at an angle for a more edgy look.

2. Face-Framing Layers

Face-framing layers are shorter layers that start around the face, usually at the cheekbones or jawline. They add dimension to the hair and highlight the face. Face-framing layers can be combined with long layers or cut into a bob or lob for a more dramatic look.

3. Short Layers

Short layers are layers that are cut shorter than the rest of the hair. They are ideal for adding volume and texture to thin or fine hair. Short layers can be placed all over the hair or in specific areas, such as the crown or the back.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to put layers into long hair:

1. Choose the Length and Type of Layers

Decide on the length and type of layers you want. Consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle when choosing the layers.

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair as you normally would. It is easier to cut hair when it is clean and dry.

3. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

4. Cut the Layers

Start cutting the layers. Take a small section of hair and cut it at an angle or straight, depending on the desired look. Work your way around the head, cutting each section of hair.

5. Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by using thinning shears or a razor to soften the edges. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

6. Style Your Hair

Style your hair as you normally would. You can use a curling iron or straightening iron to enhance the layers and create more texture.

Tips for Layered Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your layered hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage the layers.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your layers.
  • Use hair products that are designed for your hair type and texture.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as it can cause breakage and damage the layers.

FAQs

Q. Will layers make my hair look thinner?

A. No, layers can actually make your hair look thicker and fuller. They add volume and texture to the hair.

Q. How often should I get my layers trimmed?

A. It is recommended to get your layers trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and health.

Q. Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with layers?

A. Yes, you can still wear your hair in a ponytail with layers. However, the layers may stick out or fall out of the ponytail, depending on their length and placement.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after getting layers:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair, as it can weigh down the layers.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as it can damage the layers.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

Adding layers to long hair can give it a new lease on life. With the right length and placement, layers can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. Follow our step-by-step guide, and use our tips and hair care advice to keep your layered hair looking its best.
