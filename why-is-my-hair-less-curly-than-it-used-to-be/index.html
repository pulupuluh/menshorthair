Why Is My Hair Less Curly Than It Used To Be

Written by Christine Jun 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As we age, our hair changes, and often, it's not for the better. One of the most common hair changes that people experience is a decrease in curliness. If you've noticed that your hair is less curly than it used to be, you're not alone. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why this happens and what you can do to get your curls back.

Description

Curly hair is caused by a natural protein called keratin. This protein is responsible for creating the shape of your hair, and it's also what gives it its strength and elasticity. As we age, our bodies produce less keratin, which can cause our hair to become weaker and less elastic. This can result in hair that is less curly and more prone to breakage.

How to

If you're wondering why your hair is less curly than it used to be, there are a few things you can do to help restore your curls. Here are some tips:

1. Use hair products specifically designed for curly hair

Using products that are designed for curly hair can help enhance your natural curls and make them more defined. Look for products that contain ingredients like glycerin, which can help lock in moisture and prevent frizz.

2. Avoid using heat styling tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause it to become less curly over time. If you must use these tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

3. Practice good hair care habits

Good hair care habits can help keep your hair healthy and strong, which can help enhance your natural curls. This includes things like washing your hair regularly, using a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoiding tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair.

Step by Step

If you're still struggling with getting your curls back, here's a step-by-step guide to help you:

Step 1: Assess your hair care routine

Take a look at your current hair care routine and see if there are any changes you can make to help promote healthy, curly hair. This may include switching to a different shampoo or conditioner, using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, or incorporating a leave-in conditioner into your routine.

Step 2: Experiment with different styling techniques

Try different styling techniques to see what works best for your hair. This may include using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer, plopping your hair with a t-shirt to help enhance your curls, or using a curl cream to define your curls.

Step 3: Get a haircut

If your hair is damaged or has split ends, getting a haircut can help remove the damaged hair and promote healthy growth. Be sure to ask your stylist for a haircut that is designed to enhance your natural curls.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get your curls back:

1. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can cause it to become dry and brittle. Try to limit washing your hair to once or twice a week.

2. Use a satin pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help reduce friction on your hair, which can help prevent breakage and frizz.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause it to become less curly over time. Be sure to protect your hair with a hat or scarf when you're outside for extended periods of time.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause my hair to become less curly?

A: Yes, stress can cause your hair to become weaker and less curly over time. If you're experiencing a lot of stress, be sure to practice self-care techniques like meditation or yoga to help reduce your stress levels.

Q: Can genetics play a role in why my hair is less curly?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the texture and curliness of your hair. If your parents or grandparents have less curly hair, it's possible that you may have inherited this trait.

Hair Care

When it comes to hair care, it's important to remember that everyone's hair is different. What works for one person may not work for another, so it's important to experiment and find what works best for your hair. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this article, you can help restore your curls and promote healthy, strong hair.
