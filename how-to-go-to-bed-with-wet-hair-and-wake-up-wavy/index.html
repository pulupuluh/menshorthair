How To Go To Bed With Wet Hair And Wake Up Wavy

Written by Christine Feb 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you want to achieve effortless, beachy waves without the use of heat styling tools, going to bed with wet hair is a great option. Not only is it a time-saver, but it's also a gentler way to style your hair. In this blog post, we'll be sharing some tips on how to go to bed with wet hair and wake up with beautiful, wavy locks.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Go to Bed with Wet Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Achieving Wavy Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips

How to Go to Bed with Wet Hair

If you're planning to go to bed with wet hair, there are a few things you need to keep in mind:

  • Make sure your hair is detangled before going to bed.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water from your hair.
  • Apply a styling product to enhance your natural waves.
  • Use a scrunchie or a hair tie to loosely tie your hair in a bun or a braid.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to go to bed with wet hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair and gently towel-drying it.
  2. Apply a styling product of your choice, such as a mousse, a gel, or a texturizing spray.
  3. Using your fingers, scrunch your hair to enhance your natural waves.
  4. Tie your hair in a loose bun or a braid using a scrunchie or a hair tie.
  5. Wrap a silk or satin scarf around your head to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  6. Go to bed and get a good night's sleep.
  7. In the morning, gently untie your hair and shake it out.
  8. Apply a texturizing spray or a sea salt spray to enhance your waves and add volume.
  9. Scrunch your hair again to distribute the product evenly.
  10. Finish off with a hairspray to hold your waves in place.

Tips for Achieving Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve beautiful, wavy hair:

  • Experiment with different styling products to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • Try different hairstyles, such as a loose bun, a braid, or a twist, to see which one gives you the best results.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair after you've styled it, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about going to bed with wet hair:

Is it bad to go to bed with wet hair?

No, it's not necessarily bad to go to bed with wet hair. However, if you have thick or long hair, it may take longer to dry and can cause discomfort while sleeping. It's also important to use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

How long does it take for hair to dry overnight?

The amount of time it takes for hair to dry overnight depends on several factors, such as the length and thickness of your hair, the humidity level in your room, and the type of hairstyle you've chosen. Generally, it can take anywhere from 4 to 8 hours for hair to air-dry overnight.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair over time.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as they can strip it of its natural oils and cause damage.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, going to bed with wet hair is a great way to achieve beautiful, wavy locks without the use of heat styling tools. By following the tips and tricks outlined in this blog post, you can wake up with gorgeous, effortless waves every morning. Remember to take care of your hair by using nourishing products, eating a healthy diet, and getting regular trims to keep it healthy and strong.
