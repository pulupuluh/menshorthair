How To Keep Curly Hair Healthy After Bleaching

Written by Frank Apr 15, 2023 · 9 min read

Bleaching curly hair can be a tricky process, as curly hair tends to be more fragile and prone to damage than straight hair. However, if done correctly and with proper care, bleached curly hair can look stunning and healthy. In this article, we will discuss how to keep curly hair healthy after bleaching, with tips and tricks to maintain your curls’ health and vitality.

Table of Contents

  • How to Bleach Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Bleached Curly Hair
  • Tips for Keeping Bleached Curly Hair Healthy
  • FAQs About Bleached Curly Hair
  • The Importance of Hair Care for Bleached Curly Hair

How to Bleach Curly Hair

Bleaching curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair, as curly hair is more delicate and prone to damage. Here is a step-by-step guide to bleaching curly hair:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional

Before bleaching your curly hair, it is best to consult with a professional stylist. They will be able to evaluate your hair’s condition and determine if it is healthy enough to be bleached.

Step 2: Pre-Treat Your Hair

Pre-treating your hair before bleaching can help protect it from damage. You can use a deep conditioning treatment or hair mask to moisturize and nourish your hair.

Step 3: Choose the Right Bleach

Not all bleaches are created equal. When bleaching curly hair, it is important to choose a bleach that is gentle and formulated for delicate hair.

Step 4: Bleach Your Hair

Follow the instructions on your bleach kit carefully, and apply the bleach to your hair. Be sure to keep an eye on your hair, and do not leave the bleach on for too long.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

Once the bleach has lifted your hair to the desired shade, rinse it out thoroughly and condition your hair. This will help restore moisture and prevent damage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Bleached Curly Hair

Caring for bleached curly hair requires a little extra effort and attention. Here is a step-by-step guide to maintaining healthy, vibrant hair after bleaching:

Step 1: Moisturize

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it is important to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

Step 2: Avoid Heat

Heat can damage curly hair, especially after it has been bleached. Avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners or curling irons, and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

Step 3: Trim Regularly

Bleaching can make curly hair more prone to split ends and breakage. Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent further damage.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair

When out in the sun or wind, protect your hair with a hat or scarf. This will prevent further damage and help your hair retain its moisture.

Step 5: Use a Protein Treatment

Curly hair needs protein to stay healthy and strong. Use a protein treatment once a month to help maintain your hair’s health and vitality.

Tips for Keeping Bleached Curly Hair Healthy

Here are some additional tips for keeping your bleached curly hair healthy and vibrant:

Tip 1: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair is prone to tangling, so use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.

Tip 2: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentler on curly hair than cotton ones, and can help prevent frizz and breakage.

Tip 3: Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals like sulfates and parabens can strip your hair of its moisture and damage it further. Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners to keep your hair healthy.

Tip 4: Protect Your Hair While Swimming

Chlorine and salt water can damage bleached curly hair. Wear a swim cap or wet your hair with fresh water before swimming to prevent damage.

Tip 5: Be Patient

It takes time and effort to keep bleached curly hair healthy, so be patient and persistent. With proper care and attention, your hair will look beautiful and vibrant.

FAQs About Bleached Curly Hair

Q: Can I dye my hair after bleaching it?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair after bleaching it. However, it is best to wait a few weeks to allow your hair to recover from the bleach before dyeing it.

Q: How often should I wash bleached curly hair?

A: Curly hair should be washed once or twice a week to prevent it from drying out. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and a hydrating conditioner.

Q: Can I straighten my bleached curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your bleached curly hair, but it is best to avoid heat styling tools whenever possible. Use a heat protectant spray and keep your straightening sessions brief to prevent damage.

The Importance of Hair Care for Bleached Curly Hair

Caring for bleached curly hair requires a little extra effort and attention, but it is well worth it. By following a few simple steps and using the right products, you can keep your hair healthy and vibrant. Remember to be patient and persistent, and your bleached curly hair will look stunning and healthy.
