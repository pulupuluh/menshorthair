How To Cut A Layered Bob On Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair and are looking for a new haircut, a layered bob might be the perfect choice. Layered bobs are versatile, easy to style, and can give your curls more bounce and movement. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a layered bob on curly hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand way. Read on for our step-by-step guide.

Description

A layered bob is a haircut that features different lengths of hair, with shorter layers on top and longer layers on the bottom. This creates a layered look that can add volume and texture to curly hair. A layered bob can be cut to suit any face shape and hair type, and can be styled in a variety of ways, from messy and tousled to sleek and polished.

How to Cut a Layered Bob on Curly Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a good pair of hair-cutting scissors, a comb, and clips to section off your hair. Here's how to cut a layered bob on curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Let it air dry or use a diffuser to dry it gently. You want your curls to be as natural as possible before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Clip each section up as you go.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and cut it to the desired length. Use this as a guide to cut the rest of the back section, working your way up in small sections.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and cut it to the same length as the back. Use this as a guide to cut the rest of the side sections, working your way up in small sections.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair from the crown of your head and cut it to the desired length. Use this as a guide to cut the rest of the top section, working your way down in small sections.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Take small sections of hair from each section and cut them at an angle to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless, layered look.

Step 7: Check for Balance

Before you finish, check for balance. Make sure the length and layers are even on both sides.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a layered bob on curly hair:

  • Start with small sections and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.
  • Use a light hand when cutting. Curly hair shrinks up when it dries, so you don't want to cut too much off.
  • When cutting the top section, take into account how your curls will fall. You want the top layers to complement the natural shape of your curls.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, so it's always a good idea to have a friend or family member help you out.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a layered bob on curly hair:

Can I cut a layered bob on my own?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it can be tricky, especially if you're not experienced. We recommend having a friend or family member help you out, or going to a professional stylist.

What length should I cut my layered bob?

The length of your layered bob will depend on your face shape and personal preference. We recommend consulting with a stylist to find the best length for you.

What products should I use to style my layered bob?

Curly hair can be dry and prone to frizz, so it's important to use products that hydrate and define your curls. Look for moisturizing shampoos and conditioners, as well as styling products like curl creams, gels, and mousses.

Hair Care

To keep your layered bob looking its best, it's important to take care of your curly hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair no more than once or twice a week to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to hydrate your curls and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your curls.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut a layered bob on curly hair with confidence. Good luck!
