Does Your Hair Fall Out After Having A Baby

Written by Oliver May 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are a new mom or a soon-to-be mom, you might be wondering if the rumors are true - does your hair fall out after having a baby? The answer is yes, it's a common occurrence among new moms. This post will explain why it happens, how to deal with it, and give you some tips on how to care for your hair postpartum.

Description

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is a temporary form of hair loss that occurs after giving birth. It usually starts around 3 to 6 months after delivery and can last up to 12 months. During pregnancy, hormones cause your hair to stay in the growing phase longer than usual, resulting in thicker and fuller hair. After giving birth, those hormones drop, and your hair goes into a resting phase, causing it to shed more than usual.

How to Deal with Postpartum Hair Loss

Postpartum hair loss can be stressful, but there are ways to manage it. Here are some tips:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid tight hairstyles, harsh chemicals, and excessive heat styling. Be gentle when brushing and washing your hair to avoid pulling out more than necessary.

2. Consider a New Haircut

A shorter haircut can make hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage.

3. Eat a Nutritious Diet

Eat a well-balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth. Foods high in biotin, iron, and protein are especially beneficial.

4. Take Supplements

Supplements such as biotin, vitamin D, and iron may help support hair growth. Consult with your doctor before taking any supplements.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Postpartum Hair Loss

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils. Rinse with cool water to help stimulate blood flow to the scalp.

Step 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid excessive heat styling with blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant and keep the heat at a low setting.

Step 3: Be Gentle When Brushing

Be gentle when brushing your hair to avoid pulling out more than necessary. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles.

Step 4: Consider a Haircut

A shorter haircut can make hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage.

Step 5: Eat a Nutritious Diet

Eat a well-balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth. Foods high in biotin, iron, and protein are especially beneficial.

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

Tip 1: Use Hair Accessories

Use hair accessories such as headbands, hair clips, and scarves to conceal hair loss and add some style to your hair.

Tip 2: Try a Hair Growth Treatment

Consider trying a hair growth treatment such as minoxidil or hair growth serums to promote hair growth.

Tip 3: Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss is temporary and will eventually subside. Be patient and take care of your hair in the meantime.

FAQs

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss usually starts around 3 to 6 months after delivery and can last up to 12 months.

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair loss. It may take several months for your hair to fully recover.

Q: Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

A: Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to manage it and promote hair growth.

Hair Care Postpartum

Postpartum hair loss can be stressful, but it's a normal part of the postpartum experience. Be gentle with your hair, eat a nutritious diet, and consider trying hair growth treatments. Remember to be patient, and your hair will eventually grow back.
