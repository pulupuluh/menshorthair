What Is The Best Temperature To Flat Iron Natural Hair

Written by Robby Sep 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have natural hair, you know that flat ironing can be a daunting task. One of the most important factors to consider when flat ironing natural hair is the temperature. Using the wrong temperature can cause damage to your hair, so it's important to know what the best temperature is for your hair type.

Description

The best temperature to flat iron natural hair varies depending on your hair type. Generally, the ideal temperature range for flat ironing natural hair is between 300Â°F and 380Â°F. If your hair is fine or damaged, you should use a lower temperature setting. If your hair is thick or coarse, you can use a higher temperature setting.

It's important to note that using a temperature that is too high can damage your hair. Over time, excessive heat can cause split ends, breakage, and dryness. It's best to start with a lower temperature and gradually increase it until you find the right setting for your hair.

How to Flat Iron Natural Hair

Before flat ironing your natural hair, it's important to prepare your hair by washing and conditioning it. You should also use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage from heat styling. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair without missing any sections.

Step 2: Start with a Low Temperature

Start with a low temperature setting on your flat iron. If your hair is fine or damaged, use a temperature between 300Â°F and 325Â°F. If your hair is thick or coarse, use a temperature between 350Â°F and 380Â°F.

Step 3: Straighten Your Hair

Using your flat iron, straighten each section of your hair from root to tip. Be sure to move the flat iron slowly and smoothly to prevent burning your hair.

Step 4: Repeat

Repeat steps 2 and 3 for each section of your hair until you have straightened all of your hair.

Tips for Flat Ironing Natural Hair

Here are some tips to help you flat iron your natural hair without causing damage:

  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Don't flat iron your hair too often. Heat styling can cause damage over time.
  • Use a low temperature setting if your hair is fine or damaged.
  • Use a higher temperature setting if your hair is thick or coarse.
  • Don't flat iron wet hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using a flat iron.

FAQs

What temperature should I use to flat iron my natural hair?

The best temperature to use when flat ironing natural hair is between 300Â°F and 380Â°F. If your hair is fine or damaged, use a lower temperature setting. If your hair is thick or coarse, use a higher temperature setting.

Can flat ironing natural hair cause damage?

Yes, flat ironing natural hair can cause damage if you use a temperature that is too high or if you flat iron your hair too often. It's important to use a heat protectant and to choose the right temperature for your hair type.

Hair Care for Flat Ironed Natural Hair

After flat ironing your natural hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain your style. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to prevent friction and tangling.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Limit heat styling and use a low temperature setting when possible.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the right temperature for your hair type, you can flat iron your natural hair without causing damage. Remember to always use a heat protectant and to take care of your hair after heat styling.
