How To Cut Your Own Hair Short Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 17, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts every few weeks? Or maybe you're just feeling adventurous and want to try cutting your own hair. Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're going for a layered look.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Short Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Description

Short layers are a great way to add texture and volume to your hair, and they're also easy to maintain. Cutting your own hair can be a bit tricky, but with some patience and practice, you can achieve a salon-worthy look right at home.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Short Layers

Before you begin, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. Once you have everything you need, it's time to get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see where you need to cut and ensure that your hair is even.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle and then again from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight down and cut it to the desired length. Be sure to cut in a straight line, and don't cut too much at once.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Cut the top layer to the desired length, making sure to keep it even with the bottom layer.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Using your scissors, blend the layers together by cutting into the hair at an angle. This will create a natural, layered look.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Using your mirror, check the back of your head to make sure your hair is even. If you notice any uneven areas, trim them as needed.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small snips and work your way up
  • Don't cut too much at once
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Take breaks if you start to feel overwhelmed
  • Watch tutorials online for additional guidance

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your hair while it's dry and to cut in small sections.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Mistakes happen, and hair grows back. If you make a mistake, try to fix it as best as you can or seek professional help.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish, layered look right at home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
