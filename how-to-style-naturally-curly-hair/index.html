How To Style Naturally Curly Hair Tips Tricks And Techniques

Written by Petter Jun 13, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy, unruly curls? Do you want to learn how to style your naturally curly hair in a way that looks chic and effortless? Look no further! In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about styling curly hair, including how to enhance your natural texture, create defined curls, and manage frizz.

Table of Contents:

  • Section 1: How to Enhance Your Natural Curl Pattern
  • Section 2: How to Create Defined Curls
  • Section 3: How to Manage Frizz
  • Section 4: Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curls
  • Section 5: FAQ

Section 1: How to Enhance Your Natural Curl Pattern

If you have naturally curly hair, the first step to styling it is to work with your natural texture. Here are some tips for enhancing your curls:

1. Use a curl-enhancing product.

There are a variety of products on the market specifically designed to enhance and define curls. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil, which can help moisturize and define your curls.

2. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry.

Curly hair is prone to frizz, and brushing it when it's dry can make the problem worse. Instead, comb your hair with a wide-toothed comb or your fingers when it's wet, and avoid touching it too much after it dries.

3. Diffuse your hair with a hair dryer.

Using a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment can help enhance your natural curl pattern. Simply scrunch your hair with the diffuser while drying it on a low heat setting.

Section 2: How to Create Defined Curls

If you want to create more defined curls, here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Use a curl-defining product.

In addition to a curl-enhancing product, you may want to use a product specifically designed to define curls. Look for gels, creams, or mousses that contain ingredients like wheat protein or polymers, which can help hold your curls in place.

2. Use a technique like "plopping" or "twisting."

"Plopping" involves using a t-shirt or microfiber towel to help dry your hair in a way that enhances your curls. Simply lay the towel out, flip your hair over onto it, and wrap the towel around your head. Leave it on for 10-20 minutes, then remove the towel and let your hair air dry. "Twisting" involves twisting sections of hair around your finger to create more defined curls.

3. Consider getting a curly haircut.

If you're struggling to create defined curls, it may be worth getting a haircut specifically designed for curly hair. A stylist who specializes in curly hair can help you find a cut that enhances your natural texture and makes it easier to style.

Section 3: How to Manage Frizz

Frizz is a common problem for those with curly hair, but there are ways to manage it:

1. Use a smoothing product.

Products like serums, oils, or leave-in conditioners can help smooth frizzy hair. Look for products that contain silicone, which can help create a barrier against humidity.

2. Avoid touching your hair too much.

The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to frizz. Try to avoid touching it too much, and use a silk or satin pillowcase to minimize friction while you sleep.

3. Use a diffuser attachment when blow drying.

As mentioned earlier, using a diffuser attachment when blow drying can help enhance your natural curl pattern. It can also help minimize frizz by reducing the amount of direct heat your hair is exposed to.

Section 4: Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curls

Finally, it's important to take care of your curly hair in order to keep it looking and feeling healthy:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo.

Sulfates can be harsh on curly hair, so look for shampoos that are sulfate-free. These will be gentler on your hair and help prevent it from drying out.

2. Deep condition your hair regularly.

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep your curls moisturized and healthy. Look for products that contain ingredients like avocado oil or keratin, which can help strengthen and nourish your hair.

3. Get regular trims.

Split ends can make curly hair look frizzy and dull, so it's important to get regular trims to keep your ends looking healthy. Aim to get a trim every 8-12 weeks.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair using a flat iron or other heat styling tools. However, it's important to use a heat protectant product and not to straighten it too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Q: What's the best way to detangle curly hair?

A: The best way to detangle curly hair is to use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being gentle and avoiding tugging or pulling.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle, but in general, it's best to wash curly hair no more than 2-3 times per week. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it look dry and frizzy.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style your naturally curly hair with confidence and ease. Remember to work with your natural texture, use the right products, and take care of your curls to keep them looking their best!
