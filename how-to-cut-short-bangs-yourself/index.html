How To Cut Short Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your own bangs can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. If you're looking to cut short bangs yourself, this guide will walk you through the process step-by-step. Keep reading for tips, tricks, and hair care advice to ensure that your bangs turn out perfectly.

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors (preferably hair cutting scissors)
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Hair clips or bobby pins
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid any jagged cuts or split ends.

Prepping Your Hair and Bangs

Start by washing and blow-drying your hair. Comb your hair straight down and separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. If you have longer bangs, you may want to consider trimming them to the desired length before you begin cutting shorter bangs. Spritz your bangs with the water bottle to make them easier to work with.

Sectioning Your Hair

Using your comb, create a center part in your hair. This will help you create even bangs on both sides. Next, divide your bangs into three sections: left, middle, and right. Use your hair clips or bobby pins to clip the side sections back, leaving only the middle section down.

Cutting Your Bangs

Starting with the middle section, hold your bangs between your index and middle fingers and twist them slightly. This will help you create a more natural-looking, slightly curved shape for your bangs. Use your scissors to cut straight across, making small snips instead of one large cut. Start with a longer length and work your way shorter until you reach your desired length.

Release the side sections and repeat the process, cutting the bangs slightly shorter as you move towards the sides. Use the middle section as a guide to make sure your bangs are even on both sides. Take your time and make small cuts, as it's much easier to take more hair off than it is to put it back on.

Blending Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs to the desired length, use your comb to blend the sides of your bangs into the rest of your hair. This will create a natural-looking transition and help your bangs blend in seamlessly.

Finishing Touches

After you've finished cutting and blending your bangs, take a step back and assess your work. Make any final adjustments as needed. Style your hair as desired, and enjoy your new short bangs!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

  • Take your time and make small, precise cuts.
  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter.
  • Use the twist-and-cut technique for a natural-looking shape.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet, as they'll shrink when they dry.
  • Have a backup plan (such as a headband or hair clips) in case your bangs don't turn out as you hoped.

FAQs

Q: How often do I need to trim my bangs?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want your bangs to be. As a general rule, you should plan on trimming your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try to fix it with small snips. If you're really unhappy with your bangs, you can always pin them back or wear a headband until they grow out.

Hair Care for Short Bangs

Short bangs require a bit more maintenance than longer bangs, as they can get greasy and lose their shape more quickly. To keep your bangs looking great, try the following:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and fresh.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume.
  • Use dry shampoo to absorb oil and sweat throughout the day.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them greasy and flat.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut short bangs yourself at home. Remember to take your time, make small cuts, and use the twist-and-cut method for a natural-looking shape. Happy cutting!
