How To Fix Dry Damaged Hair From Bleaching The Ultimate Guide

Written by William Sep 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with dry, damaged hair from bleaching? You're not alone. Many people love the look of bleached hair, but it can come at a cost. Luckily, there are steps you can take to revive your hair and bring it back to life. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to fix dry damaged hair from bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Dry Damaged Hair from Bleaching
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Bleaching can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. If you've bleached your hair and are now dealing with the aftermath, don't worry. There are ways to fix dry damaged hair from bleaching and restore your locks to their former glory.

How to Fix Dry Damaged Hair from Bleaching

The first step in fixing dry damaged hair from bleaching is to stop further damage. This means avoiding any further chemical treatments, such as coloring or perming your hair. Stick to gentle, natural hair care products that will help nourish and strengthen your hair.

Next, you'll want to focus on deep conditioning your hair to help restore moisture. Look for a high-quality deep conditioning treatment that contains nourishing ingredients like avocado oil, coconut oil, or shea butter. Apply the treatment once a week, leaving it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

You may also want to consider using a hair mask to help repair your damaged locks. Look for a mask that contains keratin, which is a protein that helps strengthen and repair hair. Apply the mask once a week and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to fixing dry damaged hair from bleaching:

  1. Stop further damage by avoiding chemical treatments.
  2. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  3. Apply a hair mask with keratin once a week.
  4. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  5. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help fix dry damaged hair from bleaching:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid further drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Consider taking hair supplements, such as biotin or collagen, to help strengthen your hair from within.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing dry damaged hair from bleaching:

Can I color my hair after bleaching it?

We recommend waiting at least a few weeks after bleaching your hair before coloring it again. This will give your hair time to recover and avoid further damage.

How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week to help restore moisture and repair damage.

Will cutting my hair help fix dry damaged hair from bleaching?

While cutting your hair won't necessarily fix the damage, it will help get rid of split ends and make your hair appear healthier.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and tugging on your hair.
  • Avoid tying your hair up tightly, as this can lead to breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid damaging your hair while you sleep.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can help fix dry damaged hair from bleaching and restore your locks to their former glory. Remember to be patient, as it may take some time to see results. With a little TLC, your hair will be healthy and shiny once again.
