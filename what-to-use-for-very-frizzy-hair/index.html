What To Use For Very Frizzy Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Frizz is a common hair problem that can be frustrating to deal with. If you have very frizzy hair, you may find it difficult to style and maintain. However, there are many products and techniques that can help you tame your frizz and achieve the smooth, shiny hair you desire. In this guide, we will explore what to use for very frizzy hair, including tips, products, and hair care routines to help you manage your frizz.

Table of Contents

  • How to Manage Very Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Frizz
  • Tips for Managing Frizz
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Routine for Frizzy Hair

Description

Frizz occurs when your hair is dry, damaged, or lacks moisture. This can cause the hair cuticles to lift, creating a rough, uneven texture. Frizz can be caused by humidity, heat styling, chemical treatments, and even genetics. If you have very frizzy hair, you may notice that it's difficult to style, prone to tangles, and lacks shine. However, with the right products and techniques, you can manage your frizz and achieve smooth, healthy-looking hair.

How to Manage Very Frizzy Hair

Managing very frizzy hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. Here are some tips to help you manage your frizz:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible
  • Protect your hair from humidity
  • Use a hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Frizz

If you're struggling with very frizzy hair, here's a step-by-step guide to help you tame your frizz:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for frizzy hair.
  2. Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub or tangle it.
  3. Apply a hair oil or serum to your damp hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.
  4. Comb your hair with a wide-tooth comb to detangle it.
  5. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much as it dries, as this can cause frizz.
  7. Once your hair is dry, apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends to add shine and smoothness.

Tips for Managing Frizz

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your frizz:

  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about managing frizzy hair:

  • What causes frizzy hair? Frizz can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, heat styling, chemical treatments, and genetics.
  • What products are best for managing frizz? Look for products that are specifically designed for frizzy hair, such as smoothing shampoos and conditioners, hair oils, and serums.
  • What should I avoid if I have frizzy hair? Avoid using hot tools, products that contain alcohol, and over-washing your hair.

Hair Care Routine for Frizzy Hair

If you have very frizzy hair, it's important to establish a hair care routine that will help you manage your frizz. Here's a sample routine:

  • Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner every 2-3 days.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray if you do use hot tools.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to your damp hair after washing to add shine and smoothness.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it when it's wet.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from humidity by using a hair product with anti-frizz properties.

In conclusion, managing very frizzy hair can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve smooth, healthy-looking hair. Remember to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, avoid heat styling as much as possible, protect your hair from humidity, and use a hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness. By following these tips and establishing a hair care routine, you can keep your frizz under control and enjoy beautiful, manageable hair.
