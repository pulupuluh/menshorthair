How To Find Out If Short Hair Suits You

Written by Bardi Aug 15, 2023 · 11 min read

Are you considering cutting your hair short but not sure if it will suit you? Short hair can be a great way to change up your look and show off your features, but it's important to make sure it's the right choice for you. In this article, we'll go over how to find out if short hair suits you, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Consider Your Hair Type
  • Think About Your Lifestyle
  • Accentuate Your Features

  • Conclusion

Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out if short hair suits you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Knowing your face shape can help you choose a haircut that flatters your features.

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror. Use a measuring tape or ruler to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin.

If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, you have a square face shape. If your face is longer than it is wide and your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width, you have an oval face shape. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline, you have a heart-shaped face.

Once you know your face shape, you can start looking for hairstyles that will complement your features.

Consider Your Hair Type

Your hair type is another important factor to consider when deciding if short hair suits you. Short hair can work for any hair type, but some styles may be easier to maintain than others.

If you have thick, curly hair, a short cut can help you manage your mane and show off your curls. A pixie cut or a bob can work well for this hair type. If you have fine hair, a layered bob can add volume and texture. If you have straight hair, a blunt bob or a lob (long bob) can be a great option.

Keep in mind that some short hairstyles require more maintenance than others. If you don't have a lot of time to style your hair each day, a low-maintenance cut may be a better option.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Before you make the decision to cut your hair short, think about your lifestyle. Short hair can be a great option if you're looking for a low-maintenance style, but it may not be the best choice if you're used to having longer hair.

If you're someone who likes to experiment with different hairstyles, short hair can be a lot of fun. However, if you're someone who likes to wear your hair up in a ponytail or bun, you may find short hair limiting.

Consider your job and daily activities as well. If you work in a conservative environment, a dramatic short cut may not be appropriate. If you're someone who likes to exercise or play sports, a short cut may be more practical.

Accentuate Your Features

Short hair can help accentuate your features, so think about what you want to highlight. If you have beautiful eyes, a short cut can help draw attention to them. If you have a strong jawline, a pixie cut can help show it off.

Think about the parts of your face and body that you want to highlight and choose a hairstyle that will complement them.

Try On Short Wigs or Hair Extensions

If you're not sure if short hair suits you, try on some short wigs or hair extensions. This will give you an idea of what you would look like with short hair without making a permanent change.

Visit a wig shop or salon that specializes in hair extensions to try on different styles. You can also use virtual makeover tools online to see what you would look like with different haircuts.

Get a Professional Haircut

If you've decided to take the plunge and cut your hair short, make sure you go to a professional stylist. A good stylist can help you choose a style that flatters your features and works with your hair type.

Bring photos of hairstyles that you like and be open to your stylist's suggestions. They may recommend a slightly different cut based on your hair type and face shape.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Once you've cut your hair short, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny. Consider using a texturizing spray or wax to add volume and texture to your style.

Visit your stylist regularly for trims to keep your style looking fresh. If you want to change up your style, consider adding highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair.

FAQs

How do I know if I have the right face shape for short hair?

Before cutting your hair short, determine your face shape. Short hair can work with any face shape, but certain styles may be more flattering than others. If you're not sure, consult with a professional stylist.

What if I don't like my short hair?

If you're not happy with your short hair, don't worry. Hair grows back! Talk to your stylist about ways to make your hair look longer while it grows out.

What if I have a bald spot or thinning hair?

If you have a bald spot or thinning hair, short hair may not be the best option for you. Consider a longer style or a hairpiece to cover up any problem areas.

Conclusion

Cutting your hair short can be a great way to change up your look, but it's important to make sure it's the right choice for you. Consider your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, and features before making the decision to go short. Try on short wigs or hair extensions to get an idea of what you would look like with short hair, and consult with a professional stylist before making the cut. With the right style and proper maintenance, short hair can be a chic and stylish option for anyone.
