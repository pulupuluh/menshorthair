How To Know If You Can Pull Off A Pixie Cut

Written by Aprilia Aug 03, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you're thinking about going for a drastic haircut, such as a pixie cut, it's important to consider whether it will suit your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. In this article, we'll give you some tips on how to know if you can pull off a pixie cut and what to expect from this bold and trendy hairstyle.

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that is usually cut close to the scalp on the sides and back, with longer layers on top. It's a versatile and low-maintenance haircut that can suit many face shapes and hair types, but it's not for everyone. Pixie cuts can make your features stand out, highlight your neck and jawline, and add a touch of edginess to your look, but they can also be challenging to style and grow out.

How to

If you're considering a pixie cut, here are some factors to consider:

Face shape

Pixie cuts can flatter many face shapes, but they tend to look best on oval, heart-shaped, or square faces. If you have a round or long face, a pixie cut may not be the most flattering option, as it can make your face appear wider or longer. To determine your face shape, measure your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, and compare their widths.

Hair texture

Pixie cuts can work with any hair texture, but they can look different depending on whether you have straight, wavy, curly, or kinky hair. If you have fine or thin hair, a pixie cut can add volume and texture, but if you have thick or coarse hair, it may require more maintenance and styling. Consider your hair's natural texture and how much time and effort you're willing to put into styling it.

Lifestyle

Pixie cuts can be a great choice if you're active, busy, or simply want a low-maintenance hairstyle. They're easy to wash, dry, and style, and they don't require as much product or heat as longer haircuts. However, if you're used to having long hair, a pixie cut may take some getting used to, both physically and emotionally. Be prepared for some stares, compliments, and criticism, and don't expect to be able to hide behind your hair anymore.

Step by Step

If you've decided to go for a pixie cut, here are some steps to follow:

Research

Before you book an appointment with a hairstylist, do some research on the different types of pixie cuts, the latest trends, and the celebrities who have rocked this haircut. Look for inspiration pictures that match your face shape, hair texture, and personal style, and bring them to your hairstylist to discuss your options.

Consultation

Make sure to schedule a consultation with your hairstylist before the actual cut, so that you can discuss your expectations, concerns, and preferences. Your hairstylist can also help you determine the best length, shape, and style for your face shape and hair texture, and recommend some products and tools to help you maintain your pixie cut.

Maintenance

Once you have your pixie cut, you'll need to maintain it regularly to keep it looking fresh and neat. Depending on your hair texture and length, you may need to trim your hair every 4-6 weeks, and style it daily with some pomade, wax, or gel. You can also experiment with different accessories, such as headbands, scarves, or hats, to add some variety to your look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you pull off a pixie cut:

  • Choose a hairstylist who has experience with pixie cuts and can tailor the cut to your face shape and hair texture.
  • Consider getting a short or shaved undercut to add some edge and dimension to your pixie cut.
  • Invest in some high-quality hair products, such as a sulfate-free shampoo, a nourishing conditioner, and a heat protectant spray.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques, such as blow-drying, diffusing, or air-drying, to find the best method for your hair texture.
  • Accessorize your pixie cut with some bold earrings, necklaces, or glasses to draw attention to your face.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about pixie cuts:

Will a pixie cut suit my face shape?

It depends on your face shape and the type of pixie cut you choose. Generally, pixie cuts look best on oval, heart-shaped, or square faces, but they can also work on round or long faces with some adjustments.

How do I style a pixie cut?

You can style a pixie cut with some pomade, wax, or gel, and a blow-dryer or diffuser. You can also experiment with different partings, textures, or accessories to change up your look.

How often do I need to trim a pixie cut?

You'll need to trim your pixie cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and length, and avoid split ends. You can also ask your hairstylist to add some layers or textures to keep the cut fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your pixie cut healthy and shiny, here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition your hair regularly with a moisturizing conditioner that will nourish and soften your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often, as they can damage your hair and make it brittle.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate hair growth and improve blood circulation.
  • Eat a balanced and nutritious diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals that are essential for healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently rock a pixie cut that flatters your face shape, highlights your hair texture, and suits your lifestyle. Whether you're going for a classic, edgy, or romantic look, a pixie cut can be a bold and empowering choice that shows off your personality and confidence.
