How To Do A Men s Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, a fade haircut is a popular and versatile option for men. A fade haircut is characterized by a gradual length change from short to longer hair, creating a tapered look. It's a classic style that can be adapted to fit any hair type or length. In this article, we'll be discussing how to do a men's fade haircut in easy-to-follow steps.

How to:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of hair clippers with different guard sizes, a comb, scissors, and hair clips. It's also important to have a clean and well-lit space to work in.

Step 2: Decide on Your Fade Style

There are many different types of fade haircuts, including low, mid, and high fades. Decide on which style you want to go for before you begin cutting.

Step 3: Start with a Longer Guard Size

Begin by using a longer guard size on your hair clippers. This will ensure that you don't cut your hair too short too quickly. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up, using the clippers to create a gradual length change.

Step 4: Gradually Decrease Guard Size

Once you've established the longer length, switch to a shorter guard size and work your way up the head again. Make sure to blend the shorter length into the longer length, creating a smooth gradient.

Step 5: Use Scissors to Blend

Once you reach the top of your head, switch to scissors to blend any harsh lines. Use the comb to lift sections of hair and cut at an angle to create a natural look.

Step 6: Define the Hairline

Use the clippers to define the hairline around the ears and neckline. This will create a clean and polished look.

Step 7: Style as Desired

Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can use hair product to create a textured or slicked-back look.

Tips:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use scissors to blend any harsh lines and create a natural look.
  • Take breaks and step back to assess your progress.
  • Practice makes perfect - don't be discouraged if your first attempt isn't perfect.

FAQ:

Q: Can I do a fade haircut on myself?

A: It's possible to do a fade haircut on yourself, but it can be difficult to achieve a polished look without help. Consider enlisting the help of a friend or professional barber.

Q: How often should I get a fade haircut?

A: This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how polished you want your look to be. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care:

To maintain your fade haircut, keep your hair clean and well-groomed. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type, and consider using hair product to style and protect your hair.

Conclusion:

Learning how to do a men's fade haircut can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a polished and versatile look that's perfect for any occasion. Remember to take your time, practice, and don't be afraid to ask for help if needed.
