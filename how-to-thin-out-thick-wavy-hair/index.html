How To Thin Out Thick Wavy Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick, wavy hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and full of life, it can also be difficult to style and manage. One solution to this problem is to thin out your hair, which can help make it more manageable and easier to style. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out thick wavy hair in a step-by-step guide.

Description

Thick, wavy hair can be beautiful, but it can also be difficult to manage. It can be tough to style, and it can take a long time to dry. One way to make thick, wavy hair more manageable is to thin it out. Thinning your hair can make it easier to style, and it can also help reduce frizz and tangles.

How to Thin Out Thick Wavy Hair

Thinning out thick wavy hair is a process that requires a bit of knowledge and skill. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry completely.
  2. Section your hair into four equal parts using hair clips.
  3. Starting with one section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight up from the scalp.
  4. Using hair thinning scissors, cut about an inch off the hair, starting at the mid-lengths and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process with the other three sections of hair.
  6. Once you've finished cutting, wash and condition your hair again.
  7. Let your hair air dry or blow dry it on a low heat setting.
  8. Style your hair as desired using a heat protectant spray.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Wavy Hair

Thinning out your hair can be a bit tricky, so here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use hair thinning scissors, which have teeth that are designed to remove hair without leaving blunt ends.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut gradually. You can always cut more if you need to, but you can't put hair back.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, or you may end up with uneven, choppy layers.
  • If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist. They can thin out your hair for you and give you a great style.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out thick wavy hair:

  • How often should I thin out my hair? It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. In general, you may need to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.
  • Can I thin out my hair if it's already layered? Yes, you can. Just be careful not to cut too much hair at once, and make sure you're cutting from the mid-lengths to the ends.
  • Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner? No, thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner. It will simply remove some of the bulk and make it more manageable.

Hair Care for Thick Wavy Hair

Thick, wavy hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of your hair to keep them hydrated and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

Thinning out thick wavy hair can be a great solution for those looking to make their hair more manageable. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a gorgeous, easy-to-style look that you'll love.
