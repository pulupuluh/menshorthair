How To Cut Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Mar 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to cut properly, but with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve beautiful, bouncy curls. In this article, we’ll go over how to cut curly hair, including tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Advice for Curly Hair

How to Cut Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. It’s important to understand the unique properties of curly hair, including its texture, curl pattern, and density. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Curly hair shrinks when it dries, so it’s important to cut it when it’s wet to get an accurate length.
  • Using a comb or brush can disrupt the curl pattern, so it’s best to cut curly hair using your fingers.
  • Curly hair can be dry and prone to breakage, so it’s important to use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of cutting curly hair, let’s go over a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition the Hair

Start by washing and conditioning the hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Curly hair tends to be dry, so it’s important to use products that won’t strip the hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Detangle the Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the hair is evenly distributed.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don’t miss any sections.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Starting with the bottom section, use your fingers to pull the hair straight and trim the ends. Work your way up to the top sections, cutting each one in the same manner.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Once you’ve finished cutting, check for evenness by running your fingers through the hair. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them until the hair is even.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to trim small sections at a time to avoid cutting off too much.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the hair. It’s easy to miss sections when cutting the back of the hair, so use a mirror to ensure that it’s even.
  • Consider the hair’s natural curl pattern. Cutting curly hair too short can disrupt the curl pattern and cause the hair to look frizzy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you cut curly hair when it’s dry?

It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s wet to get an accurate length. However, some stylists prefer to cut curly hair when it’s dry to see the natural curl pattern and avoid any surprises.

How often should I cut curly hair?

It’s recommended that you cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own curly hair?

While it’s possible to cut your own curly hair, it’s recommended that you go to a professional stylist to avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care Advice for Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair is just the first step in maintaining healthy, bouncy curls. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair, as a brush can disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define and hydrate the curls.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that will turn heads. Remember to take your time and be patient when cutting curly hair, as it requires a different approach than cutting straight hair.
