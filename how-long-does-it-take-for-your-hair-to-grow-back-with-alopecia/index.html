How Long Does It Take For Your Hair To Grow Back With Alopecia

Written by William Feb 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are suffering from alopecia, you might be wondering how long it will take for your hair to grow back. Alopecia is a condition that causes hair loss, and it can be a scary experience for anyone. The good news is that hair can grow back, but the amount of time it takes can vary depending on the type of alopecia and the individual's body.

What is Alopecia?

Alopecia is a hair loss condition that affects both men and women. There are several types of alopecia, including:

  • Androgenetic alopecia
  • Alopecia areata
  • Traction alopecia
  • Scarring alopecia

Each type of alopecia has different causes and treatments, but they all have one thing in common: hair loss. Alopecia can be caused by genetics, autoimmune disorders, medications, and other factors.

How Does Hair Grow?

Before we discuss how long it takes for hair to grow back with alopecia, it's important to understand how hair grows. Hair growth occurs in three stages:

  • Anagen (growth) phase
  • Catagen (transition) phase
  • Telogen (resting) phase

The anagen phase is when hair grows actively, and it can last anywhere from two to seven years. The catagen phase is a short transitional phase that lasts about two weeks. The telogen phase is when hair rests, and it can last up to three months. After the telogen phase, hair falls out and the cycle starts again.

How Long Does It Take for Hair to Grow Back with Alopecia?

The amount of time it takes for hair to grow back with alopecia depends on the type of alopecia and the individual's body. In some cases, hair can grow back within a few months. In other cases, it can take several years.

Androgenetic Alopecia

Androgenetic alopecia, also known as male or female pattern baldness, is the most common type of alopecia. It is caused by genetics and hormone imbalances. Hair loss with androgenetic alopecia is gradual, and it can take several years for hair to be lost completely. However, hair can also grow back with treatment, such as medication or hair transplant surgery.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss in patches. The hair loss can happen suddenly, and it can grow back just as quickly. However, in some cases, the hair loss can be permanent. Treatment for alopecia areata includes corticosteroid injections, topical medications, and immunosuppressants.

Traction Alopecia

Traction alopecia is caused by wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids, cornrows, and ponytails. The constant pulling on the hair can cause it to break and fall out. Hair can grow back with traction alopecia, but it can take several months to a year. Treatment includes avoiding tight hairstyles and using hair growth products.

Scarring Alopecia

Scarring alopecia is a rare form of alopecia that causes permanent hair loss. It is caused by inflammation that damages hair follicles. Treatment for scarring alopecia includes medications, such as corticosteroids and antibiotics, and hair transplant surgery.

How to Promote Hair Growth with Alopecia

While hair growth with alopecia can take time, there are things you can do to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and biotin.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  • Use gentle hair care products that don't contain harsh chemicals.
  • Massage your scalp to increase blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Use hair growth products, such as minoxidil and finasteride, under the guidance of a doctor.

FAQ

Can hair grow back with alopecia?

Yes, hair can grow back with alopecia, but it depends on the type of alopecia and the individual's body. Hair growth can take several months to a year or more.

What causes alopecia?

Alopecia can be caused by genetics, autoimmune disorders, medications, and other factors.

What are the types of alopecia?

The types of alopecia include androgenetic alopecia, alopecia areata, traction alopecia, and scarring alopecia.

Hair Care for Alopecia

If you are suffering from alopecia, it's important to take care of your hair to promote growth and prevent further loss. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid brushing or combing wet hair.
  • Consider using a scalp treatment, such as a hair growth oil or serum.

Conclusion

If you are experiencing hair loss due to alopecia, it can be a frustrating and scary experience. However, hair can grow back with the right treatment and care. The amount of time it takes for hair to grow back with alopecia can vary, but following the tips above can help promote growth and prevent further loss.
