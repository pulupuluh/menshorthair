Best Way To Put Extensions In Thin Hair

Written by Bardi Aug 03, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have thin hair, you may feel like there's not much you can do to add volume and length to your locks. But luckily, hair extensions can come to the rescue! With the right technique, you can add some serious volume and length to your hair without causing any damage or discomfort. In this blog post, we'll discuss the best way to put extensions in thin hair. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Extensions in Thin Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Putting Extensions in Thin Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair with Extensions

How to Put Extensions in Thin Hair

The first thing you need to do is choose the right type of extensions. Clip-in extensions are a great option for thin hair because they are easy to apply and remove, and they don't cause any damage to your natural hair. You can also choose between synthetic or human hair extensions, depending on your budget and preference.

Once you've chosen your extensions, it's important to match the color and texture of the extensions to your natural hair. This will create a seamless and natural-looking blend. You can also choose to add highlights or lowlights to your extensions to create a more dimensional look.

Before you start putting in your extensions, it's important to prepare your hair. Wash and condition your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add some extra lift and body. Blow dry your hair and use a round brush to create some volume at the roots.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that your hair is prepped and ready, it's time to put in your extensions. Follow these steps for the best results:

  1. Section off your hair. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting at the nape of your neck. Clip the rest of your hair up and out of the way.
  2. Clip in the first weft. Take the smallest weft and clip it in underneath the section of hair you just parted. Make sure the weft is secure and close to the scalp, but not too tight.
  3. Continue clipping in wefts. Work your way up to the crown of your head, clipping in larger wefts as you go. Make sure to leave some of your natural hair out at the top of your head to create a natural-looking part.
  4. Blend the extensions. Use a comb or your fingers to blend your natural hair with the extensions. You can also use a curling iron or straightener to create some waves or curls for a more natural look.

Tips for Putting Extensions in Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when putting in extensions:

  • Be gentle. Don't tug or pull on your hair or the extensions, as this can cause damage and discomfort.
  • Don't sleep with your extensions in. Take them out before you go to bed to prevent tangling and damage.
  • Use heat protectant. If you plan on using heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your natural hair and the extensions.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about putting extensions in thin hair:

Can extensions damage thin hair?

If you use the right technique and take proper care of your extensions, they should not cause any damage to your thin hair.

How long do clip-in extensions last?

Clip-in extensions can last anywhere from a few months to a year, depending on how often you wear them and how well you take care of them.

Can I dye my extensions?

Yes, you can dye human hair extensions to match your natural hair color. However, it's best to consult with a professional stylist to ensure that the dye is applied correctly and doesn't damage the extensions.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair with Extensions

If you have thin hair with extensions, it's important to take extra care to keep your hair healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products. Use lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair or the extensions.
  • Don't wash your hair too often. Washing your hair every day can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.
  • Be gentle when brushing your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically designed for extensions to prevent tangling and damage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can add some serious volume and length to your thin hair with extensions. Just remember to choose the right type of extensions, match the color and texture, and take proper care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
