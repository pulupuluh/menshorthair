How Do You Cut Your Hair To Make It Look Thicker

Written by Christine Jun 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having thin and lifeless hair? Do you want to add more volume and thickness to your hair without using any hair products or extensions? The answer lies in the way you cut your hair! In this blog post, we will discuss how you can cut your hair to make it look thicker and fuller.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Hair to Make It Look Thicker
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Keep in Mind
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Cutting your hair is an art, and the right technique can make all the difference. When you cut your hair to make it look thicker, you are creating layers that add volume and dimension to your hair. The layers remove the weight from the bottom of your hair, making it look fuller and bouncier.

How to Cut Your Hair to Make It Look Thicker

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to decide on the length and style you want. If you have long hair, you can opt for long layers that will give your hair more movement and body. If you have short hair, you can go for a choppy bob that will add texture and volume.

The key to cutting your hair to make it look thicker is to create layers that start from your cheekbones and go all the way down. The layers should be cut at an angle to add more movement and dimension to your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair to make it look thicker:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair in the middle and then dividing it into two sections.
  3. Take the front section of your hair and comb it straight down.
  4. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from your cheekbone and going all the way down.
  5. Repeat this process on the other side of your hair.
  6. Once you have cut the front sections of your hair, move to the back.
  7. Divide the back section of your hair into two parts.
  8. Take the top section and cut it at an angle, starting from the crown of your head and going all the way down.
  9. Repeat this process on the bottom section of your hair.
  10. Once you have cut all the sections, blend them together by combing your hair and checking for any unevenness.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair to make it look thicker:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and uneven haircuts.
  • Cut your hair when it is dry, as wet hair tends to shrink and look shorter.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to a drastic change that you may not like.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the layers and avoid split ends.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair to make it look thicker if I have thin hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair to make it look thicker even if you have thin hair. The layers will add volume and movement to your hair, making it look fuller and bouncier.

How often should I cut my hair to maintain the layers?

You should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers and avoid split ends.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking thick and healthy, you need to take care of it. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add more body to your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage the strands and make them look thinner.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this will minimize breakage and damage.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to nourish your hair from the inside out.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and cutting your hair to make it look thicker, you can achieve the voluminous and bouncy hair of your dreams!
