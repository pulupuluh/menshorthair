Layer Cut Short Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 30, 2023 · 6 min read

Short hair can be fun and trendy, but sometimes it can feel like it’s lacking dimension. That’s where layering comes in. Layering can add shape, texture, and volume to your short hair, but going to a salon can be expensive. Luckily, with a few tools and some patience, you can layer cut your short hair yourself in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps of how to layer cut short hair yourself.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Layer Cut Short Hair Yourself
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips to Keep in Mind
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Layer Cutting

How to Layer Cut Short Hair Yourself

The first step to successfully layering your short hair is to prepare the right tools. Here are the tools you need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Once you have all the tools ready, it’s time to start layering!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to layer cut your short hair:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally or blow-dry it.
  2. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section to keep them separate.
  4. Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to where you want to cut.
  5. Cut the hair using sharp scissors. Make sure to cut at an angle, not straight across, to create layers.
  6. Take another small section of hair and repeat the same process, working your way up to the top of the back section.
  7. Repeat the same process for each side section, making sure to match the length of the layers on both sides.
  8. Finally, move to the front sections. Comb the hair forward and cut it to the desired length.
  9. Blend the layers by combing and snipping any stray hairs.
  10. When you’re done layering, style your hair as desired.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to help you layer cut your short hair like a pro:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid mistakes.
  • Use sharp scissors to get a clean cut.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear shorter when it dries.
  • If you’re unsure about the length or style, start by cutting less and work your way up.
  • Be patient and take your time to avoid mistakes.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about layer cutting short hair:

Q: Can I layer cut my short hair if it’s curly?
A: Yes, you can layer cut curly hair. However, it’s best to do it when your hair is dry to avoid cutting too much length. Q: How often should I layer cut my short hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much you want to maintain the style. Generally, layer cutting short hair every 6-8 weeks is recommended. Q: What if I make a mistake while layer cutting my short hair?
A: Don’t panic! Mistakes happen, and you can always trim the hair to fix it. If it’s a major mistake, consider going to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care After Layer Cutting

After layer cutting your short hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Use hair masks or oils to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim the ends every few weeks to maintain the style.

Layer cutting short hair yourself can be a rewarding experience, but it requires patience and practice. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and layered look without breaking the bank. Happy cutting!
