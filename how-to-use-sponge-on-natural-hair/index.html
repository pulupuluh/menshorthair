How To Use Sponge On Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 30, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking for an easy way to achieve defined curls and coils on your natural hair? Look no further than a sponge! In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to use sponge on natural hair, from choosing the right sponge to styling your hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Choosing the Right Sponge
  2. Prepping Your Hair
  3. Using the Sponge
  4. Styling Your Hair
  5. Tips for Using Sponge on Natural Hair
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care Tips

Choosing the Right Sponge

The first step in using sponge on natural hair is choosing the right sponge. Look for a sponge with small, evenly spaced holes, as this will create tighter coils in your hair. You can also choose a sponge with larger holes if you prefer looser curls.

When choosing a sponge, consider the size and shape as well. A larger sponge will cover more hair at once, but may be more difficult to maneuver. A smaller sponge will be easier to use on shorter hair, but may take longer to cover all of your hair.

Prepping Your Hair

Before using the sponge, it’s important to prep your hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual, then apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream. This will help your hair hold its shape and prevent frizz.

Next, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to use the sponge and ensure that your curls are defined.

Using the Sponge

To use the sponge, start by dampening your hair with water. You can use a spray bottle or run your hair under the faucet. Make sure your hair is damp, but not soaking wet.

Next, place the sponge on your hair and move it in a circular motion. You can use the sponge on your entire head or focus on specific areas for more definition.

Continue using the sponge until you achieve your desired curl pattern. You can use more pressure for tighter curls or less pressure for looser curls.

Styling Your Hair

Once you’ve used the sponge on your hair, you can style it as desired. You can leave your curls as is for a natural look, or use a pick or your fingers to fluff them out for more volume.

You can also apply a styling gel or oil to your hair to add shine and hold. Just be sure to use a small amount to avoid weighing down your curls.

Tips for Using Sponge on Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of using sponge on natural hair:

  • Start with damp hair to make it easier to work with
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or styling cream for added hold
  • Experiment with different sponge sizes and hole patterns to find what works best for your hair
  • Use the sponge in the direction you want your curls to go
  • Try using the sponge on twist-outs or braid-outs for even more defined curls

FAQs

Q: Can I use a sponge on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can use a sponge on relaxed hair. Just be sure to start with damp hair and use a light touch to avoid causing damage.

Q: How often should I use a sponge on my hair?

A: You can use a sponge on your hair as often as you’d like. Just be sure to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Hair Care Tips

Using sponge on natural hair can be a great way to achieve defined curls and coils. However, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage.

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy
  • Avoid using heat styling tools that can cause damage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, which can cause breakage
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and nourished

By following these tips and using sponge on natural hair correctly, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls and coils without damaging your hair.
