How To Cut Your Own Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Cutting your own hair at home can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a great way to save money and maintain your hairstyle between salon visits. In this post, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair at home, including tips and tricks for achieving a professional-looking cut. Whether you're looking to trim your bangs or give yourself a full haircut, this guide will help you get started.

How to Cut Your Own Hair at Home

Before you begin cutting your hair at home, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Clippers (if you want to give yourself a buzz cut or shorter hairstyle)
  • A comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • A handheld mirror (or a larger mirror that you can position behind you)
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A towel or cape to protect your clothing

Once you have your tools, you're ready to get started. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it dry completely. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you're working with a clean canvas. If you have curly hair, consider cutting it while it's dry to avoid any surprises once it dries.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section up and out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting a small amount of hair at a time. It's better to cut too little than too much, as you can always go back and trim more later. If you're not sure how much to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up. Remember to cut in a straight line and avoid jagged edges.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

When you're ready to cut the sides of your hair, start by trimming the hair around your ears. Use your comb to hold the hair in place and cut along the comb's edge. Then, work your way up, cutting small sections at a time.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, but with a handheld mirror, you can see what you're doing. Use your comb to section off the hair and cut in a straight line. Remember to take your time and cut small sections at a time.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

To blend the hair, use your scissors to make small, upward cuts at the ends of the hair. This will create a more seamless look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 7: Trim Your Bangs

If you have bangs, use your comb to section them off and cut them at an angle. Start by cutting a small amount and work your way up. Remember that bangs tend to shrink when they dry, so cut them a little longer than you want them to be.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it as you normally would. You can use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to achieve your desired look. Remember to be gentle with your hair, as it may be more delicate after cutting.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair at Home

Cutting your own hair can be a challenge, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking cut:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Invest in quality scissors and clippers
  • Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head
  • Cut small sections at a time
  • Be patient and take your time

FAQ: Cutting Your Own Hair at Home

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair at home:

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. It's best to cut it while it's dry so you can see how it will look once it's styled.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your hairstyle. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Can I give myself a buzz cut?

Yes, you can give yourself a buzz cut using clippers. Be sure to start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter length.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair at Home

After cutting your own hair at home, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

Cutting your own hair at home can be a great way to save money and maintain your hairstyle between salon visits. By following these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking cut that you can be proud of.
