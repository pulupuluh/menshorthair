Why Is My Hair Spiky After Shaving

Written by Bardi Jul 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you've ever shaved your head or buzzed your hair, you may have noticed that your hair becomes spiky once it starts growing back. This can be frustrating, especially if you were hoping for a smooth, clean-shaven look. In this article, we'll explore why is my hair spiky after shaving and what you can do to prevent it.

Description

Hair spikiness after shaving is a common phenomenon that occurs due to the natural structure of hair. Every hair strand has a cuticle layer that protects it from damage and helps it retain moisture. When we shave, the razor cuts the hair at an angle, leaving a sharp edge that sticks out from the skin. As the hair starts to grow back, the sharp edges of the cut hair push past the skin, creating a spiky, prickly feeling.

How-To

There are a few things you can do to prevent your hair from becoming spiky after shaving.

Step 1: Exfoliate

Exfoliating your scalp before shaving can help remove dead skin cells and oil buildup, which can make it easier for the razor to glide smoothly over your skin. Use a gentle exfoliating scrub or a soft-bristled brush to massage your scalp in circular motions.

Step 2: Use a Sharp Razor

A dull razor can pull and tug at your hair, leaving behind uneven, jagged edges that can cause irritation and spikiness. Make sure to use a sharp razor and replace the blades regularly.

Step 3: Shave in the Right Direction

Shaving against the grain of your hair can cause more irritation and spikiness. Instead, shave in the direction of your hair growth to create a smoother, more even finish.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help prevent spikiness after shaving:

  • Avoid shaving too frequently, as this can cause more irritation and spikiness
  • Moisturize your scalp regularly to help soften the hair and reduce spikiness
  • Consider using a hair growth inhibitor to slow down the regrowth of hair

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for hair to be spiky after shaving?

A: Yes, it's completely normal for hair to be spiky after shaving. This is due to the natural structure of hair and the way it grows back after being cut.

Q: How long does it take for hair to stop being spiky after shaving?

A: It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks for hair to stop being spiky after shaving, depending on how fast your hair grows and how often you shave.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair after shaving can help reduce spikiness and promote healthy hair growth. Here are a few things you can do:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep your scalp clean and moisturized
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it more prone to spikiness
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth

In conclusion, hair spikiness after shaving is a common occurrence that can be prevented with proper hair care and shaving techniques. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can achieve a smoother, more comfortable shave without the spikiness.
