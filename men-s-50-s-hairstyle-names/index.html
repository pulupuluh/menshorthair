Men s 50 S Hairstyle Names A Guide To Classic Looks

Written by Aprilia Jun 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to channel the iconic style of the 1950s, then you'll need to know the right hairstyles to complete your look. In this guide, we'll take a closer look at the most popular men's 50's hairstyle names, from the classic crew cut to the slicked-back pompadour.

Description

The 1950s was a decade of classic style and timeless fashion, and this extends to the hairstyles of the era. Men's hair was typically short and well-groomed, with a focus on neat, clean lines and a polished finish. Popular cuts included the crew cut, the flat top, and the side part, while longer styles like the pompadour and the quiff were also in vogue.

How to Achieve the Look

If you're looking to recreate a classic 50's hairstyle, then there are a few key steps you'll need to follow. First, you'll want to make sure your hair is the right length for your chosen cut. Shorter styles like the crew cut and the flat top will require regular trips to the barber to maintain their shape, while longer styles like the pompadour will need to be styled with a hairdryer, comb, and product.

Once you have the right length, you'll need to work on the shape and style of your hair. This may involve using a comb to create a side part or slicking your hair back with pomade for a classic greaser look. Practice makes perfect, so be prepared to spend some time experimenting with different products and techniques until you find the perfect style.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to achieve some of the most popular men's 50's hairstyle names:

The Crew Cut

The crew cut is a short, low-maintenance style that's perfect for guys who want a classic, no-fuss look. Here's how to achieve it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head to an even length.
  3. Use scissors to trim the hair on top of your head to a slightly longer length.
  4. Use a comb to create a defined side part.
  5. Finish with a light hold styling product to keep your hair in place.

The Pompadour

The pompadour is a more daring style that's perfect for guys who want to make a statement. Here's how to achieve it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use a hairdryer and a round brush to create volume and height at the front of your hair.
  3. Use a comb to create a side part.
  4. Slick the sides of your hair back with pomade.
  5. Finish with a strong hold styling product to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your 50's Hairstyle

Once you've achieved your desired 50's hairstyle, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Visit the barber regularly to maintain the shape and length of your cut.
  • Invest in quality haircare products like pomade, wax, and hairspray.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a comb or brush to detangle your hair gently and avoid pulling or tugging.

FAQs

What is the most popular men's 50's hairstyle?

The crew cut is probably the most iconic men's 50's hairstyle, thanks to its clean, classic look and low-maintenance nature.

Can I achieve a 50's hairstyle with longer hair?

Yes, longer styles like the pompadour and the quiff were also popular in the 1950s. However, these styles will require more maintenance and styling than shorter cuts like the crew cut.

What products do I need to achieve a 50's hairstyle?

You'll need a few key products, like pomade, wax, and hairspray, to achieve a classic 50's hairstyle. These products will help you create shape, volume, and hold for a polished, finished look.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heating tools on your hair, as these can damage it over time.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote strong, healthy hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.

Conclusion

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve a classic 50's hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion. Whether you prefer a low-maintenance crew cut or a daring pompadour, there's a look to suit every style and taste. So why not give it a try and see how you can channel the timeless style of the 1950s?
