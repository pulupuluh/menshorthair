How To Style Medium Choppy Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have medium length hair with choppy layers, you're in luck! This versatile hairstyle can be styled in many different ways, from messy and relaxed to sleek and sophisticated. In this guide, we'll show you how to achieve some of the most popular looks for medium choppy layered hair.

How to Style Medium Choppy Layered Hair

Before we get started on the styling, it's important to make sure your hair is clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant spray if you plan on using heat tools.

Look 1: Messy Waves

This look is perfect for a casual day out or a night out with friends.

  1. Start by parting your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.
  2. Take a 1-inch section of hair and wrap it around a curling iron. Hold for a few seconds before releasing.
  3. Continue curling all sections of your hair, alternating the direction of the curls for a more natural look.
  4. Once you've curled all sections, run your fingers through your hair to loosen the curls and create a messy, beachy look.
  5. Finish off with a light hold hairspray to keep the waves in place.

Look 2: Sleek and Straight

If you're looking for a more polished look, this is the style for you.

  1. Start by blow drying your hair straight using a round brush.
  2. Once your hair is completely dry, use a flat iron to straighten any remaining kinks or waves.
  3. Apply a small amount of hair serum to add shine and tame any frizz.
  4. Finish off with a medium hold hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Look 3: Half-Up Bun

This style is perfect for those days when you want to keep your hair out of your face but still want to show off your choppy layers.

  1. Start by parting your hair in the middle or on the side.
  2. Take the top section of your hair and pull it back into a ponytail.
  3. Twist the ponytail into a bun and secure with bobby pins.
  4. Leave the rest of your hair down or add some loose waves with a curling iron for a more relaxed look.
  5. Finish off with a light hold hairspray to keep the bun in place.

Tips for Styling Medium Choppy Layered Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your medium choppy layered hair:

  • Experiment with different partings to see what works best for your face shape.
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to play around with different accessories, such as headbands or hair clips.
  • If you're using heat tools, always use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

FAQ

Can I still style my hair if it's not cut in choppy layers?

While choppy layers do add texture and movement to your hair, you can still achieve similar styles with other types of layered haircuts.

How often should I get my hair cut to maintain the choppy layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your choppy layers.

Can I style my hair without heat tools?

Absolutely! You can achieve many of these styles without using heat tools. For example, you can create messy waves by braiding your hair overnight or using a salt spray.

Hair Care Tips for Medium Choppy Layered Hair

To keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. Here are some tips to help you take care of your medium choppy layered hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's specifically formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a heat protectant spray.

With these tips and styling ideas, you'll be able to make the most out of your medium choppy layered hair. Whether you're going for a casual or polished look, this versatile hairstyle is perfect for any occasion.
