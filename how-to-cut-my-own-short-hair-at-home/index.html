How to Cut My Own Short Hair at Home - Step by Step Guide

Cutting your own hair at home can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut your own short hair at home. Whether you're looking to save money or just want to try something new, follow our step-by-step guide to get the perfect haircut.

Table of Contents

  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair

  9. Hair Care after Cutting Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror (preferably a handheld and a standing mirror)

Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid split ends and uneven haircuts.

Preparation

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. Brush your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles. Make sure you are cutting your hair in a well-lit area and use a cape or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Sectioning Your Hair

Section your hair by creating a part from the middle of your forehead to the nape of your neck. Clip the top section of your hair up, leaving the bottom section down. Take a small section of hair in the middle of the bottom section and comb it straight down.

For a Pixie Cut:

Start by cutting the back of your hair. Hold your hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Work your way around your head, cutting the sides and top of your hair in small sections.

For a Bob Cut:

Start by cutting your hair in a straight line across the back, using the nape of your neck as a guide. Work your way around your head, cutting the sides and front of your hair to the desired length.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you have sectioned your hair, start cutting. If you are using clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard. Use your fingers to measure the length of your hair and cut accordingly. For a more precise cut, use your comb to separate and hold the hair in place.

For a layered look, take a small section of hair, hold it straight up, and cut at an angle. Repeat this process for the rest of your hair, making sure to blend the layers together.

Checking Your Work

After you have finished cutting your hair, check for any uneven sections or missed spots. Use your comb to identify any areas that need to be trimmed down. Make sure to cut small sections at a time to avoid cutting off too much hair.

Cleaning Up

Once you have finished cutting your hair, clean up any hair clippings on the floor and on your clothes. Take a shower to wash off any stray hairs and to freshen up.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use your fingers to measure the length of your hair to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Take breaks if you get tired or frustrated.
  • Watch tutorials or ask a friend to help if you are unsure.
  • Invest in good quality scissors for a better haircut.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my hair?

It is recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

2. Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

It is best to cut your hair when it is dry as wet hair can be longer and heavier, leading to uneven haircuts.

3. Is it possible to fix a bad haircut?

Yes, it is possible to fix a bad haircut by going to a professional stylist. They can help to even out the hair and give it a better shape.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, use a nourishing hair mask to keep it healthy and shiny. Avoid using heating tools and harsh chemicals on your hair for a few days. Visit a professional stylist for regular trims to maintain your haircut.

Now that you have learned how to cut your own short hair at home, give it a try and see how it turns out! Remember, practice makes perfect.
