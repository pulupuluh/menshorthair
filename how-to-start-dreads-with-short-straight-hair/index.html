How To Start Dreads With Short Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 21, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have short straight hair and are interested in starting dreadlocks, you might be wondering where to begin. Luckily, with patience, dedication, and the right tools, anyone can start dreadlocks—even those with short, straight hair. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps to start your own dreads at home.

Table of Contents

  • Section 1: Preparation
  • Section 2: Starting the Dreadlocks
  • Section 3: Maintaining Your Dreadlocks
  • Section 4: FAQ about Starting Dreads with Short Straight Hair
  • Section 5: Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

Section 1: Preparation

Before you start your dreadlocks, it’s important to prepare your hair. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Dread wax or locking gel
  • A comb
  • A hair tie or rubber bands
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • Bobby pins (optional)

It’s also a good idea to wash your hair before starting your dreads. This will remove any product buildup and make it easier for your hair to lock up.

Section 2: Starting the Dreadlocks

Now that you have everything you need, it’s time to start your dreadlocks:

  1. Section off a small piece of hair where you want to start your dread.
  2. Apply a small amount of dread wax or locking gel to the section of hair.
  3. Using the comb, backcomb the section of hair. This means that you’ll comb the hair from the ends to the roots, pushing the hair up towards your scalp.
  4. Once you’ve backcombed the hair, twist it tightly in one direction. If necessary, use bobby pins to secure the twist.
  5. Repeat this process with the rest of your hair, sectioning off small pieces and backcombing before twisting.
  6. Once you’ve twisted all of your hair, use a hair tie or rubber band to secure the ends of each dread.
  7. Mist your hair with water to help the dreads set.

It’s important to note that your dreads might not look perfect right away. It can take several weeks or even months for them to fully mature and lock up.

Section 3: Maintaining Your Dreadlocks

Once you’ve started your dreadlocks, it’s important to maintain them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but avoid using conditioner as it can loosen your dreads.
  • Retwist your dreads every few weeks to keep them tight and prevent them from unraveling.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils on your hair, as they can build up and make your dreads look greasy.
  • Keep your scalp clean and healthy by using a tea tree oil or rosewater spray.

Section 4: FAQ about Starting Dreads with Short Straight Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with short straight hair:

Q: How short can my hair be to start dreadlocks?

A: Your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long to start dreadlocks.

Q: Do I need to have natural hair to start dreadlocks?

A: No, anyone can start dreadlocks regardless of their hair type or whether they have chemically treated hair.

Q: Will starting dreadlocks damage my hair?

A: If done correctly, starting dreadlocks should not damage your hair. However, it’s important to avoid using too much product or pulling your hair too tightly when backcombing.

Section 5: Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

Here are some general hair care tips for maintaining healthy dreadlocks:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can dry out your hair and make it brittle.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to keep your hair healthy from the inside out.

Starting dreadlocks with short straight hair might seem daunting, but with patience and the right tools, anyone can do it. Remember to take care of your dreads and maintain them regularly to keep them looking their best.
