How To Air Dry Fine Frizzy Wavy Hair

Written by Petter Jun 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have fine, frizzy, and wavy hair, you know how difficult it can be to style it. Blow drying can make it look even more frizzy, and using hot tools can damage it. The good news is that air-drying your hair is a great way to maintain its health and natural texture. In this article, we'll show you how to air dry fine frizzy wavy hair and provide some tips to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Wash Your Hair
  • Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner
  • Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair
  • Step 4: Let Your Hair Air Dry
  • Tips for Air Drying Fine Frizzy Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Fine Frizzy Wavy Hair

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step to air drying your hair is to wash it. Use a sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, and then rinse it out with lukewarm water. Don't use hot water, as it can dry out your hair and make it frizzier.


Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and moisturize your hair. Use a product that's specifically designed for fine, frizzy, and wavy hair. Apply the conditioner to the ends of your hair and work your way up towards your scalp. Don't use too much product, as it can weigh down your hair.


Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair

Next, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out the excess water from your hair. Don't rub your hair with a towel, as it can cause frizz. Instead, scrunch your hair upwards towards your scalp. This will help enhance your hair's natural texture and create waves.


Step 4: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Once you've scrunched out the excess water, let your hair air dry. Don't touch your hair while it's drying, as this can cause frizz. If you need to leave the house before your hair is dry, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the process. Use a low heat setting and scrunch your hair while you blow dry it.


Tips for Air Drying Fine Frizzy Wavy Hair

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before washing it.
  • Don't brush your hair when it's wet, as it can cause breakage and frizz.
  • If you have trouble with frizz, try using a silk pillowcase at night to prevent your hair from rubbing against cotton.
  • Don't touch your hair while it's drying, as this can cause frizz.
  • If you need to leave the house before your hair is dry, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the process.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have fine, frizzy, and wavy hair, you may want to wash it every other day or every two days to prevent it from getting oily.


Q: Can I use a hair dryer on my hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair dryer on your hair, but make sure to use a diffuser attachment and a low heat setting to prevent damage and frizz.


Q: How can I prevent my hair from getting frizzy?

A: Use a sulfate-free shampoo, apply a leave-in conditioner, and avoid touching your hair while it's drying. You can also try using a silk pillowcase and a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.


Hair Care for Fine Frizzy Wavy Hair

If you have fine, frizzy, and wavy hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a leave-in conditioner that are specifically designed for fine, frizzy, and wavy hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and don't brush it when it's wet.
  • Avoid touching your hair while it's drying, and use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer if you need to blow dry it.

Air drying your fine, frizzy, and wavy hair can be a great way to maintain its health and natural texture. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve beautiful, frizz-free waves without damaging your hair. Remember to use hair care products that are specifically designed for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools as much as possible. Happy air-drying!
