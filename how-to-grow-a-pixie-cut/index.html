How To Grow A Pixie Cut A Guide For 2023

If you've recently chopped off your hair into a pixie cut, you might be wondering how to grow it out. Fear not, because we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to grow a pixie cut. From hair care tips to frequently asked questions, we'll cover everything you need to know to get those luscious locks back.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow a Pixie Cut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

How to Grow a Pixie Cut

Growing out a pixie cut can be a daunting task, but with some patience and TLC, you can get through it. The first step is to have a game plan. Decide on a length you're aiming for and create a timeline for achieving it. Remember to be realistic with your expectations, as hair growth varies from person to person.

Set a Timeline

Depending on how short your pixie cut is, it can take anywhere from six months to a year to grow out. Keep this in mind when setting your timeline. Be patient and don't get discouraged if progress seems slow at first.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

While it may seem counterintuitive, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow faster. It prevents split ends and breakage, which can hinder growth. Aim for a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and promote growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have a game plan in place, it's time to start growing out your pixie cut. Follow these steps to help speed up the process.

Step 1: Be Patient

As mentioned earlier, growing out a pixie cut takes time. Be patient and don't get discouraged if it seems like your hair isn't growing fast enough.

Step 2: Experiment with Styles

While growing out your hair, experiment with different styles to keep things fresh. Try braids, top knots, and headbands to switch up your look.

Step 3: Get Regular Trims

As previously mentioned, regular trims can help your hair grow faster. Make sure to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage.

Step 4: Use Hair Growth Products

There are a variety of hair growth products on the market that can help speed up the process. Look for products containing biotin, keratin, and other essential vitamins and minerals for healthy hair growth.

Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

In addition to following the step-by-step guide, there are a few tips you can keep in mind to help you grow out your pixie cut.

Tip 1: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid harsh brushing and styling, as it can lead to breakage and damage. Use a wide-tooth comb and be gentle when detangling.

Tip 2: Take Care of Your Scalp

A healthy scalp is essential for healthy hair growth. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing your hair.

Tip 3: Consider Hair Extensions

If you're really struggling to grow out your hair, consider getting hair extensions. They can help give the appearance of longer hair while you wait for your natural hair to grow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to a year to grow out a pixie cut, depending on the length you're aiming for.

Q: Can I still style my hair while I'm growing out a pixie cut?

A: Absolutely! Experiment with different styles to keep things fresh while you wait for your hair to grow.

Q: Will getting regular trims really help my hair grow faster?

A: Yes! Regular trims prevent split ends and breakage, which can hinder hair growth. Make sure to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

Hair Care for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

In addition to following the step-by-step guide and tips, it's important to take care of your hair while you're growing out a pixie cut.

Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for a shampoo and conditioner that's gentle on your hair and scalp. Avoid formulas with harsh chemicals and sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Use a Hair Mask Once a Week

A weekly hair mask can help nourish and strengthen your hair, promoting healthy growth. Look for masks containing ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, and shea butter.

Protect Your Hair from Heat Damage

Heat styling can damage your hair, leading to breakage and split ends. When possible, air-dry your hair and avoid using heat tools like straighteners and curling irons.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to growing out your pixie cut. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair, and you'll have long, luscious locks in no time.
