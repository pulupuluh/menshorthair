Is There A Way To Keep Your Hair During Chemo

Written by Luffy Apr 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Chemo is a difficult time for anyone who has to go through it. One of the most devastating side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. For many people, losing their hair can be a significant source of distress and anxiety. Fortunately, there are ways to minimize or even prevent hair loss during chemo. In this article, we will explore the options available to you and provide you with some tips for maintaining healthy hair during this challenging time.

Description

Chemotherapy is a treatment for cancer that involves the use of drugs to kill cancer cells. Unfortunately, these drugs also attack healthy cells in the body, including hair follicles. This can lead to hair loss, which can be temporary or permanent, depending on the type of drugs used and the dosage. Hair loss can occur on the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and other parts of the body.

How to Keep Your Hair During Chemo

If you are undergoing chemo and want to minimize or prevent hair loss, there are several options available to you. These include:

Scalp Cooling

Scalp cooling is a technique that involves lowering the temperature of the scalp to reduce the blood flow to the hair follicles. This can help protect the follicles from the damaging effects of chemotherapy drugs. Scalp cooling can be done using a cooling cap or helmet, which is worn before, during, and after chemo treatment. Scalp cooling has been shown to be effective in reducing hair loss in some people, but it is not suitable for everyone.

Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a medication that is used to treat hair loss. It works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles and stimulating hair growth. Minoxidil can be applied topically to the scalp before and during chemo treatment. Studies have shown that minoxidil can reduce hair loss in some people undergoing chemotherapy.

Wigs and Hairpieces

Wigs and hairpieces are a popular way to cover hair loss during chemo. They come in a variety of styles, colors, and materials, and can be customized to fit your head and natural hair. Wigs and hairpieces can be made from synthetic or human hair and can be styled and cut to your liking. They are an excellent option for people who want to maintain their appearance and self-esteem during chemo.

Step by Step Guide

If you are considering scalp cooling, minoxidil, or wigs and hairpieces, here is a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Scalp Cooling

  1. Talk to your doctor about scalp cooling and whether it is right for you.
  2. Find a scalp cooling provider in your area.
  3. Schedule a consultation and fitting appointment.
  4. Wear the cooling cap or helmet before, during, and after chemo treatment.
  5. Follow the provider's instructions for care and maintenance of the cooling cap or helmet.

Minoxidil

  1. Talk to your doctor about minoxidil and whether it is right for you.
  2. Obtain a prescription for minoxidil from your doctor or purchase it over-the-counter.
  3. Apply minoxidil topically to the scalp before and during chemo treatment.
  4. Follow the instructions on the label for dosing and application.
  5. Monitor your hair growth and talk to your doctor if you have any concerns.

Wigs and Hairpieces

  1. Research wig and hairpiece providers in your area.
  2. Make an appointment for a consultation and fitting.
  3. Select a wig or hairpiece that matches your natural hair color and style.
  4. Have the wig or hairpiece customized to fit your head and natural hair.
  5. Learn how to care for and maintain your wig or hairpiece.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair During Chemo

Even if you choose to undergo scalp cooling, use minoxidil, or wear a wig or hairpiece, it is still essential to take care of your natural hair during chemo. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of sulfates and parabens.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, which can damage hair.
  • Do not color or perm your hair during chemo.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair, such as braids and ponytails.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQ

Can I prevent hair loss during chemo?

There is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemo, but scalp cooling, minoxidil, and wigs and hairpieces can help reduce hair loss in some people.

Will my hair grow back after chemo?

Yes, in most cases, hair will grow back after chemo. However, it may take several months for hair to grow back fully, and the texture and color of the hair may be different than before.

Can I color or perm my hair during chemo?

No, it is not recommended to color or perm your hair during chemo, as these treatments can damage already fragile hair.

Hair Care During Chemo

Going through chemo can be a challenging time, but there are ways to minimize hair loss and maintain healthy hair. Whether you choose scalp cooling, minoxidil, or wigs and hairpieces, it is essential to take care of your natural hair during chemo. Use gentle hair products, avoid heat styling tools, and protect your hair from the sun and wind. With a little care and attention, you can emerge from chemo with healthy, beautiful hair.
