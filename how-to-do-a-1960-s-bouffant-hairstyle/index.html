How To Do A 1960 S Bouffant Hairstyle Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a retro hairstyle that's both elegant and chic, a 1960s bouffant is the perfect choice. This iconic hairstyle is characterized by its voluminous crown, sleek sides, and smooth back. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to achieve this classic look in just a few simple steps.

Description

The 1960s bouffant is a timeless hairstyle that was popularized during the mod era. It's a glamorous and sophisticated look that features a voluminous crown and sleek sides that are combed back into a low ponytail or bun. This hairstyle is perfect for special occasions, such as weddings, proms, or parties. It's also a great option for anyone who wants to add some retro flair to their everyday look.

How To

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's slightly damp. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots and blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume. Once your hair is dry, brush it out to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Tease Your Crown

Take a small section of hair from the crown of your head and tease it using a fine-toothed comb. Hold the section of hair straight up and backcomb it in short, rapid strokes towards your scalp. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you've teased your entire crown.

Step 3: Smooth Your Sides

Using a comb, smooth the sides of your hair back and secure them with bobby pins. Make sure the sides are sleek and tight, without any bumps or flyaways.

Step 4: Create Your Ponytail

Gather the rest of your hair into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck. Secure it with an elastic band and use a comb to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 5: Add Some Volume

Take a small section of hair from your ponytail and tease it using a fine-toothed comb. Hold the section of hair straight up and backcomb it in short, rapid strokes towards your scalp. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you've teased your entire ponytail.

Step 6: Smooth Your Ponytail

Using a comb, smooth the top layer of your ponytail back and over the teased section. This will create a smooth, polished look on top of your voluminous crown.

Step 7: Finish with Hairspray

Spray your entire hairstyle with a strong-hold hairspray to keep it in place. You can also use a bit of shine serum or spray to add some extra gloss and polish to your hair.

Tips

  • For added volume, try using hot rollers or a curling iron on the crown of your head before teasing.
  • If you have fine or thin hair, use a volumizing spray or dry shampoo to add some texture and grip to your hair.
  • If you're short on time, skip the teasing and opt for a volumizing hairpiece or clip-in extensions instead.

FAQ

Q: Can I do a bouffant if I have short hair?

A: Yes, you can still do a mini bouffant with short hair. Just tease the crown and use bobby pins to secure the sides back. You can also use a volumizing spray or mousse to add some extra lift and texture.

Q: How do I keep my bouffant from falling flat?

A: To keep your bouffant from falling flat, use a strong-hold hairspray and avoid touching or brushing your hair too much throughout the day. You can also use a bit of dry shampoo or texturizing spray to refresh your hair and add some extra grip.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, it's important to use quality hair care products and avoid over-styling or using too much heat. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type, and try to limit your use of hot tools like curling irons and straighteners. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

With these simple steps and tips, you can easily achieve a stunning 1960s bouffant hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion. Whether you're attending a wedding or just want to add some retro flair to your everyday look, this classic hairstyle is sure to turn heads and make you feel fabulous.
