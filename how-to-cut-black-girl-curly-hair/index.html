How To Cut Black Girl Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting black girl curly hair can be a daunting task, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be done with ease. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to cut black girl curly hair and provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your curls looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Black Girl Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Black Girl Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Black Girl Curly Hair

How to Cut Black Girl Curly Hair

Before you start cutting black girl curly hair, it's important to have the right tools. You will need a good pair of hair cutting scissors, a spray bottle filled with water, a wide-tooth comb, and a mirror.

Step 1: Wash and Detangle the Hair

Start by washing the hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Once the hair is clean, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it. Be gentle and work from the ends to the roots to avoid breakage.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Start cutting the hair in small sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use the hair cutting scissors to trim the ends and shape the hair. Be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After cutting each section, use a mirror to check for evenness. Make any necessary adjustments before moving on to the next section.

Step 5: Finish the Haircut

Once you have cut all the sections, remove the hair clips and comb through the hair to check for any unevenness. Make any final adjustments before styling the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut black girl curly hair:

  1. Wash and detangle the hair
  2. Section the hair
  3. Cut the hair in small sections
  4. Check for evenness
  5. Finish the haircut

Tips for Cutting Black Girl Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting black girl curly hair:

  • Use hair cutting scissors, not regular scissors
  • Start with small sections and work your way up
  • Be careful not to cut too much off at once
  • Check for evenness after cutting each section
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the hair

FAQs

Can I cut my own black girl curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own black girl curly hair, but it's important to have the right tools and techniques. It may be easier to have a friend or professional stylist help you.

How often should I cut my black girl curly hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my black girl curly hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to cut black girl curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in uneven cuts.

Hair Care for Black Girl Curly Hair

After cutting your black girl curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls hydrated
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause breakage
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

Cutting black girl curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and check for evenness after each cut. With these tips and hair care advice, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful.
