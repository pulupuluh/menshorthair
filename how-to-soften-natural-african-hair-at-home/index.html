How To Soften Natural African Hair At Home

Written by Daniel Aug 05, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of having dry, brittle, and hard-to-manage natural African hair? Softening your hair can help make it more manageable, easier to style, and less prone to breakage. In this article, we will show you how to soften natural African hair at home using simple and affordable methods.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Soften Natural African Hair at Home
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Softening Natural African Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Routine for Softened Natural African Hair

Description

Natural African hair is often tightly coiled and can be difficult to manage. It can be prone to dryness, breakage, and tangling. Softening your hair can help make it more manageable, easier to style, and less prone to breakage. There are several methods you can use to soften your natural African hair at home.

How to Soften Natural African Hair at Home

Here are some simple and affordable methods you can use to soften your natural African hair at home:

1. Hot Oil Treatment

Hot oil treatments are a great way to moisturize and soften your hair. You can use any natural oil like coconut, olive, or avocado oil. Simply warm up the oil and apply it to your hair, focusing on the ends. Cover your hair with a shower cap and let it sit for 30-60 minutes. Rinse out the oil with warm water and shampoo as usual.

2. Deep Conditioning

Deep conditioning is another effective way to soften natural African hair. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner or make your own using natural ingredients like honey, avocado, and banana. Apply the deep conditioner to your hair and cover it with a shower cap for 30-60 minutes. Rinse out the conditioner with warm water and shampoo as usual.

3. Apple Cider Vinegar Rinse

Apple cider vinegar is a natural hair conditioner that can help soften and detangle your hair. Mix one part apple cider vinegar with two parts water and apply it to your hair after shampooing. Let it sit for a few minutes and then rinse out with warm water.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to soften your natural African hair at home:

Step 1: Choose a Softening Method

Choose a softening method that works best for your hair type and needs. You can try one or all of the methods mentioned above.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Gather all the supplies you will need for the softening method you have chosen. This may include natural oils, deep conditioner, apple cider vinegar, shower cap, and towel.

Step 3: Apply the Softening Method

Apply the softening method to your hair according to the instructions. Make sure to focus on the ends of your hair and avoid applying too much product to the roots.

Step 4: Cover Your Hair

After applying the softening method, cover your hair with a shower cap or plastic wrap. This will help trap the moisture and heat, allowing the product to penetrate your hair more effectively.

Step 5: Wait

Wait for the softening method to work its magic. Most methods require you to wait for 30-60 minutes. Use this time to relax or do some other activities.

Step 6: Rinse and Shampoo

Rinse out the softening method with warm water and shampoo your hair as usual. Make sure to rinse out all the product to avoid buildup.

Tips for Softening Natural African Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you soften your natural African hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to avoid breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can dry out your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by using a satin or silk pillowcase and wrapping your hair in a silk scarf.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and health.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I soften my natural African hair without using heat?

A: Yes, there are several methods you can use to soften your natural African hair without using heat, such as hot oil treatments and deep conditioning.

Q: How often should I soften my natural African hair?

A: It is recommended to soften your natural African hair once a week or as needed, depending on your hair type and needs.

Q: Can I use the same softening method every time?

A: It is recommended to switch up your softening methods to avoid buildup and to ensure that your hair is getting all the nutrients it needs.

Hair Care Routine for Softened Natural African Hair

After softening your natural African hair, it is important to maintain its softness and health. Here is a simple hair care routine you can follow:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair once a week.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to moisturize your hair daily.
  • Avoid using heat tools and harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can soften your natural African hair at home and maintain its softness and health. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you will see results over time.
