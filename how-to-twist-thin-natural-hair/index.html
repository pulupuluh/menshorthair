How To Twist Thin Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Apr 08, 2023 · 6 min read

Twisting thin natural hair can be a bit challenging, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be done successfully. In this article, we will discuss how to twist thin natural hair and provide you with helpful tips to make the process easier.

How to Twist Thin Natural Hair

Before you begin twisting your hair, make sure it is clean and well moisturized. Here are the steps to twist thin natural hair:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thin or thick your hair is, but aim for sections that are about 1/4 to 1/2 inch in width.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product such as a twisting cream or gel to each section of hair. Make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout the section.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Take two strands of hair from the section and twist them around each other, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Keep twisting until you reach the end of the section.

Step 4: Secure the Twist

Once you reach the end of the section, use a hair clip or bobby pin to secure the twist. Repeat these steps for each section of hair.

Tips for Twisting Thin Natural Hair

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when twisting thin natural hair:

  • Use a lightweight styling product to avoid weighing down your hair
  • Avoid using too much tension when twisting your hair to prevent breakage
  • Twist your hair in the direction that it naturally curls
  • Consider using perm rods or flexi rods to set your twists

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about twisting thin natural hair:

How long will my twists last?

The longevity of your twists will depend on a variety of factors such as the products you use, how well you maintain them, and your hair type. On average, twists can last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

Can I wash my hair while it's in twists?

Yes, you can wash your hair while it's in twists. Just be sure to be gentle and avoid manipulating the twists too much.

How do I maintain my twists?

To maintain your twists, you can moisturize your hair daily with a lightweight oil or moisturizer. You can also use a satin scarf or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.

Hair Care for Twisted Natural Hair

It's important to take care of your hair while it's in twists to prevent breakage and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for caring for your twisted natural hair:

  • Keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using heat on your hair
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin scarf or bonnet
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully twist your thin natural hair and maintain healthy hair.
