How To Cut Mullet Hairstyle A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 22, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle that's both edgy and versatile, the mullet might be the perfect choice for you. This iconic haircut was popularized in the '80s and has since made a comeback, with many celebrities sporting the look. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut mullet hairstyle in relaxed English language, so you can achieve the look at home.

Description of Mullet Hairstyle

The mullet hairstyle is characterized by shorter hair on the top and sides, with longer hair in the back. The length of the hair can vary, as can the style of the cut. Some mullets are more dramatic, with a distinct contrast between the short and long sections, while others are more subtle. The mullet can be styled in many different ways, from messy and textured to sleek and polished.

How to Cut Mullet Hairstyle

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to hold sections of hair in place. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water, as damp hair is easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use the comb to create a part down the center of your head, separating the top and bottom sections. Clip the top section of hair out of the way, so you can focus on the bottom half.

Step 3: Cut the Sides and Top

Using your scissors, start cutting the sides and top of your hair. The length of the hair will depend on the style you're going for. For a more dramatic mullet, you'll want to leave the hair shorter on the sides and longer on top. For a subtler look, you can leave the hair a bit longer on the sides.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Once you've finished cutting the sides and top, move on to the back of your hair. This is where the mullet really comes to life. Start by sectioning off a small section of hair at the nape of your neck. This will be the shortest part of the mullet.

Next, use the comb to lift up a section of hair just above the first section. This will be the longest part of the mullet. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length.

Continue working your way up the back of your head, cutting sections of hair to create the mullet shape. Remember to take your time and be precise.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet Hairstyle

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect mullet:

  • Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and ensure a more even cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
  • Don't be afraid to take it slow. It's better to take your time and get it right than rush and end up with a bad cut.
  • If you're not sure about the length, start with a longer length and gradually go shorter. You can always cut more off, but you can't add it back once it's gone.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about mullet hairstyles:

Q: Who can pull off a mullet?

A: Anyone can pull off a mullet, regardless of gender or hair type. It's a versatile hairstyle that can be tailored to suit your individual style and preferences.

Q: How long does a mullet take to grow out?

A: The length of time it takes to grow out a mullet depends on the length of your hair and how quickly it grows. On average, it can take anywhere from 6 months to a year to fully grow out a mullet.

Q: Can I style my mullet in different ways?

A: Absolutely! The mullet is a versatile hairstyle that can be styled in many different ways. You can play around with different textures, lengths, and products to create a look that's uniquely your own.

Hair Care for Mullet Hairstyle

To keep your mullet looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your mullet.

With these tips and a bit of practice, you'll be able to cut a mullet hairstyle that's both stylish and easy to maintain. So why not give it a try?
