Why Glasses Don t Suit Me A Guide To Finding The Right Eyewear

Description

Do you find that glasses never seem to suit you no matter how many pairs you try on? You're not alone. Many people struggle to find the right pair of glasses that complements their face shape, personal style, and overall aesthetic.

This guide is designed to help you understand why glasses might not suit you and provide tips and tricks for finding the perfect pair. From determining your face shape to choosing the right frame material, we'll cover everything you need to know to rock your glasses with confidence.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the perfect pair of glasses is to determine your face shape. Your face shape will help you narrow down the types of frames that will work best for you. There are six main face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Triangle

To determine your face shape, start by pulling your hair back and looking in the mirror. Take note of the widest part of your face, the length of your face, and the shape of your jawline. Use these measurements to determine your face shape.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, your face is roughly the same length and width. Your jawline is soft and rounded, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. Look for frames that are angular and narrow to balance out the softness of your face shape.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, your face is longer than it is wide. Your forehead is slightly wider than your jawline, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. Lucky for you, most frame shapes will suit an oval face. Look for frames that are as wide as or wider than the broadest part of your face to maintain balance.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, your face is roughly the same length and width. Your jawline is strong and angular, and your forehead is the same width as your jawline. Look for frames that are round or oval to soften the angles of your face shape.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, your face is wider at the temples and narrows at the jawline. Your forehead is wider than your jawline, and your chin is pointed. Look for frames that are wider at the bottom to balance out the narrowness of your chin.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, your face is widest at the temples and narrows at the forehead and jawline. Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face. Look for frames that are wider than your cheekbones to maintain balance.

Triangle Face Shape

If you have a triangle face shape, your face is wider at the jawline and narrows at the forehead. Your chin is pointed. Look for frames that are wider at the top to balance out the narrowness of your forehead.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding the Right Eyewear

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to start looking for the right pair of glasses. Follow these steps to find the perfect pair:

  1. Consider your personal style. Do you prefer bold and trendy or classic and understated?
  2. Determine your budget. Glasses can range from budget-friendly to high-end designer.
  3. Choose a frame material. Do you prefer plastic, metal, or a combination of both?
  4. Pick a frame shape that complements your face shape. Refer back to our face shape guide if you need help.
  5. Try on different frame sizes. The frame should fit snugly but not feel tight or uncomfortable.
  6. Take into account your prescription. Some prescriptions require certain types of frames or lenses.
  7. Consider any additional features you might need, such as anti-glare or UV protection.
  8. Choose a color that complements your skin tone and personal style.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect Pair of Glasses

Still struggling to find the right pair of glasses? Try these tips:

  • Ask for a second opinion from a friend or family member.
  • Consider purchasing glasses online for a wider range of options.
  • Visit a reputable optometrist or eyewear retailer for professional advice.
  • Don't be afraid to try on different styles and shapes until you find the perfect pair.
  • Remember that glasses are a fashion statement and should reflect your personal style and aesthetic.

FAQ

Why don't glasses suit me?

Glasses might not suit you for a variety of reasons, including your face shape, personal style, and aesthetic preferences. It's important to find the right pair that complements your features and makes you feel confident.

How do I choose the right frame size?

The right frame size should fit snugly but not feel tight or uncomfortable. Look for frames that sit comfortably on your nose and ears without slipping or sliding. Try on different sizes to find the perfect fit.

Can I wear glasses with any hairstyle?

How to Style Your Hair with Glasses

A common concern for glasses wearers is how to style their hair. Here are some tips:

  • Choose a hairstyle that complements your face shape and features.
  • Avoid hairstyles that cover your face or clash with your glasses.
  • Show off your frames with a sleek ponytail or bun.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands or clips.
  • Consider getting a haircut that frames your face and complements your glasses.

In conclusion, glasses might not suit you for a variety of reasons, but with the right frame shape, size, and material, you can find the perfect pair that complements your features and personal style. Remember to take your time, try on different options, and ask for professional advice if needed.
