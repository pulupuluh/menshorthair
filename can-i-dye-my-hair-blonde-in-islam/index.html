Can I Dye My Hair Blonde In Islam

Written by Luffy Jun 17, 2023 Â· 15 min read

If you are a Muslim woman who wants to change her hair color, you may be wondering whether it is permissible to dye your hair blonde in Islam. The short answer is yes, you can dye your hair blonde in Islam, as long as you follow certain guidelines and conditions. In this article, we will explore the Islamic rulings on hair dyeing, provide step-by-step instructions on how to dye your hair blonde, and share some useful tips and FAQs to help you take care of your hair.

Islamic Rulings on Hair Dyeing

Islamic scholars have different opinions on whether hair dyeing is allowed or not in Islam. Some argue that it is haram (forbidden) because it involves changing the natural color of the hair, which is a form of altering the creation of Allah. Others believe that it is permissible as long as it does not cause harm or lead to excessive vanity or imitation of non-Muslims. According to the majority of scholars, hair dyeing is allowed, as long as it meets the following conditions:

1. It should be temporary and not permanent.

This means that you should use a hair dye that washes out after a few weeks, rather than a permanent dye that changes the structure of your hair and lasts for months. Permanent dyes may also contain harmful chemicals that can damage your hair or scalp.

2. It should not cause harm or allergies.

You should choose a hair dye that is safe and suitable for your hair type and skin sensitivity. Some dyes may contain ingredients that can cause allergic reactions, itching, or burning. You should also avoid dyeing your hair too frequently or using excessive amounts of dye, as this can weaken your hair and make it prone to breakage or split ends.

3. It should not lead to excessive vanity or pride.

You should avoid dyeing your hair for the sole purpose of showing off or attracting attention, as this goes against the modesty and humility that Islam promotes. Hair dyeing should be done in moderation and with the intention of enhancing your natural beauty, not masking it or seeking approval from others.

How to Dye Your Hair Blonde in Islam

If you have decided to dye your hair blonde in Islam, here is a step-by-step guide to help you do it safely and correctly:

Step 1: Choose the right shade of blonde.

There are many shades of blonde, ranging from light to dark, warm to cool, and natural to artificial. You should choose a shade that complements your skin tone, eye color, and personal style, and that looks natural and modest. Avoid very light or platinum shades, as these can be too striking or attention-grabbing.

Step 2: Prepare your hair and skin.

Before dyeing your hair, you should wash it with a mild shampoo and dry it thoroughly. You should also apply a barrier cream or petroleum jelly around your hairline, ears, and neck, to protect your skin from staining. Wear gloves to avoid getting the dye on your hands.

Step 3: Mix and apply the dye.

Follow the instructions on the hair dye package to mix the dye and developer in a non-metallic bowl. Use a tint brush or applicator bottle to apply the dye evenly to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way to the tips. Make sure to saturate each strand of hair and avoid overlapping or dripping.

Step 4: Wait and rinse.

After applying the dye, cover your hair with a shower cap or plastic wrap, and wait for the recommended processing time (usually 20-30 minutes). Do not leave the dye on for too long, as this can result in uneven or brassy color. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water, until the water runs clear. Do not shampoo or condition your hair immediately after dyeing, as this can strip away the color and cause fading.

Step 5: Style and maintain your hair.

Once your hair is dry, you can style it as usual, using heat tools or hair accessories if desired. You should also take care of your dyed hair by using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, avoiding excessive heat or sun exposure, and trimming your ends regularly to prevent split ends. You can also apply a hair mask or oil treatment once a week, to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Tips for Dyeing Your Hair Blonde in Islam

Here are some additional tips to help you dye your hair blonde in Islam:

1. Consult with a professional stylist.

If you are not sure how to dye your hair or what shade to choose, you can consult with a professional stylist who is knowledgeable about Islamic guidelines and practices. They can also help you avoid common mistakes or risks, such as using the wrong type of dye or damaging your hair.

2. Test the dye on a small patch of hair.

Before applying the dye to your entire head, you should test it on a small section of hair, to see how it reacts and whether you like the color. This can also help you avoid allergic reactions or unexpected results.

3. Avoid dyeing your hair during menstruation or pregnancy.

According to Islamic teachings, women are advised to avoid certain acts of worship or hygiene during menstruation or postnatal bleeding, such as prayer, fasting, or touching the Quran. Some scholars also recommend avoiding hair dyeing during these times, as it may cause unnecessary harm or discomfort. Similarly, pregnant women should consult with their doctors before using any hair dye, as some chemicals may affect the fetus.

FAQ about Dyeing Hair Blonde in Islam

Here are some frequently asked questions about dyeing hair blonde in Islam:

1. Is it haram to dye your hair blonde in Islam?

No, it is not haram to dye your hair blonde in Islam, as long as it meets the conditions and guidelines mentioned above.

2. Can Muslim men dye their hair blonde?

Yes, Muslim men can dye their hair blonde, as long as it is done in moderation and with the intention of enhancing their appearance or correcting a defect, such as gray hair or baldness.

3. Can I dye my hair blonde for my wedding or special occasion?

Yes, you can dye your hair blonde for a special occasion or event, as long as it does not lead to vanity or extravagance, and as long as it is done in accordance with Islamic guidelines and values.

4. Will dyeing my hair blonde affect my wudu or ghusl?

No, dyeing your hair blonde will not affect your wudu or ghusl, as long as the dye does not prevent water from reaching your scalp or hair roots. You should also avoid using waterproof or oil-based dyes, which may create a barrier between your hair and water.

5. Can I dye my hair blonde if I wear hijab?

Yes, you can dye your hair blonde if you wear hijab, as long as it is not visible to non-mahram men or leads to pride or arrogance. You should also avoid using strong or pungent dyes, which may affect your hijab or cause discomfort.

Hair Care for Blonde Dyed Hair

If you have dyed your hair blonde, it is important to take care of it properly, to maintain its color, shine, and health. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip away the natural oils and color from your hair. You should use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that is designed for color-treated hair, to avoid fading or dryness.

2. Avoid excessive heat or sun exposure.

Blonde hair is more vulnerable to heat damage and UV rays, which can cause fading, dryness, or breakage. You should avoid using hot tools such as curling irons, flat irons, or hair dryers too often, and protect your hair from sun exposure by wearing a hat or scarf.

3. Trim your ends regularly.

Split ends can make your hair look dull and frizzy, and can also cause breakage or tangling. You should trim your ends every 6-8 weeks, to remove the damaged or split parts and promote healthy growth.

4. Apply a hair mask or oil treatment.

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore the moisture and nutrients to your hair, and make it smoother and shinier. You can use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week, by applying it to your damp hair and leaving it on for 15-30 minutes, before rinsing it off.

In conclusion, dyeing your hair blonde in Islam is allowed, as long as it is done in accordance with Islamic guidelines and values, and with the intention of enhancing your natural beauty and not showing off or imitating non-Muslims. You should also take care of your dyed hair by using gentle and safe products, avoiding excessive heat or sun
