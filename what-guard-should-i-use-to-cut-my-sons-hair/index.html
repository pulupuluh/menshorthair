What Guard Should I Use To Cut My Son s Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 01, 2023 · 7 min read

As a parent, it's important to learn how to cut your child's hair. Whether you're trying to save money or simply want to bond with your child, cutting their hair can be a rewarding experience. However, one common question parents have is what guard should they use to cut their son's hair. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about selecting the right guard for your child's haircut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Guard
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Son's Hair
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Son

Description

Before we dive into the specifics of what guard to use, let's talk about what a guard is. A guard is a small attachment that clips onto the blade of your clippers. It determines the length of the hair that is cut. Guards come in different sizes, ranging from 0.5mm to 25mm. The smaller the number, the closer the cut.

How to Choose the Right Guard

Choosing the right guard can be overwhelming, especially if you're new to cutting hair. Here are some things to consider:

  • Hair Type: The type of hair your son has will play a role in what guard to use. If he has thick, coarse hair, you may need a larger guard. If he has thin hair, you may need a smaller guard.
  • Length: Think about how long you want your son's hair to be. If you want a shorter cut, you'll need a smaller guard. If you want a longer cut, you'll need a larger guard.
  • Style: Consider the hairstyle you want to achieve. Different styles require different guards. For example, a crew cut requires a shorter guard, while a buzz cut requires an even shorter guard.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Son's Hair

Now that you know what guard to use, let's talk about how to cut your son's hair. Follow these steps for a successful haircut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Choose the right guard for your desired length.
  3. Attach the guard to your clippers.
  4. Start at the base of the neck and work your way up.
  5. Use a comb to guide the clippers in a straight line.
  6. Work in small sections, moving from the back to the sides.
  7. Blend the sides into the top using a longer guard.
  8. Use scissors to trim any excess hair.
  9. Clean up the hairline with a razor or trimmer.
  10. Style as desired.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a successful haircut:

  • Invest in Quality Clippers: Cheap clippers can snag and pull hair, making the haircut uncomfortable for your son.
  • Work in a Well-Lit Area: Good lighting is essential for a precise cut.
  • Take Your Time: Rushing through the haircut can lead to mistakes.
  • Don't Cut Too Much: It's better to cut less and have to go back than to cut too much and have to wait for it to grow back.

FAQs

Here are some common questions parents have about cutting their son's hair:

  • How Often Should I Cut My Son's Hair? This depends on how fast his hair grows and the style you're trying to achieve. On average, most boys need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.
  • Can I Cut My Son's Hair While It's Wet? It's best to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be harder to cut evenly.
  • What Should I Do if I Make a Mistake? Don't panic! Mistakes happen. You can always go back and trim the hair again or take your son to a professional for a fix.

Hair Care Tips for Your Son

Once you've cut your son's hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash Hair Regularly: Regular washing helps keep hair clean and healthy.
  • Use a Gentle Shampoo: Look for a shampoo that is gentle and free of harsh chemicals.
  • Condition Hair: Conditioning helps keep hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid Heat Styling: Heat styling can damage hair, so it's best to avoid it whenever possible.
  • Trim Hair Regularly: Regular trims help keep hair looking neat and healthy.

In conclusion, cutting your son's hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. By choosing the right guard and following the steps outlined in this article, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and have fun with it!
